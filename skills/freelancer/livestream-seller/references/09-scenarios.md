# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Zero Sales During Prime Time Stream

**User:** "I have 500 viewers but no one is buying. What am I doing wrong?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Low conversion with high viewership points to a few common issues:
>
> 1. **Product price mismatch:** Are you selling to your audience's price range?
> 2. **Missing urgency:** No "limited stock," no countdown, no exclusive deals
> 3. **Weak CTA:** Simply showing a product isn't enough; explicitly say "Link in bio, 20% off for the next 10 minutes"
> 4. **No social proof:** Read real reviews, show real testimonials
> 5. **Poor product display:** Lighting, camera angle, or demo quality
>
> Test: Offer a time-limited exclusive discount and track if sales spike. If yes, the issue is urgency and pricing perception.

## 9.2 Fake Follower Bot Activity Detected

**User:** "My follower count spiked overnight but engagement is zero. What happened?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> You likely purchased fake followers or a follow-for-follow scheme:
>
> 1. **Do not panic buy** - fake followers hurt algorithm more than having few real ones
> 2. **Audit account health** via platform analytics (Instagram Insights, TikTok Analytics)
> 3. **Focus on organic growth** - quality content, consistent posting
> 4. **Report suspicious accounts** - helps platform clean up
> 5. **Never buy followers again** - violates most platform terms
>
> Key considerations:
> - Bots damage algorithm ranking
> - Authentic engagement matters more than follower count
> - Brands check engagement rate before partnerships (2-3% is good)

## 9.3 Platform Suspends Account During Major Sale

**User:** "My account was suspended right as I hit 1,000 viewers during a flash sale. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Account suspension during peak time requires immediate action:
>
> - **Do not create a new account** - violates terms and compounds problems
> - **File appeal immediately** - use official appeals form with account ID
> - **Switch to backup platform** - redirect viewers via Instagram Story, Twitter, email list
> - **Communicate with viewers** - apologize for disruption, give timeline
> - **Document everything** - screenshot the suspension notice, save viewership data
> - **Review community guidelines** - identify what triggered suspension
>
> Consult the platform's help center for appeal templates. Most suspensions can be resolved within 48 hours if properly appealed.

## 9.4 Copyrighted Music Strikes

**User:** "I received a copyright strike for using a popular song. My account has 3 strikes now. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Copyright strikes are serious and can lead to account termination:
>
> - **Dispute if fair use applies** - but have evidence ready (transformative content, educational context)
> - **Delete or mute the content** if strike is valid - prevents additional strikes
> - **Use royalty-free music** - Epidemic Sound, Artlist, YouTube Audio Library
> - **Build a music library** before next stream
> - **Request permission** from artists for future use
>
> Consult an IP attorney for commercial streaming music licensing requirements.

## 9.5 Competitor Sabotage in Comments

**User:** "A competitor keeps leaving negative comments about my products during my streams. Can I stop them?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Competitive interference requires strategic response:
>
> - **Enable comment moderation** - filter keywords, require approval
> - **Block the account** - repeatedly disruptive accounts should be blocked
> - **Report to platform** - competitor sabotage may violate terms of service
> - **Do not publicly respond** - gives them attention and platform
> - **Document evidence** - screenshot harassment for potential legal action
> - **Focus on your community** - loyal viewers will defend you
>
> If harassment escalates to threats or defamation, consult a lawyer about cease-and-desist options.
