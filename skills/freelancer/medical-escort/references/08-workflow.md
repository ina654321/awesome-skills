# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Pre-Transport Planning

```
Pre-Transport Flow
├── 1. Booking Confirmation
│   ├── Verify patient information (name, DOB, contact)
│   ├── Confirm pickup location and destination addresses
│   ├── Obtain authorization number from insurance/facility
│   └── Note special requirements (oxygen, wheelchair, attendant)
│
├── 2. Route Planning
│   ├── Map route for accessibility (avoid stairs, rough terrain)
│   ├── Account for traffic patterns and appointment time
│   ├── Identify emergency hospitals along route
│   └── Plan for potential delays (weather, traffic)
│
├── 3. Vehicle Preparation
│   ├── Verify equipment readiness
│   ├── Ensure appropriate temperature for patient condition
│   ├── Stock water, tissues, plastic bags
│   └── Confirm vehicle cleanliness
│
└── 4. Day-of Confirmation
    ├── Call patient/facility 30-60 min before pickup
    ├── Verify any last-minute changes
    └── Confirm all paperwork is ready
```

## 8.2 Patient Interaction Protocol

### Initial Contact

1. Introduce yourself professionally: name, service name, purpose
2. Verify patient identity using two identifiers
3. Explain the transport process clearly
4. Obtain signed consent forms
5. Review medications and special needs

### During Transport

1. Secure patient safely (wheelchair tie-down or stretcher straps)
2. Maintain conversation appropriate to patient condition
3. Monitor for signs of distress (pallor, sweating, agitation)
4. Keep environment comfortable (temperature, music if appropriate)
5. Provide updates on arrival time

### Arrival Protocol

1. Confirm facility contact is expecting patient
2. Wheel patient to appropriate department/reception
3. Transfer care to nursing staff or receptionist
4. Provide verbal handoff summary
5. Obtain signature on transport completion form
6. Return equipment to vehicle, prep for next transport

## 8.3 Emergency Response During Transport

```
Medical Emergency Protocol
├── Step 1: Pull over safely immediately
├── Step 2: Assess patient condition (ABC: Airway, Breathing, Circulation)
├── Step 3: Call 911 - do not transport to ER yourself
├── Step 4: Contact dispatch and family/emergency contact
├── Step 5: Administer first aid if trained and appropriate
├── Step 6: Stay with patient until EMS arrives
└── Step 7: Document incident thoroughly on incident report form
```

## 8.4 Documentation Workflow

1. Complete pre-transport assessment form
2. Document mileage (odometer readings start/end)
3. Note any delays, unusual circumstances, or concerns
4. File all paperwork within 24 hours
5. Submit billing with authorization numbers
6. Update patient tracking system
