# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Pre-Transport Cancellation Scenario

**User:** "I arrive for a scheduled pickup and the patient's family says they've changed their minds. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Cancellations happen regularly. Here's the professional handling:
>
> 1. Remain calm and professional - do not show frustration
> 2. Explain any cancellation fees per your service agreement
> 3. Offer to reschedule if the patient needs transport in the future
> 4. Contact dispatch to report the cancellation
> 5. Document the cancellation reason if disclosed
>
> If a cancellation fee applies, request it professionally. If not, leave your business card for future needs.

## 9.2 Patient Becomes Combative Mid-Transport

**User:** "A dementia patient is becoming agitated and trying to exit the vehicle. I can't physically restrain them. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> This is a high-risk situation requiring de-escalation:
>
> 1. **Pull over immediately** to a safe location
> 2. **Do not attempt physical restraint** - risk of injury to both parties
> 3. **Use calm, reassuring tones** - lower voice, simple sentences
> 4. **Redirect attention** - offer water, ask about music preferences
> 5. **Contact family/facility** for guidance
> 6. **Call 911** if patient is a danger to themselves or others
>
> Key considerations:
> - Patient dignity must be maintained throughout
> - Document the incident thoroughly
> - Consider whether transport should continue or be rescheduled
> - Know your state's laws on patient autonomy

## 9.3 Wheelchair Securement Failure Scenario

**User:** "I arrive to transport a bariatric patient and my wheelchair's rear securement strap has snapped. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Equipment failures require immediate response:
>
> - **Do not transport** until equipment is replaced
> - Contact dispatch for backup vehicle/equipment
> - Request rental equipment from medical supply
> - Explain situation to patient/family with apology
> - Reschedule if no alternative can be arranged
> - Document equipment failure and report to maintenance
>
> Consult equipment manual for weight capacities and replacement procedures. Never jury-rig temporary solutions.

## 9.4 Patient Falls During Transfer

**User:** "A patient slipped while transferring from wheelchair to car seat. They're conscious and say they're fine but I'm concerned. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Falls must be treated seriously even when patient denies injury:
>
> 1. **Do not move the patient** - assess for spinal injury first
> 2. **Call 911** for professional assessment
> 3. **Contact dispatch** to report incident
> 4. **Notify family/emergency contact** immediately
> 5. **Document the incident** with timeline and circumstances
> 6. **File incident report** within 24 hours
> 7. **Complete insurance notification** if required
>
> Even minor falls require medical evaluation. Document your assessment and patient's responses. Liability protection requires thorough documentation.

## 9.5 Family Demands Unscheduled Stop

**User:** "During transport, the patient's family (who is following in their own car) demands I stop at a pharmacy. The patient is stable but this wasn't planned."

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Unscheduled stops require judgment calls:
>
> - Assess patient condition first - can they tolerate a stop?
> - If patient needs pharmacy items (prescription, medical supplies): reasonable to accommodate
> - If it's personal errand for family: politely decline, explain schedule constraints
> - If you agree to stop: clarify time limit (5-10 min maximum)
> - Document the unscheduled stop in your trip log
>
> Key: Your responsibility is to the patient, not to the family. Patient safety and timely arrival take priority.
