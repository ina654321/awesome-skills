# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|--------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Transporting without verifying patient identity** | High | Use two-identifier check every time |
| 2 | **Skipping pre-transport inspection** | High | Daily equipment checklist prevents emergencies |
| 3 | **Improper wheelchair securement** | High | Follow manufacturer guidelines; practice with test weight |
| 4 | **Leaving patient unattended during transfer** | High | Always maintain contact or visual supervision |
| 5 | **Ignoring patient complaints of pain/discomfort** | High | Take all complaints seriously; reassess |
| 6 | **Transporting without authorization number** | Medium | Verify insurance authorization before transport |
| 7 | **Poor communication with receiving facility** | Medium | Call ahead to confirm expected arrival |
| 8 | **Not maintaining patient confidentiality (HIPAA)** | High | No patient info on paperwork visible in vehicle |
| 9 | **Rushing transfers to save time** | High | Safe transfer takes priority over schedule |
| 10 | **Inadequate documentation** | High | Complete all forms within 24 hours |

## 10.2 Liability Red Flags

- Operating without proper licensure in your state
- Expired certifications (CPR, first aid)
- Transporting patients beyond your certification level
- Failure to carry required insurance coverage
- No signed service agreement or consent form
- Transporting patients with communicable diseases without proper precautions
- Leaving equipment unattended in unlocked vehicle

## 10.3 Patient Care Mistakes

- **Not checking oxygen levels** before transport of respiratory patients
- **Assuming patient can walk** without verifying mobility
- **Forgetting to bring medications** the patient may need during transport
- **Not accounting for restroom needs** on long transports
- **Playing music too loudly** or displaying content that may disturb patients
- **Discussing other patients** within hearing range of current patient

## 10.4 Business Mistakes

- **Not verifying insurance coverage** before accepting booking
- **Underestimating transport time** leading to missed appointments
- **Not having backup equipment** for emergencies
- **Poor communication with dispatch** during delays
- **Failure to collect payment/co-pays** at time of service
- **Not maintaining HIPAA training documentation**

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Patient identity verified with two identifiers
- [ ] Consent forms signed
- [ ] Special needs documented and equipment prepared
- [ ] All certifications current
- [ ] Vehicle inspection completed
- [ ] Route planned for accessibility
- [ ] Emergency contacts confirmed
- [ ] HIPAA compliance maintained
- [ ] Documentation completed within 24 hours
- [ ] Insurance authorization verified
- [ ] Patient feedback collected (when appropriate)
- [ ] Equipment maintained and inspected regularly
