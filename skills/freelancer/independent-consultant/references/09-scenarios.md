# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scope Creep After Contract Signed

**User:** "The client keeps adding 'small requests' that are outside scope. We've already spent 20 extra hours. How do I address this?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Scope creep is the most common consulting profitability killer:
>
> 1. **Document everything**: Track all out-of-scope requests with time spent
> 2. **Have a conversation**: "I want to make sure I'm delivering maximum value. Can we review the scope to see if we need to adjust our approach?"
> 3. **Offer options**:
>    - Expand scope and budget
>    - Prioritize: "Which 2-3 of these additional items matter most?"
>    - Phase 2: "Let's add these to a follow-on engagement"
> 4. **Be firm but professional**: "I'm happy to help with this, but it falls outside our current scope. Per our agreement, I'll document it and we can address it in our next conversation"
>
> Prevention: Include explicit scope boundaries and change order process in contract.

## 9.2 Client Won't Sign Off on Completed Work

**User:** "The client says they're 'not satisfied' but won't provide specific feedback. We delivered everything in the SOW. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> Unsatisfied without specifics requires professional navigation:
>
> 1. **Request specific feedback**: "I want to make sure we've met your expectations. What specific elements are falling short?"
> 2. **Review SOW deliverables**: Point-by-point comparison of what was committed vs. delivered
> 3. **Offer clarification call**: Sometimes written feedback misses nuance
> 4. **Escalate internally**: "Should we involve your manager or another stakeholder?"
> 5. **Know your contract**: If deliverables met SOW and client won't specify issues, you may be entitled to payment
>
> Key considerations:
> - "Not satisfied" without specifics is not a valid reason to withhold payment
> - Document all communication
> - Seek legal advice if significant payment is at stake

## 9.3 Client Asks for Employee Reference

**User:** "A former client wants me to recommend their project manager for a job. I found them difficult to work with. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Professional reference ethics:
>
> 1. **Be honest but diplomatic**: "I can speak to [name]'s work on our project. What specifically would you like to know?"
> 2. **Stick to facts**: "They delivered the project scope on time" vs. "They were wonderful to work with"
> 3. **Decline if uncomfortable**: "I'm not comfortable providing a professional reference. I'd suggest reaching out to their direct reports"
> 4. **Set boundaries going forward**: "I generally only provide references for engagements where the working relationship was positive"
>
> You are not obligated to provide a positive reference. Offer to provide a neutral factual reference or decline professionally.

## 9.4 Competitor Undercutting Your Price

**User:** "A prospect says a competitor quoted half my price for similar work. How do I justify my rate?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Price objections are opportunities:
>
> 1. **Don't panic-discount**: This signals your value isn't real
> 2. **Ask questions**: "Can you share what's included in their proposal?" Often apples-to-oranges comparison
> 3. **Differentiate**: Focus on your unique approach, methodology, or experience
> 4. **Share proof**: Case studies, ROI data, client outcomes
> 5. **Offer choice**: "I can offer a reduced scope version if budget is the constraint"
> 6. **Know your floor**: Never go below your minimum viable rate
>
> If they're genuinely cheaper with comparable qualifications, consider whether you want to compete on price or differentiate on value.

## 9.5 Client Goes Silent Mid-Project

**User:** "The client hasn't responded to 3 emails and 2 calls in 2 weeks. We have a deliverable due next week. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Client silence is a warning sign:
>
> 1. **Document all outreach**: Times, dates, methods
> 2. **Send formal status update**: "Per our contract, I'm proceeding with [deliverable] based on our last discussion. Please confirm by [date] or I'll proceed with my best interpretation"
> 3. **Check with internal contact**: Is there something going on at their company?
> 4. **Review contract terms**: Are there milestones tied to client input?
> 5. **Consider pause**: "I've paused work on [deliverable] until I hear back. Please contact me at your earliest convenience"
>
> Never continue full steam ahead if key decisions are blocked by silence. Protect yourself with documented outreach.
