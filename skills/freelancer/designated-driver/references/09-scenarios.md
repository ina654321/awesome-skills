# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Standard Friday Night Scenario

**User:** "I'm booked to pick up a group of 4 from downtown at 11pm. They seem moderately intoxicated. How do I handle loading?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Moderate intoxication is routine for this work. Follow this protocol:
>
> 1. Position vehicle at pickup spot, engine running
> 2. Step out and briefly greet the group
> 3. Help open doors but don't physically lift unless clearly needed
> 4. Count heads before closing doors - verify party size
> 5. Ensure all seatbelts are fastened
> 6. Confirm destination verbally with the designated speaker
> 7. Drive defensively, anticipating slower reactions
>
> Maintain professional demeanor - you're not there to judge, just to ensure safe transport.

## 9.2 Passenger Refuses to Wear Seatbelt

**User:** "A passenger insists they don't need to wear a seatbelt. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> Seatbelt laws apply to all passengers in most states. Handle diplomatically:
>
> 1. Explain it's a legal requirement and company policy
> 2. Offer to pull over until compliance is achieved
> 3. If still refusing: "I'm not able to drive until everyone is buckled for your safety"
> 4. If still refusing after pull-over: end the ride, charge cancellation fee per policy
>
> Key considerations:
> - Your vehicle, your rules - enforce firmly but respectfully
> - Document refusal in ride log
> - Do not physically force a seatbelt on anyone

## 9.3 Passenger Cannot Afford Fare

**User:** "We arrive at the destination and the passenger says their card was declined. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> This situation requires careful handling to avoid confrontation:
>
> - Remain calm and professional
> - Suggest trying another card or payment app
> - Offer to wait 5 minutes while they contact someone for payment
> - If unable to pay: request ID for small claims court filing (varies by service)
> - For platform-based services: contact dispatch for guidance
> - Never leave an intoxicated passenger stranded; arrange safe alternative
>
> Consult your service agreement for unpaid fare protocols.

## 9.4 Suspicious Activity Scenario

**User:** "During the ride, I overhear passengers discussing distributing drugs. Do I continue the ride?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> This is a high-stakes scenario requiring immediate judgment:
>
> - Do not engage with or acknowledge the conversation
> - Complete the ride as planned (don't create confrontation)
> - Drop off at the requested destination
> - If you feel unsafe: pull over in a safe, well-lit public area and ask them to exit
> - After drop-off: contact local police non-emergency line to report
> - Document license plate, passenger descriptions, and conversation
> - Report to your service platform immediately after
> - Your safety comes first - trust your instincts

## 9.5 Large Group Exceeds Vehicle Capacity

**User:** "I arrive to pick up 6 people but my vehicle only fits 4. The fifth person says they can squeeze in."

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Over-capacity transport is both illegal and unsafe:
>
> - Politely but firmly explain vehicle capacity limits
> - Offer to make multiple trips if time allows
> - Contact dispatch/request backup if available
> - If no backup available: "I cannot proceed until everyone has safe transport"
> - Charge appropriate surge/multi-trip pricing for additional runs
>
> Never allow passengers to ride in cargo areas, trunks, or挤 in ways that obstruct exits.
