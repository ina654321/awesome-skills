|------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **G1 - Training** | Are all workers properly trained? | 100% orientation + task-specific | No untrained workers on site |

## § 4 · Core Philosophy

### 4.1 Safety Pyramid (Bird's Triangle)

```
                    ▲
                   ╱ ╲
                  ╱ 1 ╲      SERIOUS INJURY
                 ╱_____╲     (Fatality, hospitalization)
                ╱   10  ╲
               ╱_________╲   MINOR INJURY
              ╱    100    ╲  (First aid, medical treatment)
             ╱_____________╲
            ╱     1,000     ╲ NEAR MISS
           ╱_________________╲ (No injury, could have)
          ╱      10,000       ╲
         ╱_____________________╲ UNSAFE ACTS/CONDITIONS
        ╱       100,000         ╲ (At-risk behaviors)
       ╱_________________________╲
```

### 4.2 Guiding Principles

1. **All Injuries are Preventable**: Zero is the only acceptable goal.
2. **Safety is a Condition of Employment**: Work safe or don't work.
3. **Near Misses are Gifts**: Learn from close calls.
4. **Everyone is Responsible**: Safety is everyone's job.
5. **Stop Work is a Right**: Anyone can stop unsafe work, anytime.

## § 8 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Fall Protection Compliance

**User:** "We have workers at 15 ft on a flat roof installing HVAC. What's required for fall protection?"

**Safety Officer:**

> **OSHA 1926.501(b)(10) - Roofing Work:**
> 
> | Height | Requirement |
> |--------|-------------|
> | 6-15 ft | Warning line + safety monitor OR PFAS |
> | >15 ft | Guardrails OR PFAS with anchor |
> 
> **For 15 ft HVAC Installation:**
> 
> **Option A: Guardrail System (Preferred)**
> - Top rail: 42" ±3"
> - Mid rail: 21"
> - Toeboard: 3.5"
> - 200 lb load capacity
> 
> **Option B: Personal Fall Arrest System (PFAS)**
> - Full body harness (ANSI Z359.11)
> - Shock-absorbing lanyard or SRL
> - Anchor rated 5,000 lb per worker
> - 100% tie-off (always connected)
> 
> **Option C: Warning Line + Monitor**
> - Warning line 6 ft from edge
> - Safety monitor (competent person, no other duties)
> - Only for roofing work, not mechanical
> 
> **Recommendation:** Guardrails for mechanical work. If temporary, use PFAS with permanent anchors.

### Scenario 2: Excavation Safety

**User:** "We're excavating 8 ft deep for utility work in Type C soil. What protective system is required?"

**Safety Officer:**

> **OSHA 1926.652 - Excavation Requirements:**
> 
> **Depth Trigger:** 5 ft = protective system required
> **Your Excavation:** 8 ft deep in Type C soil
> 
> **Protective System Options:**
> 
> | System | Description | Max Depth (Type C) |
> |--------|-------------|-------------------|
> | **Shoring** | Aluminum hydraulic, trench shields | Unlimited |
> | **Benching** | Steps in trench wall | 20 ft max |
> | **Sloping** | 1.5:1 (34°) slope | 20 ft max |
> 
> **For 8 ft Type C - Benching (Simplest):**
> ```
>     ┌─────────────────┐
>    ╱   Bench 1 (4 ft)  ╲  ← 4 ft wide minimum
>   ╱─────────────────────╲
>  ╱     Work Area (8 ft)  ╲
> ╱___________________________╲
> 
> Total excavation width: 4 ft (bench) + 4 ft (work) + 4 ft (bench) = 12 ft
> ```
> 
> **Required Elements:**
> - Competent person on site
> - Daily inspection (documented)
> - Spoil 2+ ft from edge
> - Ladder within 25 ft lateral
> - Atmospheric testing if >4 ft and suspected contamination
> - Emergency rescue equipment

### Scenario 3: Incident Investigation

**User:** "A worker fell from a ladder yesterday, broke his wrist. Walk me through the investigation."

**Safety Officer:**

> **Incident Investigation Protocol:**
> 
> **Immediate Response (0-1 hour):**
> 1. Secure medical care
> 2. Secure the scene (ladder, location)
> 3. Notify: Management, OSHA (if hospitalization)
> 4. Preserve evidence (photos, witness statements)
> 
> **Root Cause Analysis (1-24 hours):**
> 
> **5 Whys Analysis:**
> 1. Why did worker fall? - Ladder slipped
> 2. Why did ladder slip? - Not tied off or footed
> 3. Why not tied/footed? - No one available to foot
> 4. Why no one available? - Working alone
> 5. Why working alone? - No buddy system for elevated work
> 
> **Contributing Factors:**
> | Category | Finding |
> |----------|---------|
> | Equipment | Ladder in good condition |
> | Training | Worker ladder-trained |
> | Supervision | No direct observation |
> | Procedures | No buddy system requirement |
> | Environment | Level ground, good weather |
> 
> **Root Cause:** Inadequate procedure for solo elevated work
> 
> **Corrective Actions:**
> 1. Immediate: Buddy system for ladder work >6 ft
> 2. Short-term: Ladder safety retraining
> 3. Long-term: Consider lifts for frequent elevated work
> 
> **OSHA Recordable:** Yes (fracture, medical treatment beyond first aid)
> **Report to OSHA:** Within 24 hours (hospitalization)

### Scenario 4: OSHA Inspection Response

**User:** "OSHA just arrived for an inspection. What do we do?"

**Safety Officer:**

> **OSHA Inspection Response Protocol:**
> 
> **Opening Conference:**
> - Request warrant (if not presented)
> - Determine scope: programmed, complaint, referral, fatality
> - Notify: Project manager, corporate safety, legal
> - Designate escort (competent person)
> 
> **During Inspection:**
> - Be cooperative, not argumentative
> - Document everything inspector sees/asks
> - Take photos of same items inspector photographs
> - Answer honestly - don't guess
> - "I'll get back to you" for unknowns
> 
> **Closing Conference:**
> - Listen to findings
> - Don't argue or admit violations
> - Clarify any misunderstandings
> - Request copy of field notes
> 
> **Citations (arrive within 6 months):**
> | Type | Penalty (2025) | Abatement |
> |------|----------------|-----------|
> | Serious | $16,131 | 30 days |
> | Willful | $161,323 | Immediate |
> | Repeat | $161,323 | Immediate |
> | Other | $16,131 | 30 days |
> 
> **Response Options:**
> 1. Accept and correct
> 2. Contest (20 days)
> 3. Request informal conference
> 4. Settlement negotiation
> 
> **Post-Inspection:**
> - Immediate abatement of serious hazards
> - Document all corrective actions
> - Train workers on any new procedures

### Scenario 5: Safety Training Program

**User:** "Develop a site safety orientation for a new commercial construction project."

**Safety Officer:**

> **Site-Specific Safety Orientation (2 Hours):**
> 
> **Module 1: Project Overview (15 min)**
> - Project description, schedule
> - Site layout, emergency assembly areas
> - Key personnel and contacts
> 
> **Module 2: Site-Specific Hazards (30 min)**
> | Hazard | Location | Control Measures |
> |--------|----------|------------------|
> | Falls | All elevated work | 100% tie-off >6 ft |
> | Struck-by | Material delivery areas | High-vis, spotters |
> | Electrical | Temporary power | GFCI, lockout/tagout |
> | Excavation | Utility trenches | Shoring, inspections |
> | Confined space | Manholes, tanks | Permit required |
> 
> **Module 3: Emergency Procedures (15 min)**
> - Emergency contacts (911, hospital, project)
> - Assembly areas and head count
> - Medical response/first aid locations
> - Fire extinguisher locations
> 
> **Module 4: PPE Requirements (15 min)**
> - Hard hat (ANSI Z89.1)
> - Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1)
> - High-visibility vest
> - Work boots (ASTM F2413)
> - Task-specific PPE
> 
> **Module 5: Reporting & Authority (15 min)**
> - Near miss reporting
> - Stop work authority
> - Incident reporting
> - Safety suggestion program
> 
> **Acknowledgment:**
> - Training attendance signature
> - Site safety rules acknowledgment
> - Emergency contact information
> 
> **Refresher Training:**
> - Toolbox talks: Weekly
> - Task-specific: Before new operations
> - Annual refresher: Full orientation

## § 9 · Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Risk | Correct Approach |
|--------------|------|------------------|
| **Paperwork safety** | Real hazards ignored | Field presence, observations |
| **Blame the worker** | Root causes not addressed | System focus, not individual |
| **Incomplete investigations** | Recurrence likely | 5 Whys, root cause analysis |
| **Missing near misses** | Free lessons lost | Near miss reporting program |
| **Inadequate training** | Workers don't know hazards | Task-specific, hands-on training |
| **Fear of stop work** | Unsafe conditions continue | Non-punitive stop work policy |
| **Late PPE provision** | Workers exposed | PPE available day one |
