# Interior Design — Standards & Reference

## Official Documentation & Standards

### Lighting Standards

- [IESNA (Illuminating Engineering Society) Lighting Handbook](https://www.ies.org/standards/handbooks/) — The authoritative reference for lighting levels, color temperature, and fixture specifications. Provides recommended foot-candle (FC) and lux levels by room type and task.
- [CIE (International Commission on Illumination)](https://cie.co.at/) — Publishes international lighting standards including color rendering index (CRI), correlated color temperature (CCT), and photobiological safety.
- [ASHRAE 90.1 (Energy Standard for Buildings)](https://www.ashrae.org/technical-resources/standards-and-guidelines) — Sets minimum energy efficiency standards for lighting systems. Affects luminaire selection and control requirements.

### Building & Accessibility Codes

- [IBC (International Building Code)](https://www.iccsafe.org/products-and-services/i-codes/2018-i-codes/ibc/) — The base model building code. Interior designers must comply with egress requirements, fire ratings, and occupancy classifications.
- [ADA Standards for Accessible Design](https://www.ada.gov/regs2010/2010ADAStandards/2010ADAStandards.pdf) — Federal accessibility requirements for public accommodations. Covers reach ranges, clear floor space, turning spaces, and accessible routes.
- [ANSI/BHFA (National Standard for Building Hardware)](https://www.builderhardware.org/) — Standards for door hardware, including ADA-compliant lever handles and accessible mounting heights.
- [NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code)](https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-norms/nfpa-101-standard-development/101) — Occupancy requirements for means of egress, door swing, and emergency lighting.

### Material Standards

- [ASTM International](https://www.astm.org/) — Standards for flooring materials (ASTM F1066 for vinyl, F710 for concrete), finish materials, and testing methods.
- [UL (Underwriters Laboratories)](https://www.ul.com/) — Safety testing for electrical fixtures, lighting equipment, and building materials. UL listing required for many installed products.
- [Greenguard Gold Certification](https://www.ul.com/segments/greenguard) — Low chemical emission standards for interior finish materials. Required for LEED projects and healthcare environments.

## Regulatory Bodies & Professional Associations

### Design Organizations

- [ASID (American Society of Interior Designers)](https://www.asid.org/) — The leading professional organization for interior designers. ASID has a rigorous code of ethics and professional standards. NCIDQ certification is administered through ASID.
- [IIDA (International Interior Design Association)](https://www.iida.org/) — Focuses on commercial interior design. Publishes industry research and design standards.
- [NKBA (National Kitchen & Bath Association)](https://www.nkba.org/) — Standards for kitchen and bathroom design. Publishes planning guidelines, clearance dimensions, and accessibility requirements for wet environments.
- [USGBC (U.S. Green Building Council)](https://www.usgbc.org/) — Manages the LEED rating system. Interior designers working on LEED projects must understand materials credits, indoor air quality, and sustainable sourcing requirements.
- [CIDA (Council for Interior Design Qualification)](https://www.cidq.org/) — Administers the NCIDQ examination, the primary certification for interior designers in the US and Canada.

### Sustainability & Environmental Standards

- [Declare Label (International Living Future Institute)](https://living-future.org/declare/) — Transparency database for building products. Lists material ingredients, responsible sourcing, and end-of-life options. Required for Living Building Challenge projects.
- [Cradle to Cradle Certified](https://c2ccertified.org/) — Material health certification requiring safe material selection, material reutilization, renewable energy use, water stewardship, and social fairness.
- [FSC (Forest Stewardship Council)](https://www.fsc-usa.org/) — Certification for responsibly sourced wood and paper products. FSC-certified materials score higher on LEED and other green building ratings.
- [SCS Global Services](https://www.scsglobalservices.com/) — Third-party certification for recycled content, indoor air quality, and sustainable sourcing.

## Environmental & Building Performance

### LEED Interior Standards

- **LEED v4.1 Interior Design & Construction** — Covers sustainable sites, water efficiency, energy & atmosphere, materials & resources, and indoor environmental quality.
- **EQ Credit: Low-Emitting Materials** — Requires adhesives, sealants, paints, coatings, flooring, and composite wood to meet VOC limits and UL/Greenguard certification.
- **MR Credit: Building Product Disclosure and Optimization** — Requires documentation of material ingredient reporting (Declare label) and sourcing (FSC, recycled content).

### Indoor Air Quality

- [CDPH/EHLB Standard Method (Section 01350)](https://www.cdph.ca.gov/Programs/CDEE/OHRES/CDPH%20Document%20Library/Section01350.pdf) — California's standard for evaluating VOC emissions from interior products. Required for many state-funded and green building projects.
- [OSHA PELs (Permissible Exposure Limits)](https://www.osha.gov/pel) — Sets maximum exposure limits for airborne contaminants including VOCs from paints and finishes.
- [MERV Rating System](https://www.epa.gov/indoor-air-quality-iaq/what-merv-rating-0) — Minimum Efficiency Reporting Value for air filters. MERV 8-13 for residential; MERV 14+ for commercial and healthcare.

## Industry Reference Data

### Space Planning Standards

- **Open Office**: 60-110 sq ft per person (open plan with shared amenities); 130-150 sq ft per person (with private offices)
- **Private Office**: 100-200 sq ft (standard); 250+ sq ft (executive)
- **Conference Room**: 20-25 sq ft per person seated
- **Workstation (Open Plan)**: 48-60" wide × 30" deep minimum
- **Reception Area**: 15-20 sq ft per person expected in peak hour

### Ergonomic Standards

- [BIFMA (Business and Institutional Furniture Manufacturers Association)](https://www.bifma.org/) — Publishes safety and performance standards for office furniture including ergonomic seating, work surfaces, and storage.
- [ANSI/HFES 100 (American National Standard for Human Factors Engineering of Computer Workstations)](https://www.hfes.org/) — Ergonomic guidelines for computer workstations including monitor height, keyboard placement, and chair adjustment.
- **Chair Seat Height**: 16-21" adjustable range
- **Desk/Work Surface Height**: 28-30" (standard); 25-30" (sit-stand)
- **Monitor Distance**: 20-26" from eyes
- **Keyboard Height**: Elbows at 90° when hands on keyboard

### Finish Durability Standards

- **Wyzenbeek Double Rubs** (abrasion resistance for upholstery): Residential = 15,000+; Commercial = 30,000+; Heavy-duty = 100,000+
- **Martindale Abrasion** (European standard, cycles): Residential = 20,000+; Commercial = 40,000+
- **AC Rating** (laminate flooring): AC1 = moderate residential; AC3 = heavy residential/moderate commercial; AC4 = general commercial; AC5 = heavy commercial
- **Peel Strength** (adhesives): Measured in lb/in; must meet ASTM D903 for floor coverings
- **Lightfastness** (textiles, ASTM D5303): Rating 4+ required for direct sunlight exposure

## Color & Materials Reference

### Paint Standards

- **VOC Limits by Type** (g/L, EPA regulations): Flat = 250; Non-flat = 380; Primers = 200
- **Zero-VOC Paints**: <5 g/L (Benjamin Moore Aura, Sherwin-Williams Emerald, Farrow & Ball)
- **Washability Rating**: Scrub cycles per ASTM D2486 — Economy = 200; Standard = 1,000; Premium = 5,000+
- **Sheen Types**: Flat (0-5%), Eggshell (3-10%), Satin (25-35%), Semi-Gloss (35-70%), High-Gloss (70-85%)
