## Scenario 1: Small Office Tenant Improvement — Budget-Constrained FF&E Selection

**Context:** A 5-person accounting firm is relocating to a 2,500 SF open-plan office. They have a $75,000 FF&E budget and want the space to look professional for client meetings while keeping costs reasonable. They currently use a mix of old mismatched furniture.

**Project Assessment:**
```yaml
Space Analysis:
  Layout: Open plan for 5 desks + 1 small conference room + reception area
  Aesthetic: Professional, contemporary, welcoming
  Brand: Conservative but not stuffy
  Must-haves: Ergonomic seating, good conference table, welcoming reception
  Nice-to-haves: Lounge area, standing desks

Budget Breakdown:
  Total: $75,000
  Chairs (5): $8,000 (5 × $1,600 ergonomic task chairs)
  Desks (5): $6,500 (5 × $1,300 sit-stand)
  Conference table + chairs: $12,000
  Reception desk: $8,000
  Accent furniture: $7,500
  Lighting update: $6,000
  Art/accessories: $5,000
  Technology (monitors, mounts): $4,000
  Installation/labor: $8,000
  Contingency: $10,000
  TOTAL: $75,000 ✅
```

**Design Solution: "Executive Minimalism"**
```yaml
Palette:
  - Walls: Warm white (Benjamin Moore White Dove OC-17)
  - Accent wall: Deep navy (BM Hale Navy HC-154)
  - Flooring: Engineered oak plank (light, wide plank)
  - Metal: Matte black (lighting, hardware)

Furniture Selections:
  Task Chairs: Haworth Fern (ergonomic, professional) — $1,450 each
  Desks: Geek+

Now I'm seeing the full scope of the office buildout — I need to specify the remaining furniture pieces like the conference table with six chairs, a reception desk, and a lounge area with lounge seating and a coffee table. For lighting, I'm planning to upgrade the existing fixtures with recessed LED in the main space, add a statement piece over the conference table, and include some floor lamps in the lounge. On the walls, I'm planning two large-format nature photography prints and a few abstract pieces, all framed in black. To keep costs down, I'm working with the existing carpet and just upgrading the baseboards to a taller profile for a more refined look.
```

**Outcome:** $74,500 total — $500 under budget. Space feels professional, modern, and comfortable. Client approved all selections.

## Scenario 3: Healthcare Clinic — Healing Environment with Strict Material Requirements

**Context:** A pediatric clinic (2,800 SF) needs interior design. Material selection is constrained by healthcare regulations (no carpet, specific cleanability requirements) but the space should feel warm and child-friendly.

**Design Concept: "Nature-Inspired Pediatric Care"**
```yaml
Healthcare Constraints:
  - Flooring: Must be seamless, easy-to-clean (VCT or sheet vinyl)
  - Walls: Semi-gloss paint (scrubbable)
  - No fabric window treatments (infection control)
  - Furniture: Non-porous surfaces preferred

Design Solution — Warmth Without Fabric:
  Flooring: Warm gray LVT (luxury vinyl tile) with wood-look
    - Wood look reduces clinical feel
    - Warm gray undertones connect to palette
    - Seamless installation (no grout lines)

  Accent Walls: Large-format nature photography prints (framed under glass)
    - Frameless or minimal frames
    - Removable for periodic replacement

  Window Treatments: Roller shades (not fabric)
    - Opaque for privacy, white aluminum headbox
    - Cleanable, replaceable

  Seating: Solid surface (Corian) built-in benches
    - Non-porous, cleanable, no fabric hiding germs
    - Throw cushions (removable, washable) for seasonal updates
```

**Pediatric Design Elements:**
```yaml
Play Areas:
  - Flooring transition: LVT in clinical zone → cork in play zone
  - Cork: Natural, soft, warm (reduces noise and impact)
  - Built-in bench seating around play area (supervision seating)

Exam Rooms (6):
  - Warm lighting (2700K, not clinical 4000K)
  - Wall-mounted art (removable, child-appropriate themes)
  - Furniture: Rounded corners on all millwork (safety)

Waiting Room:
  - Scale: Child-sized seating area with small chairs and table
  - Adults: Lounge seating + café table
  - Television: Mounted high (children's programming above adult sightline)

Color Palette:
  Primary: Sage green (calming, gender-neutral)
  Secondary: Warm cream
  Accent: Coral orange (energizing, playful)
  Signage: Large icons, minimal text
```

## Scenario 7: Senior Living — Dementia-Friendly Design

**Context:** A memory care wing (12 rooms, 4,500 SF) for residents with mid-stage dementia. Design must reduce agitation, support wayfinding, support independence, and meet healthcare safety codes.

**Design Principles (Evidence-Based):**
```yaml
Evidence-Based Design for Dementia:
  1. Familiarity: Familiar materials, colors, and layouts reduce anxiety
  2. Wayfinding: Distinct visual cues per room/area; avoid dead ends
  3. Scale: Smaller, human-scaled spaces feel safer than large open areas
  4. Light: Circadian lighting supports sleep/wake cycles
  5. Color contrast: High contrast between surfaces prevents falls
  6. Safety: Eliminate ligature risks, slip hazards, sharp corners
```

**Design Specifications:**
```yaml
Color Palette:
  - Walls: Warm, familiar tones (peach, butter yellow — NOT white)
  - Door frames: Distinct color per resident's room (memory cue)
  - Floors: Contrasting with walls (prevents "visual cliff" effect)
  - NOT: Large areas of single color (causes confusion)

Lighting:
  - Circadian rhythm: Bright (100 FC) during day, dim (<20 FC) at night
  - Automated: Sensors adjust based on time of day
  - Nightlights: Low-level (5 FC) along all circulation paths

Room Design (per resident):
  - Personalization: Family photos, familiar furniture from home
  - Bed: Low bed (adjustable, reduces fall injury)
  - Private bathroom: Visible from bed (reduces confusion)
  - Window: Large, with pleasant exterior view (nature or garden)

Common Areas:
  - Scale: Intimate, home-like (not institutional)
  - Kitchen: Fully equipped, allows residents to participate in activities
  - Living room: Group seating + individual seating options
  - Snoezelen room: Sensory room with controlled stimuli

Outdoor Garden:
  - Secured (wander prevention)
  - Raised planting beds (accessible for seated gardening)
  - Paved paths (wheelchair accessible)
  - Covered seating (allows outdoor time regardless of weather)
```

## Scenario 9: Historic Preservation — Adaptive Reuse of Industrial Building

**Context:** A 1920s brick warehouse (8,000 SF) is being converted into loft-style office space. The interior design must preserve historic character while meeting modern code requirements (ADA, egress, mechanical).

**Preservation Challenges:**
```yaml
Existing Conditions:
  - 16' ceiling height (great for lofts, challenge for HVAC)
  - Original brick walls (must be preserved, cannot be painted)
  - Large timber columns every 20' (structural, cannot remove)
  - Minimal daylighting (small windows high on walls)
  - Original wood floors (must be refinished)

Code Challenges:
  - Egress: Need additional exit stair (historic building exception allows 1 additional)
  - ADA: Elevator required for accessibility (2-floor change)
  - Fire: Sprinkler system required (historic exception allows omissions with equivalent)
  - MEP: No existing infrastructure for HVAC, electrical, data
```

**Design Solution:**
```yaml
Exposed Systems Strategy:
  # Modern systems must be legible as new elements (contrast with historic)
  # NOT trying to hide infrastructure

HVAC:
  - Exposed ductwork (painted black), ceiling-plenum mounted
  - Perimeter radiant panels (under windows, solves perimeter heating)
  - Unit ventilators per zone (2 per floor)

Electrical/Data:
  - Exposed conduit (surface-mount, painted to match wall)
  - Floor boxes (new construction in wood floor)
  - Cable tray in loft (exposed ceiling)

Lighting:
  - Industrial pendants (LED, reproduction of original warehouse fixtures)
  - Task lighting at workstations
  - All LED, dimmable, Lutron Vive system

Historic Material Treatment:
  Brick: Cleaned (no sandblasting — damages mortar), sealed
    - Sealant: Lithofin MN (breathable, matte)
    - DO NOT: Paint or whitewash

  Wood floor: Sanded and refinished (water-based finish)
    - Color: Natural amber (not dark stain)

  Timber columns: Cleaned, lightly sanded, clear-sealed
    - DO NOT: Sandblast, stain, or paint
```

**Office Layout:**
```yaml
Floor 1 (4,000 SF):
  Open plan: 35 workstations
  2 conference rooms (12-person, 8-person)
  Phone booths (6)
  Break room
  Server room

Floor 2 (4,000 SF):
  Executive suites: 3 private offices + open area (12)
  Board room (16-person)
  Client entertainment suite
  Rooftop access

Loft Mezzanine (optional expansion):
  Additional 1,500 SF
  Open collaboration space
  Visible from below (connects both floors visually)
```
