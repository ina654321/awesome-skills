# Quality Inspector — Glossary

## Quality Systems & Standards

**ISO 9001** — International standard for Quality Management Systems.

**IATF 16949** — Automotive QMS standard for automotive supply chain.

**AQL (Acceptable Quality Level)** — Maximum percentage of defective items considered acceptable in a sample.

**SPC (Statistical Process Control)** — Use of statistical methods to monitor and control a process.

**Six Sigma** — Methodology for process improvement targeting 3.4 defects per million opportunities.

## Defect Classifications

**Critical Defect (Class A)** — Defect that could cause injury or death to user. Zero tolerance. Full rejection.

**Major Defect (Class B)** — Defect that seriously affects product function or usability. Disrupts primary purpose.

**Minor Defect (Class C)** — Defect that does not significantly affect product function. Cosmetic, affects appearance only.

**Inconsequential Defect** — Minor deviation that has no practical effect on product usability.

## Sampling Terms

**Lot (Batch)** — A collection of units produced under essentially the same conditions.

**Sample** — Subset of lot selected for inspection.

**Sample Size (n)** — Number of units inspected from the lot.

**Accept Number (Ac)** — Maximum number of defective units in sample to accept the lot.

**Reject Number (Re)** — Minimum defective units in sample to reject the lot.

**Double Sampling** — Two-stage inspection where first sample may allow acceptance, rejection, or requires second sample.

**Skip-Lot Sampling** — Reduced inspection for suppliers with excellent quality history.

**Switching Rules** — Rules for moving between normal, tightened, and reduced inspection.

## Inspection Types

**100% Inspection** — Inspecting every unit. Required for critical characteristics or small lots.

**AQL Sampling** — Statistical sampling per ANSI/ASQ Z1.4. Normal, tightened, or reduced inspection.

**First Article Inspection (FAI)** — Complete dimensional verification of first-off parts.

**CQI (Customer Quality Inspection)** — Customer or third-party inspection of supplier product.

**In-Process Inspection** — Inspection at defined points during manufacturing.

**Final Inspection** — Complete inspection before shipment or delivery.

**Dimensional Inspection (Variables)** — Measuring actual numeric values vs. tolerance limits.

**Attribute Inspection** — Pass/fail inspection without numeric measurement.

## Measurement Terms

**Tolerance** — Allowable deviation from a specified dimension or quality.

**Specification Limits** — Upper and lower bounds defining acceptable range.

**USL (Upper Spec Limit)** — Maximum allowable value.

**LSL (Lower Spec Limit)** — Minimum allowable value.

**Target** — Nominal or ideal value within tolerance.

**Cp (Process Capability Index)** — Ratio of tolerance width to process spread. Target: ≥1.33.

**Cpk (Process Capability Index with Centering)** — Capability accounting for process centering. Target: ≥1.33.

**Gauge R&R (Repeatability and Reproducibility)** — Study of measurement system variation.

## Non-Conformance Terms

**NCR (Non-Conformance Report)** — Formal documentation of a specification deviation.

**CAR (Corrective Action Request)** — Request to supplier or internal team to identify and eliminate root cause.

**SCAR (Supplier Corrective Action Request)** — CAR issued to a supplier.

**Deviation (Waiver)** — Written authorization to use or accept material outside specification limits.

**Disposition** — Decision on what to do with non-conforming material: use as is, rework, repair, or scrap.

**Quarantine** — Isolation of suspect material pending disposition decision.

**Rework** — Repairing defective unit to meet specifications.

**Scrap** — Removing defective material from production; no value recovery.

**Repair** — Modifying product to make it functional, but not to original specifications.

## Statistical Terms

**Mean (X-bar)** — Average value of a set of measurements.

**Standard Deviation (σ)** — Measure of variation in a dataset.

**Normal Distribution** — Bell-shaped probability distribution common in manufacturing.

**Confidence Level** — Probability that a statement about a population is correct.

**Confidence Interval** — Range within which true value is expected to fall.

**Statistical Significance** — Probability that observed difference is not due to chance.

**OC Curve (Operating Characteristic)** — Graph showing probability of acceptance vs. actual lot defective percentage.

## Calibration Terms

**Calibration** — Comparing instrument against a standard and documenting differences.

**Traceability** — Unbroken chain of calibrations linking instrument to national/international standards.

**Master Gauge** — Reference standard used to calibrate working gauges.

**Calibration Certificate** — Document proving calibration was performed, showing results.

**Calibration Interval** — Time period between required calibrations.

**Out-of-Calibration** — Status when instrument exceeds acceptable error limits.

## Process Control Terms

**Control Chart** — Graph of process data with control limits used to monitor stability.

**X-bar and R Chart** — Process control chart for variables data (mean and range).

**p Chart** — Control chart for proportion of defective items.

**u Chart** — Control chart for defects per unit.

**Run Rules** — Criteria for identifying non-random patterns in process data.

**Out of Control** — Process condition where data shows patterns indicating special cause variation.

**Common Cause Variation** — Natural, expected variation inherent in the process.

**Special Cause Variation** — Unexpected variation from assignable external source.
