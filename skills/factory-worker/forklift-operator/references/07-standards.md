# Forklift Operator — Standards & Reference

## Regulations & Standards

### US Federal Standards

**OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178** — Powered industrial trucks. The primary regulation governing forklift operation in the US. Covers operator training, truck selection, maintenance, and operational requirements. Website: osha.gov

**OSHA 29 CFR 1910.22** — Walking-working surfaces. Covers floor load capacity and aisle maintenance.

**OSHA 29 CFR 1910.176** — Materials handling and storage. Forklift-specific aisle and floor requirements.

**OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178(p)** — Operator training requirements. Mandates formal evaluation and refresher training every 3 years.

### ANSI/ITSDF Standards

**ANSI/ITSDF B56.1** — Safety standard for powered industrial trucks. Design and manufacturing requirements. The basis for forklift safety features.

**ANSI/ITSDF B56.6** — Safety standard for rough terrain counterbalanced fork lift trucks.

**ANSI/ITSDF B56.5** — Safety standard for manually propelled high lift industrial trucks.

### International Standards

**ISO 45001:2018** — Occupational health and safety management systems.

**ISO 22915** — Industrial truck stability — overturning, static and dynamic.

**EN 1175** — Safety of industrial trucks — electrical requirements.

### Professional Associations

**Industrial Truck Association (ITA)** — Trade association for manufacturers and suppliers of powered industrial trucks. Website: indtrk.org

**National Safety Council (NSC)** — Forklift safety training resources and materials.

**American Society of Safety Professionals (ASSP)** — Website: assp.org

## Forklift Classifications (OSHA/ITA)

| Class | Type | Power | Application |
|-------|------|-------|-------------|
| **I** | Electric motor rider | Battery | Indoor warehouses, climate-controlled areas |
| **II** | Electric narrow aisle | Battery | High-density racking, very narrow aisles |
| **III** | Electric hand/rider | Battery | Low-level lifting, pallet jack replacement |
| **IV** | IC cushion tire | Gas/LP/Diesel | Indoor/outdoor, smooth floors only |
| **V** | IC pneumatic tire | Gas/LP/Diesel | Outdoor, rough terrain, heavy loads |
| **VI** | Electric/Gas | Combination | Indoor/outdoor |
| **VII** | Rough terrain | Diesel/Gas | Construction, lumber yards, uneven surfaces |
| **VIII** | Specialized | Various | Personnel carriers, tow tractors |

## Load Capacity Reference

### Capacity Factors

**Load Center** — The horizontal distance from the vertical face of the forks to the center of gravity of the load.

**Rated Capacity** — Maximum load the truck can lift to maximum height at load center specified on nameplate.

**Formula for different load centers:**
If actual load center > rated load center, reduce capacity proportionally.

Example: Rated 4,000 lbs at 24" load center.
At 36" load center: Capacity = 4,000 × (24/36) = 2,667 lbs
At 48" load center: Capacity = 4,000 × (24/48) = 2,000 lbs

### Fork Dimensions & Capacity

| Fork Length | Weight Capacity Reduction |
|-------------|------------------------|
| 42" (standard) | 100% of rated |
| 48" | 95% of rated |
| 60" | 88% of rated |
| 72" | 80% of rated |

## Pre-Operation Inspection Checklist (OSHA Required)

**Daily before first use:**

1. **Tires** — Cracks, chunks missing, proper inflation
2. **Forks** — Cracks, wear ≤10% of thickness, proper mounting
3. **Hydraulic Fluid** — Level, leaks, discoloration
4. **Battery** — Charge level (>50% for full shift), terminal corrosion, cable condition
5. **Horn** — Audible at 200 feet minimum
6. **Lights** — Headlights, rear lights, warning beacon functional
7. **Steering** — Smooth, no excessive play
8. **Brakes** — Service brakes and parking brake functional
9. **Mirrors/View** — Clean, provides blind spot visibility
10. **Warning Labels** — Nameplate legible, capacity visible

## Training Requirements (OSHA 1910.178(l))

**Operator evaluation must include:**
- Formal instruction (lecture, video, written material)
- Practical operation (hands-on training)
- Evaluation of operator performance

**Refresher training required when:**
- Operator has been involved in accident or near-miss
- Operator assigned to different type of truck
- Conditions in workplace change significantly
- Operator observed operating unsafely

**Training records must include:**
- Operator name
- Date of training
- Name of trainer
- Evaluation results

## Safety Signage & Markings

**Forklift Traffic Signs:**
- Pedestrian crossing areas
- Speed limits (typically 5 mph)
- Forklift only zones
- Low ceiling warnings
- Weight limit signs

**Floor Markings:**
- Yellow: Traffic lanes
- Red: No parking zones, fire equipment
- Blue/White: Storage locations
- Green: Safety equipment locations
