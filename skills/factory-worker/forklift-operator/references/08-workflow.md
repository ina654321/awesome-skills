# Forklift Operator — Troubleshooting Guide

## Load & Stability Problems

**Problem: Load feels unstable when lifting**
- Diagnosis: Center of gravity shifted or load heavier than expected
- Check: Weigh load if possible; recalculate capacity vs. actual load
- Check: Is load centered on forks evenly?
- Check: Is the load pallet damaged, causing shifting?
- Action: Lower immediately to safe height; investigate before lifting higher
- Prevention: Always verify load weight before lifting to maximum height

**Problem: Forklift tips when lifting at maximum height**
- Diagnosis: Load center exceeded capacity or ground is not level
- Immediate: Lower load immediately; move to level ground
- Check: Is ground surface solid? (concrete cracks, soft asphalt)
- Check: Is forklift on a grade, even slight?
- Prevention: Never lift to maximum height with maximum load; keep load low when traveling

**Problem: Load sways when traveling**
- Diagnosis: Load not secured or too high
- Immediate: Stop traveling; lower load to safe travel height (4-6" off floor)
- Prevention: Lower forks before traveling; center load before lifting

## Equipment Problems

**Problem: Hydraulic lift not working (mast won't raise)**
- Diagnosis: Low hydraulic fluid, pump failure, or control valve issue
- Check: Hydraulic fluid level in reservoir
- Check: Is the lift control lever engaging?
- Action: Do not operate with failed hydraulics; report to maintenance
- Prevention: Check hydraulic level during pre-op inspection daily

**Problem: Brake pedal feels spongy or ineffective**
- Diagnosis: Brake fluid issue or mechanical brake failure
- Action: Do not operate forklift; report immediately
- If must move to safe location: Travel at minimum speed with spotter
- Prevention: Test brakes at start of every shift

**Problem: Battery warning light on electric forklift**
- Diagnosis: Low charge, charging system failure, or thermal event
- Immediate: Stop operation; return to charging station
- Check: Charge level on display
- If blinking red: Battery overheating — do not charge, alert maintenance
- Prevention: Never operate below 20% charge; plan shifts around battery capacity

**Problem: Horn not working**
- Diagnosis: Electrical issue, broken horn, or low battery
- Action: Use visual signaling (flashlights, hand signals) until repaired
- Do not operate without horn — it's a primary safety device
- Prevention: Test horn daily during pre-op inspection

**Problem: Forks won't tilt forward or back**
- Diagnosis: Hydraulic issue or control valve problem
- Check: Listen for hydraulic operation sound when lever engaged
- If no sound: Electrical issue or dead battery
- If sound but no movement: Mechanical hydraulic failure
- Action: Do not operate; report for maintenance

## Travel Problems

**Problem: Forklift won't move (wheel drive failure)**
- Diagnosis: Transmission issue, drive motor failure, or safety interlock
- Check: Is parking brake engaged? (Some models won't drive with park brake on)
- Check: Is seat switch engaged? (Operator must be seated)
- Check: Is direction lever properly engaged?
- Action: Set in safe location with warning lights; report for maintenance

**Problem: Excessive vibration or shaking during travel**
- Diagnosis: Tire damage, wheel bearing issue, or loose components
- Immediate: Slow down; proceed to safe area to inspect
- Check: Tire condition (flats, chunking, unusual wear)
- Check: Wheel hub for looseness
- Action: Do not operate if vibration is severe — risk of losing control
- Prevention: Inspect tires during pre-op check

**Problem: Steering feels loose or erratic**
- Diagnosis: Steering linkage wear, hydraulic steering failure
- Immediate: Reduce speed; proceed to safe location
- Action: Do not operate until repaired; steering failure is extremely dangerous
- Prevention: Report any steering irregularity immediately

## Operational Problems

**Problem: Dock plate/bridge plate slipping**
- Diagnosis: Not properly secured, wet/slippery, overloaded
- Immediate: Stop operation; secure plate
- Check: Is plate rated for the load weight?
- Check: Is plate anchored on both sides?
- Prevention: Verify plate capacity before every use; clean oil/water from surface

**Problem: Can't fit through aisle or doorway**
- Diagnosis: Aisle too narrow or forklift too wide
- Measure: Know your forklift's overall width before entering
- Action: Stop; find alternate route; do not force through
- Prevention: Know truck dimensions; survey facility before operating in new areas

**Problem: Battery runs out mid-shift**
- Diagnosis: Insufficient charge, longer shift than planned, battery degradation
- Action: Park safely; set parking brake; report to supervisor
- Prevention: Check charge at shift start; swap to charged battery; plan for shift length

## Incident Procedures

**Problem: Minor collision (no injury)**
- Action:
  1. Stop immediately
  2. Do not move forklift until incident is assessed
  3. Notify supervisor immediately
  4. Document: What happened, where, when, witnesses
  5. Photos of scene, damage, surrounding area
  6. Do not operate forklift until cleared

**Problem: Load dropped**
- Immediate: Clear area of personnel
- Check: For damage to load, forklift, floor, equipment
- Check: For injuries (load may bounce or shift)
- Action: Notify supervisor; document incident; inspect forklift for damage before resuming

**Problem: Witness a near-miss involving another forklift**
- Action:
  1. Stop your forklift
  2. Ensure no one is injured
  3. Notify your supervisor immediately
  4. Document what you witnessed
  5. Both operators should file reports
  6. Near-misses are learning opportunities — report every one
