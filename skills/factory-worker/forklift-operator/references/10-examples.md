# Forklift Operator — Real-World Scenarios

## Scenario 1: Unknown Load Capacity Question

**Context:** A pallet of rolled steel comes in from a new supplier. The pallet is marked "2,500 lbs" but you don't know the actual weight. The forklift nameplate says 4,000 lbs at 24" load center.

**Assessment:**
- Weight: Unknown (assume until verified)
- Dimensions: Rolled steel coils may have extended load center
- Actual load center might be 30" or more

**What you must do:**
1. Do NOT lift until you know the actual weight
2. Get a scale weight — use warehouse scale or ask receiving to verify
3. Calculate capacity at actual load center:
   - If 30" center: 4,000 × (24/30) = 3,200 lbs max
   - If 2,500 lbs at 30" center: Within capacity
4. If weight exceeds capacity at actual center: Notify supervisor
5. Do not exceed nameplate capacity regardless of what anyone says

**What NOT to do:**
- "It looks about right" is not a safe assessment
- "The last one was fine" doesn't account for this specific load
- Accepting someone's verbal weight estimate without verification

## Scenario 2: Pedestrian Intersection During Rush Hour

**Context:** You need to cross a main pedestrian aisle during the 7am shift change when dozens of people are walking.

**Risk factors:**
- Maximum pedestrian density
- Many people distracted, looking at phones
- High noise level making horn harder to hear
- Multiple directions of pedestrian movement

**Required actions:**
1. Stop before entering the intersection
2. Sound horn continuously as you approach
3. Make eye contact with pedestrians before proceeding
4. If no clear path: Wait for pedestrians to pass
5. Move at walking pace (3 mph maximum)
6. Do not proceed until path is clear

**Alternative:** If possible, wait until pedestrian rush is over, or use alternate route.

**Best practice:** Schedule forklift travel to avoid shift change times when possible.

## Scenario 3: Cold Storage Operation

**Context:** Your warehouse has a cold storage area (35°F). A forklift is assigned for cold storage only.

**Special considerations:**
- Battery drains faster in cold
- Tire pressure changes in cold
- Operator comfort affects safety
- Floor may be wet/icy inside and outside cold storage

**Required adaptations:**
1. Pre-warm the battery by running forklift 5 minutes before heavy loads
2. Check tire condition more frequently (cold affects rubber)
3. Move loads quickly — minimize time in cold storage
4. Watch for floor transitions (wet to dry = slip hazard)
5. Use insulated gloves while maintaining control

**Leaving cold storage:**
- Let forklift acclimate 5 minutes before charging
- Never charge cold battery — let it warm to room temperature first
- Check for condensation on electrical components

## Scenario 4: Narrow Aisle Navigation

**Context:** A reach truck must enter a narrow aisle (6 feet wide) with stacked pallets on both sides. Visibility is limited.

**Pre-entry checklist:**
1. Know your truck width: Typical reach truck is ~45 inches wide
2. Know your aisle width: Is it rated for your truck class?
3. Check for protruding pallets or debris
4. Know the load heights you will encounter
5. Have a spotter if at all possible

**Navigation procedure:**
1. Stop at aisle entrance
2. Visually check first 10 feet of aisle
3. Enter slowly (walking pace)
4. Stop, check, proceed in short segments
5. Tilt mast back slightly for better rear visibility when traveling backward
6. If blocked: Back out and wait for clearance, do not push forward blindly

**What NOT to do:**
- "Push through" if blocked
- Travel forward without seeing path
- Rush to save time

## Scenario 5: Receiving Dock Emergency

**Context:** A semi-truck driver has backed into the dock and needs to be unloaded immediately. The dock plate is already in place but you notice it has shifted slightly to one side.

**Immediate actions:**
1. Do NOT use the dock plate until verified as secure
2. Check: Is the plate anchored on both sides?
3. Check: Does the plate span the gap completely?
4. Check: Is the plate rated for the load weight?
5. If uncertain: Report to supervisor; do not operate

**Why this matters:**
- An unsecured dock plate can slip under load
- A truck driver once drove across a shifted plate — it slid, truck went into dock
- Lives depend on proper dock plate placement

**Verification steps:**
1. Physically push on plate to verify it won't move
2. Check anchor points are engaged
3. Confirm plate is centered on dock edge
4. Report any concerns before starting

## Scenario 6: Battery Replacement Protocol

**Context:** Electric forklift shows 25% charge mid-shift. One more hour of work needed.

**Decision tree:**
- Is 25% enough for remaining work? → Yes, continue but plan swap
- Is 25% not enough? → Swap battery immediately

**Battery swap procedure:**
1. Park on level surface
2. Apply parking brake
3. Lower forks completely to floor
4. Turn key to OFF
5. Open battery compartment
6. Connect battery exchange equipment (charger lift or pallet jack)
7. Remove discharged battery — set on pallet
8. Place charged battery in compartment — verify seating
9. Close compartment
10. Check electrolyte level if visible (do not overfill)
11. Start forklift, verify operation

**Battery safety:**
- Never smoke near charging area
- Wear face shield when checking electrolyte
- Have eye wash station accessible
- Never use damaged battery
