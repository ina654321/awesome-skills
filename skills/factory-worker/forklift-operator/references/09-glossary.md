# Forklift Operator — Glossary

## Equipment & Components

**Mast** — The vertical structure that supports the carriage and provides lifting. Types: simplex (single stage), duplex (two stage), triplex (three stage).

**Carriage** — The component that slides on the mast and holds the forks or attachments.

**Fork** — The two prongs that engage the pallet. Each fork is a separate component.

**Backrest** — Extension on carriage that prevents loads from falling backward.

**Load Backrest** — Extension on carriage preventing load from falling toward operator.

**Overhead Guard (ROPS/FOPS)** — Cage structure above operator protecting from falling objects and roll-over protection.

**Counterweight** — Heavy weight at rear of forklift providing stability against forward tipping.

**Steering Axle** — Front or rear axle that provides steering function.

**Drive Axle** — Axle containing the drive motor (electric) or differential (IC).

## Load & Capacity Terms

**Rated Capacity** — Maximum load the forklift can safely lift, as listed on the nameplate.

**Load Center** — Horizontal distance from the vertical face of the fork to the load's center of gravity.

**Combined Load Center** — Center of gravity of truck + load system.

**Stability Triangle** — Invisible triangle formed by the center points of the three wheels. Combined center of gravity must stay inside.

**Tip-Over Point** — The moment when the center of gravity reaches the stability triangle boundary.

**Surcharge** — Condition when load center is further forward than fork heel.

**Dynamic Stability** — Forklift stability during movement, turning, and acceleration.

**Static Stability** — Forklift stability when stationary with load.

## Safety & Operations Terms

**PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)** — Safety equipment required: steel-toe boots, high-visibility vest, hard hat in certain facilities.

**Pre-Operation Inspection** — Daily checklist required by OSHA before first use of each shift.

**Lockout/Tagout** — Procedure for de-energizing equipment during maintenance.

**Pedestrian Zone** — Areas where foot traffic and forklift traffic mix.

**Sightline** — Clear view of travel path required before moving.

**Spotter** — Assistant who helps guide forklift operator when visibility is limited.

**Dock Plate / Dock Board** — Metal plate bridging the gap between dock and truck for loading/unloading.

**Ramp/Grade** — Inclined surface. Forklifts have maximum rated grade capacity.

**Grade Rating** — Maximum grade percentage the forklift can safely operate on.

## Battery & Fuel Terms

**Battery Charge Level** — Percentage of battery charge remaining. Never operate below 20%.

**Equalize Charge** — Extended charging cycle that balances battery cells.

**Opportunity Charging** — Brief charging during breaks to extend shift operation.

**Battery Ventilation** — Required airflow in charging areas to dissipate hydrogen gas.

**Battery Capacity** — Ampere-hours available. Decreases with age and discharge cycles.

**Fueling Area** — Designated area for refueling LP, diesel, or gasoline forklifts.

**Propane Sniffer** — Device detecting LP gas leaks.

## Control & Maneuvering Terms

**Tilt** — Mast angle adjustment: forward (dumping) or backward (securing load).

**Lift/Lower** — Vertical mast movement.

**Reach/Retract** — Moving carriage in and out (for reach trucks).

**Side Shift** — Moving carriage left and right to position forks.

**Inching** — Using brakes to control speed while clutch is partially engaged.

**Coast** — Traveling with clutch disengaged (not recommended).

**Differential** — Mechanism allowing drive wheels to spin at different speeds during turns.

**Articulated Steering** — Steering through a pivot point in the middle of the vehicle.

## Incident & Reporting Terms

**Near-Miss** — Incident that could have caused injury or damage but did not.

**OSHA Recordable** — Injury requiring medical treatment beyond first aid.

**LODD** — Loss of Life Due to accident.

**Incident Report** — Documentation of any accident or near-miss.

**Property Damage Report** — Documentation of damage to equipment, facilities, or products.

**Safety Observation** — Documented observation of unsafe conditions or behaviors.

## Warehouse & Logistics Terms

**Pick** — Removing product from storage location.

**Putaway** — Moving received product to storage location.

**Cycle Count** — Inventory verification by counting select locations.

**Cross-Docking** — Direct transfer from inbound to outbound without storage.

**Congestion** — Overcrowding that creates collision risks.

**Dock Door** — Loading/unloading opening in warehouse wall.

**Yard Spotter** — Forklift for moving trailers in yard, not in warehouse.
