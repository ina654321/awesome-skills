# Assembly Line Worker — Glossary

## Production & Quality Terms

**Takt Time** — The rate at which products must be completed to meet customer demand. Formula: Available Production Time / Customer Demand.

**Cycle Time** — The actual time to complete one work cycle at a station. Must be less than or equal to takt time.

**Lead Time** — Total time from order receipt to product shipment.

**Uptime** — Percentage of scheduled time the line is running vs. stopped.

**First Pass Yield (FPY)** — Percentage of units passing quality inspection on the first attempt, without rework.

**OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)** — Composite metric: Availability × Performance × Quality. Target: 85%+.

**WIP (Work in Process)** — Units between stations that are not yet complete. Excess WIP indicates imbalance.

**Andon** — Visual management system (light/screen) showing line status. Green = running, Yellow = attention needed, Red = stopped.

**Poka-Yoke** — Error-proofing device or method that prevents or detects assembly mistakes.

## Tool & Equipment Terms

**Torque Wrench** — Precision tool for applying specific torque to fasteners. Must be calibrated regularly.

**Go/No-Go Gauge** — Inspection tool that passes if within tolerance, stops if outside.

**Kanban** — Visual signal for material replenishment. Card or bin-level trigger.

**ECN (Engineering Change Notice)** — Documented change to product design or process.

**Work Instruction** — Detailed step-by-step procedure for performing a specific task.

**Standard Work Sheet** — Document showing the current best method with timing.

**Bill of Materials (BOM)** — Complete list of components needed to assemble one unit.

## Quality Terms

**CTQ (Critical to Quality)** — Product characteristics that must be met to satisfy customer requirements.

**AQL (Acceptable Quality Level)** — Maximum percentage of defective items considered acceptable in a sample.

**NCR (Non-Conformance Report)** — Document recording a quality deviation from specifications.

**Quarantine** — Area where rejected or suspect material is held pending disposition.

**FOD (Foreign Object Debris)** — Any foreign material (tool, fastener, debris) left in a product.

**Rework** — Repairing a defective unit to bring it within specification.

**Scrap** — Defective units that cannot be repaired and must be discarded.

## Lean Manufacturing Terms

**Kaizen** — Continuous improvement through small, daily changes.

**Gemba** — The actual place where work is performed.

**SMED (Single Minute Exchange of Dies)** — Changeover time reduction methodology.

**Heijunka** — Production leveling to reduce variation and waste.

**Jidoka** — Automation with a human touch. Stopping the line when a problem occurs.

**5S** — Workplace organization: Sort, Set in order, Shine, Standardize, Sustain.

**Value Stream** — All activities (value-added and non-value-added) that a product goes through.

## Safety Terms

**PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)** — Safety gear: glasses, gloves, hearing protection, etc.

**Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)** — Safety procedure to ensure dangerous machines are properly shut off.

**Ergonomic Injury** — Musculoskeletal disorder from repetitive motion or poor posture.

**RSI (Repetitive Strain Injury)** — Injury from repeated motions without ergonomic intervention.

**Near Miss** — An event that could have caused injury or damage but did not.

**JSA (Job Safety Analysis)** — Systematic analysis of work steps to identify hazards.

## Handoff & Communication Terms

**Shift Handoff** — Transfer of work and status information between outgoing and incoming shifts.

**Escalation** — Raising an issue to a higher authority when first-line resolution fails.

**Line Balance** — Distribution of work elements across stations to minimize wait time.

**Constraint (Drum-Buffer-Rope)** — The bottleneck station that determines overall line speed.

**Buffer** — Inventory between stations to absorb variation in cycle times.
