# Assembly Line Worker — Standards & Reference

## Industry Standards & Certification Bodies

### Manufacturing Standards

**ISO 9001:2015** — Quality Management Systems. The foundational standard for manufacturing quality. Establishes requirements for quality policy, objectives, control of documents, and continuous improvement. Website: iso.org

**IATF 16949:2016** — Automotive Quality Management System. Supersedes ISO/TS 16949. Required for automotive supply chain. Website: iatfglobalwatch.org

**ISO 14001:2015** — Environmental Management Systems. Manages environmental responsibilities systematically.

**ANSI/AIAG CQI-9** — Heat Treat System Assessment. Critical for automotive assembly with heat-treated components.

**ISO 45001:2018** — Occupational Health and Safety. Replaces OHSAS 18001.

### Lean Manufacturing Standards

**Toyota Production System (TPS)** — The foundational Lean system. Principles: Just-in-Time, Jidoka, Heijunka. Reference: "Toyota Production System" by Taiichi Ohno.

**Shingo Model** — Enterprise-wide assessment of lean principles. Dimensions: Cultural Enablers, Continuous Improvement, Standard Work, Enterprise Alignment. Website: shingo.org

**Society of Manufacturing Engineers (SME)** — Professional organization for manufacturing professionals. Resources, certifications, and best practices. Website: sme.org

**American Society of Quality (ASQ)** — Quality certifications (CQT, CQI, CQE). Website: asq.org

### Assembly-Specific Standards

**JIS (Japanese Industrial Standards)** — Assembly precision standards. JIS B 0652 for surface roughness comparison specimens.

**GD&T (ASME Y14.5)** — Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing. The standard language for engineering drawings in North America.

**ISO 1101** — Geometrical product specifications. International tolerancing standard.

## Manufacturing Methods & Techniques

### Assembly Methods Reference

| Method | Description | Application | Cycle Time |
|--------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| **Continuous Flow** | One-piece flow through sequential stations | High-volume, simple products | ~5-15 sec |
| **Batch Assembly** | Multiple units processed in groups | Medium-volume, complex products | ~30-60 sec |
| **Cell Assembly** | Single worker completes entire unit | Low-volume, high-mix | ~10-30 min |
| **U-Line Assembly** | Workers stationary, product moves | High-volume, standardized | ~10-20 sec |
| **Zone Assembly** | Work divided by zones, multiple workers per zone | Large products (vehicles, appliances) | Variable |

### Standard Work Documentation

**Standard Work Combination Sheet** — Visual document showing work element sequence, time, method, and balance between stations.

**Standard Work Sheet** — Documents the current best method including takt time, cycle time, and work sequence.

**Takt Time Formula:** Available Production Time / Customer Demand = Takt Time

Example: 480 min shift - 40 min break = 440 min available / 880 units demand = 30 sec takt time

### Key Lean Terms

**Uptime** — Percentage of scheduled time the line is actually running. Target: >90%.

**OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness)** — OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality. World-class target: 85%.

**SMED (Single Minute Exchange of Dies)** — Reducing changeover time. Goal: under 10 minutes.

**Kaizen** — Continuous improvement. Small, daily improvements that accumulate.

**Poka-Yoke** — Error-proofing. Designing processes to prevent mistakes.

**Andon** — Visual signal system for production status. Enables immediate response to problems.

## Professional Certifications

**Certified Production Technician (CPT)** — MSSC certification covering safety, quality, manufacturing processes, and maintenance awareness. Website: mssctraining.org

**Certified Lean Practitioner** — Shingo Institute certification for lean principles.

**IPC Certification (J-STD-001)** — Requirements for Soldered Electrical and Electronic Assemblies. Essential for electronics assembly workers.

**AWS Certified Welding Inspector** — For assembly involving welded components.
