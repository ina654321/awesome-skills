## § 8 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: High-Growth Scale-Up HR Setup

**Context:** Tech startup growing from 50 to 500 employees in 18 months.

**Challenges:**
- No formal HR processes
- Ad-hoc hiring with quality issues
- No performance management
- Equity and compensation inconsistency
- Culture dilution risk

**Solution:**
- Implement Greenhouse ATS with structured interviews
- Create leveling framework (5 levels, 10 competencies)
- Launch quarterly OKR process
- Standardize compensation bands
- Define values and behavioral interview questions

**Results:**
- Time to hire: 60 days → 35 days
- Quality of hire (manager rating): 3.2 → 4.1
- Voluntary turnover: 25% → 12%
- Engagement score: 3.8 → 4.3

### Scenario 2: Post-Merger Integration

**Context:** Two 1,000-person companies merging, different cultures.

**Challenges:**
- Compensation disparities (20% gaps)
- Duplicate roles (30% overlap)
- Different performance systems
- Culture clash (hierarchical vs. flat)
- Retention risk for key talent

**Integration Plan:**
- Day 1: Communication, retention bonuses for top 10%
- Month 1-3: Organization design, role mapping
- Month 4-6: Compensation harmonization
- Month 7-12: Culture integration, values alignment

**Actions:**
- Retention rate for key talent: 95%
- Redundancies handled with dignity and packages
- New performance system launched
- Culture workshops for all managers

**Results:**
- Synergies achieved 6 months early
- Employee NPS: -10 → +25 in 12 months
- Key customer retention: 98%

### Scenario 3: Performance Management Transformation

**Context:** Annual reviews hated by employees and managers, no impact.

**Current State:**
- Annual review only
- 360 feedback (dreaded, little value)
- Forced ranking (gaming, politics)
- No ongoing feedback

**New Approach:**
- Continuous feedback (weekly 1:1s)
- Quarterly goal check-ins
- Peer recognition system
- Calibration with growth focus
- Remove forced ranking

**Change Management:**
- Manager training (4 hours)
- Tool implementation (Lattice)
- Pilot with volunteer teams
- Iterate based on feedback

**Results:**
- Manager time on reviews: 40 hrs → 15 hrs/year
- Employee satisfaction with process: 35% → 78%
- Goal achievement rate: 65% → 82%
- Voluntary turnover: -20%

### Scenario 4: Diversity & Inclusion Initiative

**Context:** Tech company with demographic imbalances, inclusion concerns.

**Current State:**
- Engineering: 85% male, 70% white/Asian
- Leadership: 90% male
- Inclusion score: 3.2/5

**Strategy:**
1. Pipeline: Partner with HBCUs, women's coding bootcamps
2. Process: Bias training, structured interviews, diverse panels
3. Culture: ERGs, inclusive leadership training, zero tolerance policy
4. Measurement: D&I dashboard, targets by level

**Initiatives:**
- Unbiased job descriptions
- Diverse slate requirement
- Mentoring program
- Inclusive leadership training

**Results (24 months):**
- Engineering diversity: 15% → 28% female
- Leadership diversity: 10% → 20% female
- Inclusion score: 3.2 → 4.1
- Representation targets set for all levels

### Scenario 5: Compensation Restructuring

**Context:** Company with pay inequities, retention issues, unclear structure.

**Issues:**
- Pay gaps by gender (8%), ethnicity (12%)
- Inconsistent offers for same roles
- No promotion criteria
- Equity grants inconsistent

**Project:**
1. Market study: Radford data, 75th percentile target
2. Job architecture: 50 job families, 5 levels each
3. Pay equity audit: Statistical analysis, adjustments
4. Communication: Transparent about ranges
5. Manager training: Compensation philosophy

**Actions:**
- Adjustments for 15% of population ($2M investment)
- Published salary ranges
- Clear promotion criteria
- Equity refresh guidelines

**Results:**
- Pay gaps: <3% across all dimensions
- Voluntary turnover: -30%
- Offer acceptance rate: +15%
- Manager confidence in comp decisions: +40 points
