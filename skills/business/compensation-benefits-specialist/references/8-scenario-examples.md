## § 8 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Startup Compensation Structure Build

**Context:** Series A startup (50 employees) needs formal comp structure.

**Current State:**
- No salary bands
- Offers based on negotiation
- Equity grants inconsistent
- Pay compression emerging

**Solution:**
- Job architecture: 5 families, 4 levels each
- Market data: Radford Series A benchmark
- Philosophy: Match P50 for base, lead P75 for equity
- Structure: 50% ranges, geo-adjusted

**Results:**
- 8 salary bands defined
- Equity grant matrix by level
- Pay equity: 3% gap identified, remediated
- Offer consistency improved
- Budget predictability achieved

### Scenario 2: Pay Equity Audit & Remediation

**Context:** Public company, pay transparency law coming, proactive audit.

**Audit Scope:**
- 2,500 employees
- Gender and ethnicity analysis
- Base, bonus, equity, total comp

**Findings:**
- Overall gender gap: 4% (statistically significant)
- Unexplained gap after controls: 2%
- 45 individuals with >5% unexplained gap

**Remediation:**
- $450,000 in adjustments
- Focus on underpaid women in technical roles
- Process fixes: offer guidelines, promotion review

**Results:**
- Gap reduced to <1%
- Legal risk mitigated
- Employee trust improved
- Transparent pay bands published

### Scenario 3: Sales Compensation Redesign

**Context:** SaaS company, sales team missing targets, high turnover.

**Current Plan:**
- Base + 10% commission on revenue
- No cap
- Quarterly payout
- Individual quota only

**Issues:**
- Commission too low to motivate
- Quota setting inaccurate
- No team component
- High performers leaving for better OTE

**Redesign:**
- OTE split: 50/50 base/variable
- Accelerators: 1.5x for 100-125%, 2x for 125%+
- Components: 70% individual, 20% team, 10% company
- Monthly payout for <100%, quarterly accelerator

**Results:**
- Attrition: 40% → 20%
- Quota attainment: 60% → 85%
- Revenue per rep: +30%
- Cost of sales: Stable

### Scenario 4: Benefits Optimization

**Context:** 500-employee company, benefits costs rising 15% annually.

**Current State:**
- Rich PPO plan, low employee contribution
- No wellness program
- 401(k) match: 3%
- Low engagement with benefits

**Optimization Strategy:**
- Add HDHP + HSA option (lower premium)
- Employer HSA contribution: $500
- Wellness program with premium incentives
- 401(k) match increase to 50% up to 6%

**Communication:**
- Benefits fair
- Decision support tools
- Personalized total value statements

**Results:**
- Premium increase: 5% (vs. 15% trend)
- HDHP adoption: 40%
- Wellness participation: 60%
- 401(k) participation: 45% → 78%
- Employee satisfaction: Improved

### Scenario 5: Executive Compensation Design

**Context:** Public company board needs to redesign executive comp.

**Objectives:**
- Align with long-term shareholder value
- Retain key executives
- Market competitive
- Simple, understandable

**Design:**
- Base: P50 market (CEO: $800K)
- Target Bonus: 100% of base (performance-based)
- Long-Term Incentive: RSUs (3-year cliff vest)
  - Target: $5M (CEO)
  - Performance modifier: 0.5x to 2x based on TSR
- Benefits: Standard executive package

**Performance Metrics:**
- Revenue growth (30%)
- EBITDA margin (30%)
- Relative TSR (40%)

**Governance:**
- Compensation committee oversight
- Independent consultant
- Say-on-pay vote
- Clawback policy

**Results:**
- Shareholder approval: 92%
- Executive retention: 100%
- Performance alignment: Strong correlation
