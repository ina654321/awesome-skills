# Stripe Company Profile

## Overview

Stripe is a programmable financial services company that provides economic infrastructure for internet businesses. Founded in 2010 by Irish brothers Patrick and John Collison, Stripe has grown from a simple payments API to a comprehensive suite of financial tools powering millions of businesses worldwide.

## Founding Story

**The Problem:** Before Stripe, accepting payments online required weeks of paperwork, complex integrations with legacy banking systems, and significant upfront costs. Startups and small businesses faced enormous friction to monetize.

**The Solution:** Patrick and John Collison, both accomplished programmers, built Stripe with a developer-first approach. The original integration famously required just seven lines of code — a radical simplification compared to existing solutions.

**Timeline:**
- **2009:** Stripe concept developed
- **2010:** Company founded in Palo Alto, CA
- **2011:** Public launch after Y Combinator
- **2012:** Connect launched for marketplaces
- **2015:** Radar (fraud detection) and Atlas (incorporation) launched
- **2018:** Terminal, Issuing, and Billing expanded Stripe's scope
- **2020:** Climate and Treasury added
- **2024-2025:** AI-powered billing features, $1.9T payment volume

## Leadership

### Patrick Collison — CEO
- Born 1988 in Ireland
- Self-taught programmer from age 10
- Youngest ever winner of the Young Scientist and Technology Exhibition (2005)
- Studied at MIT (left to start Stripe)
- Known for: Intellectual curiosity, first-principles thinking, long-term vision

### John Collison — President
- Born 1990 in Ireland
- Studied at Harvard (left to start Stripe)
- Youngest self-made billionaire (at age 27)
- Known for: Product discipline, engineering rigor, international expansion

### Key Leadership Principles
1. **"Talking up to the user"** — Design for intelligence, don't patronize
2. **First-principles thinking** — Solve from fundamentals, not by copying
3. **Long-term infrastructure building** — Prioritize over short-term gains
4. **Global parity** — Stripe should work as well in Lagos as in San Francisco

## Strategic Direction (2025-2026)

### Four Pillars Strategy

1. **Payments** — Core money movement (still the foundation)
2. **Connect** — Platforms and marketplaces (major growth area)
3. **Revenue Automation** — Billing, Tax, Invoicing (~$1B ARR projected)
4. **Financial Services** — Issuing, Treasury, Capital

### Key Initiatives

**AI Integration:**
- AI-powered billing for LLM token usage
- Radar Assistant (AI chatbot for fraud rules)
- Automated revenue recovery
- Smart Retries for failed payments

**Global Expansion:**
- Tax collection expanding to 102 countries
- Terminal available in 23 countries
- Strong push into emerging markets

**Crypto/Stablecoins:**
- Reintroduced crypto payments (USDC) in 2024
- $1.1B acquisition of Bridge (2024) for stablecoin infrastructure
- Focus on cross-border payments where fiat fails

**Climate:**
- Frontier: $925M advance market commitment for carbon removal
- Stripe Climate: Automatic revenue donation option
- Funding from Stripe, Shopify, Alphabet, Meta, McKinsey

## IPO Considerations

Stripe remains private as of March 2026, though widely expected to IPO eventually:

**Reasons for Delay:**
- No need for capital (profitable, recent valuations provide liquidity)
- Tender offers provide exit for employees/investors
- Avoid quarterly pressure and disclosure requirements
- Focus on long-term infrastructure building

**Recent Liquidity Events:**
- Feb 2025: $91.5B tender offer
- Feb 2026: $159B tender offer (Thrive, Coatue, a16z)
