# Google Antitrust & Regulatory Timeline

Comprehensive record of antitrust cases, fines, and regulatory actions against Google/Alphabet.

## United States Cases

### DOJ v. Google (Search Monopoly)
**Filed:** October 2020

**Allegations:**
- Illegal monopoly in general search (90%+ market share)
- Monopoly maintenance through exclusive distribution deals
- Payments to Apple ($20B/year), Mozilla, Android OEMs
- Suppression of competition through default placement

**Liability Ruling:** August 2024
- Court found Google violated Section 2 of Sherman Act
- Monopoly maintained through exclusive agreements

**Remedies Trial:** September 2025

**DOJ Proposed Remedies:**
- Divestiture of Chrome browser
- Data sharing with competitors
- End to default payment agreements
- Mandatory syndication of search results
- API access to search index

**Google's Position:**
- Appeal filed for liability ruling
- Argues remedies would harm consumers
- Proposes behavioral modifications instead of structural

**Status:** Appeals expected to take 2-3 years

### DOJ v. Google (Ad Tech)
**Filed:** January 2023 (Virginia), December 2020 (Texas)

**Allegations:**
- Monopolization of digital advertising technology
- Self-preferencing in ad tech stack
- Acquisition of DoubleClick, AdMeld anti-competitive
- Control of publisher ad server and ad exchange

**Trial:** September 2024

**Ruling:** April 2025 (Judge Brinkema)
- Found Google holds illegal monopolies in:
  - Publisher ad servers
  - Ad exchanges
- Violated Sherman Act Section 2

**Potential Remedies:**
- Divestiture of Ad Manager
- Divestiture of AdX exchange
- Operational separation of ad tech business

**Status:** Remedies phase pending

### Epic v. Google (Android)
**Filed:** 2020

**Allegations:**
- Anti-competitive practices in Play Store
- Forced use of Google Billing (30% fee)
- Anti-steering provisions

**Verdict:** December 2023 (Jury trial)
- Found Google liable on all counts

**Remedies (Injunction):**
- Must allow third-party app stores
- Cannot require Google Billing exclusively
- Cannot restrict sideloading
- Anti-steering provisions removed

**Status:** Implementation ongoing; Google appeal pending

## European Union Cases

### Google Shopping (2017)
**Decision:** June 2017

**Allegations:**
- Self-preferencing in search results
- Demoting competitor shopping services
- Promoting Google Shopping

**Fine:** €2.4 billion ($2.7B)

**Remedies:**
- Equal treatment of shopping services
- Separate auction for shopping ads

**Appeal:** 
- 2024: Fine upheld by General Court
- Appeal to CJEU pending

**Compliance:** 
- Google made payment in 2024: $3.0 billion
- Shopping service changes implemented

### Android (2018)
**Decision:** July 2018

**Allegations:**
- Illegal tying of Google Search and Chrome
- Anti-fragmentation agreements
- Exclusive revenue sharing agreements

**Fine:** €4.3 billion (reduced to €4.1B on appeal)

**Remedies:**
- Choice screen for search/browser
- Unbundling of Google apps
- Modified licensing terms

**Appeal:**
- 2022: General Court affirmed liability, reduced fine
- Appeal to CJEU pending

**Compliance:**
- Choice screen implemented in EU
- Changes to licensing terms

### AdSense for Search (2019)
**Decision:** March 2019

**Allegations:**
- Restrictive clauses in AdSense contracts
- Prevented publishers from showing competitor ads

**Fine:** €1.5 billion

**Appeal:**
- September 2024: General Court overturned decision
- Fine annulled
- EC appeal to CJEU pending

### Ad Tech (2025)
**Decision:** September 2025

**Allegations:**
- Self-preferencing in ad tech services
- Favoring own ad tech on buy-side and sell-side
- Distorting competition in ad tech

**Fine:** €2.95 billion ($3.5 billion)

**Remedies:**
- Cease self-preferencing practices
- Structural/behavioral changes required

**Status:** Google announced intention to appeal

## Other Jurisdictions

### Japan (JFTC)
**Investigation:** October 2023

**Ruling:** April 2025

**Findings:**
- Android agreements violated Japanese antitrust law
- Restrictions on OEM customization

**Remedy:**
- Cease-and-desist order
- Changes to Android agreements
- No monetary penalty

**Status:** Google engaging on compliance

### South Korea (KFTC)
**Cases:** Multiple investigations (2021-2024)

**Rulings:**
- App store billing restrictions: $177M fine
- Android OS abuse: $207M fine

**Remedies:**
- Alternative payment systems allowed
- Changes to Android practices

### India (CCI)
**Cases:** Multiple investigations

**Rulings:**
- 2022: $162M fine for Android abuse
- 2023: $113M fine for Play Store policies
- 2024: Finding on search bias

**Remedies:**
- Choice screen requirements
- Unbundling of apps
- Third-party billing allowed

### Australia (ACCC)
**Investigation:** June 2022

**Focus:** Search distribution practices

**Status:** Ongoing

### UK (CMA)
**Investigations:** Multiple ongoing

**Focus:**
- Privacy Sandbox concerns
