### Fleet Strategy Highlights

- **A321neo Deployment**: 92 in service, 97 on order (189 total) — largest-scale fleet type in Delta history
  - 20-30% more fuel efficient than aircraft replaced
  - Lowest unit cost of any narrowbody in fleet
  - More First and Comfort+ seats than any other narrowbody
  
- **A220 Deployment**: Replacing regional jets and older 717s; optimal for 100-150 seat markets
  - 20% better fuel burn per seat vs. aircraft replaced

- **A350 Flagship**: Premium long-haul service with Delta One suites
  - 37 in service, 35 additional on order
  - 25% fuel efficiency advantage vs. previous generation

- **787-10 Order**: 30 aircraft, deliveries begin 2031
  - 336 passengers, 25% lower fuel burn
  - Supports Asia, Europe, Middle East expansion
  - Diversifies widebody fleet beyond Airbus

- **767 Retirement**: Phasing out by 2030; replaced by A330neo and 787-10

- **757 Retirement**: Limited replacements; routes upgauged to A321neo

## Centennial Milestones (1925-2025)

Delta celebrated its 100th anniversary in 2025, marking a century of aviation excellence:

- **1925**: Founded as Huff Daland Dusters (crop dusting)
- **1929**: Became Delta Air Service (passenger service began)
- **1953**: Chicago and Southern Air Lines merger
- **1972**: Northeast Airlines merger
- **1987**: Western Airlines merger
- **1991**: Pan Am transatlantic routes acquisition
- **2008**: Northwest Airlines merger
- **2015**: Industry-leading profit sharing program launch ($1B+ annually)
- **2024**: Air Transport World Airline of the Year
- **2025**: Centennial year, record $63.4B revenue, 200M+ customers served
