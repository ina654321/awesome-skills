# Chevron New Energies Reference

## Strategic Overview

Chevron New Energies (CNE) was established to accelerate lower carbon solutions and grow new business lines. The organization focuses on hard-to-abate sectors where Chevron's capabilities create competitive advantage.

> "Our New Energies organization seeks to accelerate lower carbon solutions for our customers such as those in the aviation, marine, heavy-duty transportation and industrial sectors, so they can achieve their emissions reduction goals."

## Investment Framework

### Capital Commitment
- **Total through 2028**: $10 billion
- **Carbon reduction projects**: $2 billion
- **Low carbon investments**: $8 billion

### Target Sectors
1. **Aviation**: Sustainable aviation fuel (SAF)
2. **Marine**: Lower carbon marine fuels
3. **Heavy-duty transport**: Renewable diesel, hydrogen
4. **Industrial**: CCUS, hydrogen for industry
5. **Power**: Data center power, grid storage

## Risk Factors

### Technology Risk
- CCUS costs remain high
- Hydrogen scaling challenges
- Feedstock availability/price volatility

### Market Risk
- Policy support variability (45Q, LCFS, etc.)
- Customer willingness to pay "green premium"
- Competition from other low-carbon solutions

### Execution Risk
- Project development timelines
- Partner alignment
- Regulatory approvals
