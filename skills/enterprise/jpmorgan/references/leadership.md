## The Dimon Leadership Doctrine

### Core Principles

#### 1. Facts Over Narrative
> *"Don't try to use numbers to prove what you think. Try to use numbers to understand what you are doing."*

- Data-driven decision making
- Intellectual honesty
- Challenge assumptions
- Let evidence guide strategy

#### 2. Honest Assessment
> *"Confront reality, however unpleasant."*

- No sugar-coating problems
- Early identification of issues
- Transparent communication
- Willingness to change course

#### 3. Disciplined Team Assembly
> *"A lot of people who run stuff, they're a hot mess. Don't let them run something because they'll be a disaster."*

- Meritocracy above politics
- Competence over charisma
- Character assessment
- Continuous evaluation

#### 4. Institutional Humility
> *"Good leaders have the humility to know that they don't know everything."*

- No "friends of the boss"
- Everyone gets equal treatment
- Open door policy
- Collaborative problem-solving

#### 5. Resilience and Grit
> *"The best way to fight a crisis is to be prepared."*

- Through-the-cycle thinking
- Prepare for worst scenarios
- Decisive action under pressure
- Learn from failures

### The Apprenticeship Culture

**Core Tenets:**
- Learning by doing
- Mentorship at every level
- Real responsibility early
- Error management over error avoidance

**Dimon on Apprenticeship:**
> *"Younger people are being left behind [with remote work]. You learned a little bit from the apprentice system. You were with other people who took you on a sales call or told you how to handle a mistake or something like that. It doesn't happen when you're in a basement on Zoom."*

### Performance Culture

| Aspect | Characteristic |
|--------|---------------|
| **Metrics** | ROTCE, revenue growth, market share |
| **Accountability** | Individual P&L responsibility |
| **Compensation** | Performance-based, highly competitive |
| **Promotion** | Merit-based, rigorous evaluation |
| **Feedback** | Direct, frequent, actionable |

### Return-to-Office Philosophy

Dimon has been a vocal advocate for in-person work:
- **5-day in-office** policy (2025)
- **2,000+ employees** signed petition for hybrid (rejected)
- **Rationale**: Apprenticeship, collaboration, culture
- **Quote**: *"I've had it with this stuff. I've been working seven days a goddamn week since COVID, and I come in, and – where is everybody else?"*
