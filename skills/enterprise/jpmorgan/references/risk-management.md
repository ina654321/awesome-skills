# JPMorgan Chase Risk Management

## The Fortress Balance Sheet Philosophy

> *"We maintain a fortress balance sheet, evidenced by $547 billion of total loss-absorbing capacity and $1.4 trillion of cash and marketable securities."* — Jamie Dimon

The fortress philosophy prioritizes **survival and stability** over short-term optimization. This approach enabled JPMorgan to:
- Acquire Bear Stearns (March 2008)
- Acquire Washington Mutual (September 2008)
- Acquire First Republic (May 2023)

While competitors failed or required government bailouts.

## Risk Management Framework

### The Three Lines of Defense

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  LINE 1: Business Units                                     │
│  • Own risk identification and management                   │
│  • Execute controls and monitoring                          │
│  • First line risk management                               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LINE 2: Independent Risk & Compliance                      │
│  • Enterprise risk oversight                                │
│  • Policy setting and monitoring                            │
│  • Challenge and assurance                                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤

│  • Independent assessment                                   │
│  • Control effectiveness testing                            │
│  • Board reporting                                          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Crisis Management

### Crisis Response Protocol

**Activation Levels:**
1. **Monitoring**: Elevated watch
2. **Alert**: Specific risk emerging
3. **Crisis**: Active threat to firm
4. **Recovery**: Post-crisis management

**Response Team:**
- CEO leadership
- Business unit heads
- Risk and compliance
- Legal and communications
- Operations and technology

### Lessons from 2008 Crisis

**What Worked:**
- Fortress balance sheet
- Conservative underwriting
- Diversified revenue streams
- Decisive leadership

**What Changed:**
- Enhanced liquidity requirements
- Stress testing (CCAR)
- Resolution planning (living wills)
- Capital buffers (G-SIB surcharge)
