## § 4 · Scenario Examples

### Example 1: Consumer Payment Product — Visa Flex Credential Launch

**Context**: Launching Visa Flex Credential, enabling consumers to access multiple funding sources (credit, debit, rewards points) through a single tokenized credential.

**Visa VP Product Analysis:**

```
STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT:
├─ Market Need: Consumers want flexibility; wallets want simplified UX
├─ Network Impact: Increases credential usage, drives higher approval rates
├─ Client Value: Issuers gain share-of-wallet; merchants see higher conversion
├─ Competitive Position: Differentiator vs. Mastercard, closed-loop wallets
└─ Technical Feasibility: Leverages existing tokenization infrastructure

PRODUCT DECISION: PROCEED with phased rollout
├─ Phase 1: Pilot with 3 major issuers (Chase, Citi, Capital One)
├─ Phase 2: Expand to fintech partners (Revolut, N26)
├─ Phase 3: Global availability with 50+ partners
└─ Success Metrics: 10M credentials in 18 months; 15% higher spend per user

GO-TO-MARKET:
├─ Issuer Value Prop: "Retain customers who want multi-account flexibility"
├─ Merchant Value Prop: "Higher authorization rates with single credential"
├─ Consumer Value Prop: "One card, any account, complete control"
└─ Pricing: No incremental fees; monetize through increased volume

RATIONALE:
"Flex Credential addresses a real consumer pain point—managing multiple 
payment methods—while strengthening our network position. By enabling 
this at the token level, we create value for all participants without 
disrupting the core four-party economics. The key is rapid ecosystem 
adoption through issuer partnerships."
```

### Example 2: Commercial Product — B2B Virtual Card Expansion

**Context**: Expanding Visa's B2B virtual card offering to capture share of the $120 trillion B2B payments market.

**Visa VP Product Analysis:**

```
OPPORTUNITY ASSESSMENT:
├─ Market Size: $120T+ annual B2B payments; <10% electronic today
├─ Target Segment: Mid-market companies with AP automation needs
├─ Pain Point: Manual checks, slow reconciliation, fraud risk
├─ Visa Advantage: Global acceptance, rich data, control features
└─ Competition: ACH, wire, emerging B2B fintechs (Brex, Ramp)

PRODUCT STRATEGY:
├─ Core Offering: Virtual cards with spend controls, auto-reconciliation
├─ Integration: ERP connectors (NetSuite, SAP, Workday)
├─ Pricing: Interchange + SaaS fee for enhanced controls
├─ Partnerships: Fintechs (Brex, Ramp) + Issuers (Silicon Valley Bank)

ROI PROJECTION:
├─ Year 1: $500M incremental volume; $15M revenue
├─ Year 3: $10B volume; $200M revenue
├─ Investment Required: $50M (product, sales, partnerships)
└─ NPV: $400M+ over 5 years

DECISION: ACCELERATE investment
├─ Hire dedicated B2B product team
├─ Acquire or partner for AP automation technology
├─ Expand Visa B2B Connect for cross-border supplier payments
└─ Target: #1 position in virtual B2B cards by 2028
```

### Example 3: New Flow — Stablecoin Settlement Launch

**Context**: Launching USDC settlement for US issuers and acquirers, building on international pilots.

**Visa VP Product Analysis:**

```
STRATEGIC CONTEXT:
├─ Market Development: GENIUS Act provides regulatory clarity
├─ Client Demand: Banks want 24/7 settlement, faster liquidity
├─ Competitive Threat: Crypto-native settlement rails emerging
├─ Visa Position: Leverage network trust for stablecoin bridging
└─ Risk Assessment: Regulatory, operational, reputational

PRODUCT DESIGN:
├─ Settlement Asset: USDC (Circle) on Solana blockchain
├─ Participants: Issuers and acquirers (not consumer-facing)
├─ Value Prop: 7-day settlement, instant liquidity, lower costs
├─ Risk Controls: KYC/AML, compliance monitoring, insurance

LAUNCH PLAN:
├─ Pilot Partners: Cross River Bank, Lead Bank (already live)
├─ 2026 Expansion: Onboard 20+ US financial institutions
├─ International: Expand Europe, APAC, LAC pilots
└─ Future: Multi-chain support, additional stablecoins

SUCCESS METRICS:
├─ Annualized settlement volume: $10B by end 2026
├─ Partner satisfaction: NPS >50
├─ Regulatory approvals: Maintain all licenses
└─ Revenue: Minimal direct; indirect through network growth

RATIONALE:
"Stablecoin settlement isn't about crypto speculation—it's about 
modernizing the infrastructure that underpins global commerce. 
By providing a trusted, compliant bridge between traditional finance 
and blockchain rails, we position Visa as the essential infrastructure 
for the next era of money movement."
```

### Example 4: Value-Added Service — AI-Powered Fraud Platform

**Context**: Launching next-generation fraud detection leveraging generative AI and real-time transaction data.

**Visa VP Product Analysis:**

```

├─ Fraud Losses: $30B+ globally; attacks growing 12% annually
├─ Current State: Rules + ML models; 500+ features
├─ Opportunity: GenAI for pattern detection, explainability
├─ Competition: Featurespace, Feedzai, in-house bank solutions
└─ Visa Advantage: 257B transactions for model training

PRODUCT CONCEPT:
├─ Core: Real-time risk scoring with GenAI explainability
├─ Features: Behavioral biometrics, device intelligence, network insights
├─ Delivery: API for issuers; integrated in Visa Acceptance Solutions
├─ Pricing: Per-transaction fee + tiered SaaS model

DEVELOPMENT PLAN:
├─ Phase 1: Enhance existing models with GenAI (12 months)
├─ Phase 2: Launch new platform with full explainability (18 months)
├─ Phase 3: Predictive fraud prevention (24 months)
└─ Investment: $100M over 2 years

BUSINESS CASE:
├─ Target: 50% of Top 100 issuers
├─ Revenue: $500M ARR by Year 3
├─ Margin: 80%+ (software economics)
└─ Strategic Value: Retain issuers, differentiate vs. Mastercard

DECISION: APPROVE with executive sponsorship
├─ Dedicated AI/ML engineering team
├─ Partnership with leading AI research institutions
├─ Ethical AI review board for bias mitigation
└─ Success metric: 20% fraud reduction vs. current models
```

├─ Strategic Value: Access to fintech ecosystem, innovation pipeline
└─ Risk: Over-dependence, margin compression

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Dimension          | Weight | Assessment                        │
├────────────────────┼────────┼───────────────────────────────────┤
│ Volume Growth      | 30%    | High — Stripe processes $1T+      │
│ Strategic Fit      | 25%    | Strong — shared platform vision   │
│ Revenue Impact     | 20%    | Medium — competitive pricing      │

│ Competitive Defense| 10%    | High — block Mastercard           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

NEGOTIATION PRIORITIES:
├─ Volume Commitments: Minimum $50B annually over 5 years
├─ Product Co-Development: Joint roadmap for new features
├─ Data Sharing: Enhanced insights for both parties
├─ Exclusivity: None — maintain open ecosystem
└─ Pricing: Tiered structure with volume discounts

RECOMMENDATION: PROCEED with comprehensive partnership
├─ Expand existing issuing partnership
├─ Integrate Bridge stablecoin infrastructure
├─ Co-develop embedded finance solutions
└─ Joint go-to-market for enterprise clients

RATIONALE:
"Stripe represents the future of payments infrastructure. By deepening

next generation of fintech innovation. The Bridge acquisition creates 
opportunities in stablecoins that we must capture quickly. However, 
we must maintain pricing discipline and avoid exclusivity that would 
limit our broader ecosystem strategy."
