# Visa Partnership Ecosystem

## Partnership Philosophy

Visa's partnership strategy centers on **enabling ecosystem participants** rather than competing with them. The goal is to extend Visa's network effects by making it easy for fintechs, banks, and technology platforms to build on Visa infrastructure.

> *"We are focused on extending, enhancing and investing in our proprietary advanced transaction processing network, VisaNet, to offer a single connection point for facilitating money movement to multiple endpoints through various form factors and innovative technologies."*

## Partnership Governance

### Evaluation Criteria

| Dimension | Weight | Assessment |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Strategic Fit** | 25% | Alignment with Visa's growth levers |
| **Volume Potential** | 25% | Incremental transaction forecast |
| **Innovation Value** | 20% | Technology or capability addition |
| **Risk Profile** | 15% | Compliance, reputation, operational |
| **Financial Return** | 15% | Revenue and profit contribution |

### Partnership Models

| Model | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Standard Commercial** | Licensing, per-transaction fees | Most acquirers |
| **Strategic Investment** | Minority equity stakes | Marqeta, Klarna |
| **Joint Venture** | Shared ownership, combined capabilities | China JV |
| **Acquisition** | Full ownership | Plaid, Cybersource |
| **Co-Innovation** | Joint R&D, shared IP | Apple Pay |

### Partnership Lifecycle

```
1. Identification
   ├── Market scanning
   ├── Client requests
   └── Competitive intelligence

2. Evaluation
   ├── Due diligence
   ├── Business case
   └── Risk assessment

3. Negotiation
   ├── Commercial terms
   ├── Legal agreements
   └── Technical integration plan

4. Integration
   ├── Technical implementation
   ├── Compliance approval
   └── Launch preparation

5. Management
   ├── Performance monitoring

```

## Competitive Partnership Dynamics

### vs. Mastercard

**Exclusivity Considerations:**
- Most large issuers work with both networks
- Some fintechs choose single network initially
- Co-badged cards in certain markets

**Partnership Differentiation:**
- Visa: Larger global acceptance
- Mastercard: Stronger in certain regions (Europe)
- Both: Competing for fintech relationships

### vs. Alternative Payment Methods

**RTP Network Partnerships:**
| Network | Visa Relationship |
|---------|-------------------|
| **FedNow** | Integration, not competition |
| **PIX (Brazil)** | Partnership approach |
| **UPI (India)** | Coexistence, limited overlap |

**Big Tech Dynamics:**
- Apple/Google: Partners (wallet enablement)
- Amazon: Partner + potential competitor
- Meta: Partner (Novi pilot, now ended)

## Partnership Success Stories

### Case Study: Apple Pay Launch (2014)

**Partnership Structure:**
- Joint development of tokenization
- Revenue sharing on transactions
- Co-marketing and launch

**Results:**
- Billions of tokens issued
- Significant contactless adoption driver
- Model for subsequent wallet partnerships

### Case Study: Plaid Acquisition (2024)

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Open banking infrastructure
- Account connectivity for Visa Direct
- Data enrichment for risk products

**Integration:**
- Maintained Plaid brand and operations
- Combined product roadmaps
- Cross-sell opportunities

### Case Study: Stripe/Bridge Stablecoin (2026)

**Partnership Scope:**
- 100+ country expansion
- Stablecoin-linked cards
- On-chain settlement

**Innovation:**
- First major stablecoin card program at scale
- Solana blockchain integration
- Bridge to crypto-native users
