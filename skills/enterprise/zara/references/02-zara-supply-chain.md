# Zara Supply Chain & Logistics Reference

**The Vertically Integrated Fast Fashion Machine**

## Supply Chain Philosophy

### Core Principles

1. **Speed Over Cost** - Accept 15-20% higher manufacturing costs for 2-week lead times
2. **Control Over Scale** - Own critical value chain activities
3. **Proximity Over Distance** - 50-60% production near European markets
4. **Responsiveness Over Prediction** - Small batches, test-and-replicate model

### The Zara Advantage

| Factor | Traditional Fashion | Zara Model |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| Design-to-shelf | 6-12 months | 2-5 weeks |
| Inventory turns | 3-4x/year | 10-12x/year |
| Markdown rate | 30-40% | 10-15% |
| Production proximity | 10-15% near market | 50-60% near market |
| Batch size | Large (economies) | Small (scarcity) |

### Design & Product Development (Arteixo)

**The Creative Hub:**
- **Location:** Arteixo, A CoruГұa, Galicia, Spain
- **Designers:** 200+ professionals organized by product category
- **Structure:**
  - Women's wear (largest team)
  - Men's wear
  - Children's wear (Zara Kids)
  - Accessories
  - Zara Home

**Design Process:**

| Stage | Timeline | Activities |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Trend Analysis | Ongoing | Runway, street, social media, store feedback |
| Concept Development | 1-2 days | Sketching, fabric selection from inventory |
| Pattern Making | 1-2 days | Digital patterns, sample creation |
| Sample Production | 2-3 days | In-house at Arteixo workshops |
| Commercial Review | 1 day | Pricing, margin analysis, assortment planning |
| **Total Concept-to-Approval** | **5-10 days** | - |

**Key Design Principles:**
- Design with available fabrics (undyed inventory ready for quick dyeing)
- Real-time store feedback directly influences design
- No individual designers credited (team-based approach)
- "Copy, don't invent" - Interpret trends, don't create avant-garde

## Manufacturing Strategy

### The CMT Model

**Cut-Make-Trim:**
- Zara provides: Design, patterns, fabric
- Supplier provides: Labor, equipment, facility
- Quality control: Zara inspectors on-site

### Geographic Production Mix

| Region | % of Production | Characteristics |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Spain** | 20-25% | High-fashion items, complex designs |
| **Portugal** | 15-20% | Quality basics, reliable delivery |
| **Morocco** | 15-20% | Cost-competitive, proximity advantage |
| **Turkey** | 5-10% | Denim, woven fabrics |
| **Asia** | 30-35% | Basic items, larger volumes, lower cost |

### Production Timing

| Product Type | Sourcing Region | Lead Time | Volume |
|--------------|-----------------|-----------|--------|
| Fashion/trendy | Spain/Portugal/Morocco | 2-3 weeks | Small batches |
| Seasonal basics | Turkey/Asia | 4-6 weeks | Medium batches |
| Core basics | Asia | 8-12 weeks | Large batches |

### Factory Network

**Company-Owned Facilities:**
- Zara operates owned factories in Spain for most complex items
- Proprietary dyeing and finishing capabilities
- Technology labs for fabric innovation

**Partner Network:**
- 1,800+ suppliers globally
- 43 countries
- Long-term relationships with key partners
- Exclusive arrangements for capacity guarantee

## Store Replenishment Model

### The Twice-Weekly Rhythm

**Delivery Schedule:**
- **Tuesday deliveries:** Mid-week refresh
- **Thursday deliveries:** Weekend preparation

**Store Operations:**
- Staff receive and merchandise new products immediately
- Visual merchandising updated 2x/week
- Mannequins refreshed with new arrivals
- Windows changed frequently (sometimes weekly)

### Inventory Management

**Scarcity Strategy:**
- Limited quantities per style
- No replenishment of sold-out items (usually)
- Creates urgency: "Buy now or miss out"
- Reduces end-of-season markdowns

**Store Segmentation:**
- Flagship stores: Full collection (2,000+ SKUs)
- Standard stores: Curated selection (1,000-1,500 SKUs)
- Small stores: Core items only (500-800 SKUs)

### Real-Time Inventory System

**Technology Stack:**
- RFID tags on all garments
- Real-time POS integration
- Automated reorder suggestions
- "Store Mode" app feature: See inventory by location

### Supplier Code of Conduct

**Key Requirements:**
- No child labor
- Safe working conditions
- Fair wages (living wage commitment)
- Environmental compliance
- Transparency and traceability

### Audit Program

|------------|-----------|-------|
| Social audits | Annual | Labor practices, working conditions |
| Environmental audits | Annual | Water, energy, chemical management |
| Quality audits | Quarterly | Product specifications |
| Traceability audits | Ongoing | Supply chain mapping |

### Strategic Suppliers

**Long-Term Partnerships:**
- Multi-year contracts for capacity guarantee
- Joint investment in technology/automation
- Shared sustainability goals
- Co-location near Inditex facilities where possible
