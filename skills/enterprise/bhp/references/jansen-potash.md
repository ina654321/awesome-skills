# Jansen Potash Project

## Overview

The Jansen Potash Project represents BHP's strategic entry into potash—a new commodity aligned with global food security and sustainable agriculture. Located in Saskatchewan, Canada, it's one of the world's largest undeveloped potash resources.

## Why Potash?

### Strategic Rationale
1. **Food Security**: Essential fertilizer for crop yields and sustainable farming
2. **Commodity Diversification**: Reduces reliance on iron ore and copper cycles
3. **Growth Option**: Positions BHP for population-driven demand growth
4. **ESG Alignment**: Contributes to UN Sustainable Development Goals

### Market Context
- Global potash demand grows with population and protein consumption
- Concentrated supply (few major producers)
- Saskatchewan offers stable jurisdiction with established potash industry

## Project Details

### Location
- **Province**: Saskatchewan, Canada
- **Region**: ~140 km east of Saskatoon
- **Geology**: World's best potash deposit—thick, flat-lying, high-grade

### Resource
- High-grade sylvinite ore
- Multi-decade mine life potential
- Favorable mining conditions (room and pillar)

## Stage 1

### Status
- **Construction Progress**: 63%+ complete (as of FY2025)
- **Investment**: US$5.7 billion
- **Capacity**: 4.4 million tonnes per annum (Mtpa)
- **First Production**: Late calendar year 2026
- **Ramp-up**: 2027-2028 to full capacity
