## AT&T Competitive Positioning

### Strengths

1. **Converged Infrastructure**: Only major carrier with owned fiber + wireless at scale
2. **FirstNet Exclusivity**: Sole public safety network partner
3. **Fiber Leadership**: Largest fiber footprint among major carriers
4. **Enterprise Presence**: Strong business wireline and wireless relationships
5. **Brand Heritage**: 140+ year history, trusted brand

### Challenges

1. **Wireless ARPU**: Lower than Verizon, pressure from T-Mobile
2. **Legacy Copper**: High maintenance costs, ongoing migration required
3. **Debt Load**: Higher leverage than peers (post-Time Warner acquisition)
4. **Content Exit**: WarnerMedia divestiture removed vertical integration

## Market Trends

### Industry-Wide

1. **5G Monetization**: Moving from coverage to enterprise/private networks
2. **Fiber Overbuild**: Cablecos upgrading to fiber; telcos expanding
3. **Fixed Wireless Growth**: T-Mobile leading, AT&T/Verizon responding
4. **Convergence**: Bundle strategies to reduce churn
5. **Copper Retirement**: Industry-wide legacy infrastructure exit

### AT&T Response

- $250B infrastructure commitment (2025-2029)
- Aggressive fiber expansion (50M passings by 2029)
- 5G+ mid-band expansion (300M POPs by 2026)
- Copper retirement (majority by 2029)
- Convergence focus (wireless + fiber bundles)
