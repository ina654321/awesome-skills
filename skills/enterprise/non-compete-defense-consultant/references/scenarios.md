# Scenario Examples

## Layer 2: Strategic Options

```
OPTION A: COMPLIANCE (Conservative)
Approach: Honor agreement, find non-competing work
Best for: Short restriction period, high enforcement risk, risk-averse client
Pros: Zero litigation risk, clean professional record
Cons: Career disruption, skill atrophy, income loss
Timeline: Duration of竞业 period

OPTION B: NEGOTIATED RELEASE (Preferred)
Approach: Negotiate waiver or buyout with employer
Best for: Employer willing to deal, client has leverage or resources
Pros: Clean break, defined cost, preserves relationship
Cons: Requires payment or concessions
Timeline: 1-4 weeks typical

OPTION C: MODIFIED COMPLIANCE (Creative)
Approach: Structure new role to technically comply
Best for: Ambiguous scope, creative structuring possible
Pros: Take desired job with reduced risk
Cons: Gray area, some risk remains, may limit role
Examples: Different geographic market, non-competing product line, advisory vs operational
Timeline: Immediate with proper structuring

OPTION D: LEGAL CHALLENGE (Aggressive)
Approach: Preemptive lawsuit to invalidate agreement
Best for: Clearly invalid agreement, strong case, client willing to litigate
Pros: Permanent freedom, potential damages
Cons: Expensive, time-consuming, uncertain outcome
Timeline: 6-18 months

OPTION E: DEFEND IF SUED (Reactive)
Approach: Proceed with new job, defend if employer sues
Best for: Low enforcement likelihood, client risk-tolerant
Pros: Take desired job immediately
Cons: Litigation stress, potential damages if lose
Timeline: Immediate, but with litigation risk
```

## China Legal Framework

```
VALIDITY REQUIREMENTS:
1. ELIGIBLE EMPLOYEES ONLY
   - Senior management (高管)
   - Senior technical personnel (高级技术人员)
   - Others with confidentiality obligations (其他负有保密义务的人员)
   - ❌ Ordinary employees cannot be bound

2. COMPENSATION DURING RESTRICTION PERIOD
   - Minimum: 30% of average monthly wage for last 12 months
   - Must be paid monthly during竞业 period
   - If not paid for 3 months, employee can request termination

3. REASONABLE SCOPE
   - Geographic: Limited to actual business area
   - Temporal: Maximum 2 years (often less)
   - Industry: Limited to actual competitive business

4. LEGITIMATE BUSINESS INTEREST
   - Trade secrets
   - Customer relationships
   - Technical know-how
   - ❌ Cannot protect general skills or knowledge

INVALIDITY GROUNDS:
□ Employee doesn't qualify (not senior/technical/confidential)
□ No compensation or below 30%
□ Scope unreasonably broad
□ Duration excessive
□ No legitimate business interest
□ Signed under duress
□ Unclear or contradictory terms
```

## Negotiation Framework

```
PREPARATION:
- Assessment of employer's litigation likelihood
- Determination of client's BATNA (Best Alternative)
- Identification of employer's interests beyond litigation
- Calculation of reasonable buyout range

OPENING:
- Frame as mutual benefit: "Let's find a solution that works for both"
- Acknowledge employer's legitimate interests
- Present alternatives (not just "let me out")

PROPOSAL OPTIONS:
1. Full Release
   - Ask: Complete waiver of竞业限制
   - Offer: Lump sum payment, extended notice, consulting help
   - Rationale: Cost of enforcement > buyout price

2. Modified Scope
   - Ask: Narrow geographic or industry scope
   - Offer: Acceptance of core restrictions
   - Rationale: Addresses main concerns while allowing some mobility

3. Accelerated Timeline
   - Ask: Reduce duration (e.g., 12 months to 6)
   - Offer: Faster transition help, knowledge transfer
   - Rationale: Shorter pain for employer, faster freedom for employee

4. Mutual Non-Disparagement
   - Ask: Release in exchange for confidentiality about departure
   - Offer: Positive reference, smooth transition
   - Rationale: Protects employer reputation

CLOSING:
- Written agreement essential
- Clear scope of release
- Confidentiality of deal terms
- No admission of wrongdoing
```
