# Roche Company History

## Foundation and Early Years (1896-1950)

### 1896 — The Beginning
- **October 1, 1896**: Fritz Hoffmann-La Roche founds F. Hoffmann-La Roche & Co. in Basel, Switzerland
- **Vision**: Industrialize medicine production for standardized, high-quality pharmaceuticals
- **Initial Capital**: Family funding from Hoffmann's father (silk merchant)
- **First Products**: 
  - Airol (wound disinfectant)
  - Sirolin (cough syrup)
  - Theelin

### 1910s-1920s — International Expansion
- **1919**: First subsidiary outside Switzerland (Italy)
- **1920s**: Expansion to UK, Germany, France
- **1926**: Entry into US market
- **1931**: First overseas subsidiary in Vienna, Austria

### 1930s — Vitamin Pioneer
- **1934**: Launch of **Redoxon** — world's first synthetic vitamin C
- Industrial-scale synthesis of vitamins revolutionizes nutrition
- Establishes Roche as innovation leader in chemical synthesis

## The Pharmaceutical Revolution (1950-1990)

### 1950s-1960s — Psychopharmacology
- **1958**: Launch of **Librium** — first benzodiazepine
- **1963**: Launch of **Valium** (diazepam)
  - Becomes world's best-selling pharmaceutical in the 1970s
  - Icon of "mother's little helper" era
  - Peak sales exceeded $1 billion annually

### 1970s-1980s — Diversification and Crisis
- **1976**: **Seveso disaster** — toxic dioxin release at Italian subsidiary
  - Major environmental and reputational crisis
  - Led to improved safety standards industry-wide
  
- **1978**: Breakthrough in genetically pure human insulin
  - Collaboration with subsidiary Genentech
  - Entry into biotechnology

### 1990 — The Genentech Acquisition
- **March 1990**: Roche acquires 56% stake in **Genentech** for $2.1 billion
- **Significance**: 
  - First major pharma-biotech merger
  - Provides access to recombinant DNA technology
  - Pipeline includes future blockbusters Herceptin and Rituxan
- Genentech maintains operational independence as US subsidiary

## The Biotechnology Era (1990-2010)

### 1990s — Building the Portfolio
- **1990**: Inhibace (antihypertensive) — first Roche product designed via computer modeling
- **1994**: Acquisition of Syntex Corporation (becomes Roche Bioscience)
- **1997**: Launch of **MabThera/Rituxan** — first monoclonal antibody for cancer
- **1997**: Zenapex (immunosuppressant)
- **1998**: Acquisition of Boehringer Mannheim and Corange Group
  - Creates world-leading Diagnostics division

### 1998 — Herceptin Revolution
- **September 1998**: FDA approval of **Herceptin** (trastuzumab)
- **Significance**:
  - First targeted cancer therapy (HER2+ breast cancer)
  - Establishes Roche as oncology leader
  - Validates biotech approach to drug development
- Launches era of personalized medicine in oncology

### 2004 — Avastin
- **February 2004**: FDA approval of **Avastin** (bevacizumab)
- First anti-angiogenic therapy for cancer
- Peak sales exceed $7 billion

### 2009 — Full Genentech Integration
- **March 2009**: Roche acquires remaining Genentech shares for **$46.8 billion**
- **Total Genentech investment**: ~$49 billion
- **Integration approach**:
  - Genentech becomes Roche's US pharmaceutical operations
  - South San Francisco campus becomes gRED (Genentech R&D)
  - Maintains Genentech brand in US market
  - Combines Roche's scale with Genentech's innovation culture

### 2000s — Diagnostics Leadership
- **2001**: Launch of **cobas 6000** analyzer series
- Revolutionizes laboratory diagnostics with full automation
- Establishes market leadership position

## The Modern Era (2010-Present)

### 2010s — Portfolio Diversification
- **2012**: Launch of **Perjeta** — advances HER2+ breast cancer treatment
- **2013**: Launch of **Kadcyla** — first ADC for breast cancer
- **2014**: Acquisition of **InterMune** for $8.3 billion
  - Adds Esbriet for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis
- **2017**: Broadens portfolio beyond oncology
  - Focus on neuroscience and immunology

### 2018 — Data & Genomics Acquisitions
- **February 2018**: Acquisition of **Flatiron Health** for $1.9 billion
  - Real-world evidence platform for oncology
  - Electronic health records from 280+ cancer clinics
  
- **June 2018**: Acquisition of **Foundation Medicine** for $2.4 billion
  - Comprehensive genomic profiling
  - Companion diagnostic development
  - Positions Roche at center of precision medicine

### 2019 — Gene Therapy Entry
- **December 2019**: Acquisition of **Spark Therapeutics** for $4.8 billion
  - Gene therapy pioneer (Luxturna)
  - AAV vector expertise
  - Hemophilia A gene therapy (SPK-8011)

### 2020s — COVID-19 Response
- **March 2020**: Launch of cobas SARS-CoV-2 test
- **2020-2021**: Comprehensive COVID-19 portfolio
  - Diagnostic tests (PCR, antigen, antibody)
  - Actemra/RoActemra for COVID-19 cytokine storm
  - Peak COVID-related sales: >CHF 10 billion

## Corporate Culture Evolution

### Swiss Heritage
- Basel headquarters since 1896
- Long-term orientation (38 years dividend growth)
- Scientific excellence and quality focus
- Multinational from inception

### Genentech Influence (Post-2009)
- "Patient-first" culture from South San Francisco
- Biotech innovation mindset
- Scientific risk-taking
- "Doing now what patients need next" mission

### Current Culture
- Blend of Swiss precision and biotech agility
- Dual identity: Pharma + Diagnostics
- Personalized healthcare mission
- Innovation through internal + external R&D
