## Strategic Competitive Advantages

**Unique to Roche:**
- Only company with world-leading positions in both
- Companion diagnostics development alongside drugs
- NAVIFY platform for integrated clinical decision support
- Real-world data from Flatiron + Foundation Medicine

**Competitive Impact:**
- Higher probability of successful drug launches
- Better patient identification and outcomes
- Differentiated value proposition to payers
- Higher barriers to entry for competitors

## Key Competitive Threats

### 1. Keytruda Dominance

**Challenge**: Merck's Keytruda is the world's best-selling drug ($25B+ sales)
- Dominates 1L NSCLC
- Expanding into adjuvant settings
- Strong data in combination trials

**Roche Response:**
- Focus on Tecentriq strengths (SCLC, HCC, combinations)
- Tiragolumab (anti-TIGIT) Phase III readout 2025
- Companion diagnostics integration
- Cost-effective positioning

### 2. Biosimilar Erosion

**Challenge**: Herceptin, Avastin, Rituxan face biosimilar competition
- $5B+ annual sales at risk
- Price erosion 20-50%
- International markets most affected

**Roche Response:**
- Phesgo lifecycle innovation (+62% growth)
- Subcutaneous formulations
- Focus on combinations
- New products offsetting LOE

### 3. China Pricing Pressure

**Challenge**: Volume-Based Procurement impacting Diagnostics
- 50%+ price cuts on many tests
- Expansion into IVD from pharma
- Local competition increasing

**Roche Response:**
- Focus on high medical value tests
- NAVIFY digital solutions
- Companion diagnostic pull-through
- Selective portfolio optimization

### 4. Gene Therapy Competition

**Challenge**: One-time curative therapies threaten chronic treatments
- Hemophilia gene therapies (BioMarin, etc.)
- Potential impact on Hemlibra long-term
- High upfront costs vs. chronic therapy

**Roche Response:**
- Spark Therapeutics gene therapy portfolio
- Next-gen Hemlibra (NXT007) development
- Evaluate gene therapy partnerships

### 5. Obesity Market Entry

**Challenge**: GLP-1 agonists (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) transforming healthcare
- Roche has limited obesity presence
- Large market opportunity being captured by Novo Nordisk, Lilly
- Potential impact on diabetes, cardiovascular portfolios

**Roche Response:**
- Carmot acquisition (CT-388, CT-996 GLP-1 assets)
- Zealand Pharma partnership (petrelintide)
- Focus on differentiated mechanisms

## Strategic Outlook

**Competitive Advantages to Maintain:**
1. Diagnostics-pharma integration
2. Biologics manufacturing excellence
3. Oncology leadership
4. MS franchise dominance
5. R&D productivity

**Areas for Improvement:**
1. 1L NSCLC immuno-oncology position
2. Obesity market entry
3. China market dynamics
4. Gene therapy competitiveness
5. Cost structure vs. biosimilar competition

**Long-term Positioning:**
Roche is well-positioned to remain a top-3 global pharmaceutical company through its unique integrated model, strong pipeline, and financial capacity. The key risks are competitive pressure in oncology from Merck/Keytruda and the need to successfully enter high-growth markets (obesity, gene therapy).
