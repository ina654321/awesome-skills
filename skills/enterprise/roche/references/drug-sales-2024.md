## Key Product Details

### Growth Drivers (>15% growth)

#### Vabysmo (faricimab) — CHF 3.86B (+68%)
- **Mechanism**: Bispecific antibody (anti-VEGF + anti-Ang2)
- **Indications**: Neovascular AMD, diabetic macular edema (DME), retinal vein occlusion
- **Competitive Advantage**: First bispecific in ophthalmology; longer durability vs. Eylea
- **Administration**: Every 8-16 weeks (vs. monthly for competitors)
- **Markets**: Strong US uptake, expanding EU presence
- **Peak Sales Potential**: CHF 5B+

#### Phesgo — CHF 1.70B (+62%)
- **Mechanism**: Fixed-dose combination of Perjeta + Herceptin
- **Administration**: Subcutaneous injection (vs. IV infusion)
- **Administration Time**: ~8 minutes (vs. 60-150 minutes IV)
- **Strategic Importance**: Lifecycle management for Herceptin/Perjeta franchise
- **Target**: Convert remaining IV patients to subcu

#### Xolair — CHF 2.45B (+16%)
- **Mechanism**: Anti-IgE antibody
- **Indications**: Allergic asthma, chronic idiopathic urticaria
- **New Opportunity**: Food allergy (pediatric) approval expanding market
- **Partnership**: Co-promoted with Novartis

#### Polivy — CHF 1.11B (+39%)
- **Mechanism**: CD79b-targeting ADC
- **Indication**: Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL)
- **Positioning**: 1L DLBCL standard of care (with R-CHP)
- **Growth Driver**: Global uptake in 1L setting

### Stable Performers

#### Ocrevus (ocrelizumab) — CHF 6.72B (+9%)
- **Mechanism**: Anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody
- **Indications**: Relapsing MS (RMS), Primary Progressive MS (PPMS)
- **Market Position**: #1 MS therapy globally
- **Formulations**: IV (600mg Q6mo), Subcu Zunovo (Q6mo, 10-min injection)
- **Competition**: Kesimpta (Novartis), BTK inhibitors in development
- **Patent Protection**: Through late 2020s

#### Hemlibra (emicizumab) — CHF 4.52B (+12%)
- **Mechanism**: Bispecific antibody mimicking Factor VIII function
- **Indication**: Hemophilia A (with/without inhibitors)
- **Administration**: Weekly, biweekly, or monthly subcu
- **Competitive Advantage**: Eliminates need for factor VIII infusions
- **Market Position**: Standard of care for inhibitor patients; growing in non-inhibitor
- **Next-Gen**: NXT007 in development

#### Tecentriq (atezolizumab) — CHF 3.60B (0%)
- **Mechanism**: PD-L1 inhibitor
- **Indications**: NSCLC, SCLC, HCC, bladder cancer, breast cancer (TNBC)
- **Challenge**: Keytruda dominance in 1L NSCLC
- **Differentiation**: Strong in SCLC (IMpower133), HCC (IMbrave150)
- **Pipeline**: Tiragolumab combinations for PD-L1-high NSCLC

## New Product Launches (2024-2025)

### Itovebi (inavolisib)
- **Launch**: Q3 2024 (US), Q3 2025 (EU)
- **Indication**: PIK3CA-mutant HR+ metastatic breast cancer
- **Peak Sales Potential**: CHF 2B+
- **Competitive Context**: Competes with Novartis Piqray; differentiated profile

### Ocrevus Zunovo
- **Launch**: Q4 2024 (US)
- **Differentiation**: Subcutaneous 10-minute injection vs. IV infusion
- **Target**: 50% of new US starts are Ocrevus-naïve (market expansion)

### Susvimo (ranibizumab port)
- **Launch**: 2025
- **Indication**: Diabetic retinopathy
- **Technology**: Port delivery system with 6-month refill
- **Competition**: Competes with Eylea, Vabysmo
