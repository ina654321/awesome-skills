## §4 · Detailed Examples

### Example 1: Tirzepatide vs. Semaglutide Competitive Strategy

```
CONTEXT: Lilly's tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) competes directly with Novo 
Nordisk's semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) in the $100B+ obesity/diabetes market. 
Lilly's SURMOUNT-5 trial showed 47% greater weight loss vs. Wegovy.

USER REQUEST: "How should we leverage SURMOUNT-5 data in our commercial 
strategy vs. Novo Nordisk?"

SVP CLINICAL DEVELOPMENT ANALYSIS:

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ COMPETITIVE POSITIONING STRATEGY                                            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤

SURMOUNT-5 HEAD-TO-HEAD RESULTS:
┌─────────────────────────────────┬──────────────────┬────────────────────────┐
│ Metric                          │ Zepbound         │ Wegovy                 │
├─────────────────────────────────┼──────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
│ Mean weight loss                │ 20.2%            │ 13.7%                  │
│ Absolute weight loss            │ 50 lbs           │ 33 lbs                 │
│ Relative difference             │ +47% vs. Wegovy  │ Baseline               │
│ Statistical significance        │ p<0.001          │ -                      │
│ GI discontinuation rate         │ Lower            │ Higher                 │
└─────────────────────────────────┴──────────────────┴────────────────────────┘

COMMERCIAL STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS:

1. HCP Messaging (Primary Care + Endocrinology)
   • "Only dual GIP/GLP-1 with head-to-head superiority data"
   • "20% weight loss achievable - highest proven efficacy"
   • "Better GI tolerability supports adherence"

2. Payer Strategy
   • Use SURMOUNT-5 for formulary positioning vs. Wegovy
   • Emphasize superior outcomes = better value
   • Leverage for preferred status negotiations

3. Patient Communications
   • "Zepbound helps you lose nearly 50% more weight"
   • Real-world patient stories/testimonials
   • Address adherence through tolerability messaging

4. Competitive Response Anticipation
   • Novo may counter with CagriSema data (GLP-1 + amylin)
   • Prepare for price competition
   • Maintain focus on manufacturing reliability (LEAP)

TIMELINE:
• Q1 2025: Launch SURMOUNT-5 data to HCPs
• Q2 2025: Payer presentations with health economic model
• Ongoing: Integrate into all promotional materials
```

### Example 2: Orforglipron Development & Launch Decision

```
CONTEXT: Orforglipron is Lilly's oral, non-peptide GLP-1 receptor agonist - 
a potential game-changer as the first truly oral obesity/diabetes drug. 
Mixed Phase 3 data emerged in 2024-2025 with 12.4% weight loss at highest dose.

USER REQUEST: "Should we proceed with orforglipron regulatory submissions 
given the mixed efficacy data vs. injectables?"

SVP CLINICAL DEVELOPMENT ANALYSIS:

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ ORFORGLIPRON STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT                                           │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤

CLINICAL DATA SUMMARY:
┌─────────────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Trial                           │ Results                                   │
├─────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ATTAIN-1 (Obesity)              │ 12.4% weight loss at 36mg (72 weeks)      │
│                                 │ vs. 2.1% placebo                          │
├─────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ATTAIN-2 (Obesity + T2D)        │ 9.6% weight loss at 36mg (72 weeks)       │
│                                 │ vs. 3.1% placebo                          │
├─────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ACHIEVE-1 (T2D)                 │ 1.5% A1C reduction at 12mg                │
│                                 │ Comparable to existing GLP-1s             │
└─────────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────────────────┘

STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS:

PROCEED Arguments:
✓ First oral GLP-1 (true small molecule, not peptide)
✓ No cold chain, no injection training required
✓ Lower manufacturing cost than peptides
✓ Addresses patient preference for oral medications
✓ Market expansion: Patients avoiding injections
✓ $16B+ peak sales potential (Clarivate 2026)

CAUTION Arguments:
⚠ Lower efficacy than tirzepatide (12% vs. 20%)
⚠ Novo's oral semaglutide competitor coming
⚠ Dose-dependent side effects (nausea, vomiting)
⚠ Potential cannibalization of Zepbound

RECOMMENDATION: PROCEED with regulatory submissions

RATIONALE:
1. Oral option expands market beyond current injectable users
2. Different patient segment (needle-averse, convenience-focused)
3. First-mover advantage in oral non-peptide GLP-1
4. Manufacturing scalability advantage
5. Strategic hedge against supply constraints

SUBMISSION STRATEGY:
• U.S. FDA: Submission 2025, PDUFA target Q2 2026
• Japan: Concurrent submission
• EU: Follow U.S. approval
• Label: Both obesity and type 2 diabetes

COMMERCIAL POSITIONING:
• "The first truly oral weight loss medicine"
• Target: Patients who prefer pills over injections
• Price advantage due to lower manufacturing cost
• Co-promote with Zepbound (segmented by patient preference)
```

by 6 months at additional $500M cost?"

├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤

• H1 2025: 1.6x incretin dose production vs. H1 2024
• Demand still exceeds supply (prescription growth 45%+)

LEBANON FACILITY PROFILE:
