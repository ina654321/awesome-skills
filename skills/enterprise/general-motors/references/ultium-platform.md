# Ultium Platform Technical Reference

## Overview

Ultium is General Motors' dedicated battery electric vehicle (BEV) platform, announced in March 2020 and first deployed in 2022 with the GMC Hummer EV.

## Technical Architecture

### Battery Chemistry

**NCMA (Nickel-Cobalt-Manganese-Aluminum)**
- Energy density: ~90-100 Wh/kg at pack level
- Target cost: $70/kWh (industry competitive threshold)
- Thermal management: Liquid cooling/heating system

**Future Chemistry Evolution**
- **LMR (Lithium Manganese-Rich)**: Lower cost chemistry targeted for 2026-2027
- Solid-state evaluation: Partnership with Solid Power for next-gen batteries

### Modular Design

| Configuration | Use Case | Example Vehicles |
|--------------|----------|------------------|
| 12 modules | Entry EV, compact | Equinox EV (FWD) |
| 16 modules | Mid-range SUV | Blazer EV, Lyriq |
| 20 modules | Performance/extended range | Silverado EV, Hummer EV |
| 24 modules | Maximum range/commercial | Potential future HD trucks |

### Electric Drive Units

**Primary Motors:**
- Front-wheel drive: 180-255 kW
- Rear-wheel drive: 255-370 kW
- Performance dual-motor: 560+ kW combined

**Efficiency:**
- Target: 4+ miles/kWh for compact EVs
- Real-world: Equinox EV ~3.5 mi/kWh, Lyriq ~3.2 mi/kWh

## Platform Partnerships

### Honda/Acura
- **Prologue**: Mid-size SUV (Blazer EV architecture)
- **ZDX**: Performance SUV (Lyriq architecture)
- **Terms**: GM supplies platform, battery; Honda provides body, interior, tuning
- **Volume**: 50,000+ units annually (combined)

### Vertical Integration Strategy
- Cell manufacturing: JV with LG Energy Solution
- Raw materials: Offtake agreements with Glencore, LG Chem
- Battery pack assembly: In-house at vehicle plants
- Software: Ultifi platform (GM proprietary)

## Challenges & Strategic Response

### Current Issues (2025)
1. **Production ramp slower than planned**: Target 400K EVs by 2025; actual ~100K
2. **Cost competitiveness**: Tesla's manufacturing cost advantage remains
3. **Consumer demand**: EV adoption curve flatter than projected

### Strategic Adjustments
- **Hybrid reintroduction**: PHEV options for regulatory compliance
- **ICE pivot**: Orion Assembly retooling (EV→ICE) to match demand
- **Cost reduction**: LMR battery chemistry, manufacturing efficiency
- **Focus on profitable segments**: Full-size trucks, luxury SUVs first
