# Broadcom AI Business Strategy

## Executive Summary

Broadcom has emerged as a leading provider of AI infrastructure, generating **$12.2B in AI revenue in FY2024** (+220% YoY). The company serves the custom silicon and networking needs of hyperscale AI deployments, positioning itself as an essential infrastructure partner for the AI revolution.

## Business Model

### Custom AI Accelerators (XPUs)

**Engagement Structure:**
- Multi-year co-development agreements
- $100M-300M development investment per generation
- Volume commitments from customers
- Revenue scales with hyperscaler deployment

**Customer Economics:**
- Hyperscalers spend $5B-$20B annually on AI infrastructure
- Custom silicon offers 2-3x performance/watt improvement
- TCO advantage at scale justifies development investment
- Reduces dependency on merchant silicon suppliers

**Broadcom Economics:**
- Breakeven at $500M+ annual revenue per customer
- Target 60%+ gross margins at scale
- 25%+ IRR over 5-year engagement
- IP reuse across customers improves returns

## Competitive Landscape

### Broadcom vs. NVIDIA

| Dimension | Broadcom | NVIDIA |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Approach** | Custom ASICs | Merchant GPUs |
| **Customers** | Hyperscalers (direct) | Broad market + cloud |
| **Software** | Customer-managed | CUDA ecosystem |
| **Flexibility** | Workload-optimized | General-purpose |
| **Performance** | 2-3x for specific workloads | Consistent across workloads |
| **TCO** | Lower at hyperscale | Higher but flexible |
| **Time-to-market** | 2-3 years | Immediate availability |

**Coexistence Thesis:**
- NVIDIA dominates training and general-purpose inference
- Broadcom captures custom inference at hyperscale
- Market large enough for multiple winners
- Different value propositions for different needs

### Competitive Moats

**Broadcom Advantages:**
1. **Integration Expertise** - 10+ years custom silicon experience
2. **Hyperscaler Relationships** - Trusted partner status
3. **Networking Synergies** - Complete AI cluster solutions
4. **Financial Scale** - Can invest $100M+ per design
5. **IP Portfolio** - Reusable across customers

**Competitive Threats:**
- Marvell (custom silicon competitor)
- AMD (growing AI portfolio)
- Intel (Gaudi accelerators)
- In-house silicon (Google TPU team, etc.)

## Strategic Priorities

### FY2025-2027 Objectives

1. **Scale Existing Engagements**
   - Ramp next-gen designs for Google, Meta
   - Expand ByteDance deployment
   - Achieve $20B+ AI revenue by FY2026

2. **Add New Customers**
   - Close Apple engagement
   - Pursue OpenAI and others
   - Target 5+ major hyperscaler relationships

3. **Expand Networking Share**
   - Capture 800G/1.6T upgrade cycle
   - Maintain Ethernet switching leadership
   - Integrate networking with XPU offerings

4. **Maintain Technology Leadership**
   - Invest in 2nm and beyond
   - Develop advanced packaging
   - Build optical interconnect capabilities

### Risk Factors

**Customer Concentration:**
- Top 3 customers = majority of AI revenue
- Loss of one = material financial impact
- Mitigation: Diversify customer base

**Competition:**
- NVIDIA's CUDA ecosystem stickiness
- Marvell competitive pressure
- In-house silicon development

**Technology Disruption:**
- New architectures (neuromorphic, photonic)
- Process node limitations
- Memory bandwidth bottlenecks

**Execution Risk:**
- Complex chip development cycles
- Yield challenges at leading edge
- Supply chain dependencies (TSMC)
