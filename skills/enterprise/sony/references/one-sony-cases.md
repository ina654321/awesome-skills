# One Sony: Historical Synergy Case Studies

## Success Stories

### Case 1: Spider-Man — The Ultimate Cross-Divisional Franchise

**Timeline**: 2018-Present

**Overview**: Spider-Man represents the perfect One Sony execution—originating in Pictures (film rights), expanding to G&NS (PlayStation games), and amplified through Music (soundtracks), ET&S (merchandise), and marketing integration.

**Divisional Contributions**:

| Division | Contribution | Result |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| **Pictures** | Spider-Man: No Way Home ($1.9B box office) | Highest-grossing film of 2021 |
| **G&NS** | Marvel's Spider-Man games (Insomniac) | 30M+ units sold; 90+ Metacritic |
| **Music** | Soundtracks, score albums | Grammy nominations; streaming hits |
| **ET&S** | Bravia "perfect for PS5" marketing; headphones | Hardware attach sales |
| **Marketing** | Coordinated global campaigns | Unified brand presence |

**Key Success Factors**:
1. **Creative alignment**: All divisions understood the character's core appeal
2. **Timeline coordination**: Game and film releases strategically spaced
3. **Shared technology**: Game engine improvements benefited both development and marketing
4. **Executive sponsorship**: High-level coordination ensured resource allocation

**Lessons Learned**:
- ✓ Start with creative excellence in one division, then expand
- ✓ Respect each division's expertise while maintaining quality standards
- ✓ Plan for 5-10 year franchise lifecycle, not one-off projects

### Case 2: Demon Slayer — Anime-to-Multimedia Phenomenon

**Timeline**: 2019-Present

**Overview**: Originally a manga, Demon Slayer became a One Sony showcase through Aniplex (production/music), Crunchyroll (distribution), and coordinated consumer products.

**Value Chain**:
```
Manga (Shueisha) →
  Anime Production (Aniplex/Ufotable) →
    Music/Soundtrack (SME) →
      Global Streaming (Crunchyroll) →
        Theatrical Distribution (Crunchyroll/SPE) →
          Consumer Products (licensing) →
            Game Collaborations (G&NS)
```

**Financial Impact**:
| Revenue Stream | Estimated Contribution |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Anime production/distribution | $200M+ |
| Music streaming/albums | $50M+ |
| Theatrical (Mugen Train) | $500M worldwide |
| Consumer products | $1B+ (industry estimates) |
| Game collaborations | $30M+ licensing |

**Key Success Factors**:
1. **Vertical integration**: Aniplex controlled production quality
2. **Global distribution**: Crunchyroll provided worldwide reach
3. **Quality obsession**: Ufotable animation set new standards
4. **Cultural timing**: Pandemic-era comfort viewing

**Lessons Learned**:
- ✓ Quality over speed: Production delays to maintain standards paid off
- ✓ Theatrical anime works: Mugen Train proved viability
- ✓ Consumer products require early planning

### Case 3: The Last of Us — Transmedia Excellence

**Timeline**: 2013-Present

**Overview**: Originally a PlayStation exclusive game, The Last of Us expanded to HBO television series, creating a blueprint for IP 360° expansion.

**Evolution**:

| Year | Milestone | Division | Impact |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| 2013 | Original game release | G&NS | 10M+ sales; 95 Metacritic |
| 2014 | Left Behind DLC | G&NS | Narrative DLC model |
| 2020 | Part II release | G&NS | 10M+ sales; 93 Metacritic |
| 2023 | HBO series (Season 1) | Pictures/HBO | Emmy winner; 30M+ viewers |
| 2025 | HBO Season 2 | Pictures/HBO | Continued acclaim |
| Future | Film adaptation? | Pictures | Under discussion |

**Synergy Mechanisms**:

| Mechanism | Implementation | Result |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| **Production integration** | PlayStation Productions oversight | Authentic adaptation |
| **Music continuity** | Gustavo Santaolalla (both games + series) | Unified emotional tone |
| **Marketing coordination** | Game sales boosted by series | 1M+ additional game units (2023) |
| **Talent sharing** | Neil Druckmann (creative director) involved in series | Creative continuity |

**Key Success Factors**:
1. **Patient development**: 10 years to television; not rushed
2. **Creator involvement**: Original creators had meaningful input
3. **Premium positioning**: HBO partnership signaled quality
4. **Timing**: Post-apocalyptic themes resonated with pandemic era

**Lessons Learned**:
- ✓ Creator involvement is non-negotiable for authenticity
- ✓ Wait for the right partner (HBO) vs. rushing to any studio
- ✓ Game and TV can coexist; one doesn't replace the other

### Case 4: α (Alpha) Camera × Content Creator Ecosystem

**Timeline**: 2018-Present

**Overview**: Sony leveraged its imaging sensor dominance to build a content creator ecosystem connecting ET&S (cameras), I&SS (sensor technology), and entertainment marketing.

**Strategy Components**:

| Element | Description | Division |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Product** | Full-frame mirrorless cameras (α7 III, α7 IV) | ET&S |
| **Technology** | Industry-leading sensors, Eye AF | I&SS |
| **Marketing** | "Shot on Sony" creator campaigns | ET&S + Corporate |
| **Integration** | PlayStation streamer partnerships | G&NS |
| **Education** | Alpha Universe platform; workshops | ET&S |

**Market Impact**:
- Full-frame mirrorless market leadership (by value)
- #1 camera brand among YouTube creators
- α7 III became the benchmark for hybrid photo/video

**Key Success Factors**:
1. **Technology transfer**: I&SS sensors enabled ET&S camera performance
2. **Creator focus**: Designed for content creators, not just photographers
3. **Ecosystem thinking**: Lenses, software, support integrated
4. **Marketing authenticity**: Real creator stories, not celebrity endorsements

### Case 5: PlayStation Productions — Institutionalizing Transmedia

**Timeline**: 2019-Present

**Overview**: Rather than relying on ad-hoc collaborations, Sony created a dedicated unit to manage game-to-film/TV adaptations.

**Structure**:
```
PlayStation Productions
├── Creative Development (game IP evaluation)
├── Production (oversight of adaptations)
├── Studio Partnerships (HBO, Netflix, etc.)
└── Franchise Strategy (long-term IP planning)
```

**Projects**:

| Project | Partner | Status | Result |
|---------|---------|--------|--------|
| Uncharted | Columbia Pictures | Released 2022 | $400M+ box office |
| The Last of Us (TV) | HBO | Released 2023 | Emmy winner; critical acclaim |
| Twisted Metal (TV) | Peacock | Released 2023 | Renewed Season 2 |
| Gran Turismo | Columbia Pictures | Released 2023 | Solid performance |
| Ghost of Tsushima (film) | Columbia Pictures | In development | Director attached |

**Key Success Factors**:
1. **Dedicated team**: Full-time focus vs. side project
2. **Creative control**: PlayStation maintains meaningful involvement
3. **Strategic patience**: Not rushing every IP simultaneously
4. **Partner selection**: Right studio for each project's tone

## Lessons from Failures

### Case: PlayStation Vue

**What**: Live TV streaming service (2015-2020)
**Why it failed**:
- ✗ Underestimated content costs
- ✗ Overcrowded market (YouTube TV, Hulu Live)
- ✗ No unique Sony differentiation
- ✗ Loss of $100M+ per year

**Lesson**: Not every Sony business needs to be "One Sony" connected. Some markets require focused specialists.

### Case: Sony Mobile (Xperia) Contraction

**What**: Smartphone business scale-back
**Why it struggled**:
- ✗ Could not compete with Apple/Samsung scale
- ✗ Late to carrier partnerships in US
- ✗ Camera differentiation not enough

**Pivot**: Focused on niche (photography enthusiasts, Japan market)

**Lesson**: Strategic retreat is sometimes correct. Resources reallocated to I&SS (sensor supply) and entertainment.

## Best Practices for One Sony Initiatives

### Governance
| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|---------------|
| Leadership | Executive sponsor at C-level or above |
| Team | Dedicated cross-divisional working group |
| Metrics | Shared KPIs; individual division incentives aligned |
| Cadence | Monthly steering committee; weekly working sessions |

### Creative Process
| Phase | Activity |
|-------|----------|
| Ideation | Cross-divisional creative workshops |
| Validation | Market research across all target segments |
| Development | Division autonomy with integration checkpoints |
| Launch | Coordinated marketing and distribution |
| Post-launch | Shared analytics; optimization across divisions |

### Cultural Considerations
| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Divisional silos | Rotational assignments; shared goals |
| Different metrics | Create "synergy bonus" compensation |
| Creative disagreements | Executive escalation with Kando as tie-breaker |
| Resource competition | Corporate-level portfolio allocation |
