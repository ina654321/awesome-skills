# Advanced Crisis Negotiation Reference

## Advanced Workflows

### Scenario-Specific Adaptations

#### Hostage Situation Adaptation
1. Lead with safety: establish that no one gets hurt is the #1 priority
2. Identify the subject's primary concern (money, revenge, political statement, mental health crisis)
3. Separate the subject from any hostages with demands vs. subject with personal crisis
4. Use the "no harm" framing: "Help me understand what you need to feel safe enough to let everyone go"
5. Coordinate with law enforcement on timing—don't make promises you can't keep

#### Suicide Intervention Adaptation
1. Direct ask: "Are you thinking about killing yourself right now?"
2. Assess means and plan specificity
3. Focus entirely on survival — defer all other issues
4. Engage a support person or professional before ending the conversation
5. Follow up within 24 hours

#### Business Conflict Adaptation
1. Acknowledge the business impact before personal feelings
2. Identify the underlying interest (money, recognition, control, security)
3. Separate the people from the problem
4. Use objective criteria to evaluate options
5. Document agreements in writing

#### Domestic / Relationship Conflict Adaptation
1. Do not take sides — both parties need to feel heard
2. Focus on interests, not positions
3. Identify shared goals (children, financial stability, peace)
4. Build small agreements first — don't try to solve everything at once
5. Refer to professional mediation for legal/financial separation

## Phase-Gate Decision Trees

### Gate 1: Can you establish communication?
- **Yes** → Continue to Phase 1
- **No** → Seek alternative channels (message, intermediary, trusted person)

### Gate 2: Is the subject de-escalated enough to talk?
- **Yes** → Continue to Phase 2
- **No** → Return to Phase 1 (more mirroring, labeling, FM DJ voice)

### Gate 3: Do you understand the subject's real needs?
- **Yes** → Continue to Phase 3
- **No** → Return to Phase 2 (more calibrated questions, silence)

### Gate 4: Is there a viable path to agreement?
- **Yes** → Continue to Phase 4
- **No** → Reassess: Can the situation be resolved through negotiation? Consider escalation.

### Gate 5: Is there an imminent safety risk?
- **Yes** → Recommend immediate professional intervention. End negotiation support.
- **No** → Continue with current phase

## Simulation Exercises

### Exercise 1: Mirror Response Timing
Practice mirroring the last 3 words within 2 seconds of the subject finishing. Record yourself and review.

### Exercise 2: Labeling Without Agreeing
Practice labeling emotions that are different from your own opinion:
- Subject: "This is the worst company I've ever dealt with!"
- Label: "It sounds like you're really frustrated and feel like you've been let down."
- Note: You didn't agree it's "the worst" — you just named the feeling.

### Exercise 3: The 4-Second Silence
Practice not filling silence. Count to 4 Mississippi before responding after a significant statement.

### Exercise 4: Calibrated Question Cascade
Practice a sequence of 3+ calibrated questions in a row:
- "What happened that led to this situation?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What would an ideal outcome look like for you?"
- "What would need to be true for us to reach that outcome?"

### Exercise 5: Ackerman Bargaining Role Play
One person is the buyer with a $100K limit. Other is seller asking $150K. Practice the Ackerman sequence with exact non-round numbers.
