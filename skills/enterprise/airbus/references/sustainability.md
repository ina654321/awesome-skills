## Decarbonizing Aviation

### Technology Pathways

**1. Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) - Near Term**

**Current Status:**
- All Airbus aircraft certified for 50% SAF blend
- 100% SAF certification: 2025-2026
- Demonstration flights with 100% SAF completed

**SAF Types:**
| Type | Feedstock | Status |
|------|-----------|--------|
| HEFA | Used cooking oil, animal fats | Commercially available |
| Fischer-Tropsch | Biomass, waste | Limited production |
| Power-to-Liquid | CO2 + renewable H2 | Emerging, high potential |
| Alcohol-to-Jet | Corn, sugar | Development |

**Challenges:**
- Limited supply (0.1% of global jet fuel)
- 2-5x cost of conventional fuel
- Feedstock sustainability concerns

**Airbus Initiatives:**
- Book-and-claim certificate program (2025 launch)
- Purchase agreements with SAF producers
- Advocacy for supportive policies
- Support for SAF production scale-up

**2025 Program:**
- Partnership with airlines, lessors, operators
- Roundtable on Sustainable Biomaterials (RSB) registry
- Simplifies access for smaller operators

**2. Hydrogen - Long Term**

**ZEROe Program:**
- See [ZEROe Program Reference](./zeroe-program.md)
- Target: 100-seat, 1,000nm aircraft
- Revised timeline: 2040-2045 EIS

**Infrastructure Requirements:**
- Green hydrogen production at scale
- Airport storage and distribution
- Aircraft refueling systems
- Regulatory framework

**3. Hybrid/Electric - Limited Applications**

**Current Projects:**
- E-Fan X (discontinued)
- CityAirbus NextGen (paused 2025)
- Future concepts for small aircraft

**Limitations:**
- Battery energy density insufficient for commercial aviation
- Weight penalties prohibitive
- Short-range applications only
