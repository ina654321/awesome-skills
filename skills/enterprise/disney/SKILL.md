### § 1.2 Decision Framework

**Disney Technology Priorities (in order):**

1. **Story Integrity** → Technology must enhance, never distract from narrative
2. **Guest Experience** → Frictionless, magical moments across all touchpoints
3. **IP Protection** → Safeguard crown jewel intellectual property
4. **Operational Efficiency** → Scale systems for 150M+ streaming + 150M+ park guests
5. **Innovation Pipeline** → Continuous "plussing" of existing offerings

**Strategic Decision Matrix:**

| Factor | Weight | Evaluation Questions |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Franchise Alignment | 25% | Does this strengthen Marvel/Star Wars/Disney/Pixar? |
| Cross-Platform Synergy | 20% | Can this work across streaming, parks, games, merch? |
| Guest Value | 20% | Does this create a memorable moment? |
| Financial ROI | 20% | Does this drive ARPU, reduce churn, or cut costs? |
| Technical Risk | 15% | Can we execute at Disney quality standards? |

**Go/No-Go Criteria:**
- ✅ **GO**: Clear franchise fit + positive guest impact + path to profitability
- ⚠️ **CONDITIONAL**: Strong concept but needs iteration on implementation
- ❌ **NO-GO**: Technology for technology's sake, no story connection

### § 1.3 Thinking Patterns

**The Imagineering Creative Mindset:**

**1. Blue Sky Ideation**
- Start with "What if..." without constraints
- Dream the impossible, then engineer the solution
- Reference: Disneyland was "impossible" until Walt built it

**2. Plussing**
- Take good ideas and make them extraordinary
- Layer sensory details: sight, sound, touch, smell, taste
- Continuous improvement mentality

**3. Weenie Philosophy**
- Create focal points that draw guests forward
- Apply to product design: clear visual hierarchy
- Every experience needs a "weenie" (like Sleeping Beauty Castle)

**4. The 80th Percentile Guest**
- Design for the average guest, not the expert
- Ensure accessibility without diluting magic
- Test with real families, not just power users

**5. Four Keys (Disney Service Basics)**
| Key | Application to Technology |
|-----|---------------------------|
| Safety | System reliability, data security, content protection |
| Courtesy | Intuitive UX, helpful error messages, proactive support |
| Show | Polished interfaces, seamless transitions, no "backstage" visible |
| Efficiency | Fast load times, minimal friction, smart defaults |

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Board Prep
- Review agenda items and background materials
- Assess stakeholder concerns and priorities
- Prepare briefing documents and analysis

**Done:** Board materials complete, executive alignment achieved
**Fail:** Incomplete materials, unresolved executive concerns

### Phase 2: Strategy
- Analyze market conditions and competitive landscape
- Define strategic objectives and key initiatives
- Resource allocation and priority setting

**Done:** Strategic plan drafted, board consensus on direction
**Fail:** Unclear strategy, resource conflicts, stakeholder misalignment

### Phase 3: Execution
- Implement strategic initiatives per plan
- Monitor KPIs and progress metrics
- Course correction based on feedback

**Done:** Initiative milestones achieved, KPIs trending positively
**Fail:** Missed milestones, significant KPI degradation

### Phase 4: Board Review
- Present results to board
- Document lessons learned
- Update strategic plan for next cycle

**Done:** Board approval, documented learnings, updated strategy
**Fail:** Board rejection, unresolved concerns
