# Regulatory & Clinical Genomics Reference

## Regulatory Pathways

### FDA (United States)

**Research Use Only (RUO)**
- No regulatory oversight
- Cannot be used for clinical decision-making
- Common for early-stage products

**Laboratory Developed Tests (LDTs)**
- Developed and performed by CLIA-certified labs
- Currently under FDA enforcement discretion
- FDA proposing to phase out LDT oversight exemption

**FDA-Cleared/Cleared Devices**

| Pathway | Description | Timeline | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------|
| 510(k) | Substantial equivalence to predicate | 3-6 months | MiSeq Dx Cystic Fibrosis |
| De Novo | Novel devices, low-moderate risk | 6-12 months | First NGS platforms |
| PMA | High-risk, novel technology | 12-24 months | Companion diagnostics |

**Key FDA Guidance Documents**
- "Use of Standards in FDA Regulatory Oversight of Next Generation Gene Sequencing"
- "Considerations for Design, Development, and Analytical Validation"
- "Clinical Validation of NGS-based Oncology Panels"

### CE-IVDR (European Union)

**Regulation Timeline**
- IVDR (2017/746) replaced IVDD (98/79/EC)
- Transition period: 2022-2028
- Notified Body involvement required for most devices

**Risk Classes**
- Class A: Low risk (e.g., general lab equipment)
- Class B: Moderate risk (e.g., NGS library prep)
- Class C: High risk (e.g., genetic testing devices)
- Class D: Highest risk (e.g., blood screening)

**Key Requirements**
- Quality Management System (ISO 13485)
- Performance evaluation/clinical evidence
- Post-market surveillance
- Unique Device Identification (UDI)

## Clinical Applications

### Oncology

**Comprehensive Genomic Profiling (CGP)**
- Tests 50+ genes simultaneously
- Identifies actionable mutations
- Guides therapy selection
- Companion diagnostic development

**Liquid Biopsy**
- Cell-free DNA (cfDNA) from blood
- Minimal residual disease (MRD) monitoring
- Resistance mutation detection
- Early cancer detection (multi-cancer)

**Key Guidelines**
- NCCN Guidelines (tumor-specific)
- AMP/ASCO/CAP Standards for CGP
- FDA Table of Pharmacogenomic Biomarkers

### Reproductive Health

**Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing (NIPT)**
- Screens for fetal aneuploidy
- Cell-free fetal DNA from maternal blood
- >99% sensitivity for T21, T18, T13
- Average risk now standard of care

**Carrier Screening**
- Pre-conception testing
- Expanded carrier screening (200+ conditions)
- ACMG recommended panel

**Preimplantation Genetic Testing**
- PGT-A: Aneuploidy screening
- PGT-M: Monogenic disorders
- PGT-SR: Structural rearrangements

### Rare Disease

**Exome Sequencing**
- First-tier test for undiagnosed genetic disease
- ~20,000 genes sequenced
- 25-40% diagnostic yield
- Recommended by ACMG/NSGC

**Genome Sequencing**
- Detects non-coding variants
- Identifies structural variants
- Higher cost, emerging standard

## Reimbursement

### United States

**CMS (Medicare)**
- Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs)
- National Coverage Determinations (NCDs)
- MolDX program (molecular diagnostics)

**Private Payers**
- Varied coverage policies
- Medical necessity documentation required
- Prior authorization common

**Key CPT Codes**
- 814xx series: Genomic sequencing
- 0001M-0156M: Multianalyte assays
- 96360-96379: Administration codes

### Coverage Criteria

Typical requirements for NGS reimbursement:
1. Medical necessity documented
2. FDA-approved/cleared test or validated LDT
3. Results will directly impact management
4. No simpler testing alternative
5. Genetic counseling offered

## Quality Standards

### CLIA (Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments)

**Certification Levels**
- CLIA-waived: Simple tests
- Moderate complexity: Most molecular tests
- High complexity: NGS, complex assays

**Requirements**
- Qualified personnel
- Quality control procedures
- Proficiency testing
- Quality assurance program

### CAP (College of American Pathologists)

- Laboratory accreditation program
- Molecular pathology checklist
- NGS-specific requirements
- Bi-annual inspections
