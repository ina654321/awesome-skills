# Berkshire Hathaway Subsidiary Profiles

## Overview

Berkshire Hathaway owns 60+ wholly-owned businesses across diverse industries. This diversification provides stable cash flows, reduces risk, and generates capital for reinvestment.

**2024 Revenue by Segment:**
- Insurance: 27%
- Railroad (BNSF): 11%
- Utilities (BHE): 8%
- Manufacturing/Service/Retail: 54%

## Transportation Group

### BNSF Railway (Burlington Northern Santa Fe)

**Founded:** 1849 (predecessors)  
**Acquired:** 2010  
**Purchase Price:** ~$34 billion  
**Headquarters:** Fort Worth, Texas

**Scale:**
- 32,500 route miles across 28 states
- 3 continents connectivity (via interchange)
- 8,000+ locomotives
- 40,000+ employees

**2024 Metrics:**
- Revenue: ~$24 billion
- Earnings: $5+ billion
- Freight volume: Diverse (consumer, industrial, agricultural, energy)

**Freight Mix:**
- Consumer products (40%)
- Industrial products (25%)
- Agricultural (20%)
- Coal (declining, ~15%)

**Competitive Advantages:**
- **Duopoly**: Only Union Pacific competes on transcontinental routes
- **Replacement cost**: Building new railroad exceeds $100B
- **Energy efficiency**: 1 gallon moves 1 ton ~500 miles
- **Infrastructure moat**: Physical network cannot be replicated

**Dividend History:**
- 2010-2020: $41.8 billion in dividends to Berkshire
- Paid for acquisition ~1.2x over

## Subsidiary Governance Model

### The Berkshire Way

**Decentralized Management:**
- Subsidiary CEOs run their businesses
- Berkshire provides capital, not directives
- Minimal headquarters staff
- No quarterly earnings pressure

**Capital Allocation:**
- Excess cash sent to Omaha
- Reinvestment decisions at subsidiary level
- Major capital projects require approval
- Acquisitions through Berkshire

**Compensation:**
- Simple, performance-based
- Equity ownership encouraged
- No complicated formulas

### Benefits of Structure

1. **Retention**: Entrepreneurs stay after selling
2. **Autonomy**: Preserves competitive culture
3. **Focus**: CEOs manage, not navigate bureaucracy
4. **Scale**: Access to Berkshire capital when needed

## The Subsidiary Advantage

### Why Wholly-Owned Businesses Matter

1. **Cash Generation**: Profits flow directly to Berkshire
2. **Control**: Permanent ownership, no market prices
3. **Tax Efficiency**: No dividend taxes between entities
4. **Float**: Insurance subsidiaries provide investment capital
5. **Resilience**: Diversification across industries

### The Collection

Berkshire's subsidiaries represent a cross-section of American business:
- **Essential infrastructure**: Rail, utilities, pipelines
- **Daily products**: Insurance, candy, batteries, paint
- **Industrial strength**: Aerospace, manufacturing, chemicals
- **Consumer touchpoints**: Auto dealers, furniture, jewelry

Together, they generate billions in annual cash flow — fuel for Berkshire's investment machine.
