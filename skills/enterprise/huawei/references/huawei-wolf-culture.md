# 狼性文化 (Wolf Culture) Deep Dive

## Origin
Ren Zhengfei's military background influenced Huawei's culture. The "wolf" metaphor was introduced to describe the aggressive, team-oriented approach needed to compete against global giants like Ericsson, Nokia, and Cisco.

## Three Characteristics (三性)

### 1. 嗅觉敏锐 (Keen Smell)
- Detect market opportunities before competitors
- Identify customer pain points proactively
- Sense industry shifts and technology trends
- Market intelligence as competitive advantage

**Practices:**
- Weekly market intelligence briefings
- Customer immersion programs (3-6 months)
- Competitor analysis and benchmarking
- Technology scouting teams

### 2. 不屈不挠 (Offensive Spirit)
- Relentless pursuit of strategic objectives
- Persistence through setbacks and failures
- "Victory at all costs" mentality
- Refuse to accept defeat

**Practices:**
- Battle reviews (战斗复盘) after every major project
- Red team vs blue team exercises
- Stretch goals and ambitious targets
- Recognition for "never give up" stories

### 3. 团队协作 (Collective Struggle)
- Pack mentality over individual glory
- Shared success and shared failure
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Information sharing across teams

**Practices:**
- Collective KPIs (not just individual)
- Cross-functional project teams
- Knowledge sharing sessions
- "One Huawei" mentality

## Supporting Concepts

### 压强原则 (Pressure Principle)
```
F = P × A
Force = Pressure × Area

Engineering Translation:
Breakthrough = Concentrated Resources × Narrow Focus
```

**Application:**
1. Identify single bottleneck
2. Allocate 10x resources to that point
3. Breakthrough achieved
4. Move to next bottleneck
5. Never dilute effort

**Famous Example:** 5G RAN development - 10,000 engineers on Massive MIMO optimization

### 自我批判 (Self-Criticism)
- Ruthless self-reflection before declaring victory
- Find problems before customers do
- Blame process, not people
- Continuous improvement culture

**Rituals:**
- Self-criticism sessions (自我批判会)
- 5 Whys root cause analysis
- Post-mortem meetings
- Red team reviews

### 备胎计划 (Plan B)
- Every critical system has tested backup
- Developed in parallel with primary solution
- Quarterly disaster drills
- Ready for <24h switchover

**Origin:** US sanctions demonstrated the importance of 备胎

### 以客户为中心 (Customer-Centric)
- "Serving customers is Huawei's only reason for existence"
- Customer needs drive R&D direction
- Customer immersion before coding
- Customer satisfaction as ultimate metric

## Wolf Culture Misconceptions

### What It's NOT:
- ❌ Blind aggression without strategy
- ❌ Internal competition to the point of destruction
- ❌ Workaholism without productivity
- ❌ Exploitation of employees
- ❌ "996" as a badge of honor

### What It IS:
- ✅ Strategic intensity focused on customer value
- ✅ Collective success over individual glory
- ✅ Sustainable high performance
- ✅ Continuous learning and improvement
- ✅ Results-oriented, not time-oriented

## Weekly Rituals

| Day | Activity | Purpose |
|-----|----------|---------|
| Monday | Weekly planning | Align priorities |
| Wednesday | Self-criticism session | Identify improvements |
| Friday | Battle review | Learn from week's battles |
| Monthly | Red/Blue team exercise | Find weaknesses proactively |
| Quarterly | Plan B drill | Test backup systems |

## Cultural Artifacts

### Mat Culture (床垫文化)
- Engineers keep mats under desks for late-night work
- Symbol of dedication, not requirement
- Being phased out in favor of sustainable practices

### Huawei Basic Law (华为基本法)
- Codified corporate culture (1996)
- 6 chapters, 103 articles
- Foundation for all subsequent culture development

## Modern Adaptations

### Balanced Intensity
- Focus on sustainable high performance
- Rotation programs to prevent burnout
- Mental health support
- Work-life balance initiatives

### Global Context
- Adapt intensity to local labor laws
- Cultural sensitivity in international teams
- Inclusive leadership practices
- Psychological safety for innovation
