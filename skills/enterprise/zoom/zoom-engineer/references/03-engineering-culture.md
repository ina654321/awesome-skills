# Zoom Engineering Culture

## "Deliver Happiness" Philosophy

Zoom's culture is built on a single core value: **Care**

Eric Yuan defines it as:
> "Every day, we care about community, customer, company, teammates, and service"

### The Happiness Flywheel

```
    Build Product That Works
              ↓
    Make It Delightfully Simple
              ↓
    Scale Without Compromising Quality
              ↓
    Deliver Happiness to Customers
              ↓
    Word of Mouth Drives Growth
              ↓
    Reinvest in Better Product
```

## Engineering Values

| Value | Engineering Application | Example |
|-------|------------------------|---------|
| **Care** | Put customer success first | 24/7 support during COVID; engineers personally responded to customer issues |
| **Simplicity** | One-click join, no downloads needed | Browser-based meetings; frictionless UX |
| **Scale** | 10x headroom in design | Pre-COVID data centers at 50% capacity |
| **Quality** | "It just works" reliability | 99.99% uptime target; obsessive testing |
| **Innovation** | AI-first transformation | AI Companion integration across all products |

## Eric Yuan's Leadership Principles

### From Interviews and All-Hands Meetings

1. **Customer Obsession**
   > "We don't have a sales culture, we have a happiness culture"
   
   - Engineers are encouraged to understand customer pain points
   - Customer support tickets are shared with engineering teams
   - "Office hours" where engineers join customer calls

2. **Long-Term Thinking**
   > "A great product with patience — the world will recognize it"
   
   - Focus on sustainable growth over quick wins
   - Invest in architecture that lasts 10+ years
   - Willing to delay features for quality

3. **Culture as Foundation**
   > "If you do not have a great culture, you really cannot scale"
   
   - Culture fit is critical in hiring
   - "No a**holes" policy — kindness is non-negotiable
   - Transparency through regular all-hands meetings

4. **Engineering Excellence**
   - Senior engineers code and review
   - Technical debt is aggressively managed
   - Architecture decisions made by engineers, not managers

## "Delivering Happiness" in Practice

### Real Examples from COVID-19

1. **24/7 Engineering Support**

   - Direct Slack channels with major enterprise customers
   - CEO personally joined war rooms

2. **Free Tier Expansion**

   - Prioritized societal good over short-term profit

3. **Security Transparency**
   - Published detailed E2EE design for peer review
   - Acquired Keybase for encryption expertise
   - CEO personally addressed security concerns

## Interview Process

Zoom's hiring reflects its culture:

### Engineering Interview Stages

1. **Recruiter Screen** (30 min)
   - Culture fit discussion
   - Career motivations

2. **Technical Phone Screen** (60 min)
   - Coding exercise
   - System design basics

3. **Virtual Onsite** (4-5 hours)
   - Coding (algorithm + data structures)
   - System design (distributed systems focus)
   - Behavioral ("Deliver Happiness" alignment)
   - Cross-functional collaboration scenario

## Meeting Culture

### Internal Meeting Norms

1. **Camera On Culture**
   - Default to video for human connection
   - Exception: Large all-hands, walking meetings

2. **Meeting-Free Wednesdays**
   - Deep work focus time
   - Async communication preferred

3. **AI Companion Usage**
   - Summaries auto-shared
   - Async catch-up encouraged

4. **Documentation First**
   - Decisions documented in Zoom Docs
   - Meeting notes captured by AI Companion

## Key Takeaways for Engineers

1. **Start with Customer Happiness** — Every technical decision should map to user value
2. **Design for Scale** — 10x headroom isn't optional, it's required
3. **Simplicity Wins** — Remove friction at every step
4. **Security is Foundational** — Privacy is not a feature, it's a requirement
5. **Culture Enables Scale** — Without great culture, technical excellence fails
