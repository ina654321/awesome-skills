# Vertex M&A History

## Acquisition Timeline

### 2019: Semma Therapeutics
- **Price**: $950 million ($310M upfront + milestones)
- **Technology**: Stem cell-derived islet cells for Type 1 Diabetes
- **Strategic Rationale**: Entered cell therapy, laid groundwork for VX-880
- **Status**: Core T1D program now in Phase 3

### 2019: Exonics Therapeutics
- **Price**: Up to $1 billion ($245M upfront + milestones)
- **Technology**: Gene editing for Duchenne muscular dystrophy, myotonic dystrophy
- **Strategic Rationale**: Expanded gene editing capabilities beyond CRISPR partnership
- **Status**: Research programs ongoing

### 2022: ViaCyte
- **Price**: Undisclosed (estimated $300-500M)
- **Technology**: Stem cell manufacturing capabilities, encapsulation devices
- **Strategic Rationale**: Manufacturing scale-up for T1D programs, device expertise
- **Status**: Integrated into Memphis cell therapy operations

### 2024: Alpine Immune Sciences
- **Price**: $4.9 billion ($65/share, all cash)
- **Technology**: Povetacicept (dual BAFF/APRIL antagonist)
- **Strategic Rationale**: Entered immunology/nephrology, "pipeline-in-a-product"
- **Status**: Phase 3 RAINER trial ongoing, potential 2026-2027 approval
- **Largest acquisition in Vertex history**

## Strategic Partnerships

### CRISPR Therapeutics (2017-present)
**Casgevy Co-Development**
- **Structure**: 60% Vertex / 40% CRISPR Tx profit split
- **Investment**: Shared development costs
- **Scope**: Sickle cell disease, beta thalassemia, potentially other indications
- **Status**: Approved and commercializing globally
- **Value Creation**: First approved CRISPR therapy

### Moderna (2020-present)
**mRNA Therapies**
- **Focus**: CF mRNA for patients unresponsive to modulators (~10%)
- **Structure**: Collaboration agreement
- **Status**: Phase 1/2 ongoing
- **Strategic Value**: Addresses remaining CF population

### Arbor Biotechnologies (2018-present)
**CRISPR Engineering**
- **Technology**: Novel CRISPR nucleases
- **Scope**: Research collaboration for improved gene editing

### KSQ Therapeutics (2020-present)
**CRISPR Screens**
- **Technology**: CRISPR-based target discovery
- **Scope**: Oncology and other therapeutic areas

## Deal-Making Philosophy

### Vertex's M&A Strategy

1. **Science-First Approach**
   - Acquire platforms with transformative potential
   - Prioritize mechanism-of-action validation
   - Focus on serious diseases with high unmet need

2. **Complementary Capabilities**
   - Cell therapy manufacturing (Semma, ViaCyte)
   - Immunology expertise (Alpine)
   - Gene editing expansion (Exonics)

3. **Disciplined Pricing**
   - Willing to pay premium for best-in-class assets
   - Structure deals with milestone payments
   - Avoid speculative early-stage acquisitions

4. **Integration Excellence**
   - Preserve scientific talent
   - Integrate into Boston-centric culture
   - Maintain focus on patient outcomes
