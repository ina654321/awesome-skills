# Vertex Product Portfolio

## Approved Products

### Cystic Fibrosis Franchise

#### TRIKAFTA/KAFTRIO (elexacaftor/tezacaftor/ivacaftor)
- **Approval**: FDA October 2019, EU August 2020
- **Indication**: CF patients ≥2 years with at least one F508del mutation
- **Mechanism**: Triple CFTR modulator—corrects protein processing and opens chloride channel
- **Coverage**: Treats ~90% of CF patients globally
- **2025 Revenue**: $10.31 billion
- **Dosing**: Two tablets every 12 hours (morning/evening)
- **Patents**: Into late 2030s

#### ALYFTREK (vanzacaftor/tezacaftor/deutivacaftor)
- **Approval**: FDA December 2024, EU 2025
- **Indication**: CF patients ≥6 years with responsive mutations
- **Mechanism**: Next-gen triple with deuterated ivacaftor
- **Differentiation**: Once-daily dosing vs. TRIKAFTA twice-daily
- **2025 Revenue**: $838 million
- **Strategic Role**: Patent extension, improved adherence

#### KALYDECO (ivacaftor)
- **Approval**: FDA January 2012
- **Indication**: Gating mutations (~4% of CF patients)
- **Status**: Legacy product, declining revenue

#### ORKAMBI (lumacaftor/ivacaftor)
- **Approval**: FDA July 2015
- **Indication**: F508del homozygous patients
- **Status**: Superseded by TRIKAFTA

#### SYMDEKO/SYMKEVI (tezacaftor/ivacaftor)
- **Approval**: FDA February 2018
- **Status**: Superseded by TRIKAFTA

### Sickle Cell Disease & Beta Thalassemia

#### CASGEVY (exagamglogene autotemcel)
- **Partnership**: Co-developed with CRISPR Therapeutics (60/40 profit split)
- **Approval**: FDA December 2023 (SCD), January 2024 (TDT)
- **EU Approval**: February 2024
- **Mechanism**: CRISPR/Cas9 editing of BCL11A enhancer → fetal hemoglobin reactivation
- **Administration**: Ex-vivo cell editing, autologous infusion
- **2025 Revenue**: $116 million
- **Patients Treated**: 64 in 2025
- **ATC Network**: 50+ authorized treatment centers globally
- **Key Data**: 93.5% of evaluable SCD patients VOC-free at follow-up

### Pain Management

#### JOURNAVX (suzetrigine)
- **Formerly**: VX-548
- **Approval**: FDA January 30, 2025
- **Indication**: Moderate-to-severe acute pain in adults
- **Mechanism**: Selective NaV1.8 voltage-gated sodium channel inhibitor
- **Differentiation**: First non-opioid analgesic in new class; no addiction risk, no respiratory depression
- **Dosing**: 100mg loading dose, then 50mg every 12 hours
- **2025 Revenue**: $60 million (post-launch)
- **Pipeline Expansion**: Phase 3 in diabetic peripheral neuropathy (DPN)

## Clinical-Stage Pipeline

### Type 1 Diabetes

#### VX-880 (zimislecel)
- **Stage**: Phase 3 (FORWARD study)
- **Mechanism**: Allogeneic stem cell-derived fully differentiated islets
- **Administration**: Infusion into hepatic portal vein
- **Key Data (Phase 1/2)**:
  - 10/12 full-dose patients achieved insulin independence at Day 365
  - All patients eliminated severe hypoglycemic events
  - >70% time in target glucose range
- **Indication**: T1D with severe hypoglycemia and hypoglycemic unawareness
- **Timeline**: Regulatory submissions expected 2026
- **Market**: ~60,000 addressable US patients initially

#### VX-264
- **Status**: Discontinued (2025)
- **Mechanism**: Encapsulated stem cell-derived islets (immunoprotection)
- **Reason**: Strategic focus on VX-880 with immunosuppression

### Renal & Immunology

#### Povetacicept (ALPN-303)
- **Origin**: Alpine Immune Sciences acquisition ($4.9B)
- **Mechanism**: Dual BAFF/APRIL antagonist fusion protein
- **Stage**: Phase 3 (RAINER for IgAN)
- **Key Data (Phase 1b/2a)**:
  - 64.1% reduction in proteinuria at 36 weeks
  - 67% disease remission rate
  - Stable renal function
- **Indications**: IgA nephropathy (lead), membranous nephropathy, lupus nephritis
- **Dosing**: Subcutaneous every 4 weeks
- **Timeline**: Phase 3 interim data H1 2026

#### Inaxaplin (VX-147)
- **Mechanism**: APOL1 inhibitor for APOL1-mediated kidney disease (AMKD)
- **Stage**: Phase 3 (AMPLITUDE study)
- **Target**: Patients with two APOL1 risk variants
- **Status**: Ongoing enrollment
