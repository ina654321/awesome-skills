# Vertex Leadership

## Executive Leadership Team

### Reshma Kewalramani, M.D., FASN
**Chief Executive Officer and President**

- **Joined Vertex**: 2017 as Chief Medical Officer
- **Became CEO**: April 2020 (first woman to lead a major US biotech)
- **Background**: 
  - Nephrologist by training
  - 12+ years at Amgen (VP roles in R&D, US Medical Organization)
  - Boston University School of Medicine (MD)
  - Massachusetts General Hospital residency
- **Key Achievements**:
  - Led TRIKAFTA approval and launch
  - Navigated COVID-19 while maintaining supply chain
  - Executed diversification strategy (Casgevy, VX-880, Journavx)
  - Acquired Alpine Immune Sciences ($4.9B)
  - Revenue growth from $4B to $12B during tenure
- **Board Service**: 
  - Ginkgo Bioworks
  - Biomedical Science Careers Program
  - RIZE Massachusetts (opioid crisis)
  - Massachusetts General Hospital Board of Trustees

**Leadership Philosophy**:
> "We don't do incremental innovation. We don't take medicines that are once a day and make it once a week. We are not serial price increasers. We don't do direct-to-consumer TV advertising. We are a different company."

## Historical Leadership

### Founding Era (1989-2000)
- **Joshua Boger**: Founder, CEO (1989-2000)
  - Chemist from Merck
  - Established Vertex's scientific culture
  - Led company through IPO (1991)

### Growth Era (2000-2012)
- **Matthew Emmens**: CEO (2000-2009)
  - Merck veteran
  - Established CF focus
  - Created KALYDECO partnership

### Transformation Era (2012-2020)
- **Jeffrey Leiden**: CEO (2012-2020)
  - Led TRIKAFTA development
  - Transformed from hepatitis C to CF focus
  - Revenue grew from $500M to $4B+

## Leadership Culture

### Core Values
1. **Fearless Pursuit of Excellence**: Scientific rigor without compromise
2. **Patients First**: Every decision measured against patient impact
3. **Innovation**: First-in-class medicines, not me-too products
4. **Integrity**: Ethical conduct in all operations
5. **Diversity**: Inclusive culture, STEM education advocacy

### Organizational Structure
- Relatively flat for a company of its size
- Science-driven decision making
- Boston-centric culture (Fan Pier headquarters)
- Strong R&D investment (32% of revenue)

### External Recognition
- Fortune "Best Workplaces in Biotechnology and Pharmaceuticals"
- Forbes "World's Most Innovative Companies"
- CEO Kewalramani: Fortune Most Powerful Women (2023)
