# Casgevy Launch Status

## Product Overview

**Casgevy (exagamglogene autotemcel)**
- First FDA-approved CRISPR/Cas9 gene-editing therapy
- Co-developed with CRISPR Therapeutics
- Profit split: 60% Vertex / 40% CRISPR Tx
- Indications: Sickle cell disease (SCD) and transfusion-dependent beta thalassemia (TDT)

## Clinical Data

### Sickle Cell Disease
- **Primary Endpoint**: 93.5% of evaluable patients VOC-free at follow-up
- **Secondary**: Hospitalizations reduced, quality of life improved
- **Long-term Follow-up**: Ongoing, patients followed for 15+ years

### Beta Thalassemia
- **Primary Endpoint**: Transfusion independence achieved in majority
- **Secondary**: Hemoglobin stabilization, iron burden reduction

## Manufacturing & Supply Chain

### Production Process
1. **Patient cell collection** (apheresis)
2. **Cell processing** (manufacturing facility)
3. **CRISPR editing** (BCL11A enhancer modification)
4. **Quality control** (extensive testing)
5. **Patient conditioning** (chemotherapy)
6. **Cell infusion** (hospital-based)
7. **Recovery monitoring** (ATC follow-up)

### Timeline
- **Total process**: 6-12 months
- **Manufacturing time**: Variable based on patient cells
- **Hospital stay**: ~1 month for conditioning and initial recovery

### Manufacturing Capacity
- Dedicated gene therapy facility
- Scaling to support 100+ patients annually
- Quality control complexity significant

## Launch Learnings

### Success Factors
1. Strong clinical data (best-in-class efficacy)
2. Historic first-mover advantage in CRISPR
3. Robust ATC network deployment
4. Payer engagement on value-based pricing

### Challenges
1. Complex patient journey (6-12 months)
2. Manufacturing capacity constraints
3. Quarter-to-quarter revenue variability
4. Competition from Bluebird's Lyfgenia

### Strategic Outlook
Casgevy represents the vanguard of gene editing therapeutics. While early revenues are modest, the platform validates Vertex's ability to execute complex cell and gene therapies—directly supporting VX-880 and future pipeline programs.
