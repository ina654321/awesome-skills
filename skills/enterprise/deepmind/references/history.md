# DeepMind History & Milestones

## Founding and Early Years (2010-2013)

### Founding (2010)

**Founders:**
- **Demis Hassabis**: CEO, AI researcher, former game designer, chess prodigy
- **Shane Legg**: Chief Scientist, formal AI theory researcher
- **Mustafa Suleyman**: Head of Applied AI, policy and operations

**Location:** London, UK (King's Cross area)

**Mission:** "Solve intelligence, then use it to solve everything else."

**Initial Focus:**
- General AI/AGI research
- Reinforcement learning
- Neuroscience-inspired approaches
- Long-term fundamental research

### Early Funding

| Round | Date | Amount | Investors |
|-------|------|--------|-----------|
| Seed | 2010 | ~£2M | Founders, angels |
| Series A | 2012 | ~£5M | Founders Fund (Peter Thiel), others |

### Key Early Hires

- **David Silver**: PhD student at UCL, RL researcher (would lead AlphaGo)
- **Koray Kavukcuoglu**: Deep learning researcher
- **Volodymyr Mnih**: DQN (Deep Q-Networks) researcher
- **Alex Graves**: LSTM, sequence modeling expert

## Google Acquisition (2014)

### The Deal

- **Date:** January 2014
- **Acquisition price:** ~$500-600 million
- **Structure:** DeepMind becomes subsidiary of Google
- **Condition:** Google establishes AI ethics board (unprecedented at the time)

### Post-Acquisition Structure

- Maintained independence in London
- Access to Google compute resources (TPUs, data centers)
- Continued focus on long-term research
- Expanded hiring globally

## Breakthrough Era (2015-2019)

### 2015: Deep Q-Networks

**Publication:** "Human-level control through deep reinforcement learning" (Nature)

**Achievement:** First AI to play Atari games at human level from raw pixels

**Significance:** Demonstrated deep RL could learn complex control tasks

### 2016: AlphaGo

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Jan 2016 | Nature paper published |
| Mar 2016 | Defeated Lee Sedol 4-1 |
| May 2017 | Defeated Ke Jie at Future of Go Summit |

**Impact:**
- 280 million viewers for Lee Sedol match
- Global awakening to AI capabilities
- "Move 37" — creative, never-before-seen play
- China announced $10B AI investment following match

### 2016-2017: AI for Science Expansion

**DeepMind Health:**
- AI for eye disease diagnosis (diabetic retinopathy)
- Partnerships with NHS hospitals
- Later spun into Google Health

**WaveNet:**
- Revolutionary speech synthesis
- Used in Google Assistant
- Significantly more natural-sounding voice

### 2017: AlphaZero

**Achievement:** Mastered Go, Chess, and Shogi from self-play without human data

**Significance:** Demonstrated general learning algorithm across domains

### 2018: AlphaFold Launch

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Dec 2018 | AlphaFold enters CASP12 competition |
| | Won competition, but not yet breakthrough accuracy |

**Status:** Promising but not yet revolutionary

### 2019: AlphaStar

**Achievement:** Mastered StarCraft II, one of the most complex real-time strategy games

**Challenges:**
- Imperfect information (fog of war)
- Real-time decisions
- Long-term planning
- Multiple agents/units

## The AlphaFold Breakthrough (2020-2024)

### 2020: AlphaFold2

**CASP14 Results (November 2020):**
- GDT_TS score: 92.4 (near-experimental accuracy)
- Solved 50-year protein folding problem
- Considered one of AI's greatest scientific achievements

**Hassabis Quote:**
> "This is the most significant thing we've done so far."

### 2021: Open Source & Isomorphic Labs

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| July 2021 | AlphaFold2 open-sourced |
| | AlphaFold Protein Structure Database launched |
| | 350,000 structures released |
| Nov 2021 | Isomorphic Labs founded (drug discovery spinoff) |

### 2022: AlphaFold Expansion

- Database grew to 200+ million structures
- Used by 1+ million researchers
- Breakthrough Prize in Life Sciences awarded to Hassabis & Jumper

### 2024: AlphaFold3 and Nobel Prize

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| May 2024 | AlphaFold3 published (Nature) |
| | Extends to DNA, RNA, ligands, ions |
| Oct 2024 | Nobel Prize in Chemistry |
| | Awarded to Hassabis & Jumper (DeepMind) + David Baker (UW) |

**Nobel Significance:**
- First Nobel for AI in natural sciences
- Recognized "solving a 50-year grand challenge"
- Shared with computational protein design (Baker)

**Honors for Hassabis:**
- Nobel Prize in Chemistry (October 2024)
- Knighted (2024 Birthday Honours) — Sir Demis Hassabis

## Google Brain Merger (2023-Present)

### The Merger (April 2023)

**Context:**
- ChatGPT launched November 2022
- Google scrambled to respond
- Bard launched March 2023 with mixed results
- Internal pressure to unify AI efforts

**Announcement:**
> "We are combining the Brain team from Google Research and DeepMind 
> to form a single unit called Google DeepMind."  
> — Sundar Pichai

**Leadership:**
- CEO: Demis Hassabis
- Chief Scientist: Jeff Dean
- Product lead: Sissie Hsiao

**Rationale:**
- End internal competition for resources
- Combine Brain's infrastructure expertise with DeepMind's research
- Accelerate development of Gemini

### The Gemini Project

| Phase | Timeline | Details |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Formation | Apr 2023 | Teams combined, project begins |
| Development | May-Dec 2023 | Intensive training and iteration |
| Launch | Dec 2023 | Gemini 1.0 (Pro, Ultra, Nano) |
| Evolution | 2024-2025 | Gemini 1.5, 2.0, 2.5 |

**Name Origin:**
- Hassabis wanted "Titan"
- Jeff Dean suggested "Gemini" (the twins)
- Represents merged Brain + DeepMind

### Organizational Evolution (2024-2025)

| Date | Change |
|------|--------|
| Feb 2024 | Gemini product team moved to DeepMind |
| Jan 2025 | AI Studio team moved to DeepMind |
| Jan 2025 | Gemini API team moved to DeepMind |
| Ongoing | Increasing consumer product responsibility |

**Significance:** DeepMind transitioning from pure research to product ownership.

## Cultural Values

### DeepMind Research Culture

1. **Long-term thinking**: Willing to pursue 10-year bets
2. **Scientific rigor**: Peer review before claims
3. **Multi-disciplinary**: Neuroscience, physics, math, CS
4. **Openness**: Publish in Nature/Science, open-source when possible
5. **Safety first**: Ethics board since founding, safety research priority

### Post-Merger Evolution

- Increased product focus (Gemini)
- Faster iteration cycles
- More engineering, still strong research
- Balancing academic and commercial priorities
