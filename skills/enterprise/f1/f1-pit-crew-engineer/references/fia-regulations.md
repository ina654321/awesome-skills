# FIA Regulations Summary

## Pit Lane Rules

### Speed Limits

| Area | Speed Limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pit lane (main) | 80 km/h | Strictly enforced by FIA |
| Pit entry | 80 km/h from line | Must slow before line |
| Pit exit | 80 km/h to line | Accelerate after line |
| Pit lane during Safety Car | 80 km/h | No increase permitted |

**Penalty for speeding:** Drive-through penalty (typically 5-10 seconds depending on severity)

### Pit Lane Dimensions

- Minimum pit box width: 5m
- Minimum pit box length: 12m
- Pit lane minimum width: 12m
- Pit wall height: Minimum 1m (protective barrier)

## Pit Crew Safety

### Required Equipment

All personnel in the pit lane must wear:
- FIA-approved fire-resistant suit (8856-2000 or later)
- FIA-approved helmet (8860-2018 for A-grade, 8858-2002 for other)
- FIA-approved gloves, boots, balaclava
- Hearing protection (100+ dB from wheel guns)

### Restricted Areas

- Pit lane: Authorized personnel only during pit stop
- Pit box: Only essential crew (16 for pit stop)
- Fastened areas: Dedicated safe zones behind barriers
- Hot pit (post-race refueling era): Full fire-resistant equipment

## Pit Stop Timing

### No Minimum Pit Stop Time

Unlike some championships, F1 has **no minimum pit stop time**. However:

- Cars must be stationary for minimum time required for tire change
- Race director can investigate abnormally fast stops (suspected driver aid)
- Equipment must meet FIA technical specifications

### Pit Stop Equipment Rules

| Equipment | FIA Requirement |
|---|---|
| Wheel guns | Pneumatic only, regulated torque |
| Jacks | No electronic activation during stop |
| Wheel nuts | Must be same design as pre-season test |
| Tire warmers | Mandatory for dry tires until 2019, now optional |

## Wheel Nut Regulations

### Specification Requirements

- Single-lug design (not multi-lug)
- Thread direction: As specified by team (verified in pre-season)
- Material: High-strength alloy (crash-safe)
- Retention: Must prevent wheel separation in crash

### Technical Inspection

- Pre-season: All wheel nut designs inspected
- Race weekend: Random checks for compliance
- Post-race: Check for modifications

## Lollipop / Traffic Light

### FIA Requirements

1. **Red light**: Car must not move
2. **Green light**: Car may move when ready
3. **Automatic system**: Must default to red
4. **Manual override**: Crew chief or designated person must have override capability
5. **Driver responsibility**: Driver must not move until green AND driver confirms readiness

### Violations

| Violation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Release with personnel in danger zone | Minimum 10-second stop-and-go |
| Unsafe release causing collision | Drive-through + possible further penalty |
| Red light violation | 5-second time penalty |

## Safety Car Procedures

### Pit Lane During Safety Car

- All cars must enter pit lane at 80 km/h
- Lap count continues (no frozen race)
- Cars may pit without losing a lap (field bunched behind SC)
- Blue flag rules apply in pit lane (overtaking prohibited)

### Free Stop Opportunity

During Safety Car, time loss for pit stop is reduced because all cars are going slow. This makes it an attractive strategy to pit during SC.

Formula:
- Normal pit stop time loss: 20-25 seconds (vs. cars on track)
- Safety Car pit stop time loss: 5-10 seconds equivalent

## Tire Regulations

### Compound Naming (2022+)

| Type | Usage | Naming |
|---|---|---|
| Dry weather | Dry track | C1, C2, C3, C4, C5 (softest to hardest) |
| Intermediate | Light wet | I (green sidewall stripe) |
| Wet | Heavy rain | W (blue sidewall stripe) |

### Mandatory Tire Usage

- Race: Must use at least 2 compounds
- Q3 (Sprint races): Top 10 must use softest available
- Parc Fermé: Limits setup changes between qualifying and race

## Pit Strategy Data

### Time Cost Analysis

| Action | Time Cost (approx.) |
|---|---|
| Pit stop itself | 2.5-3.5s (normal) |
| Time to leader lap back: | ~20-25s (normal racing) |
| Time to leader lap back (SC): | ~5-10s |
| Undercut threshold: | ~1.5s per lap tire advantage |
| Pit entry (braking): | ~0.3s vs. staying out |
| Pit exit (acceleration): | ~0.5s vs. staying out |

### Fuel Impact

- Each 10kg of fuel: ~0.03s per lap (weight vs. competitors)
- Fuel burn during race: Strategic consideration for pit timing
- Minimum fuel at race start: As per technical regulations

## Key Race Control Communications

| Code | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| "Box, box, box" | Pit immediately | Execute pit stop |
| "Delta plus" | Running below minimum time | Speed up or pit |
| "Safety Car deployed" | SC in operation | Enter pit lane if planning stop |
| "Virtual Safety Car" | VSC in operation | 60% speed limit in sector |
| "Green flag" | Track clear | Normal racing resumes |
| "Code 60" | Slow zone (medical intervention) | 60 km/h limit |

## Penalties Summary

| Offense | Typical Penalty |
|---|---|
| Speeding in pit lane | 5-second time penalty |
| Unsafe release | 10-second stop-and-go |
| Pit lane collision | Case-by-case (drive-through to DSQ) |
| Ignoring red light | 5-second time penalty |
| Wheel detachment | Possible DSQ + further investigation |
