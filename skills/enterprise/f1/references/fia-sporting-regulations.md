# FIA Sporting Regulations 2024

## Race Weekend Format

### Standard Weekend Schedule

| Day | Session | Duration | Purpose |
|-----|---------|----------|---------|
| Friday | FP1 | 60 min | Setup evaluation, data collection |
| Friday | FP2 | 60 min | Long-run simulation, tire testing |
| Saturday | FP3 | 60 min | Final setup confirmation |
| Saturday | Qualifying | ~60 min | Grid determination |
| Sunday | Race | 305 km or 2 hours | Championship points |

### Qualifying Format

**Three-Session Elimination:**

| Session | Duration | Elimination | Target |
|---------|----------|-------------|--------|
| Q1 | 18 min | Positions 16-20 | Advance to Q2 |
| Q2 | 15 min | Positions 11-15 | Advance to Q3, tire selection |
| Q3 | 12 min | Positions 1-10 | Pole position |

**Q2 Tire Rule:**
- Drivers advancing to Q3 must start race on tires used in Q2
- Strategic implication: Tire compound selection for Q2 vs. race start

### Sprint Weekend Format (6 per season)

**2024 Sprint Schedule:**

| Day | Session | Duration |
|-----|---------|----------|
| Friday | FP1 | 60 min |
| Friday | Sprint Qualifying | ~30 min |
| Saturday | Sprint Race | 100 km (~30 min) |
| Saturday | Grand Prix Qualifying | ~60 min |
| Sunday | Grand Prix | Full distance |

**Sprint Race Points (Top 8):**
| Position | 1st | 2nd | 3rd | 4th | 5th | 6th | 7th | 8th |
|----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Points | 8 | 7 | 6 | 5 | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 |

### Race Rules

**Start Procedure:**
- Formation lap mandatory
- Grid positions maintained unless penalty applied
- Jump start detection via transponders
- Aborted start procedures for incidents

**DRS Regulations:**
- Activation: After 1 complete lap (changed from 2 in 2024)
- Gap requirement: Within 1.0 second of car ahead at detection point
- Zones: Vary by circuit (1-4 zones typical)
- Safety Car restart: DRS available after 1 lap

**Overtaking Rules:**
- One defensive move permitted
- Must leave racing room (one car width)
- Track limits: White line defines track edge
- Off-track overtaking generally prohibited

### Pit Stop Regulations

**Safety Requirements:**
- Pit lane speed limit: 80 km/h (varies by circuit)
- Wheel gun torque verification required
- Traffic light release system mandatory
- Unsafe release penalties: Sporting and financial

**Tire Change Requirements:**
- No minimum pit stop time
- All four tires must be changed for wet-to-dry or dry-to-wet changes

### Safety Car Procedures

**Virtual Safety Car (VSC):**
- Neutralizes race without full Safety Car
- Delta time displayed to all drivers
- No overtaking, minimum time between marshaling points
- Pit stops permitted (time loss varies)

**Full Safety Car:**
- Safety Car picks up leader
- Double-file formation, no overtaking
- Lapped cars may unlap themselves
- Race restart: Standing start after formation lap

**Safety Car Pit Window:**
- "Free" pit stop opportunity (reduced time loss)
- Strategic advantage: Minimize position loss
- Risk: Queue in pit lane if multiple cars pit

### Penalty System

**Grid Penalties:**
- Power unit component changes beyond allocation
- Gearbox changes before 6-race minimum life
- Accumulated penalty points on license

**In-Race Penalties:**
- 5-second time penalty: Served at next pit stop or added to race time
- 10-second time penalty: Same procedure
- Drive-through: Enter and exit pit lane at speed limit
- Stop-go: 10-second stationary in pit box

**Stewarding:**
- Race Director oversees event
- Stewards panel reviews incidents
- Teams may request reviews (limited)
- Penalties may be appealed to FIA Court of Appeal

### Points System

**Grand Prix Points (Top 10):**
| Position | 1st | 2nd | 3rd | 4th | 5th | 6th | 7th | 8th | 9th | 10th |
|----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|------|
| Points | 25 | 18 | 15 | 12 | 10 | 8 | 6 | 4 | 2 | 1 |

**Fastest Lap Point:**
- Awarded to driver in top 10 who sets fastest lap
- Must be set on valid lap within 107% of winner's time
- 2024: Fastest lap point eliminated

**Championship Tie-Breaks:**
1. Number of wins
2. Number of second places
3. Number of third places
4. And so on...
