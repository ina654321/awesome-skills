# FIA Technical Regulations 2024

## Overview

The FIA Technical Regulations govern the design, construction, and operation of Formula 1 cars. Compliance is mandatory for championship participation.

## Key Technical Specifications (2024)

### Chassis Requirements

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Minimum Weight | 798 kg (including driver) |
| Maximum Width | 2,000 mm |
| Wheelbase | 3,600 mm (nominal) |
| Track Width | 1,800 mm (front), 1,650 mm (rear) |
| Floor Width | 1,800 mm |

### Power Unit Regulations

**1.6L V6 Turbo Hybrid Configuration:**
- Internal Combustion Engine: 1.6L, V6, 90° angle
- Turbocharger: Single turbo, unlimited boost pressure
- Rev limit: 15,000 RPM (practical: ~12,000 RPM)
- Fuel flow rate: 100 kg/hour maximum

**Energy Recovery Systems:**
- MGU-K (Kinetic): 120 kW max power, 4 MJ/lap deployment
- MGU-H (Heat): Unlimited, connected to turbocharger
- Energy Store: 4 MJ capacity maximum

**2024 Power Unit Allocation:**
- 4 ICE (Internal Combustion Engine) per driver per season
- 4 MGU-K per driver per season
- 4 MGU-H per driver per season
- 4 Turbocharger per driver per season
- 2 Energy Store per driver per season
- 2 Control Electronics per driver per season
- 8 Exhaust systems per driver per season

### Aerodynamic Regulations

**Front Wing:**
- Maximum 4 elements
- Width: 2,000 mm (full car width)
- Height: 75 mm above reference plane minimum
- No movable elements except DRS

**Rear Wing (DRS):**
- 2 elements maximum
- Width: 1,050 mm
- Height: 870 mm above reference plane
- DRS opening: 85 mm maximum

**Floor and Diffuser:**
- Ground effect tunnels permitted
- Diffuser height: 350 mm maximum
- Floor edge: 50 mm radius required

### Tire Regulations

**Pirelli Compounds (2024):**

| Compound | Color | Operating Window | Typical Use |
|----------|-------|------------------|-------------|
| C1 | White (Hard) | 90-110°C | High degradation tracks |
| C2 | Yellow (Medium) | 95-115°C | Balanced performance |
| C3 | Red (Soft) | 105-125°C | Qualifying, short stints |
| C4 | Red* (Super Soft) | 110-130°C | Street circuits |
| C5 | Red** (Ultra Soft) | 115-135°C | Maximum grip, minimum life |

*Compound selection varies by race (3 compounds selected from C1-C5)

**Tire Allocation per Weekend:**
- 13 dry tire sets per driver (from 3 selected compounds)
- 4 intermediate sets per driver
- 3 full wet sets per driver

### Parc Fermé Regulations

**Entry Conditions:**
- Begins when car first leaves pit lane in qualifying
- Continues until race start

**Permitted Changes:**
- Brake pad changes (same specification)
- Tire pressure adjustments
- Front wing angle adjustments (within specified range)
- Engine and gearbox mappings

**Prohibited Changes:**
- Suspension geometry
- Aerodynamic components (except front wing)
- Power unit components
- Gear ratios

### Cost Cap Compliance

**2024 Cost Cap:** $135 million (adjusted for inflation: ~$165 million)

**Inclusions:**
- Car design and development
- Manufacturing and materials
- Most personnel salaries
- Race operations
- Transport and logistics
- Wind tunnel and simulator operations

**Exclusions:**
- Driver salaries
- Top 3 executive salaries
- Marketing and hospitality
- Property costs
- Legal and finance costs
- Entry fees and licenses

## 2026 Regulation Changes Preview

### Power Unit Simplification
- MGU-H eliminated
- MGU-K power increased to 350 kW
- 50/50 thermal/electric power split target
- 100% sustainable synthetic fuels mandatory

### Active Aerodynamics
- Movable front and rear wings
- "Z Mode" (high downforce) and "X Mode" (low drag)
- DRS eliminated, replaced by manual override

### Chassis Changes
- Weight reduction: 798 kg → 768 kg
- Wheelbase: 3,600 mm → 3,400 mm
- Width: 2,000 mm → 1,900 mm
- Tire width: -25mm front, -30mm rear
