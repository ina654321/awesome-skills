# F1 Track Guides (2024 Calendar)

## Circuit Characteristics Overview

### High-Speed Circuits

#### Monza (Italian Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Permanent road course
- Length: 5.793 km
- Corners: 11 (4 slow, 7 high-speed)
- Average speed: 260+ km/h (highest on calendar)
- DRS zones: 2

**Strategy Considerations:**
- Lowest downforce setup on calendar
- High tire wear on rears (traction out of chicanes)
- Slipstreaming critical for overtaking
- Safety car probability: Low

**Optimal Tire Strategy:**
- Qualifying: Minimize drag, maximize straight-line speed
- Race: One-stop typical (high speed = lower degradation)
- Pit loss time: ~22 seconds

---

#### Spa-Francorchamps (Belgian Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Permanent road course
- Length: 7.004 km (longest on calendar)
- Corners: 19 (mix of slow and high-speed)
- Elevation change: 102 meters
- Weather: Highly variable (microclimates)

**Strategy Considerations:**
- Balanced downforce setup
- Eau Rouge/Raidillon: Full throttle, high compression
- Kemmel Straight: Primary overtaking zone
- Weather strategy critical (rain possible on parts of track only)

**Optimal Tire Strategy:**
- Elevation changes create tire temperature challenges
- Two-stop common due to high energy loads
- Intermediates often viable option

---

### Street Circuits

#### Monaco (Monaco Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Temporary street circuit
- Length: 3.337 km (shortest on calendar)
- Corners: 19 (all low-speed)
- Average speed: 160 km/h (lowest on calendar)
- Overtaking spots: Minimal (T1, T10, chicane)

**Strategy Considerations:**
- Maximum downforce setup
- Qualifying position critical (70%+ races won from pole)
- Track evolution: Significant (rubber build-up)
- Safety car probability: Very high (75%+)

**Optimal Tire Strategy:**
- Qualifying: C5 (ultrasoft) mandatory for Q3
- Race: One-stop typical, but Safety Cars create opportunities
- Pit loss time: ~23 seconds (but track position paramount)

---

#### Singapore (Singapore Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Temporary street circuit
- Length: 4.940 km
- Corners: 19
- Night race: High temperatures, humidity
- Duration: 2-hour time limit often reached

**Strategy Considerations:**
- High downforce setup
- Bumpy surface (challenges setup)
- Physical demands on drivers (high heat, no straights for rest)
- Safety car probability: High

**Optimal Tire Strategy:**
- High degradation due to heat and constant cornering
- Two-stop typical
- Track evolution: Significant (green → rubbered-in)

---

### Modern Purpose-Built Circuits

#### Bahrain International Circuit

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Purpose-built desert circuit
- Length: 5.412 km
- Corners: 15
- Sand issues: Can affect grip
- Night race since 2014

**Strategy Considerations:**
- High tire degradation (rear-limited)
- Track evolution: Significant
- Multiple overtaking zones (DRS into T1, T4, T11)
- Desert conditions: Temperature drops during race

---

#### Jeddah Street Circuit (Saudi Arabian Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Temporary street circuit
- Length: 6.174 km
- Corners: 27 (most on calendar)
- Average speed: 250+ km/h (second highest)
- Wall proximity: Very high

**Strategy Considerations:**
- Medium-low downforce (high speed despite many corners)
- Qualifying critical (difficult to overtake)
- Safety car probability: Very high
- Three DRS zones for overtaking

---

### Classic European Circuits

#### Silverstone (British Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Permanent road course
- Length: 5.891 km
- Corners: 18 (including high-speed section)
- Maggotts-Becketts-Chapel: Formula 1's fastest sequence

**Strategy Considerations:**
- High-speed corners demand high downforce
- Wind sensitive (affects balance)
- Multiple strategy options viable
- Weather: Highly variable

---

#### Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Permanent road course
- Length: 4.657 km
- Corners: 16
- Tire testing venue (representative of average track)

**Strategy Considerations:**
- High tire degradation (front and rear)
- Temperature sensitive
- Difficult to overtake (emphasis on qualifying)
- Wind affects car balance significantly

---

### Americas Circuits

#### Circuit of The Americas (United States)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Purpose-built
- Length: 5.513 km
- Corners: 20 (including signature esses T3-T6)
- Elevation change: Significant

**Strategy Considerations:**
- Medium-high downforce
- Multiple overtaking opportunities
- Tire management critical through esses
- Weather: Can be variable

---

#### Interlagos (Brazilian Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Permanent road course
- Length: 4.309 km
- Corners: 15
- Elevation: Significant (start is high, middle section low)
- Atmosphere: Often unpredictable weather

**Strategy Considerations:**
- Medium downforce
- Overtaking opportunities at T1 and T4
- Weather strategy often decisive
- Short lap = traffic management important

---

### Asia-Pacific Circuits

#### Suzuka (Japanese Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Permanent figure-8 course
- Length: 5.807 km
- Corners: 18 (including iconic esses)
- Unique: Figure-8 crossover

**Strategy Considerations:**
- High downforce required
- Demanding on drivers and cars
- Qualifying critical (difficult to overtake)
- Weather: Typhoon season risk

---

#### Albert Park (Australian Grand Prix)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Track type: Temporary street circuit
- Length: 5.278 km (modified layout)
- Corners: 14
- Parkland setting

**Strategy Considerations:**
- Medium downforce
- Track evolution: Very high (season opener, green track)
- Multiple DRS zones (4 in 2024)
- Safety car probability: High
