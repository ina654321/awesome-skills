- Date: January 23, 2017
- Equity Value: $4.4 billion

- Cash: $3.05 billion

## Netflix "Drive to Survive" Impact

### Series Overview

**Premiere:** March 8, 2019 (Season 1)
**Production:** Box to Box Films / Netflix
**Access:** Full paddock access granted by Liberty Media

### Viewership Impact

**Audience Growth:**
- 2017 US viewership: ~500,000 per race (ESPN)
- 2024 US viewership: 1.3 million per race (ESPN record)
- Growth: +160% in US market

**Demographic Shifts:**
- Average viewer age: 36 → 32 years
- Female audience: Significant increase (42% of fanbase)
- New fans: 53% of US fans cite DTS as primary reason

**Global Reach:**
- Season 4: Most-watched Netflix series in 33 countries
- Season 5: +5% viewership vs. Season 4
- 2024 global viewership: 1.5 billion per season

### Business Impact

**Sponsorship:**
- New brand entrants: Google, Salesforce, Zoom, etc.
- Existing sponsors: Increased investment
- Team valuations: All teams now valued >$1B

**Race Attendance:**
- 2024: 6.5 million attendees (+9% YoY)
- Miami GP: 270,000+ (record for first-time race)
- Las Vegas GP: 315,000+ (inaugural)

**Media Rights Value:**
- Previous ESPN deal: $5M/year
- Current ESPN deal: $75-90M/year
- Rumored Apple deal: $2B/year (2026-2030)

## F1 Team Economics

### Cost Cap (2024)

**Budget Cap:** $135 million base (~$165M with adjustments)

**Inclusions:**
- Car development and production
- Most personnel salaries
- Race operations
- Transport and logistics
- Wind tunnel and simulator

**Exclusions:**
- Driver salaries
- Top 3 executive salaries
- Marketing and hospitality
- Property costs
- Engine costs (separate PU cost cap)

## 2026 Regulation Changes: Business Impact

### New Manufacturers

**Confirmed Entrants:**
- Audi: Full factory team (acquired Sauber stake)
- Honda: Return as works supplier
- Ford: Partnership with Red Bull Powertrains
- GM/Cadillac: New team entry (11th team approved)

**PU Manufacturer Landscape (2026):**
- Mercedes
- Ferrari
- Red Bull Ford
- Honda
- Audi
- Renault (status uncertain)

### Cost Cap Evolution

**2026 Changes:**
- Cost cap increases to $215 million
- Sprint races included in cap
- Geographic cost adjustments (high-wage countries)
- Additional allocation per race increased ($1.2M → $1.8M)

### Strategic Implications

**Manufacturer Appeal:**
- Simplified PU rules (MGU-H removed)
- Road relevance increased
- Cost reduction achieved
- Sustainability focus

**New Team Entry:**
- GM/Cadillac entry approved
- $200 million anti-dilution payment
- 11th team increases grid size
- American manufacturer presence
