# Overtaking Analysis

## Overtaking Fundamentals

### DRS (Drag Reduction System)

**Mechanism:**
- Rear wing flap opens, reducing drag
- Top speed increase: 10-20 km/h
- Overtaking aid on designated straights

**Activation Requirements:**
- Gap to car ahead: < 1.0 second at detection point
- Location: After specific corners (varies by circuit)
- Timing: Available from lap 2 onwards (lap 1 after restart)

### Non-DRS Overtaking

**Braking Zone Attacks:**
- Late braking into corners
- Risk: Lock-up, contact, running wide
- Reward: Track position without straight-line speed deficit

**Alternative Lines:**
- Using different trajectory through corners
- Risk: Compromised exit, vulnerability to counter-attack
- Reward: Surprise, unexpected passing opportunity

**Eau Rouge/Raidillon (Spa):**
- Unique overtaking opportunity
- Requires commitment and trust
- High risk, high reward

## Overtaking Probability Model

### Factors Affecting Success

**Attacker Advantages:**
- DRS available: +40% success probability
- Fresher tires: +25% success probability
- Tire compound advantage: +15% success probability
- Track position advantage: +10% success probability

**Defender Advantages:**
- Inside line into corner: +30% defense probability
- Racing experience: +15% defense probability
- Team cooperation (teammate ahead): +20% defense probability

**Base Success Rates:**

## Strategic Considerations

### When to Attack

**Optimal Conditions:**
1. Tire advantage significant (> 5 laps fresher)
2. DRS available on long straight
3. Defender struggling with degradation
4. Track position more valuable than tire life
5. Championship points at stake

**When to Defend:**
1. Track position critical (Monaco, Hungary)
2. Defender has tire advantage
3. Upcoming Safety Car probability high
4. Risk of damage outweighs position gain

### Multi-Car Battles

**Three-Wide Situations:**
- Risk: Collision, track limits violations
- Strategy: Wait for cars ahead to battle, attack survivor
- Patience often rewarded

**Train Situations:**
- Multiple cars with DRS
- Leader vulnerable to concerted attack
- Strategy: Coordinate with car behind to alternate attacks

### Energy Management in Battles

**ERS Strategy:**
- Pre-battle: Harvest ERS for 2-3 laps
- Attack phase: Full deployment
- Defense phase: Maintain battery for counter-attacks
- Post-battle: Recover while maintaining gap

**Fuel Management:**
- Battles consume more fuel (acceleration/braking)
- Extended battles may compromise race strategy
- Balance: Attack for position vs. fuel targets

## Track-Specific Overtaking Analysis

### High Overtaking Tracks

**Baku:**
- 2.2 km straight: Longest in F1
- DRS extremely effective
- Drafting critical
- Braking into Turn 1: Heavy braking zone

**Monza:**
- Slipstream effect: Strongest on calendar
- DRS + slipstream = massive speed advantage
- Defensive driving: Minimal opportunity
- Strategy: Plan for being overtaken, counter-attack

**Spa:**
- Kemmel Straight: DRS + draft effective
- Eau Rouge: Unique opportunity
- Multiple overtaking zones allow strategic planning
- Weather can create additional opportunities

### Low Overtaking Tracks

**Monaco:**
- Track width: Minimal
- Overtaking zones: Turn 1, chicane, tunnel exit
- Strategy: Qualifying paramount
- Race strategy: Defensive, maintain position

**Hungary:**
- Track characteristics: Twisty, narrow
- DRS zone: Limited effectiveness
- Overtaking: Requires significant tire advantage
- Strategy: Track position over tire freshness

**Singapore:**
- Street circuit limitations
- DRS zones: Short, technical
- Overtaking: Requires bold moves
- Strategy: Qualifying critical, pit strategy for position
