# International Expansion — Reference

DoorDash has expanded from a US-only service to a global platform operating in 40+ countries through organic expansion and strategic acquisitions (Wolt in 2022, Deliveroo in 2025).

## Strategic Acquisitions

### Wolt (Acquired 2022)

**Deal Terms:**
- Acquisition price: ~$8.1B (all-stock)
- Founded: 2014 (Finland)
- Founder: Miki Kuusi (joined DoorDash leadership)

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Instant European and Asian presence
- Proven local expertise in 25 countries
- Wolt+ subscription program
- "Local commerce" alignment

**Wolt Markets:**
- **Nordics**: Finland (HQ), Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Iceland
- **Europe**: Germany, Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary, Slovakia, Croatia, Serbia, Greece, Austria, Switzerland, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania
- **Asia**: Japan, Israel, Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, Georgia
- **Africa**: South Africa

**Integration Approach:**
- Kept Wolt brand in most markets
- Shared technology platform
- Cross-pollination of best practices
- Unified subscription (Wolt+ / DashPass)

### Deliveroo (Acquired 2025)

**Deal Terms:**
- Acquisition price: $3.9B
- Founded: 2013 (UK)
- Focus: Premium delivery, grocery, rapid delivery

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Entry into UK market (Wolt's weak spot)
- Strong presence in premium segments
- Editions (dark kitchens) infrastructure
- Hop (rapid grocery) operations

**Deliveroo Markets:**
- **UK & Ireland**: Core markets
- **Europe**: France, Italy, Belgium
- **Asia-Pacific**: Singapore, Hong Kong
- **Middle East**: Kuwait, Qatar (exited), UAE

**Integration Approach:**
- Ongoing integration process
- Technology platform consolidation
- Cross-market learnings

## Market Exit Strategy (2026)

In February 2026, DoorDash announced exits from 4 markets:

| Market | Reason | Alternative |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Japan | Challenging unit economics, strong local competition | Focus resources elsewhere |
| Qatar | Small market size, consolidation | Middle East via UAE/Kuwait |
| Singapore | Competitive intensity, profitability | Regional focus on other APAC |
| Uzbekistan | Early-stage market, resource allocation | CIS via other markets |

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Focus on profitable markets
- Consolidate resources for core growth
- Deliveroo integration priorities
- Disciplined capital allocation

## International Product Strategy

### Localization Requirements

| Dimension | Localization Approach |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Language** | Full app localization |
| **Cuisine** | Local restaurant partnerships |
| **Payments** | Local methods (e.g., iDEAL in Netherlands) |
| **Delivery** | Local norms (contactless preferences) |
| **Regulation** | Compliance with local labor laws |
| **Marketing** | Local campaigns, influencers |

### Wolt Brand Strategy

Wolt brand maintained in most markets due to:
- Strong local brand recognition
- Different market positioning (premium)
- Successful subscription program (Wolt+)
- Local team expertise

### Technology Platform

Unified platform with local adaptations:
- Core dispatch engine (DeepRed)
- Localized ETA models
- Regional demand forecasting
- Local payment integrations
- Compliance systems

## Success Factors

### What Works
1. **Local Expertise**: Wolt/Deliveroo teams understand markets
2. **Suburban Strategy**: Replicate US success in suburbs
3. **Subscription Programs**: DashPass/Wolt+ drive loyalty
4. **Merchant Selection**: Local favorites + national chains
5. **Dasher Incentives**: Flexible earning opportunities

### Challenges
1. **Regulatory**: Gig worker laws vary by country
2. **Competition**: Well-funded local players
3. **Unit Economics**: Dense cities vs. suburban tradeoffs
4. **Cultural**: Different dining habits, delivery expectations
5. **Integration**: Merging acquired platforms
