## Leadership Philosophy

### Core Principles

#### 1. Customer Obsession
> *"We are in the business of delivering delight"*

- Every decision starts with customer impact
- All three sides of marketplace are "customers"
- Four deliveries per year rule for all employees

#### 2. Operational Excellence
> *"Details matter at scale"*

- Relentless focus on execution
- Data-driven decision making
- Continuous improvement (1% better every day)

#### 3. Long-term Thinking
> *"We invest in businesses that will matter in 10 years"*

- Willingness to sacrifice short-term for long-term
- Patient capital allocation
- Building enduring infrastructure

#### 4. Ownership Mindset
> *"Every employee thinks like an owner"*

- Transparent metrics sharing
- Employee stock ownership
- Accountability at all levels

#### 5. Urgency
> *"Days feel like weeks, weeks feel like months"*

- Move fast, but don't break things (too much)
- Competitive intensity
- Seize opportunities quickly

## Strategic Thinking

### The Three-Sided Marketplace

> *"In a three-sided marketplace, you have to win on all three sides. There's no other way."*

**Framework:**
```
Every decision must benefit:
├── Consumers (convenience, selection, speed)
├── Merchants (revenue, growth, efficiency)
└── Dashers (earnings, flexibility, respect)
```

### Local Commerce Vision

> *"We're not a food company. We're a logistics company that happens to deliver food."*

**Evolution of Vision:**
| Phase | Focus | Statement |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| 2013-2018 | Food delivery | "Delivering good food" |
| 2019-2022 | Local commerce | "More than food" |
| 2023+ | Logistics infrastructure | "Grow and empower local economies" |

### Mission Statement
> *"Grow and empower local economies"*

**Interpretation:**
- Support small businesses
- Create earning opportunities
- Build community infrastructure
- Enable local success

## Cultural Rituals

### Four Deliveries Per Year

**Rule:** Every salaried employee must complete at least 4 deliveries per quarter.

**Purpose:**
- Understand Dasher experience
- Stay close to operations
- Identify improvement opportunities
- Build empathy

**Execution:**
- Tracked in internal systems
- Leadership participation (including Tony)
- Debriefs to share learnings

### All-Hands Transparency

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Frequency** | Monthly |
| **Content** | Business metrics, strategy, challenges |
| **Format** | Open Q&A with leadership |
| **Attendees** | All employees |

### Incident Response

**Approach:**
- Executives participate in critical issues
- No delegation of important problems
- Customer-first resolution
- Post-mortems for learning

## Leadership Lessons

### From Tony Xu's Example

1. **Stay close to operations** — Even as CEO, understand frontline work
2. **Balance all stakeholders** — No side of marketplace can suffer
3. **Think long-term** — Build for sustainability, not just growth
4. **Lead with values** — Mission drives decisions
5. **Empower teams** — Trust people closest to problems
6. **Learn continuously** — Iterate, improve, adapt
7. **Communicate clearly** — Direct, honest, transparent
