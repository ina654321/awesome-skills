## Investment Philosophy

Tencent's investment strategy is unique among technology companies - combining venture capital returns with strategic ecosystem synergy. Led by President Martin Lau (ex-Goldman Sachs) and CSO James Mitchell (ex-Goldman), Tencent has built one of the world's most successful corporate venture portfolios.

**Core Principles:**
1. **Strategic First:** Portfolio companies must enhance WeChat ecosystem
2. **Distribution Advantage:** Leverage WeChat data for investment sourcing
3. **Platform Enablement:** Provide unfair advantages to portfolio companies
4. **Long-term Holding:** Patient capital, strategic value over quick exits
5. **Founder-Friendly:** Support, don't control (minority stakes preferred)

**Comparison:**
- More companies than SoftBank Vision Fund
- Better returns than most VC firms
- Strategic value beyond financial returns

## Investment Process

### Sourcing

**Channels:**
1. **WeChat Data:** Transaction patterns identify high-growth companies
2. **Network Effects:** Portfolio company referrals
3. **VC Relationships:** Co-investment opportunities
4. **Corporate Development:** Strategic targets

**The WeChat Advantage:**
```
WeChat Transaction Data
         │
         ▼
    Identify Growth
    (Mini Program GMV spikes)
         │
         ▼
   Investment Team
   Evaluation
         │
         ▼
   Strategic Fit
   Assessment
         │
         ▼
   Deal Execution
```

### Evaluation Framework

**Tencent Scorecard:**

| Criteria | Weight | Questions |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **Ecosystem Fit** | 40% | Can they leverage WeChat? Do we add unique value? |
| **Team Quality** | 30% | Are founders exceptional? Execution track record? |
| **Market Opportunity** | 20% | TAM size? Competitive dynamics? |
| **Financial Returns** | 10% | Valuation discipline? Exit potential? |

### Decision Making

**Process:**
1. **Deal Team Analysis:** CSO James Mitchell oversight
2. **President Approval:** Martin Lau sign-off
3. **Board Notification:** Major investments
4. **Pony Ma:** Strategic alignment check

**Typical Deal Size:**
- Early stage: $1-10M
- Growth: $10-100M
- Late stage: $100M-1B+
- Strategic: Unlimited (Riot, Supercell)

**China:**

**Concentration:**
- Large stakes in few companies
- Market correlation
- Liquidity challenges
