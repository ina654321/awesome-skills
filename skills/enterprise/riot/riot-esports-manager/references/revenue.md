# Riot Esports Manager — Revenue Sharing Calculator

Full reference for §7.2 (Business Model Framework). League revenue distribution and team economics.

## League Revenue Distribution (Franchise Era)

```
Total League Revenue Sources:
├── Media Rights           40%  ($200M/yr for LCS, $180M for LEC, $300M+ for LPL)
├── Sponsorship            30%  (multiple tier-1 brands)
├── Merchandise            15%  (team jerseys, in-game items)
├── Ticketing / Live Events 10% ( Worlds tickets, VIP experiences)
└── Other (licensing, data)  5%

Distribution to Teams:
├── Equal Share (base)     35%  ($X per team regardless of standing)
├── Performance Bonus      15%  (playoff qualification, titles)
└── Total to Teams         50%

League Operations:
├── Broadcast Production   20%
├── Event Operations        10%
├── Anti-Cheat & Integrity  5%
└── Admin & Commissioner    15%
```

## Team Economics Floor & Ceiling

| Metric | Floor | Target | Ceiling |
|--------|-------|--------|---------|
| Minimum player salary | $75K/yr (LCS), $50K (LEC), region-adjusted | $150K avg | No hard cap |
| Team roster size | 5 active + 2 staff minimum | 8 + coaching staff | — |
| Revenue share % to players | 40% of team revenue | 50–60% | 70% |
| Operating expense ratio | — | <85% of revenue | 100% (breakeven) |

## Franchise Valuation Formula

```
Team Valuation = 
  (Annual Revenue × 4–6 multiple) + Franchise Slot Premium − Debt Obligations

Example (LCS mid-tier team):
  Revenue: $8M/yr (media share + team sponsors + merch)
  Multiple: 5×
  Slot Premium: $15M (LCS franchise slot value)
  Debt: $3M
  = ($8M × 5) + $15M − $3M = $52M valuation
```

## Team Bankruptcy Risk Indicators

- Owner equity < 6 months operating runway
- Debt-to-revenue ratio > 2.0
- Sponsor retention rate < 60%
- Star player departure with no replacement strategy
- Multiple consecutive losing seasons (>3 years)
