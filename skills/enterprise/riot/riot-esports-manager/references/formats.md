# Riot Esports Manager — Tournament Format Matrix

Full reference for §7.1 (Format Selection). Contains detailed comparison of all competitive formats used in professional esports.

## Format Comparison Matrix

| Format | Teams | Match Count | Fairness | Viewer Appeal | Best For |
|--------|-------|-----------|----------|--------------|----------|
| **Double Round Robin** | 8–12 | 14–22 per team | ★★★★★ | ★★★ | Regular season (LEC, LCS) |
| **Single Round Robin** | 8–16 | 7–15 per team | ★★★★ | ★★★★ | Compact leagues |
| **Swiss System** | 8–32 | 5 per team (fixed) | ★★★★ | ★★★★ | Majors, Worlds qualification |
| **Group Stage + Bracket** | 16–24 | Variable | ★★★ | ★★★★★ | International tournaments |
| **Double Elimination** | 4–16 | 2× (losers bracket) | ★★★★★ | ★★★ | High-stakes playoffs |
| **Single Elimination** | 4–32 | 1 per team | ★★ | ★★★★★ | Finals, rapid brackets |

## Selection Decision Tree

```
Team Count?
├── 8–12 teams
│   └── Round Robin (double for fairness, single for compact)
├── 13–24 teams
│   ├── Time unlimited → Swiss (5 rounds) → Top 8 bracket
│   └── Time limited → Group Stage (4 groups × 4) → Top 8 bracket
└── 25–32 teams
    └── Swiss (5 rounds) → Play-in elimination → Group Stage → Bracket
```

## Historical Precedents

| Tournament | Year | Format | Outcome |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|
| Worlds | 2011 | Single elimination | Too few meaningful matches |
| Worlds | 2017 | Group Stage + Bracket | Gold standard; used 2017–2022 |
| Worlds | 2023 | Swiss + Bracket | Improved group stage; all teams had stakes |
| MSI | 2024 | Double Elim bracket | Exciting loser's run storylines |
| VCT Masters | 2023 | Swiss + Bracket | Regional diversity maintained |
