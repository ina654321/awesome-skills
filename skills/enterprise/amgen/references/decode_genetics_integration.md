# deCODE Genetics Integration Reference

**Acquisition Date:** December 2012  
**Acquisition Value:** $415 million (all-cash)  
**Headquarters:** Reykjavik, Iceland  
**Founder/CEO:** Kari Stefansson, MD, Dr. Med.

---

## Executive Summary

deCODE Genetics, acquired by Amgen in 2012, provides the company with industry-leading human genetics capabilities. This subsidiary has fundamentally transformed Amgen's approach to target identification and validation, significantly improving R&D productivity by identifying validated disease targets before major clinical investment.

---

## deCODE Background

### History
- **Founded:** 1996 in Iceland
- **IPO:** 2000 ($173M raised)
- **Bankruptcy:** 2009 (financial crisis impact)
- **Private Restructuring:** 2010
- **Amgen Acquisition:** December 2012

### Unique Assets

**Iceland Population Database:**
- Nearly half of Iceland's population genotyped (~140,000 individuals)
- Comprehensive genealogical records (dating to original settlement, 1000+ years)
- Universal healthcare records spanning decades
- High degree of genetic homogeneity (isolated population)

**Key Advantage:**
The Icelandic population represents a unique genetic laboratory - relatively homogeneous, excellent record-keeping, and high participation rates in research.

## Capabilities

### 1. Whole Genome Sequencing

**Technology:**
- High-throughput sequencers producing short and long reads
- Long reads: Structural variation detection (deletions, insertions, duplications)
- Short reads: High accuracy variant calling
- Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS): Bias-free genome exploration

**Scale:**
- ~2,600 whole genomes sequenced (sufficient to infer entire population given kinship)
- Continuous expansion of sequencing depth

### 2. Genome-Wide Association Studies (GWAS)

**Methodology:**
- Identify single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) across genome
- Correlate genetic variants with diseases/traits
- Discover associations between common variants and conditions

**Discoveries:**
- Thousands of sequence variants associated with traits
- BMI, height, cardiovascular disease
- Neurodegenerative diseases, inflammatory conditions
- Cancer susceptibility factors

### 3. Target Validation

**Process:**
```
Genetic Discovery → Functional Analysis → Target Validation → Amgen R&D
       │                    │                   │              │
       ▼                    ▼                   ▼              ▼
   GWAS Studies         In Vitro          Preclinical    Clinical
   Population Data      Validation        Development    Development
```

**Key Principle:**
"There is hardly anything more dangerous to a scientist than a great hypothesis. You have to be humble and accept what the data give you." - Kari Stefansson

## Integration with Amgen R&D

### Biology-First Approach

**Traditional Drug Discovery:**
- Start with disease hypothesis
- Test in animal models
- Proceed to human trials
- High failure rate (90%+ in clinical development)

**Amgen/deCODE Approach:**
- Start with human genetic associations
- Understand disease mechanisms in humans first
- Design therapeutics based on human biology
- Improved success rates through better target selection

### Impact on Pipeline

**CEO Statement (Bob Bradway):**
> "A substantial portion of Amgen's research and development pipeline is now supported by genetic insights from deCODE."

**Quantified Impact:**
- Reduced clinical failure rates
- Earlier termination of programs unlikely to succeed
- Better patient selection through genetic biomarkers
- Prioritization of first-in-class opportunities

### Research Focus Areas

**Cardiovascular Disease:**
- Genetic risk factors for heart disease
- LDL cholesterol pathway variants
- PCSK9 discovery validation (supported Repatha development)

**Inflammatory Diseases:**
- Autoimmune disease genetics
- Cytokine pathway variants
- TSLP biology (supports TEZSPIRE)

**Neurodegenerative Diseases:**
- Alzheimer's disease genetics
- Parkinson's disease risk factors
- Multiple sclerosis associations

**Oncology:**
- Cancer susceptibility genes
- Tumor progression markers
- Immunotherapy response predictors

## Wet Lab + Dry Lab Integration

### Amgen's Integrated Approach

**Wet Lab (Traditional Biology):**
- Protein science and biochemistry
- Cell-based assays
- Animal models
- Structural biology

**Dry Lab (Computational Biology):**
- Bioinformatics and genomics
- AI/ML for target prediction
- Data science platforms
- Computational chemistry

**Integration Point:**
deCODE human genetic insights guide wet lab experimental design, ensuring research focuses on biologically validated targets.

**Example Workflow:**
1. deCODE identifies genetic variant associated with disease
2. Functional studies validate mechanism (wet lab)
3. Target validation in cellular systems (wet lab)
4. Therapeutic modality selection (integrated)
5. Clinical biomarker development (deCODE data)
