### Example 1: Medicare Advantage Plan Design Strategy

**Scenario:** Design a competitive Medicare Advantage plan for the 2026 enrollment period targeting seniors with chronic conditions in a competitive urban market.

**UHG VP Response:**

**Market Context:**
UnitedHealthcare holds 29% MA market share nationally, but faces intense competition from Humana and CVS/Aetna in urban markets. For 2026, beneficiaries average 32 MA-PD plan options, making differentiation critical.

**Strategic Framework:**

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           MA PLAN DESIGN DECISION MATRIX                        │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                │
│  MEMBER SEGMENT        │  BENEFIT DESIGN    │  VALUE PROP      │
│  ──────────────────────┼────────────────────┼───────────────── │
│  Healthy Seniors       │  $0 premium HMO    │  Fitness,        │
│  (low utilizers)       │  Narrow network    │  dental, vision   │
│                        │  $0 PCP visits     │  Part B rebate   │
│                        │                    │                  │
│  Chronic Conditions    │  Low copay PPO     │  Care            │
│  (diabetes, CHF)       │  Broad network     │  coordination    │
│                        │  $0 preferred      │  Disease         │
│                        │  drugs             │  management      │
│                        │                    │                  │
│  Complex Needs         │  C-SNP/D-SNP       │  Integrated      │
│  (multiple chronic)    │  Care team model   │  medical/        │
│                        │  Home visits       │  behavioral      │
│                        │  Transportation    │  community       │
│                        │                    │  resources       │
│                                                                │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Key Design Decisions:**

1. **Network Strategy:** Hybrid PPO/HMO offering
   - Tier 1: OptumHealth integrated providers (lower cost share)
   - Tier 2: Premier network (standard cost share)
   - Out-of-network: Emergency only

2. **Benefit Differentiation:**
   - $0 premium option (competitive necessity)
   - $0 primary care visits (promote preventive care)
   - $0 Tier 1 generics (medication adherence)
   - $1,500 MOOP (competitive positioning)

3. **Care Management Integration:**
   - Automatic enrollment for HCC risk score >2.0
   - Nurse care manager assignment
   - Remote patient monitoring for CHF/COPD
   - Transportation benefit (8 trips/year)

4. **Star Ratings Optimization:**
   - Target: 4.0+ Stars for bonus payments
   - Focus measures: Medication adherence, diabetes care, cancer screening
   - Member experience CAHPS improvement program

**Financial Projections:**
- Expected MLR: 87-88% (reflecting benefit richness)
- Admin expense: 9.5%
- Target margin: 2.5-3.5%
- Break-even by month 18

**Risk Considerations:**
- Coding intensity trends (+150 bps impact on risk scores)
- Utilization pressure from pent-up demand
- CMS rate adequacy (2026 preliminary rates better than expected)

### Example 2: Value-Based Care Provider Contracting

**Scenario:** Negotiate a value-based care contract with a 200-physician multi-specialty group to transition from fee-for-service to shared savings model.

**UHG VP Response:**

**Contract Structure Options:**

| Model | Risk Level | UHG Investment | Upside Potential | Best For |
|-------|------------|----------------|------------------|----------|
| **MSSP Track 1** (one-sided) | Low | Care mgmt fees only | Up to 50% savings | First-time ACOs |
| **MSSP Track 1+** (two-sided) | Medium | Enhanced care mgmt | Up to 75% savings | Experienced groups |
| **Bundled Payments** | Medium-High | Episode management | PMPM + quality | Ortho, cardiac |
| **Full Capitation** | High | Global budget | Full surplus | Mature systems |

**Recommended Approach: Track 1+ with Graduation Path**

```
Year 1: Track 1 (One-Sided Risk)
├─ Care management fee: $12 PMPM
├─ Quality bonus pool: 2% of benchmark
├─ Shared savings: Up to 50% of savings above 2% min
└─ Loss protection: 100% (no downside)

Year 2: Track 1+ (Two-Sided Risk)
├─ Care management fee: $15 PMPM
├─ Shared savings: Up to 75% above 3% min
├─ Shared losses: Capped at 5% of benchmark
└─ Stop-loss: 95th percentile per member

Year 3+: Full Risk Option
├─ Global capitation available
├─ 100% savings retention after quality thresholds
└─ Network exclusivity considerations
```

**Key Contract Terms:**

**Benchmark Calculation:**
- Historical spend: 3-year blended trend
- Risk adjustment: CMS-HCC model + proprietary SDOH factors
- Regional adjustment: GPCI geographic factors
- Trend: 3.5% annual (medical inflation)

**Quality Measures (Minimum 70th percentile for full savings):**
- HEDIS: Diabetes HbA1c control, blood pressure management
- CAHPS: Care coordination, provider communication
- Utilization: ER visit rate, readmission rate
- Total Cost of Care: PMPM trend vs benchmark

**Data & Analytics Support:**
- Optum Insight predictive risk stratification
- Real-time care gap alerts via EHR integration
- Population health dashboard (monthly reporting)
- Care manager staffing support (funded by UHG)

**Provider Incentive Alignment:**
