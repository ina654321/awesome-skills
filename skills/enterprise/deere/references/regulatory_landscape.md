# John Deere Regulatory Landscape Reference

## Right to Repair Overview

The "right to repair" movement advocates for consumers' ability to repair or modify their devices themselves or through independent repair shops, rather than being restricted to using the manufacturer's authorized service network.

### Current Legal Status (as of March 2026)

#### Federal Actions

**FTC Lawsuit Against Deere (January 15, 2025)**

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Plaintiff** | Federal Trade Commission |
| **Co-Plaintiffs** | Illinois, Minnesota, Wisconsin, Iowa, Michigan, Arizona |
| **Allegations** | Monopolization of repair market through software restrictions |
| **Core Claim** | Deere maintains 100% market share in repair software/tools |
| **Alleged Harm** | $4.2B annual cost to U.S. farmers |
| **Deere's Defense** | Lawsuit "meritless," filed "eve of Administration change" |

**Key Allegations:**
1. Deere restricts access to diagnostic software (EQUIP system)
2. Only authorized dealers can perform software-dependent repairs
3. Contractual prohibitions prevent dealers from sharing tools
4. Farmers forced into dealer network for critical repairs
5. Results in higher costs, delays during planting/harvest

**Timeline:**
- **September 2021**: FTC investigation authorized
- **October 2024**: Investigation confirmed via court filings
- **January 2025**: FTC files lawsuit
- **February 2025**: Michigan, Arizona join suit
- **August 2025**: Court rules Deere must face antitrust claims
- **Ongoing**: Discovery and pre-trial proceedings

#### International Developments

**Canada:**
- **Bill C-244**: Passed House of Commons 2024
- **Status**: Under Senate review
- **Concern**: Industry argues bill enables emissions control circumvention

**Australia:**
- Farmers abandoned voluntary agreement with Deere (January 2025)
- Demanding mandatory right to repair legislation
- Competition regulator investigating

**European Union:**
- Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR)
- Right to repair provisions for appliances
- Agricultural equipment may be included in future amendments

#### Competitive Positioning

**John Deere Advantages:**
- Dealer network quality and expertise
- Genuine parts availability
- Warranty protection
- Integrated precision ag services
- Predictive maintenance capabilities

**Independent Repair Value Proposition:**
- Potentially lower labor rates
- Faster response times (localized)
- Longer operating hours
- Relationship-based service

### Key Legal Arguments

#### FTC Position
- Deere violates Section 5 of Federal Trade Act (unfair practices)
- Aftermarket monopolization through design
- Tying arrangements (equipment + service)
- Consumer harm through inflated prices

#### Deere Defense
- Software complexity requires specialized tools
- Safety and environmental compliance concerns
- Intellectual property protection
- Dealer network investment justification
- Innovation incentives

### Strategic Recommendations

#### Short-Term (2025-2026)
1. **Accelerate voluntary repair access** - Expand Operations Center PRO
2. **Pilot transparency** - Demonstrate good faith to regulators
3. **Dealer support** - Maintain network profitability through value-add
4. **Legal defense** - Continue vigorous defense of core IP

#### Medium-Term (2026-2028)
1. **Service model transformation** - Shift from repair to outcomes
2. **Technology differentiation** - AI/predictive maintenance advantages
3. **Partnership ecosystem** - Certified independent repair networks
4. **Regulatory engagement** - Shape legislation favorably

#### Long-Term (2028+)
1. **Predictive maintenance dominance** - Prevent failures before repair needed
2. **Subscription services** - Recurring revenue independent of repairs
3. **Data monetization** - Insights value exceeds service value
4. **Platform strategy** - Ecosystem lock-in beyond repair restrictions

### Farmer Perspective

**Common Concerns:**
- Downtime during critical planting/harvest windows
- Dealer service delays (parts, technician availability)
- Cost of dealer repairs vs. independent shops
- Equipment ownership rights

**Survey Data (2024):**
- 76% of farmers support right to repair legislation
- 68% have experienced repair delays
- Average wait time for dealer service: 3.7 days
- Estimated annual cost of repair restrictions: $4.2B
