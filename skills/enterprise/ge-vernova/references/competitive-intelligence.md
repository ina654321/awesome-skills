### Competitor Deep Dives

#### Siemens Energy

**Company Profile:**
- Spun off from Siemens AG in 2020
- Combines power generation with grid technology
- Strong in offshore wind (SGRE)

**Key Products:**
- SGT5/6-9000HL (400+ MW, 63%+ efficiency)
- SGT-800 (industrial, 50+ MW)
- SGT-750 (aeroderivative)

**Strengths:**
- HL-class efficiency leadership
- Strong European installed base
- Combined cycle excellence
- Grid integration (Siemens Gamesa)

**Weaknesses:**
- Quality issues in wind segment
- Smaller services network vs. GE
- Less hydrogen progress

**GE Vernova Differentiators:**
- Larger installed base and O&M experience
- More advanced hydrogen capability (50% now, 100% pathway)
- Superior digital/AI integration
- Stronger data center market position

#### Mitsubishi Power

**Company Profile:**
- Japanese power equipment leader
- Strong in Asia-Pacific markets
- Part of Mitsubishi Heavy Industries

**Key Products:**
- M501JAC (500+ MW, 64%+ efficiency)
- M701J (largest gas turbine)
- Solid oxide fuel cells

**Strengths:**
- J-class efficiency world records
- Asian market leadership
- Air-cooled technology (no water needed)
- Long-term customer relationships

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited Western market presence
- Smaller services network
- Less fuel flexibility

**GE Vernova Differentiators:**
- Global reach and local presence
- Broader product portfolio
- Superior digital solutions
- More flexible fuel capability

#### Ansaldo Energia

**Company Profile:**
- Italian power equipment manufacturer
- Formerly part of Alstom/GE acquisition
- Strong in Europe, Middle East, Africa

**Key Products:**
- GT36 (H-class challenger)
- AE94.3A (F-class)

**Strengths:**
- Competitive pricing
- European manufacturing

**Weaknesses:**

**GE Vernova Differentiators:**
- Proven reliability and availability
- Comprehensive services
