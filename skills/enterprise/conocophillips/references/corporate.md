## Company History

### Origins

**Founding:** 2002 merger of Conoco Inc. and Phillips Petroleum Company

**Predecessor Companies:**

| Company | Founded | Key Legacy |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Continental Oil (Conoco)** | 1875 | Rocky Mountain exploration, red triangle logo |
| **Phillips Petroleum** | 1917 | Oklahoma oil, chemicals expertise |
| **Marland Oil** | 1929 merger | Acquired by Conoco, red triangle origin |

### Key Milestones

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2002 | Conoco-Phillips merger creates 3rd largest US oil company |
| 2006 | Burlington Resources acquisition ($35B) |
| 2012 | Phillips 66 spin-off (downstream separation) |
| 2017 | Cenovus asset acquisition (Surmont 50%) |
| 2020 | Concho Resources acquisition ($13.3B) |
| 2021 | Shell Permian acquisition ($9.5B); Surmont 100% |
| 2024 | Marathon Oil acquisition ($22.5B) |

### Strategic Transformation

**2012 Pivot:**
- Spun off refining, marketing, chemicals as Phillips 66 (NYSE: PSX)
- Became world's largest independent E&P company
- Focused exclusively on upstream operations

**Philosophy:**
> "We're not an integrated major. We're not a small independent. We're ConocoPhillipsвЂ”large enough for scale, focused enough for discipline."
