# National Team Partnerships

## Overview
The "National Team" refers to Blue Origin's strategic partnerships for major space programs, particularly the Human Landing System (HLS) for NASA's Artemis program. The composition has evolved from the original 2020 team to the current Sustaining Lunar Development team.

## Original National Team (2020-2021)

### Partners
| Company | Role | Heritage |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Blue Origin** | Prime contractor, Descent Element | New Shepard, BE-7 development |
| **Lockheed Martin** | Ascent Element | Orion spacecraft |
| **Northrop Grumman** | Transfer Element | Cygnus cargo vehicle |
| **Draper** | Guidance, Navigation, Control | Apollo guidance computer |

### Original Architecture (2020)
The original HLS proposal featured a **three-stage modular design:**

1. **Transfer Element (Northrop Grumman)**
   - Deliver lander from Gateway to low lunar orbit
   - Based on Cygnus cargo vehicle heritage
   - Separate refueling element for sustainability

2. **Descent Element (Blue Origin)**
   - Powered landing on lunar surface
   - Multiple BE-7 engines
   - Based on Blue Moon cargo lander

3. **Ascent Element (Lockheed Martin)**
   - Crew cabin and ascent propulsion
   - Commonality with Orion spacecraft
   - Reusable design

### Proposal Outcome
- **Competition:** Lost to SpaceX Starship (April 2021)
- **Bid Value:** ~$6 billion
- **Protest:** Filed GAO protest (denied), then federal court (dismissed)

## Current National Team (2023-Present)

### New Partnership for Sustaining Lunar Development
After losing the initial HLS competition, Blue Origin reorganized for the Sustaining Lunar Development (SLD) competition and won the contract in May 2023.

### Current Partners
| Company | Role | Contribution |
|---------|------|--------------|
| **Blue Origin** | Prime contractor | Blue Moon MK2 lander, BE-7 engine |
| **Lockheed Martin** | Cislunar Transporter | Propellant delivery to lunar orbit |
| **Draper** | GNC | Guidance, navigation, control, simulation |
| **Boeing** | Docking System | Hard dock mechanism for Orion/Gateway |
| **Astrobotic** | Cargo Systems | Payload accommodation and offloading |
| **Honeybee Robotics** | Offloading | Lunar surface payload deployment |

### Simplified Architecture (2023)
The winning proposal features a **single-stage reusable design:**

- **Single Vehicle:** Lands, stays on surface, takes off with same stage
- **Refueling:** In lunar orbit via Cislunar Transporter
- **Engine:** Single BE-7 with deep throttle capability
- **Advantage:** Simplified operations, reduced mass, lower cost

### Contract Details
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Program** | Sustaining Lunar Development |
| **Award** | $3.4 billion |
| **Mission** | Artemis V (and beyond) |
| **Target Date** | ~2029 |
| **Blue Origin Investment** | "Well north" of $3.4B (company funds) |

## Other Partnerships

### United Launch Alliance (ULA)
**Relationship:** Customer/Supplier
- **Blue Origin supplies:** BE-4 engines for Vulcan rocket
- **ULA provides:** Alternative launch capability for some payloads
- **Status:** Strong partnership, critical for national security launches

### Sierra Space
**Relationship:** Joint venture partner (Orbital Reef)
- **Sierra provides:** LIFE habitats, Dream Chaser transport
- **Blue Origin provides:** Core modules, New Glenn launch services
- **Status:** Long-term strategic partnership for commercial space station

### NASA
**Relationship:** Customer and development partner
- **Contracts:** HLS ($3.4B), Orbital Reef ($130M), CLDS
- **Collaboration:** Technical expertise, test facilities, flight heritage
- **Status:** Primary customer for major Blue Origin programs

## Strategic Significance

### Why Partnerships Matter
1. **Risk Sharing:** Distribute development costs and technical risk
2. **Heritage Leverage:** Combine proven capabilities from multiple organizations
3. **Political Support:** Broad contractor base generates congressional support
4. **Technical Breadth:** No single company has all required expertise

### Competitive Advantage
- **SpaceX:** Primarily vertical integration
- **Blue Origin:** Strategic partnerships for specialized capabilities
- **Result:** Different approaches to solving complex space challenges
