## Case 1: Post-Merger Integration (Healthcare)

**Client:** Regional hospital network acquiring competitor
**Situation:** $800M revenue hospital network acquiring $400M competitor; need integration strategy

### BCG Approach

**Phase 1: Integration Planning**
- Identify synergy opportunities (cost and revenue)
- Assess cultural compatibility
- Define integration thesis (absorption vs. best-of-both)

**Phase 2: Synergy Analysis**
| Category | Opportunity | Timeline | Investment |
|----------|-------------|----------|------------|
| Supply chain consolidation | $18M/year | 12 months | $2M |
| Administrative rationalization | $12M/year | 18 months | $1M |
| Clinical service optimization | $8M/year | 24 months | $3M |
| Revenue cycle improvement | $6M/year | 12 months | $1M |
| IT systems integration | $4M/year | 36 months | $15M |
| **Total** | **$48M/year** | | **$22M** |

**Phase 3: Integration Roadmap**
- Day 1 readiness: Legal, regulatory, communications
- First 100 days: Quick wins, cultural integration
- Months 4-12: Operational integration
- Months 12-36: Systems integration, optimization

**Key Insight:** BCG recommended "best of both" approach for clinical services while fully integrating back-office functions. Total synergy target: $48M annually by Year 3.

## Case 2: Go-to-Market Transformation (B2B Technology)

**Client:** Enterprise software company
**Situation:** Flat revenue growth, declining win rates, need go-to-market overhaul

### BCG Analysis

**Current State Assessment:**
- Sales cycle: 9 months (industry avg: 6 months)
- Win rate: 22% (industry avg: 35%)
- CAC: $85K (too high for deal size)
- Customer satisfaction: 72% (below best-in-class 85%+)

**Root Cause Analysis:**
1. **Product-Market Fit:** Legacy product misaligned with buyer needs
2. **Sales Model:** Field-heavy model not suited for mid-market
3. **Pricing:** Complex, non-transparent pricing creates friction
4. **Digital Presence:** Minimal digital engagement in buyer journey

**BCG Recommendations:**

| Initiative | Impact | Timeline | Investment |
|------------|--------|----------|------------|
| Inside sales transformation | +$40M revenue | 12 months | $8M |
| Product-led growth (PLG) | +$25M revenue | 18 months | $12M |
| Value-based pricing | +15% ASP | 6 months | $1M |
| Digital marketing engine | 3x pipeline | 12 months | $6M |
| Customer success program | +10pp NRR | 12 months | $4M |

**Transformation Roadmap:**
- Q1: Pricing redesign, inside sales pilot
- Q2-Q3: PLG product launch, digital marketing scale
- Q4: Full rollout, customer success implementation

## Case 3: Supply Chain Resilience (Consumer Goods)

**Client:** Global CPG company
**Situation:** Supply disruptions during COVID-19 revealed vulnerabilities; need resilient supply chain

### BCG Supply Chain Assessment

**Vulnerability Analysis:**
| Risk Category | Current State | Risk Level |
|---------------|---------------|------------|
| Single-source suppliers | 45% of critical inputs | High |
| Geographic concentration | 60% from one region | High |
| Inventory buffers | 15 days average | Medium |
| Supplier visibility | 2 tiers deep | Medium |
| Digital integration | Limited | Medium |

**BCG Resilience Framework:**

**1. Segment Supply Chain Strategy**
- **Resilience-critical products:** Dual sourcing, strategic inventory
- **Efficiency-critical products:** Optimized for cost, lean inventory
- **Hybrid products:** Balanced approach based on risk profile

**2. Network Redesign**
- Regionalize supply chains to reduce geographic concentration
- Add 3 regional distribution centers
- Implement dynamic safety stock optimization

**3. Supplier Relationship Transformation**
- Reduce single-source dependencies from 45% to 20%
- Develop strategic partnerships with top 20 suppliers
- Implement supplier risk monitoring system

**4. Digital Enablement**
- End-to-end supply chain visibility platform
- AI-powered demand sensing
- Automated supplier risk alerts

**Financial Impact:**
- Resilience investment: $45M
- Annual cost increase: $12M (higher inventory, dual sourcing)
- Risk reduction: 60% lower disruption probability
- Payback: Business case justified on risk-adjusted NPV

## Case 5: Sustainability Strategy (Energy)

**Client:** Traditional energy company
**Situation:** Pressure to decarbonize; need net-zero strategy

### BCG Decarbonization Analysis

**Emissions Baseline:**
| Category | Emissions (MtCO2e) | % of Total |
|----------|-------------------|------------|
| Scope 1 (Direct) | 12.5 | 15% |
| Scope 2 (Energy) | 3.2 | 4% |
| Scope 3 (Value chain) | 68.3 | 81% |
| **Total** | **84.0** | **100%** |

**Abatement Cost Curve:**
| Initiative | Abatement (MtCO2e) | Cost ($/tCO2e) | Investment |
|------------|-------------------|----------------|------------|
| Renewable energy PPAs | 3.2 | -$15 (savings) | $50M |
| Operational efficiency | 2.1 | -$5 (savings) | $30M |
| Electrification | 1.8 | $25 | $80M |
| Carbon capture (pilot) | 0.5 | $80 | $120M |
| Nature-based offsets | 2.0 | $15 | $30M |
| Technology innovation | TBD | TBD | $200M |

**Strategic Options:**

**Option A: Compliance Path**
- Meet regulatory minimums
- Purchase offsets for remainder
- Investment: $100M
- Risk: Regulatory tightening, reputational damage

**Option B: Transition Leadership**
- Aggressive decarbonization of operations
- Strategic pivot to low-carbon businesses
- Investment: $800M over 10 years
- Upside: New revenue streams, premium valuation

**BCG Recommendation:** Option B with phased approach
- Phase 1 (Years 1-3): Operational decarbonization, quick wins
- Phase 2 (Years 4-7): Business model transformation
- Phase 3 (Years 8-10): Net-zero operations, new growth engines

## Case 6: Digital Platform Strategy (Retail)

**Client:** Traditional retailer
**Situation:** E-commerce growing 30% annually; need platform strategy

### BCG Digital Assessment

**Current State:**
- E-commerce: 8% of sales (industry: 25%+)
- Digital capabilities: Fragmented, legacy systems
- Customer data: Minimal integration across channels
- Mobile experience: Poor ratings (2.8 stars)

**Market Opportunity:**
- Digital commerce market: $2.5T by 2027
- Customer expectations: Seamless omnichannel
- Competitive threat: Digital-native retailers, marketplaces

**BCG Platform Strategy:**

**1. Unified Commerce Platform**
- Single view of customer across channels
- Real-time inventory visibility
- Flexible fulfillment options (BOPIS, ship-from-store)

**2. Personalization Engine**
- AI-powered product recommendations
- Dynamic pricing and promotions
- Personalized marketing automation

**3. Marketplace Expansion**
- Third-party seller platform
- Expanded assortment without inventory risk
- Commission revenue stream

**4. Data Monetization**
- Retail media network
- Insights services for CPG partners
- Data-as-a-service offerings

**Investment & Returns:**
| Initiative | Investment | Revenue Impact | Timeline |
|------------|------------|----------------|----------|
| Unified commerce | $40M | +$150M/year | 18 months |
| Personalization | $25M | +$80M/year | 12 months |
| Marketplace | $30M | +$200M GMV | 24 months |
| Retail media | $15M | +$50M/year | 18 months |
| **Total** | **$110M** | **+$480M/year** | **24 months** |

## Key Success Factors Across Cases

1. **Executive Sponsorship:** Visible C-suite commitment to transformation
2. **Data-Driven Insights:** Rigorous analysis, not just intuition
3. **Clear Metrics:** Well-defined KPIs and accountability
4. **Phased Approach:** Quick wins build momentum for larger initiatives
5. **Change Management:** Organizational readiness and capability building
6. **External Benchmarking:** Understanding best-in-class performance

## BCG Engagement Principles

1. **Customized Solutions:** No standard answers; tailored to client context
2. **Fact-Based:** Decisions grounded in data and analysis
3. **Action-Oriented:** Recommendations with clear implementation paths
4. **Collaborative:** Working shoulder-to-shoulder with client teams
5. **Measurable Impact:** Quantified value creation targets
