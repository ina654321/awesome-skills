## 1MDB Scandal Aftermath

### Overview
The 1Malaysia Development Berhad (1MDB) scandal involved the embezzlement of approximately $4.5 billion from Malaysia's sovereign wealth fund. Goldman Sachs helped arrange $6.5 billion in bond sales for 1MDB, earning $600 million in fees.

### Key Figures
- **Jho Low**: Malaysian financier, mastermind (at large)
- **Tim Leissner**: Former Goldman Southeast Asia Chairman (pleaded guilty)
- **Roger Ng**: Former Goldman Malaysia MD (convicted)

### Settlements (2020)

| Jurisdiction | Settlement Amount | Key Terms |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Malaysia** | $3.9 billion | Criminal charges dismissed |
| **United States (DOJ)** | $2.9 billion | Largest FCPA penalty ever |
| **United States (SEC)** | $400 million | Civil charges |
| **United States (Fed)** | $154 million | Cease-and-desist order |
| **New York (DFS)** | $150 million | Enhanced compliance |
| **Shareholders** | $79.5 million (2022) | Derivative lawsuit settlement |
| **Total** | **$7.9 billion+** | — |

### Compliance Reforms
- Enhanced due diligence for sovereign transactions
- Independent compliance monitors
- Expanded ESG and reputational risk reviews
- Stronger internal controls and documentation

## Consumer Banking History

### Marcus by Goldman Sachs (2016-2024)

**Launch:** April 2016
**Products:**
- Online savings accounts (high yield)
- Personal loans (unsecured)
- Certificates of deposit

**Challenges:**
- High customer acquisition costs
- Regulatory scrutiny
- Losses on Apple Card portfolio
- Strategic misalignment with core franchise

**Wind-down:** Announced 2023; substantially complete by 2024

### GreenSky Acquisition (2021-2024)

**Acquisition:** $2.24 billion all-stock deal (September 2021)
**Business:** Point-of-sale lending for home improvement
**Outcome:** Sold to consortium led by Sixth Street (2024)
**Losses:** $500M+ write-down; $0.19/share Q3 2024 hit

### Apple Card Partnership (2019-2024)

**Partnership:** Exclusive issuer of Apple Card (2019)
**Scale:** $17B+ loan portfolio at peak
**Challenges:** Losses, regulatory issues, customer service
**Transition:** Sold to JPMorgan Chase (2024)

- **Launched:** 2008
- **Mission:** Provide business education to underserved women entrepreneurs

## Strategic Priorities (2026+)

### 1. Global Banking & Markets
- Maintain #1 franchise in investment banking and trading
- Capitalize on cyclical upswing in M&A and capital markets
- Leverage AI for client onboarding, risk management, sales enablement

### 2. Asset & Wealth Management
- Scale AUM to $3 trillion+
- Grow alternatives platform (private equity, credit, real estate)
- Expand private wealth client base

### 3. Platform Solutions
- Complete exit from consumer banking
- Focus on transaction banking for corporate clients
- Rationalize non-core investments

### 4. Technology & Innovation
- Six workstreams for AI integration
- Data modernization and platform retooling
- Operational efficiency through automation

### 5. Talent & Culture
- Maintain <1% selectivity for campus hiring
- Invest in partner development and succession
- Preserve "one firm" collaborative culture
