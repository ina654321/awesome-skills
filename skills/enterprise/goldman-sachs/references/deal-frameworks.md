# Goldman Sachs Deal Frameworks

## M&A Advisory Frameworks

### Sell-Side Advisory Process

```
Phase 1: Preparation (Weeks 1-4)
├── Strategic rationale assessment
├── Value creation story development
├── Financial modeling and valuation
├── Information memorandum preparation
├── Management presentation development
└── Data room preparation (VDR setup)

Phase 2: Marketing (Weeks 5-8)
├── Buyer universe identification (strategic, financial)
├── Teaser distribution
├── NDA execution
├── Information memorandum distribution
├── Initial management meetings
└── Indication of Interest (IOI) collection

Phase 3: Due Diligence & Bidding (Weeks 9-16)
├── Confirmatory due diligence
├── Management presentations (detailed)
├── Site visits
├── Letter of Intent (LOI) / Bid submission
├── Bid evaluation and selection
└── Negotiation of key terms

Phase 4: Documentation & Closing (Weeks 17-24)
├── Definitive agreement negotiation
├── Financing arrangements (if applicable)
├── Regulatory approvals (HSR, CFIUS, etc.)
├── Third-party consents
├── Closing conditions satisfaction
└── Transaction closing
```

### Valuation Methodologies

#### 1. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

```
Free Cash Flow Calculation:
EBIT
- Taxes on EBIT
+ Depreciation & Amortization
- Capital Expenditures
- Change in Net Working Capital
= Unlevered Free Cash Flow

Terminal Value:
Perpetuity Growth: FCF(n) × (1 + g) / (WACC - g)
Exit Multiple: EBITDA(n) × Multiple

WACC Components:
Cost of Equity: Risk-free rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium
Cost of Debt: Yield to maturity × (1 - Tax rate)
Weights: Market value of equity / total capital
```

#### 2. Comparable Company Analysis

| Metric | Application | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **EV/EBITDA** | Enterprise value comparison | Capital structure differences |
| **P/E** | Equity value comparison | Profitable companies |
| **EV/Revenue** | Pre-profitability companies | High-growth sectors |
| **P/B** | Book value comparison | Financial institutions |

**Trading Multiples Adjustment:**
- Illiquidity discount: 15-30% for private companies
- Control premium: 20-40% for majority stakes
- Synergy value: Case-specific adjustment

#### 3. Precedent Transaction Analysis

| Transaction Attribute | Weight | Rationale |
|----------------------|--------|-----------|
| **Timing** | High | Recent transactions most relevant |
| **Similarity** | High | Same sector, size, geography |
| **Strategic vs. Financial** | Medium | Strategic buyers pay more |
| **Cycle Position** | Medium | Bull vs. bear market pricing |

### Defensive Strategies

| Defense | When to Use | Mechanism |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Poison Pill** | Hostile approach | Dilutes hostile acquirer |
| **Staggered Board** | Ongoing protection | Limits rapid control change |
| **Golden Parachute** | Retain management | Executive severance packages |
| **White Knight** | Preferred alternative | Friendly acquirer |
| **Pac-Man** | Strong counter-position | Target acquires bidder |
| **Asset Restructuring** | Break-up defense | Sell crown jewels |

## Capital Markets Frameworks

### IPO Process

```
Phase 1: Pre-IPO Preparation (6-12 months)
├── Financial statement audit (3 years)
├── SOX compliance implementation
├── Board composition (independent directors)
├── Management team strengthening
├── Corporate governance establishment
└── Internal controls assessment

Phase 2: Underwriter Selection (2-3 months)
├── RFP to investment banks
├── Pitch meetings
├── Economics negotiation (gross spread, greenshoe)
├── Syndicate formation (bookrunners, co-managers)
└── Engagement letter execution

Phase 3: Documentation (2-3 months)
├── S-1 registration statement drafting
├── SEC review and comment process
├── Roadshow presentation development
├── Research analyst education
└── Price range establishment

Phase 4: Marketing (2-3 weeks)
├── Research coverage initiation
├── Roadshow (management presentations)
├── Bookbuilding (institutional demand)
├── Price discovery and pricing
├── Allocation strategy
└── Trading debut and stabilization
```

### Debt Issuance Framework

#### Investment Grade Process

| Step | Timeline | Key Activities |
|------|----------|----------------|
| **Ratings Engagement** | 4-6 weeks pre | S&P, Moody's, Fitch presentations |
| **Investor Targeting** | 2-3 weeks pre | Identify real money accounts |
| **Roadshow** | 3-5 days | Credit story presentation |
| **Pricing** | 1 day | Book building, spread determination |
| **Settlement** | T+2 | Delivery of bonds, payment |

#### High Yield Process

**Additional Considerations:**
- Covenant package negotiation
- 144A vs. registered offering
- Call protection terms
- Change of control provisions

### Convertible Securities

| Structure | Conversion | Best For |
|-----------|------------|----------|

| **Mandatory Convertible** | Automatic conversion | Equity-like treatment |
| **Exchangeable** | Exchange for third-party shares | Monetize stake |
| **Contingent Convertible (CoCo)** | Trigger-based | Regulatory capital |

## Restructuring Frameworks

### Distressed Assessment Matrix

| Factor | Green | Yellow | Red |
|--------|-------|--------|-----|
| **Liquidity Runway** | >18 months | 12-18 months | <12 months |
| **Covenant Cushion** | >30% | 10-30% | <10% or breached |
| **Debt Maturity** | >3 years | 1-3 years | <1 year |
| **EBITDA Trend** | Growing | Flat | Declining |
| **Industry Outlook** | Favorable | Stable | Declining |

### Out-of-Court Restructuring

**Amend and Extend:**
- Extends maturity 2-3 years
- Minimal principal reduction
- May include fee/premium
- Covenant amendments

**Discounted Payoff (DPO):**
- Partial principal repayment
- Significant discount to par
- Full release of obligations
- Requires excess cash/liquidity

**Distressed Exchange:**
- Exchange old bonds for new
- New securities: longer maturity, lower coupon
- Often includes equity component
- Requires 90%+ participation

### Chapter 11 Restructuring

```
Pre-Filing (4-8 weeks)
├── Cash collateral motion
├── Critical vendor designation
├── Employee retention (KERP)
├── Stalking horse (363 sale option)
├── Restructuring Support Agreement (RSA)
└── DIP financing arrangement

First Day Motions
├── Cash management
├── Critical vendors
├── Utility assurance
├── Insurance maintenance
├── Employee wages/benefits
└── Professional retention

Plan Process (90-180 days)
├── Disclosure statement
├── Creditor classes identification
├── Plan classification
├── Voting solicitation
├── Confirmation hearing
└── Plan effective date
```

## Risk Management Frameworks

### Credit Risk Assessment

| Factor | Weight | Analysis |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Industry Risk** | 20% | Cyclicality, competition, regulation |
| **Business Risk** | 25% | Market position, diversification, mgmt |
| **Financial Risk** | 30% | Leverage, coverage, liquidity |
| **Structural Risk** | 15% | Security, covenants, guarantees |
| **Country Risk** | 10% | Sovereign, currency, transfer risk |

### Market Risk Measurement

**Value at Risk (VaR):**
```
Parametric VaR: Portfolio Value × Z-score × Portfolio Volatility
Historical VaR: Based on actual historical returns
Monte Carlo VaR: Simulation-based distribution
```

**Stress Testing Scenarios:**
| Scenario | Trigger | Impact |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **2008 Crisis** | Credit freeze | Credit spreads +500bps |
| **COVID-19** | Pandemic | Equity -35%, vol spikes |
| **Interest Rate Shock** | Fed surprise | Rates +300bps |
| **Sovereign Crisis** | Euro breakup | Contagion across assets |

### Operational Risk Categories

| Category | Examples | Mitigation |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **People** | Inadequate training, fraud | Background checks, segregation of duties |
| **Process** | Failed transactions, documentation errors | Checklists, automation, reconciliation |
| **Systems** | Hardware/software failures | Redundancy, disaster recovery |
| **External Events** | Natural disasters, terrorism | BCP, insurance, alternate sites |

## Cross-Border Transaction Frameworks

### CFIUS Considerations

| Factor | Assessment | Mitigation |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **National Security** | Technology, infrastructure, data | Mitigation agreements, security controls |
| **Critical Technology** | Export-controlled items | License compliance, technology controls |
| **Critical Infrastructure** | Energy, telecom, transportation | Operational continuity plans |
| **Sensitive Data** | Personal data of US citizens | Data localization, access controls |
| **Foreign Government** | State-owned enterprise | Board independence, information walls |

### Tax Structuring

| Structure | Application | Benefit |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Stock Purchase** | Simple acquisition | Tax basis step-up (limited) |
| **Asset Purchase** | Distressed situations | Full depreciation/amortization |
| **338(h)(10) Election** | S-corp acquisitions | Step-up with stock deal structure |
| **F Reorganization** | Cross-border | Tax-free restructuring |
| **Check-the-Box** | Hybrid entities | Flexibility in classification |
