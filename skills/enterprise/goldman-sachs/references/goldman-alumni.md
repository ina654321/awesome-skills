# Goldman Sachs Alumni Network

## Overview

Goldman Sachs alumni hold some of the most influential positions in global finance, government, and business. The firm's partnership culture and rigorous training produce leaders who shape markets and policy worldwide.

**Key Statistics:**
- **650+** alumni in C-suite roles at companies valued >$1B or AUM >$5B
- **45%** of current partners started as campus hires
- **3** former US Treasury Secretaries
- **Multiple** central bank governors and finance ministers

## Notable Trends in Alumni Careers

### Pathways to Government
Goldman alumni disproportionately represent finance in government:
- **3 of 4** Treasury Secretaries (1995-2021) were Goldman alumni
- Strong representation in central banks globally
- Regulatory agencies (SEC, CFTC, Fed) staffed with Goldman veterans

### Entrepreneurship
Many alumni found successful investment firms:
- **Private Equity**: JC Flowers, Oaktree (Howard Marks), Silver Lake (Jim Davidson)
- **Hedge Funds**: ESL Investments (Eddie Lampert), Canyon Capital
- **Fintech**: SoFi, OakNorth, Betterment alumni

### Academia
Strong presence in business schools:
- Harvard, Wharton, Columbia, Stanford GSB
- Teaching finance, leadership, ethics

### Board Service
Alumni serve on boards of:
- **S&P 500**: 150+ companies have Goldman alumni directors
- **Nonprofits**: Museums, universities, hospitals
- **Startups**: Advisory roles for portfolio companies

## Criticism & Controversy

### "Government Sachs"
Critics label the concentration of Goldman alumni in government as evidence of undue influence:
- Revolving door between Wall Street and Washington
- Regulatory capture concerns
- Policy favoring financial services

### Defense
Supporters argue:
- Goldman trains best technical talent for complex finance roles
- Expertise needed during financial crises
- Alumni serve diverse administrations (Democrat and Republican)
