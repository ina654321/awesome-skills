## § 8 · Standard Workflow

### 8.1 M&A Advisory Process

```
Phase 1: Strategic Assessment
├── Client objectives and constraints definition
├── Market landscape and competitive dynamics
├── Preliminary valuation and value creation thesis
├── Strategic alternatives evaluation (buy, sell, hold, merge)
└── Engagement terms and conflict clearance

Phase 2: Transaction Execution
├── Target/acquirer identification and approach
├── Due diligence coordination (financial, legal, commercial)
├── Valuation modeling (DCF, comparable, precedent transactions)
├── Negotiation strategy and tactics
└── Documentation and regulatory approvals

Phase 3: Financing & Structuring
├── Capital structure optimization
├── Financing arrangement (if applicable)
├── Tax and accounting structuring
├── Risk allocation and indemnification
└── Closing conditions and execution

Phase 4: Post-Transaction
├── Integration planning (for M&A)
├── Capital markets communication
├── Ongoing advisory relationship
└── Lessons learned and case study documentation
```

### 8.2 Capital Markets Issuance

```
Equity Offering Process:
├── Pre-marketing: Management presentations to research analysts
├── Bookbuilding: Investor demand assessment and price discovery
├── Pricing: Final offering size and price based on demand
├── Allocation: Institutional investor distribution strategy
└── Stabilization: Post-IPO market support (greenshoe)

Debt Offering Process:
├── Credit ratings engagement (S&P, Moody's, Fitch)
├── Investor targeting and roadshow
├── Pricing relative to benchmark curve
├── Covenants and documentation negotiation
└── Distribution to institutional and retail investors
```

### 8.3 Risk Management Framework

```
Credit Risk Assessment:
├── Counterparty credit analysis
├── Collateral and margin requirements
├── Netting and close-out arrangements
├── Credit default swap hedging
└── Concentration limits by sector and geography

Market Risk Management:
├── VaR (Value at Risk) daily calculation
├── Stress testing (historical and hypothetical)
├── Scenario analysis for tail events
├── Position limits and stop-losses
└── Correlation and diversification analysis
```
