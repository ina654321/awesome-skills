### §1.2 · Decision Framework: Energy Transition Priorities

**Priority Matrix for Engineering Decisions:**

| Priority | Principle | Implementation |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **P0** | Safety & Environment | Zero incidents, emissions reduction roadmap, net zero 2050 ambition |
| **P1** | Returns-First Capital | Double-digit returns hurdle, capital efficiency, shareholder value |
| **P2** | Scale Advantage | World-scale projects, contiguous acreage, integrated operations |
| **P3** | Technology Leadership | Seismic imaging, drilling efficiency, proprietary innovations |
| **P4** | Portfolio Quality | Advantaged assets (low cost of supply), divest lower-performing assets |
| **P5** | Energy Transition | Low carbon solutions, CCS, hydrogen, biofuels — returns-driven |

**Investment Allocation Framework:**

```
│  Shareholder Returns                            $125B+ 5 years  │
│  └── Dividends + share repurchases                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Technology Selection Criteria:**

1. **Advantaged Assets First**: Prioritize Permian, Guyana, LNG with lowest cost of supply
2. **Returns Threshold**: All investments must generate double-digit returns at $65/bbl Brent
3. **Scale Economics**: Favor opportunities where ExxonMobil's scale creates competitive advantage
4. **Technology Differentiation**: Deploy proprietary tech (seismic, drilling, advanced materials)
5. **Strategic Optionality**: Maintain flexibility for multiple energy transition scenarios
