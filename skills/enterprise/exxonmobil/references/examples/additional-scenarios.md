## Scenario 6: LNG Final Investment Decision

**Context:** Evaluate whether to proceed with final investment decision (FID) 
on the Mozambique Rovuma LNG project.

**ExxonMobil Engineering Approach:**

### Strategic Assessment

```
Project Overview:
├── Location: Offshore Mozambique, Area 4 block
├── Resource: 85+ Tcf natural gas discovered
├── Partners: ExxonMobil (operator), Eni, CNPC, Galp, KOGAS, ENH
├── Concept: Onshore LNG plant, 15+ MTPA capacity
├── Timeline: FID 2026, first cargo 2030
└── Investment: $25-30 billion

Strategic Rationale:
├── Supply diversity: Non-OPEC, non-Russian gas
├── Market proximity: Asian demand growth
├── Resource scale: World-class, 30+ year life
├── Partner quality: Strong technical capabilities
└── Timing: Aligns with LNG demand growth
```

### Economic Analysis

```
Base Case Economics (@ $10/MMBtu JKM, 2025 dollars):
├── Capacity: 15.2 MTPA (2 trains × 7.6 MTPA)
├── Plant cost: $15 billion ($1,000/tonne)
├── Upstream cost: $8 billion
├── Shipping: $2 billion
├── Total: $25 billion
├── NPV10: $12 billion
├── IRR: 18%
└── Payback: 8 years

Stress Testing:
├── $8/MMBtu: IRR 12% (marginal)
├── $6/MMBtu: IRR 6% (below hurdle)
├── $12/MMBtu: IRR 24% (strong)

Key Assumptions:
├── Construction: 5 years
├── Utilization: 95%
├── OPEX: $1.50/MMBtu
├── Financing: 60% debt at 6%
└── Fiscal terms: Stable PSA
```

### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Security/Insurgency** | Medium | High | Partner with government, security planning |
| **Construction Cost Overrun** | Medium | Medium | EPC contract, contingency |
| **LNG Price Decline** | Medium | High | Long-term offtake contracts |
| **Partner Issues** | Low | Medium | Strong governance, aligned interests |
| **Regulatory Delays** | Medium | Medium | Early engagement, local content plan |

### Recommendation

**PROCEED with FID** — Conditional on:
1. Finalizing offtake agreements (minimum 70% of capacity)
2. Security situation stabilization
3. EPC contract within budget
4. Partner alignment on final terms

## Scenario 7: Refinery Optimization

**Context:** Optimize product slate at Baytown refinery to maximize margins 
in changing market conditions (diesel shortage, gasoline surplus).

**ExxonMobil Engineering Approach:**

### Market Analysis

```
Product Price Environment:
├── Crude (WTI): $70/bbl
├── Gasoline: $85/bbl (weak demand)
├── Diesel: $110/bbl (strong demand, global shortage)
├── Jet Fuel: $105/bbl (recovery post-COVID)
├── Fuel Oil: $55/bbl (low value)
└── Cracks: Diesel +$40, Gasoline +$15
```

### Optimization Strategy

```
Current Operations (Baseline):
Input: 560,000 bpd crude
├── Gasoline: 45% yield (252,000 bpd)
├── Diesel: 30% yield (168,000 bpd)
├── Jet: 10% yield (56,000 bpd)
├── Fuel Oil: 8% yield (45,000 bpd)
└── Other: 7% (39,000 bpd)
Margin: $9/bbl crude = $5.0M/day

Optimized Operations:
Input: 560,000 bpd (same)
├── Maximize Diesel/Jet production
├── FCC severity increase
├── Hydrocracker to maximum diesel mode
├── Gasoline to aromatics/alkylation
└── Reduced fuel oil production

New Yields:
├── Gasoline: 35% (196,000 bpd) [-56,000]
├── Diesel: 38% (213,000 bpd) [+45,000]
├── Jet: 12% (67,000 bpd) [+11,000]
├── Fuel Oil: 6% (34,000 bpd) [-11,000]
└── Other: 9% (50,000 bpd)

New Margin: $12.50/bbl crude = $7.0M/day
Incremental Value: $2.0M/day = $730M/year
```

### Implementation

```
Short-term (Weeks 1-4):
├── Adjust FCC reactor temperature
├── Modify hydrocracker catalyst selectivity
├── Optimize crude slate (more heavy sour)
└── Reduce gasoline blending components

Medium-term (Months 2-6):
├── Catalyst change in hydrocracker
├── Revamp distillation tower operations
├── Alkylation unit optimization
└── Staff training on new operating modes

Constraints:
├── Product quality specifications
├── Emissions limits
├── Equipment reliability
└── Hydrogen balance
```

### Results

- **Margin improvement**: $2.0M/day
- **Annual value**: $730 million
- **Capital required**: $20 million (catalyst, minor modifications)
- **Payback**: 10 days

## Scenario 8: Water Management Strategy

**Context:** Develop comprehensive water management strategy for Permian Basin 
operations to minimize freshwater use and reduce costs.

**ExxonMobil Engineering Approach:**

### Current State Assessment

```
Permian Water Usage (Annual):
├── Total water needed: 15 million barrels
├── Freshwater current use: 30% (4.5 MMbbl)
├── Produced water recycling: 50% (7.5 MMbbl)
├── Brackish water: 20% (3.0 MMbbl)
├── Disposal: 8 MMbbl produced water
└── Cost: $2.50/bbl blended

Challenges:
├── Freshwater scarcity
├── Disposal capacity constraints
├── Trucking costs rising
├── Regulatory scrutiny on seismicity
└── ESG pressure to reduce freshwater
```

### Strategy Development

```
Target State (3-year horizon):
├── Freshwater use: <10% (1.5 MMbbl)
├── Produced water recycling: 70% (10.5 MMbbl)
├── Brackish water: 20% (3.0 MMbbl)
├── Zero discharge to disposal (reuse all)
└── Cost: $1.80/bbl blended

Infrastructure Plan:
├── Central water treatment facilities: 3 locations
├── Pipeline network: 200 miles
├── Storage: 2 million barrels capacity
├── Treatment capacity: 100,000 bpd
└── Investment: $300 million
```

### Economics

```
Cost Comparison:

Current (Trucking + Freshwater):
├── Freshwater purchase: $1.50/bbl
├── Trucking: $3.00/bbl
├── Treatment: $0.50/bbl
└── Total: $5.00/bbl for 30% of needs

Future (Pipeline + Recycling):
├── Brackish water: $0.50/bbl
├── Recycling: $1.00/bbl
├── Pipeline transport: $0.30/bbl
└── Total: $1.80/bbl for 90% of needs

Savings:
├── Volume: 13.5 MMbbl/year (90% of needs)
├── Savings: $3.20/bbl
├── Annual savings: $43 million
├── Investment: $300 million
└── Simple payback: 7 years
```

### Additional Benefits

- **ESG**: Freshwater reduction aligns with sustainability goals
- **Regulatory**: Reduced trucking reduces road wear, accidents
- **Seismicity**: Reduced disposal injection reduces induced seismicity risk
- **Reliability**: Pipeline vs. trucking reduces weather/operational risk

## Scenario 10: Capital Reallocation Decision

**Context:** Oil price has dropped to $50/bbl. Review capital program and 
reallocate to maintain returns and shareholder distributions.

**ExxonMobil Engineering Approach:**

### Portfolio Review at $50/bbl

```
Project Viability at Reduced Prices:

TIER 1: PROCEED (Returns >15% IRR at $50/bbl)
├── Permian Basin development
│   ├── Break-even: $35/bbl
│   ├── IRR at $50: 25%+
│   └── Decision: ACCELERATE (lowest cost supply)
│
├── Guyana developments
│   ├── Break-even: $28/bbl
│   ├── IRR at $50: 30%+
│   └── Decision: MAINTAIN (world-class returns)
│
└── LNG projects (Golden Pass, Qatar)
    ├── Break-even: $6/MMBtu (oil-linked)
    ├── IRR at current: 20%+
    └── Decision: MAINTAIN

TIER 2: MODERATE (Returns 10-15% IRR at $50/bbl)
├── Select Permian infill drilling
├── Optimization projects
└── Decision: REDUCE PACE, high-grade only

TIER 3: DEFER (Returns <10% IRR at $50/bbl)
├── International exploration (non-core)
├── Marginal development projects
├── Low carbon projects (policy-dependent)
└── Decision: PAUSE, preserve optionality
```

### Revised Capital Program

```
Original Plan (at $65/bbl):
├── Upstream growth: $22B/year
├── Base business: $5B/year
├── Low carbon: $4B/year
└── Total: $31B/year

Revised Plan (at $50/bbl):
├── Permian acceleration: $15B/year (+$3B)
├── Guyana maintain: $8B/year (-$2B)
├── Base business: $5B/year (unchanged)
├── Other upstream: $2B/year (-$3B)
├── Low carbon: $2B/year (-$2B, policy dependent)
└── Total: $32B/year (reallocated, not cut)

Shareholder Returns: MAINTAINED
├── Dividend: $15B/year (growing)
├── Buybacks: $15B/year (reduced from $20B)
└── Total: $30B/year
```

### Risk Management

```
Flexibility Preserved:
├── Short-cycle projects (Permian): Can adjust within 6 months
├── Long-lead items: Minimal commitments
├── Optionality: Major projects not yet sanctioned
└── Balance sheet: Strong, no need to cut dividends

Contingency Plans:
├── If $40/bbl sustained: Reduce buybacks further
├── If $60/bbl returns: Resume original program
└── If $70/bbl+: Accelerate all priorities
```

### Communication Strategy

```
Key Messages:
1. Returns-focused capital allocation maintained
2. Advantaged assets (Permian, Guyana) prioritized
3. Shareholder returns protected
4. Flexibility preserved for price recovery
5. Long-term strategy unchanged
```
