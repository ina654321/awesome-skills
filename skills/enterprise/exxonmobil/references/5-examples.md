## §5 · Examples

### §5.1 · Permian Basin Development Optimization

**Context:** Evaluate a new development area in the Midland Basin portion of the Permian.

**ExxonMobil Engineering Approach:**

**Phase 1: Resource Assessment**
```
Geologic Analysis:
- Target Formation: Lower Spraberry, Wolfcamp A, Wolfcamp B
- Estimated EUR per well: 1.2-1.8 MMboe (based on analogs)
- Recovery factor: ~8% primary, potential for 12%+ with infill
- Contiguous acreage: 15,000 net acres (post-Pioneer style)

Seismic Evaluation:
- 3D seismic survey: 100 sq mi
- Proprietary imaging algorithms for fracture detection
- DUC (drilled uncompleted) inventory analysis
```

**Phase 2: Development Planning**
```
Pad Design:
- 12-well pads (standard for scale efficiency)
- 3-mile laterals where possible
- 660-1,000 ft inter-well spacing
- Stacked pay development (3-4 targets)

Economics (at $65/bbl WTI):
- Well cost: $8-10 million (drill & complete)
- IP30: 1,500-2,500 BOE/d per well
- IRR: 25-35% (double-digit hurdle exceeded)
- Payout: 18-24 months

Scale Economics:
- 150-200 wells/year development pace
- Dedicated frac crews (2-3)
- Water recycling infrastructure
- Centralized facilities
```

**Phase 3: Technology Deployment**
```
Drilling Optimization:
- Rotary steerable systems for precise wellbore placement
- Real-time geosteering (gamma ray, resistivity)
- Longer laterals: 12,000-15,000 ft (vs. 7,500 ft industry avg)

Completions Enhancement:
- 50+ stage fracs with 2,000+ lbs proppant/ft
- Precision landing in target zone
- Flowback optimization for EUR maximization

Digital Integration:
- Real-time production monitoring
- Predictive analytics for artificial lift optimization
- Automated well testing and allocation
```

**Expected Outcome:** 
- 150-200 wells/year, 200+ MMboe cumulative production
- $2-3 billion annual capital program
- 25%+ blended returns, consistent with Pioneer synergies

### §5.2 · Guyana Deepwater Field Development

**Context:** Plan development for a new discovery in the Stabroek Block, offshore Guyana.

**ExxonMobil Engineering Approach:**

**Phase 1: Subsurface Evaluation**
```
Reservoir Characterization:
- Discovery: Hammerhead (latest in string of successes)
- Water depth: 4,000-6,000 ft
- Reservoir quality: community-permeability turbidite sands
- Oil quality: community-32° API, low GOR
- Estimated recoverable: 400-600 MMbbl

Appraisal Program:
- 3-4 appraisal wells
- Extended well testing for productivity validation
- Core and fluid sampling
- 4D seismic planning for reservoir monitoring
```

**Phase 2: Development Concept**
```
Facility Selection:
- FPSO (Floating Production Storage Offloading) vessel
- Capacity: 200,000+ bpd oil, 400+ MMcfd gas handling
- Storage: 2+ million barrels
- Design life: 30 years

Subsea Architecture:
- 40-60 subsea wells (producers, injectors, gas handlers)
- Drill centers: 3-4 locations
- Subsea tiebacks: 5-10 km from FPSO
- Water injection for pressure maintenance

Execution Strategy:
- EPCIC contract (Engineering, Procurement, Construction, Installation, Commissioning)
- Modular construction approach
- Hull construction: 24-30 months
- Topsides integration: 12-18 months
- First oil target: 48 months from FID
```

**Phase 3: Economics & Risk**
```
Capital Estimate:
- FPSO: $1.5-2.0 billion
- Subsea: $1.0-1.5 billion
- Drilling: $1.0-1.5 billion
- Total: $3.5-5.0 billion development cost

Economics (at $65/bbl):
- Peak production: 200,000+ bpd
- Unit operating cost: <$10/bbl
- Project IRR: 20-25%+
- NPV (10%): $2-4 billion

Risk Mitigation:
- Staged development (phased investment)
- Proven reservoir performance (analogs from Liza, Payara)
- Contractual protections (PSC terms)
- Partner diversification (Exxon 45%, Hess 30%, CNOOC 25%)
```

**Outcome:** Another world-class development adding 200+ MMbbl recoverable, 
1.7 MMb/d total block capacity by 2030.

### §5.3 · Low Carbon CCS Hub Development

**Context:** Design a Gulf Coast CCS hub to capture and store CO₂ from multiple industrial sources.

**ExxonMobil Engineering Approach:**

**Phase 1: Market Assessment**
```
Customer Analysis:
- Target emitters: Refineries, chemical plants, power generation
- CO₂ volumes: 10-50 Mtpa total addressable market
- Customer willingness to pay: $40-80/tonne (policy dependent)
- Contract structures: Long-term offtake agreements

Policy Environment:
- 45Q tax credits: $85/tonne for sequestration
- California LCFS: Credit value for low-carbon fuels
- EU CBAM: Border carbon adjustments driving demand
- State regulations: CCS mandates in development
```

**Phase 2: Infrastructure Design**
```
Capture Facilities:
- Post-combustion capture (amine-based)
- Pre-combustion capture (gasification)
- Industrial source capture (cement, steel)
- Direct air capture (future optionality)

Transport Network:
- CO₂ pipeline network: 100-500 miles
- Pipeline specifications: Dense phase, corrosion-resistant
- Hub-and-spoke design
- Injection site aggregation

Storage Sites:
- Saline formations: 10,000+ ft depth
- Storage capacity: 1-10 billion tonnes
- Monitoring: Seismic, wellhead, atmospheric
- EPA Class VI permits (wells)
```

**Phase 3: Commercial Structure**
```
Business Model:
- Tolling arrangement: $50-70/tonne CO₂ captured and stored
- Take-or-pay contracts: 10-20 year terms
- Anchor customers: 3-5 large industrial emitters
- Risk allocation: Capture (customer), transport/storage (ExxonMobil)

Capital Requirements:
- Capture equipment: Customer-funded or ExxonMobil investment
- Pipeline network: $1-3 billion
- Storage wells: $100-500 million
- Total hub investment: $2-5 billion

Returns Analysis:
- Revenue: $500M-1.5B/year at full capacity
- OPEX: $200-400M/year
- Project IRR: 12-18% (returns-focused)
- Payback: 8-12 years
```

**Outcome:** First end-to-end CCS system operational, 9 Mtpa contracted, 
path to 30 Mtpa by 2030, $3B+ earnings potential by 2040.

### §5.5 · Energy Transition Capital Allocation

**Context:** Allocate capital between hydrocarbon growth and low carbon investments.

**ExxonMobil Engineering Approach:**

**Phase 1: Strategic Framework**
```
Investment Principles:
1. All investments must generate double-digit returns
2. Maintain optionality across energy transition scenarios
3. Focus on areas where ExxonMobil has competitive advantage
4. Capital discipline: Stay within $27-29B annual upstream budget
5. Low carbon: $20B (2025-2030), returns-dependent expansion

Portfolio Balance:
- Hydrocarbons: Core cash generation, dividend support
- Low Carbon: Growth option, policy-dependent scaling
- Dividends: Growing shareholder returns priority
- Buybacks: Flexibility for commodity price cycles
```

**Phase 2: Investment Priorities**
```
Tier 1: Advantaged Hydrocarbons (70% of capital)
├── Permian Basin: Double production to 2.3-2.5 MMboe/d by 2030
├── Guyana: 8 projects, 1.7 MMb/d capacity
├── LNG: 40+ MTPA by 2030
└── Target: 5.4-5.5 MMboe/d total production

Tier 2: Low Carbon Solutions (20% of capital)
├── CCS: 30 Mtpa by 2030, $3B earnings potential
├── Hydrogen: Baytown facility, 45V dependent
├── Biofuels: SAF, renewable diesel
├── Proxxima: $100B+ market by 2030
└── Lithium: Arkansas extraction demo

Tier 3: Maintenance & Optionality (10%)
├── Base business sustaining capital
├── Technology R&D
├── Exploration (Brazil, Guyana expansion)
└── Strategic option preservation
```

**Phase 3: Decision Criteria**
```
Returns Analysis:
Metric            │ Hydrocarbon │ Low Carbon │ Hurdle
──────────────────┼─────────────┼────────────┼───────
Project IRR       │ 20-35%      │ 12-18%     │ >10%
Full-cycle return │ 15-25%      │ 10-15%     │ >8%
Payback period    │ 2-5 years   │ 8-15 years │ <15 yrs
Strategic value   │ Cash flow   │ Optionality│ High

Risk Assessment:
- Hydrocarbon: Commodity price, regulatory, execution
- Low Carbon: Policy, technology, market formation
- Mitigation: Portfolio diversification, staged investments
```

**Decision Output:**
- 2025-2030: $140B+ major projects (mostly hydrocarbon)
- Low carbon: $20B, paced by returns and policy
- Shareholder returns: $125B+ (5 years)
- Flexibility: Reduce growth capital if prices decline
