# Kering Maison Profiles

> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-21

---

## Gucci

### Overview
- **Position:** Kering's largest Maison (~40% of revenue)
- **Founded:** 1921, Florence
- **Creative Director:** Demna (appointed 2025)
- **CEO:** Francesca Bellettini
- **Revenue 2025:** €6.0B (down 22%)
- **Operating Margin:** 16.1%

### Brand DNA
- Italian luxury with attitude
- GG monogram heritage
- Horsebit iconography
- Red and green web stripe

### Key Products
- Handbags (Dionysus, GG Marmont, Bamboo)
- Leather goods
- Ready-to-wear
- Shoes

### Turnaround Strategy
- Creative reset with Demna
- Return to logo-centric design
- Focus on leather goods
- Distribution cleanup

## Yves Saint Laurent

### Overview
- **Position:** Second largest Maison (~18% of revenue)
- **Founded:** 1961, Paris
- **Creative Director:** Anthony Vaccarello
- **Revenue 2025:** €2.6B (down 8%)
- **Operating Margin:** 20%

### Brand DNA
- Parisian rebel luxury
- Le Smoking tuxedo
- Bold, empowered femininity
- Rock 'n' roll attitude

### Key Products
- Handbags (Kate, Loulou, Sac de Jour)
- Ready-to-wear
- Shoes
- Beauty (transitioning from Coty)

### Strategy
- Maintain creative stability
- Expand beauty when license returns
- Asia growth opportunity

## Bottega Veneta

### Overview
- **Position:** Third largest Maison (~12% of revenue)
- **Founded:** 1966, Vicenza
- **Creative Director:** Matthieu Blazy
- **Revenue 2025:** €1.7B (up 3%)
- **Operating Margin:** 15.6%

### Brand DNA
- Italian craftsmanship
- Intrecciato weave
- Quiet luxury leader
- "When your own initials are enough"

### Key Products
- Leather goods
- Shoes
- Ready-to-wear
- Home collection

### Strategy
- Protect exclusivity
- Expand carefully
- Leverage "quiet luxury" trend
