# BP Risk Framework

## Risk Categories

### 1. Strategic Risks

#### Energy Transition Uncertainty
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Risk** | Pace and shape of energy transition unknown |
| **Impact** | Stranded assets if transition accelerates; missed opportunities if delayed |
| **Mitigation** | Reset strategy maintains optionality; selective transition investments |
| **Current Status** | Positioned for Current Trajectory; vulnerable to Accelerated Transition |

#### Strategy Execution Risk
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Risk** | Leadership changes create execution uncertainty |
| **Impact** | Failure to deliver reset strategy; continued underperformance |
| **Mitigation** | O'Neill appointment brings external discipline; advisory role for Auchincloss |
| **Current Status** | Transition period adds near-term uncertainty |

#### Competitive Positioning
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Risk** | Continued market share and valuation decline vs peers |
| **Impact** | Acquisition vulnerability; investor flight |
| **Mitigation** | Divestments, cost reduction, operational improvements |
| **Current Status** | Gap remains significant; reset aims to close it |

### 2. Operational Risks

#### Major Incident Risk
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|

| **Current Status** | Whiting refinery issues in 2024; improved to 96%+ |

| Aspect | Detail |

| **Current Status** | ACE platform demonstrates modern remote operations capability |

#### Project Execution
| Aspect | Detail |

### 3. Financial Risks

#### Commodity Price Volatility
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|

| **Impact** | Reduced earnings; dividend risk; divestment pressure |

| **Current Status** | Break-even ~$40/bbl; well-positioned for price cycles |

| Aspect | Detail |

#### Pension Obligations
| Aspect | Detail |

## Risk Mitigation Priorities

### Immediate (2025-2026)
1. Execute divestment program ($20B)
2. Reduce gearing to ~20%
3. Improve operational reliability to 96%+
4. Achieve cost reduction targets ($2B)

### Medium-term (2026-2030)
1. Deliver EBITDA growth to $55B+
2. Complete leadership transition successfully
3. Maintain strategic consistency
4. Build transition optionality without over-investment

### Long-term (2030+)
1. Position for multiple energy scenarios
2. Maintain competitive returns vs peers
3. Preserve financial flexibility
4. Retain "license to operate"
