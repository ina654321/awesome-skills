# Biogen Product Portfolio - Detailed Reference

## Multiple Sclerosis Franchise

### TYSABRI (natalizumab)
**Overview:** High-efficacy monoclonal antibody for relapsing-remitting MS

**Mechanism:** Anti-α4 integrin monoclonal antibody
- Blocks lymphocyte migration across blood-brain barrier
- Reduces inflammatory activity in CNS

**Efficacy:**
- 68% reduction in annualized relapse rate (AFFIRM study)
- 92% reduction in new/enlarging T2 lesions
- 80% reduction in Gd-enhancing lesions

**Safety Profile:**
- **PML Risk:** Primary concern; risk stratified by JC virus antibody status
  - JCV-negative: <1/10,000 risk
  - JCV-positive, prior immunosuppressants: ~11/1,000
  - JCV-positive, no prior IS: ~4/1,000
- TOUCH Prescribing Program required

**Dosing:** 300mg IV every 4 weeks

**2024 Financials:**
- Revenue: ~$1.6B
- Trend: Stable, maintaining premium positioning

**Competitive Position:**
- **Advantages:** Highest efficacy in injectable/oral MS therapies
- **Disadvantages:** PML risk, IV administration vs. oral competitors
- **Vs. Ocrevus:** TYSABRI higher efficacy but more frequent dosing; Ocrevus safer profile

### VUMERITY (diroximel fumarate)
**Overview:** Oral fumarate therapy for relapsing MS

**Mechanism:** Prodrug of monomethyl fumarate (MMF)
- Nrf2 pathway activation
- Anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects

**Efficacy:**
- 86% reduction in new/enlarging T2 lesions (EVOLVE-MS-1)
- Similar efficacy to TECFIDERA with improved GI tolerability

**Advantages vs. TECFIDERA:**
- Significantly fewer GI side effects (due to chemical structure)
- Improved patient adherence
- Lower discontinuation rates

**Dosing:** 462mg twice daily (maintenance)

**2024 Financials:**
- Revenue: ~$700M
- Trend: Growing despite TECFIDERA erosion

---

### TECFIDERA (dimethyl fumarate)
**Overview:** First-generation oral fumarate for relapsing MS

**Status:** Facing generic erosion
- US: Generic entry 2020
- EU: Market protection until February 2025

**Mechanism:** Same as VUMERITY (Nrf2 pathway)

**2024 Financials:**
- Revenue: ~$900M (down significantly from peak ~$4B)
- Q4 2025: $112M (51% YoY decline)

**Strategy:** Managed decline; patients switching to VUMERITY or other therapies

## Growth Products

### LEQEMBI (lecanemab-irmb)
**Overview:** Anti-amyloid monoclonal antibody for early Alzheimer's disease

**Mechanism:**
- Binds to soluble amyloid-beta protofibrils and plaque
- Dual action: reduces protofibrils (neurotoxic) and clears plaque
- **Differentiation:** Targets protofibrils (most toxic Aβ form) vs. competitors

**Clinical Data (CLARITY AD):**
- 27% slowing of cognitive decline (CDR-SB) at 18 months
- 59% reduction in amyloid plaque (PET)
- Time savings: Up to 8.3 years delay in progression (modeling)

**Safety:**
- **ARIA-E (edema):** 12.6% (vs. 1.7% placebo)
- **ARIA-H (hemorrhage):** 17.3% (vs. 9.0% placebo)
- **Infusion reactions:** 26.4% (first dose)
- Boxed warning for ARIA

**Dosing:**
- **IV Initiation:** 10mg/kg bi-weekly for 18 months
- **IV Maintenance:** 10mg/kg monthly after 18 months
- **SC-AI (IQLIK):** Weekly subcutaneous maintenance (approved August 2025); initiation PDUFA May 2026

**Commercial Performance:**
- 2025 Biogen share: ~$1B (50% of global sales)
- Market share: >60% of anti-amyloid market
- Approved in: US, Japan, China, EU, UK, South Korea, Israel, UAE

**Partnership:**
- Eisai leads development; Biogen/Eisai co-commercialize
- 50% profit share in US, Japan, China

### SKYCLARYS (omaveloxolone)
**Overview:** First and only approved treatment for Friedreich's ataxia

**Mechanism:**
- Nrf2 pathway activator
- Addresses mitochondrial dysfunction in FA
- Increases frataxin levels

**Clinical Data (MOXIe):**
- mFARS score improvement vs. placebo (nominal significance)
- Cardiomyopathy benefits (secondary endpoint)

**Dosing:** 150mg once daily (fasting)

**Safety:**
- Elevated LDL cholesterol (manageable with statins)
- QT prolongation (monitoring required)

**Commercial Performance:**
- 2025 Revenue: $382M (30% YoY growth)
- Patients: ~2,000+ globally (nearly doubled from 2023)
- Availability: US (2023), EU (2024), 34+ markets

**Lifecycle Expansion:**
- Pediatric Phase 3: Ages 2-16 initiated 2025
- Potential to double addressable population

**Acquisition:** Acquired through Reata acquisition

### ZURZUVAE (zuranolone)
**Overview:** First oral treatment for postpartum depression (PPD)

**Mechanism:**
- GABA-A receptor positive allosteric modulator
- Rapid onset antidepressant effect
- Neuroactive steroid mechanism

**Clinical Data:**
- ROBIN Study: Significant HAMD-17 improvement at Day 15
- Rapid onset: Effects seen within 3 days
- 14-day treatment course

**Dosing:** 50mg once daily for 14 days

**Safety:**
- Sedation/drowsiness (most common)
- Suicidal ideation warning
- REMS program required

**Commercial Performance:**
- 2025 Revenue: $131M
- Launch: December 2023
- Trend: Strong patient demand; first-line use increasing

**Partnership:**
- Developed with Sage Therapeutics; commercialized with Supernus
- 50/50 US profit share
- Biogen ex-US rights (ex-Japan, Taiwan, South Korea)

---

### QALSODY (tofersen)
**Overview:** First targeted treatment for SOD1-ALS (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis)

**Mechanism:**
- Antisense oligonucleotide (ASO)
- Binds to SOD1 mRNA, reducing toxic SOD1 protein
- Addresses genetic cause in ~2% of ALS patients

**Clinical Data (VALOR):**
- Significant CSF SOD1 reduction
- Trends in clinical function preservation
- Open-label extension shows survival benefit

**Dosing:** 100mg intrathecal injection every 14 days

**Safety:**
- CSF pleocytosis (monitoring required)
- Myelitis (rare, serious)

**Commercial Performance:**
- 2025 Revenue: $32M
- Limited by: Genetic testing requirement, small addressable population (~400 US patients)
- EU approved 2024

**Partnership:** Licensed from Ionis Pharmaceuticals

---

### SPINRAZA (nusinersen)
**Overview:** First approved treatment for spinal muscular atrophy (SMA)

**Mechanism:**
- Antisense oligonucleotide (ASO)
- Modifies SMN2 pre-mRNA splicing
- Increases functional SMN protein

**Clinical Data:**
- ENDEAR (infantile-onset): 47% survival vs. 0% historical control
- CHERISH (later-onset): Motor function improvement

**Dosing:**
- Loading: 4 doses over 2 months
- Maintenance: Every 4 months
- **New:** High-dose regimen (28mg vs. 12mg) approved Japan/EU 2025; US PDUFA April 2026

**Commercial Performance:**
- 2025 Revenue: $1.5B+ (mature product)
- Facing competition: Zolgensma (gene therapy), Evrysdi (oral)
- Strategy: High-dose regimen for competitive positioning

**Partnership:** Licensed from Ionis Pharmaceuticals

## Discontinued Products

### ADUHELM (aducanumab)
**History:**
- Approved June 2021 via accelerated pathway
- Controversial approval: conflicting Phase 3 data, FDA advisory committee opposition
- CMS restricted coverage (only clinical trials)
- **Withdrawn:** 2024 (company decision)

**Lessons Learned:**
- Importance of clear clinical benefit demonstration
- Payer/reimbursement alignment critical
- Reputation impact drove management changes
