# Biogen Partnerships & Collaborations - Detailed Reference

## Strategic Partnership Overview

Biogen's business model relies heavily on strategic partnerships to access external innovation, share development risk, and leverage commercial infrastructure. This document details the major collaborations that drive Biogen's current portfolio and pipeline.

---

## Eisai Co., Ltd. - Alzheimer's Disease Collaboration

### Partnership Overview
**Status:** Active (2014-present, expanded 2022-2023)  
**Primary Asset:** LEQEMBI (lecanemab)  
**Deal Value:** Multi-billion dollar potential

### Structure & Terms

#### Development & Commercialization
| Aspect | Arrangement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Development Lead** | Eisai (global) |
| **Commercialization** | Co-promotion in US, Japan, China |
| **Profit Sharing** | 50/50 in co-promotion territories |
| **Decision Rights** | Eisai has final decision authority |
| **Manufacturing** | Shared responsibilities |

#### Financial Terms
| Component | Terms |
|-----------|-------|
| **Upfront** | Not publicly disclosed (research collaboration) |
| **Milestones** | Various development/regulatory milestones |
| **Royalties** | 50% profit share (not royalty-based) |
| **Commercial Split** | Equal sharing of costs and profits |

### Strategic Rationale

**For Biogen:**
- Access to late-stage Alzheimer's asset after Aduhelm challenges
- Eisai's Alzheimer's expertise and Japanese market presence
- Shared commercial investment reduces risk

**For Eisai:**
- Biogen's US commercial infrastructure
- Biogen's neuroscience credibility
- Shared development costs for global program

### Collaboration Evolution

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 2014 | Initial research collaboration on Alzheimer's |
| 2017 | Phase 3 commitment for lecanemab |
| 2022 | Full FDA approval for LEQEMBI |
| 2023 | CMS coverage achieved |
| 2024 | EU approval; China approval |
| 2025 | SC-AI maintenance approval; initiation PDUFA May 2026 |

### Governance
- Joint steering committee
- Joint commercialization committee
- Eisai maintains final decision rights on regulatory and development matters

## Ionis Pharmaceuticals - Antisense Oligonucleotide Platform

### Partnership Overview
**Status:** Active (multi-program collaboration)  
**Primary Assets:** SPINRAZA, QALSODY, salanersen  
**Deal Type:** Licensing with milestones and royalties

### SPINRAZA (Nusinersen) Agreement

#### Structure
| Aspect | Arrangement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Licensor** | Ionis Pharmaceuticals |
| **Licensee** | Biogen |
| **Territory** | Global |
| **Commercial Rights** | Biogen exclusive |

#### Financial Terms
| Component | Terms |
|-----------|-------|
| **Upfront** | $75M (2012) |
| **Milestones** | Paid through development/approval |
| **Royalties** | Tiered (reported as 8-15% range) |
| **Development Costs** | Biogen bears post-licensing costs |

### QALSODY (Tofersen) Agreement

#### Structure
- Same framework as SPINRAZA
- Licensed for SOD1-ALS
- Global commercial rights to Biogen

### Salanersen Agreement

#### Status
- Phase 1b/3 development
- Next-generation SMA ASO
- Same commercial framework

### Strategic Rationale

**For Biogen:**
- Access to leading ASO technology platform
- Validated delivery to CNS via intrathecal injection
- Multiple shots on goal (SMA, ALS, potentially Alzheimer's)

**For Ionis:**
- Monetization of platform technology
- Biogen's commercial infrastructure in rare diseases
- Risk-sharing on late-stage development

### Key Success Factors
- SPINRAZA peak sales >$2B annually
- QALSODY: First targeted ALS therapy
- Platform expansion to BIIB080 (tau ASO for Alzheimer's)

## Sage Therapeutics / Supernus Pharmaceuticals - ZURZUVAE

### Partnership Overview
**Status:** Active (2018-present; Supernus joined 2024)  
**Primary Asset:** ZURZUVAE (zuranolone) for postpartum depression  
**Deal Value:** $1.0B+ potential

### Structure & Terms

#### Original Sage Partnership (2018)
| Aspect | Arrangement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Upfront** | $875M ($575M cash + $300M equity) |
| **Milestones** | Up to $448M development milestones |
| **Commercial Split** | 50/50 profit share US; Biogen ex-US |

#### Supernus Collaboration (2024)
- Supernus acquired commercial rights from Sage
- Biogen/Supernus 50/50 US profit share
- Biogen retains ex-US rights (ex-Japan, Taiwan, South Korea)

### Commercial Responsibilities
| Territory | Commercial Lead | Economics |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **US** | Biogen + Supernus | 50/50 profit share |
| **Europe** | Biogen | Biogen records sales |
| **Japan/Taiwan/S. Korea** | Supernus | Biogen receives royalty |

### Strategic Rationale

**For Biogen:**
- Entry into neuropsychiatry (new therapeutic area)
- First-in-class oral PPD treatment
- Platform potential for other GABA-A indications (MDD - discontinued)

**For Sage/Supernus:**
- Monetization of zuranolone asset
- Shared commercial investment
- Supernus CNS commercial expertise

## Partnership Strategy Framework

### Biogen's Partnership Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Assessment |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Scientific Rationale** | 30% | Strong mechanism validation |
| **Strategic Fit** | 25% | Alignment with neuroscience focus |
| **Stage of Development** | 20% | Preference for Phase 2-3 |
| **Commercial Rights** | 15% | US rights highly valued |
| **Financial Terms** | 10% | ROI and risk-sharing balance |

### Partnership Evolution (2019-2025)

| Period | Strategy | Key Deals |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| 2019-2021 | Broad research partnerships | Multiple early-stage |
| 2022-2023 | Late-stage focus | Reata acquisition, Eisai expansion |
| 2024-2025 | Targeted bolt-ons | Alcyone, Stoke |

### Future Partnership Priorities
1. **Neurology:** Movement disorders, genetic diseases
2. **Immunology:** Late-stage assets (complement litifilimab)
3. **Rare Disease:** Gene therapy, novel modalities
4. **Technology:** ASO delivery, protein degradation
