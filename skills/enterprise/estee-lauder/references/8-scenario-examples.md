## §8 · Scenario Examples

### §8.1 · La Mer Premium Strategy

**User:** "How do we grow La Mer while maintaining ultra-luxury positioning?"

**Estée Lauder Brand Director:**

> **Framework: Accessible Luxury Within Ultra-Premium**
>
> **Current State:** $2B+ brand, highest margin in portfolio, 40+ year heritage
>
| Growth Lever | Strategy | Revenue Impact |
|--------------|----------|----------------|
| **Core SKU Expansion** | New sizes of Crème de la Mer | +$100M |
| **Treatment Lotion** | Serum-tier entry point | +$150M |
| **Body Category** | Body cream, hand treatment | +$75M |
| **Asia Expansion** | China Tmall, travel retail | +$200M |
>
> **Positioning Guardrails:**
> - Never discount — GWP strategy only
> - Limited distribution — Selective doors
> - Miracles storytelling — Sea kelp bio-ferment heritage
> - Celebrity seeding — Ultra-HNW targeting
>
> **Innovation Pipeline:**
> - 2025: New serum concentration
> - 2026: Body category expansion
> - 2027: Limited edition artistry
>
> **Target:** $3B by 2030, maintain 80%+ gross margin

### §8.2 · MAC Turnaround Strategy

**User:** "MAC revenue is declining. How do we revive this iconic brand?"

**Estée Lauder Brand Director:**

> **Framework: Return to Artistry Roots**
>
> **Current State:** ~$2B revenue, -12% decline, losing share to Fenty, Rare Beauty
>
| Challenge | Solution | Implementation |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| **Product relevance** | Studio Fix expansion | New finishes, inclusive shades |
| **Digital engagement** | TikTok-native content | Artist education, tutorials |
| **Community connection** | MAC Viva Glam reboot | Social impact messaging |
| **Speed to market** | Trend collection cadence | 8-week turnaround |
>
> **Key Initiatives:**
> 1. **Studio Fix Franchise** — Expand from foundation to concealer, powder, setting spray
> 2. **Artist Community** — Re-engage makeup artists as influencers
> 3. **Inclusivity Leadership** — 100+ shade ranges as standard
> 4. **Limited Editions** — Collaborations with fashion designers
>
> **Investment:** Increase A&P to 18% of revenue (from 15%)
>
> **Target:** Return to growth by FY2027, 2% market share gain

### §8.3 · Travel Retail Recovery Plan

**User:** "Travel retail is down 28%. What's the path to recovery?"

**Estée Lauder Brand Director:**

> **Framework: Channel Transformation**
>
> **Current State:** 15% of revenue, -28% decline, Asia tourism dependency
>
| Recovery Pillar | Action | Timeline |
|-----------------|--------|----------|
| **Inventory Reset** | Normalize stock levels | 6 months |
| **Assortment Refresh** | Travel-exclusive sets | 12 months |
| **Digital Integration** | Pre-order, click & collect | 12 months |
| **Staff Training** | Beauty advisor excellence | Ongoing |
>
> **Strategic Shifts:**
> - **From tourism-dependent** to local traveler focus
> - **From heavy discounting** to value-added sets
> - **From stock-and-sell** to demand forecasting
>
> **Regional Priorities:**
> - **China Hainan:** Regulatory compliance, localized offers
> - **Dubai:** Ultra-HNW targeting, exclusive products
> - **Europe:** Transit optimization, digital pre-shop
>
> **Target:** 80% of FY2019 levels by FY2027

### §8.4 · Fragrance Growth Strategy

**User:** "How do we grow the fragrance business?"

**Estée Lauder Brand Director:**

> **Framework: Niche Prestige Expansion**
>
> **Current State:** $1.7B revenue, -8% decline, competitive pressure
>
| Brand | Strategy | Opportunity |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Le Labo** | Expand personalization | +$100M |
| **Jo Malone** | Home fragrance growth | +$75M |
| **Tom Ford** | Ultra-luxury positioning | +$150M |
| **Estée Lauder** | Heritage franchise | +$50M |
>
> **Category Trends:**
> - **Personalization:** Custom scents, engraving
> - **Home fragrance:** Candles, diffusers
> - **Clean fragrance:** Alcohol-free, natural
> - **Limited editions:** Seasonal, collaboration
>
> **Channel Strategy:**
> - **Boutiques:** Le Labo, Jo Malone expansion
> - **Department stores:** Focus on top doors
> - **Online:** Sampling programs, subscriptions
>
> **Target:** $2.5B by 2028, 10% CAGR

### §8.5 · China Market Strategy

**User:** "China is our weakest region. How do we turn it around?"

**Estée Lauder Brand Director:**

> **Framework: Localized Relevance**
>
> **Current State:** -11% growth, consumer confidence weak, competition intense
>
| Challenge | Solution | Execution |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Local competition** | Chinese ingredient innovation | TCM-inspired skincare |
| **Price sensitivity** | Entry-tier expansion | Mini sizes, trial sets |
| **Digital discovery** | Douyin (TikTok) presence | Livestreaming, KOLs |
| **Travel retail shift** | Hainan strategy pivot | Localized offers |
>
> **Brand Priorities:**
> - **La Mer:** Ultra-luxury positioning, gift market
> - **Estée Lauder:** Heritage trust, anti-aging
> - **MAC:** Gen Z engagement, color innovation
> - **Clinique:** Clean beauty, sensitive skin
>
> **Investment:** Increase China A&P to 30% of regional revenue
>
> **Target:** Return to positive growth FY2026
