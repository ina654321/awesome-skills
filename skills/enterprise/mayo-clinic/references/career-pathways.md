# Career Pathways at Mayo Clinic

## Physician Career Progression

### Traditional Advancement Track

| Stage | Title | Typical Timeline | Requirements |
|-------|-------|------------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Resident/Fellow** | 3-7 years | Medical degree, matched training program |
| 2 | **Consultant** | 0-5 years | Board certification, staff appointment |
| 3 | **Senior Associate Consultant** | 5-10 years | Demonstrated excellence, publications |
| 4 | **Senior Consultant** | 10-15 years | National recognition, leadership |
| 5 | **Professor** | 15+ years | Academic leadership, major contributions |

### Compensation Philosophy

**Salaried Model (No RVU Pressure):**
- Physicians paid fixed salary regardless of patient volume
- No fee-for-service incentives
- No bonus for procedures ordered
- Evaluated on quality of work, not quantity

**Executive Compensation Approach:**
- Administrative physicians initially paid below market average
- Over several years, increased to mid-range of market
- Large year-over-year increases reflect "catch-up," not excess

| Track | Roles | Focus |

| **Research** | Center Director, Principal Investigator | Research enterprise |
| **Education** | Program Director, Dean | Training programs |
| **Administrative** | CEO (site), CMO, VP | Operations leadership |

## Comparison: Mayo Clinic vs. Other Top Institutions

### Mayo Clinic vs. Cleveland Clinic

| Dimension | Mayo Clinic | Cleveland Clinic |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Structure** | Physician-led partnership | Hospital-centric with employed physicians |
| **Model** | Integrated multispecialty practice | Organ-based institute model |
| **Geography** | Rochester, Jacksonville, Phoenix/Scottsdale | Cleveland, Florida, Abu Dhabi, London, Toronto |
| **Culture** | "Needs of the Patient Come First" | "Patients First" with caregiver emphasis |
| **Compensation** | Salaried, no RVU pressure | Salaried with quality incentives |
| **Notable** | Destruction of silos, transparent quality | Heart center reputation, global expansion |
| **Career Path** | Residency → Fellowship → Staff → Senior Staff → Consultant → Professor | Similar academic track with institute focus |

### Mayo Clinic vs. Johns Hopkins

| Dimension | Mayo Clinic | Johns Hopkins |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Structure** | Physician-led non-profit | University-affiliated academic |
| **Model** | Integrated practice | Academic medical center |
| **Governance** | Physician partnership | University system |
| **Research** | $1.27B annually | ~$2.5B (university-wide) |
| **Focus** | Clinical integration | Research and discovery |
| **Culture** | Midwest collaborative | East Coast competitive |
| **Notable** | Team medicine | Nobel laureates (multiple) |

### Mayo Clinic vs. Other Academic Centers
