# Mayo Clinic History and Heritage

## Founding (1889)

### The Mayo Family
- **Dr. William Worrall Mayo** (1819-1911) — British immigrant who settled in Rochester, Minnesota in 1863; established general practice; served as examining physician for US Army recruitment
- **Dr. William James Mayo ("Will")** (1861-1939) — Specialized in abdominal/pelvic surgery; University of Michigan medical education
- **Dr. Charles Horace Mayo ("Charlie")** (1865-1939) — Specialized in head, neck, and neurological surgery; Chicago Medical College education

### The Tornado and Partnership
- **August 21, 1883**: Devastating tornado struck Rochester, Minnesota
- Mother Alfred Moes and Sisters of St. Francis volunteered to help the wounded
- Partnership formed between Mayo family and Sisters of St. Francis
- **1889**: Saint Mary's Hospital founded — marked beginning of Mayo Clinic

### Revolutionary Model
The Mayo Clinic pioneered concepts that were revolutionary for the time:
- **Team-based medicine** — Physicians collaborating rather than competing
- **Multispecialty group practice** — Specialists working together for patient benefit
- **Integrated medical record** — Standardized documentation from early 1900s
- **Salaried physicians** — No fee-for-service incentives to prioritize patient care

### Nobel Prize Era (1950)
- **Dr. Edward C. Kendall** and **Dr. Philip S. Hench** awarded Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine
- **Discovery**: Cortisone for rheumatoid arthritis treatment
- **Significance**: First effective treatment for inflammatory diseases

## Modern Mayo Clinic (2025)

### Scale and Reach
- **Revenue**: $21.5 billion (2025)
- **Employees**: ~85,000 worldwide
- **Patients**: 1M+ annually from 135+ countries
- **Rankings**: #1 U.S. Hospital (U.S. News), #1 World's Best Hospital (Newsweek)

### Leadership
- **President & CEO**: Dr. Gianrico Farrugia (since 2019)
- **Compensation Philosophy**: Executive pay starts below market, increases to mid-range over time
- **Governance**: Physician-led with shared governance model

## The Mayo Model Legacy

> "The best interest of the patient is the only interest to be considered." — Dr. William J. Mayo, 1910

### Core Principles (Still in Practice)
1. **Needs of the Patient Come First** — Patient-centered in every decision
2. **Integrated Expertise** — Combined knowledge of 4,000+ physicians available to every patient
3. **Measurement Drives Excellence** — Public outcomes data creates accountability
4. **Physician Leadership** — Doctors lead clinical governance and strategy
5. **No Unnecessary Care** — Value-based practice eliminates waste

### Healthcare Hall of Fame (2009)
William J. Mayo, M.D., and Charles H. Mayo, M.D. were inducted into the Healthcare Hall of Fame, recognizing their creation of the cooperative group practice model.

### Memorial
- **"My Brother and I" Monument** — Bronze sculpture of Mayo brothers installed 2002 outside Mayo Clinic in Rochester
- Located on steps between buildings, symbolizing their collaborative spirit
