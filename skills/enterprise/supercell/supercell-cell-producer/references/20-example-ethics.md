# Example 5: Monetization Ethics Question

## Context

New monetization feature being designed. Could increase revenue 20% but feels potentially exploitative.

## User Request

"We've designed a gacha system that could boost revenue significantly, but I'm worried it's too close to gambling. Should we ship it?"

## Ethics Framework Application

Supercell's monetization philosophy: **"Don't sell power, sell time and customization."**

Any feature that violates this needs serious scrutiny.

## Gacha-Specific Considerations

### Regulatory Risk

- **Belgium, Netherlands**: Loot boxes banned or regulated
- **Other countries**: Increasing scrutiny
- **Apple/Google**: Policy changes possible
- **Risk**: Feature removal, legal issues, reputational damage

### Player Trust

- Supercell brand = fair play
- "Free to play, fair to play"
- Exploitative mechanics damage long-term trust
- Recovery from "pay-to-win" reputation is nearly impossible

### Alternative Approaches

- Direct purchase cosmetics (no randomness)
- Battle pass (clear progression, no gambling)
- Earned through gameplay (grindable)
- Limited offers (scarcity without randomness)

## Decision Framework

Would we be proud to explain this to:
- Our players?
- Our families?
- The media?
- Regulators?

**If uncomfortable with any of these: DON'T SHIP.**

## Recommendation: Redesign Without Randomness

Keep the rewards, remove the gambling:
- Direct purchase paths for desired items
- Earned through skill/engagement
- Clear value proposition
- No loot box mechanics

### Historical Precedent

Clash Royale has chests (random) BUT everything is also craftable/collectible through play. The randomness is a bonus, not the only path.

## Key Principle

> Revenue that comes from player joy is sustainable. Revenue that comes from player exploitation eventually backfires.

## If Revenue is Critical

If you need the 20% revenue boost:
1. Find ethical ways to achieve it (better LiveOps, more content)
2. Accept lower revenue this quarter for sustainable growth
3. Remember: Supercell became a $10B company without exploitative monetization

## Final Decision

**DO NOT SHIP** the gacha system as designed. Redesign with player-first ethics.
