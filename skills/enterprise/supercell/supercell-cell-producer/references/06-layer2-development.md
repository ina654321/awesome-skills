# Layer 2: Game Development & Iteration - Full Details

## Purpose

Rapidly prototype, test, and refine games until they're hits—or kill them quickly.

## Core Expertise

### Rapid Prototyping

Playable versions in days/weeks, not months.

**Principles**:
- Build the core loop first
- Use placeholder art
- Focus on FEEL, not LOOK
- Iterate daily based on feedback

**Timeline**: 2-6 weeks for first playable

### Vertical Slice Development

One perfect level/feature that proves the game.

**Definition**: A "slice" of the game at final quality, demonstrating:
- Core gameplay
- Art style
- Technical systems
- Monetization
- Backend infrastructure

**Why**: Proves the game can be great before investing in full production.

### Continuous Player Testing

Real players from day one, not just internal testing.

**Playtest Frequency**:
- Internal: 3+ times per week
- External: Weekly once prototype is playable
- Friends & family: As soon as possible
- Strangers: During soft launch

### Data-Informed Design

Analytics inform, but don't dictate, creative decisions.

**Metrics to Track**:
- Session length
- Retention (D1, D7, D30)
- Completion rates
- Drop-off points
- Monetization (soft launch)

### Platform Optimization

Mobile-first, performance on mid-tier devices.

**Targets**:
- 60fps on 3-year-old devices
- <100MB initial download
- Quick load times (< 5 seconds)
- Graceful degradation

### Technical Excellence

Clean code that scales, no shortcuts that hurt later.

**Principles**:
- Stateless servers for horizontal scaling
- Database sharding from day one
- CDN for assets
- Monitoring and alerting

## Development Phases

### Phase 1: Concept (Days to 2 Weeks)

**Goal**: Validate the idea before investing in prototype.

**Activities**:
- Write 1-page concept document
- Create paper prototype or simple mockup
- Play competitive games
- Define target audience and market opportunity

**Deliverables**:
- Core gameplay loop (1 sentence)
- Target audience definition
- Differentiation from competitors
- Monetization hypothesis

**Kill/Continue Gate**:
- ✅ Team is excited about the concept
- ✅ Clear differentiation from competitors
- ✅ Addressable market > 10M potential players
- ❌ Generic idea without unique angle
- ❌ No clear target audience

### Phase 2: Prototype (2-6 Weeks)

**Goal**: Determine if the core loop is fun.

**Activities**:
- Build one core loop playable end-to-end
- Use placeholder art
- Internal playtests 3+ times per week
- Iterate rapidly based on feedback

**Success Criteria**:
- Team plays voluntarily outside work hours
- "One more game" feeling is present
- Core loop takes < 2 minutes to complete
- Can explain what's fun in one sentence

**Kill/Continue Gate**:
- ✅ Team is obsessed with playing it
- ✅ Clear "fun" factor
- ✅ Differentiation is working
- ❌ Team plays only during scheduled sessions
- ❌ Core loop is "okay" but not compelling
- ❌ No "one more game" feeling

### Phase 3: Vertical Slice (1-3 Months)

**Goal**: Prove the game can reach final quality.

**Activities**:
- Create one slice at final quality
- Implement all core systems
- Establish art style
- Build monetization systems
- Operational backend infrastructure

**Success Criteria**:
- This slice could ship as-is
- Performance targets met
- Monetization feels fair
- All systems working together

**Kill/Continue Gate**:
- ✅ Quality bar proven
- ✅ Technical foundation solid
- ✅ Art style compelling
- ✅ Monetization model clear
- ❌ Quality is "good enough for now"
- ❌ Performance issues on target devices
- ❌ Systems don't work together

### Phase 4: Soft Launch (2-6 Months)

**Goal**: Test with real players in limited markets.

**Markets**:
- Canada (iOS/Android behavior similar to US)
- Philippines (Android-heavy, good for testing)
- Finland (home market, easy to monitor)
- Sweden (similar to Finland)

**Metrics to Track**:
- D1/D7/D30 retention
- ARPDAU (Average Revenue Per Daily Active User)
- CPI (Cost Per Install)
- Engagement (sessions, playtime)

**Target Metrics**:

| Metric | Good | Great | World Class |
|--------|------|-------|-------------|
| D1 Retention | 40% | 50% | 60%+ |
| D7 Retention | 15% | 20% | 25%+ |
| D30 Retention | 8% | 10% | 15%+ |
| ARPDAU | $0.05 | $0.10 | $0.20+ |
| CPI | <$1 | <$0.50 | <$0.20 |

**Kill/Continue Gate**:
- ✅ D1 > 50%, D7 > 20%, D30 > 10%
- ✅ Clear path to profitability
- ✅ Team still passionate
- ❌ Retention below thresholds after 2+ iterations
- ❌ No clear improvement path
- ❌ Team has lost faith

### Phase 5: Global Launch

**Goal**: Launch worldwide and begin 10+ year operations.

**Activities**:
- Worldwide release
- Major user acquisition campaigns
- Live operations team activation
- 24/7 monitoring
- Rapid response to issues

**Success Indicators**:
- Growing player base
- Strong retention
- Sustainable monetization
- Positive community sentiment

## Development Velocity

**Typical Timelines**:
- Concept to Prototype: 2-8 weeks
- Prototype to Vertical Slice: 1-3 months
- Vertical Slice to Soft Launch: 2-6 months
- Soft Launch to Global: 2-6 months (if metrics hit)

**Total Time to Global Launch**: 6-15 months (for games that make it)

**Comparison to Industry**:
- Supercell: 6-15 months (for launched games)
- Typical AAA: 3-5 years
- Other mobile: 12-24 months

**Why Faster?**
- Small teams communicate faster
- Kill fast, don't waste time on dead ends
- Focus on core loop, not features
- Rapid iteration based on player feedback
