## Development Anti-Patterns

### Anti-Pattern 1: Feature Creep

**Symptoms**:
- Constantly adding new features
- "Wouldn't it be cool if..." driving roadmap
- Scope expanding without focus
- Team spread too thin

**Consequences**:
- Nothing gets polished
- Core loop suffers
- Team burnout
- Launch delays

**Solutions**:
- Ruthless focus on core loop
- Every feature must justify its existence
- "Kill features" as often as you add them
- Regular scope reviews and cuts

**Supercell Approach**:
> "More wood behind fewer arrows"—focus beats breadth.

---

### Anti-Pattern 2: Data Worship

**Symptoms**:
- Making decisions purely based on metrics
- Ignoring team intuition and player feedback
- A/B testing every minor detail
- Losing sight of what makes games fun

**Consequences**:
- Optimizing for metrics over fun
- Homogenized, soulless games
- Team disengagement
- Missing breakthrough innovations

**Solutions**:
- Data informs, doesn't dictate
- Player interviews for WHY, not just WHAT
- Trust team intuition on fun
- Remember: "If we had listened to focus groups, Clash of Clans would have been killed"

**Supercell Approach**:
> Data tells you WHAT is happening. Player interviews tell you WHY. Your intuition synthesizes both.

---

### Anti-Pattern 3: Premature Scaling

**Symptoms**:
- Hiring before product-market fit
- Building enterprise infrastructure early
- Complex systems for simple problems
- "We need to prepare for success"

**Consequences**:
- Team bloat and communication overhead
- Technical complexity without value
- Burn rate increasing before revenue
- Slower iteration when speed matters most

**Solutions**:
- Stay small until metrics prove need
- Build for current scale, refactor when needed
- YAGNI (You Aren't Gonna Need It) principle
- "More wood behind fewer arrows"

**Supercell Approach**:
> 5-15 person cells. Even Clash of Clans at $2B/year was run by ~40 people.

---

### Anti-Pattern 4: Sunk Cost Fallacy

**Symptoms**:
- "We've already spent 6 months on this"
- Continuing because of investment, not potential
- Delaying kill decisions
- Hoping things will magically improve

**Consequences**:
- Wasted resources on dead projects
- Team morale declining
- Opportunity cost of not working on better ideas
- Cultural acceptance of mediocrity

**Solutions**:
- Celebrate kills, don't punish them
- Kill decisions based on future potential, not past investment
- Regular kill reviews with fresh eyes
- Remember: Supercell kills 90%+ of projects

**Supercell Approach**:
> Killing fast is success. Each kill is a learning investment.

## Cultural Anti-Patterns

### Anti-Pattern 5: Hierarchy Creep

**Symptoms**:
- Creating manager roles
- Approval processes and sign-offs
- Information flowing through channels
- Titles and status symbols

**Consequences**:
- Slow decision making
- Politics and power games
- Loss of autonomy
- Best ideas not surfacing

**Solutions**:
- No titles on business cards
- Direct communication encouraged
- Decisions made by those closest to the work
- CEO sits at the same desk as everyone else

**Supercell Approach**:
> Everyone's business card says "Cell Member."

---

### Anti-Pattern 6: Politeness Over Honesty

**Symptoms**:
- Sugarcoating feedback
- Avoiding difficult conversations
- Consensus seeking to avoid conflict
- "Let's take this offline"

**Consequences**:
- Problems not addressed
- Resentment building
- Lower quality output
- Lack of trust

**Solutions**:
- Finnish directness: honest, not rude
- Challenge ideas, not people
- Silence means disagreement
- Best idea wins, regardless of source

**Supercell Approach**:
> Be honest, not polite.

---

### Anti-Pattern 7: Hero Culture

**Symptoms**:
- Celebrating individuals who "saved" projects
- Rewarding overtime and crunch
- Single points of failure
- Knowledge silos

**Consequences**:
- Team resentment
- Burnout of "heroes"
- Bus factor problems
- Unsustainable practices

**Solutions**:
- Celebrate team wins, not individual heroes
- Sustainable pace over crunch
- Knowledge sharing required
- No single points of failure

**Supercell Approach**:
> Shared success, shared failure. Bonuses distributed equally within the cell.

---

### Anti-Pattern 8: Planning Theater

**Symptoms**:
- Obsessive planning with little execution
- Gantt charts and detailed timelines
- "We need more analysis before deciding"
- Analysis paralysis

**Consequences**:
- Slow to market
- Plans outdated before executed
- Team waiting instead of building
- Missed opportunities

**Solutions**:
- Build to learn, not to plan
- Rapid prototyping over detailed specs
- Decide with 70% of information
- Iterate based on feedback

**Supercell Approach**:
> 2-6 week prototypes. Real players from day one.

---

## LiveOps Anti-Patterns

### Anti-Pattern 9: Content Treadmill

**Symptoms**:
- Team constantly grinding to release content
- No time for innovation
- Content quality declining
- Team burnout

**Consequences**:
- Content fatigue
- Player churn
- Team turnover
- Unsustainable operations

**Solutions**:
- Tools that enable designers
- Content pipelines that don't require engineering
- Rotate team members
- Scheduled innovation time

---

### Anti-Pattern 10: Metric Myopia

**Symptoms**:
- Optimizing for short-term metrics
- Ignoring player experience
- Dark patterns to boost numbers
- Sacrificing long-term trust

**Consequences**:
- Player resentment
- Regulatory attention
- Brand damage
- Unsustainable business

**Solutions**:
- Player first, business last
- Long-term metrics over short-term spikes
- Ethical monetization
- Sustainable engagement

**Supercell Approach**:
> "Don't sell power, sell time and customization."
