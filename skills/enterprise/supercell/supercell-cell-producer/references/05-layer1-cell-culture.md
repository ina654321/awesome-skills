# Layer 1: Cell Autonomy & Culture - Full Details

## Purpose

Maintain small-team agility and creative freedom while ensuring alignment and accountability.

## Core Expertise

### Self-Organization

No traditional managers. Teams self-organize around problems.

**How It Works**:
- Game Lead sets vision and makes final decisions
- Team members choose their tasks
- No micromanagement
- Natural leaders emerge based on expertise, not title

**Example**:
```
Instead of: "John, implement the matchmaking system"
Try: "We need matchmaking. Who has expertise here?"
```

### Consensus-Driven Decision Making

Everyone's voice is heard, but someone ultimately decides.

**Process**:
1. Present the decision to be made
2. Gather input from everyone
3. Discuss trade-offs
4. Game Lead makes final call
5. Team commits to decision

**Principle**: "Disagree and commit"—once decided, everyone aligns.

### Kill Culture

Celebrating failures as learning investments, not punishments.

**Key Elements**:
- Hall of Fail office display
- Kill presentations are celebrated events
- Team members reassigned quickly
- Learnings shared company-wide
- No blame, no stigma

### Radical Transparency

Everyone sees revenue, player data, company performance.

**What’s Shared**:
- Daily player metrics
- Monthly revenue numbers
- Company financials
- Compensation bands
- Strategic plans

**Why**: Transparency creates trust; secrets create politics.

### Flat Hierarchy

Open debate, challenging ideas, zero politics.

**Practices**:
- No titles on business cards
- Open office seating
- Anyone can challenge anyone
- Best idea wins
- CEO sits with everyone else

### Shared Success

Equal bonus distribution, shared ownership of outcomes.

**How It Works**:
- When a game succeeds, bonuses are shared equally within the cell
- No individual hero rewards
- Everyone owns the outcome
- Success is collective

### Meeting Cadence (Minimal)

**Daily Standup** (10 minutes):
- What did you do yesterday?
- What will you do today?
- Any blockers?
- Async when possible

**Weekly Playtest** (1 hour):
- Everyone plays the latest build
- Raw feedback, no filter
- Focus on "is it fun?"
- No PowerPoints, just playing

**Monthly Planning** (half day):
- Roadmap decisions
- What's next?
- What's cut?
- Resource allocation

**Kill/Continue Review** (as needed):
- Data presentation
- Team sentiment
- Market analysis
- Decision

### Autonomy Boundaries

**✅ CAN Decide**:
- Game direction, features, art style
- Tech stack and tools
- When to kill or continue
- Team composition and hiring
- Roadmap and priorities

**❌ CANNOT Decide**:
- Company-wide platform strategy
- IP licensing and major partnerships
- Corporate M&A
- Office locations

**📊 MUST Report**:
- Player metrics (retention, engagement, monetization)
- Revenue and financial performance
- Team health and morale
- Major risks and blockers
