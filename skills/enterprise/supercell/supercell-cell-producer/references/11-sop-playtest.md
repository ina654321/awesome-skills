# SOP 2: Running a Playtest

## Purpose

Gather actionable feedback on a playable build.

## Input

Playable build

## Output

Actionable feedback and prioritized action items

## Process

### Preparation (Day Before)

**Define Playtest Goals**:
- What are we testing? (core loop, specific feature, etc.)
- What questions do we need answered?
- What decisions will this inform?

**Prepare Specific Questions**:
- Don't ask "did you like it?"
- Do ask "what were you thinking when...?"
- Focus on behavior, not opinions

**Set Up Observation Environment**:
- Quiet room
- Screen recording setup
- Note-taking materials
- Observation area (team watches)

**Recruit Participants (5-10 per session)**:
- Target demographic match
- Mix of gamers and non-gamers
- Some familiar with genre, some not
- Pay or incentivize participation

**Prepare Consent Forms**:
- Recording consent
- NDA if needed
- Data usage agreement

### During Playtest

**Welcome Players** (5 min):
- Thank them for coming
- Set context: "We're testing the game, not you"
- Encourage thinking out loud
- No right or wrong answers

**Observe Without Interfering** (15-30 min):
- Let them play naturally
- Don't help or guide
- Watch for confusion, delight, frustration
- Note body language

**Ask Open-Ended Questions**:
- "What were you thinking when...?"
- "How did that make you feel?"
- "What would you expect to happen next?"
- "What was confusing?"
- "What was fun?"

**Watch For**:
- Confusion (furrowed brows, hesitation)
- Delight (smiles, excitement)
- Frustration (sighs, giving up)
- Engagement (leaning forward, focused)

**Record Sessions** (with permission):
- Screen capture
- Audio of player thinking out loud
- Video of player reactions

### Analysis (Same Day)

**Transcribe Key Moments**:
- Critical quotes
- Confusion points
- Delight moments
- Frustration events

**Identify Patterns Across Players**:
- What did multiple people struggle with?
- What did everyone love?
- Where did people drop off?

**Categorize Feedback**:
- Critical (must fix)
- Important (should fix)
- Nice-to-have (could fix)

**Compare to Previous Playtests**:
- Are we improving?
- New issues or old ones?
- Trends over time

**Prioritize Action Items**:
- What will have biggest impact?
- What's easiest to fix?
- What requires major changes?

### Follow-Up

**Share Findings with Cell**:
- Summarize key insights
- Show video clips of important moments
- Discuss as a team

**Create Action Items**:
- Specific, assignable tasks
- Prioritized by impact
- Timeline for implementation

**Schedule Next Playtest**:
- When will changes be ready?
- What will we test next?
- Same or different participants?

**Thank Participants**:
- Send thank you note/email
- Provide incentive/reward
- Invite them to future playtests

## Deliverables

- Playtest report
- Video clips of key moments
- Prioritized feedback list
- Action plan with owners and dates
