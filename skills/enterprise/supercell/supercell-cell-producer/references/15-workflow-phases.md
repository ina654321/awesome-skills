# § 8 · Workflow Phases - Full Details

## Phase 1: Discovery & Assessment

### Objective

Evaluate game concept and determine if prototype is warranted.

### Key Activities

1. **Concept Validation**
   - 1-page pitch
   - Competitive analysis
   - Market sizing

2. **Team Formation**
   - Recruit voluntary cell members
   - Define roles
   - Set up collaboration tools

3. **Market Research**
   - Interview potential players (5-10)
   - Analyze competitors
   - Size the opportunity

4. **Go/No-Go Decision**
   - Data-driven decision
   - Clear rationale
   - Defined next steps

### Done Criteria

- [✓] Clear concept documented with differentiation
- [✓] Team of 5-15 committed members formed
- [✓] Player interviews completed (5-10 participants)
- [✓] Go/No-Go decision made with clear rationale

### Fail Criteria

- [✗] Concept is a clone without differentiation
- [✗] Team is assembled by assignment, not voluntary
- [✗] No player research completed
- [✗] Decision based on hope, not evidence

---

## Phase 2: Prototype Development

### Objective

Build playable core loop and determine if game is fun.

### Key Activities

1. **Rapid Prototyping**
   - Build playable version in 2-6 weeks
   - Focus on core loop
   - Use placeholder art

2. **Continuous Playtesting**
   - 3+ internal playtests per week
   - Gather feedback
   - Iterate daily

3. **Iteration**
   - Rapid changes based on feedback
   - Kill features that don't work
   - Double down on what does

4. **Kill/Continue Decision**
   - Is it fun?
   - Do people play voluntarily?
   - Evidence-based decision

### Done Criteria

- [✓] Core loop playable end-to-end
- [✓] Team plays voluntarily outside work hours
- [✓] "One more game" feeling present
- [✓] Decision to continue or kill based on evidence

### Fail Criteria

- [✗] Prototype took > 8 weeks
- [✗] Team plays only during scheduled sessions
- [✗] Core loop is "okay" but not compelling
- [✗] Decision to continue based on sunk cost fallacy

---

## Phase 3: Vertical Slice

### Objective

Create one slice of the game at final quality to prove the vision.

### Key Activities

1. **Quality Bar Setting**
   - Define "shippable quality"
   - Establish art style
   - Set technical standards

2. **Full Feature Implementation**
   - All core systems working
   - Art at final quality
   - Monetization in place

3. **Art Style Finalization**
   - Visual identity established
   - Consistent across slice
   - Approved by team

4. **Technical Validation**
   - Backend infrastructure operational
   - Performance targets met
   - Scalability proven

### Done Criteria

- [✓] One slice could ship as-is (quality proven)
- [✓] All core systems implemented
- [✓] Performance targets met on target devices
- [✓] Monetization systems in place and fair

### Fail Criteria

- [✗] Quality is "good enough for now" but not final
- [✗] Core systems missing or broken
- [✗] Performance issues on target devices
- [✗] Monetization feels exploitative

---

## Phase 4: Soft Launch

### Objective

Test with real players in limited markets and gather data.

### Key Activities

1. **Limited Release**
   - Canada, Philippines, Finland, Sweden
   - Small user base
   - Real player behavior

2. **Data Collection**
   - D1/D7/D30 retention
   - ARPDAU, CPI
   - Engagement metrics

3. **Weekly Iteration**
   - Rapid updates based on data
   - A/B testing
   - Feature adjustments

4. **Kill/Global Launch Decision**
   - Data-driven decision
   - Team sentiment
   - Market analysis

### Done Criteria

- [✓] D1 Retention > 50% (Good) or > 60% (Great)
- [✓] D7 Retention > 20% (Good) or > 25% (Great)
- [✓] D30 Retention on track to hit > 10%
- [✓] Clear decision: Kill, Iterate, or Global Launch

### Fail Criteria

- [✗] Retention metrics below thresholds after 2+ iterations
- [✗] Team has lost passion despite decent metrics
- [✗] No clear path to improvement
- [✗] Decision delayed due to sunk cost

---

## Phase 5: Global Launch & LiveOps

### Objective

Launch globally and sustain the game for 10+ years.

### Key Activities

1. **Global Release**
   - Worldwide launch
   - Marketing campaign
   - User acquisition

2. **User Acquisition**
   - Paid campaigns
   - Organic growth
   - Influencer partnerships

3. **Live Operations**
   - Weekly content
   - Events
   - Community engagement

4. **Long-term Evolution**
   - Major expansions
   - New features
   - Platform expansion

### Done Criteria

- [✓] Game growing sustainably
- [✓] Content pipeline delivering weekly updates
- [✓] Community engaged and growing
- [✓] Revenue funding continued development

### Fail Criteria

- [✗] Player base declining post-launch
- [✗] Content pipeline broken or inconsistent
- [✗] Community sentiment turning negative
- [✗] Revenue insufficient to sustain team

---

## Phase Transition Summary

| Phase | Duration | Key Decision | Success Rate |
|-------|----------|--------------|--------------|
| Discovery | 1-2 weeks | Go/No-Go to Prototype | ~50% |
| Prototype | 2-6 weeks | Kill/Continue | ~30% |
| Vertical Slice | 1-3 months | Kill/Continue | ~20% |
| Soft Launch | 2-6 months | Kill/Global Launch | ~10% |
| Global Launch | Ongoing | Sustain/Grow | ~5% |

**Overall Success Rate**: ~5% of concepts reach global launch

**This is by design**: Killing fast saves resources for the hits.
