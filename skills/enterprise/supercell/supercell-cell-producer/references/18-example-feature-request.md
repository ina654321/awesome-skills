# Example 3: Live Operations Feature Request

## Context

Clash Royale-like game has been live for 2 years. Revenue is flat, engagement is slowly declining.

## User Request

"Leadership wants us to add a battle royale mode to boost engagement. The team is skeptical but leadership is pushing hard. What should we do?"

## Situation Analysis

Leadership Pressure vs. Team Skepticism is a classic tension. At Supercell, autonomy means **cells decide**, not leadership.

## Decision Framework Application

### 1. Player First

Do our players want battle royale?
- Survey data? Community requests?
- Does it fit the 2-5 minute session design?
- Would it fragment the player base?

### 2. Long-term

Will this be good in 5 years?
- Battle royale is a trend, not timeless
- Core game has 10-year potential
- Risk: Chasing trends dilutes brand

### 3. Quality

Can we make it excellent?
- Battle royale requires significant tech
- 5-15 person team can't do everything
- Half-baked mode hurts more than helps

### 4. Team Health

Is the team excited?
- Team is skeptical = red flag
- Forced features never succeed
- Team needs to own the outcome

## Recommendation: Decline Battle Royale, Propose Alternative

### Response to Leadership

> "We appreciate the input. We've analyzed battle royale and don't believe it aligns with our core game's strengths or player expectations. Instead, we propose [ALTERNATIVE] which we believe has better player fit and team alignment."

### Alternative Approaches to Explore

1. **Depth expansion**: New cards, deeper meta
2. **Social features**: Better clan systems, tournaments
3. **Quality of life**: Address player pain points
4. **Seasonal events**: Limited-time creative modes
5. **Esports**: Competitive scene building

### Key Principle

> "More wood behind fewer arrows"—double down on what makes your game special rather than chasing trends.

### If Leadership Insists

Remind them: **Cells decide**. That's the model. If they override cells, the model breaks.

Quote Ilkka Paananen (Supercell CEO):
> "Hire great people and let them do their job."
