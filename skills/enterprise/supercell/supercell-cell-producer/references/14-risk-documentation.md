## Risk Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy | Early Warning Signs |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Game Killed After Months** | High | Medium | Celebrate kills, reassign quickly, capture learnings | Retention declining, team enthusiasm dropping |
| **Team Burnout** | Medium | High | Sustainable pace, no crunch, team health priority | Overtime increasing, morale surveys declining |
| **LiveOps Fatigue** | Medium | High | Rotate teams, fresh cells, sabbaticals | Content quality declining, team turnover |
| **Competitor Copy** | High | Medium | Move fast, build brand, community loyalty | Similar games appearing in soft launch |
| **Technical Debt** | Medium | High | Code quality standards, refactoring time | Bug rates increasing, development slowing |
| **Key Person Departure** | Medium | High | Knowledge sharing, no single points of failure | Single person owning critical systems |
| **Market Saturation** | Medium | High | Innovation, new genres, platform expansion | Genre growth slowing, CPI rising |
| **Platform Policy Change** | Low | High | Diversify, maintain platform relationships | App store guideline updates |
| **Monetization Backlash** | Medium | Medium | Fair design, player communication | Negative reviews mentioning pay-to-win |
| **Security Breach** | Low | Critical | Security audits, penetration testing | Unusual account activity |

## Kill Celebration Process

When a cell decides to kill a game, follow this process to extract maximum learning:

### Phase 1: Kill Presentation (1 hour)

**What Was Built**:
- Show the game, its best moments
- Demonstrate key features
- Acknowledge the effort invested

**What Was Learned**:
- Key insights about players
- Market learnings
- Technical discoveries
- Process improvements

**What Would We Do Differently**:
- Retrospective insights
- What worked?
- What didn't?

**Celebration of the Journey**:
- Acknowledge the effort
- Thank the team
- Recognize the courage to kill

### Phase 2: Documentation (Within 1 week)

**Post-Mortem Document**:
- Project overview
- What was built
- Why it was killed
- Key learnings
- Recommendations for future

**Video Walkthrough**:
- Record gameplay
- Capture the best moments
- Archive for future reference

**Code/Asset Archive**:
- Organize and store
- Tag searchable
- Available for reuse

**Lessons Learned Database Entry**:
- Add to company knowledge base
- Searchable by future teams
- Shareable insights

**Share Findings Company-Wide**:
- All-hands presentation
- Blog post
- Internal wiki update

### Phase 3: Transition (Within 2 weeks)

**Team Members Choose Next Cell**:
- Voluntary selection
- Match skills to needs
- Consider career development

**Knowledge Transfer Sessions**:
- Document systems
- Share insights
- Train replacements

**Asset/Code Handoff** (if applicable):
- Transfer to other cells
- Document dependencies
- Ensure accessibility

**Welcome to New Teams**:
- Onboarding
- Context sharing
- Integration support

### Phase 4: Celebration (Ongoing)

**Team Dinner/Drinks**:
- Celebrate the learning
- Acknowledge the effort
- No blame, no shame

**Add to Hall of Fail**:
- Physical or digital display
- Include lessons learned
- Normalize failure

**Share Learnings at All-Hands**:
- Company-wide presentation
- Q&A session
- Celebrate the courage

**Recognize the Courage to Kill**:
- Public acknowledgment
- Career development credit
- Cultural reinforcement

## Crisis Response Playbooks

### Scenario 1: Major Bug/Crash

**Trigger**: Crash rate > 1% or game unplayable

**Response**:
1. Assess scope (how many players affected?)
2. Determine cause
3. Decision: Hotfix or rollback
4. Deploy fix
5. Communicate to players
6. Post-mortem

### Scenario 2: Revenue Drop

**Trigger**: Revenue down > 20% week-over-week

**Investigation**:
1. Is it a data issue? Verify tracking
2. Is it a technical issue? Check purchase flow
3. Is it a competitive issue? New releases, events
4. Is it a game issue? Recent changes, balance issues
5. Is it a market issue? Seasonality, economic factors

**Response by Cause**:
- **Technical**: Emergency fix
- **Game/Balance**: Revert or adjust
- **Competitive**: Accelerate content, special events
- **Market**: Monitor, adjust forecasts

**Communication**:
- Internal: Transparent about situation
- Leadership: Context and plan
- Players: Only if directly relevant

### Scenario 3: Negative Viral Moment

**Trigger**: Negative story gaining traction on social media

**Assessment**:
1. Is it true? Verify facts
2. Is it serious? Evaluate player impact
3. Is it growing? Monitor spread

**Response Framework**:

**If TRUE and SERIOUS**:
- Acknowledge, apologize, commit to fix
- Fix quickly
- Follow up with results

**If TRUE but MINOR**:
- Monitor, address in regular updates

**If FALSE**:
- Clarify with facts
- Don't argue with individuals
- Let community correct misinformation

**Principles**:
- Speed matters more than perfection
- Authenticity over corporate speak
- Actions speak louder than words
- Don't feed the trolls

### Scenario 4: Security Breach

**Trigger**: Unauthorized access detected

**Immediate Response**:
1. Contain the breach
2. Assess scope
3. Notify security team
4. Document everything
5. Prepare player communication

**Short-term**:
- Fix vulnerability
- Reset passwords if needed
- Monitor for further issues

**Long-term**:
- Security audit
- Penetration testing
- Process improvements
- Player trust rebuilding
