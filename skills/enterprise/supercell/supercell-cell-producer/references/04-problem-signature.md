# § 2 · Problem Signature - Full Details

## When to Use This Skill

### High-Impact Cell Challenges

**1. Creating Hit Games with Tiny Teams**
- Competitors use 100+ person teams
- You have 5-15 people
- Need to compete on quality, not scale
- Constraint forces creativity

**2. Kill vs. Persevere Decisions**
- 90%+ of projects should be killed
- When to cut losses vs. double down
- Sunk cost fallacy is the enemy
- Speed of kill decision matters

**3. Balancing Creative Freedom with Business Reality**
- Complete autonomy but accountability
- Player first, business last
- Long-term over short-term
- Making money by not focusing on money

**4. Sustaining Games for 10+ Years**
- Not launch-and-forget
- Weekly content for years
- Community engagement over time
- Technical debt management

**5. Scaling Infrastructure Overnight**
- From 1K to 10M players
- Backend must handle viral growth
- No downtime during spikes
- Graceful degradation

**6. Maintaining Quality with Weekly Releases**
- Fast iteration without breaking things
- Content pipeline efficiency
- QA at scale
- Player trust maintenance

**7. Building Culture of Autonomy + Accountability**
- No managers, self-organization
- Shared success, shared failure
- Kill culture without blame
- Transparency without chaos

**8. Fair Monetization Design**
- "Don't sell power, sell time and customization"
- Free-to-play ethics
- Regulatory compliance (loot boxes)
- Long-term player trust

### Complexity Indicators

| Factor | Supercell Approach | Typical Studio |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Team Size | 5-15 (deliberately small) | 50-200+ |
| Timeline | Kill decisions in months | Years of development |
| Budget | Frugal by design | Raise and spend big |
| Success Rate | 90%+ killed (celebrated) | Launch everything |
| Market Pressure | Thousands of games/day | Moderate competition |
| LiveOps | 10+ year operations | Launch and move on |

## User Signals

### Invoke This Skill When Users:

**Leadership Context**:
- Need to lead small autonomous teams with high stakes
- Want to create culture of experimentation
- Need to balance creative vision with business metrics
- Are building products meant to last 5-10+ years

**Decision Context**:
- Face decisions about continuing or killing projects
- Need frameworks for prioritization
- Want to understand sustainable live operations
- Need to resolve team conflicts

**Operational Context**:
- Scaling infrastructure for viral growth
- Designing fair monetization systems
- Managing live operations crises
- Building content pipelines

**Cultural Context**:
- Want to reduce hierarchy and politics
- Need to increase transparency
- Want to celebrate failure as learning
- Building teams that self-organize

2. "How do we prioritize features?"
3. "When is the right time to soft launch?"
4. "How do we handle a live operations crisis?"
5. "What metrics matter for mobile games?"
6. "How do we design fair monetization?"
7. "How do we resolve team conflicts?"
8. "What makes a game 'good enough' to launch?"
