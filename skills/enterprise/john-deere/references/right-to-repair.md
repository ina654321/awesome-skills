# Right to Repair Guide

## Overview

The "Right to Repair" movement addresses farmers' ability to diagnose and repair their own agricultural equipment without being forced to use authorized dealers. John Deere has been at the center of this controversy due to software restrictions on its modern equipment.

## The Core Issue

### Software Locks

Modern John Deere equipment contains Engine Control Units (ECUs) that control virtually all functions:
- Engine performance and emissions
- Hydraulic systems
- Transmission control
- Precision agriculture features
- Safety systems

These ECUs require authorization codes for many repairs, which only authorized dealers can access through the Dealer Technical Assistance Center (DTAC).

### Dealer Technical Assistance Center (DTAC)

**Dealer-Only Access:**
- Comprehensive diagnostic software
- Service Advisor tool (full version)
- Product Improvement Programs (PIPs)
- Secret PIPs (undisclosed to public)
- ECU reprogramming authorization

**Farmer Limitations:**
- Cannot access full diagnostic codes
- Cannot reprogram ECUs
- Cannot access all repair documentation
- Cannot perform emissions-related repairs

## Legal Landscape

### FTC Lawsuit (January 15, 2025)

**Plaintiffs:**
- Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
- State of Illinois
- State of Minnesota

**Allegations:**
- Monopolization of repair services market
- Withholding software and repair tools
- Forcing farmers to use authorized dealers
- Inflating repair costs
- Causing unnecessary delays during critical seasons

**John Deere Response:**
- Called lawsuit "meritless"
- Claimed it was based on "flagrant misrepresentations"
- Noted active negotiations were underway when filed
- Pointed to dissenting FTC commissioners

**FTC Chair Lina Khan Statement:**
> "Unfair repair restrictions can mean farmers face unnecessary delays during tight planting and harvest windows. In rural communities, the restrictions can sometimes mean that farmers need to drive hours just to get their equipment fixed."

### Class Action Lawsuits

**Consolidated Case:**
- U.S. District Court, Northern District of Illinois
- Multiple farmers as plaintiffs
- Discovery concluded October 2024
- Trial expected 2026

**Key Allegations:**
- Antitrust violations
- Supracompetitive pricing
- Dealer-facilitated monopoly
- Secret repair information

### Farm Bureau Memorandums of Understanding (2023)

John Deere signed MOUs with the American Farm Bureau Federation (AFBF) promising:
- Access to manuals and documentation
- Diagnostic software availability
- Speciality tool access
- Fair parts pricing

**Criticism:**
- MOUs not legally binding
- AFBF agreed not to lobby for legislation
- Farmers claim access remains limited
- Does not include all diagnostic tools

## Current Self-Repair Capabilities

### What Farmers CAN Do (2024-2025)

**Routine Maintenance:**
- Oil and filter changes
- Hydraulic fluid service
- Belt and hose replacement
- Greasing and lubrication
- Tire maintenance

**Component Replacement:**
- Electrical sensors
- Switches and relays
- Hydraulic cylinders
- Basic wiring repairs

**Software Access:**
- Equipment Mobile app (launched 2023)
- Basic diagnostic code reading
- Software updates via JDLink (4G equipped)
- Some calibration procedures

### What Requires Dealer Tools

**Restricted Repairs:**
- ECU reprogramming (limited exceptions)
- Advanced diagnostics
- Safety system modifications
- Emissions-related repairs (EPA requirement)
- Access to PIPs
- Dealer Service Advisor (full version)

## Customer Service ADVISOR

**Expanded Access (2023-2024):**
- Now available for purchase by customers
- Available through JohnDeereStore.com
- Enhanced diagnostic capabilities
- Still not equivalent to dealer version

**Limitations:**
- Some functions dealer-only
- Subscription pricing model
- Requires compatible hardware

## Equipment Mobile App

**Features:**
- Diagnostic trouble code reading
- Basic troubleshooting
- Maintenance reminders
- Documentation access

**Launch:** 2023

**Platform:** iOS and Android

## Industry Implications

### Dealer Business Model

**Revenue Streams:**
- Parts sales (higher margins than equipment)
- Service labor
- Software subscriptions
- Warranty work

**Titan Machinery SEC Filing (2022):**
> "Enactment of 'right to repair' legislation could adversely affect the sales and profitability of our parts and service business."

### Competitive Response

**Other Manufacturers:**
- Case New Holland: Similar restrictions
- AGCO: Developing more open approach
- Kubota: Limited diagnostics access

**Aftermarket Solutions:**
- Trimble: More open ecosystem
- Topcon: ISOBUS compatibility focus
- Farmers turning to older, pre-software equipment

## Practical Farmer Options

### Option 1: Use Official Channels

**Dealer Service:**
- Pros: Factory-trained technicians, warranty protection, latest tools
- Cons: Higher cost, potential delays, distance from farm

**Customer Service ADVISOR:**
- Purchase for enhanced access
- Still limited vs. dealer tools
- Learning curve for diagnostics

### Option 2: Workarounds

**Eastern European Software:**
- Unauthorized diagnostic tools available
- Legal gray area
- Warranty voiding risk
- Safety concerns

**Older Equipment:**
- Pre-2010 equipment has no software locks
- Lower purchase prices
- Simpler mechanical systems
- Growing market for legacy tractors

### Option 3: Advocacy

**Legislative Action:**
- Support state right-to-repair bills
- Contact representatives
- Join farmer advocacy groups

**Legal Participation:**
- Class action lawsuits
- Document repair issues
- Share experiences publicly

## Cybersecurity Arguments

### Manufacturer Position

**John Deere Claims:**
- Open software enables IP theft
- Competitors (China) could copy technology
- Safety systems could be compromised
- Emissions controls could be bypassed
- Cybersecurity risks increase

**Canadian Equipment Dealers Association (2023):**
> "Cybersecurity and the threat of somebody stealing corporate secrets is top of mind... I think the whole industry is at risk of having intellectual property stolen."

### Farmer Position

**Counterarguments:**
- Other industries manage open repair (automotive)
- Safety and emissions can be regulated separately
- Farmers own the equipment and should control it
- Monopoly pricing hurts rural economies
- Delays during planting/harvest are unacceptable

   - Track downtime costs
   - Save dealer communications

2. **Stay Informed:**

   - Follow class action progress

3. **Evaluate Equipment:**

   - Research brand policies
   - Factor service availability into ROI

4. **Build Relationships:**
   - Work with dealers when possible
   - Negotiate service agreements
   - Explore independent shop capabilities
