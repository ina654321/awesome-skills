# Nike FY2025 Annual Report Summary

> **Source:** NIKE, Inc. Reports Fiscal 2025 Fourth Quarter and Full Year Results (June 26, 2025)

---

## Executive Summary

Nike's fiscal year 2025 (ended May 31, 2025) was a year of significant challenge and strategic transition. Under outgoing CEO John Donahue, the company's DTC-focused strategy failed to deliver, resulting in a 10% revenue decline and $27.5 billion in lost market value. In October 2024, Elliott Hill, a 32-year Nike veteran, took the helm with the "Win Now" turnaround strategy.

## Key Strategic Developments

### Leadership Transition

**October 2024:** Elliott Hill succeeded John Donahoe as President and CEO. Hill, who retired from Nike in 2020 after 32 years, previously led the company's Europe and Americas operations. His appointment signaled a return to Nike's wholesale and sport-focused roots.

### "Win Now" Turnaround Strategy

Launched December 2024, the five-pillar strategy:

1. **Culture** — Reconnect with Nike's athletic DNA
2. **Product** — Performance innovation first
3. **Marketing** — Sport storytelling over lifestyle
4. **Marketplace** — Rebuild wholesale partnerships
5. **In-Person** — Physical experience investment

### Organizational Restructuring

- Eliminated consumer-segment structure (Men's/Women's/Kids') in favor of sport-specific teams
- Created "Sport Offense" initiative, realigning 8,000 employees
- Brought geographic leaders (Greater China, EMEA, North America, APLA) directly to senior leadership team
- Combined supply chain and technology under new COO Venkatesh Alagirisamy

## Segment Analysis

### Nike Direct (DTC): $18.8B (-13%)

The DTC decline reflects both strategic pullback and channel normalization:
- Nike Brand Digital: -20%
- Nike-owned Stores: Flat
- The Donahue-era "Consumer Direct Acceleration" strategy overestimated DTC scalability

### Wholesale: $25.9B (-7%)

While still declining, wholesale showed relative resilience and strategic priority under Hill:
- Renewed partnerships with Foot Locker, Dick's Sporting Goods
- Return to Amazon announced for 2025
- Better product allocation and retail media investments

### Jordan Brand: $7.3B (-16%)

After years of outperformance (doubling revenue 2020-2024), Jordan faced:
- Saturation in retro/lifestyle market
- Increased competition from Adidas
- Transition in leadership with Sarah Mensah as President

### Converse: $1.7B (-19%)

Converse continues to struggle with brand relevance:
- Declines across all territories
- Lifestyle positioning losing to both Nike mainline and competitors
- Turnaround strategy TBD

## Operational Metrics

### Manufacturing & Supply Chain

- **Vietnam**: ~50% of footwear production
- **Indonesia**: ~18% of footwear production  
- **China**: ~16% (down from ~27%), focused on premium/innovation
- Tariff headwind: $1.5B in FY2025; targeting <$1B by FY2026 through diversification

### Sustainability Progress (Move to Zero)

| Renewable Electricity | 96% of global operations |
| Waste Diversion | 100% at strategic suppliers |
| Environmentally Preferred Materials | 48% (target: 50% by 2025) |

### Digital Ecosystem

- Nike Run Club: Active but engagement metrics not disclosed
- Nike Training Club: Part of membership strategy
- SNKRS: Key platform for sneaker culture and full-price sell-through
- Nike App: Central membership hub

## Risk Factors

1. **China Market Weakness**: Six consecutive quarters of revenue decline; competitive pressure from local brands (Anta, Li-Ning)
2. **Tariff Exposure**: Potential $1B+ annual impact if trade tensions escalate
3. **DTC Transition Costs**: Store closures, digital investment write-downs
4. **Brand Heat**: Lifestyle franchises (Air Force 1, Dunk) showing saturation
5. **Competition**: On Running, Hoka gaining running specialty share; Adidas resurgence

---

## Forward Guidance

CEO Elliott Hill stated in Q4 FY2025 earnings call:

> "While our financial results are in line with our expectations, they are not where we want them to be. Moving forward, we expect our business to improve as a result of the progress we are making through our Win Now actions."

**FY2026 Priorities:**
- Return to sport-specific organizational structure
- Wholesale revenue growth (already +8% in Q2 FY2026)
- Product innovation cadence acceleration (Vomero 18 success: $100M+ sales)
- China market reset

## Key Takeaways

1. **FY2025 was a reset year** — The DTC strategy failure forced strategic and leadership changes
2. **Wholesale is back** — Hill's "Win Now" prioritizes partnerships over channel conflict
3. **Product innovation matters** — Vomero 18 success shows Nike can still win with performance
4. **China remains the wildcard** — 17% decline needs addressing; local competition intensifying
5. **Sustainability is embedded** — Move to Zero progress continues despite financial headwinds
