# Nike "Win Now" Turnaround Strategy

> **Source:** Nike earnings calls, Elliott Hill statements, WWD, SportsPro, Business Chief (2024-2026)

---

## Strategy Origin

In October 2024, Elliott Hill returned from retirement to become Nike's CEO. A 32-year Nike veteran who had led the company's Europe and Americas operations before retiring in 2020, Hill was tasked with reversing the damage from the failed DTC strategy under predecessor John Donahue.

The "Win Now" strategy was formally announced in December 2024, representing Nike's most significant strategic pivot in a decade.

---

## The Five Pillars

### 1. Culture: Return to Athletic DNA

**Problem:** Under Donahue, Nike eliminated category-specific teams and became overly focused on digital metrics rather than sport expertise.

**Solution:**
- Reorganize around sport-specific pillars (Running, Basketball, Football, etc.)
- Bring back "swoosh-crazed" culture of product obsession
- Remove management layers to get closer to athletes and marketplace
- Realign 8,000 employees to "Sport Offense" structure

**Elliott Hill Quote:**
> "We need to reset our approach. What we've done is a start, but it's not happening at the level or the pace we need to drive wider change."

---

### 2. Product: Performance Innovation First

**Problem:** Over-reliance on lifestyle franchises (Air Force 1, Dunk) and retro products led to brand heat decline and competition gaining ground in performance categories.

**Solution:**
- Accelerate NSRL (Nike Sport Research Lab) output
- Launch cadence focused on solving athlete problems
- Vomero 18 success model: $100M+ in sales, specialty retailer support
- Reduce dependence on "evergreen" franchises

**Key Product Wins:**
- Vomero 18: Revamped running shoe surpassing $100M sales
- Project Amplify: Wearable mechanized assist (2028 target)
- Mind 001/002: Neuroscience-informed recovery footwear

---

### 3. Marketing: Sport Storytelling

**Problem:** Marketing became too data-driven and performance-marketing focused under Donahue's digital-first approach, losing brand storytelling magic.

**Solution:**
- Return to brand marketing over pure performance marketing
- Athlete storytelling as core narrative driver
- Sport moments over lifestyle trends
- "Just Do It" ethos reactivation

**Key Insight:**
Marketing veteran Massimo Giunco (20+ years at Nike) noted the previous strategy's flaw:
> "The tactification of marketing: Brand marketing vs. performance marketing—an unnecessary war."

---

### 4. Marketplace: Wholesale Partnership Revival

**Problem:** DTC push alienated wholesale partners (Foot Locker, Dick's, independent retailers), reducing brand visibility and ceding shelf space to competitors.

**Solution:**
- Return to Amazon (pulled under Donahue)
- Expand shelf space at Foot Locker and other key accounts
- Better product allocation to partners
- Retail media partnerships
- "Growing the complete marketplace" (Hill's phrase)

**Results (Q2 FY2026):**
| Channel | Revenue | Change |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Wholesale | $7.5B | +8% |
| Nike Direct | $4.6B | -8% |

**Strategic Logic:**
Nike serves athletes through retailers, not around them. The wholesale comeback recognizes that discovery happens in stores.

---

### 5. In-Person: Experience Investment

**Problem:** Over-indexing on digital led to neglect of physical retail experience.

**Solution:**
- "House of Innovation" flagship upgrades (Fifth Avenue store reconfigured)
- In-store experience integration with digital tools
- Nike Run Club local activation
- Community event investment

**Flagship Example:**
Nike's Fifth Avenue store at 52nd Street was reconfigured with sport-focused "House of Innovation" concept, showcasing the new experience model.

## Organizational Changes

### Leadership Restructuring (December 2025)

| Change | From | To |
|--------|------|-----|
| CEO | John Donahue (ousted) | Elliott Hill |
| COO Role Created | N/A | Venkatesh Alagirisamy |
| Technology | Standalone CTO (eliminated) | Under COO |
| Commercial | Craig Williams (EVP/CCO, departed) | Matthew Friend (CFO absorbing role) |
| Geography | Buried in hierarchy | Direct to Hill |

**Key Departures:**
- **Craig Williams**: EVP, Chief Commercial Officer and Jordan Brand President; departed despite growing Jordan Brand 37% over 3 years
- **Dr. Muge Dogan**: Chief Technology Officer; role eliminated

**Key Appointments:**
- **Venkatesh Alagirisamy**: 20-year veteran becomes COO, combining supply chain + technology
- **Geographic Leaders**: Angela Dong (Greater China), Carl Grebert (EMEA), Tom Peddie (North America), Cathy Sparks (APLA) now report directly to Hill

### Structural Changes

**Old Structure (Donahue Era):**
- Organized by consumer segments: Men's, Women's, Kids'
- Centralized marketing
- DTC-first channel strategy

**New Structure (Hill Era):**
- Organized by sport: Running, Basketball, Football, etc.
- Sport-specific marketing
- Balanced wholesale + DTC

### Positive Indicators

1. **Wholesale rebound** validates marketplace strategy
2. **North America strength** (+9%) shows core market response
3. **Running category** gaining momentum vs. On/Hoka
4. **Vomero 18** success proves product innovation still wins

### Ongoing Challenges

1. **China weakness** (-17%): Six consecutive quarters of decline
2. **Converse decline** (-19%): Brand turnaround needed
3. **Margin pressure** from discounting and channel mix
4. **Tariff headwinds**: $1.5B impact in FY2025

## Strategic Philosophy

### The Core Belief

Hill's "Win Now" represents a return to Nike's fundamental identity:

> "We're not really just a sportswear company. We're a 'better solutions for athletes' company."
> — Michael Donaghu, VP of Nike Space Kitchen

### Key Principles

1. **Athletes First**: Every decision starts with athlete benefit
2. **Sport Not Segments**: Organize around sports, not demographics
3. **Partnership Over Control**: Wholesale relationships are assets, not liabilities
4. **Innovation At Scale**: Breakthrough products that reach millions
5. **Culture of Excellence**: "Swoosh-crazed" obsession with product and athletes

---

## Competitive Response

### vs. On Running and Hoka

Nike's wholesale focus directly addresses On and Hoka's specialty running strength:
- On and Hoka built relationships with run-specialty retailers while Nike pursued DTC
- Hill's strategy: "We're not trying to beat them at their game; we're returning to our own."

### vs. Adidas

Adidas resurgence under new leadership mirrors Nike's challenges:
- Both brands restructuring around sport-specific teams
- Both prioritizing wholesale relationships
- Competition intensifying in lifestyle and performance

### vs. Local Chinese Brands (Anta, Li-Ning)

China's -17% decline reflects local brand strength:
- Anta's multi-brand strategy (including Amer Sports) gaining share
- Li-Ning's premium positioning working with Chinese consumers
- Nike's "Win Now" China reset still in early stages

## Conclusion

"Win Now" is Nike's most significant strategic reset since the original "Consumer Direct Offense" of 2017. It represents an admission that the DTC-first strategy failed while reaffirming Nike's core identity as a product innovation company serving athletes through multiple channels.

The early results—particularly the wholesale rebound and North America strength—validate the strategy's direction. However, challenges in China, margin pressure, and competitive intensity mean the turnaround is far from complete.

The ultimate measure of success will be whether Nike can return to consistent revenue growth while maintaining its position as the world's leading athletic brand.
