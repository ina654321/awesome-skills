# Nike Sport Research Lab (NSRL)

> **Source:** Sneaker Freaker, Nike communications, Innovation Kitchen features (2024-2025)

---

## Overview

The Nike Sport Research Lab (NSRL) is Nike's premier athlete research and innovation facility, located within the LeBron James Innovation Center at Nike World Headquarters in Beaverton, Oregon. The NSRL represents the scientific foundation of Nike's product innovation, combining cutting-edge technology with deep athlete insight.

**Mission:** "To bring inspiration and innovation to every athlete by solving new problems that are unsolved."

---

## Facility

### Location

**LeBron James Innovation Center**
- Nike World Headquarters, Beaverton, Oregon
- Part of the Philip H. Knight Campus
- 750,000+ square feet
- Opened 2021

### Key Features

**The NSRL Includes:**
- Motion capture laboratories
- Environmental chambers
- 3D printing and prototyping labs
- Material testing facilities
- Biomechanics research areas
- Physiology testing equipment

**Advanced Equipment:**
- High-speed cameras (1000+ fps)
- Force plates and pressure mapping
- 3D body scanning
- Climate simulation (heat, cold, altitude)
- Treadmills with variable surfaces
- EMG (muscle activity) sensors

## Research Capabilities

### Biomechanics

**Motion Analysis:**
- Full-body 3D motion capture
- Joint angle and velocity measurement
- Gait analysis for running and walking
- Sport-specific movement patterns

**Force Measurement:**
- Ground reaction forces
- Pressure distribution mapping
- Impact absorption analysis
- Stability and balance assessment

### Physiology

**Environmental Testing:**
- Heat chamber (up to 120°F / 49°C)
- Cold chamber (down to -4°F / -20°C)
- Altitude simulation
- Humidity control

**Athlete Monitoring:**
- Core temperature tracking
- Sweat rate analysis
- Heart rate variability
- Oxygen consumption (VO2 max)

### Materials Science

**Testing Capabilities:**
- Abrasion and durability
- Flex and fatigue
- Water resistance
- Thermal properties
- Chemical analysis

**Prototyping:**
- 3D printing (multiple technologies)
- Rapid molding
- Material combination testing
- Custom foam formulations

### Phase 1: Athlete Problem Identification

**Process:**
- Elite athlete testing
- Movement dysfunction identification
- Performance limitation analysis

**Process:**

- Rapid prototyping

**Output:** Validated concept

### Phase 3: Engineering

**Process:**
- Detailed design

### Phase 4: Validation

**Process:**
- Large-sample athlete testing
- Statistical analysis
- Real-world validation
- Final adjustments

**Output:** Product approval for manufacturing
