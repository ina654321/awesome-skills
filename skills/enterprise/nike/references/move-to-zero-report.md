# Nike Move to Zero Sustainability Progress

> **Source:** Nike Sustainability Reports FY2024-2025, Nike.com/sustainability, FY24 Sustainability Data PDF

---

## Executive Summary

"Move to Zero" is Nike's sustainability mission to achieve zero carbon and zero waste. Launched in 2019, the initiative represents Nike's commitment to protecting the future of sport by addressing climate change through ambitious emissions reductions, circular design, and sustainable materials.

As of FY2025, Nike has achieved significant progress, including a 69% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions and 96% renewable electricity across global operations.

---

## Move to Zero Framework

### Two Core Goals

1. **Zero Carbon**: Achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions
2. **Zero Waste**: Eliminate waste across the value chain

|--------|------|----------|--------|

| Renewable Electricity | 100% | 2025 | 96% achieved |
| Environmentally Preferred Materials | 50% of key materials | 2025 | 48% (on track) |

## Key Initiatives

### 1. Sustainable Materials

**Recycled Polyester**
- 183,619 metric tons used in FY2024
- Two-thirds recycled content
- 13 billion plastic bottles diverted from landfills (fiscal 2023)

**Circular Polyester Innovation**
- Partnership with Syre for textile-to-textile recycling
- Shift from bottle-based to textile-based recycled polyester
- Goal: Reduce reliance on virgin plastic

**Organic and Recycled Cotton**
- Expansion of organic cotton sourcing
- Recycled cotton integration
- Third-party certified cotton programs

**Flyleather**
- Made with at least 50% recycled natural leather fiber
- Lower carbon footprint than traditional leather
- Performance benefits: lighter, more consistent

### 2. Nike Grind

**Program Overview:**
Nike Grind converts manufacturing scrap and end-of-life shoes into new products and surfaces.

**Output Categories:**
- **Nike Grind Rubber**: Outsoles, track surfaces
- **Nike Grind Foam**: Cushioning components
- **Nike Grind Fiber**: Textile applications
- **Nike Grind Upper**: Material for new shoes

**Impact:**
- 120+ million pounds of material diverted from landfill since program launch
- Used in 71% of Nike footwear and apparel products
- Partners: 10,000+ sporting locations worldwide

### 3. Space Hippie

**The Product:**
Space Hippie represents Nike's lowest carbon footprint shoe, made from factory scraps and recycled materials.

**Specifications:**
- Upper: 85-90% recycled content (plastic bottles, t-shirts, post-industrial scraps)
- Midsole: "Crater Foam" with Nike Grind rubber
- Packaging: FSC-certified, minimal design

**Significance:**
- Proof that sustainability and performance aren't mutually exclusive
- Demonstrates circular design principles at scale
- Price point accessible to mass market ($130-$180)

### 4. Circular Design

**Principles:**
1. **Design for Disassembly**: Products that can be easily taken apart for recycling
2. **Material Efficiency**: Minimize waste in production
3. **Longevity**: Products built to last
4. **Recyclability**: End-of-life material recovery

**Implementation:**
- Circular design training for product teams
- Material passport system for traceability
- Take-back programs for end-of-life products

### 5. Renewable Energy

**Owned Facilities:**
- 96% renewable electricity in FY2024
- 100% target by 2025
- Solar installations at major facilities

**Manufacturing Partners:**
- Renewable energy programs for contract factories
- Supplier incentives for clean energy adoption
- Vietnam and Indonesia factory solar projects

### 6. Water Stewardship

**Freshwater Reduction:**
- 40% reduction in freshwater use by material suppliers over past decade
- 15% reduction in dyeing and finishing vs FY2020
- Automation and wastewater recycling technologies

**Zero Discharge of Hazardous Chemicals (ZDHC):**
- 90%+ of strategic suppliers meeting ZDHC wastewater guidelines
- Chemical management across supply chain
- Water quality monitoring in priority basins

**Partnerships:**
- The Nature Conservancy (TNC)
- World Wildlife Fund (WWF)
- Focus: Australia, Brazil, India, Pakistan, U.S. cotton regions

## Manufacturing & Supply Chain Integration

### Carbon Footprint by Source (FY2024)

| Source | Share of Emissions |
|--------|-------------------|
| Scope 3 (Supply Chain) | 99.16% |

- Environmental standards for contract manufacturers

**Strategic Supplier Program:**

## Challenges & Trade-offs

### 1. Leather Usage

**Challenge:**
- Demand for iconic leather products (Air Force 1, Jordan 1) has increased 35%
- Leather has high carbon footprint
- Conflict with Move to Zero goals

**Response:**
- Lower Carbon Impact Leather certification
- Leather Working Group (LWG) certification for 100% of leather
- Exploring alternatives like Flyleather

### 2. Growth vs. Sustainability

**Challenge:**
- Revenue growth requires production growth
- Absolute emissions reduction vs. intensity metrics
- Consumer demand for new products

**Response:**
- Circular business models (refurbishment, resale)
- Product durability and longevity
- "Quality over quantity" positioning

### 3. Supply Chain Complexity

**Challenge:**
- Thousands of suppliers across dozens of countries
- Varying local regulations and capabilities
- Traceability at scale

**Response:**
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 supplier focus
- Digital supply chain tools
- Strategic supplier partnerships

## Critical Assessment

### Strengths

1. **Ambitious Targets**: Science-based, Paris Agreement-aligned
2. **Integration**: Sustainability embedded in product creation
3. **Innovation**: Material and manufacturing breakthroughs
4. **Transparency**: Public reporting with third-party assurance
5. **Scale**: Global supply chain transformation

### Areas for Improvement

1. **Absolute Emissions**: Growth offsets efficiency gains in Scope 3
2. **Leather**: Iconic products conflict with carbon goals
3. **Consumer Behavior**: Fast fashion model vs. sustainability messaging
4. **Supply Chain Depth**: Tier 2+ supplier visibility limited
5. **Circularity**: End-of-life product recovery rates low

### Industry Context

Nike's Move to Zero is among the most comprehensive sustainability programs in apparel/footwear. Compared to peers:

- **Adidas**: Similar targets, partnership with Parley for Oceans
- **Patagonia**: Smaller scale, deeper integration
- **Allbirds**: Born sustainable, limited product range
- **H&M**: Fast fashion model creates structural tension

Nike's advantage: Scale enables impact; challenge is aligning growth with absolute reduction.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Move to Zero is on track**: Scope 1&2 target exceeded; Scope 3 ahead of schedule

3. **Supply chain is the battleground**: 99% of emissions are Scope 3
4. **Challenges remain**: Leather usage growth conflicts with carbon goals
5. **Industry leadership**: Nike's scale makes Move to Zero a sector benchmark
