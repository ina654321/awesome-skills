# Nike Manufacturing & Supply Chain

> **Source:** Nike financial reports, Sellers Union China, Supply Chain World (2024-2025)

---

## Executive Summary

Nike operates one of the world's most complex apparel and footwear supply chains, producing approximately 780 million pairs of shoes annually. Manufacturing is concentrated in Vietnam (50%), Indonesia (18%), and China (16%), with a strategic shift away from China to mitigate tariff exposure.

The supply chain employs over 950,000 workers directly in contract factories and supports millions more in related industries. Nike's "Manufacturing Revolution" focuses on sustainability, automation, and supply chain resilience.

### Vietnam: The Manufacturing Powerhouse

**Overview:**
- Largest Nike manufacturing base globally
- ~50% of all Nike footwear produced here
- 450,000+ direct workers in contract factories
- $8.7B annual export value (2024)
- 0.8% of Vietnam's total GDP

**Key Production Regions:**
- **Ho Chi Minh City**: Largest factory concentration
- **Dong Nai Province**: High-capacity facilities
- **Binh Duong Province**: Specialized athletic footwear
- **Long An Province**: Growing manufacturing hub

**Factory Characteristics:**
- Average size: 5,000-15,000 workers
- Annual capacity: 80-120 million pairs per region
- Specialization: Running, basketball, lifestyle footwear

**Economic Impact:**
- Direct employment: 450,000+
- Indirect employment: 1.2 million
- Annual wages paid: $2.1 billion USD

### Indonesia: The Rising Star

**Growth Drivers:**
- Competitive labor costs
- Government support for foreign investment
- Expanding manufacturing infrastructure
- Strategic Southeast Asia location

**Production Focus:**
- Casual footwear and lifestyle products
- Basic athletic shoes
- Apparel manufacturing

**Economic Impact:**
- Direct employment: 180,000+
- Annual wages paid: $720 million USD
- Export value: $2.8 billion USD

### China: The Innovation Center

**Strategic Shift:**
- Manufacturing share declining (from ~27% to ~16%)
- Focus on premium products and innovation
- Prototype development and limited editions
- High-end product lines

**Regional Focus:**
- **Guangdong Province**: Traditional manufacturing base, Dongguan hub
- **Fujian Province**: Specialized athletic footwear
- **Jiangsu Province**: Technology and innovation centers

**Economic Impact:**
- Direct employment: 320,000+
- Annual wages paid: $1.8 billion USD
- Export value: $4.2 billion USD
- Technology transfer to supplier ecosystem

## Supply Chain Structure

### Tier System

**Tier 1: Finished Goods Manufacturers**
- Contract factories producing final products
- Nike's direct manufacturing partners
- 100+ factories globally
- Represent ~80% of production volume

**Tier 2: Material Suppliers**
- Textile mills, dyeing facilities
- Component manufacturers
- Rubber, foam, leather processors
- Represent ~60% of material volume

**Tier 3: Raw Material Suppliers**
- Petrochemical inputs
- Natural rubber plantations
- Leather tanneries
- Cotton farms

### Key Suppliers

**Raw Materials:**
- **Leather**: Brazil, Argentina, India, Vietnam
- **Rubber**: Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, Vietnam
- **Textiles**: China, Taiwan, South Korea, Turkey
- **Synthetic Materials**: Germany, USA, Japan, South Korea
- **Foam Components**: China, Vietnam, USA, Germany

**Component Suppliers:**
- Outsoles: Specialized rubber molding
- Midsoles: Foam injection molding
- Hardware: Metal components, eyelets
- Laces and textiles: Specialized textile manufacturers
- Packaging: Boxes, tissue paper, bags

## Sustainability Integration

### Move to Zero in Manufacturing

**Carbon Reduction:**
- 69% reduction in Scope 1&2 emissions (FY2024 vs FY2020)
- 36% reduction in Scope 3 manufacturing/transportation
- Renewable energy programs at supplier factories

**Water Stewardship:**
- 15% freshwater reduction in dyeing/finishing (vs FY2020)
- 90%+ of strategic suppliers meeting ZDHC wastewater guidelines
- Water recycling technologies

**Waste Reduction:**
- 100% waste diversion at strategic finished goods suppliers
- 60%+ reused or recycled
- Nike Grind program integration

### Sustainable Materials

**Environmentally Preferred Materials (EPM):**
- Target: 50% of key materials by 2025
- Current: 48% achieved
- Includes recycled polyester, organic cotton, sustainable leather

**Recycled Polyester:**
- 2/3 of total polyester usage
- 13 billion plastic bottles diverted (FY2023)
- Shift to textile-to-textile recycling (Syre partnership)

## Tariff & Trade Strategy

### Current Tariff Exposure

**FY2025:**
- Average U.S. customs tax rate: Mid-teens for imported footwear
- Tariff headwind: $1.5 billion
- Target FY2026: <$1 billion

### Mitigation Strategies

**1. China Diversification**
- Reduce China footwear imports from 16% to high single digits
- Shift production to Vietnam, Indonesia, Cambodia
- Maintain China for premium/innovation products

**2. Supplier Negotiations**
- Shared cost responsibility with contract manufacturers
- Production scheduling optimization
- Transportation cost management

**3. Pricing Strategy**
- Strategic price increases to offset tariffs
- Focus on full-price sell-through
- Reduce promotional dependency

### Geographic Risk Management

**Multi-Country Sourcing:**
- No single country dominates (Vietnam 50%, but diversified within)
- Backup suppliers in multiple regions
- Flexible capacity agreements

## Conclusion

Nike's manufacturing and supply chain is a massive, complex operation balancing cost, speed, quality, and sustainability. The current strategic priorities are:

1. **Diversification**: Reducing China exposure
2. **Sustainability**: Move to Zero integration
3. **Resilience**: Multi-country sourcing
4. **Automation**: Efficiency and cost management
5. **Tariff Mitigation**: Supply chain optimization

The supply chain remains both a competitive advantage and a vulnerability—scale enables efficiency and innovation, but complexity creates risk. The "Win Now" strategy under Elliott Hill recognizes that manufacturing excellence is foundational to product innovation and athlete service.
