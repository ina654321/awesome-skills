# Strategic Partnerships

## Overview

Regeneron's partnership strategy centers on **"following the science"** to find collaborators who complement and expand upon its internal capabilities. The company maintains a balanced approach between in-house development and strategic alliances.

> "We look for partners who can complement and expand upon Regeneron's extensive in-house capabilities, strengthening our collective ability to address serious diseases and help people in need."

## Major Partnerships

### Sanofi (Antibody Collaboration)

**Relationship Timeline:**
| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2007 | Initial collaboration established |
| 2015 | Immuno-oncology collaboration added |
| 2018 | Agreement expanded and extended |
| 2022 | Amended to increase Regeneron profit share (10% → 20% for cost reimbursement) |

**Deal Structure:**
| Component | Terms |
|-----------|-------|
| **Scope** | Monoclonal and bispecific antibodies |
| **Development** | Shared costs |
| **Commercialization** | Sanofi leads (ex-US primarily) |
| **Profit Sharing** | Regeneron receives share of profits |
| **Development Balance** | ~$1.6B (to be reimbursed by end 2026) |

**Products:**
| Drug | Status | 2024 Global Sales |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| **Dupixent®** | Approved (7+ indications) | $14.15 billion |
| **Kevzara®** | Approved | Modest |
| **Itepekimab** | Phase 3 | - |

**Dupixent Economics:**
- Sanofi records global net product sales
- Regeneron records share of profits (~20% of sales after cost reimbursement)
- Regeneron's 2024 share: ~$4B+
- 1.3+ million patients globally

**Strategic Importance:**
- Sanofi's largest revenue contributor
- Both companies' most valuable asset
- Ongoing lifecycle expansion

**Recent Challenges:**
- November 2024: Regeneron filed lawsuit against Sanofi
- **Allegation:** Failure to provide "full access to material information" on PBM contracts, discounts, rebates
- **Regeneron claim:** Undisclosed errors costing "many multiples of $75,000"
- **Sanofi response:** "Fully compliant with collaboration agreement"
- **Lesson:** Governance and transparency critical in partnerships

## Partnership Philosophy

### When to Partner vs. Build

| Factor | Build Internally | Partner |
|--------|------------------|---------|
| **Core expertise** | Yes | No |
| **Time to market critical** | No | Yes |
| **High technical risk** | No | Yes |
| **Proven external platform** | Maybe | Yes |
| **Geographic expansion** | No | Yes |
| **Cost sharing needed** | No | Yes |

### Deal Structure Preferences

**Preferred Terms:**
- Co-development with shared control
- Profit sharing vs. pure royalties
- US co-promotion rights
- Transparent pricing/rebate access
- Freedom to use learnings elsewhere

**Avoid:**
- Loss of development control
- Undisclosed commercial terms
- Restrictive non-competes
- Pure licensing without collaboration

### Governance Best Practices

**Lessons from Sanofi Experience:**
1. **Transparency:** Full access to commercial information
2. **Governance:** Equal representation in decision-making
3. **Dispute Resolution:** Clear escalation procedures
4. **Audit Rights:** Regular financial audits
5. **Communication:** Frequent executive alignment
