## Strategic Framework: Powering Progress

Shell's strategy delivers **"more value with less emissions"** through the energy transition.

### Three Strategic Pillars

1. **Performance** — Competitive returns, operational excellence, cost discipline
2. **Discipline** — Capital allocation rigor, value-over-volume, financial resilience
3. **Simplification** — Reduced organizational complexity, focused portfolio

### Vision

> "To become the world's leading integrated energy company—delivering impact at scale, connecting energy and people, matching supply to demand."

## Business Segments

### 1. Integrated Gas

**Role:** World-leading LNG business, core cash generator through transition

**Key Statistics (2025):**
- Income: $8.8 billion
- Adjusted Earnings: $8.0 billion
- Cash Flow from Operations: $14.1 billion
- Production: 931 thousand boe/d
- LNG Liquefaction: 28 million tonnes
- LNG Sales: 73 million tonnes (+11% YoY, record high)

**Strategy:**
- Grow LNG sales 4-5% annually (CAGR) through 2030
- Maintain leadership position through acquisitions (Pavilion Energy, 2025)
- Major projects: LNG Canada (40% interest, started production 2025), Qatar North Field Expansion

**Leadership:** Cederic Cremers, President

---

### 2. Upstream

**Role:** Oil and gas production, stable cash generation

**Strategy:**
- Maintain ~1.4 million barrels/day liquids production through 2030
- Lower carbon intensity of production
- Grow combined Upstream + Integrated Gas production 1% annually to 2030

**Key Projects:**
- Vito platform (Gulf of Mexico): 80% lower emissions than original design
- Whale and Sparta platforms (following Vito design)
- Mina West (Egypt), Aphrodite (Trinidad and Tobago), Gorgon Stage 3 (Australia)

---

### 3. Chemicals and Products

**Role:** Refining, chemicals manufacturing, trading and optimization

**Key Statistics (2025):**
- Income: $0.3 billion
- Adjusted Earnings: $1.1 billion
- Cash Flow: $5.4 billion
- Refinery Processing: 1,217 thousand b/d
- Product Sales: 1,005 thousand b/d
- Chemicals Sales: 9,260 thousand tonnes

**Portfolio Transformation:**
- Divested Shell Energy and Chemicals Park Singapore (2025)
- Sold Colonial Pipeline interest ($1B proceeds)
- Repositioning to 4 regional energy and chemicals parks
- Paused Rotterdam biofuels facility construction (2025)

**Leadership:** Machteld de Haan, President, Downstream, Renewables and Energy Solutions

## Dividend History

### The 2020 Dividend Cut

**April 30, 2020:** Shell announced a historic dividend cut from $0.47 to $0.16 per share—a 66% reduction.

**Context:**
- First dividend cut since 1945
- Driven by COVID-19 pandemic and oil price collapse
- Part of strategic restructuring and simplification
- Job reductions: 7,000-9,000 positions by 2022

**Restoration:**
- Progressive restoration since 2020
- Current quarterly dividend: ~$0.34+ per share
- Progressive dividend policy maintained
