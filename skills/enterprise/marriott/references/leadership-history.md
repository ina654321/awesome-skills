# Marriott Leadership & History

## Executive Leadership Timeline

### Founding Era (1927-1957)

**J. Willard Marriott (1900-1985)**
- Founded The Hot Shoppe in 1927 (root beer stand)
- Expanded to restaurant chain throughout 1930s-1940s
- Opened first hotel in 1957 (Twin Bridges Marriott Motor Hotel)
- Philosophy: "Take care of your people and they will take care of your customers"
- Legacy: Established the service culture that defines Marriott today

### Expansion Era (1957-2012)

**J.W. "Bill" Marriott, Jr. (born 1932)**
- Joined company in 1956 at age 24
- Became CEO in 1972
- Expanded from 4 hotels to 2,800+ properties
- Led international expansion and brand diversification
- Stepped down as CEO in 2012, remains Executive Chairman
- Legacy: Transformed Marriott from family business to global hospitality leader

**Key Achievements:**
- Split company into Marriott International (operating) and Host Marriott (REIT) in 1993
- Launched Marriott Rewards loyalty program (predecessor to Bonvoy)
- Expanded into timeshare (Marriott Vacation Club)
- Entered luxury segment with Ritz-Carlton acquisition

### Modern Era (2012-2021)

**Arne Sorenson (1958-2021)**
- First non-family CEO in Marriott history (appointed 2012)
- Background: Attorney, M&A specialist, joined Marriott 1996
- Led transformational $13.6B Starwood acquisition (2016)
- Oversaw creation of world's largest hotel company
- Championed diversity, inclusion, and sustainability
- Navigated company through COVID-19 pandemic
- Died February 15, 2021 after battle with pancreatic cancer

**Arne Sorenson's Legacy:**
- **Scale**: Grew from 3,800 to 7,600+ properties
- **Portfolio**: Added Sheraton, Westin, St. Regis, W Hotels through Starwood
- **Loyalty**: Created unified Marriott Bonvoy program
- **Culture**: Advocated for LGBTQ+ rights, racial equality, environmental sustainability
- **Service**: Spoke directly on social issues; established "Serve 360" platform
- **Crisis Leadership**: Forewent salary during COVID-19; prioritized associate retention

**Quotes:**
> "What we can do is to bring out the best in society and the best in each of us."

> "We are defined by how we treat people—our associates, our guests, and our communities."

### Current Leadership (2021-Present)

**Anthony Capuano (born 1966)**
- Appointed CEO February 2021 following Arne Sorenson's death
- Background: Cornell Hotel School graduate (1987)
- Joined Marriott 1995 in Market Planning and Feasibility
- Rose through development ranks to lead Global Development
- Former Group President: Global Development, Design & Operations Services
- Also serves on McDonald's Corporation Board of Directors
- Chair of U.S. Travel and Tourism Advisory Board

**Capuano's Priorities:**
1. **Growth**: Expanded to 9,800+ properties (from 7,600 at start of tenure)
2. **Efficiency**: Enterprise-wide reorganization ($80-90M savings)
3. **Luxury**: Aggressive expansion of luxury portfolio (260+ in pipeline)
4. **Midscale**: Entry into midscale segment (City Express, StudioRes)
5. **Sustainability**: Net-zero by 2050 commitment
6. **Technology**: AI-driven operations, mobile-first guest experience

**Leadership Style:**
- Data-driven, operations-focused
- Emphasizes listening and humility
- Strong owner relationships (development background)
- Steady hand through economic uncertainty

**Key Achievements (2021-2025):**
- 4.3% net rooms growth annually
- $355M acquisition of citizenM (2024)
- Record pipeline of 4,100 properties
- Integration of MGM Resorts partnership (38,000 rooms)
- Post-pandemic recovery leadership

**Quotes:**
> "The entirety of the exercise was not simply a cost-cutting exercise. It was a recognition that we were in 70 more countries than we were the last time we looked at the organizational structure."

> "We welcome all to our hotels and we create opportunities for all—and fundamentally those will never change."

## The Starwood Acquisition (2016)

### Deal Overview

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Announced** | November 16, 2015 |
| **Closed** | September 23, 2016 |
| **Transaction Value** | $13.6 billion |
| **Payment Structure** | $21 cash + 0.8 Marriott shares per Starwood share |
| **Starwood Shareholders** | Received ~34% of combined company |
| **Regulatory Approval** | Required in US, EU, China, and 10+ jurisdictions |

### Competitive Bidding

| Bidder | Offer | Outcome |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Marriott (initial)** | $12.2 billion | Starwood agreed |
| **Anbang Consortium** | $14 billion (March 2016) | Starwood terminated Marriott deal |
| **Marriott (revised)** | $13.6 billion (March 21, 2016) | Starwood re-engaged |
| **Anbang (withdrawn)** | — | Withdrew due to regulatory concerns |

### Strategic Rationale

**For Marriott:**
- Instant global scale: 1.1M rooms combined
- Luxury portfolio: Added St. Regis, W Hotels, Luxury Collection
- International footprint: 75% of Starwood revenue from non-US markets
- Loyalty program: Starwood Preferred Guest had passionate following
- Fee revenue: Accelerated asset-light strategy

**Challenges:**
- $100M+ integration costs
- Cultural integration (Starwood more design-focused)
- Technology platform merger
- Data breach disclosure (383M guests, 2018)

### Post-Acquisition Results

| Metric | Pre-Merger (2015) | Post-Merger (2025) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Properties | 4,400 | 9,800+ |
| Rooms | 760,000 | 1,780,000 |
| Brands | 19 | 30+ |
| Countries | 85 | 145 |
| Bonvoy Members | 55M | 271M |

## Marriott-Sorenson Center for Hospitality Leadership

### Establishment (2021)

In honor of Arne Sorenson, the **Marriott-Sorenson Center for Hospitality Leadership** was established at Howard University School of Business.

**Funding:**
- J. Willard and Alice S. Marriott Foundation gift
- Ian Schrager: $1 million donation
- Marriott Bonvoy member donations (matched by Marriott)
- Total: Multi-million dollar endowment

**Mission:**
- Foster diverse leadership in hospitality industry
- Create pipeline of underrepresented talent for executive roles
- Honor Arne Sorenson's commitment to diversity and inclusion

**Programs:**
- Executive education
- Mentorship programs
- Research on hospitality leadership
- Career development initiatives

## Corporate Culture & Values

### Core Values ("The Marriott Way")

1. **Put People First**
   - Associates are the foundation
   - 90% employee endorsement rate (vs. 57% industry average)
   - "Take Care" culture

2. **Pursue Excellence**
   - Continuous improvement
   - Quality standards across all brands
   - Innovation in guest experience

3. **Act with Integrity**
   - Ethical business practices
   - Transparency with owners and guests
   - Corporate governance excellence

4. **Embrace Change**
   - Adaptability to market shifts
   - Technology adoption
   - Brand evolution

5. **Serve Our World**
   - Environmental sustainability (Serve 360)
   - Community engagement
   - Diversity and inclusion

### Notable Cultural Initiatives

**"Bridging the Gap" Program (2022)**
- $50 million commitment
- Goal: 3,000 diverse owners by 2025
- Support for Black, Latino, women, and Native American hotel owners
- Reduced fees, key money, training support

**DEI Commitment (2025)**
- Public reaffirmation of diversity, equity, and inclusion values
- CEO Capuano received 40,000+ supportive emails from associates after public stance
- Fortune 100 Best Companies to Work For (#8 in 2025)
