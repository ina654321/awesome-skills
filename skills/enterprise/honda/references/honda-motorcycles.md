# Honda Motorcycles Business

## Overview

Honda is the **world's largest motorcycle manufacturer** by volume, with a history dating to the company's founding in 1948. The motorcycle division remains a core business alongside automobiles.

## Business Scale

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Annual Sales** | ~17-19 million units globally |
| **Revenue Share** | ~15% of Honda total |
| **Global Rank** | #1 motorcycle manufacturer |
| **Key Markets** | Southeast Asia, India, Latin America, North America |
| **Employees** | Significant portion of ~197,000 total |

## Historical Milestones

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| **1949** | Dream D-Type, first complete motorcycle |
| **1958** | Super Cub debut - becomes best-selling motor vehicle ever |
| **1959** | Entry into Isle of Man TT |
| **1961** | First Isle of Man TT wins (125cc and 250cc) |
| **1969** | CB750 Four - "original superbike" |
| **1975** | Gold Wing GL1000 - touring legend begins |
| **1983** | NSR500 - GP dominance begins |
| **1992** | CBR900RR FireBlade - redefines sportbike |
| **2025** | Gold Wing 50th Anniversary |

## Technology Features

### DCT (Dual Clutch Transmission)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Availability** | Gold Wing, Africa Twin, Rebel 1100, NT1100 |
| **Modes** | Manual, D (Drive), S (Sport) |
| **Benefit** | Automatic convenience, manual control option |
| **Technology** | Two clutches for seamless shifts |

### E-Clutch (2024+)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Function** | Eliminates manual clutch lever |
| **Availability** | CBR650R, CB650R |
| **Operation** | Automatic clutch actuation |
| **Benefit** | Easier riding, retains manual shifting |

### Electronics

| Feature | Application |
