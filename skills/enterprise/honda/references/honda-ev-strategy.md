# Honda Electrification & EV Strategy

## Overview

Honda's electrification strategy aims for **carbon neutrality by 2050** with intermediate milestones for 2030 and 2040.

## Key Targets

| Year | Target |
|------|--------|
| **2026** | Launch 0 Series EV platform |
| **2030** | 2 million EV sales annually globally |
| **2035** | 100% EV sales in China |
| **2040** | 100% EV/FCV sales globally |
| **2050** | Carbon neutrality across all operations |

## Current EV: Prologue (2024-)

### Specifications

| Feature | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Platform** | GM Ultium (shared with Chevy Blazer EV) |
| **Battery** | 85 kWh lithium-ion |
| **Range (EPA)** | 308 miles (FWD) / 294 miles (AWD) |
| **Power** | 220 HP (FWD) / 300 HP (AWD) |
| **Charging** | DC fast charge up to 155 kW |
| **Price** | $47,400 - $57,400 (before incentives) |
| **Tax Credit** | $7,500 federal credit eligible |

### Sales Performance

- **2024**: 33,017 units sold (debut year, partial)
- Outperformed GM Equinox EV, Blazer EV, and Cadillac Lyriq
- Positioned as Honda's first mainstream EV

### Acura ZDX

- Luxury variant of Prologue platform
- Higher power output (up to 500 HP in Type S)
- Premium positioning vs. Honda Prologue

## Future EV: 0 Series (Launching 2026)

### Development Philosophy: "Thin, Light, Wise"

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Thin** | Low floor design, sleek proportions, maximized interior space |
| **Light** | Weight reduction ~100kg vs competitors, structural optimization |
| **Wise** | Intelligent systems, OTA updates, connected services |

| Feature | Target |

## Battery Strategy

### Near-term (2024-2026): Partnership Model

| Partner | Application | Details |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **GM (Ultium)** | Prologue, ZDX | Proven technology, immediate market entry |
| **LG Energy Solution** | Ohio production | 40+ GWh JV facility |
| **CATL** | China EVs | World's largest battery supplier |

### Mid-term (2026-2028): Joint Venture Production

**Honda-LG Energy Solution Joint Venture**
- Location: Fayette County, Ohio
- Investment: $4.4 billion (with GM participation)
- Capacity: 40+ GWh annually
- Production start: 2025
- Co-located with Honda EV Hub

### Long-term (2028+): Next-Generation Batteries

**All-Solid-State Batteries (ASSB)**
- Test production line: Operational 2024
- Production target: Late 2020s
- Advantages: Higher energy density, faster charging, improved safety
- Investment in SES (US) for semi-solid-state development

### Raw Materials & Recycling

| Initiative | Partner | Purpose |
|------------|---------|---------|
| Strategic metals | Hanwa Kogyo | Nickel, cobalt, lithium procurement |
| Battery recycling | Ascend Elements | Closed-loop material recovery |
| Recycling | Cirba Solutions | North American recycling |
| Cathode materials | POSCO Holdings | Precursor material production |

## Regional EV Strategy

|--------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Prologue/ZDX via GM partnership |
| **2026+** | 0 Series from Ohio EV Hub |
| **Production** | Marysville Auto Plant conversion |

- Marysville Auto Plant transformation

- On-site supplier park

## Manufacturing Transformation

### Production Capacity Plan

| Year | EV Target | Key Facilities |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| 2024 | ~50,000 | GM partnership (Prologue) |
| 2026 | ~200,000 | Ohio EV Hub ramp-up |
| 2030 | 2,000,000 | Global 0 Series production |

### Supply Chain Adaptation

| Component | Current State | Future State |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Engines** | In-house ICE production | Transition to motors |
| **Transmissions** | CVT, DCT, Manual | Single-speed reduction |
| **Fuel systems** | Complete supply chain | Eliminated for BEVs |
| **Battery cells** | Sourced externally | JV production + in-house |
| **Software** | Tier 1 suppliers | In-house development |

## Challenges & Risk Mitigation

### Current Challenges (2025)

| Challenge | Impact | Mitigation |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Slower EV demand | Canada delay | Focus on hybrids as bridge |
| US tariffs | $4.4B impact | Increase US production |
| China price wars | Margin pressure | Localized products, partnerships |
| Battery costs | EV profitability | Vertical integration, scale |

### Strategic Flexibility

Honda maintains optionality through:
1. **Hybrid bridge strategy**: e:HEV as primary offering during transition
2. **Partnership approach**: Reduces capital risk vs. full in-house development
3. **Platform flexibility**: 0 Series designed for multiple vehicle types
4. **Regional adaptation**: Different pace by market

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. Tesla
- 0 Series targets Model 3/Y with "Wise" connected features
- Traditional automotive quality and dealer network
- NACS compatibility removes charging barrier

### vs. VW ID. Series
- Similar timeline for dedicated EV platform
- Honda emphasizes driving dynamics heritage
- Focus on efficiency over pure range

### vs. Hyundai/Kia
- Hyundai ahead in current EV offerings
- Honda catching up with 0 Series
- Brand reliability reputation as advantage

### vs. Toyota
- Honda more aggressive on pure EVs
- Toyota betting on hybrid + hydrogen
- Different strategic bets on timeline
