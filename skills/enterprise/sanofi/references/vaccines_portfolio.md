# Sanofi Vaccines Portfolio

## Overview

Sanofi is the world's leading vaccine company with a diverse portfolio spanning pediatric, influenza, travel, and specialty vaccines. The vaccines division achieved record sales of €8.3 billion in 2024 (+13.5% growth).

## 2024 Performance Summary

| Metric | Value | YoY Change |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Net Sales | €8.30B | +13.5% |
| % of Sanofi Total | 20% | +1pp |
| Key Growth Driver | Beyfortus | Blockbuster first year |

## Portfolio Breakdown

### 1. Influenza Vaccines

**Revenue:** ~€2.4B (2024)

**Products:**
- Fluzone (high-dose for elderly)
- Flublok (recombinant)
- Vaxigrip (standard)

**Market Position:**
- Global leader in influenza vaccines
- Strong presence in US (high-dose segment)
- European public health contracts

**Key Trends:**
- Shift toward high-dose and adjuvanted vaccines
- Combination vaccine development (COVID-flu)
- Pricing pressure in competitive markets

---

### 2. Pediatric Combination Vaccines

**Revenue:** ~€2.0B (2024)

**Products:**
- Pentacel (DTaP-IPV/Hib)
- Daptacel (DTaP)
- IPOL (polio)
- ActHIB (Hib)

**Market Position:**
- Established relationships with pediatricians
- Strong in US, Europe
- Part of routine immunization schedules

---

### 3. Beyfortus (Nirsevimab) ⭐

**Revenue:** €1.76B (2024) - **Blockbuster in first year**

**Product Profile:**
- Type: Long-acting monoclonal antibody
- Indication: RSV prevention in infants
- Dosing: Single dose for season-long protection
- Differentiation: vs. Synagis (monthly dosing)

**Partnership:**
- Partner: AstraZeneca
- Structure: AZ develops/manufactures, Sanofi commercializes
- Profit share: 50% outside US (gross profits)
- Update (April 2024): Sanofi gained full US commercial control

**Market Opportunity:**
- Target: All infants entering first RSV season
- US eligible population: ~3.6M annually
- Global market potential: €3B+ at peak

**Real-World Evidence:**
- 73-90% effectiveness against hospitalization
- 6M+ babies immunized globally
- Strong safety profile

---

## Pipeline Vaccines

### PCV21 (Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine)
- Target: 21 serotypes
- Status: Phase 3
- Competition: Pfizer (Prevnar 20, Capvaxive)
- Timeline: ~2027 approval

### RSV Portfolio Expansion

| Product | Target Population | Status | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|--------|-------|
| RSV toddler | 12-24 months | Phase 3 | Second season protection |
| RSV adult 18-49 | Young adults | Phase 3 | Differentiated segment |
| RSV adult 60-75 | Older adults | Phase 3 | Competition with Pfizer/Moderna/GSK |

### mRNA Vaccines

**Partnership:** Novavax
- Phase 1/2: COVID-flu combination
- Goal: Demonstrate non-inferior efficacy vs. separate vaccines
- Timeline: Phase 3 readiness 2025 if Phase 2 positive

### Acne mRNA Vaccine
- Status: Phase 1/2 initiation
- Target: Novel approach to acne prevention
