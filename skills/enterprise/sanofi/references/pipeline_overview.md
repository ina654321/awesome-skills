# Sanofi Pipeline Overview

## Executive Summary

Sanofi has a robust late-stage pipeline with 12+ NMEs (New Molecular Entities) having €2-5B+ peak sales potential. The pipeline is heavily weighted toward immunology (~80% of late-stage assets), reflecting the company's strategic focus.

## Tier 1 Assets (€5B+ Peak Potential)

### 1. Amlitelimab (Anti-OX40L)

**Profile:**
- Mechanism: Anti-OX40L monoclonal antibody
- Acquired: Kymab acquisition (€1.1B, 2021)
- Differentiation: Targets ligand (not receptor), potential for quarterly dosing

**Indications & Status:**
| Indication | Phase | Timeline | Notes |
|------------|-------|----------|-------|
| Atopic dermatitis | Phase 3 | Readout 2026-27 | COAST program |
| Asthma | Phase 2b | Data 2025 | TIDE study |
| Hidradenitis suppurativa | Phase 2 | Ongoing | High unmet need |

**Peak Sales Potential:** €5B+

---

### 2. Frexalimab (Anti-CD40L)

**Profile:**
- Mechanism: Anti-CD40L monoclonal antibody
- Licensed: ImmuNext (up to $500M milestones, 2017)
- Differentiation: Second-generation, improved safety profile vs. earlier CD40L agents

**Indications & Status:**
| Indication | Phase | Timeline | Notes |
|------------|-------|----------|-------|
| Relapsing MS | Phase 3 | Initiated 2024 | 89% lesion reduction in Phase 2 |
| Secondary progressive MS | Phase 3 | Initiated 2024 | High efficacy potential |
| Sjögren's syndrome | Phase 2 | Data pending | |
| Type 1 diabetes | Phase 2b | Ongoing | Recently diagnosed |
| Systemic lupus erythematosus | Phase 2 | Ongoing | |

**Phase 2 Data Highlights:**
- 89% reduction in new Gd+ lesions (high dose)
- 96% of patients lesion-free at Week 24
- Well-tolerated, no serious thrombotic events

**Peak Sales Potential:** €5B+

---

### 3. SAR441566 (Oral TNFR1si)

**Profile:**

- Differentiation: Oral alternative to anti-TNF biologics

**Indications & Status:**
| Indication | Phase | Timeline |
|------------|-------|----------|
| Rheumatoid arthritis | Phase 2b | Ongoing |
| Psoriasis | Phase 2b | Ongoing |

**Peak Sales Potential:** €5B+

## Tier 2 Assets (€2-5B Peak Potential)

### 4. Tolebrutinib (BTK Inhibitor)

**Profile:**
- Mechanism: Bruton's tyrosine kinase inhibitor (brain-penetrant)
- Acquired: Principia Biopharma ($3.7B, 2020)
- Differentiation: CNS-penetrant for progressive MS

**Status:**
- Phase 3 for all forms of MS
- Brief FDA clinical hold resolved (liver toxicity signal monitored)
- Competes with Roche (fenebrutinib), Novartis (evobrutinib)

---

### 5. Riliprubart (C1s Inhibitor)

**Profile:**
- Mechanism: Complement C1s inhibitor
- Indications: CIDP (chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy)
- Status: Phase 3 initiated

---

### 6. TEV'574 (Anti-TL1A)

**Profile:**
- Mechanism: Anti-TL1A monoclonal antibody
- Partner: Teva Pharmaceuticals
- Indications: Inflammatory bowel disease
- Status: Phase 2
- Potential: Best-in-class TL1A for GI diseases

---

### 7. Lunsekimig (Anti-IL-13/TSLP)

**Profile:**
- Mechanism: Bispecific antibody targeting IL-13 and TSLP
- Indications: Asthma
- Status: Phase 2b
- Differentiation: Dual targeting for broad type 2 inflammation

---

### 8. Itepekimab (Anti-IL-33)

## Vaccines Pipeline

### PCV21 (Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine)
- Status: Phase 3
- Target: Broader serotype coverage than Prevnar 20
- Timeline: Approval ~2027

### RSV Toddler
- Status: Phase 3
- Target: Second season protection for toddlers

### RSV Adult (18-49, 60-75)
- Status: Phase 3
- Competition: Pfizer, Moderna, GSK adult RSV vaccines

### mRNA Flu Vaccine
- Status: Phase 1/2
- Partner: Novavax
- Target: Combination COVID-flu vaccine

## Investment Highlights

### Strengths
- Pipeline-in-a-product assets (amlitelimab, frexalimab)
- Late-stage immunology leadership
- Strong probability of technical success
- Complementary to Dupixent growth

### Risks
- Concentration in immunology
- Competitive intensity (MS, AD markets)
- Regulatory uncertainty (novel mechanisms)
