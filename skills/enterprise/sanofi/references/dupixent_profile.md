# Dupixent (Dupilumab) Profile

## Overview

Dupixent is Sanofi's flagship product - a monoclonal antibody targeting IL-4Rα that blocks both IL-4 and IL-13, key drivers of type 2 inflammation. Co-developed and co-commercialized with Regeneron since 2007.

## Approved Indications

### 1. Atopic Dermatitis (2017)
- Population: 6.6M+ moderate-to-severe patients (US)
- Positioning: First-line biologic
- Key data: EASI-75 response in 70%+ of patients

### 2. Asthma (2018)
- Population: ~10M moderate-to-severe type 2 patients (US)
- Positioning: Add-on for uncontrolled patients
- Key data: 50% reduction in exacerbations

### 3. Chronic Rhinosinusitis with Nasal Polyps (2019)
- Population: 2-4M patients (US)
- Positioning: First biologic approval
- Key data: Reduced need for surgery

### 4. Eosinophilic Esophagitis (2022)
- Population: ~150K patients (US)
- Positioning: First and only approved treatment
- Key data: Histological remission in 60%

### 5. Prurigo Nodularis (2022)
- Population: ~80K patients (US)
- Positioning: First and only approved treatment
- Key data: Rapid itch reduction

### 6. COPD (2024) ⭐
- Population: ~300K eligible type 2 patients (US)
- Positioning: **First biologic approved for COPD**
- Key data: 30% reduction in exacerbations (BOREAS)

## Partnership Structure (Regeneron)

### Development History
- **2007:** Collaboration agreement signed

- **Status:** Partnership continues operationally; legal dispute ongoing

## Clinical Data Highlights

### BOREAS Study (COPD)
- Design: Phase 3, 939 patients
- Primary endpoint: 30% reduction in exacerbations (p<0.001)
- Key secondary: Improved lung function, quality of life
- Label: First biologic approved for COPD

### LIBERTY Studies (Various indications)
- Consistent efficacy across type 2 inflammatory diseases
- Well-established safety profile
- No dose adjustment needed for elderly/renal/hepatic impairment
