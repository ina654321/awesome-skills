# Zhanjiang Verbund Site Reference

## Overview

The **Zhanjiang Verbund site** is BASF's largest single investment and the third-largest Verbund site globally upon completion.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Location | Zhanjiang, Guangdong Province, China |
| Total investment | €10 billion |
| Timeline | 2019–2030 |
| Status | Commissioning phase |
| Ownership | 100% BASF (first major wholly foreign-owned chemical complex in China) |

**Strategic Significance:**
- Largest BASF investment ever
- Gateway to southern China market
- Model for sustainable chemical production
- Third-largest Verbund site (after Ludwigshafen, Antwerp)

## Production Units

### Phase 1 (Operational 2025-2026)

**Steam Cracker:**
| Specification | Detail |
|---------------|--------|
| Ethylene capacity | 1,000,000 tonnes/year |
| Technology | State-of-the-art |
| Integration | Fully integrated into Verbund |
| Startup | December 2025/January 2026 |

**Acrylics Complex:**
| Unit | Capacity | Products |
|------|----------|----------|
| Glacial Acrylic Acid (GAA) | World-scale | Acrylic acid |
| Butyl Acrylate (BA) | 400,000 tonnes/year | Coating monomer |
| Applications | — | Coatings, adhesives, superabsorbents |

**Syngas Plant:**
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Technology | CO₂ utilization from ethylene oxide |
| Innovation | First of its kind in China |
| Benefit | Significant CO₂ emission reduction |
| Capacity | Internal use for Verbund |

**Other Units:**
- Methyl glycols plant
- Engineering plastics unit
- Thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU) production

### Future Phases (By 2030)

Additional plants will be added based on market demand:
- Downstream derivative plants
- Specialty chemicals
- Performance materials

## Sustainability Features

### Renewable Energy

| Feature | Target | Status |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Renewable electricity | 100% upon completion | PPAs signed |
| Source | Offshore wind, solar | Contracts in place |
| CO₂ reduction | Significant vs. conventional | Designed for net zero pathway |

### Carbon Management

**Syngas Plant Innovation:**
- Uses CO₂ off-gas from ethylene oxide process
- Surplus fuel gas from steam cracker
- Unparalleled technical concept in China
- Major reduction in direct CO₂ emissions

**Circular Economy:**
- Designed for chemical recycling integration
- Waste heat utilization across plants
- By-product valorization

### Environmental Standards

| Aspect | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| Emissions | Best available technology (BAT) |
| Water | Closed-loop systems where possible |
| Waste | Minimization and recovery |
| Monitoring | Real-time environmental monitoring |
