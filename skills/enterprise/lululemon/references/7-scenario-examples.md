## § 7 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: New Store Opening

**User**: "We're opening a new Lululemon flagship in London. How should we approach community building?"

**Expected Response**:
```
**Lululemon Flagship Community Launch Framework**

**Pre-Opening (12 weeks):**

**Week 1-4: Ambassador Recruitment**
- Identify 8-12 local fitness leaders (yoga, running, HIIT, wellness)
- Criteria: Authentic community following, values alignment, teaching excellence
- Offer: Ambassador program benefits, early access, event collaboration

**Week 5-8: Community Seeding**
- Host pop-up sweat sessions in local parks/studios
- Partner with existing studios for cross-promotion
- Build Instagram/TikTok presence with local content

**Week 9-12: Hype Building**
- "Countdown to Regent Street" social campaign
- Exclusive preview for Sweat Collective members
- Local media/PR placement

**Opening Strategy:**

**Week 1: Grand Opening**
- Daily complimentary classes (yoga, HIIT, meditation)
- Local ambassador takeovers
- First 100 guests: Exclusive London-themed product

**Ongoing Programming:**
- Weekly run club (Tuesday 6:30 PM)
- Sunday morning yoga (community class, pay-what-you-can)
- Monthly goal-setting workshops
- Quarterly wellness panels

**KPIs:**
- Community event attendance: 200+ weekly
- Ambassador social reach: 500K+ combined
- 90-day guest retention: 40%+
- Comp penetration target: 70% (Year 1)
```

### Scenario 2: Product Line Extension

**User**: "We're considering expanding our footwear line. What's the Lululemon approach?"

**Expected Response**:
```
**Lululemon Footwear Expansion Strategy**

**Current State:**
- Blissfeel (women's running): Launched 2022
- Chargefeel (training): Moderate success
- Strongfeel (studio): Niche following
- Men's footwear: Limited selection

**Strategic Framework:**

**Phase 1: Women's Running Deepening (2025)**
- Blissfeel 2 iteration based on feedback
- Trail running extension
- Positioning: "Running gear for your feet"

**Phase 2: Men's Footwear Launch (2025-2026)**
- Running first (parallel to women's success)
- Training/studio second
- Avoid basketball (authenticity gap)

**Phase 3: Category Expansion (2026+)**
- Lifestyle crossover (casual comfort)
- Studio-specific (pilates, barre)

**Success Criteria:**
- Technical credibility: Must meet running shoe standards
- Design language: Recognizably Lululemon
- Guest demand: Pre-order indicators, waitlist data
- Margin target: 55%+ (in line with apparel)

**Investment:**
- R&D: $50M over 3 years
- Marketing: Integrated with apparel campaigns
- Distribution: Flagship stores + online first

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Partner with established sole technology provider
- Extensive wear-testing (6+ months)
- Limited SKU count (focus, not breadth)
```

### Scenario 3: Guest Experience Recovery

**User**: "We've had complaints about product quality declining. How do we respond?"

**Expected Response**:
```
**Lululemon Quality Response Protocol**

**Immediate Actions (Week 1):**

**1. Guest Recovery**
- No-questions-asked returns/exchanges
- Personal outreach to vocal detractors
- Quality Promise reaffirmation

**2. Product Hold**
- Pause shipments of flagged SKUs
- Quarantine inventory for inspection
- Review supplier batch records

**Investigation (Week 2-4):**

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Fabric testing (in-house lab)
- Supplier audit (factory visit)
- Pattern/construction review
- Compare to historical quality baselines

**Communication Strategy:**

**Internal:**
- Educator briefing: "What to say to concerned guests"
- Quality Promise reinforcement
- Feedback loop to product team

**External:**
- Social response (not defensive, solution-focused)
- Email to affected guests (if identified)
- No broad PR unless necessary

**Long-term Remediation:**
- Enhanced QC checkpoints
- Supplier performance review
- Guest satisfaction monitoring
- Product team quality KPIs

**Never:**
- Deny the guest's experience
- Blame suppliers publicly
- Compromise on Quality Promise
```


### Scenario 5: International Market Entry

**User**: "We're evaluating entry into India. What factors should we consider?**

**Expected Response:**
```
**Lululemon India Market Entry Assessment**

**Market Opportunity:**
- Growing affluent class: 100M+ potential guests
- Yoga origin story: Authentic connection
- Fitness trend acceleration post-COVID
- Limited premium athleisure competition

**Entry Strategy:**

**Phase 1: Market Testing (Year 1)**
- E-commerce launch (Pan-India)
- Pop-up experiences (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore)
- Sweat Collective recruitment (yoga teachers, trainers)
- Local ambassador identification

**Phase 2: Flagship Launch (Year 2)**
- First company-operated store (Mumbai or Delhi)
- Community programming (yoga, meditation focus)
- Local designer collaboration potential

**Phase 3: Expansion (Year 3+)**
- Tier 1 cities: 5-10 stores
- Tier 2 consideration: Pune, Hyderabad, Chennai
- Adapted product: Climate-appropriate fabrics

**Critical Success Factors:**
- Local community building (yoga studio partnerships)
- Educator hiring (values-aligned, wellness-passionate)
- Supply chain (import duties, local production consideration)
- Brand positioning (premium but accessible)

**Challenges:**
- Price sensitivity (adapted pricing strategy needed)
- Competition from local + global players
- Real estate costs in prime locations
- Climate (hot weather product adaptation)

**Investment:**
- Initial: $10-15M (Year 1-2)
- Break-even: 3-4 years per store
- Long-term potential: $200M+ revenue (5-year horizon)
```
