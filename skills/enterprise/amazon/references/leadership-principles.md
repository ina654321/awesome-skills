# Amazon 16 Leadership Principles — Detailed Reference

> Source: [Amazon Jobs](https://www.amazon.jobs/content/en/our-workplace/leadership-principles)

---

## 1. Customer Obsession

**Definition:**
> Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust. Although leaders pay attention to competitors, they obsess over customers.

**What It Means:**
- The customer is the empty chair in every meeting
- Managers spend time in call centers
- Working Backwards process ensures customer focus
- Customer trust is harder to earn than to lose
- Customers are "beautifully, wonderfully dissatisfied" — they don't know what they want until you show them

**Interview Questions:**
- Tell me about a time you solved a pain point for customers.
- How do you prioritize customer needs when dealing with many customers?
- Share an example where you used customer feedback to drive improvement.
- What is the difference between customer focus and customer obsession?

**Behavioral Examples:**
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Start with customer problem | Start with technology |
| Earn trust through actions | Assume trust is given |
| Anticipate unspoken needs | Only respond to stated needs |
| Measure customer outcomes | Measure internal metrics only |

## 2. Ownership

**Definition:**
> Leaders are owners. They think long term and don't sacrifice long-term value for short-term results. They act on behalf of the entire company, beyond just their own team. They never say "that's not my job."

**What It Means:**
- 7-year time horizons, not quarterly thinking
- "You build it, you run it"
- Willing to cannibalize your own business (Kindle vs physical books)
- Picking up trash when you see it — no task is beneath you

**Interview Questions:**
- Tell me about a time you took on a task beyond your job responsibilities.
- Describe a tough decision you made that sacrificed short-term gains for long-term value.
- Give an example of acting on behalf of the whole company, not just your team.

---

## 3. Invent and Simplify

**Definition:**
> Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams and always find ways to simplify. They are externally aware, look for new ideas from everywhere, and are not limited by "not invented here." As we do new things, we accept that we may be misunderstood for long periods of time.

**What It Means:**
- Willing to be misunderstood for years
- Door desks symbolize frugal innovation
- AWS invented cloud computing from internal infrastructure needs
- Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication

**Key Quote:**
> "Invention is by its very nature disruptive. If you want to be understood at all times, then don't do anything new." — Jeff Bezos

---

## 4. Are Right, A Lot

**Definition:**
> Leaders are right a lot. They have strong judgment and good instincts. They seek diverse perspectives and work to disconfirm their beliefs.

**What It Means:**
- Being right is about outcome, not ego
- Seek disconfirming evidence
- Change your mind when presented with new data
- Best leaders are intrigued by challenge, not threatened

**Common Misconception:**
This doesn't mean leaders whose ideas are chosen most often. It means leaders who get to the best answer through strong judgment.

---

## 5. Learn and Be Curious

**Definition:**
> Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves. They are curious about new possibilities and act to explore them.

**What It Means:**
- Day 1 mentality — always beginning
- "Why" culture (YQ - Why Quotient)
- Biggest differentiator in company/personal growth is learning aptitude
- The day we stop learning is the day we risk undermining our future

**Andy Jassy on Learning:**
> "In the nearly 28 years I've been at Amazon, the biggest difference in the relative growth of companies and individuals has been their aptitude to learn."

---

## 6. Hire and Develop the Best

**Definition:**
> Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion. They recognize exceptional talent, and willingly move them throughout the organization. Leaders develop leaders and take seriously their role in coaching others. We work on behalf of our people to invent mechanisms for development like Career Choice.

**What It Means:**
- Bar Raiser program ensures quality
- Better than 50% of current employees at level
- Promote internally
- Career Choice: Pre-pay 95% of tuition for in-demand fields

---

## 7. Insist on the Highest Standards

**Definition:**
> Leaders have relentlessly high standards — many people may think these standards are unreasonably high. Leaders are continually raising the bar and drive their teams to deliver high-quality products, services, and processes. Leaders ensure that defects do not get sent down the line and that problems are fixed so they stay fixed.

**What It Means:**
- "Good enough" is not good enough
- Defect reduction obsession
- Raising the bar continuously
- Fix problems so they stay fixed

---

## 8. Think Big

**Definition:**
> Thinking small is a self-fulfilling prophecy. Leaders create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results. They think differently and look around corners for ways to serve customers.

**What It Means:**
- AWS as "infrastructure for the internet"
- 6-page memos for big ideas
- Looking around corners for future customer needs
- Creating bold missions that inspire

---

## 9. Bias for Action

**Definition:**
> Speed matters in business. Many decisions and actions are reversible and do not need extensive study. We value calculated risk taking.

**What It Means:**
- 70% confidence threshold for two-way doors
- Most decisions are reversible
- Don't let the 10% of one-way doors slow down the 90%
- High-velocity decision making

---

## 10. Frugality

**Definition:**
> Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness, self-sufficiency and invention. There are no extra points for growing headcount, budget size, or fixed expense.

**What It Means:**
- Door desks as cultural symbol
- "Frupidity" — frugal but not stupid
- Self-service tools over human processes
- Constraints drive innovation

---

## 11. Earn Trust

**Definition:**
> Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully. They are vocally self-critical, even when doing so is awkward or embarrassing. Leaders do not believe their or their team's body odor smells of perfume. They benchmark themselves and their teams against the best.

**What It Means:**
- 360-degree feedback
- Candid criticism even when uncomfortable
- Admit mistakes publicly
- Benchmark against the best

---

## 12. Dive Deep

**Definition:**
> Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics and anecdotes differ. No task is beneath them.

**What It Means:**
- Senior leaders know operational details
- "Gemba walks" — go to where work happens
- Data over anecdotes
- Audit frequently

---

## 13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit

**Definition:**
> Leaders are obligated to respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree, even when doing so is uncomfortable or exhausting. Leaders have conviction and are tenacious. They do not compromise for the sake of social cohesion. Once a decision is determined, they commit wholly.

**What It Means:**
- "I told you so" has no currency
- Challenge in meetings, commit after decision
- No pocket vetoes
- Disagree respectfully, then commit fully

**Andy Jassy on Disagree and Commit:**
> "We don't just empower people to challenge one another, we obligate them to do so if they disagree."

---

## 14. Deliver Results

**Definition:**
> Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion. Despite setbacks, they rise to the occasion and never settle.

**What It Means:**
- Focus on controllable input metrics
- Monitor output results
- Rise to occasion despite setbacks
- Never settle

---

## 15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer

**Definition:**
> Leaders work every day to create a safer, more productive, higher performing, more diverse, and more just work environment. They lead with empathy, have fun at work, and make it easy for others to have fun. Leaders ask themselves: Are my fellow employees growing? Are they empowered? Are they ready for what's next? Leaders have a vision for and commitment to their employees' personal success, whether or not they stay at Amazon.

**Added:** 2021
**Context:** Post-pandemic focus on employee experience

---

## 16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

**Definition:**
> We started in a garage, but we're not there anymore. We are big, we impact the world, and we are far from perfect. We must be humble and thoughtful about the secondary effects of our actions. Our local communities, planet, and future generations need us to be better every day. We must begin each day with a determination to make better, do better, and be better for our customers, our employees, our partners, and the world at large. And we must end every day knowing we can do even more tomorrow. Leaders create more than they consume and always leave things better than they found them.

**Added:** 2021
**Context:** The Climate Pledge; sustainability; social responsibility

## Using Leadership Principles in Interviews

### The STAR Method
For each Leadership Principle, prepare 2-3 stories using:
- **Situation**: Context and stakes
- **Task**: Your specific responsibility
- **Action**: What YOU did (not "we")
- **Result**: Quantified outcomes
- **What would you do differently**: Self-awareness

### Common LP Interview Patterns
| Principle | Typical Question Structure |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Customer Obsession | "Tell me about a time you..." advocated for/went above for a customer |
| Ownership | "Tell me about a time you..." took on extra responsibility/made a tough long-term decision |
| Dive Deep | "Describe a time you..." analyzed a complex problem in detail |
| Disagree and Commit | "Tell me about a time you..." disagreed with your manager/team |
| Bias for Action | "Describe a time you..." took action with incomplete information |
| Deliver Results | "Tell me about a time you..." achieved results despite obstacles |
