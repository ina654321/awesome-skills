# Samsung 2nm Foundry Technology

## Overview

Samsung Foundry's 2nm process represents a critical battle for the company's semiconductor future. After losing major customers (Qualcomm) due to 3nm yield issues, 2nm is Samsung's all-or-nothing bet to reclaim foundry credibility.

## Technology Specifications

### 2nm Process (SF2)

| Metric | SF2 (2nm) | Improvement vs 3nm |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Performance** | Baseline | +5% vs 2nd-gen 3nm |
| **Power Efficiency** | Industry-leading | +8% vs 2nd-gen 3nm |
| **Area** | Optimized | -5% vs 3nm |
| **Transistor Architecture** | GAA (Gate-All-Around) | Mature vs TSMC's first GAA |
| **Mass Production** | December 2025 | World's first |

### Exynos 2600: 2nm Proof of Concept

Samsung's first 2nm product is the Exynos 2600 mobile AP:

| Specification | Value |
|---------------|-------|
| **Process** | Samsung 2nm GAA |
| **CPU** | 10-core Arm architecture |
| **Performance Boost** | +39% vs Exynos 2500 |
| **AI Performance** | +113% generative AI |
| **Thermal Technology** | HPB (Hot Path Blocking) - 16% lower thermal resistance |
| **Target Device** | Galaxy S26 series (2026) |

## Customer Wins

### Tesla: $16.5 Billion Contract

- **Product:** AI6 vehicle chip
- **Value:** $16.5B contract (major validation)
- **Significance:** Tesla diversifying from TSMC; Samsung's largest foundry win

### AMD Partnership (In Negotiation)

- **Product:** EPYC Venice CPUs (next-gen server processors)
- **Process:** Samsung 2nm SF2P (2nd-gen 2nm)
- **Terms:** Samsung provides chip prototype trial production (MPW technology)
- **Strategic Value:** Major CPU customer would validate Samsung's HPC capabilities

### Internal Customer: Samsung MX

- **Product:** Exynos 2600 for Galaxy S26
- **Purpose:** Proof of concept for external customers
- **Message:** Samsung trusts its own foundry for flagship products

### Qualcomm (Potential Return)

- **History:** Qualcomm moved all sub-4nm orders to TSMC in 2022 due to yield issues
- **Opportunity:** Samsung pitching 2nm for future Snapdragon chips
- **Status:** Under evaluation

## Competitive Analysis

### Foundry Market Share (2025)

| Company | Market Share | 2nm Status |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| **TSMC** | 70.2% | Production H2 2025 |
| **Samsung** | 7.3% | Production started Dec 2025 |
| **Intel** | ~5% | 18A process (2nm equivalent) 2025 |
| **Others** | ~17% | Various mature nodes |

### 2nm Comparison

| Aspect | Samsung SF2 | TSMC N2 | Intel 18A |
|--------|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **GAA Experience** | 2nd generation (since 3nm) | 1st generation | RibbonFET (new) |
| **Production Start** | Dec 2025 | H2 2025 | 2025 |
| **Major Customers** | Tesla, (AMD discussing) | Apple, NVIDIA | Internal first |
| **Pricing Strategy** | Flexible/aggressive | 10-20% price hike | Competitive |

### Samsung Advantages

1. **GAA Experience:** Only foundry with production GAA experience (since 3nm)
2. **Vertical Integration:** Memory + Logic + Packaging capabilities
3. **Pricing Flexibility:** More aggressive vs TSMC's premium pricing
4. **Internal Validation:** Uses own process for flagship chips

### Samsung Challenges

1. **Customer Trust:** Rebuilding after 3nm yield issues
2. **Ecosystem:** TSMC's IP/library ecosystem more mature
3. **Scale:** TSMC's capacity and customer relationships dominant

## Strategic Significance

### Why 2nm Matters

1. **Foundry Credibility:** Must succeed to remain viable foundry player
2. **Customer Recapture:** Win back Qualcomm, attract new customers
3. **Technology Leadership:** Prove Samsung can compete at leading edge
4. **Financial Returns:** Leading-edge processes generate highest margins
