# Lee Kun-hee and the New Management Philosophy

## The Frankfurt Declaration (1993)

On June 7, 1993, Samsung Chairman Lee Kun-hee gathered his top executives at the Kempinski Hotel Frankfurt Gravenbruch near Frankfurt Airport, Germany. Over three days, he delivered what would become known as the **"New Management"** (신경영 / 新經營) declaration.

### Famous Quotes

> **"Change everything except your wife and children."**
> (아내와 자녀만 빼고 다 바꾸자)

> **"Change begins with me."**
> (먼저 바꾸는 것은 나 자신부터)

> **"Let's work to correct what we have done wrong."**

### The Context

In the early 1990s, Samsung was known as a quantity-focused manufacturer of low-cost electronics. Lee Kun-hee recognized that this strategy would not enable Samsung to compete with Japanese giants like Sony on the global stage.

The quality problem was epitomized by Samsung's early mobile phones—the 1986 SC-100 car phone and 1988 SH-100 were commercial failures due to poor quality compared to Motorola benchmarks.

### Symbolic Actions

**The Phone Burning (1995)**
- Lee ordered 150,000 Anycall mobile phones burned publicly
- Included phones from production lines and already in market
- Cost: ₩50 billion (approximately $45 million at the time)
- Message: Quality defects will not be tolerated

**Organizational Changes**
- Shift from seniority-based to performance-based promotion
- Introduction of open recruitment system (merit-based hiring)
- First exclusive recruitment for female college graduates (1993)
- Regional expert system to develop global talent (1990)

### Key Principles

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Quality First** | Defect rates measured in parts per million; zero-defect goal |
| **Speed** | "First in volume, then in value" — scale before premium positioning |
| **Global Standards** | Benchmark against best global competitors, not local ones |
| **Continuous Innovation** | Reinvest profits in R&D and capital equipment |
| **Talent Excellence** | "Talent First" — hire the best, develop continuously |

### Results of New Management

| Metric | 1987 (Lee took over) | 2014 (Hospitalized) | Growth |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|--------|
| Revenue | ₩10 trillion | ₩387 trillion | 39x |
| Operating Profit | ₩200 billion | ₩72 trillion | 259x |
| Market Cap | ₩1 trillion | ₩396 trillion | 396x |

Samsung transformed from a Korean electronics company into the world's largest technology company by revenue.

### Legacy Today

**Lee Kun-hee (1942-2020)**
- Led Samsung for 27 years (1987-2014)
- Hospitalized in 2014 after heart attack; passed away October 25, 2020
- His children: Jay Y. Lee (Chairman), Lee Boo-jin (Hotel Shilla), Lee Seo-hyun (Samsung C&T)

**New Management in Contemporary Samsung**
- Still referenced in executive communications
- Quality management systems (SPS - Samsung Production System)
- Counter-cyclical investment philosophy maintained
- Annual memorial ceremonies at Suwon cemetery

### Academic Recognition

- 30th Anniversary Conference held October 2023
- Scholars compared his impact to the Medici family's patronage
- "Strategic theorist and integrative thinker" — academic assessment
- Transformed not just Samsung but South Korean corporate culture

### The Chaebol Context

Samsung is the largest of South Korea's **chaebol** (재벌) — family-controlled business conglomerates:

- **Founded:** 1938 by Lee Byung-chul (Lee Kun-hee's father)
- **Samsung Group:** 80+ interconnected companies
- **Samsung Electronics:** Flagship subsidiary, founded 1969
- **Economic Impact:** ~20% of South Korea's GDP
- **Nickname:** "The Republic of Samsung"
