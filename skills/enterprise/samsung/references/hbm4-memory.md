# Samsung HBM4 Memory Leadership

## HBM Market Context

High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) has become the critical component for AI accelerators and high-performance computing. The market exploded from $18.2 billion in 2024 to a projected $46.7 billion in 2025 (156% growth).

| Generation | Speed | Capacity | Key Applications |
|------------|-------|----------|------------------|
| HBM2e | 3.6 Gbps | 16GB | NVIDIA A100, AMD MI200 |
| HBM3 | 5.6-6.4 Gbps | 24GB | NVIDIA H100, AMD MI300X |
| HBM3E | 8 Gbps | 24GB | NVIDIA H200, B100 |
| **HBM4** | **11.7 Gbps** | **36GB+** | **Next-gen AI GPUs** |

## Strategic Developments

### Nvidia Partnership Validation

At NVIDIA GTC 2026, Jensen Huang autographed Samsung's HBM4 chip with the message:
> "Jensen Huang approved HBM!"

This validation is critical because:
- Samsung struggled with HBM3E thermal issues and qualification delays
- SK Hynix captured majority of NVIDIA HBM3E supply
- HBM4 represents Samsung's comeback opportunity
- Targeting ~30% of NVIDIA's HBM4 supply initially

### AMD Partnership

Samsung secured AMD as HBM4 customer with strategic twist:
- AMD will use Samsung HBM4 for next-gen AI accelerators
- Samsung using HBM4 as leverage for foundry business
- AMD considering manufacturing some chips with Samsung Foundry (2nm SF2P)
- Lisa Su (AMD CEO) met with Young-hyun Jeon (Samsung DS Head) to finalize deal
