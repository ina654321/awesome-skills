# Crisis Classification & Stakeholder Mapping

## Crisis Classification System

### Level 1 — Critical (Life Safety / Regulatory)

Examples: Product causes death/injury, data breach affecting millions, regulatory fraud
Response: CEO-led response, board notification, regulator pre-notification, global statement

### Level 2 — Major (Reputation at Risk)

Examples: Executive misconduct, environmental incident, major service outage
Response: CCO/PR lead, internal notification, regional statement, media monitoring

### Level 3 — Moderate (Containable)

Examples: Product defect (no injury), minor data exposure, internal dispute made public
Response: PR team, targeted statement, customer notification, monitor for escalation

### Escalation Criteria

A Level 3 crisis must escalate to Level 2 if:
- [ ] Social media mentions exceed 10,000 in 24 hours
- [ ] Mainstream media picks up the story (national or above)
- [ ] A regulator makes contact
- [ ] An affected customer goes public with media
- [ ] The issue is linked to a broader systemic problem

A Level 2 crisis must escalate to Level 1 if:
- [ ] Death or serious injury is confirmed
- [ ] Regulatory investigation is formally opened
- [ ] CEO or board member is directly implicated
- [ ] Stock price drops >10% in one day
- [ ] The crisis spans multiple markets simultaneously

## Stakeholder Mapping Framework

| Stakeholder | Priority | Notification Timing | Channel | Tone |
|-------------|----------|---------------------|---------|------|
| **Employees** | #1 | Before any public statement | Internal email + town hall | Transparent, reassuring |
| **Regulators** | #1 | Before or simultaneous with public | Formal letter + call | Formal, factual |
| **Board/Investor** | #2 | Immediate for L1/L2 | Board call + investor relations | Complete facts |
| **Media** | #3 | After internal & regulatory | Press release + media briefing | Factual, accessible |
| **Customers** | #3 | After regulatory notification | Direct communication + website | Empathetic, actionable |
| **Public/Social** | #4 | After primary stakeholders | Social media + website FAQ | Simple, direct |

### Critical Rule

Never surprise a regulator with a press release. The regulatory notification and public release should be simultaneous or the regulatory first.

## Geographic Regulatory Contacts

### China (APAC)
- **SAMR** (State Administration for Market Regulation): Product safety, consumer protection
- **CAC** (Cyberspace Administration of China): Data breach notification under PIPL (3-day window)
- **Ministry of Transport**: Ride-hailing, platform regulations (Didi pattern)
- **CSRC** (China Securities Regulatory Commission): Listed company disclosures

### United States
- **CPSC** (Consumer Product Safety Commission): Product safety incidents
- **FDA**: Food, drug, medical device incidents
- **SEC**: Public company disclosures, Reg FD considerations
- **FTC**: Consumer protection, deceptive practices
- **State AGs**: State-level data breach notification (majority have 72h requirements)

### European Union
- **GDPR**: Data breach notification to supervisory authority within 72 hours
- **GPSD** (General Product Safety Directive): Product safety obligations
- **National consumer protection authorities**: Consumer complaints and incidents

### United Kingdom
- **FCA** (Financial Conduct Authority): Financial services and listed companies
- **ICO** (Information Commissioner's Office): Data breach notification under UK GDPR
- **MHRA** (Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency): Medical products

## Decision Checklist

**First 15 minutes — Triage:**
- [ ] What happened? (Brief, factual description)
- [ ] Is anyone in physical danger right now?
- [ ] Is this already public?
- [ ] Is a regulator already aware?
- [ ] Who are the key internal stakeholders who need to know NOW?

**First 30 minutes — Classification:**
- [ ] Assigned Level (1/2/3)
- [ ] Response leader identified
- [ ] Spokesperson designated
- [ ] Legal counsel engaged
- [ ] War room / communication channel established
- [ ] Initial fact sheet drafted

**First 60 minutes — Notification:**
- [ ] Board notified (Level 1 required; Level 2 for listed companies)
- [ ] Internal leadership notified
- [ ] Legal team engaged
- [ ] Regulatory pre-notification sent (if Level 1)
