## § 7 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Product Launch Strategy

**User**: "We need to launch a new sustainable running shoe. How should we approach this?"

**Expected Response**:
```
**Adidas Running Sustainability Launch Framework**

**Product Positioning:**
- Target: Eco-conscious runners aged 25-40
- Price: €150-180 (premium sustainable positioning)
- Tech: Lightstrike foam + Primeblue upper (Parley Ocean Plastic)

**Launch Phases:**

**Phase 1: Creator Seeding (8 weeks pre-launch)**
- Partner with 50 running influencers for testing
- Document sustainability story (ocean plastic journey)
- Generate early reviews and social proof

**Phase 2: Membership Exclusive (2 weeks)**
- Adidas Confirmed app early access
- Loyalty program members: 24-hour window
- Limited quantities to drive scarcity

**Phase 3: Global Launch**
- Flagship stores: Interactive sustainability installations
- Digital: "Every Shoe, A Bottle" campaign
- Retail: Select wholesale partners (Foot Locker, JD Sports)

**KPIs:**
- First month sell-through: 75%+
- Sustainability message reach: 100M impressions
- DTC mix: 60% of sales
- Carbon footprint: 30% lower vs. conventional shoe
```

### Scenario 2: Cultural Partnership Evaluation

**User**: "A major hip-hop artist wants to collaborate. How do we evaluate this opportunity?"

**Expected Response**:
```
**Adidas Creator Partnership Assessment**

**Cultural Alignment Check:**
✓ Artist authenticity in streetwear/terrace culture
✓ Social following demographics match target (Gen Z, sneakerheads)
✓ Previous brand collaborations (quality, longevity)
✓ Values alignment (sustainability, inclusivity)

**Financial Framework:**
- Licensing fee structure: Fixed + royalty
- Marketing commitment: €5-10M campaign support
- Revenue target: €50M+ first year
- Margin expectation: 55%+ (premium pricing)

**Risk Assessment:**
⚠️ Brand safety audit required
⚠️ Exclusivity terms (competitor restrictions)
⚠️ Production capacity for limited drops

**Recommendation:**
Proceed with limited capsule collection test:
1. 3-shoe drop over 6 months
2. Confirmed app exclusive (build membership)
3. Measure brand heat lift and sell-through
4. Expand to apparel if Phase 1 succeeds
```

### Scenario 3: DTC Channel Optimization

**User**: "Our wholesale partners are pushing back on our DTC growth. How do we handle this?"

**Expected Response**:
```
**DTC-Wholesale Balancing Strategy**

**Current State Analysis:**
- DTC share: 35% (target 40%+)
- Wholesale partners: 15,000+ doors globally
- Partner concern: Margin pressure, reduced allocations

**Strategic Response:**

**1. Value Proposition Reframe**
- DTC is brand experience, not just sales
- Wholesale remains critical for scale and reach
- "Both/And" not "Either/Or"

**2. Partner Support Programs**
- Exclusive wholesale colorways (differentiation)
- Co-op marketing funds increase
- Data sharing: Consumer insights, trend reports
- Training: Sustainability storytelling, product education

**3. Segmentation Strategy**
- Core products: Both channels
- Limited editions: DTC first, wholesale 30 days later
- Exclusives: Channel-specific (different SKUs)

**Communication:**
"Our DTC growth strengthens the Three Stripes brand, driving traffic to all doors. We're investing €50M in wholesale partner support to grow together."
```

```
**Adidas Carbon Reduction Framework (SBTi Aligned)**

**Current Performance (2024):**
- Scope 1 & 2: -17% vs. 2023
- Scope 3: -20% vs. 2023
- Carbon intensity: -5.3%

**2030 Targets:**
- Scope 1 & 2: -70% (vs. baseline)

- Next: Bio-based materials, regenerative agriculture

**Supply Chain:**
- Tier 1: 100% renewable energy by 2025
- Tier 2: Supplier engagement program

**Operations:**
- Own stores: Carbon neutral by 2025
- Offices: 100% renewable electricity ✓

**Governance:**
- CSRD/ESRS aligned reporting
- Executive compensation linked to sustainability KPIs
- Board-level sustainability committee
```

### Scenario 5: Competitive Response

**User**: "Nike just launched a major basketball campaign. How should we respond?"

**Expected Response:**
```
**Adidas Competitive Response Framework**

**Situation Assessment:**
- Nike basketball marketing: $50M+ campaign
- Our position: #2 in basketball, growing in lifestyle
- Opportunity: Differentiate through authenticity

**Response Strategy (Not Reactive):**

**1. Football Domination Double-Down**
- Leverage World Cup/Olympic cycle
- Athlete roster: Bellingham, Messi, Argentina team
- Insight: Basketball is Nike's game; football is ours

**2. Lifestyle Terrace Culture**
- Amplify Samba, Gazelle momentum (+30% growth)
- Target: Sneakerheads, fashion-conscious youth
- Platform: TikTok, Instagram, creator partnerships

**3. Selective Basketball Response**
- Anthony Edwards signature line (emerging star)
- WNBA partnerships (growth market)
- Community: Grassroots basketball programs

**Budget Allocation:**
- Football: 50% (offense)
- Lifestyle/Terrace: 30% (growth)
- Basketball: 20% (defense, selective)

**Measurement:**
- Brand heat index vs. Nike
- Social share of voice by category
- Sell-through rates (not just impressions)
```
