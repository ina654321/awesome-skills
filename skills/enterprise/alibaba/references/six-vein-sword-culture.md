# 六脉神剑 (Six-Vein Sword) — Alibaba's Core Values

> The Six-Vein Sword represents Alibaba's spiritual foundation. Just as the legendary martial arts technique requires mastery of six distinct energy channels, Alibaba's culture requires embodiment of six core values.

---

## Overview

The Six-Vein Sword (六脉神剑) is Alibaba's framework of six core values. These values are not just posters on walls—they are the criteria for hiring, promotion, and performance evaluation.

## Value 1: 客户第一 (Customer First)

### Meaning
Customers are the reason Alibaba exists. Everything starts with understanding and serving customer needs.

### Behavioral Indicators

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|-------|----------|
| Spend time with customers regularly | Make decisions without customer input |
| Measure success by customer outcomes | Optimize for internal metrics only |
| Solve customer pain points proactively | Wait for complaints before acting |
| Advocate for customer needs in meetings | Dismiss customer feedback as edge cases |

### Stories from Alibaba

**Jack Ma's Customer Visit Rule**: Jack Ma required all executives to spend at least one week per year doing customer service, answering calls and responding to complaints directly.

**The 100-Renminbi Customer**: In early days, a customer paid ¥100 but received no goods. Even though the amount was small, Alibaba tracked down the seller and refunded the buyer, establishing the trust principle.

### Application in Engineering

```
Before Building:
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. Who is the customer?                         │
│ 2. What is their pain point?                    │
│ 3. How will we measure their success?           │
│ 4. How can we validate before full build?       │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Value 2: 拥抱变化 (Embrace Change)

### Meaning
Change is the only constant. Rather than fearing change, Alibaba employees see it as opportunity for growth and innovation.

### The "Change Curve" Mindset

```
Traditional Response:          Alibaba Response:
     Fear                         Curiosity
      ↓                             ↓
   Resistance                   Opportunity
      ↓                             ↓
   Acceptance                   Innovation
      ↓                             ↓
   Adaptation                   Leadership
```

### Behavioral Indicators

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|-------|----------|
| See change as opportunity | Complain about disruption |
| Adapt quickly to new priorities | Cling to "how we've always done it" |
| Help others navigate change | Spread negativity during transitions |
| Propose solutions during change | Only point out problems |

### Historical Context

Alibaba's major transformations:
- **2003**: B2B to C2C (Taobao launch)
- **2008**: Taobao to Tmall (B2C pivot)
- **2014**: IPO and global expansion
- **2020**: Post-pandemic digitalization acceleration
- **2023**: 1+6+N restructuring

## Value 3: 团队合作 (Teamwork)

### Meaning
Alibaba believes in "平凡人做非凡事" (ordinary people achieving extraordinary things) through collaboration.

### The "Alipay Story"

When Alipay was created, it required cooperation between:
- Taobao (e-commerce platform)
- Banks (payment infrastructure)
- Merchants (adoption)
- Consumers (trust building)

No single team could succeed alone. The "escrow" model (holding payment until delivery) required deep trust and collaboration across all parties.

### Teamwork Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Shared Goals** | Team success > individual success |
| **Open Communication** | Transparent information flow |
| **Mutual Support** | Help colleagues without being asked |
| **Constructive Conflict** | Debate ideas, respect people |

## Value 4: 诚信 (Integrity)

### Meaning
Trust is Alibaba's most valuable asset. Integrity means honesty in communication and consistency between words and actions.

### The "Fake Goods" Battle

Alibaba invested billions in fighting counterfeit goods, even when it meant losing merchant relationships:
- **Technology**: AI-powered counterfeit detection
- **Partnerships**: Collaboration with brands and law enforcement
- **Policy**: Strict seller penalties and blacklisting
- **Culture**: Making integrity non-negotiable

### Integrity in Practice

```
Decision Test:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Would you be comfortable if this decision was:  │
│ • Published on the front page?                  │
│ • Explained to your family?                     │
│ • Reviewed by regulators?                       │
│                                                 │
│ If no to any → Reconsider                       │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Value 5: 敬业 (Passion/Commitment)

### Meaning
Bring energy, dedication, and excellence to everything you do. Be proud of your work.

### The "996" Discussion

Alibaba's work culture has been characterized by intense dedication. The "996" (9am-9pm, 6 days) philosophy reflects extreme commitment, though modern Alibaba emphasizes sustainable passion:

- **Sustainable Pace**: Long-term marathon, not sprint
- **Purpose-Driven**: Work on meaningful problems
- **Continuous Growth**: Never stop learning
- **Balance**: Health and family matter

### Passion Indicators

| Sign of Passion | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Treat company resources as your own |
| **Excellence** | Good enough is never enough |
| **Initiative** | See something, do something |
| **Resilience** | Persist through setbacks |

## Value 6: 敬业 (Commitment/Dedication)

### Meaning
Follow through on commitments. Deliver what you promise, when you promise it.

### The "Last Man Standing" Culture

During early Alibaba days and major crises (SARS, financial crisis), employees demonstrated commitment through:
- Working from home during SARS quarantine
- Taking salary cuts to preserve jobs
- Working through holidays for major events

### Commitment Framework

```
Making Commitments:
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. Understand the full scope before committing │
│ 2. Build in buffers for uncertainty            │
│ 3. Communicate early if risks emerge           │
│ 4. Do whatever it takes to deliver             │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Six-Vein Sword in Performance Evaluation

Alibaba's performance review (3-6-1 system) weights values heavily:

| Rating | Distribution | Criteria |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| **3 (Top)** | 30% | Exceptional business results + role model of values |
| **6 (Good)** | 60% | Solid performance + demonstrates values |
| **1 (Improve)** | 10% | Below expectations or values misalignment |

**Values Violation**: Can result in immediate termination, regardless of business results.

## Application: Values-Based Decision Making

### Scenario: Competing Priorities

**Situation**: A feature launch could increase revenue 10% but degrades user experience for 5% of users.

**Values Analysis**:
- **Customer First**: Revenue gain vs. user pain—user pain wins
- **Integrity**: Honest communication with stakeholders about tradeoffs
- **Commitment**: Deliver alternative solution that balances both

**Decision**: Don't launch; find solution that serves all customers.

### Daily Checklist

```
Start of Day:
□ How will I serve customers today?
□ What change can I embrace today?
□ How can I help my team today?

End of Day:
□ Did I act with integrity?
□ Did I give my best effort?
□ Did I deliver on my commitments?
```
