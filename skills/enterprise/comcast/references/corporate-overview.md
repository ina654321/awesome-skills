# Comcast Corporation - Corporate Overview

## Company Profile

**Comcast Corporation** (NASDAQ: CMCSA) is a global media and technology company with two primary businesses: **Comcast Cable** (Xfinity) and **NBCUniversal**. Through its subsidiaries, Comcast provides video, high-speed internet, voice, wireless, and security and automation services to residential customers under the Xfinity brand; provides similar services to business customers; operates cable networks, broadcast television, filmed entertainment, and theme parks; and owns Sky, a leading European media and entertainment company.

### Corporate History

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1963 | Founded by Ralph Roberts as American Cable Systems in Tupelo, Mississippi |
| 1969 | Renamed Comcast Corporation; acquired first pay-TV franchise |
| 1980s | Major expansion through acquisitions of regional cable systems |
| 1990s | Became nation's 3rd largest cable operator |
| 2002 | Acquired AT&T Broadband, becoming largest US cable operator |
| 2011 | Acquired 51% of NBCUniversal from General Electric |
| 2013 | Acquired remaining 49% of NBCUniversal |
| 2016 | Acquired DreamWorks Animation for $3.8B |
| 2017 | Launched Xfinity Mobile |
| 2018 | Acquired Sky Group for $40B |
| 2020 | Launched Peacock streaming service |
| 2025 | Opened Epic Universe; spun off cable networks to Versant |

### Competitive Position

**vs. Telecommunications:**
- vs. AT&T: Better content integration, superior broadband
- vs. Verizon: More broadband footprint, entertainment assets
- vs. T-Mobile: Fixed broadband advantage, content bundle

**vs. Media Companies:**
- vs. Disney: Broadband anchor, business services, sports focus
- vs. Netflix: Connectivity bundle, live content, advertising
- vs. Paramount: Larger scale, better financial position

**vs. Tech Companies:**
- vs. Amazon: Content + connectivity convergence
- vs. Apple: Broadband infrastructure, advertising scale
- vs. Google: Video content, network infrastructure

### Strategic Priorities (2025-2027)

1. **Broadband Go-to-Market Pivot**

2. **Peacock Path to Profitability**
   - Subscriber growth to 50M+

   - Ad revenue expansion

3. **Theme Park Growth**
   - Epic Universe ramp

   - Texas Kids Resort

4. **Wireless Expansion**
   - 15M+ lines target

   - 50% broadband penetration

5. **Cost Optimization**
