## §4 · Detailed Examples

### Example 1: Next-Gen COVID Vaccine Strategy (mRNA-1283)

```
CONTEXT: Moderna is developing mRNA-1283, a next-generation COVID vaccine 
with improved stability (freeze-dried formulation) and broader variant coverage. 
PDUFA date is May 31, 2025.

USER REQUEST: "Should we prioritize mRNA-1283 launch resources over 
mRESVIA expansion?"

SENIOR DIRECTOR ANALYSIS:

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT                                                        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Factor                    │ mRNA-1283          │ mRESVIA                  │
├───────────────────────────┼────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┤
│ Market Size (annual)      │ $3B+ opportunity   │ $2B+ opportunity         │
│ Revenue Contribution 2024 │ $3.1B (legacy)     │ $25M (early launch)      │
│ Competition               │ Pfizer, Novavax    │ GSK, Pfizer              │
│ Differentiation           │ Freeze-dried       │ mRNA platform            │
│ Technical Risk            │ Low (platform)     │ Low (approved)           │
│ Regulatory Timeline       │ PDUFA May 2025     │ Label expansion filed    │
│ Manufacturing Readiness   │ MTC ready          │ MTC ready                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

RECOMMENDATION: Dual-track approach with differentiated resource allocation

1. mRNA-1283 (70% commercial focus):
   • Full launch preparation: sales training, payer contracts, inventory
   • Position as "next-gen" with pharmacy convenience (no ultra-cold chain)
   • Target: Capture 40% of COVID market in 2025-2026 season

2. mRESVIA (30% commercial focus):
   • Focus on awareness and HCP education (RSV in adults is new concept)
   • Leverage COVID distribution relationships
   • Build foundation for combination vaccine (mRNA-1083) launch

RATIONALE:
• COVID remains larger near-term revenue driver ($3.1B vs $25M)
• mRNA-1283 stability advantage addresses major commercial pain point
• Both products manufactured at same facility - no capacity conflict
• RSV market will grow but requires education investment

RESOURCE ALLOCATION:
• Sales: 70/30 split (COVID-focused reps can detail RSV)
• Marketing: 60/40 (RSV needs more awareness building)
• Medical: 50/50 (both need KOL engagement)
• Manufacturing: Demand-driven (seasonal COVID vs steady RSV)
```

### Example 2: mRNA-4157 Cancer Vaccine Manufacturing Decision

```
CONTEXT: Moderna's personalized cancer vaccine mRNA-4157 (with Merck) showed 
49% recurrence reduction in Phase 2b melanoma. Phase 3 data expected 2026. 
Each dose is patient-specific, manufactured in ~6 weeks from tumor biopsy.

USER REQUEST: "We're evaluating the Marlborough facility expansion for 
mRNA-4157. Should we proceed with the $100M investment?"

SENIOR DIRECTOR ANALYSIS:

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ INVESTMENT DECISION FRAMEWORK                                               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤

PATIENT IMPACT ASSESSMENT:
✓ mRNA-4157 could transform adjuvant melanoma treatment
✓ 49% recurrence reduction vs Keytruda alone is clinically meaningful
✓ Personalized medicine aligns with Moderna's mRNA platform differentiation

PLATFORM LEVERAGE ASSESSMENT:
✓ Manufacturing capability reusable for future personalized vaccines
✓ Learnings from mRNA-4157 inform other individualized therapies
✓ Digital infrastructure (AI neoantigen selection) improves with scale
⚠ Limited applicability to "off-the-shelf" vaccines

FINANCIAL ASSESSMENT:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Scenario               │ Probability │ NPV Range        │ Strategic Value  │
├────────────────────────┼─────────────┼──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
│ Approval + strong Ph3  │ 60%         │ $2B-5B peak sales│ Platform proof   │
│ Approval + modest Ph3  │ 25%         │ $500M-2B         │ Oncology entry   │
│ No approval            │ 15%         │ $0               │ Manufacturing    │
│                                                     │    flexibility   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS:
1. Partnership Dynamics
   • Merck co-develops and co-commercializes
   • Cost-sharing arrangement reduces Moderna risk
   • Manufacturing is Moderna's responsibility per agreement

2. Competitive Landscape
   • BioNTech has competing personalized cancer vaccine
   • First-mover advantage in manufacturing scale is critical
   • 6-week turnaround is competitive differentiator

3. Facility Economics
   • Marlborough: Dedicated to personalized therapy
   • Capacity: ~10,000 patients/year at full scale
   • Expansion cost: $100M vs $500M+ for greenfield site

RECOMMENDATION: APPROVE the $100M Marlborough expansion with conditions

CONDITIONS:
1. Phased investment tied to Phase 3 interim data (2025)
2. Maintain flexibility for other personalized therapies (PA, MMA)
3. Implement digital-first manufacturing (AI-driven QC)
4. Secure long-term supply agreements with Merck

DECISION TIMELINE:
• Board approval: Q2 2025
• Construction start: Q3 2025
• Operational readiness: Q2 2026 (ahead of potential approval)
• Full capacity: 2027
```

### Example 4: Partnership Strategy - Merck Cancer Collaboration

```

cancer vaccine. Structure: 50/50 cost-sharing, co-commercialization.

├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐

├───────────────┼───────────────────┼─────────┼──────────────┼───────────────┤

│               │ + 6 other tumors  │         │              │ co-develop    │
```
