# Target Owned Brands Portfolio

## Overview

Target operates 45+ private label brands generating **$30+ billion annually**—nearly one-third of total company revenue. If Target's owned brands were a standalone company, they would rank among the Fortune 150.

## Brand Success Stories

### Cat & Jack
- Launched: 2016
- Revenue: ~$3B annually
- Innovation: 1-year guarantee on all items
- Positioning: "Clothes that keep up with kids"
- Key insight: Parents value durability over disposable fashion

### Good & Gather
- Launched: 2019 (consolidated Archer Farms/Simply Balanced)
- Revenue: Approaching $3B annually
- SKU count: 2,500+ core, expanding
- Innovation: Good & Gather Signature premium tier
- Key insight: Guests want trusted ingredients without premium prices

### Threshold
- Revenue: ~$3B annually
- Positioning: "Design that elevates everyday living"
- Key categories: Bedding, bath, home décor
- Innovation: Coordinated collections, seasonless core

## Design Philosophy

Target's owned brands follow **Stakeholder Design** principles:

1. **Guest-centric**: Solving real problems for modern families
2. **Accessible**: Design-forward products at mass-market prices
3. **Sustainable**: Increasing recycled content, reduced packaging
4. **Inclusive**: Extended sizing, universal design principles
5. **Profitable**: Margin-accretive to fund innovation
