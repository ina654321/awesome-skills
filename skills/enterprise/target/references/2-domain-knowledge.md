## 2. Domain Knowledge

### 2.1 Core Competencies

**Owned Brand Development**
- 45+ private labels generating $30B+ annually (nearly 1/3 of revenue)
- 11 brands exceed $1B each; 4 flagships near/above $3B
- Vertical integration from design to shelf—525+ trademark applications in 2024 alone
- Stakeholder design methodology: accessibility, aesthetics, affordability, sustainability integrated from concept

**Omnichannel Fulfillment**
- Drive Up: Industry-leading 4-minute average, $2B+ quarterly sales
- Order Pickup: Traffic driver with incremental in-store purchases
- Shipt: Wholly-owned same-day delivery platform
- Store fulfillment: Majority of digital orders picked and packed locally

**Loyalty & Personalization**
- Target Circle: 100M+ members, three-tier structure
- Target Circle 360: $99/year paid tier with unlimited same-day delivery
- Target Circle Card (formerly RedCard): 5% everyday discount, free shipping
- AI-driven personalization powering custom offers and predictive inventory

**Strategic Partnerships**
- Shop-in-shop: Ulta Beauty, Apple, Disney, Levi's, Starbucks
- Designer collaborations: 200+ since 1999 (Michael Graves, Isaac Mizrahi, Jason Wu, Diane von Furstenberg, Christopher John Rogers)
- Licensed exclusives: Magnolia (Chip & Joanna Gaines), exclusive toy lines

### 2.4 Historical Lessons

**2022 Inventory Crisis**
- Root cause: Demand velocity misread; overstocked discretionary as consumers shifted to services
- Impact: $5B+ lost operating income, 87% Q2 profit drop
- Response: Aggressive markdowns, order cancellations, supply chain reset
- Recovery: 17% inventory reduction, 273% operating profit rebound by mid-2023
- Lesson: Segment inventory strategies (essentials vs. discretionary), invest in flexibility over capacity

**Leadership Transition (2026)**
- Brian Cornell stepping down after 11 years; Michael Fiddelke (COO, 20-year veteran) becoming CEO
- Cornell remains Executive Board Chair
- Challenge: Maintaining "Expect More, Pay Less" momentum amid softening discretionary demand
