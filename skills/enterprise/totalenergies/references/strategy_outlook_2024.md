# TotalEnergies Strategy & Outlook 2024

**Presentation Date**: October 2, 2024  
**Location**: New York, USA  
**Presenters**: Patrick Pouyanné (Chairman & CEO) and Executive Committee

---

## Strategic Theme

> "**More Energy, Less Emissions, More Free Cash Flow**"

TotalEnergies advances its balanced and profitable transition strategy anchored on two pillars: **Oil & Gas, notably LNG**, and **Electricity**—the energy at the heart of the transition.

## Pillar 1: Oil & Gas Strategy

### Production Growth (~3% CAGR)
**Target**: ~3 Mboe/d by 2030 (from ~2.4 Mboe/d in 2024)

**Key Characteristics:**
- Predominantly LNG-driven growth
- Low-cost asset focus
- Low-emission operations
- Portfolio high-grading

### Major Project Pipeline (2024 FIDs)
| Project | Location | Type | TotalEnergies Stake |
|---------|----------|------|---------------------|
| Field 1 | Brazil | Deepwater Oil | 30% |
| Field 2 | Brazil | Deepwater Oil | 22% |
| Block 58 | Suriname | Deepwater | 50% |
| CLOV 3 | Angola | Deepwater | 30% |
| Marsa LNG | Oman | LNG | 100% |
| Ubeta | Nigeria | Gas | 40% |

### LNG Leadership
**Target**: +50% volume growth (2023-2030)

**Current Position:**
- 3rd largest global LNG player (40 Mt sold in 2024)
- Leading US exporter (>10 Mt in 2024)
- Diversified supply portfolio

**Key Growth Projects:**
| Project | Location | Expected Start-up |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| QatarEnergy LNG NFE | Qatar | 2026-2027 |
| QatarEnergy LNG NFS | Qatar | 2027-2028 |
| Plaquemines LNG | USA | 2026 |
| Rio Grande LNG | USA | 2027-2028 |
| Mozambique LNG | Mozambique | 2028-2029 |

### Investment Allocation
- **Upstream**: ~60% of Oil & Gas capex
- **LNG**: ~40% of Oil & Gas capex
- Focus on projects <$30/bbl breakeven

## Pillar 2: Integrated Power Strategy

### Electricity Generation Target
> **>100 TWh by 2030**

**Mix:**
- 70% Renewable (solar, wind)
- 30% Flexible (CCGT, storage)

This represents ~20% of TotalEnergies' total energy production by 2030.

### Renewable Capacity Roadmap
| Year | Target | Status |
|------|--------|--------|
| 2024 | ~25 GW | Achieved |
| 2025 | 35 GW | On track |
| 2030 | 100 GW | Pipeline secured |

**Technology Mix (2030):**
| Technology | Share | GW Target |
|------------|-------|-----------|
| Solar | 50% | ~50 GW |
| Onshore Wind | 30% | ~30 GW |
| Offshore Wind | 10% | ~10 GW |
| Storage | 10% | ~10 GW |

### Market Focus
**Geographic Distribution:**
- United States: ~40%
- European Union: ~25%
- Brazil: ~15%
- India: ~10%
- Other: ~10%

### Clean Firm Power
**Definition**: 24/7 renewable electricity complemented by flexible generation

**Value Proposition:**
- Premium pricing vs. pay-as-produced PPAs
- Baseload power supply
- Grid stability services
- Corporate PPA demand (data centers, industrials)

**Progress:**
- 500 GWh Clean Firm Power contracts secured in 2024
- Google: 30 TWh over 15 years (US data centers)
- Airbus: 3.3 TWh over 10 years (Germany/UK)

### Profitability Targets
| Metric | Target | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| ROACE | ≥12% | 2028-2030 |
| Cash Flow | Net cash positive | By 2028 |
| Merchant Exposure | ~30% | By 2030 |

## Value Creation Levers

### Upstream: Develop & Produce Better
- Scale and cost reduction
- Digital transformation
- Operational excellence
- Carbon intensity reduction

### LNG: Sell Better
- Long-term contracts with Asian buyers
- Brent-indexed pricing
- Trading optimization
- Flexibility services

### Integrated Power: Integrated Model
- Development to generation
- Vertical integration
- Trading & optimization
- Premium pricing (Clean Firm Power)

### Portfolio Management
- **Farm-Downs**: Target 2 GW/year run rate
- **Acquisitions**: Selective development pipeline purchases
- **Divestments**: Non-core asset sales to recycle capital

## Risk Management & Flexibility

### Sensitivities
| Variable | Impact on Net Income |
|----------|---------------------|
| $10/bbl oil price change | ±$2.4B |
| $2/MMBtu gas price change | ±$0.5B |
| $10/t refining margin change | ±$0.5B |
| 15¢ EUR/USD change | ∓$0.2B income, ∓$0.9B cash flow |

### Flexibility Mechanisms
1. **Capex Flexibility**: $2B reduction capacity within guidance
2. **Portfolio Optimization**: Asset sales to maintain buybacks
3. **Variable Buybacks**: Adjust based on price environment
4. **Dividend Floor**: Protected as base commitment

## Asia: Heart of Energy Market Growth

### Market Opportunity
**Drivers:**
- Growing population and living standards
- Net zero commitments by major economies
- Coal-to-gas switching
- Renewable electricity growth

### LNG Demand
- ~70% of global LNG demand comes from Asia
- China, Japan, Korea: Major buyers
- Long-term contract demand strong

### TotalEnergies Position
- Strong LNG sales growth to Asia
- 4 Mtpa long-term contracts signed in 2024
- ~20% of 2030 LNG target already contracted

> "The Company retains flexibility to reduce its net investments by $2 billion in case of a sharp drop in prices."

**On Value Creation:**
> "Thanks to this clear and disciplined investment policy and the perspective for +$10 billion of free cash flow growth by 2030."

**On Shareholder Returns:**

> "By actively completing in 2024 its integrated model in key targeted deregulated markets, Integrated Power is making progress on its main levers to achieve at least 12% ROACE by 2028-2030."

## Implementation Priorities

### 2024-2025 Immediate Actions
1. **Execute Major FIDs**: Six upstream projects, LNG expansions
2. **Accelerate Renewables**: Progress toward 35 GW by 2025
3. **Portfolio Optimization**: Farm-downs, selective divestments
4. **Mozambique LNG**: Resume construction activities
5. **Integrated Power**: Scale Clean Firm Power offerings

### Medium-Term (2025-2030)
1. **Production Growth**: Deliver ~3% CAGR in oil & gas
2. **LNG Expansion**: Achieve +50% volume growth
3. **Electricity Scale**: Reach >100 TWh and 100 GW capacity
4. **Returns Leadership**: Maintain best-in-class ROACE
5. **Transition Delivery**: Progress toward net zero 2050
