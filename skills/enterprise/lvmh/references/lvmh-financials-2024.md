# LVMH Financial Intelligence 2024-2025

## Executive Summary

LVMH Moët Hennessy Louis Vuitton SE reported €84.68 billion in revenue for fiscal year 2024, marking the first organic revenue decline since the pandemic (-2% organic, -5% reported). Despite headwinds, the group maintained industry-leading profitability with €19.6 billion in operating profit (23.1% margin).

## 2024 Strategic Priorities & Performance

### 1. China Market Recovery
- **Challenge:** Asia (excl. Japan) revenue declined 10% YoY
- **Response:** Increased investment in domestic Chinese experiences
- **Q4 2024 Signal:** "Improving trends" per management
- **2025 Outlook:** Cautious optimism; stimulus impact monitored

### 2. Wines & Spirits Restructuring
- **Challenge:** Division revenue -11%; cognac demand soft
- **Actions:** 
  - Jean-Jacques Guiony appointed CEO Wines & Spirits (Nov 2024)
  - Charles Delapalme named Hennessy CEO
  - Focus on premiumization and channel optimization

### 3. Tiffany Integration Progress
- **Investment:** €15.8B acquisition (2021)
- **Status:** Revenue stabilization; margin improvement ongoing
- **Key Wins:**
  - "Tiffany T" and "Knot" collections successful
  - Supreme collaboration: 2B+ social impressions
  - 5th Avenue flagship renovation completed

### 4. Digital & E-commerce
- **Share:** ~15% of total revenue
- **Growth:** Mid-single digits
- **Initiatives:**
  - LVMH Luxury Ventures investments
  - Alibaba partnership expansion
  - AI-powered personalization rollout

### 5. Sustainability (Life 360)
- **Target:** 30% water reduction by 2030
- **Renewable Energy:** 73% of operations
- **Packaging:** 100% eco-design by 2030
