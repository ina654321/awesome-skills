# The History of Coca-Cola

## The Beginning (1886)

### Dr. John Pemberton's Invention

**May 8, 1886**: Dr. John Stith Pemberton, an Atlanta pharmacist, created the syrup for Coca-Cola. He carried a jug of the new product to Jacobs' Pharmacy in downtown Atlanta, where it was sampled, pronounced "excellent," and placed on sale for five cents a glass.

**The Original Formula**:
- Caramel-colored syrup
- Carbonated water
- Coca leaf extract (containing small amounts of cocaine, legal at the time)
- Kola nut (caffeine source)
- Other flavoring oils (lemon, orange, nutmeg, cinnamon)

**First Year Sales**: About nine drinks per day, totaling approximately $50 in revenue (with $70 in expenses—a loss).

### Frank M. Robinson

Pemberton's bookkeeper, Frank M. Robinson:
- Suggested the name "Coca-Cola" (the two Cs would "look well in advertising")
- Designed the Spencerian script logo still used today
- Created the first newspaper ad

## Asa Candler Era (1888-1923)

### Acquisition

**1887-1891**: Asa Griggs Candler, an Atlanta businessman, purchased the formula and brand from Pemberton and other investors for a total of approximately **$2,300**—one of history's greatest business bargains.

**1888**: Dr. John Pemberton died at age 57, largely forgotten and in poverty. Atlanta druggists closed their stores to attend his funeral.

### Building the Foundation

**1892**: The Coca-Cola Company was incorporated (January 29).

**1893**: "Coca-Cola" trademark registered with U.S. Patent Office (January 31).

**Marketing Innovations**:
- First newspaper ad: $11,401 advertising budget (1892)
- Branded calendars, clocks, and signage
- Coupons for free samples
- Painted wall advertisements

**1895**: Coca-Cola sold in every U.S. state and territory.

### The Bottling System

**1899**: Asa Candler granted exclusive bottling rights to Benjamin F. Thomas and Joseph B. Whitehead for **$1**. This established the franchise bottling system that continues today.

## Robert W. Woodruff Era (1923-1954)

### "The Boss"

Robert W. Woodruff (born 1889, died 1985) became president in 1923 and led the company for over three decades, transforming Coca-Cola into a global brand.

### Global Expansion

**1920s-1930s**:
- Expansion into Europe, Asia, Latin America
- Olympic Games partnership begins (1928)
- FIFA World Cup partnership begins

**World War II**:
- Woodruff decreed that every U.S. serviceman should get a Coke for 5 cents, regardless of cost
- Established 64 bottling plants behind military lines
- Created global production infrastructure
- Cemented Coca-Cola as symbol of American culture

### Iconic Marketing

**1931**: Haddon Sundblom's Santa Claus advertisements established the modern image of Santa—red suit, white beard, jolly demeanor.

**Contour Bottle**: Developed in 1915 by Earl R. Dean and Alexander Samuelson of the Root Glass Company. Designed to be recognizable even in the dark or broken.

## The Post-War Era (1954-1981)

### Continued Growth

- International expansion acceleration
- Diversification into new products
- Fanta, Sprite, Tab introductions

**1960**: Acquisition of Minute Maid (first non-cola product)

**1971**: "I'd Like to Buy the World a Coke" (Hilltop) commercial—one of the most famous ads in history

## The Goizueta Era (1981-1997)

### Roberto Goizueta

Cuban-born chemist who became CEO in 1981. Transformed Coca-Cola into a modern marketing company.

### Key Moves

**1982**: Diet Coke introduction—first extension of the Coca-Cola trademark

**1985**: **New Coke**—the infamous formula change
- Launched April 23, 1985
- Consumer backlash was immediate and intense
- "Old" Coca-Cola returned as "Coca-Cola Classic" 79 days later
- Actually strengthened brand loyalty

**Global Expansion**: Aggressive international growth, particularly in emerging markets

**Acquisition Strategy**: Expanded portfolio through acquisitions

## The Secret Formula

### The Myth

The Coca-Cola formula has been one of the world's most closely guarded trade secrets for over 130 years.

### History of Secrecy

- **Original**: Kept in various secure locations
- **1925-2011**: Stored in SunTrust Bank vault in Atlanta
- **2011-present**: Moved to World of Coca-Cola museum vault
- Only two executives know the complete formula at any time

### Reality

While the "7X" flavoring formula is secret, the basic ingredients are public knowledge. The magic is in the proportions and production process.

## Lessons from History

### Brand Management

1. **Consistency**: Core brand elements unchanged for 130+ years
2. **Adaptation**: Willingness to evolve (sugar-free, flavors, new categories)
3. **Resilience**: Recovery from New Coke proves brand strength
4. **Heritage**: Leveraging history while staying contemporary

### Business Strategy

1. **Franchise Model**: Bottling system enables global scale
2. **Portfolio Expansion**: From 1 product to 200+ brands
3. **M&A Discipline**: Acquiring to fill gaps or gain capabilities
4. **Global/Local Balance**: Think global, act local

### Cultural Relevance

1. **Marketing Excellence**: Creating campaigns that become cultural moments
2. **Consumer Connection**: Making the personal universal (Share a Coke)
3. **Values Alignment**: Happiness, togetherness, refreshment
4. **Consistency**: Same brand promise across generations
