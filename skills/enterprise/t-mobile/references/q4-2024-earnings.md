## Q&A Session Highlights

### On Competitive Environment

**Question**: How is T-Mobile maintaining growth in a maturing market?

**Mike Sievert Response**:
- "Flight to value" benefiting T-Mobile as consumers become cost-conscious
- Highest-end rate plan is also most popular — customers want the T-Mobile network
- Strategy remarkably consistent, delivering remarkably consistent performance
- Rural market expansion driving new growth vectors

### On Rural Strategy

**Key Points:**
- Rural markets: 40% of US population, 17.5% current penetration
- Target: 20% penetration in 2025, 33%+ long-term
- US Cellular acquisition accelerates rural presence
- Brand awareness and retail presence key to growth

### On ARPA Growth

**Analysis:**
- Postpaid ARPA: $146.30 (+4.3% YoY)
- Growth drivers: Selective price adjustments on older plans, premium plan adoption
- Churn remained at record lows despite pricing actions
- Customers see value in network quality and service

### On B2B Growth

**Callie Field (Business Group President):**
- NYC Public Safety contract validates network for most demanding use cases
- 5G Advanced capabilities differentiate enterprise offerings
- Growth across public sector, healthcare, manufacturing verticals
- Fixed wireless gaining traction with business customers

### On Fiber Strategy

**Peter Osvaldik:**
- Joint ventures with EQT (Lumos) and KKR (MetroNet)
- 2.3 million fiber-passed locations acquired
- Target: 12-15 million fiber locations by 2030
- Complements 5G FWA strategy where fiber economics justify

### On Starlink Partnership

**Mike Sievert:**
- First US carrier with direct-to-cell satellite capability
- Activated emergency services during California wildfires
- Eliminates coverage gaps in remote areas
- Customers can send texts via satellite when no terrestrial coverage
