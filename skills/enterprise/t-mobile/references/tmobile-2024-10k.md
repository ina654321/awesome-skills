# T-Mobile 2024 Annual Report (10-K) - Key Highlights

## Company Overview

T-Mobile US, Inc. (NASDAQ: TMUS) is a leading provider of wireless communications services in the United States, Puerto Rico, and the United States Virgin Islands. The company operates as the second-largest wireless carrier in the U.S. as measured by total postpaid and prepaid customers.

## Network Excellence

### 5G Leadership
- **Coverage**: 300+ million people covered by high-capacity 5G
- **Spectrum Depth**: 3.1 MHz sub-6 GHz per million subscribers
  - T-Mobile: 3.1 MHz
  - AT&T: 2.7 MHz
  - Verizon: 2.1 MHz
- **Awards**: 
  - 5 consecutive years of Opensignal overall network experience wins
  - Fastest 5G network per Ookla Speedtest
  - Most consistent, most available 5G per Ookla

### Infrastructure
- Macro cell sites: 102,000+
- Small cell/DAS sites: 41,000+
- 5G Standalone (SA) core network operational

### Innovations
- **Starlink Partnership**: First US provider with direct-to-cell satellite capabilities
- **5G Advanced**: America's only 5G Advanced network
- **Emergency Response**: Activated satellite-to-cell during CA wildfires (99% network restoration within days)

## Strategic Initiatives

### US Cellular Acquisition
- **Value**: $4.4 billion
- **Assets**: Mobile operations, retail stores, ~30% of spectrum
- **Customers**: ~4.4 million
- **Status**: Pending regulatory approval
- **Strategic Value**: Rural market expansion, spectrum augmentation

### Fiber Broadband Strategy
- **Partnerships**: Joint ventures with EQT (Lumos) and KKR (MetroNet)
- **Locations**: 2.3 million fiber-passed locations acquired
- **Target**: 12-15 million fiber locations by 2030
- **Purpose**: Complement 5G FWA with fiber where economics justify

### 5G Home Internet (FWA)
- **Performance**: 12 consecutive quarters of broadband growth leadership
- **Q4 Additions**: 428,000 net new customers
- **Total 2024**: 1.7 million net additions
- **Market Position**: #1 in 5G FWA nationally

## Competitive Position

### Market Share (as of December 2024)
- T-Mobile: 35%
- Verizon: 34%
- AT&T: 27%
- Others: 4%

### Key Differentiators
1. **5G Network Quality**: Mid-band spectrum advantage, speed leadership
2. **Customer Value**: Competitive pricing, perks (Netflix On Us), transparent billing
3. **Innovation Velocity**: First-to-market features, aggressive Uncarrier moves
4. **Customer Experience**: TEX care model, industry-leading satisfaction

## Risk Factors

### Competitive Risks
- Intense price competition from Verizon and AT&T
- Cable MVNOs (Comcast, Charter) gaining market share
- Potential for industry consolidation

### Operational Risks
- Spectrum availability and auction outcomes
- Network equipment supply chain (vendor concentration)
- Integration execution (US Cellular acquisition)

### Regulatory Risks
- FCC and state regulatory changes
- Merger approval uncertainties
- Privacy and data security requirements
