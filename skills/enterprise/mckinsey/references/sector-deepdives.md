## Energy & Materials

### Oil & Gas

**Energy Transition Scenarios**:
```
Pathway Options for Integrated O&G Companies:

Option A: Accelerated Transition
- 50% low-carbon investment by 2030
- Net-zero operations by 2035
- Pivot to renewables, hydrogen, CCUS
- Risk: Premature asset stranding

Option B: Gradual Transition
- 30% low-carbon investment by 2030
- Net-zero by 2050
- Optimize hydrocarbons, selective new energy
- Risk: Missing green growth

Option C: Return Focus
- Maximize hydrocarbon cash flows
- Return capital to shareholders
- Minimal transition investment
- Risk: Regulatory, social license
```

**Key Transition Metrics**:
| Metric | 2020 | 2030 Target | 2050 Target |
|--------|------|-------------|-------------|
| Carbon intensity (kg/boe) | 75 | 40 | 0 |
| Low-carbon revenue % | 1% | 15% | 50%+ |
| Methane emissions (Mt) | 0.5 | 0.1 | 0 |

## Advanced Industries

### Automotive

**EV Transition**:
```
EV Market Evolution:

Phase 1 (2020-2025): Early Adopters
- Premium segment focus
- Regulation-driven (EU, China)
- Supply constraints

Phase 2 (2025-2030): Mass Market
- Price parity with ICE
- Charging infrastructure mature
- Mainstream adoption

Phase 3 (2030+): Dominance
- ICE becomes niche
- Battery recycling ecosystem
- Autonomous fleets

McKinsey Projections:
- 2030: 50% of new car sales EV
- 2035: 75% of new car sales EV
- 2040: 80%+ of vehicles on road EV

**Industry Dynamics**:
- Commercial aviation recovery post-COVID
- Supply chain bottlenecks (engines, chips)
- Sustainable aviation fuels (SAF)
- Defense spending increases (geopolitics)

**SAF Opportunity**:
```
Sustainable Aviation Fuel Market:
- Current: 0.1% of jet fuel
- 2030 target: 5% (EU mandate)
- 2050 target: 65% (net-zero aviation)
- Investment needed: $1.5T

Pathways:
├── HEFA (used cooking oil, tallow)
│   └── Current production, feedstock limited
├── Alcohol-to-jet
│   └── Corn, sugar cane, waste
├── Power-to-liquid
│   └── Green hydrogen + CO2
└── Gasification + Fischer-Tropsch
    └── Municipal solid waste
