## Interview Format Overview

### McKinsey Interview Structure

**Round 1 (Two 45-60 minute interviews)**:
- Case interview (30-35 minutes)
- PEI (10-15 minutes)
- Your questions (5 minutes)

**Round 2 (Two 45-60 minute interviews)**:
- Senior partner involvement
- Similar structure, higher stakes

**Final Round (EM/Partner)**:
- Often more behavioral
- Culture fit assessment

### Evaluation Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | What Assessors Look For |
|-----------|--------|------------------------|
| **Problem Solving** | 40% | Structured thinking, creativity, quantitative skills |
| **Personal Impact** | 30% | Leadership, collaboration, persuasion |
| **Entrepreneurial Drive** | 20% | Initiative, resilience, achievement orientation |
| **Inclusive Leadership** | 10% | Diversity mindset, empathy, team development |

## Case Interview Framework

### The 4S Framework

**1. Summarize (1-2 minutes)**
- Restate the problem in your own words
- Confirm understanding with interviewer
- Identify key question to answer

**2. Structure (2-3 minutes)**
- Build MECE issue tree
- Prioritize branches (80/20)
- State initial hypothesis

**3. Solve (20-25 minutes)**
- Drive the analysis
- Request data as needed
- Show calculations clearly
- Synthesize insights

**4. Synthesize (2-3 minutes)**
- State recommendation clearly (Pyramid Principle)
- Support with 2-3 key arguments
- Address risks and next steps

---

### Opening Questions Template

Always ask 2-3 clarifying questions before structuring:

**Clarifying Questions**:
1. "What is the client's specific objective? (revenue, profit, market share)"
2. "What is the timeline for this decision?"
3. "Are there any constraints I should be aware of? (financial, regulatory)"

**NOT Recommended**:
- "What does the company do?" (should research beforehand)
- "Tell me more about the industry" (wastes time)

---

### Framework Development

**Rule #1: Never use canned frameworks**

❌ Bad: "I'll use Porter's 5 Forces"

✅ Good: "I'll structure this around three key questions: Is the market attractive? Can we compete effectively? And how should we enter?"

**Framework Structure Example**:
```
Market Entry Decision
├── Market Attractiveness
│   ├── Size and growth
│   ├── Profitability
│   └── Key trends
├── Competitive Position
│   ├── Our capabilities
│   ├── Competitive advantages
│   └── Barriers to entry
└── Entry Strategy
    ├── Entry mode options
    ├── Investment required
    └── Risk assessment
```

### Math Tips

**Mental Math Shortcuts**:

| Calculation | Shortcut |
|-------------|----------|
| 15% of X | 10% + 5% |
| 20% of X | Divide by 5 |
| 25% of X | Divide by 4 |
| Square 25 | 2×3=6, add 25 → 625 |
| Compound growth | Rule of 72 (years to double = 72/rate) |

**Market Sizing Approach**:
```
Top-down: Total Market × Segment Share × Penetration
Bottom-up: Customers × Frequency × Price

Always triangulate with both methods
```

### Synthesis Template

**30-Second Answer Structure**:

> "I recommend [ACTION] because [REASON 1], [REASON 2], and [REASON 3]. 
> Key risks include [RISK], which can be mitigated by [MITIGATION]. 
> Next steps should focus on [NEXT STEP]."

**Example**:
> "I recommend entering the German market through acquisition because: 
> (1) the $4B market is growing at 15% annually, 
> (2) acquisition provides immediate scale and customer relationships, and 
> (3) the NPV of $300M exceeds our hurdle rate. 
> The main risk is integration challenges, which we can address through a dedicated integration team and retention packages. 
> Our next step should be identifying target acquisition candidates."

## Personal Experience Interview (PEI)

### PEI Dimensions

#### 1. Personal Impact

**Definition**: Ability to persuade, influence, and work with others

**Sample Questions**:
- "Tell me about a time you changed someone's mind"
- "Describe a situation where you had to convince a skeptical stakeholder"
- "Give an example of working with a difficult teammate"

**STAR Format**:
- **S**ituation: Set the context (1 sentence)
- **T**ask: Your specific challenge (1 sentence)
- **A**ction: What YOU did (focus on your actions)
- **R**esult: Quantified outcome and lessons learned

**Example Story**:
> "In my consulting project team, a senior member disagreed with my analytical approach (S). I needed to convince him to adopt my methodology to meet our deadline (T). I scheduled a 30-minute meeting where I walked through my logic, showed data from similar projects, and proposed a pilot test (A). He agreed to try my approach, which ultimately reduced our analysis time by 40% and became the team's standard method (R)."

#### 2. Entrepreneurial Drive

**Definition**: Taking initiative, creating opportunities, resilience

**Sample Questions**:
- "Tell me about a time you started something new"
- "Describe achieving a goal despite significant obstacles"
- "Give an example of going beyond your defined role"

**What Assessors Look For**:
- Self-starting behavior
- Comfort with ambiguity
- Persistence through setbacks
- Innovative problem-solving

#### 3. Inclusive Leadership

**Definition**: Leading diverse teams, fostering belonging, developing others

**Sample Questions**:
- "Tell me about leading a diverse team"
- "Describe mentoring someone different from you"
- "Give an example of creating an inclusive environment"

#### 4. Courageous Change

**Definition**: Driving transformation, challenging status quo, managing uncertainty

**Sample Questions**:
- "Tell me about a time you challenged established practices"
- "Describe leading through significant change"
- "Give an example of taking a calculated risk"

---

### PEI Story Preparation

**Recommended Story Bank**:

| Dimension | Stories Needed | Timeframe |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Personal Impact | 2-3 | Recent (last 2 years) |
| Entrepreneurial Drive | 2-3 | Recent and significant |
| Inclusive Leadership | 2 | Team-focused |
| Courageous Change | 2 | Transformation examples |

**Story Development Checklist**:
- [ ] Clear, concise setup (30 seconds)
- [ ] "I" focused (not "we")
- [ ] Specific actions taken
- [ ] Quantified results
- [ ] Lessons learned
- [ ] Relevant to consulting

## Practice Cases

### Case 1: Pharma Profitability

**Prompt**: "Our client is a pharmaceutical company whose profits have declined 20% over the past year. They're not sure why. How would you help them?"

**Sample Structure**:
```
Profit = Revenue - Costs
├── Revenue Analysis
│   ├── Price (any changes?)
│   ├── Volume (by product)
│   └── Mix (shifting to lower-margin products?)
└── Cost Analysis
    ├── COGS (raw materials, manufacturing)
    ├── R&D (pipeline investments)
    └── SG&A (marketing, overhead)
```

**Key Insights to Look For**:
- Patent expiration impact on volume
- Generic competition
- R&D cost increases
- Marketing spend efficiency

### Case 2: Retail Market Entry

**Prompt**: "A US-based clothing retailer wants to expand internationally. Should they enter the UK market?"

**Sample Structure**:
```
Market Entry Decision
├── Market Attractiveness
│   ├── Market size (£X billion apparel market)
│   ├── Growth rate (X% annually)
│   └── Competitive dynamics (Zara, H&M, ASOS)
├── Capability Assessment
│   ├── Brand awareness in UK
│   ├── Supply chain capabilities
│   └── Local market knowledge
└── Entry Strategy
    ├── Entry mode (acquisition, organic, JV)
    ├── Investment required
    └── Expected returns
```

### Case 3: Tech Acquisition

**Prompt**: "A large tech company is considering acquiring a smaller AI startup for $500M. Should they do it?"

**Sample Structure**:
```
Acquisition Evaluation
├── Strategic Rationale
│   ├── Capabilities gained
│   ├── Market access
│   └── Competitive defense
├── Financial Analysis
│   ├── Standalone valuation
│   ├── Synergies (revenue, cost)
│   └── Integration costs
└── Risk Assessment
    ├── Integration risk
    ├── Technology risk
    └── People retention
```

## Common Mistakes

### Case Interview Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Better Approach |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| Jumping to solution | Shows no structured thinking | Always build framework first |
| Asking for data randomly | Appears directionless | Request data to test hypothesis |
| Ignoring interviewer hints | Misses guidance | Listen carefully, adjust approach |
| Poor math | Shows weak quant skills | Practice mental math daily |
| No synthesis | Leaves hanging | Always summarize with recommendation |
| Generic frameworks | Shows no critical thinking | Customize framework to case |

### PEI Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Better Approach |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| Using "we" too much | Can't assess YOUR impact | Focus on "I" |
| No quantified results | Weak evidence | Include specific numbers |
| Rambling stories | Poor communication | Practice concise delivery |
| Negative tone | Red flag for culture | Stay positive, focus on solutions |
| Irrelevant stories | Wastes time | Prepare consulting-relevant examples |

## Preparation Checklist

### 4-6 Weeks Before
- [ ] Read Case in Point or similar case book
- [ ] Practice 5+ cases with peers
- [ ] Build PEI story bank
- [ ] Research McKinsey deeply

### 2-3 Weeks Before
- [ ] Practice 10+ live cases
- [ ] Record yourself, review
- [ ] Do mock interviews
- [ ] Refine PEI stories

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Read McKinsey Quarterly articles
- [ ] Practice math daily
- [ ] Review frameworks
- [ ] Get good sleep

### Day Before
- [ ] Light case practice (1-2 max)
- [ ] Review PEI stories
- [ ] Prepare questions for interviewers
- [ ] Relax and sleep well
