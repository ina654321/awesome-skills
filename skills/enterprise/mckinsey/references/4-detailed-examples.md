## § 4 · Detailed Examples

### Example 1: Market Entry Strategy (German EV Market)

**Client Situation**: US automotive parts manufacturer considering European expansion

**McKinsey Approach:**

**Step 1: Problem Definition**
```
Question: Should our client enter the German EV components market,
and if so, through what mode?

Scope: Focus on battery management systems, 5-year horizon
Constraints: Max $200M investment, need positive NPV in Year 3
```

**Step 2: Issue Tree**
```
Market Entry Decision
├── Market Attractiveness
│   ├── Market size and growth (EV sales projections)
│   ├── Profitability (margin benchmarks)
│   └── Competitive dynamics (incumbents, barriers)
├── Capability Assessment
│   ├── Product fit (technical requirements)
│   ├── Cost position (manufacturing, logistics)
│   └── Brand recognition (Europe vs US)
└── Entry Strategy
    ├── Mode (greenfield, acquisition, JV, partnership)
    ├── Timing (first-mover vs fast-follower)
    └── Resource requirements (investment, talent)
```

**Step 3: Key Analyses**
- Top-down market sizing: German EV sales × BMS value per vehicle
- Bottom-up validation: OEM supplier lists × contract values
- Competitive benchmarking: Cost curves vs. Bosch, Continental
- Entry mode comparison: NPV analysis for each option

**Step 4: Hypothesis & Testing**
```
Initial Hypothesis: Acquisition of mid-tier German BMS 
provider is optimal due to speed-to-market and relationships.

Test Result: PARTIALLY VALIDATED
- Confirmed: Speed advantage (18 vs 36 months)
- Confirmed: OEM relationships valuable
- New insight: Cultural integration risk underestimated
- Adjustment: Recommend JV with acquisition option
```

**Step 5: Recommendation (Pyramid Structure)**
```
RECOMMENDATION: Enter German EV market through 50/50 JV 
with Tier 2 supplier AutoTech GmbH, with call option to 
acquire full ownership in Year 3.

SUPPORTING ARGUMENTS:
1. Market is highly attractive ($4.2B by 2030, 22% CAGR)
   - Evidence: OEM electrification commitments, regulatory push
   
2. JV addresses speed and capability gaps
   - Evidence: 18-month faster vs greenfield, local engineering talent
   
3. Financial returns exceed hurdles
   - Evidence: NPV of $340M, IRR of 28%, payback in 2.5 years

RISKS & MITIGATION:
- Integration risk → Dedicated integration team, retention bonuses
- Technology obsolescence → R&D commitment, partnership with university
```

### Example 2: Cost Transformation (Healthcare System)

**Client Situation**: 15-hospital health system facing margin pressure from reimbursement cuts

**Target**: $150M annual cost reduction (8% of operating costs)

**McKinsey Approach:**

**Step 1: Cost Structure Analysis**
```
Total Operating Costs: $1.9B
├── Labor (55% = $1.04B)
│   ├── Clinical staff (70%)
│   └── Administrative (30%)
├── Supplies (25% = $475M)
│   ├── Medical devices
│   ├── Pharmaceuticals
│   └── General supplies
└── Other (20% = $380M)
    ├── Facilities
    ├── IT
    └── Corporate overhead
```

**Step 2: Opportunity Identification (80/20 Rule)**
| Category | Current Spend | Benchmark | Gap | Priority |
|----------|---------------|-----------|-----|----------|
| Nursing labor | $420M | -12% vs peers | $50M | 🔴 High |
| Physician spend | $280M | -8% vs peers | $22M | 🔴 High |
| Medical devices | $180M | -15% vs GPO | $27M | 🔴 High |
| Pharmacy | $150M | -5% vs peers | $8M | 🟡 Medium |
| Administrative | $95M | -20% vs peers | $19M | 🟡 Medium |

**Step 3: Initiative Development**

**Initiative A: Clinical Labor Optimization ($65M)**
- Standardize nurse staffing ratios across system
- Implement float pool for flexibility
- Reduce agency nurse usage by 60%
- Optimize shift patterns

**Initiative B: Supply Chain Consolidation ($40M)**
- Renegotiate medical device contracts
- Standardize to lower-cost equivalents where clinically appropriate
- Implement value analysis committees

**Initiative C: Administrative Efficiency ($25M)**
- Consolidate back-office functions
- Automate revenue cycle processes
- Reduce management layers

**Step 4: Implementation Roadmap**
```
Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Quick Wins ($40M)
- GPO renegotiation
- Agency nurse reduction
- Administrative headcount freeze

Phase 2 (Months 6-12): Operational Changes ($75M)
- Staffing model implementation
- Supply standardization
- Revenue cycle automation

Phase 3 (Months 12-18): Structural Changes ($35M)
- Organizational redesign
- Facility optimization
- Long-term contracts
```

**Step 5: Value Tracking Dashboard**
| Initiative | Target | Month 6 | Month 12 | Month 18 |
|------------|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| Clinical Labor | $65M | $15M | $45M | $65M |
| Supply Chain | $40M | $18M | $32M | $40M |
| Administrative | $25M | $8M | $18M | $25M |
| **Total** | **$130M** | **$41M** | **$95M** | **$130M** |

### Example 3: Digital Transformation (Retail Bank)

**Client Situation**: Regional bank losing market share to digital-native competitors

**Objective**: Build industry-leading digital banking platform

**McKinsey Approach:**

**Step 1: Current State Assessment**
```
Digital Maturity Score: 2.3/5.0 (Industry avg: 3.1, Leaders: 4.5+)

Gaps:
- Mobile app rating: 2.8 stars (leaders: 4.5+)
- Digital sales: 15% (leaders: 60%+)
- Time to open account: 45 minutes (leaders: 5 minutes)
- Customer effort score: High
```

**Step 2: Customer Journey Mapping**
```
Key Journey: Open New Account
├── Discover (Current: Poor)
│   └── Issues: Limited digital discovery, poor SEO
├── Apply (Current: Poor)
│   └── Issues: 45-min process, 20+ fields, document upload friction
├── Verify (Current: Fair)
│   └── Issues: Manual review takes 2-3 days
├── Fund (Current: Good)
│   └── Issues: Limited funding options
└── Activate (Current: Poor)
    └── Issues: No onboarding flow, low feature adoption
```

**Step 3: Target State Design**
```
Vision: "Bank anywhere, in under 5 minutes"

Target Metrics:
- Account opening: <5 minutes
- Digital sales ratio: >50%
- App store rating: >4.5
- NPS: >50 (from current 12)
```

**Step 4: Transformation Program**

**Workstream A: Platform Modernization**
- Cloud-native core banking migration
- API-first architecture
- Microservices transformation

**Workstream B: Experience Redesign**
- Mobile app redesign (design system)
- AI-powered personalization
- Omnichannel integration

**Workstream C: Data & Analytics**
- Customer data platform
- Real-time decisioning engine
- Predictive analytics

**Workstream D: Operating Model**
- Agile at scale transformation
- Product-centric organization
- New talent acquisition (500+ engineers)

**Step 5: Business Case**
```
Investment: $180M over 3 years
Returns:
- Cost savings: $45M/year (branch reduction, automation)
- Revenue uplift: $85M/year (digital sales, cross-sell)
- NPV: $220M
- IRR: 35%
```

### Example 4: PE Due Diligence (SaaS Company)

**Client Situation**: Private equity firm evaluating $500M acquisition of B2B SaaS company

**McKinsey Role**: Commercial due diligence

**Step 1: Investment Thesis Validation**
```
Thesis: Company has 40% revenue growth with potential for 
expanded TAM and margin expansion through pricing optimization

Key Questions:
1. Is the growth sustainable?
2. What's the true market size?
3. Can margins expand?
4. What are key risks?
```

**Step 2: Revenue Analysis**
```
Growth Decomposition:
Total Growth (40%) = Organic Growth (25%) + M&A (15%)

Organic Growth Drivers:
- New customer acquisition: +15%
- Existing customer expansion: +8%
- Pricing: +2%

Concerns Identified:
- Customer acquisition cost up 35% YoY
- Sales cycle lengthening (45 → 62 days)
- Increasing discounting (avg 18% off list)
```

**Step 3: Market Sizing**
```
TAM Analysis:
- Top-down: $12B (Gartner data)
- Bottom-up: 50,000 target companies × $50K ACV = $2.5B
- Serviceable: $800M (realistic near-term)

Company Position:
- Current: 2% market share
- 5-year target: 8% share (ambitious but achievable)
```

**Step 4: Competitive Positioning**
```
Competitive Matrix:
                    Features   Price   Service   Brand
Company (Target)      ★★★☆☆    ★★★☆☆   ★★★★☆    ★★★☆☆

Competitor B          ★★★★☆    ★★★★☆   ★★★★☆    ★★★★☆

Finding: Company is "stuck in the middle" — not premium 
enough for enterprise, not cheap enough for SMB
```

**Step 5: Risk Assessment & Recommendation**
```
KEY RISKS IDENTIFIED:
🔴 HIGH: Growth decelerating, CAC increasing
🔴 HIGH: Product differentiation eroding
🟡 MEDIUM: Key man risk (CTO)

RECOMMENDATION: PROCEED WITH CAUTION
- Reduce valuation by 15% to account for growth deceleration
- Negotiate 15% escrow for 18 months
```

### Example 5: Sustainability Strategy (Energy Company)

**Client Situation**: Integrated oil & gas company seeking net-zero pathway

**Objective**: Develop credible 2050 net-zero strategy with near-term milestones

**McKinsey Approach:**

**Step 1: Emissions Baseline**
```
Current Emissions (Scope 1+2+3): 450 MT CO2e/year
├── Scope 1 (Operations): 25 MT
├── Scope 2 (Purchased energy): 15 MT
└── Scope 3 (Product use): 410 MT (90%)

Emissions Intensity: 65 kg CO2e/boe
Benchmark (industry avg): 58 kg CO2e/boe
```

**Step 2: Abatement Opportunity Assessment**
```
Abatement Curve (cost per ton avoided):

Low-cost wins (<$50/t):
- Methane leak detection/repair: -5 MT, -$10/t
- Energy efficiency: -8 MT, $15/t
- Renewable electricity: -15 MT, $25/t
- CCUS (high concentration): -20 MT, $45/t

Medium cost ($50-100/t):
- Electrification: -12 MT, $65/t
- Green hydrogen pilots: -5 MT, $80/t
- Nature-based solutions: -10 MT, $90/t

High cost (>$100/t):
- Advanced CCUS: -25 MT, $120/t
- Hydrogen at scale: -30 MT, $150/t
- DAC (direct air capture): -50 MT, $300/t
```

**Step 3: Scenario Analysis**
```
Scenario A: Current Policies (1.5°C unlikely)
- Investment: $5B by 2030
- Emissions reduction: 25% by 2030, 50% by 2040
- Risk: Stranded assets, regulatory penalties

Scenario B: Accelerated Transition (1.5°C aligned)
- Investment: $15B by 2030
- Emissions reduction: 50% by 2030, net-zero by 2050
- Opportunity: First-mover advantage, green premiums

Scenario C: Business Transformation (1.5°C + growth)
- Investment: $25B by 2030
- Strategy: Pivot to new energy (50% revenue by 2035)
- Emissions: Net-zero by 2040, carbon negative by 2050
```

**Step 4: Strategic Priorities**
```
RECOMMENDATION: Pursue Scenario B with elements of C

Priority 1: Core Business Decarbonization (2025-2030)
- Eliminate routine flaring by 2025
- 50% methane reduction by 2027
- Net-zero operations (Scope 1+2) by 2030

Priority 2: Portfolio Transition (2025-2035)
- Grow renewables to 20% of capex by 2027
- Develop carbon capture business unit
- Exit high-carbon assets (ranked by abatement cost)

Priority 3: New Energy Growth (2030-2050)
- Hydrogen production at scale
- Biofuels and e-fuels
- Carbon removal services
```

**Step 5: Implementation & Governance**
```
Governance Structure:
- Board Sustainability Committee (quarterly oversight)
- Chief Sustainability Officer (reporting to CEO)
- Business unit carbon budgets (linked to compensation)

Key Metrics:
- Absolute emissions (MT CO2e)
- Emissions intensity (kg/boe)
- Percentage low-carbon investment
- Green revenue share
- Internal carbon price ($75/t)

Value at Risk/Opportunity:
- Stranded asset risk without action: $8B
- Green premium opportunity: $3B annually by 2035
- Cost of inaction exceeds cost of action by 2x
```
