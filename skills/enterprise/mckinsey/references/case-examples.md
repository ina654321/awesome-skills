## Strategy Cases

### Case: Airline Network Optimization

**Client**: Major US airline struggling with profitability

**Problem**: Load factors declining, costs rising, need route optimization

**Analysis Approach**:

```
Profitability per Route = (Load Factor × Capacity × Yield) - Operating Costs

Route Analysis Framework:
├── Revenue Optimization
│   ├── Load factor improvement (pricing, scheduling)
│   ├── Yield management (fare classes, overbooking)
│   └── Network connectivity (hub efficiency)
└── Cost Management
    ├── Fuel efficiency (fleet, routing)
    ├── Labor optimization (crew scheduling)
    └── Airport fees (slot negotiations)
```

**Key Findings**:
- 20% of routes generate 80% of losses
- Hub-and-spoke model outdated for point-to-point demand
- Fleet mix suboptimal (too many large aircraft)

**Recommendations**:
1. Eliminate 15 underperforming routes (save $200M)
2. Add 8 point-to-point routes with smaller aircraft
3. Renegotiate airport contracts at 3 hubs
4. Implement dynamic pricing system

**Result**: 12% margin improvement, $340M annual profit increase

### Case: B2B Pricing Strategy

**Client**: Industrial equipment manufacturer

**Problem**: Price pressure from competitors, margin compression

**Pricing Analysis**:
```
Current State:
- List price: $100K
- Average discount: 25%
- Realized price: $75K
- Cost: $60K
- Margin: 20%

Opportunity Areas:
├── Value-Based Pricing
│   ├── ROI quantification ($500K savings = premium justified)
│   ├── Total cost of ownership messaging
│   └── Performance guarantees
├── Segmentation
│   ├── Price-sensitive: Standard offering, lower price
│   ├── Value-seeking: Premium offering, higher price
│   └── Strategic accounts: Custom pricing
└── Discount Discipline
    ├── Approval thresholds
    ├── Bundle requirements
    └── Volume commitments
```

**Recommendations**:
1. Implement value-selling training for sales team
2. Create "good-better-best" product tiers
3. Establish discount authority matrix
4. Deploy CPQ (Configure-Price-Quote) software

**Result**: Average selling price +8%, margins +400bps

## Operations Cases

### Case: Manufacturing Plant Efficiency

**Client**: Auto parts manufacturer with 5 plants

**Problem**: OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness) at 65% vs industry best 85%

**Analysis**:
```
OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality

Current State:
- Availability: 85% (15% downtime)
- Performance: 80% (running slow)
- Quality: 95% (5% defects)
- OEE: 0.85 × 0.80 × 0.95 = 65%

Opportunity Breakdown:
├── Availability Improvement
│   ├── Preventive maintenance (reduce unplanned downtime)
│   ├── Changeover optimization (SMED methodology)
│   └── Supply reliability (inventory buffers)
├── Performance Improvement
│   ├── Speed optimization (remove artificial constraints)
│   ├── Micro-stoppage elimination (Andon system)
│   └── Shift handoff standardization
└── Quality Improvement
    ├── Statistical process control
    ├── Poka-yoke (error-proofing)
    └── Supplier quality management
```

**Implementation**:
- Phase 1: Quick wins (3 months) → OEE to 72%
- Phase 2: TPM rollout (6 months) → OEE to 78%
- Phase 3: Advanced analytics (12 months) → OEE to 83%

### Case: Supply Chain Optimization

**Client**: Consumer goods company with supply chain costs 15% above benchmark

**Diagnostic Framework**:
```
Supply Chain Cost Structure:
├── Procurement (35% of total)
│   ├── Supplier consolidation
│   ├── Specification optimization
│   └── Payment term improvements
├── Manufacturing (25%)
│   └── (see manufacturing case above)
├── Warehousing (15%)
│   ├── Network optimization
│   ├── Automation opportunities
│   └── Slotting optimization
├── Transportation (20%)
│   ├── Mode optimization
│   ├── Route efficiency
│   └── Carrier negotiation
└── Inventory (5% carrying cost)
    ├── Safety stock optimization
    ├── Forecast accuracy
    └── Vendor managed inventory
```

**Key Initiatives**:
1. Supplier consolidation: 200 → 80 suppliers, 8% savings
2. DC network redesign: 12 → 8 facilities, $15M savings
3. Transportation optimization: $22M savings
4. Inventory reduction: $50M working capital release

**Total Impact**: $85M annual savings, 12% cost reduction

## M&A Cases

### Case: Vertical Integration Strategy

**Client**: Food retailer considering backward integration into farming

**Strategic Analysis**:
```
Integration Decision Framework:
├── Strategic Rationale
│   ├── Supply security
│   ├── Cost reduction potential
│   ├── Quality control
│   └── Differentiation opportunity
├── Financial Analysis
│   ├── Acquisition cost: $500M
│   ├── Synergies: $80M/year
│   ├── Integration costs: $50M
│   └── NPV: $120M (positive)
├── Capability Assessment
│   ├── Core competency gap
│   ├── Management bandwidth
│   └── Risk profile change
└── Alternative Options
    ├── Long-term contracts
    ├── Strategic partnerships
    └── Joint ventures
```

**Recommendation**: Partner, don't acquire
- Full acquisition too risky (no farming expertise)
- Joint venture with established farmer preferred
- 5-year exclusive supply agreement with quality standards

### Case: Divestiture Decision

**Client**: Conglomerate with 5 business units, one underperforming

**Portfolio Analysis**:
```
Business Unit Evaluation:

Unit A (Core): 40% revenue, 18% margin → INVEST
Unit B (Core): 30% revenue, 15% margin → MAINTAIN  
Unit C (Growth): 15% revenue, 8% margin, 25% growth → INVEST
Unit D (Decline): 10% revenue, 5% margin, -5% growth → DIVEST
Unit E (New): 5% revenue, -2% margin, 40% growth → EVALUATE

Divestiture Analysis for Unit D:
├── Market attractiveness: Declining, competitive
├── Strategic fit: Low synergy with other units
├── Fix vs Exit: Turnaround would take 3+ years, $50M investment
├── Buyer universe: 3 strategic, 2 financial
└── Valuation: $200M - $280M range
```

**Recommendation**: Divest Unit D to strategic buyer
- Use proceeds to invest in Unit C expansion
- Focus management attention on core

### Case: Geographic Expansion

**Client**: SaaS company seeking international growth

**Market Prioritization**:
```
Market Attractiveness vs Capability Fit:

                CAPABILITY FIT
                Low       High
            ┌─────────┬─────────┐
     High   │ AVOID   │ PRIORITY│
            │ Russia  │ UK, DE  │
MARKET      ├─────────┼─────────┤
ATTRACT.    │ DEPRIORITIZE │ SELECTIVE │
            │ Brazil  │ Japan   │
     Low    └─────────┴─────────┘

Priority Market Deep-Dive (UK example):
├── Market Size: £2.5B addressable
├── Growth: 15% CAGR
├── Competition: Moderate (3 players)
├── Regulatory: GDPR compliant (already done)
├── Language: English (no localization needed)

```

**Entry Strategy**:
1. Hire UK country manager
2. Localize product (currency, regulations)
3. Build reference customers (3 pilot accounts)
4. Scale sales team (5 → 20 people in Year 2)

## Market Entry Cases

### Case: Emerging Market Entry

**Client**: Medical device company considering India

**Market Assessment**:
```
India Healthcare Market:
├── Market Size: $12B medical devices, growing 15%
├── Segments
│   ├── Premium (10%): Global standards, import
│   ├── Mid-market (30%): Local manufacturing, quality
│   └── Value (60%): Price-sensitive, basic
├── Regulatory
│   ├── CDSCO approval required (18-24 months)
│   ├── Make in India incentives
│   └── Price controls on some devices
├── Distribution
│   ├── Hospital direct (40%)
│   ├── Dealer network (45%)
│   └── Government tenders (15%)
└── Competition
    ├── Global leaders (Siemens, GE)
    ├── Local champions (Trivitron)
    └── Chinese imports (low-end)
```

**Entry Recommendation**:
- **Mode**: Joint venture with local manufacturer
- **Timing**: 24-month preparation (regulatory, setup)
- **Product**: Mid-market portfolio (price 40% below premium)
- **Target**: $50M revenue in 5 years

### Case: Digital Platform Entry

**Client**: Traditional bank launching neobank competitor

**Strategic Options**:
```
Entry Mode Comparison:

Option A: Separate Brand (Chase → Finn)
- Pros: Clean slate, no legacy constraints
- Cons: No trust transfer, expensive build
- Investment: $500M
- Success probability: 30%

Option B: Digital-First Sub-brand (Goldman → Marcus)
- Pros: Parent backing, selective features
- Cons: Some legacy constraints
- Investment: $300M
- Success probability: 50%

Option C: Transform Core (BBVA)
- Pros: Full customer base, unified data
- Cons: Complex transformation, execution risk
- Investment: $1B
- Success probability: 40%

Option D: Partnership (Bank + Fintech)
- Pros: Speed, innovation, lower cost
- Cons: Less control, margin sharing
- Investment: $100M
- Success probability: 60%
```

**Recommendation**: Option B (Sub-brand approach)
- Launch Marcus-style digital bank
- Start with savings/CDs (low complexity)
- Add checking, lending over 2 years
- Target: 1M customers, $5B deposits in 3 years
