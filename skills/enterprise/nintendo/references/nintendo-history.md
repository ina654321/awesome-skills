# Nintendo Company History

## Origins (1889-1960s)

### Hanafuda Playing Cards (1889)
- Founded by Fusajiro Yamauchi on September 23, 1889
- Initially named "Nintendo Koppai" (later Nintendo Playing Card Co.)
- Produced handmade hanafuda (flower cards) for Japanese gambling games
- Location: Kyoto, Japan (headquarters still there today)

### Business Evolution
| Era | Focus | Key Development |
|-----|-------|-----------------|
| 1889-1929 | Playing cards | Established distribution network |
| 1930s-1950s | Diversification | Taxis, hotels, instant rice (failed ventures) |
| 1959 | Disney partnership | Licensed Disney characters on cards |
| 1963 | Company renamed | Nintendo Co., Ltd. |
| 1966 | Toy development | Ultra Hand (first toy hit) |

## Entry into Electronic Gaming (1970s-1980s)

### Arcade Beginnings
- **1973**: Laser Clay Shooting System (bowling alley attraction)
- **1974**: Wild Gunman (light gun game)
- **1975**: EVR Race (electronic horse racing)
- **1978**: Computer Othello (first video game)

### The Golden Age (1980-1989)

**1980**: Game & Watch
- Handheld LCD games designed by Gunpei Yokoi
- Introduced D-pad (cross-shaped directional controller)
- Sold 43.4 million units

**1981**: Donkey Kong
- Created by Shigeru Miyamoto
- Introduced Jumpman (later renamed Mario)
- Saved Nintendo of America from bankruptcy
- First game with cutscenes and narrative

**1983**: Famicom (Family Computer)
- Released in Japan
- Redesigned as NES for North America (1985)
- Revitalized US video game industry after crash

**1985**: Super Mario Bros.
- Defined side-scrolling platformers
- Sold 40+ million copies
- Established Mario as gaming icon

**1986**: The Legend of Zelda
- First console game with save battery
- Invented action-adventure genre template
- Open-world exploration concept

## Console Wars Era (1990s)

### Super Nintendo Era (1990-1996)
- Technical superiority over Sega Genesis
- Mode 7 graphics (3D-like rotation/scaling)
- Genre-defining RPGs: Final Fantasy VI, Chrono Trigger
- Super Mario World launch title

### Nintendo 64 Era (1996-2001)
- 3D gaming transition
- Analog stick innovation
- Super Mario 64: 3D platforming template
- The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time
- Rare partnership: GoldenEye 007, Banjo-Kazooie

### Game Boy Phenomenon (1989-2000s)
- 118 million units sold (original + Color)
- Tetris bundle phenomenon
- Pokémon Red/Blue (1996): 31 million units
- Established portable gaming market

## Modern Era (2001-Present)

### GameCube (2001-2007)
- Mini-DVD format
- Super Smash Bros. Melee
- Metroid Prime
- Pikmin (new Miyamoto IP)

### Wii Revolution (2006-2013)
- Motion control innovation
- "Blue Ocean" strategy: expand gaming demographics
- 101.6 million units sold
- Wii Sports bundled phenomenon
- Wii Fit: fitness gaming

### DS Phenomenon (2004-2014)
- Touch screen innovation
- Dual screens
- Brain Age: expanded to adult demographic
- 154 million units sold (all models)

### 3DS (2011-2020)
- Glasses-free 3D
- Animal Crossing: New Leaf phenomenon
- 75.9 million units sold

### Wii U (2012-2017)
- GamePad second screen
- Commercial disappointment (13.6 million units)
- Foundation for Switch concept
- Splatoon (new IP success)

### Switch Renaissance (2017-Present)
- Hybrid portable/home console
- The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild launch
- 154+ million units sold
- Third-best-selling console of all time

### Switch 2 (2025-Present)
- Launched June 5, 2025
- Mouse-mode Joy-Con
- 4K docked output
- Backward compatible with Switch
- 10.36 million units sold (first 5 months)

## Cultural Impact

### Innovations Pioneered
- D-pad (Game & Watch)
- Shoulder buttons (SNES)
- Analog stick (N64)
- Rumble pak (N64)
- Motion controls (Wii)
- Touch screen gaming (DS)
- Hybrid console (Switch)

### Industry Influence
- Saved US video game industry (1985)
- Established modern 3D gaming standards
- Expanded gaming to casual/family demographics
- Proved gameplay > graphics
- Demonstrated value of first-party software

### Business Philosophy
- "Lateral thinking with seasoned technology"
- Conservative financial management
- Quality over quantity
- Hardware-software integration
- Long-term IP stewardship
