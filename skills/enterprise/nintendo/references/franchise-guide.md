# Nintendo Franchise Guide

## Super Mario

### Overview
- **Debut:** Donkey Kong (1981)
- **Creator:** Shigeru Miyamoto
- **Units Sold:** 939+ million (all games)
- **Revenue:** $64+ billion
- **Core Philosophy:** Joy of movement

### Main Sub-Series

| Sub-Series | Debut | Key Innovation | Best-Selling Title |
|------------|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| 2D Platformers | SMB (1985) | Side-scrolling precision | New Super Mario Bros. DS (30M) |
| 3D Platformers | SM64 (1996) | 3D movement/camera | Super Mario Odyssey (27M) |
| Kart Racing | MK (1992) | Items, drifting | Mario Kart 8 Deluxe (67M) |
| RPGs | SMRPG (1996) | Timed attacks | Mario & Luigi series |
| Party Games | MP (1998) | Board game + minigames | Mario Party Superstars |
| Sports | MT (1995) | Arcade sports | Mario Tennis/Golf series |

### Design Principles
- **Jump physics:** Predictable arc, variable height
- **Power-ups:** Risk/reward (Super Mushroom vs. Fire Flower)
- **Level design:** Teach → Test → Twist progression
- **Accessibility:** One button primary action

## The Legend of Zelda

### Overview
- **Debut:** The Legend of Zelda (1986)
- **Creator:** Shigeru Miyamoto, Takashi Tezuka
- **Units Sold:** 182+ million
- **Core Philosophy:** Discovery and wonder

### Era Breakdown

| Era | Games | Characteristics |
|-----|-------|-----------------|
| 2D Era (1986-2004) | Zelda 1, LTTP, LA, OoA/OoS | Top-down, item-gated progression |
| 3D Era (1998-2011) | OoT, MM, WW, TP, SS | Z-targeting, cinematic storytelling |
| Open-Air Era (2017-) | BotW, TotK | Physics sandbox, complete freedom |

### Key Innovations
- **Save battery** (Zelda 1): First console game with saves
- **Z-targeting** (OoT): Lock-on combat system
- **Musical instruments:** Ocarina, Wind Waker baton
- **Open-air physics:** BotW chemistry engine

### Design Principles
- "See that mountain, go there" freedom
- Multiplicative gameplay (systems interact)
- Minimal hand-holding
- Environmental storytelling

## Pokémon

### Overview
- **Debut:** Pokémon Red/Green (1996)
- **Creators:** Satoshi Tajiri, Ken Sugimori
- **Developer:** Game Freak (Nintendo publishing)
- **Units Sold:** 489+ million games
- **Total Revenue:** $150+ billion (all media)
- **Core Philosophy:** Collection, bonding, trading

### Main Series Structure
- Core RPGs: Mainline games (Red/Blue → Scarlet/Violet)
- Remakes: Updated versions (FireRed, HeartGold, etc.)
- Third versions: Enhanced editions (Yellow, Crystal, Platinum)
- Legends: Open-world experiments (Arceus)

### Key Innovations
- Link Cable trading/battling (social gaming)
- Two-version strategy (collect them all)
- EV/IV systems (hidden depth for competitive)
- Type matchups (strategic depth)

### Design Principles
- Gotta catch 'em all: Collection compulsion
- Bond with your Pokémon: Emotional attachment
- Social trading: Community interaction
- Accessible depth: Easy to learn, years to master

## Metroid

### Overview
- **Debut:** Metroid (1986)
- **Creators:** Gunpei Yokoi, Yoshio Sakamoto
- **Units Sold:** 20+ million
- **Core Philosophy:** Isolation and empowerment

### Sub-Series

| Sub-Series | Style | Notable Entries |
|------------|-------|-----------------|
| 2D Metroidvania | Side-scrolling exploration | Super Metroid, Fusion, Dread |
| Prime Trilogy | First-person adventure | Metroid Prime 1-3 |
| Other M | Cinematic action | Metroid: Other M |

### Key Innovations
- **Sequence breaking:** Player-discovered shortcuts
- **Atmospheric storytelling:** Environmental narrative
- **Power progression:** Gaining abilities opens world
- **Female protagonist:** Samus Aran revealed

### Design Principles
- Isolation: Alone on alien world
- Empowerment: Start weak, become unstoppable
- Exploration: Non-linear world
- Atmospheric: Sound design paramount

## Kirby

### Overview
- **Debut:** Kirby's Dream Land (1992)
- **Creator:** Masahiro Sakurai
- **Units Sold:** 50+ million
- **Core Philosophy:** Accessible platforming

### Characteristics
- **Easy difficulty:** First platformer for many children
- **Copy abilities:** Transform by swallowing enemies
- **Cute aesthetic:** Appeals to all ages
- **Spin-offs:** Many experimental games (Canvas Curse, Air Ride)

### Design Principles
- "Kirby is for everyone": Accessibility first
- Experimental freedom: Try weird mechanics
- Spin-off innovation: Test concepts in side games

## Animal Crossing

### Overview
- **Debut:** Dōbutsu no Mori (2001, N64 Japan)
- **Creator:** Katsuya Eguchi
- **Units Sold:** 80+ million (all versions)
- **Core Philosophy:** Relaxing life simulation

### Key Innovations
- Real-time clock: Game continues when you're away
- Seasonal changes: Time of year affects gameplay
- Social simulation: Villager relationships
- No failure state: Cannot lose, only progress

### Design Principles
- Anti-stress: No deadlines, no punishment
- Self-expression: Home decoration, fashion
- Daily routine: Brief, satisfying daily tasks
- Social: Visit friends' towns

## Splatoon

### Overview
- **Debut:** Splatoon (2015, Wii U)
- **Directors:** Yusuke Amano, Tsubasa Sakaguchi, Hisashi Nogami
- **Units Sold:** 35+ million (both games)
- **Core Philosophy:** Fresh multiplayer innovation

### Key Innovations
- **Turf War:** Paint coverage wins, not kills
- **Kid/squid transformation:** Verticality and stealth
- **Motion controls:** Precise gyro aiming
- **Fashion focus:** Gear with abilities

### Design Principles
- Fresh: Youth culture aesthetic
- Accessible: Paint = contribution (everyone helps)
- Deep: Motion control mastery
- Live service: Regular Splatfests, updates

## Donkey Kong

### Overview
- **Debut:** Donkey Kong (1981)
- **Creator:** Shigeru Miyamoto
- **Core Philosophy:** Challenging platforming

### Sub-Series
- Country series (Rare/Retro): Pre-rendered 3D graphics, tight platforming
- 3D platformers: Jungle Beat, etc.
- Vs. series: Kong battles

### Key Innovations
- Pre-rendered graphics (Country on SNES)
- Tag-team gameplay (Diddy/Dixie)
- Mine cart levels (industry standard now)
