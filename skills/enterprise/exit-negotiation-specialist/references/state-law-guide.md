# State Employment Law Quick Reference

> Key variations in employment law by US state that affect exit negotiations

## Severance Pay Requirements

| State | Required Severance | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------|
| California | None (unless contract) | WARN Act applies (60 days for 50+ layoffs) |
| New York | None (unless contract) | WARN Act: 90 days for 25+ employees |
| Texas | None | Texas is at-will employment state |
| Florida | None | Florida is at-will employment state |
| Illinois | None (unless contract) | Illinois WARN Act for layoffs |
| Massachusetts | None (unless contract) | Post 2022: non-compete must be disclosed at offer |
| Washington | None (unless contract) | Washington WARN Act applies |
| Colorado | None (unless contract) | Colorado Equal Pay for Equal Work Act |

## At-Will Employment

All US states recognize at-will employment with exceptions:
- **Discrimination**: Cannot terminate based on protected class (race, sex, age 40+, disability, religion, national origin)
- **Retaliation**: Cannot terminate for exercising legal rights (workers comp, OSHA, etc.)
- **Public Policy**: Cannot terminate for refusing to break the law

## WARN Act (Federal)

**Trigger**: Employer with 100+ employees must give 60 days notice for:
- Plant closings affecting 50+ workers
- Mass layoffs (33% of workforce or 500+ workers)

**Penalties**: Back pay and benefits for each day of violation

## State-Specific Protections

### California
- **竞业限制**: Generally void (Edel v. Yelp exception; SB 699/2023 made non-competes unenforceable)
- **Non-disparagement**: Cannot contractually prevent truthful statements about employment
- **Trade secrets**: California Uniform Trade Secrets Act (CUTSA)
- **Paid time off**: Must pay out all accrued PTO at separation
- **Severance**: Not required by state law but common in practice

### New York
- **竞业限制**: Enforceable if reasonable (geography, time, scope)
- **Gardening leave**: Commonly used; severance often tied to it
- **NDA limits**: Cannot prevent disclosure of sexual harassment
- **Pay transparency**: Salary range must be disclosed in job postings

### Texas
- **竞业限制**: Broadly enforceable if reasonable
- **No income tax**: Makes total comp comparison different from other states
- **Trade secrets**: Texas Uniform Trade Secrets Act

### Florida
- **竞业限制**: Broadly enforceable post-2021 reforms (HB 600)
- **Non-compete standard**: "Restrictive covenants are presumed reasonable"

## Unemployment Insurance

| State | Max Weekly Benefit | Duration |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| California | $450/week | 26 weeks |
| New York | $504/week | 26 weeks |
| Texas | $535/week | 26 weeks |
| Florida | $275/week | 12–23 weeks |
| Washington | $1,290/week | 26 weeks |

**Key**: Quitting without "good cause" disqualifies you from UI. Being fired for misconduct disqualifies you. Being fired for "gross misconduct" may create disqualification.

## Key Exit Documents by State

### California
- Always request: Signed release of claims
- Get in writing: Any agreements about non-disparagement scope
- Verify: All PTO paid out (mandatory)

### New York
- Check: Separation agreement for age discrimination release (ADEA compliance)
- Verify: All wages paid (can sue for 100% of unpaid wages as penalty + attorney's fees)

### Texas
- Confirm: Non-compete scope and duration explicitly stated
- Check: Any mandatory arbitration agreements survive termination

### Florida
- Verify: Non-compete agreement signed and dated
- Check: Any forum selection clause (must be in Florida to be enforceable)

## Multi-State Considerations

If you worked in multiple states or were remote:
- **Governing law**: Check employment agreement for choice of law clause
- **Jurisdiction**: Where can you sue/be sued?
- **Arbitration**: Is arbitration required? Where?
- **Tax**: Which state(s) will tax your severance?

## International Considerations

### UK
- Wrongful dismissal: 3 months' notice minimum
- Unfair dismissal: Can claim after 2 years
- Settlement agreements: Typically include reference to ACAS
- 竞业限制: Only enforceable if reasonable

### Canada
- Common law reasonable notice: Typically 3–24 months based on seniority
- Constructive dismissal: When employer fundamentally changes terms
- No federal non-compete for non-senior executives (2021 law)

### EU
- Varies by country significantly
- Germany: 3 months notice standard; severance negotiation common
- Netherlands: Transition payment (transitieverlof) for 2+ year tenure
- France: Very protective; severance negotiation typically required
