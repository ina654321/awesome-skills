# Equity Vesting Deep Dive

> Comprehensive reference for equity compensation in exit negotiations

## Equity Types

### RSUs (Restricted Stock Units)
- **No ownership** until vesting and settlement
- At vesting: Ordinary income on fair market value
- At exit: Typically forfeited if unvested (no acceleration unless negotiated)
- Post-termination: Usually 90-day exercise window

### Stock Options (ISO / NSO)
- **ISO**: Tax-advantaged; only taxed at sale; $100K annual exercise limit applies
- **NSO**: Taxed at exercise (ordinary income) and at sale (capital gains)
- At exit: Exercise window opens (90 days typical)
- **Warning**: If company is private, exercise may create illiquid tax liability

### Restricted Stock
- You own shares (not units)
- At grant: Tax consequence unless restricted
- At vesting: Ordinary income on FMV
- At exit: Subject to repurchase right at lower of cost or FMV if unvested

### Performance Shares
- Vesting tied to metrics (company targets, stock price)
- Negotiate at exit: May have different treatment than time-based equity

## Vesting Schedules

### Standard: 4-Year with 1-Year Cliff
| Period | Vested | % of Grant |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| 12 months (cliff) | 25% | 1/4 |
| 18 months | 31.25% | 3/48 monthly thereafter |
| 24 months | 37.5% | 1/4 + 12/48 |
| 36 months | 62.5% | |
| 48 months | 100% | |

### Negotiating at Exit

**Acceleration Triggers:**
- **Single-trigger**: Vesting accelerates on change of control (acquisition/IPO)
- **Double-trigger**: Vesting accelerates only if both (1) acquisition AND (2) termination within 12 months
- At exit: Neither automatically triggers — must negotiate

**Negotiation Leverage:**
- Time remaining to next vesting event
- Proximity to IPO or acquisition
- Your role and contributions to value creation
- Company needs (do they need you during transition?)

**Acceleration Formulas to Negotiate:**
- "Pro-rata acceleration": X months of additional vesting
- "Immediate acceleration": Full remaining grant
- "Modified cliff": Vest next 12 months immediately

### Exercise Windows

| Type | Standard Window | Extended Window |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| ISO (after 3/31/2013) | 3 months | If company is acquired: 12 months |
| NSO | 90 days | Negotiated |
| Restricted Stock | Immediate (already vested) | N/A |
| RSUs (vested) | Settle per plan | Negotiated |

**Extended Exercise Window Negotiation:**
- Request 1–3 years post-termination
- Particularly valuable if company is not yet public
- New employer may buy out or extend as part of hiring package

### Tax Implications

**At Exercise:**
- NSO: FMV - Exercise price = ordinary income
- ISO: No regular tax (but AMT may apply)
- RSUs: FMV at vesting = ordinary income

**At Sale:**
- Short-term capital gain (< 1 year held): Ordinary income rates
- Long-term capital gain (≥ 1 year): Lower rates (0/15/20%)

**83(b) Election:**
- Filed within 30 days of restricted stock grant
- Elect to pay tax now at grant price
- All future appreciation taxed as capital gains
- **Critical**: If missed, cannot make late election

### IPO and Lock-Up Considerations

**Pre-IPO Exit:**
- Negotiate equity treatment before company goes public
- Extended exercise window is crucial for private company equity
- Consider whether equity has 409A valuation

**Lock-Up Period (Post-IPO):**
- Typically 180 days after IPO
- Negotiate carve-out for: estate planning, tax withholding, charitable giving
- Consider negotiating at exit if company is preparing for IPO

### Golden Parachute Specifics

For executives (Section 280G calculations):

**Double Trigger Parachute:**
1. Change of control occurs, AND
2. Covered executive is terminated (voluntary with good reason OR involuntary)

**280G Excise Tax:**
- If parachute payments exceed 3x average W-2, excise tax (20%) + income tax applies
- Negotiation point: Gross-up clause for excise taxes
- Alternative: Cap parachute to avoid triggering 280G threshold

**Key Terms to Negotiate:**
- Definition of "cause" (narrower = better for exec)
- Definition of "good reason" (broader = better for exec)
- Severance multiple (typically 2–3x base + bonus)
- Benefit continuation period
- Outplacement scope
- Equity treatment (acceleration, exercise windows)
