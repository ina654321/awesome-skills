# Negotiation Scripts Library

> Ready-to-adapt scripts and phrases for exit negotiation conversations

## Phase 1: The Announcement

### Voluntary Resignation Script
```
Manager: "I need to share something with you. I've decided to pursue 
a new opportunity and will be leaving [company]. Before I discuss 
details, I want you to know that I'm committed to making this 
transition as smooth as possible.

I've greatly valued my time here and the [project/relationship/team] 
we've built. My goal is to ensure nothing falls through the cracks.

I wanted to speak with you first before anything is communicated further. 
What does a smooth transition look like from your perspective?"
```

### Responding to the Manager's Response

**If manager seems surprised:**
"I'm sorry for the timing — I wanted to give you as much notice as possible. 
I'm happy to discuss transition details whenever works for you."

**If manager asks why:**
"I've learned so much here. This opportunity aligns with where I want 
to take my career next. I'd prefer to keep the details between us for now."

**If manager reacts emotionally:**
"I understand this may be unexpected. I'm committed to handling this 
professionally and want to ensure we're both in a good place."

## Phase 2: HR Meeting

### Opening Statement for HR
```
"Thank you for meeting with me. I'm excited about my next chapter and 
want to ensure my departure reflects the good relationship we've built 
over [X years/months].

I've given this considerable thought and believe a well-structured 
departure is in everyone's best interest. I'd like to discuss what 
a positive transition looks like, including the support [company] 
provides in these situations.

I've put together some thoughts I'd like to share, and I'm equally 
interested in hearing what [company] has traditionally offered in 
similar situations."
```

### Responding to Initial Offer

**When they name a low number:**
"I appreciate you sharing [company]'s standard package. I'd like to 
discuss a few elements that I think make my situation a bit different. 
Given [my tenure / the timing / specific contributions], I believe 
enhanced terms are appropriate. Would you be open to discussing 
an adjustment?"

**When they say "we don't negotiate on severance":**
"I understand [company] has standard policies. I'm not looking to 
create difficulties — I'm looking for a fair outcome that reflects 
my contributions and ensures a positive relationship going forward. 
Is there anyone with authority to make exceptions I could speak with?"

## Phase 3: Counter-Negotiation

### Countering on Severance
```
"Based on my research and understanding of comparable situations, 
I believe [X weeks/months] is more appropriate given:
- [My tenure of X years and track record]
- [My role in [specific project/metric/client]]
- [Market standards for this level / this industry]

I'm happy to be flexible on other terms, but I'd like to ask that 
you reconsider the severance component."
```

### Requesting Equity Acceleration
```
"Regarding equity, I understand my unvested shares won't vest automatically. 
However, given that my departure comes at [an important time / during 
a critical project / after [X] months of service], I'd like to 
discuss partial acceleration as transition support.

Specifically, I'd ask for [X months] of acceleration, which I believe 
is reasonable given [my role in getting [project] to [milestone] / 
the value I've contributed / etc.].

If that's not possible, I'd ask for an extended exercise window of 
[3 years] post-termination, which would allow me to participate 
in the upside I've helped create."
```

### Requesting 竞业限制 Release
```
"One important element for me is career flexibility. I'd like to propose 
that the 竞业限制 not be activated in my separation.

My understanding is that [new company] operates in a different space, 
so there shouldn't be competitive overlap. Releasing this restriction 
would allow me to transition smoothly without concern about compliance, 
which I think benefits both of us.

Alternatively, I'm happy to discuss a narrow scope that specifically 
addresses any legitimate concerns."
```

### Requesting Garden Leave
```
"Given the [seniority/sensitivity] of my role, I'd like to propose 
garden leave for the notice period. This would allow me to:
- Be available for transition questions
- Avoid any awkward dynamics during a job search
- Ensure [company] gets full value from my notice period

I think garden leave is standard for [departments/levels] here, and 
I believe it serves everyone's interests."
```

## Phase 4: Handling Pushback

### When They Say "Best We Can Do"
```
"I hear that this is your best offer. I'd like to understand better — 
is this a firm policy, or is there flexibility on any component?

I'm not looking to create an adversarial situation. I'm simply trying 
to reach an agreement that feels fair. If [company] has flexibility 
on [benefit X] but not [Y], I'd like to know that so we can work 
toward a solution."
```

### When They Pressure for Immediate Decision
```
"I appreciate you moving this along quickly. This is an important 
decision and I'd like to take [24 hours / until tomorrow] to 
review the details and make sure I'm making the right choice.

I'm not looking to renegotiate — I just want to make sure I fully 
understand everything before I sign."
```

### When They Threaten Negative Reference
```
"I'm surprised to hear that. I've always aimed to maintain a positive 
professional relationship, and I was hopeful we could part on good terms.

I'm committed to a smooth transition and positive relationship. 
I'd prefer to resolve any concerns directly. If there are specific 
issues, I'm happy to discuss them."
```

## Phase 5: Finalizing Agreement

### Email Follow-Up to Verbal Agreement
```
Subject: Departure Terms — Confirmation

Hi [HR contact],

Thank you for our conversation today. Per our discussion, I'd like 
to confirm the agreed terms:

1. Separation date: [date]
2. Severance: [X weeks/months of salary]
3. Equity: [acceleration details / exercise window]
4. Benefits: [COBRA details]
5. 竞业限制: [released / scope confirmed]
6. Reference: [positive/neutral from X]
7. Garden leave: [yes/no, dates]

Please send the formal separation agreement to [email address]. 
I'll have my attorney review it and expect to sign within [X business days].

Thank you for making this transition smooth."
```

### Getting Reference in Writing
```
"[Manager name] has agreed to serve as a reference. Could you please 
confirm this in writing as part of the separation agreement?

Specifically: 'The Company agrees that [Manager name] will provide 
[Employee name] with a positive professional reference if contacted 
by prospective employers.'"
```

## Advanced Tactics

### Anchoring High
Always make the first offer in your favor (or let them anchor first and then counter high):
```
"I'm not sure what [company] has in mind, but based on my research, 
I believe a package of [X] would be appropriate. Here's why..."
```

### Trading Concessions
```
"I'm flexible on [low-value item, e.g., exact end date]. In exchange, 
I'd ask that we finalize agreement on [high-value item, e.g., equity acceleration]."
```

### Walking Away (when appropriate)
```
"I've given this considerable thought, and I'm not comfortable with 
these terms. I think it makes sense for us to part ways without a 
separation agreement. I'll provide [required notice period] and 
can be available for questions during that time.

I'd prefer to reach an agreement, but I'm not willing to accept 
terms that don't reflect my contributions. Let me know if anything 
changes on your end."
```

### Involving an Attorney (seamlessly)
```
"Before I proceed, I'd like to have my attorney review the separation 
agreement. This is standard practice for me, not a reflection of 
any concern. I'll turn around the review within [X business days]."
```
