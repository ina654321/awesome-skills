# Non-Compete (竞业限制) Enforceability Map

> State-by-state reference for non-compete enforceability and negotiation

## Enforceability Overview

| State | Enforceability | Key Laws / Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| **California** | **VOID** | Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code §16600; SB 699 (2023) + AB 1076 (2023): Cannot enforce any non-compete, including out-of-state |
| **North Dakota** | **VOID** | N.D. Cent. Code §9-08-06 |
| **Oklahoma** | **VOID** | 15 Okl. St. §217; only enforceable if selling business |
| **Minnesota** | **VOID** | Effective 7/1/2023; retroactively voids existing |
| **Colorado** | **Limited** | C.R.S. §8-2-113; only enforceable if reasonably necessary to protect trade secrets + employer interest |
| **Illinois** | **Limited** | Requires showing of adequate consideration + employer interest |
| **Massachusetts** | **Limited** | M.G.L. c.149 s.24L; only enforceable for founders/sellers |
| **New York** | **Moderate** | Reasonable scope, duration, geography enforceable |
| **Texas** | **Broad** | Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §15.50; rebuttable presumption of validity |
| **Florida** | **Broad** | §542.335; employer must show legitimate business interest |
| **Washington** | **Moderate** | RCW 49.62; restricted by statute; courts can modify (blue pencil) |
| **Nevada** | **Moderate** | NRS 613.200; enforceable if employer can show legitimate business interest |
| **Oregon** | **Limited** | ORS 653.295; employer must provide notice + pay during restriction |
| **Hawaii** | **Limited** | HRS §480-4; only for trade secrets |
| **Virginia** | **Moderate** | Must be reasonable in scope, duration, geography |

## Federal Status (2024-2025)

- **FTC Proposed Rule (April 2024)**: Would ban virtually all non-competes for workers nationwide
- **Status**: Rule challenged in courts; injunction issued by federal court in Texas (Aug 2024); Supreme Court case uncertain
- **Practical impact**: Some employers suspended new non-competes while awaiting final ruling; existing non-competes remain enforceable under state law

**Recommendation**: Monitor federal developments but plan based on state law currently in effect.

## Enforceability Factors

### Time Duration
| Duration | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| 6 months or less | Usually reasonable |
| 6–12 months | Reasonable in many states |
| 12–24 months | May be enforceable with strong justification |
| 24+ months | Disfavored; likely unenforceable in most states |

### Geographic Scope
| Scope | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|
| National | Usually too broad unless truly nationwide business |
| Regional (specific states) | More defensible if business is truly regional |
| Metropolitan area | Usually reasonable for service businesses |
| Customer-based | Enforceable if specific customers and relationship |
| "Where employer does business" | Courts often narrow to actual territory |

### Scope of Activity
| Scope | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|
| Exact job title / role | Most defensible |
| Similar roles at competitors | Moderate |
| Any role in industry | Too broad; likely unenforceable |
| Industry-agnostic | Rarely enforceable |

## Blue Pencil Doctrine

**States that modify (blue pencil) rather than void overly broad agreements:**
- Texas
- Florida
- Virginia
- Georgia
- Washington
- Illinois
- New York

**States that void entirely if any part is unenforceable:**
- California
- Arizona
- Oklahoma

## Trade Secret Specifics

**Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA - Federal, 2016):**
- Applies nationwide regardless of non-compete enforceability
- Covers actual trade secrets (not general skills/knowledge)
- Non-disclosure agreements (NDA) remain enforceable even in states that void non-competes

**Uniform Trade Secrets Act (UTSA - State):**
- Most states have adopted some version
- Applies to misappropriation of trade secrets
- Can pursue damages even without non-compete

## Negotiation Strategies

### At Exit: Getting Released

**Approach 1: Direct Request**
"I'd like to propose that the 竞业限制 not be activated. To ensure a clean break and avoid any potential conflict, I suggest we proceed without restrictions."

**Approach 2: Scope Narrowing**
"I'm joining [Company X] in a [role Y] capacity. This is clearly in the [A] market, not the [B] market where [your company] operates. Can we confirm in writing this falls outside the scope?"

**Approach 3: Buyout**
"I'm willing to accept the 竞业限制 in exchange for [X] payment. My understanding is that 50% of base salary for the restricted period is standard."

**Approach 4: New Employer Contribution**
"[New employer] has agreed to [contribute to buyout / accept garden leave / other consideration] to facilitate my transition. Could [current employer] match or complement this?"

### If You Cannot Get Released

**Compliance checklist:**
- Read exact language of restriction
- Know your geographic scope
- Know your prohibited activities
- Know the time period
- Consult attorney about specific new role

**Gray area handling:**
- If new role is adjacent but not directly competitive, get written confirmation
- Document everything you do to comply
- If uncertain about new role, get legal opinion before starting

### Legal Challenges

**Grounds to challenge:**
- Unreasonable duration (time)
- Unreasonable geography (place)
- Overly broad scope (activity)
- Inadequate consideration (nothing received for signing)
- No legitimate business interest
- Changed circumstances (company was sold, different business)

**Process:**
1. Consult employment attorney (many offer free initial consultation)
2. Do NOT ignore the restriction while challenging it
3. Seek declaratory judgment or file in appropriate court
4. Consider arbitration if agreement requires it
