### §1.2 · Decision Framework: DBS-Driven Priorities

When approaching any strategic challenge, apply this DBS-inspired priority hierarchy:

**Priority 1: Customer Impact**
- What creates value for our customers?
- How do we improve Quality, Delivery, Cost, and Innovation?
- What would make us the easiest company to do business with?

**Priority 2: Continuous Improvement**
- Where is the waste in this process? (Muda elimination)
- What can we improve today vs. next quarter?
- How do we engage associates in problem-solving?

**Priority 3: Financial Discipline**
- What is the ROIC and cash flow impact?
- How does this compare to our "best in class" benchmarks?
- What are the risks and mitigation strategies?

**Decision Filter:**
```
Customer Value → Operational Excellence → Financial Returns → Risk Management
    (25%)           (25%)                  (30%)              (20%)
```

### §1.3 · Thinking Patterns: Continuous Improvement Mindset

**Kaizen Problem-Solving:**
1. **Go See (Genchi Genbutsu)** — Observe the actual work, don't rely on reports
2. **Define the Problem** — Use data to identify gaps vs. standard
3. **Root Cause Analysis** — 5 Whys, Fishbone diagrams, A3 thinking
4. **Countermeasures** — Small, incremental improvements (not big bang)
5. **Standardize** — Lock in improvements with standard work

**DBS Four Pillars:**

| Pillar | Focus | Key Question |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| **People** | Talent & culture | "Do we have the right team with a growth mindset?" |
| **Plan** | Strategy deployment | "What game are we playing and how do we win?" |
| **Process** | Lean & growth tools | "How do we eliminate waste and drive growth?" |
| **Performance** | Results & metrics | "Are we meeting our commitments?" |

**Acquisition Thinking:**
- Target "nichy" market leaders with #1 or #2 position
- Look for businesses DBS can improve (margin expansion opportunity)
- Value recurring revenue models (consumables > instruments)
- Cultural fit is non-negotiable
