| EBITDA Margin | 18% | 25% | +700 bps |

| OTD | 88% | 96% | +8pp |

**Strategic Fit Analysis:**
- ✓ #2 position in growing niche (cells rule)
- ✓ 70% recurring revenue (consumables)
- ✓ Synergies with Cytiva bioprocessing
- ✓ Margin expansion opportunity
- ⚠ Integration complexity (overseas facilities)

**Danaher Recommendation:**
> Pursue acquisition at $2.0-2.2B (5-5.5x revenue). Post-DBS, expect 25% EBITDA margins within 36 months. Integrate into Biotechnology segment. Deploy DBS Office within 30 days of close. Target $50M+ annual synergies through procurement, manufacturing efficiency, and cross-selling.

### Example 2: Operational Excellence & Margin Expansion

**Situation:**
Beckman Coulter Diagnostics operating company has seen EBITDA margins decline from 28% to 22% over 3 years due to supply chain inefficiencies and quality issues.

**Danaher DBS Analysis:**

**Current State Value Stream Map:**
```
Supplier → Receive → Inspect → Store → Pick → Assemble → Test → Ship → Customer
   14d      3d       2d       12d      1d      2d        3d      2d      = 39 days
   [Waste: Inspection, Storage, Waiting]
```

**Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys):**
1. Why long lead times? → Excess inventory and waiting
2. Why excess inventory? → Unreliable supplier delivery
3. Why unreliable delivery? → No supplier scorecards or KPIs
4. Why no KPIs? → No standardized supplier management process
5. Why no process? → DBS supplier management not deployed

**Countermeasures:**

**Phase 1: Quick Wins (Months 1-3)**
- Deploy daily management boards in receiving
- Implement visual replenishment system (Kanban)
- Negotiate VMI (Vendor Managed Inventory) with top 10 suppliers
- Target: $8M inventory reduction, 5% OTD improvement

**Phase 2: Process Redesign (Months 4-9)**
- Kaizen event: Receiving process (eliminate inspection bottleneck)
- Implement supplier scorecards with monthly business reviews
- Consolidate supplier base (120 → 80 suppliers)
- Target: Additional $12M savings, 15% productivity gain

**Phase 3: Sustain & Standardize (Months 10-12)**
- Document standard work for all processes
- Train all associates in problem-solving
- Establish monthly performance reviews
- Target: Lock in gains, continuous improvement culture

**Danaher Recommendation:**
> Launch DBS transformation targeting 600+ bps margin recovery over 18 months. Deploy DBS Office resources full-time for 6 months. Focus on supply chain optimization and quality at source. Target: Return to 28% EBITDA margins with improved customer OTD.

### Example 4: New Market Entry Strategy

**Situation:**
Danaher considering entry into AI-powered drug discovery tools market. Market size: $1.2B, growing at 35% CAGR. Dominated by tech-native players.

**Danaher Analysis:**

**Market Assessment:**
| Competitor | Approach | Strength | Weakness |
|------------|----------|----------|----------|
| Schrödinger | Physics-based modeling | Scientific depth | Limited customer base |
| Recursion | Biology-first AI | Data scale | Not tool-focused |
| Atomwise | Small molecule AI | Early mover | Narrow application |
| Insilico Medicine | End-to-end AI | Platform breadth | Regulatory unproven |

**Danaher Positioning Options:**

| Option | Approach | Investment | Timeline | Success Probability |
|--------|----------|------------|----------|---------------------|
| A: Build internal | Hire AI team, develop platform | $200M | 3-4 years | 30% |
| B: Acquire startup | Buy emerging AI company | $500M-1B | 1-2 years | 45% |
| C: Partnership | Joint development with AI firm | $50M | 1 year | 60% |
| D: Adjacent play | AI-enhancement of existing tools | $30M | 6-12 months | 70% |

**Danaher Recommendation:**
> Pursue **Option D (Adjacent)** initially, followed by **Option C (Partnership)**:
> 1. Launch AI-enhancement of existing SCIEX and Leica products ($30M, 12 months)
> 2. Establish strategic partnership with leading AI drug discovery firm ($50M, 18 months)
> 3. Monitor for acquisition opportunities as market matures
>
> Rationale: Leverage existing customer relationships, test market receptivity, build AI capabilities organically before large capital deployment.
