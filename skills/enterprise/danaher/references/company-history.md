# Danaher Corporation - Comprehensive History

## Founding Era (1984-1999)

### 1984: The Beginning
- **Founders:** Steven Rales and Mitchell Rales
- **Origin:** Real estate investment firm named after a Montana stream
- **Initial Focus:** Cash flow generating manufacturing businesses
- **First Acquisition (1986):** Mojack Industries (tool manufacturer)

### The Birth of DBS (1988)
- A Danaher subsidiary facing intense competition adopted lean manufacturing principles
- Based on Toyota Production System (TPS)
- Resulted in dramatic improvement in quality, delivery, and cost
- Became the foundation of Danaher Business System (DBS)

### Key Early Acquisitions

| Year | Company | Industry | Significance |
|------|---------|----------|--------------|
| 1986 | Mojack Industries | Tools | First acquisition |
| 1988 | Chicago Pneumatic | Tools | Power tools entry |
| 1990s | Various | Industrial | Built industrial portfolio |

### 1990s Growth
- Grew from 16 operating companies to 51 operating companies
- Revenue primarily from industrial tools, motion control, environmental
- 86% of revenue from tires, rubber goods, environmental, electronic test, motion control

## Transformation Era (2000-2015)

### Entering Life Sciences (2005)

**2005: Leica Microsystems Acquisition**
- **Price:** $225 million
- **Significance:** Entry into life sciences microscopy
- **Legacy:** Foundation of current Life Sciences segment

### Diagnostic Entry (2004)

**2004: Radiometer Acquisition**
- **Price:** ~$600 million
- **Significance:** Entry into acute care diagnostics
- **Technology:** Blood gas and immunoassay testing
- **Legacy:** Still part of Diagnostics segment today

### The Beckman Coulter Deal (2011)

**Transaction Details:**
- **Price:** $6.8 billion (including debt)
- **Type:** Hostile takeover that became friendly
- **Significance:** Largest deal in Danaher history at that time
- **Business:** Clinical diagnostics, laboratory automation
- **Revenue:** ~$3.7 billion
- **Legacy:** Cornerstone of Diagnostics segment

**Integration Success:**
- Applied DBS to underperforming assets
- Improved margins significantly
- Demonstrated DBS power on large-scale acquisition

### Pall Corporation (2015)

**Transaction Details:**
- **Price:** $13.8 billion
- **Type:** Friendly acquisition
- **Significance:** Entry into filtration/separation
- **Business:** Life sciences and industrial filtration
- **Legacy:** Core of Biotechnology segment

## Modern Era (2016-Present)

### Cepheid Acquisition (2016)

**Transaction Details:**
- **Price:** $4.0 billion
- **Timing:** Pre-COVID (fortunate timing)
- **Business:** Molecular diagnostics, PCR testing systems
- **Technology:** GeneXpert platform

**COVID Impact:**
- Cepheid's PCR tests for COVID-19 became critical
- Revenue surged from ~$700M to ~$2B+
- Demonstrated value of rapid molecular diagnostics
- Post-COVID normalization created revenue headwinds

### The Dental Spin-off: Envista (2019)

**Transaction Details:**
- **Form:** Tax-free spin-off
- **Revenue:** $2.9 billion
- **Business:** Dental equipment and consumables
- **Brands:** Nobel Biocare, KaVo, Kerr, Ormco

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Focus Danaher on life sciences and diagnostics
- Allow dental business focused capital allocation
- Created pure-play dental company (NYSE: NVST)

### The Cytiva Mega-Deal (2020)

**Transaction Details:**
- **Price:** $21.4 billion
- **Asset:** GE Biopharma (formerly GE Healthcare Life Sciences)
- **Type:** Carve-out from General Electric
- **Significance:** Largest deal in Danaher history

**Business Acquired:**
- Chromatography systems and resins
- Single-use bioprocessing technologies
- Cell culture media
- Filtration products

**Renaming:**
- GE Biopharma → Cytiva
- Became core of Biotechnology segment

**Strategic Impact:**
- Transformed Danaher into bioprocessing leader
- Exposure to high-growth biologics market
- Created $7B+ revenue segment

### Aldevron (2021)

**Transaction Details:**
- **Price:** $9.6 billion
- **Business:** Genomic medicine manufacturing
- **Products:** Plasmid DNA, mRNA, proteins
- **Founders:** Michael Chambers and John Ballantyne

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Expansion into gene and cell therapy
- COVID mRNA vaccine boom highlighted opportunity
- End-to-end genomic medicine capabilities

### Abcam (2023)

**Transaction Details:**
- **Price:** $5.7 billion
- **Business:** Research antibodies and reagents
- **Market:** Life sciences research tools
- **Founder:** Jonathan Milner

**Strategic Fit:**
- Complements existing life sciences portfolio
- Strong digital channel presence
- Recurring revenue from research consumables

### The Veralto Spin-off (2023)

**Transaction Details:**
- **Form:** Tax-free spin-off
- **Revenue:** ~$5 billion
- **Business:** Environmental and applied solutions
- **Brands:** Hach, ChemTreat, Trojan, McCrometer

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Final step in portfolio transformation
- Focus exclusively on life sciences and diagnostics
- Create pure-play environmental company (NYSE: VLTO)
