# CVS Health Strategic Initiatives

## Overview

CVS Health is executing a multi-year transformation to evolve from a retail pharmacy company to a comprehensive, integrated healthcare platform. This document details the major strategic initiatives driving this transformation.

---

## 1. CostVantage & TruePrice

### Initiative Summary
**Transparent pharmacy reimbursement model addressing PBM pricing scrutiny**

### Background
Traditional PBM pricing has faced criticism for:
- Opaque rebate arrangements
- Spread pricing (difference between what PBMs pay pharmacies and charge plans)
- Patient out-of-pocket costs based on inflated list prices
- Regulatory pressure: 20+ states enacted PBM reforms (2024)

### CostVantage Model

| Component | Traditional Model | CostVantage Model |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Drug Cost** | List price minus rebate (opaque) | Actual acquisition cost (transparent) |
| **Markup** | Spread (undisclosed) | Set percentage markup (disclosed) |
| **Professional Fee** | Bundled/unseen | Explicit dispensing/service fee |
| **Patient Cost** | Based on list price | Based on actual cost + markup |
| **Plan Sponsor** | Rebate-based economics | Cost-plus transparency |

### TruePrice Component
- Real-time price transparency for patients
- Cost estimates before prescription fill
- Comparison shopping within network

### Rollout Timeline
| Phase | Timeline | Scope |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Pilot | 2024 | Select commercial clients |
| Commercial Launch | 2025 | Caremark commercial book |
| Expansion | 2026+ | Medicare, Medicaid evaluation |

### Strategic Benefits

**For Patients**:
- Lower out-of-pocket costs (especially coinsurance)
- Price transparency at point of care
- Predictable prescription costs

**For Plan Sponsors**:
- Simplified auditing and reconciliation
- Predictable pharmacy spend
- Reduced PBM skepticism

**For CVS**:
- Regulatory risk mitigation
- Competitive differentiation
- Sustainable long-term economics

### Financial Impact
- Near-term: Potential margin compression on spread
- Long-term: Volume growth, adherence improvement, client retention

## 2. Cordavis Biosimilar Strategy

### Initiative Summary
**Private-label biosimilar platform to address specialty drug cost crisis**

### Market Context
- Biologics: 46% of drug spend, treating 1-3% of patients
- Biosimilar development: $100-250M, 5-10 years
- Potential U.S. savings: $133 billion over 10 years

### Cordavis Business Model

```
Manufacturer Partnership → Co-Development/Commercialization → CVS Branding → Caremark Formulary Preference
```

### Product Portfolio

| Biosimilar | Reference Drug | Manufacturer Partner | List Price Discount | Status |
|------------|---------------|---------------------|---------------------|--------|
| Hyrimoz (adalimumab-adaz) | Humira | Sandoz | 80-81% | Launched 2023 |
| Hyrimoz Citrate-free | Humira Citrate-free | Sandoz | 80% | Launched 2024 |
| Stelara biosimilar | Stelara | TBD | TBD | Launching 2025 |
| Additional pipeline | Various | Various | TBD | Development |

### Competitive Strategy

**Differentiation**:
- Private-label branding (vs. generic manufacturer branding)
- Manufacturing oversight and quality assurance
- Preferential formulary placement in Caremark
- Aggressive pricing to drive adoption

### Adoption Metrics (2024)
| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Humira Biosimilar Market Share | 28% total |
| Cordavis Hyrimoz Share | 18% (leading individual biosimilar) |
| Caremark Conversion Rate | 97% to preferred biosimilar |
| Patient Savings | 80%+ on coinsurance |

### Financial Impact
- **Client Savings**: $4.3B projected (2025 formulary)
- **Member Savings**: $133 average per member
- **CVS Economics**: Margin capture through Cordavis ownership

### Future Pipeline
- **2025**: Stelara (ustekinumab) biosimilar launch
- **2026+**: Multiple biosimilars for high-spend biologics
- **Strategy**: Target drugs with >$5B annual U.S. sales

## 4. Medicare Advantage Turnaround

### Challenge Summary
Aetna Medicare Advantage star ratings declined for 2024 payment year, reducing quality bonus payments by hundreds of millions.

### Root Cause Analysis
| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Member Experience (CAHPS) | Below benchmark |
| Medication Adherence | Declining rates |
| Care Coordination | Gaps between Aetna and delivery assets |
| Prior Authorization | Friction impacting satisfaction |
| Utilization | Higher-than-expected medical costs |

### Recovery Plan

#### Phase 1: Immediate Stabilization (2024-2025)
| Initiative | Investment | Expected Impact |
|------------|------------|-----------------|
| CAHPS Improvement Program | $50M | +0.5 star member experience |
| Medication Adherence AI | $30M | +5% adherence key measures |
| Care Navigator Deployment | $40M | -15% complaints |
| Prior Auth Automation | $25M | -30% decision time |
| Home Health Touchpoints | $35M | +10% wellness visits |

#### Phase 2: Structural Improvements (2025-2026)
- Integrated care management platform
- Oak Street primary care integration
- Risk stratification leveraging all data sources
- Value-based provider contracting expansion

### Star Rating Targets
| Year | Target | Current |
|------|--------|---------|
| 2024 Payment Year | N/A | Declined |
| 2025 Payment Year | 3.5+ average | TBD |
| 2026 Payment Year | 4.0+ average | Target |

### Financial Impact
- Each 0.5 star improvement = ~$150M annual quality bonus
- Investment: $180M over 18 months
- Expected return: $300M+ annually

## 5. Retail Health Transformation

### Initiative Summary
**Evolve CVS stores from transactional pharmacy to comprehensive health destinations**

### HealthHub Concept

#### Traditional CVS vs. HealthHub
| Aspect | Traditional | HealthHub |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Pharmacy** | Counter service | Clinical consultation |
| **Products** | General merchandise | Health & wellness focus |
| **Services** | Prescription fill | Chronic disease management |
| **Experience** | Transactional | Relationship-based |
| **Staff** | Pharmacy techs | Health advisors, care coaches |

#### HealthHub Services
- Chronic disease management (diabetes, hypertension, asthma)
- Behavioral health services (teletherapy, counseling)
- Health screenings (diabetes, cholesterol, depression)
- Care coordination and navigation
- Wellness programs and coaching

### Store Rationalization

#### Closure Plan
- **Announced**: 2021 (3-year plan)
- **Target**: ~900 stores (10% of fleet)
- **Criteria**: Underperformance, overlap, market changes
- **Progress**: ~300 closed by end of 2024

#### Reinvestment Strategy

|-----|------------|

| Staff Training | $200M |
