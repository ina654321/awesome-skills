## § 7 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: New Product Development

**User**: "We want to develop a new storage solution. What's the MUJI approach?"

**Expected Response**:
```
**MUJI Storage Product Development Framework**

**Design Brief:**
- Problem: People need flexible, affordable storage that adapts to different spaces
- Target: Urban dwellers, small apartments, rental living
- Price: ¥3,000-15,000 ($20-100)

**MUJI Design Process:**

**Phase 1: Function Analysis (4 weeks)**
- Observe storage behaviors in homes
- Identify pain points: flexibility, durability, aesthetics
- Study existing solutions (competitors, traditional)

**Phase 2: Material Selection (2 weeks)**
- Primary: Steel (recyclable, durable)
- Finish: Powder coat (no VOCs, durable)
- Alternative: Wood (sustainable source)
- Decision: Steel frame + wood shelves combination

**Phase 3: Rational Design (6 weeks)**
- Modular: Stackable, expandable units
- Adjustable: Shelf heights at 5cm intervals
- Honest: Visible fasteners, no fake wood grain
- Minimal: No decorative elements

**Phase 4: Environmental Review (2 weeks)**
- Recyclability: 95% recyclable materials
- Packaging: Corrugated cardboard, no plastic
- Transport: Flat-pack for efficiency
- End-of-life: Take-back program consideration

**Launch Strategy:**
- MUJI flagship stores: Interactive display
- Online: AR visualization for space planning
- Price: ¥5,000-12,000 (competitive with IKEA)
- Positioning: "Storage that grows with you"

**Success Metrics:**
- Sell-through: 70% in first 6 months
- Return rate: <3%
- Customer satisfaction: 4.5/5+
```

### Scenario 2: China Market Strategy

**User**: "How should we address the challenges in our China business?"

**Expected Response**:
```
**MUJI China Market Strategy**

**Current Situation:**
- 300+ stores in China
- Revenue: ¥195B segment
- Challenge: Local competition, consumer slowdown

**Strategic Response:**

**1. Product Localization**
- Increase local design: 70% of household goods already local
- Expand Health & Beauty: Made-in-Japan skincare driving growth
- Climate adaptation: Cooling fabrics, humid weather solutions
- Size localization: Fits for Chinese body types

**2. Pricing Strategy**
- "Appropriate price" messaging: Quality at fair price
- Entry-level line: MUJI GIFT (lower price point)
- Promotional discipline: Avoid discount culture
- Value communication: Cost-per-use, durability

**3. Omni-Channel Enhancement**
- Tmall/JD optimization: Better UX, faster delivery
- Mini-programs: WeChat ecosystem integration
- Live commerce: Authentic product demonstrations
- Click-and-collect: Store pickup convenience

**4. Flagship Experience**
- MUJI Hotel Shanghai: Brand immersion
- MUJI Diner: Local food adaptation
- Community events: Workshop series
- Sustainable messaging: Environmental focus resonates

**Competitive Differentiation:**
- vs. Miniso: Quality, durability, philosophy
- vs. Nitori: Design, sustainability, brand story
- vs. Local brands: Japanese quality, global standard

**Investment:**
- Store renovation: ¥5B over 2 years
- Digital: ¥3B platform improvement
- Local design: ¥2B capability building
```

### Scenario 3: Sustainability Initiative

**User**: "We need to set ambitious sustainability targets. What should they be?**

**Expected Response:**
```
**MUJI Sustainability Roadmap**

**Current State (FY2025):**
- Recycled materials: 30% of product line
- Packaging reduction: 25% vs. 2020
- Carbon intensity: Baseline measurement underway
- Supplier compliance: 85% ethical audit pass

**2030 Targets:**

**Products:**
- 100% sustainable materials (recycled, organic, FSC)
- Zero single-use plastic packaging
- 50% carbon reduction (Scope 1&2)
- 30% carbon reduction (Scope 3)

**Operations:**
- 100% renewable energy in stores
- Zero waste to landfill
- Water neutral operations
- Circular product program (take-back)

**Supply Chain:**
- 100% ethical sourcing certification
- Local production: 50% of products
- Supplier carbon reporting mandatory
- Regenerative agriculture partnerships

**Governance:**
- CSRD/ESRS aligned reporting
- Executive compensation linked to ESG
- Board sustainability committee
- Science Based Targets initiative membership

**Consumer Communication:**
- Product labeling: Environmental impact score
- Store displays: Material sourcing stories
- Annual report: Transparent progress
- "No excess" messaging: Anti-consumption
```

### Scenario 5: Large-Format Store Design

**User**: "We're designing a new flagship store. What are the key elements?**

**Expected Response:**
```
**MUJI Flagship Store Design Framework**

**Store Concept: "A Place to Discover"**

**Zoning Strategy:**

**1. Welcome Zone (10%)**
- Open entrance, no barriers
- Seasonal display, new arrivals
- Information desk, store guide
- Rest area, simple benches

**2. Product Zones (60%)**
- Apparel: Organized by function (work, relax, sleep)
- Household: Room vignettes (kitchen, bath, storage)
- Health & Beauty: Testers, ingredient transparency
- Food: Seasonal, local specialties
- Stationery: Writing, organizing, creative

**3. Experience Zones (20%)**
- MUJI Books: Curated selection, reading space
- MUJI Café: Simple food, coffee
- Workshop Space: DIY classes, mending
- Custom Order: Furniture, curtains

**4. Community Zone (10%)**
- Event space: Local workshops
- Information: Sustainability, local initiatives
- Feedback: Guest voice, suggestions

**Design Principles:**
- Materials: Unfinished wood, concrete, steel
- Lighting: Natural where possible, warm artificial
- Sound: Quiet, no background music
- Scent: Neutral, no artificial fragrance
- Signage: Minimal, functional, bilingual

**Sustainability Features:**
- Energy: LED lighting, natural ventilation
- Materials: Recycled content, local sourcing
- Operations: Zero waste, recycling stations
- Education: Environmental information displays

**Flagship Examples:**
- Tokyo Yurakucho: Original flagship, 3 floors
- Shanghai Huaihai: China flagship, MUJI Hotel
- New York Fifth Avenue: US flagship, global assortment
- Bangkok One: SE Asia flagship, largest format
```
