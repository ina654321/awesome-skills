# Atlassian Company History

## Timeline of Key Events

### Founding Era (2002-2004)

**2002: The Beginning**
- Mike Cannon-Brookes and Scott Farquhar meet at UNSW
- Found Atlassian with $10,000 credit card debt
- Launch Jira 1.0 — a web-based issue tracker
- Revenue hits $1M by year-end
- No sales team — purely product-led growth

**2004: Confluence Launch**
- Release Confluence, a team wiki
- Decision to build second product instead of focusing solely on Jira
- Risky move that paid off — "two rocket engines"

### Growth Era (2005-2014)

**2005-2006:**
- Revenue soars to $14.9M
- Profitable without venture capital

**2007: First Acquisition**
- Acquire Cenqua (Fisheye, Crucible, Clover)
- Strategy: Buy successful tools rather than build from scratch

**2010: Accel Investment**
- $60M secondary investment from Accel
- Provides liquidity to founders/employees
- First institutional board representation
- Still no dilution of founder control

**2012:**
- Launch Stash (later Bitbucket Server)
- Enter Git hosting market

### Public Company Era (2015-Present)

**2015: IPO**
- List on NASDAQ under ticker TEAM
- Priced at $21/share
- Valuation ~$4-5B
- 1,600+ employees
- Dual-class share structure preserves founder control

**2017: Trello Acquisition**
- Acquire Trello for $425M
- Expand from developer tools to general business teams
- Visual kanban for everyone

**2018: Slack Partnership**
- Sunset HipChat and Stride
- Strategic investment in Slack
- Focus on core collaboration strengths

**2019: Enterprise Agile**
- Acquire AgileCraft, rebrand as Jira Align
- Enter enterprise agile portfolio management
- Compete at scale with Planview, Clarity

**2020: Team Anywhere**
- Launch distributed work policy
- Pandemic accelerates permanent remote-first culture
- Invest in async collaboration tools

**2021: Cloud Acceleration**
- End Server license sales
- Accelerate cloud migration programs
- Launch Atlassian Ventures ($50M fund)

**2022: Reliability Investment**
- Major cloud outage affects ~400 customers
- Significant investment in reliability engineering
- Revamped incident response and change management

**2023-2024: AI Era**
- Launch Atlassian Intelligence (AI features)
- Acquire Loom for $975M
- Launch Rovo AI assistant
- Scott Farquhar steps down as Co-CEO
- Mike Cannon-Brookes becomes sole CEO

## Leadership Evolution

### Co-CEO Era (2002-2024)
Mike Cannon-Brookes and Scott Farquhar shared leadership for 22 years.

**Division of Responsibilities:**
- Mike: Product, engineering, long-term vision
- Scott: Operations, finance, go-to-market

**August 2024 Transition:**
- Scott Farquhar steps down as Co-CEO
- Remains Co-Chair and Director
- Mike Cannon-Brookes becomes sole CEO
- Continues dual-class share structure

## Business Model Evolution

### Early Years: Self-Serve
- No sales team
- Transparent online pricing
- Credit card signups
- Product-led growth

### Expansion: Inside Sales
- Enterprise customers need assistance
- Customer success focus
- Solution engineering
- Still minimal traditional sales

### Modern: Platform Strategy
- Cloud-first subscription
- Marketplace revenue share
- Premium tiers
- Enterprise add-ons

## Product Strategy Shifts

### 2002-2010: Developer Tools
- Focus on Jira, Confluence, developer needs
- Issue tracking, wiki, code review
- Build for developers first

### 2010-2017: DevOps Expansion
- Bitbucket for Git hosting
- Bamboo for CI/CD
- HipChat for team chat
- DevOps toolchain vision

### 2017-2022: Broad Collaboration
- Trello for business teams
- Jira Service Management for ITSM
- Jira Work Management for non-technical teams
- Expand beyond developers

### 2022-Present: AI-Powered Platform
- Atlassian Intelligence
- Loom integration
- Rovo AI agent
- Platform consolidation
