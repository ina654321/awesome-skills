## 10. Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

### 🔴 High Severity

**Anti-Pattern 1: Positioning by Committee

```
❌ BAD: CMO runs a positioning workshop with 15 people from Product, Sales,
   Engineering, and CS. After 3 months, the output is a 47-word positioning
   statement that satisfies everyone internally but resonates with no one externally.
   Result: Every team continues using their own messaging. No market clarity.

✅ GOOD: CMO owns positioning. Input is gathered broadly (20+ customer interviews,
   win/loss analysis, competitive research), but the decision is made by one person.
   Then it's tested with 10 target customers for resonance and uniqueness.
   Only when customers can repeat the differentiation in their own words does it ship.
```

**Anti-Pattern 2: Campaign Before PMF

```
❌ BAD: Startup raises Series A. CMO runs $2M awareness campaign before trial-to-paid
   is measured. Thousands of trials. 2% trial-to-paid. $2M creates awareness for
   a product that doesn't convert. Money spent on demand for a leaky bucket.

✅ GOOD: Sequence correctly:
   Step 1: Validate trial-to-paid > industry baseline (typically 15-25% for SaaS)
   Step 2: Validate payback period < 12 months
   Step 3: Only then invest in demand generation at scale
   Rule: Performance marketing before brand marketing. Fix the bucket before filling it.
```

### 🟡 Medium Severity

**Anti-Pattern 3: Feature-led Messaging

```
❌ BAD: "We have AI-powered workflow automation with 200+ integrations and
   real-time dashboards with enterprise-grade security and SOC2 compliance."
   → Customer can't identify what outcome they'll get.

✅ GOOD: Lead with outcome, then feature:
   "Finance teams close month-end in 2 days instead of 2 weeks — using our
   AI-powered automation (200+ integrations, SOC2 certified)."
   Rule: Customer buys outcomes, not features. Feature list is proof, not the claim.
```

**Anti-Pattern 4: Marketing-Sales Finger-Pointing

```
❌ BAD: Marketing says "We generated 500 MQLs, sales didn't follow up."
   Sales says "Marketing MQLs are garbage, we ignore them."
   Result: 6 months, no pipeline growth, $500K in marketing spend wasted.

✅ GOOD: Establish shared MQL definition agreed by both Marketing and Sales:
   - ICP firmographic match (company size, industry, title)
   - Behavioral score (demo request, pricing page visit, key content download)
   Review win/loss together monthly; adjust MQL definition based on close rate data.
   When Marketing and Sales share pipeline as a metric, alignment follows.
```
