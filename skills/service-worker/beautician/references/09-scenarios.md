# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Allergic Reaction

**User:** "During a facial, the client starts breaking out in hives and says it feels itchy. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Allergic Reaction Protocol:**
>
> | Severity | Symptoms | Response |
> |----------|---------|----------|
> | **Mild** | Itching, mild redness | Remove product, apply cold compress |
> | **Moderate** | Hives, swelling | Remove product, antihistamine, monitor |
> | **Severe** | Difficulty breathing, anaphylaxis | Call 911 immediately |
>
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 1. **Stop the service** — remove all products from skin
> 2. **Assess severity** — breathing, throat swelling
> 3. **If severe** — call 911, don't try to move client
> 4. **If mild/moderate** — apply cold compress, give antihistamine
> 5. **Document** — what was applied, when reaction started
> 6. **Follow up** — call client later to check
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Always do patch test for new products
> - Review health history at every visit
> - Know client's product history
> - Ask about recent dental work, medications (can affect skin)

## 9.2 Client with Contagious Condition

**User:** "A client arrives for a facial and I notice she has cold sores on her lips. Can I still service her?"

**Expert:**
> **Contagious Condition Assessment:**
>
> | Condition | Serviceable? | Protocol |
> |-----------|-------------|----------|
> | **Active cold sores (HSV-1)** | No — contraindicated | Reschedule in 2+ weeks |
> | **Pink eye** | No | Reschedule until cleared |
> | **Impetigo** | No | Reschedule, referral to doctor |
> | **Ringworm** | Depends | Consult with doctor, treat area only |
> | **Warts** | Caution | Do not treat over warts |
> | **Acne** | Yes | Avoid affected areas |
>
> **Cold Sores Specifically:**
> - Herpes simplex virus (HSV-1) — very contagious
> - Never perform facial, lip waxing, or lip treatments
> - Could spread to other clients or yourself
> - Even areas around mouth should be avoided
> - Be discreet: "I'd love to help you today, but I'm not able to perform this service with the current condition. Let me schedule you for..."
>
> **If You Accidentally Treat Someone:**
> - Stop service immediately
> - Disinfect all equipment used
> - Document what happened
> - You may need to be tested

## 9.3 Product Expired or Contaminated

**User:** "I found a bottle of facial serum that's been sitting in the back bar for months. The label is peeling. Should I still use it?"

**Expert:**
> **Product Safety Decision:**
>
> | Sign | Action |
> |------|--------|
> | **Expired** | Discard immediately |
> | **Changed color/smell** | Discard immediately |
> | **Separated/chunky** | Discard immediately |
> | **Label peeling** | Check lot number, call manufacturer |
> | **Contamination suspected** | Discard immediately |
>
> **Storage Standards:**
> - Products should be stored at room temperature
> - Keep away from direct sunlight
> - Tightly sealed when not in use
> - Use spatulas, not fingers (double-dipping)
> - Mark open dates on bottles
> - FIFO (First In, First Out) rotation
>
> **Legal:**
> - Using expired products can cause injury
> - Client can sue for damages
> - License can be revoked by state board
> - Document product disposal
