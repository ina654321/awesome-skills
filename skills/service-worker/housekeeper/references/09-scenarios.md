# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Found Items

**User:** "While cleaning a room, I found a large amount of cash in the nightstand drawer, along with what looks like marijuana. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Found Items Protocol:**
>
> | Item | Action |
> |------|--------|
> | **Cash** | Turn in to supervisor, document, log as found property |
> | **Drugs/paraphernalia** | Do NOT touch, notify supervisor immediately |
> | **Weapons** | Do NOT touch, notify security/supervisor |
> | **Valuable jewelry** | Turn in to supervisor immediately |
> | **Documents (IDs, credit cards)** | Turn in, notify supervisor |
>
> **Marijuana Specifically:**
> - Recreational marijuana is legal in many states — but hotel policy varies
> - Do NOT remove it from the room
> - Notify supervisor — let management decide
> - Some hotels allow marijuana use in designated areas only
> - You are not law enforcement — don't involve police unless hotel policy requires it
>
> **Cash Protocol:**
> 1. Document where found (nightstand, drawer 2)
> 2. Never handle alone — get another staff member as witness
> 3. Turn in to supervisor with signed documentation
> 4. Log it in found property record
> 5. If unclaimed, follow state unclaimed property laws
>
> **Never:**
> - Take found items
> - Discuss with other guests
> - Post on social media
> - Use found money for any purpose

## 9.2 Health Emergency in Room

**User:** "I'm cleaning a room and I find a guest unconscious on the floor. The room is a mess and there's blood on the pillow."

**Expert:**
> **Medical Emergency Protocol:**
>
> **Immediate Response:**
> 1. **Don't enter if unsafe** — Assess for danger first
> 2. **Call 911 immediately** — If person is not responsive
> 3. **Call supervisor/security** — Hotel emergency line
> 4. **Don't touch anything** — Preserve scene
> 5. **Clear the area** — Keep other guests away
> 6. **Stay on scene** — Until security/EMS arrives
>
> **If the Person is Breathing but Unconscious:**
> 1. Don't move them — could cause injury
> 2. Call 911
> 3. Check for medical ID (bracelet, wallet card)
> 4. Monitor breathing until help arrives
>
> **Bloodborne Pathogen Note:**
> - Blood can transmit diseases (HIV, Hep B/C)
> - Don't touch blood without gloves
> - If you touched blood without PPE: Report immediately to supervisor
> - Medical evaluation may be recommended
>
> **Do NOT:**
> - Disturb the scene
> - Touch weapons or drugs
> - Discuss with media
> - Clean the room until cleared

## 9.3 Suspected Human Trafficking

**User:** "The 'guest' in this room has paid cash, shows no luggage, and changes rooms frequently. I've seen multiple men come and go at odd hours. Should I report this?"

**Expert:**
> **Human Trafficking Awareness:**
>
> | Red Flag | What It Looks Like |
> |---------|------------------|
> | **Cash payment, no card** | Avoiding documentation |
> | **No luggage** | Not living normally |
> | **Frequent room changes** | Avoiding being tracked |
> | **Multiple visitors** | Different people throughout day/night |
> | **Young appearance** | May be underage |
> | **Controlling companion** | Someone directing their behavior |
> | **Signs of abuse** | Bruises, fearful behavior |
>
> **What to Do:**
> 1. **Do NOT confront** — Don't put yourself or potential victim at risk
> 2. **Report to supervisor** — "I have concerns about activity in room [number]"
> 3. **Document** — Write down what you observed (dates, times, descriptions)
> 4. **Contact authorities** — Call National Human Trafficking Hotline: 1-888-373-7888
> 5. **Preserve privacy** — Don't accuse guests
>
> **Your Role:**
> - You are not an investigator
> - Report your concerns through proper channels
> - Don't alter your service routine (could alert traffickers)
> - Trust your instincts — if something seems wrong, report it
