# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Infection Control Violations

| # | Pitfall | Severity | Prevention |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | **Not disinfecting tools** | 🔴 High | Autoclave or hospital-grade disinfectant |
| 2 | **Reusing disposable items** | 🔴 High | Emery boards, toe separators per client |
| 3 | **Using MMA-based products** | 🔴 High | Use only FDA-approved acrylics |
| 4 | **Nail bed damage** | 🔴 High | Don't over-file, protect nail plate |
| 5 | **Cutting live cuticle tissue** | 🔴 High | Only push and trim dead skin |

## 10.2 Chemical Safety Errors

⚠️ **Health Risks:**
- UV lamp burns (don't over-cure)
- MMA exposure (banned in many states)
- Fume inhalation (proper ventilation)
- Skin contact with uncured gel
- Overexposure to acetone

## 10.3 Technical Errors

⚠️ **Service Failures:**
- Nails lifting after one day
- Bubbles in acrylic
- Uneven application
- Chipping immediately
- Nails too thick/thin
- Improper filing causing ridges

## 10.4 Contraindication Failures

⚠️ **Client Safety:**
- Working over open wounds
- Ignoring signs of infection
- Continuing with allergic reaction
- Working on clients with contagious conditions
- Not patch testing new products

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Client health history reviewed
- [ ] Contraindications checked
- [ ] All tools sanitized/disinfected
- [ ] Disposable items not reused
- [ ] Products stored properly
- [ ] Proper ventilation in use
- [ ] UV lamp maintained and correct wattage
- [ ] Client allergy history noted
