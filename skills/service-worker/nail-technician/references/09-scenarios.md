# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Suspected Infection

**User:** "A client has green discoloration under her acrylic nails. She's been coming to me monthly. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Nail Infection Assessment:**
>
> | Condition | Likely Cause | Action |
> |----------|-------------|--------|
> | **Green (pseudomonas)** | Moisture under nail | Remove acrylic, treat |
> | **Yellow** | Fungal infection | Refer to doctor |
> | **White spots** | Trauma, not infection | Monitor |
> | **Red/purple** | Bruising | Cold compress |
> | **Separation** | Injury or infection | Remove product, see doctor |
>
> **Green Nails (Pseudomonas):**
> 1. **Remove the acrylic immediately** — Don't try to fill over it
> 2. **Don't apply new nails** — Until cleared
> 3. **Recommend doctor visit** — May need oral antibiotics
> 4. **Sanitize your equipment** — Soak in hospital-grade disinfectant
> 5. **Review your practices** — Were tools properly sanitized?
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Don't file into the nail bed
> - Don't lift artificial nails from natural nail
> - Don't use MMA-based products
> - Keep nails dry between services
> - Don't over-file cuticles

## 9.2 Client Has Open Wound

**User:** "A client's thumb has an open cut and she wants a full set. Should I service her?"

**Expert:**
> **Contraindication Assessment:**
>
> **Rule: Do NOT perform services over open wounds, cuts, or damaged skin.**
>
> | Situation | Response |
> |----------|----------|
> | **Small cut on thumb** | Avoid the area, don't work directly on it |
> | **Open wound or sore** | Postpone service until healed |
> | **Fungal infection** | Refer to doctor first |
> | **Cold sores on fingers** | Postpone, don't work — highly contagious |
> | **Skin infection** | Refer to dermatologist, postpone |
>
> **How to Handle:**
> "I'd love to help you get your nails done! I notice you have a cut on your thumb. I recommend we wait until it's healed to apply artificial nails — working over an open wound could trap bacteria and cause an infection. Let's do a manicure avoiding that area for now, and we can schedule your full set when it's healed."
>
> **Why Not:**
> - Artificial products over open skin = bacterial breeding ground
> - Can cause serious infection
> - Could result in serious injury (loss of nail)
> - You'd be liable if infection occurs

## 9.3 Allergic Reaction to Products

**User:** "A client breaks out in hives around her nails after a gel manicure. She's had gel done before with no reaction. What happened?"

**Expert:**
> **Allergic Reaction Protocol:**
>
> | Possible Cause | Analysis |
> |---------------|---------|
> | **New product** | Did you switch gel brands? |
> | **Overexposure** | Uncured gel on skin = allergic reaction |
> | **Filing dust** | Allergen contact with skin |
> | **Lamp sensitivity** | UV exposure reaction |
>
> **Immediate Response:**
> 1. **Remove the gel** — Don't cure further
> 2. **Apply cold compress** — Reduce itching
> 3. **Antihistamine** — Benadryl if available
> 4. **Refer to doctor** — If spreading or severe
> 5. **Document** — What product, when applied, symptoms
>
> **Likely Cause:**
> - Gel wasn't fully cured (lamp too old, wrong wattage)
> - Gel got on skin during application
> - Overfiling caused allergic reaction to natural nail dust
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Always cure fully (check lamp wattage)
> - Don't let gel touch skin
> - Use proper filing technique
> - Patch test new products
> - Keep product off skin
