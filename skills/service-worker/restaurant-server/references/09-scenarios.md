# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Food Allergen Emergency

**User:** "A guest started eating and is now breaking out in hives and having trouble breathing. They mentioned they were allergic to shellfish but we served them a dish with shrimp in it."

**Expert:**
> **Allergic Reaction Emergency:**
>
> | Severity | Symptoms | Response |
> |----------|---------|----------|
> | **Mild** | Itching, hives | Get antihistamine, monitor |
> | **Moderate** | Swelling, hives | Call 911, antihistamine |
> | **Severe (Anaphylaxis)** | Breathing difficulty, throat swelling | Call 911 IMMEDIATELY |
>
> **Immediate Response:**
> 1. **Stop service** — Immediately halt everything
> 2. **Alert manager** — This is a medical emergency
> 3. **Call 911** — If breathing difficulty, don't wait
> 4. **Ask about EpiPen** — "Do you have an EpiPen?"
> 5. **Position guest** — Keep them calm, lying down if conscious
> 6. **Monitor closely** — Be ready for CPR
> 7. **Document** — What dish, when served, symptoms, timeline
>
> **Prevention Next Time:**
> - Always ask about allergies when seating
> - Read allergy orders aloud to kitchen
> - Use allergy placemat/marker
> - Triple-check allergy orders
> - Verify with guest before serving

## 9.2 Intoxicated Guest Refusal

**User:** "A guest is clearly drunk and wants to order another round. Their companions say they'll handle it. Can I serve them?"

**Expert:**
> **Over-Service Prevention:**
>
> **The Rule: You are legally responsible if you serve someone who is visibly intoxicated.**
>
> | Factor | Analysis |
> |-------|----------|
> | **Visible intoxication** | Don't serve — legally liable |
> | **Companion says okay** | Still don't serve |
> | **Guest promises to get home safe** | Still don't serve |
> | **Refusal of service** | Must be consistent and firm |
>
> **How to Handle:**
> 1. "I'm sorry, I won't be able to serve you another drink tonight. You've had a great evening, and I want to make sure you get home safely."
> 2. Offer water, coffee, non-alcoholic options
> 3. Offer to call a rideshare
> 4. If they insist: "I could lose my job and we could both be in trouble. I'm going to get my manager."
> 5. Alert manager to situation
>
> **Dram Shop Laws:**
> - Your establishment can be sued if you over-serve
> - Your personal liability may also be at stake
> - If in doubt, don't serve
> - Document refusal and offer of alternatives

## 9.3 Pricing Dispute at Table

**User:** "A guest is upset that theprix fixe is $20 more than they expected. They're threatening to leave without paying."

**Expert:**
> **Service Recovery Protocol:**
>
> **Guest Satisfaction First:**
> 1. **Listen** — Let them express frustration without interrupting
> 2. **Apologize sincerely** — "I'm sorry this wasn't communicated clearly."
> 3. **Offer solutions**:
>    - Manager visit to discuss
>    - Discount on meal (manager approval)
>    - Free dessert/coffee to soften
>    - Clarify menu language for future
> 4. **Don't argue** — They're a guest
> 5. **Get manager if needed** — For goodwill gestures
>
> **Menu Clarity:**
> - Always mention price when presenting specials
> - Clarify what prix fixe includes
> - Mention add-ons with prices
> - Read the room — if guests hesitate on price, mention it
>
> **If They Still Refuse to Pay:**
> 1. Get manager involved
> 2. Calmly explain what was ordered
> 3. Show them the menu if price was listed
> 4. If genuinely misleading: consider adjusting
> 5. Never forcibly detain a guest
