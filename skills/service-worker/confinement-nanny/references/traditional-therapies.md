## Chinese TCM Therapies

### 1. 艾灸 (Moxibustion)
**Purpose:** Warming, expelling cold, promoting circulation

**Method:** Burning mugwort (ai ye) near acupuncture points

**Common Points:**
- CV8 (Shenque/navel) - Warming abdomen
- ST36 - Energy, digestion
- SP6 - Women's health

**Safety:** 
- Never directly on skin without barrier
- Avoid with fever or infection
- Ventilation required

### 2. 推拿 (Tuina/Massage)
**Purpose:** Promote lochia discharge, relieve aches, relaxation

**Contraindications:**
- High blood pressure
- Fever
- Severe anemia
- Fresh C-section incision

## Malay Pantang Therapies

### 1. Bertungku (Hot Compress)
**Purpose:** Uterine contraction, expel "wind", pain relief

**Equipment:**
- Tungku stone or iron
- Kain sarong (wrapping cloth)
- Fresh herbs (lengkuas, mengkudu, daun sirih)

**Procedure:**
1. Heat tungku on stove until hot (not burning)
2. Place fresh herbs around tungku
3. Wrap in 2-3 layers of sarong
4. Roll or press on body areas:
   - Abdomen (uterus)
   - Lower back
   - Thighs
   - Shoulders
5. Duration: 30-45 minutes
6. Frequency: 2x/day for first 2 weeks

**⚠️ Safety:**
- Test temperature on inner wrist first
- Never apply directly to skin
- Remove if too hot
- Avoid fresh C-section incision

### 2. Bengkung (Belly Binding)
**Purpose:** Abdominal support, posture, "womb warming"

**Types:**
- Bengkung Jawa - Traditional long cloth
- Bengkung Panjang - Extended wrap
- Modern binders - Velcro/elastic alternatives

**Binding Method:**
1. Lie flat on back, knees bent
2. Optional: Apply herbal paste (param)
3. Start wrapping from upper ribs
4. Wrap downward in overlapping layers
5. Cover from ribs to hips
6. Firm but not tight (should allow deep breathing)

**Duration:**
- Wear 8-12 hours/day
- Remove for bathing, bathroom, sleep
- Continue 30-44 days

**⚠️ Safety:**
- Not too tight (impairs breathing/circulation)
- Remove if pain or numbness
- C-section: Wait until incision heals (1-2 weeks)

### 3. Urut (Postnatal Massage)
**Purpose:** Circulation, muscle recovery, relaxation

**When:** Week 2+ (after initial healing)

**Areas:**
- Back and shoulders
- Arms and legs
- Abdomen (gentle)
- Breast (lactation support)

**⚠️ Contraindications:**
- High blood pressure
- Fever or infection
- Fresh surgical wounds
- Severe anemia

### 4. Tangas (Herbal Steam)
**Purpose:** Vaginal healing, "shrinking" uterus

**⚠️ Modern Safety Note:** Vaginal steaming is NOT recommended by modern medical standards due to burn risk and lack of evidence. If practiced:
- Ensure safe temperature
- Limit duration
- Discontinue if discomfort

## Safety Guidelines

### General Principles
1. **Respect cultural practices but prioritize safety**
2. **Modify harmful traditions** (no 30-day no-bathing rule)
3. **Always get medical clearance** for therapies after C-section
4. **Watch for infection signs** with any therapy

### Red Flags - Stop Therapy
- Fever >38°C
- Increased bleeding
- Severe pain
- Signs of infection
- Dizziness or weakness

### When to Avoid Traditional Therapies
- Postpartum hemorrhage
- Preeclampsia/eclampsia
- Infection
- Severe anemia
- Unstable medical condition

## Modern Adaptations

### Simplified Bertungku
- Use warm towel compress instead of hot stone
- Heating pad on low (with cloth barrier)
- Warm water bottle

### Modern Belly Binding
- Postpartum recovery belts
- Adjustable binders with Velcro
- Compression garments

### Herbal Baths → Warm Showers
- Traditional: Herbal sitz baths
- Modern: Warm showers with gentle soap
- Sitz bath basin for perineal healing
