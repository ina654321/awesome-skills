# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Safety Violations

| # | Pitfall | Severity | Prevention |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | **Co-sleeping with infant** | 🔴 High | Always reinforce safe sleep (back to sleep, bare crib) |
| 2 | **Shaking baby** | 🔴 High | Calming techniques, know your limits, ask for help |
| 3 | **Unsafe bottle prep** | 🔴 High | Proper formula mixing, no microwave warming |
| 4 | **Leaving baby unattended** | 🔴 High | Never leave baby alone, even for a moment |
| 5 | **No infant CPR** | 🔴 High | Current CPR certification required |

## 10.2 Boundary Violations

⚠️ **Privacy & Professionalism:**
- Discussing the family with others
- Posting photos of baby/family on social media
- Accepting expensive gifts
- Personal relationships with family members
- Criticizing parent's parenting choices
- Overstepping into medical decisions

## 10.3 Feeding Errors

⚠️ **Newborn Feeding Risks:**
- Propping bottles (choking risk)
- Honey to infant under 1 year (botulism)
- Overfeeding/pacifier before breastfeeding established
- Not verifying formula type/mixing instructions
- Warming bottles in microwave (hot spots)

## 10.4 Medical Errors

⚠️ **Critical Mistakes:**
- Giving medication without clear authorization
- Ignoring fever in newborn (< 3 months)
- Not following prescribed care plans
- Delaying emergency response
- Treating without understanding contraindications

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Infant CPR certified and current
- [ ] Safe sleep practices followed at all times
- [ ] Feeding log maintained accurately
- [ ] Mother's rest protected
- [ ] PPD warning signs monitored
- [ ] Boundaries respected with family
- [ ] Professional confidentiality maintained
- [ ] Emergency contacts easily accessible
