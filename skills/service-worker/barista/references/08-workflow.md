# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Opening Procedures

```
Pre-Opening (30-45 min before):
├── Unlock and disarm alarm
├── Turn on lights and espresso machine
├── Pull shots of each coffee — quality check
├── Test grinder calibration
├── Brew batch brew of the day
├── Check milk quality and expiration
├── Stock cups, lids, sleeves, straws
├── Prepare syrups and modifiers
├── Set up lobby (menus, tables, trash)
└── Balanced register

Walk-Around Quality Check:
├── Floors clean and dry
├── Tables wiped
├── Bathrooms clean and stocked
├── All stations fully stocked
├── Hot water for tea ready
└── Signage current and visible
```

## 8.2 Espresso Preparation

```
Pulling a Shot (Standard Workflow):
1. Purge grouphead — flush with hot water (5 sec)
2. Dose — 18-20g of ground coffee (tamp level)
3. Tamp — level, 30 lbs pressure, straight
4. Insert portafilter — lock in firmly
5. Extract — 25-30 seconds, 36-40g out
6. Evaluate — golden crema, even distribution
7. Adjust — if sour (under-extracted), grind finer; if bitter (over-extracted), grind coarser

Troubleshooting:
- Channelling: Re-dial grind, check tamp
- Spraying: Wipe basket, redistribute grounds
- Slow extraction: Grind coarser
- Fast extraction: Grind finer
```

## 8.3 Milk Steaming

```
Steaming Latte/ Cappuccino:
1. Purge wand — blow out water
2. Texturing phase — introduce air (tip just below surface)
3. Stretching phase — incorporate air, create microfoam
4. Finish — lower tip, spin milk to polish
5. Stop at 140-150°F (60-65°C)
6. Swirl pitcher — check consistency
7. Pour immediately

Foam Quality:
- Velvety microfoam: small bubbles, glossy
- Large bubbles: not stretched enough
- Scalded milk: too hot, tastes burnt
- Runny milk: over-stretched, no body
```
