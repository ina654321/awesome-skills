# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 The Difficult Customer

**User:** "A customer is berating my coworker for getting their order wrong. It's escalating. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **De-escalation Protocol:**
>
> | Step | Response |
> |------|----------|
> | 1 | **Intervene calmly** | "Hi, I'm [name]. What's going on? Let's figure this out." |
> | 2 | **Acknowledge the frustration** | "I understand — getting the wrong order is frustrating." |
> | 3 | **Empathize** | "I'd feel the same way if I waited and got the wrong drink." |
> | 4 | **Fix it immediately** | Remake the drink, get it right |
> | 5 | **Don't argue** | Never match their energy or argue back |
> | 6 | **Know when to step back** | If it's threatening, call manager or 911 |
>
> **Recovery:**
> - Refund or remake at minimum
> - Consider a free drink as apology
> - Follow up with the customer who complained
> - Support your coworker — they may be shaken
>
> **When to Involve Management:**
> - Threats of violence
> - Discrimination or harassment
> - Physical contact
> - Customer refuses to calm down after reasonable attempt

## 9.2 Equipment Malfunction

**User:** "The espresso machine is making a grinding noise and producing no water flow. We have a line of 10 people. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Espresso Machine Troubleshooting:**
>
> | Check | Likely Cause |
> |-------|-------------|
> | **Grinding noise, no water** | Blocked grouphead, scale buildup |
> | **Weak pressure** | Scale, worn gasket, pump issue |
> | **Spraying water** | Cracked shower screen, bad basket |
> | **Burning smell** | Electrical — unplug immediately |
>
> **Immediate Response:**
> 1. **Don't panic** — communicate with customers
> 2. **Try flushing** — run plain hot water through
> 3. **Back-up plan** | Switch to batch brew (automatic drip)
> 4. **Notify manager** | Equipment failure needs management
> 5. **Be transparent** | "Our espresso machine is having issues. We'll have your drinks ready in just a moment."
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Daily backflush with cleaning tablets
> - Weekly descale
> - Annual professional service

## 9.3 Food Safety Incident

**User:** "I dropped a cake on the floor. It rolled under the pastry case. I picked it up and put it back on display. Was that right?"

**Expert:**
> **Food Safety Response:**
>
> | Rule | Why |
> |------|-----|
> | **Five-second rule doesn't apply** | Floor contamination is immediate |
> | **Dropped food goes in trash** | Never serve food that touched floor |
> | **Tell the manager** | This is a food safety deviation |
> | **Document it** | Health inspectors may ask |
>
> **Correct Response:**
> 1. Discard the item immediately
> 2. Check if items nearby were contaminated
> 3. Tell manager
> 4. Review food safety training
> 5. If in doubt, throw it out
>
> **Critical Temperatures:**
> - Hot food: Keep above 135°F
> - Cold food: Keep below 41°F
> - Frozen: Keep at 0°F or below
> - When in doubt: throw it out
