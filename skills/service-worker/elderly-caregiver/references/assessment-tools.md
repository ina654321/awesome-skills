# Assessment Tools Reference Guide

## Katz Index of Independence in Activities of Daily Living (Katz ADL)

### Overview
Developed by Sidney Katz in 1963, the Katz ADL Index is the most widely used standardized tool for assessing functional status in elderly populations. It measures independence in six basic self-care activities.

### The Six ADLs

| Activity | Independent (1 point) | Dependent (0 points) |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Bathing** | Bathes self completely or needs help only with one area (back, genitalia, disabled extremity) | Needs help with bathing more than one body part, getting in/out of tub, or total bath |
| **Dressing** | Gets clothes from closets/drawers, puts on clothes and outer garments; may need help tying shoes | Needs help with dressing or must be completely dressed by another |
| **Toileting** | Goes to toilet, gets on/off, arranges clothes, cleans genital area without help | Needs help transferring to toilet, cleaning self, or uses bedpan/commode |
| **Transferring** | Moves in/out of bed or chair unassisted; mechanical transfer aids acceptable | Needs help in moving from bed to chair or requires complete transfer |
| **Continence** | Controls bladder and bowel completely (without occasional accidents) | Partially or totally incontinent of bowel or bladder |
| **Feeding** | Gets food from plate to mouth without help; food may be prepared by another | Needs partial or complete help with feeding or fed intravenously |

### Scoring Interpretation

| Score | Functional Status | Care Needs |
|-------|------------------|------------|
| **6** | Full function | Independent or minimal assistance |
| **5** | Mild impairment | Minimal assistance with 1 ADL |
| **4** | Moderate impairment | Moderate assistance needed |
| **3** | Moderate impairment | Significant assistance with multiple ADLs |
| **2 or less** | Severe impairment | Extensive assistance or total care |

### Clinical Applications

**Reliability & Validity:**
- Internal consistency: Cronbach's alpha 0.84-0.94
- Test-retest reliability: ICC 0.89-0.96
- Strong correlation with Barthel Index (r = 0.91)

**When to Use:**
- Initial assessment and care planning
- Monitoring functional decline
- Discharge planning
- Research and outcome measurement

## Morse Fall Scale (MFS)

### Overview
Developed by Janice Morse in 1985, the MFS is one of the most widely used fall risk assessment tools in acute care and long-term care settings. It evaluates six risk factors.

### Risk Factor Assessment

| Risk Factor | Score | Criteria |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| **History of Falling** | 25 | Yes (fall within 3 months) |
| | 0 | No |
| **Secondary Diagnosis** | 15 | Yes (2+ medical diagnoses) |
| | 0 | No |
| **Ambulatory Aid** | 30 | Furniture (walks holding onto furniture) |
| | 15 | Crutches/cane/walker |
| | 0 | None/bedrest/wheelchair/nurse assist |
| **IV/Heparin Lock** | 20 | Yes |
| | 0 | No |
| **Gait** | 20 | Impaired (shuffles, stoops, loses balance) |
| | 10 | Weak (stoops, may grab furniture, short steps) |
| | 0 | Normal/bedrest/wheelchair |
| **Mental Status** | 15 | Forgets limitations or overestimates abilities |
| | 0 | Oriented to own ability |

### Risk Level Interpretation

| Total Score | Risk Level | Intervention Level |
|-------------|------------|-------------------|
| **0-24** | Low Risk | Standard precautions |
| **25-45** | Moderate Risk | Enhanced monitoring, prevention strategies |
| **>45** | High Risk | Intensive fall prevention protocol |

### High-Risk Interventions

1. **Bed/chair alarms** — Alert when patient attempts to stand
2. **Frequent rounding** — Check every 15-30 minutes
3. **Toileting schedule** — Every 2 hours, before bedtime
4. **Non-slip footwear** — Always when ambulating
5. **Environment** — Clear pathways, adequate lighting, bed low/locked
6. **Hip protectors** — For high-risk ambulatory patients
7. **Physical therapy** — Gait training, strengthening exercises

## Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE)

### Overview
Developed by Folstein et al. in 1975, the MMSE is the most widely used brief screening tool for cognitive impairment. It assesses 6 cognitive domains over 30 points.

### Domain Breakdown

| Domain | Points | Assessment Items |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| **Orientation to Time** | 5 | Year, season, month, date, day |
| **Orientation to Place** | 5 | State, county, city, building, floor |
| **Registration** | 3 | Repeat 3 words (apple, table, penny) |
| **Attention/Calculation** | 5 | Spell "WORLD" backward or serial 7s |
| **Recall** | 3 | Remember the 3 words from registration |
| **Language** | 9 | Naming, repetition, 3-step command, reading, writing, construction |

### Scoring Interpretation

| Score | Cognitive Status | Clinical Implication |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **25-30** | Normal cognition | No significant impairment |
| **21-24** | Mild cognitive impairment | Monitor, consider further testing |
| **10-20** | Moderate cognitive impairment | Significant impairment, needs support |
| **<10** | Severe cognitive impairment | Extensive care needs |

### Educational & Cultural Adjustments

**Education Correction:**
- Add 1 point for <8 years of education
- Subtract 1 point for >16 years of education

**Cultural Considerations:**
- May underestimate impairment in highly educated individuals
- May overestimate impairment in those with limited education
- Language barriers affect performance
- Use culturally appropriate norms

### Limitations
- Not diagnostic — screening tool only
- Does not assess executive function
- Ceiling effect in mild cognitive impairment
- Affected by depression, delirium, sensory deficits

## Mini Nutritional Assessment - Short Form (MNA-SF)

### Overview
The MNA-SF is a validated 6-item screening tool for identifying elderly at risk of malnutrition.

### Assessment Items

| Item | Scoring |
|------|---------|
| **Food Intake** | 0=Severe decrease, 1=Moderate decrease, 2=No decrease |
| **Weight Loss** | 0=>3kg, 1=Don't know, 2=1-3kg, 3=None |
| **Mobility** | 0=Bed/chair bound, 1=Able to get out, 2=Goes out |
| **Psychological Stress/Acute Disease** | 0=Yes, 2=No |
| **Neuropsychological Problems** | 0=Severe dementia/depression, 1=Mild dementia, 2=No problems |
| **BMI** | 0=<19, 1=19-<21, 2=21-<23, 3=≥23 |

### Interpretation

| Score | Nutritional Status | Action |
|-------|-------------------|--------|
| **12-14** | Normal nutritional status | Routine monitoring |
| **8-11** | At risk of malnutrition | Nutrition intervention |
| **0-7** | Malnourished | Immediate nutrition support |

## Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (Adapted for Elderly)

### Overview
The EPDS is a 10-item self-report scale originally for postpartum depression, validated for use in elderly populations.

### Scoring
- Each item scored 0-3
- Total range: 0-30
- Score ≥10: Possible depression
- Score ≥13: Probable depression

### Items Assessed
1. Ability to laugh and see funny side
2. Ability to look forward with enjoyment
3. Blaming oneself unnecessarily
4. Anxiety/worry for no good reason
5. Panic feelings
6. Overwhelmed by difficulties
7. Difficulty sleeping
8. Sadness and misery
9. Crying
10. Self-harm thoughts

## Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS-15)

### Overview
A 15-item yes/no self-report scale specifically designed for older adults.

### Scoring
- "Yes" to negative questions or "No" to positive questions = 1 point
- Total range: 0-15

| Score | Depression Level |
|-------|-----------------|
| **0-4** | Normal |
| **5-9** | Mild depression |
| **10-15** | Severe depression |

## Assessment Frequency Guidelines

| Tool | Initial Assessment | Reassessment | Documentation |
|------|-------------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Katz ADL** | Within 72 hours | Monthly or after change | Care plan |
| **Morse Fall Scale** | Admission, post-fall | Weekly or after change | Incident report if fall |
| **MMSE** | Within 72 hours | Every 6 months or significant change | Physician notification if <24 |
| **MNA-SF** | Within 72 hours | Monthly | Dietitian referral if <11 |
| **GDS** | Within 72 hours | Every 3 months or concern | Mental health referral if ≥10 |
