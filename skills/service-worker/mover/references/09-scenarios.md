# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Item Damaged During Move

**User:** "A customer claims their grandmother's antique mirror was damaged during the move. It wasn't noted as damaged on the pre-move inspection. What do we do?"

**Expert:**
> **Damage Claims Protocol:**
>
> | Situation | Action |
> |----------|--------|
> | **Not noted pre-move** | Document damage immediately, note pre-move condition |
> | **Damage found at delivery** | Note on delivery receipt, photograph immediately |
> | **Customer files claim** | Process per company policy, FMCSA guidelines |
>
> **At Delivery — Customer Present:**
> 1. **Walk through together** — Before signing delivery receipt
> 2. **Note all damage** — Write "Subject to Inspection" on receipt
> 3. **Photograph damage** — Document with timestamps
> 4. **Customer signs with exceptions** — "Accept with exception"
> 5. **Don't take damaged item** — Until claim resolved if possible
>
> **FMCSA Requirements:**
> - Carrier liable for damage if: Inadequate packing by carrier, improper loading
> - Carrier NOT liable if: Customer packed themselves, inherent vice
> - File claim within 9 months (federal minimum)
> - Carrier must respond within 30 days
> - Carrier must resolve or deny within 120 days
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Thorough pre-move inspection and documentation
> - Photograph all sides of fragile items
> - Use padding and blankets
> - Don't overload
> - Get signature on pre-move condition form

## 9.2 Stairs/Obstacle Damage

**User:** "The movers need to get a large couch down narrow stairs. One of them wants to drag it and risk it. What's the right approach?"

**Expert:**
> **Stairs Navigation Protocol:**
>
> | Approach | When to Use |
> |---------|------------|
> | **Team lift straight down** | Standard stairs, manageable items |
> | **Angle down staircase** | Wide items at angles |
> | **Outside fire escape/hoist** | Very large items, narrow stairs |
> | **Furniture sliders** | Downstairs only if floor can handle it |
> | **Dismantle** | Take off legs, doors, etc. |
>
> **Safety First:**
> - If it can't be done safely, it shouldn't be done
> - Risk of damage to item AND injury to movers
> - Supervisor must be consulted for difficult situations
> - Customer must be informed of any limitations
>
> **Right vs. Wrong:**
> ❌ "Just drag it down" — Scratches floors, damaged walls, risk of injury
> ✅ "Let's angle it" — Four movers, one per corner, controlled descent
> ✅ "We need to take the legs off" — Proper disassembly
> ✅ "This won't fit safely" — Notify customer, explore alternatives

## 9.3 Pricing Dispute at Delivery

**User:** "The customer refuses to pay the final balance, saying the estimate was different. They're threatening to not let us unload."

**Expert:**
> **Pricing Dispute Resolution:**
>
> | Situation | Response |
> |----------|----------|
> | **Estimate vs. Binding** | Binding = fixed price, non-binding = actual weight/services |
> | **Add-ons not discussed** | Was the customer informed of additional charges? |
> | **Weight discrepancy** | Reweigh at certified scale if disputed |
> | **Accessorial charges** | Were they disclosed and agreed upon? |
>
> **De-escalation Steps:**
> 1. **Listen** — Let customer explain their concern
> 2. **Review the agreement** — What was promised vs. delivered?
> 3. **Explain charges** — Walk through the invoice line by line
> 4. **Check for errors** — Wrong address? Wrong services?
> 5. **Negotiate if reasonable** — Supervisor approval for concessions
>
> **Customer Rights (FMCSA):**
> - Right to receive written estimate
> - Right to inspect and weigh shipment
> - Right to contest charges
> - Goods cannot be held hostage for payment
>
> **Don't:**
> - Threaten to dump goods
> - Refuse reasonable dispute resolution
> - Misrepresent charges
> - Leave goods inaccessible while disputing
