# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Allergic Reaction to Dye

**User:** "A client starts itching during a color treatment. Her scalp is red and she's complaining of burning. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Chemical Reaction Protocol:**
>
> | Severity | Symptoms | Response |
> |----------|---------|----------|
> | **Mild** | Itching, tingling | Rinse immediately, apply warm water |
> | **Moderate** | Redness, swelling, hives | Rinse, remove all product, antihistamine |
> | **Severe** | Difficulty breathing, swelling | Call 911 immediately |
>
> **Immediate Steps:**
> 1. **Stop immediately** — Don't wait to finish the service
> 2. **Rinse thoroughly** — Remove all product from hair and scalp
> 3. **Apply warm water** — Cool water can close cuticles and trap chemical
> 4. **Give antihistamine** — Benadryl if available and client can take it
> 5. **Remove client from area** — Fresh air
> 6. **Document** — What product, when applied, when reaction started
> 7. **Medical evaluation** — If moderate or severe, recommend doctor visit
>
> **Prevention:**
> - ALWAYS do patch test 24-48 hours before color
> - Check health history form
> - Never skip patch test even for "same product"
> - Document patch test in record

## 9.2 Color Correction Disaster

**User:** "A client comes in with box dye that turned her hair orange. She wants it platinum blonde. Can I fix it today?"

**Expert:**
> **Color Correction Assessment:**
>
> | Consideration | Analysis |
> |--------------|----------|
> | **Hair condition** | Is it healthy enough for more processing? |
> | **Time needed** | Color correction takes 3-6+ hours |
> | **Multiple sessions** | Often cannot fix in one visit |
> | **Realistic expectations** | Platinum may not be possible |
>
> **Correct Response:**
> "I'd love to help you. Let me assess your hair condition first. Box dye is very hard to remove and getting to platinum from orange will require multiple sessions. Here's what I recommend:
> 1. We'll start with a color remover to pull out as much orange as possible
> 2. We'll tone it down
> 3. I'll need to see how your hair responds before we go lighter
> 4. We may need 2-3 sessions to get you to where you want to be
>
> Let me examine your hair, then we can talk about a realistic plan."
>
> **Never:**
> - Promise results you can't deliver
> - Apply bleach over previously dyed hair without assessment
> - Rush a correction (will cause more damage)
> - Skip the consultation and strand test

## 9.3 Client Request for Prohibited Service

**User:** "A client with lice wants me to cut her hair shorter so the lice won't spread. Should I do it?"

**Expert:**
> **Contagious Condition Policy:**
>
> **The Rule: Do NOT serve clients with active lice, ringworm, or other contagious conditions.**
>
> | Condition | Policy |
> |----------|--------|
> | **Active lice** | Do not serve — refer to lice treatment specialist |
> | **Ringworm** | Do not serve over affected area — refer |
> | **Conjunctivitis** | Do not service — refer |
> | **Open scalp wounds** | Do not service over wounds |
> | **Contagious illness** | Postpone until cleared |
>
> **How to Handle:**
> - Be discreet: "I notice something on your scalp. For your safety and mine, I need to reschedule. Let me give you a referral for treatment."
> - Be kind — lice happen to anyone
> - Provide referral
> - Sanitize your chair and tools if in contact
> - Don't gossip — it's confidential
> - Follow up at next appointment
