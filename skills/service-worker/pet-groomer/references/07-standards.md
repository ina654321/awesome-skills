## 7. Standard Workflow

### Pre-Groom Intake

```
Health Questionnaire:
  ├── Any health conditions? (arthritis, heart, seizures)
  ├── Any injuries or sore areas?
  ├── Current medications?
  ├── Known allergies?
  ├── Previous grooming experience?
  ├── Any bite history?

Pre-Groom Inspection:
  ├── Check for mats, tangles
  ├── Check skin for irritation, lumps, fleas
  ├── Check ears for infection (smell, discharge)
  ├── Check eyes for discharge, cloudiness
  ├── Check nails — how long?
  └── Note anything unusual for owner

Owner Consultation:
  ├── Confirm desired style/breed cut
  ├── Explain any issues found
  ├── Get approval for extra services
  ├── Agree on price
  └── Emergency contact number
```

### The Grooming Process

```
Step 1: Pre-Bath Brushing
  ├── Remove loose undercoat
  ├── Work out tangles gently
  └── Apply detangler if needed

Step 2: Bath
  ├── Test water temperature (warm, not hot)
  ├── Wet thoroughly, avoiding face/ears
  ├── Apply shampoo — massage in
  ├── Condition if needed
  ├── Rinse COMPLETELY — residue causes irritation

Step 3: Drying
  ├── Towel dry until damp
  ├── Use dryer on low/cool first
  ├── Work up to warm — never hot
  ├── Dry completely to prevent hotspots

Step 4: Brushing/Combing
  ├── Blow dry while brushing for volume
  ├── Line brush (section by section)
  ├── Check for any remaining mats
  └── Final brush-out

Step 5: Haircut
  ├── Body — clipper or scissors per style
  ├── Feet — tidy up pads, between toes
  ├── Face — scissors around eyes, muzzle
  ├── Ears — trim excess hair
  └── Sanitary area — trim clean

Step 6: Finishing Touches
  ├── Nail trim/grind
  ├── Ear cleaning
  ├── Paw pad balm
  ├── Cologne/spritz (optional)
  └── Final photo for owner
```

### Handling Anxious Pets

```
Signs of Anxiety:
  ├── Panting, drooling
  ├── Trembling, hiding
  ├── Trying to escape
  ├── Whale eye
  └── Growling or snapping

Calming Techniques:
  ├── Go slow — don't rush
  ├── Talk in calm, low voice
  ├── Offer treats (if allowed)
  ├── Let them sniff tools first
  ├── Work in short bursts with breaks
  ├── Consider muzzle for safety (not punishment)

When to Stop:
  ├── Excessive struggling
  ├── Signs of exhaustion
  ├── Heat stress (panting heavily, drooling)
  ├── Refusing to calm down after breaks
  └── Owner preference to stop
```

## 8. Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Severely Matted Coat

**Context:** Owner brings in a long-haired Golden Retriever. The dog hasn't been groomed in 8 months. The coat is severely matted — thick mats around the entire body, especially behind ears and under legs.

**Expert Response:**
> **Assessment:**
>
> | Area | Condition | Action |
> |------|-----------|--------|
> | Body | Severe matting | Likely need full body shave |
> | Behind ears | Severe | Careful — likely skin sensitivity |
> | Armpits | Severe | Very sensitive area |
> | Under legs | Severe | May have skin irritation underneath |
>
> **Owner Consultation:**
> "Hi! I can see [dog] has some significant matting. I want to explain what we're dealing with and our options."
>
> | Option | Description | Price Impact |
> |--------|-------------|--------------|
> | Full shave | Use clippers, remove all matting, likely 1-2" length | Standard groom |
> | Dematting attempt | Hours of work, stress on dog, may not succeed | 2-3x price, may fail |
> | Partial shave | Shave body, leave head/legs | Reduced price |
>
> **Recommendation:**
> "Because the matting is so severe, I recommend the full shave. Trying to dematt would take hours and be very stressful for [dog]. The shave will take about 2 hours and he'll feel MUCH better afterward. His coat will grow back beautifully — it usually takes 3-4 months to grow back to a normal length."
>
> **The Process:**
> 1. Sedate? — If very stressed, discuss with vet (we don't sedate)
> 2. Use #7 blade for body, #10 for sanitary
> 3. Work in sections, checking skin as you go
> 4. Apply aloe/conditioner after
> 5. Warn owner: "New coat may be softer/different texture"

### Scenario 2: Aggressive Dog

**Context:** A new client brings in a 60-lb Labrador mix. The dog is growling, showing teeth, and lunging when you try to approach. Owner says "He's fine, just needs a bath."

**Expert Response:**
> **Immediate Assessment:**
> ```
> Signs observed:
>   • Growling on approach
>   • Showing teeth (air snap potential)
>   • Lunging toward you
>   • Stiff body language
>   • Owner says: "He's fine" (red flag — owner doesn't know)
> ```
>
> **Action:**
> 1. Step back — create space
> 2. "I need to stop here. This dog is showing clear aggression signs."
> 3. Address owner: "I want to help [dog], but for everyone's safety, I can't proceed without a proper introduction."
>
> **Alternative Approach:**
> 1. Ask owner to stay
> 2. Let dog sniff you (sitting, non-threatening)
> 3. Offer treat from your hand (if takes, progress; if not, stop)
> 4. Try gentle handling with owner present
> 5. If still aggressive: "I think we need to see if a vet behaviorist can help first, or try a groomer who specializes in aggressive dogs."
>
> **If Proceeding with Caution:**
> - Use basket muzzle (never cloth on aggressive dog)
> - Work quickly but safely
> - Have owner nearby for reassurance
> - Take breaks if needed
> - Have a plan to stop without injury
>
> **Never:**
> - Force an aggressive dog
> - Sedate without vet involvement
> - Risk a bite to finish a groom
> - Ignore warning signs

### Scenario 3: Senior Pet with Medical Conditions

**Context:** Owner brings in a 12-year-old Poodle for a grooming. You notice the dog is very thin, has trouble standing on the table, and the owner mentions she has arthritis and a heart condition.

**Expert Response:**
> **Assessment:**
> ```
> Observations:
>   • 12 years old = senior (geriatric)
>   • Thin body condition
>   • Difficulty standing
>   • History: arthritis, heart condition
>   • First groom in 6 months
> ```
>
> **Safety Considerations:**
> | Concern | Risk | Mitigation |
> |---------|------|------------|
> | Arthritis | Painful handling | Gentle, avoid stress on joints |
> | Heart condition | Stress = cardiac event | Keep it short, calm, cool |
> | Thin/weak | Exhaustion | Frequent breaks, don't rush |
> | Age | Overall vulnerability | Monitor constantly |
>
> **Approach:**
> 1. Speak with owner: "I noticed she's quite thin and has trouble standing. Tell me more about her condition."
> 2. "I want to groom her, but I need to be extra careful. I may need to do a shorter session."
> 3. Table: Use non-slip mat, support her if she wobbles
> 4. Handle: Extra gentle, check for pain responses
> 5. Duration: Shorter than normal, maybe split into 2 visits
> 6. Watch: Heavy panting, collapse, excessive drooling
> 7. Cool: Don't overheat, keep water available
>
> **If Stress Signs:**
> - Stop immediately
> - Let her rest
> - Offer water
> - Call owner if concerned
> - Better to do partial groom and return

## 9. Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|--------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Ignoring Aggression Signs** | 🔴 High | Whale eye, growling, stiff = STOP and reassess |
| 2 | **Shaving Double-Coated Dogs** | 🔴 High | Never shave Husky, Golden, Collie — damages coat forever |
| 3 | **Forcing Anxious Pets** | 🔴 High | Will cause trauma, worse next time. Go slow or stop. |
| 4 | **Cutting the Quick** | 🔴 High | Only trim small amounts on dark nails; use grinder |
| 5 | **Skipping Health Check** | 🟡 Medium | Always check skin, ears, eyes, lumps before starting |
| 6 | **Using Hot Dryer** | 🟡 Medium | Always test on your wrist; never hot air |
| 7 | **Leaving Wet Dogs Unattended** | 🟡 Medium | Heat stroke risk; always monitor in dryer |
| 8 | **Mismatching Breed Cut** | 🟡 Medium | Know breed standards; ask owner what they want |

### Wrong vs. Right

```
❌ [Shaves a Golden Retriever in summer] "It'll help them cool down"
✅ [Never shaves double coat] "Shaving damages the coat and doesn't help with heat"

❌ [Ignores growling while brushing] "He's just being dramatic"
✅ [Stops immediately] "He's telling me to stop. Let me try a different approach."

❌ [Uses scissors near eyes without caution]
✅ [Uses comb to protect, or scissor with tips pointing away]

❌ [Forces matted dog through grooming]
✅ [Shaves matted areas; explains to owner why]
```
