# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Damaged Property

**User:** "While cleaning a client's antique marble countertop, I noticed a scratch that wasn't there before. I don't know if I caused it. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Property Damage Protocol:**
>
> **Immediate Response:**
> 1. **Stop and assess** — Was it there before? Can I see the before photos?
> 2. **Document immediately** — Note the scratch, your cleaning method
> 3. **Tell the client** — Be honest and professional
>    "I noticed this scratch on the countertop. I want to bring it to your attention right away."
> 4. **Apologize sincerely** — Don't make excuses
> 5. **Offer to fix or pay** — Get estimate for repair, offer to pay
> 6. **Document in writing** — Email follow-up summarizing conversation
>
> **Prevention for Next Time:**
> - Photograph high-value items before starting
> - Use soft cloths and appropriate products
> - Know which surfaces need special care
> - Never use abrasive materials on delicate surfaces
>
> **Legal Note:**
> - You may be liable for damage caused by negligence
> - Your liability depends on your contract
> - Most cleaning companies have insurance

## 9.2 Discovering Something Concerning

**User:** "While cleaning, I found drug paraphernalia in a client's bathroom. There's also a lot of alcohol bottles. Do I report this? I'm not sure what my obligations are."

**Expert:**
> **Discovering Illegal/Concerning Items:**
>
> | Item Type | Response |
> |----------|----------|
> | **Drug paraphernalia** | Do not touch, report to supervisor |
> | **Excessive alcohol (adult)** | Not reportable unless concerning context |
> | **Unsanitary conditions** | Wear PPE, may refuse service |
> | **Weapons** | Leave alone, report to supervisor |
> | **Child safety issues** | Report to APS or law enforcement |
>
> **Key Principles:**
> 1. **You are not law enforcement** — Your job is cleaning
> 2. **Don't touch paraphernalia** — Don't expose yourself to drugs
> 3. **Report to supervisor** — Let management decide
> 4. **You can refuse service** — If conditions are unsafe or illegal
> 5. **Client privacy** — Don't discuss with others
>
> **Child Safety Exception:**
> - If you see signs of child abuse or neglect
> - Mandatory reporting laws apply
> - Report to Child Protective Services

## 9.3 Clients Home During Cleaning

**User:** "A client decided to stay home while I'm cleaning. She keeps following me around and criticizing everything I do. How do I handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Client Presence Management:**
>
> **Set Boundaries Professionally:**
> 1. **Acknowledge** — "I'm happy to do things however you prefer. Let me know your priorities."
> 2. **Redirect** — "I have a system that ensures I don't miss anything. I'll get to that area."
> 3. **Be patient** — First-time clients may need assurance
> 4. **Check in** — "Is there anything specific you'd like me to focus on?"
>
> **If Criticism Becomes Unreasonable:**
> - "I want to make sure you're satisfied. Can we go over your priorities for today?"
> - Document in notes: "Client requested [specific approach]"
> - If you feel unsafe or harassed: Complete the job and discuss with supervisor
>
> **Professional Tips:**
> - Keep personal items minimal — don't snoop
> - Work systematically — shows professionalism
> - Don't discuss clients with others
> - Stay calm under scrutiny
