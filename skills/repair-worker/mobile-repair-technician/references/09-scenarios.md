# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Screen Damage Assessment

**Customer:** "I dropped my iPhone and the screen cracked. It's still working but I can see lines and some parts don't respond to touch."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Based on the symptoms, this is a classic LCD/display flex damage from impact:
> 
> 1. **Touch unresponsive areas** - Indicates flex cable damage or disconnection
> 2. **Visible lines** - Suggests display driver damage or cable fracture
> 3. **Partial functionality** - Points to partial connection rather than complete failure
> 
> **Recommended Action:**
> - Full display assembly replacement needed (not just glass)
> - Before repair: Back up data via iCloud if possible
> - Cost estimate: OEM-quality screen vs third-party
> - Time: 45-90 minutes typically
> 
> **Note:** Touch ID/Face ID may need re-registration after screen replacement.

## 9.2 Battery Swelling Emergency

**Customer:** "The back of my phone is starting to lift up and the screen is popping out slightly."

**Expert:**
> **CRITICAL SITUATION:**
> 
> This is a lithium battery swelling emergency. DO NOT:
> - Continue using the device
> - Attempt to crush or puncture
> - Apply heat
> 
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 1. Place device in fireproof container (metal box, sand)
> 2. Move away from flammable materials
> 3. Do NOT attempt repair - battery replacement requires professional handling
> 4. Schedule immediate repair appointment
> 
> **Root Causes to Discuss:**
> - Age of battery (typically 2+ years)
> - Charging habits (overnight charging, fast charging)
> - Exposure to heat
> - Manufacturing defects
> 
> **Prevention for Customer:**
> - Avoid extreme temperatures
> - Use manufacturer-approved chargers
> - Don't drain to 0% regularly
> - Replace batteries after 2 years if heavily used

## 9.3 Water Damage Recovery

**Customer:** "My phone fell in the toilet! I grabbed it immediately and it was on for a few seconds but now it won't turn on."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Care Instructions:**
> 
> 1. **Do NOT try to turn it on**
> 2. **Do NOT plug it in to charge**
> 3. **Do NOT use a hair dryer**
> 
> **Proper Steps:**
> - Wipe exterior dry immediately
> - Remove SIM tray and SIM card
> - Place in sealed container with silica gel packets
> - Wait minimum 48 hours before attempting power on
> 
> **Professional Assessment Will Include:**
> - Disassembly and ultrasonic cleaning
> - Isopropyl alcohol bath (90%+ concentration)
> - Visual inspection for corrosion
> - Component-by-component testing
> - Data recovery attempt if logic board is salvageable
> 
> **Success Rate:** 60-70% for devices dried within minutes. Drops significantly after 24+ hours.

## 9.4 Face ID Not Working After Drop

**Customer:** "My iPhone 12 fell and now Face ID doesn't work. The phone is otherwise fine."

**Expert:**
> **Diagnosis:**
> 
> Face ID failure after physical trauma typically indicates:
> 
> 1. **Loose connector** - Drop may have dislodged the dot projector
> 2. **Damaged flex cable** - Cable runs from front camera assembly to main board
> 3. **Flood illuminator damage** - Visible damage to IR components
> 4. **TrueDepth camera module failure** - Requires full module replacement
> 
> **Troubleshooting Steps:**
> - Check Settings > Face ID for enrollment option
> - Clean front camera area gently
> - Reset Face ID settings and re-enroll
> - If still failing, hardware repair needed
> 
> **Repair Consideration:**
> - Dot projector is part of TrueDepth assembly
> - Repair requires specialized equipment for calibration
> - After replacement, Face ID must be re-enrolled
> - Third-party repairs may break Face ID functionality permanently

## 9.5 No Sound From Speakers

**Customer:** "After my kid put the phone in their pocket, there's no sound from the speaker. But when I connect headphones, they work fine."

**Expert:**
> **Diagnosis:**
> 
> This is almost certainly debris in the speaker grill:
> 
> 1. **Lint/debris blockage** - Common from pocket exposure
> 2. **Moisture in speaker** - From sweat or humidity
> 3. **Speaker mesh damage** - From sharp objects
> 
> **Try These First:**
> - Gentle brushing with soft toothbrush
> - Compressed air in short bursts
> - Speaker cleaning app (plays specific frequencies)
> - Check Settings > Accessibility > Audio/Visual for proper routing
> 
> **If Not Resolved:**
> - Water ejection feature (some phones have this)
> - Professional cleaning may be needed
> - Speaker replacement unlikely but possible
> 
> **Prevention:**
> - Use phone cases with port covers
> - Keep away from sand and small particles
