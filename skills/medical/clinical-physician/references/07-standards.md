# Clinical Physician — Standards & Reference

## Clinical Guidelines & Organizations

### Major Guideline Bodies

**American College of Cardiology / American Heart Association (ACC/AHA)** — Cardiovascular guidelines. Guidelines for heart failure, ACS, arrhythmias, hypertension. Website: acc.org / heart.org

**Infectious Diseases Society of America (IDSA)** — Antimicrobial guidelines, HIV care, fungal infections. Website: idsociety.org

**American Diabetes Association (ADA)** — Standards of Care for diabetes. Website: diabetes.org

**American College of Physicians (ACP)** — Internal medicine guidelines, clinical practice guidelines.

**U.S. Preventive Services Task Force (USPSTF)** — Evidence-based preventive care recommendations. Website: uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org

### Evidence-Based Medicine Resources

**UpToDate** — Evidence-based clinical decision support. The standard reference for hospital and clinic use. Website: uptodate.com

**Cochrane Library** — Systematic reviews of healthcare interventions. Website: cochranelibrary.com

**DynaMed** — Clinical reference tool with evidence-based summaries. Website: dynamed.com

**PubMed/MEDLINE** — National Library of Medicine database. Website: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov

**BMJ Best Practice** — Evidence-based clinical decision support. Website: bestpractice.bmj.com

## Diagnostic Criteria & Classification

### Cardiovascular

**HEART Score (Chest Pain):**

| Score | Risk | MACE Rate | Action |
|-------|------|-----------|--------|
| 0-3 | Low | 1.7% | Early discharge |
| 4-6 | Moderate | 12% | Observation, serial troponins |
| 7-10 | High | 65% | Early invasive strategy |

**Wells Score (Pulmonary Embolism):**
- <2 points: Low probability → D-dimer
- 2-6 points: Moderate probability → D-dimer or CTPA
- >6 points: High probability → CTPA immediately

**Framingham Risk Score** — 10-year cardiovascular disease risk.

### Pulmonary

**CURB-65 (Community-Acquired Pneumonia):**
- Confusion
- Urea > 19 mg/dL
- Respiratory rate ≥ 30
- BP (SBP <90 or DBP ≤ 60)
- Age ≥ 65

Score 0-1: Outpatient; 2: Hospital admission; 3-5: ICU consideration.

### Other Scores

**Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)** — Neurological status. 3-15 scale.

**Child-Pugh Score** — Cirrhosis severity. Class A (5-6), B (7-9), C (10-15).

**APACHE II** — ICU mortality prediction. Score 0-71; >25 = high mortality.

**CHADS2-VASc** — Atrial fibrillation stroke risk.

**HAS-BLED** — Atrial fibrillation bleeding risk.

## Evidence Classification

### ACC/AHA Recommendation Classes

| Class | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **Class I** | Benefit >> Risk; Procedure/treatment should be performed |
| **Class IIa** | Benefit > Risk; Reasonable to perform |
| **Class IIb** | Benefit ≥ Risk; May be considered |
| **Class III** | Risk ≥ Benefit; Should not be performed |

### Level of Evidence

| Level | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **Level A** | Multiple populations evaluated; data from multiple RCTs or meta-analyses |
| **Level B** | Limited populations evaluated; data from single RCT or non-randomized studies |
| **Level C** | Very limited populations; consensus opinion or standard of care |

## Laboratory Reference Ranges

### Common Blood Tests

| Test | Normal Range | Critical Values |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Sodium (Na+) | 136-145 mEq/L | <120, >160 |
| Potassium (K+) | 3.5-5.0 mEq/L | <2.5, >6.5 |
| Creatinine | 0.7-1.3 mg/dL | >10 (or rapid rise >3.0 in 24h) |
| Glucose | 70-100 mg/dL fasting | <50, >400 |
| Troponin I | <0.04 ng/mL | Any elevation >99th percentile |
| Hemoglobin | 12-16 g/dL (F), 14-18 (M) | <7, >20 |
| WBC | 4.5-11.0 × 10³/μL | <2, >30 |
| Platelets | 150-400 × 10³/μL | <50, >1000 |
| INR | 0.8-1.2 | >5 (on warfarin) |
| aPTT | 25-35 seconds | >100 |
