# Clinical Physician — Glossary

## Clinical Assessment Terms

**Chief Complaint** — The primary symptom causing the patient to seek care.

**History of Present Illness (HPI)** — Detailed narrative of current symptoms.

**Review of Systems (ROS)** — Systematic inquiry about symptoms across organ systems.

**Past Medical History (PMH)** — Chronic conditions, prior surgeries, hospitalizations.

**Social History** — Smoking, alcohol, drugs, occupation, living situation.

**Family History** — Hereditary conditions and familial disease patterns.

**Anchoring Bias** — Locking onto initial diagnosis despite contradictory evidence.

**Availability Bias** — Overweighting diagnoses recently seen or easily recalled.

**Premature Closure** — Accepting a diagnosis before it's fully verified.

## Diagnostic Terms

**Differential Diagnosis (DDx)** — List of possible diagnoses explaining the presentation.

**VINDICATE** — Mnemonic for differential generation: Vascular, Infectious, Neoplastic, Degenerative, Intoxication, Congenital, Autoimmune, Traumatic, Endocrine/Environmental.

**Sensitivity** — True positive rate. Of those with disease, how many tested positive?

**Specificity** — True negative rate. Of those without disease, how many tested negative?

**Positive Predictive Value** — Probability of disease given a positive test.

**Negative Predictive Value** — Probability of no disease given a negative test.

**Pre-test Probability** — Estimated prevalence of disease before testing.

**Post-test Probability** — Revised probability after test result.

**Bayesian Reasoning** — Updating probability with new evidence.

## Treatment Terms

**Indication** — Reason to use a specific treatment.

**Contraindication** — Reason NOT to use a treatment.

**Absolute Contraindication** — Must not do this under any circumstances.

**Relative Contraindication** — Proceed with caution; may be appropriate if benefits outweigh risks.

**Off-label Use** — Using an approved medication for an unapproved indication.

**Evidence-based Medicine (EBM)** — Integrating clinical expertise with best available research.

**NNT (Number Needed to Treat)** — How many patients need treatment for one to benefit.

**NNH (Number Needed to Harm)** — How many patients need treatment for one to be harmed.

## Lab & Test Terms

**Troponin** — Cardiac biomarker for myocardial injury. Elevated = myocardial necrosis.

**BNP/NT-proBNP** — B-type natriuretic peptide. Heart failure marker.

**D-dimer** — Fibrin degradation product. Elevated in thrombosis, but low specificity.

**LFTs (Liver Function Tests)** — ALT, AST, ALP, GGT, bilirubin.

**BMP (Basic Metabolic Panel)** — Na, K, Cl, CO2, BUN, Cr, glucose.

**CMP (Comprehensive Metabolic Panel)** — BMP + LFTs + albumin + total protein.

**ABG (Arterial Blood Gas)** — pH, PaCO2, PaO2, HCO3. Assesses acid-base and oxygenation.

**CBC (Complete Blood Count)** — WBC, RBC, hemoglobin, hematocrit, platelets.

## Symptom Descriptors

**Quality** — Sharp, dull, burning, pressure, cramping, aching.

**Severity** — 0-10 scale, or mild/moderate/severe.

**Timing** — Onset, duration, frequency, progression.

**Location** — Localized vs. diffuse; radiation pattern.

**Provocation/Palliation** — What makes it better or worse?

## Cardiac Terms

**ACS (Acute Coronary Syndrome)** — STEMI, NSTEMI, unstable angina.

**STEMI** — ST-elevation MI. Complete coronary occlusion.

**NSTEMI** — Non-ST-elevation MI. Troponin elevation without ST changes.

**HF (Heart Failure)** — Systolic (EF reduced) or diastolic (EF preserved).

**HFrEF** — Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (<40%).

**HFpEF** — Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (≥50%).

**Cardiomyopathy** — Primary heart muscle disease.

## Pulmonary Terms

**COPD** — Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. Emphysema and/or chronic bronchitis.

**COPD Exacerbation** — Acute worsening of COPD symptoms.

**Pneumonia** — Lung parenchymal infection. Community-acquired (CAP) vs. hospital-acquired (HAP).

**PE (Pulmonary Embolism)** — Blood clot traveling to lungs.

**DVT (Deep Vein Thrombosis)** — Blood clot in deep veins, usually legs.

**ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome)** — Severe hypoxemic respiratory failure.

## GI Terms

**GERD** — Gastroesophageal reflux disease.

**GI Bleed** — Upper (UGIB: esophagus, stomach, duodenum) vs. lower (LGIB: colon, rectum).

**UGIB** — Upper GI bleed. Presents with hematemesis, melena.

**LGIB** — Lower GI bleed. Presents with hematochezia.

**IBD** — Inflammatory bowel disease. Crohn's or ulcerative colitis.

**IBS** — Irritable bowel syndrome. Functional, not inflammatory.

## Neurological Terms

**CVA (Cerebrovascular Accident)** — Stroke. Ischemic vs. hemorrhagic.

**TIA (Transient Ischemic Attack)** — Transient stroke symptoms with no residual deficit.

**Meningitis** — Inflammation of meninges. Bacterial vs. viral.

**Encephalitis** — Brain parenchymal inflammation.

**SAH (Subarachnoid Hemorrhage)** — Bleeding into subarachnoid space.

**GCS (Glasgow Coma Scale)** — 3-15 scale for neurological status.

## Acid-Base Terms

**Respiratory Acidosis** — ↑PaCO2 → ↓pH. Hypoventilation.

**Respiratory Alkalosis** — ↓PaCO2 → ↑pH. Hyperventilation.

**Metabolic Acidosis** — ↓HCO3 → ↓pH. Anion gap vs. non-anion gap.

**Anion Gap** — Na - (Cl + HCO3). Normal 8-12.

**Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis** — MUDPILES mnemonic: Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Propylene glycol, Isoniazid, Lactic acid, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates.
