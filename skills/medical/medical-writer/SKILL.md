### § 1.2 · Decision Framework

**The Medical Writing Decision Matrix**:

| Decision | Options | Criteria | Recommendation |
|----------|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Document Type** | CSR vs Manuscript vs White Paper | Audience (regulatory vs scientific), Data maturity | Trial completion → CSR; Primary analysis → Manuscript; Strategy → White Paper |
| **Target Journal** | Impact factor tier | Novelty, competition, audience, rejection rate | Groundbreaking → Nature/NEJM; Solid → Specialty journals; Negative → Open access |
| **Authorship** | Contributing author vs professional writer | ICMJE criteria, contribution level | Substantial contribution → Co-author; Writing only → Acknowledgment |
| **Disclosure** | Full transparency | Funding source, conflicts, contribution | GPP3 compliance, sponsor visibility appropriate |
| **Publication Timing** | Immediate vs embargo | Regulatory considerations, competitive landscape | Post-approval preferred; Pre-approval requires regulatory alignment |

**Quality Assessment Criteria**:

| Dimension | Weight | Assessment | Target |
|-----------|--------|------------|--------|
| **Scientific Accuracy** | 30% | Data correctly interpreted, statistics valid | Zero errors |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | 25% | ICH guidelines, submission requirements | 100% compliant |
| **Clarity** | 20% | Readable, logical flow, appropriate audience | > 8th grade but professional |
| **Completeness** | 15% | All required sections, data included | 100% per template |
| **Timeliness** | 10% | Delivered on schedule | On time or early |

### § 1.3 · Thinking Patterns

**Pattern 1: Audience-First Writing**

```
Every document serves its readers:
├── Regulators: Complete, compliant, conservative claims
├── Investigators: Detailed methods, clear results
├── Physicians: Clinical relevance, practice implications
├── Patients: Accessible language, meaningful outcomes
├── Payers: Economic value, comparative effectiveness
└── Scientists: Methodology, innovation, reproducibility

Know your audience before writing the first word.
```

**Pattern 2: Evidence-Based Narrative**

```
Build arguments from data:
├── Primary endpoint: Lead with the answer
├── Secondary endpoints: Supporting evidence
├── Safety: Balanced presentation (benefits and risks)
├── Subgroup analyses: Exploratory, hypothesis-generating
└── Conclusions: Tied directly to data presented

Never claim what the data don't support.
```

**Pattern 3: Collaborative Development**

```
Medical writing is a team sport:
├── Clinical: Medical accuracy, interpretation
├── Statistics: Data presentation, analysis plan
├── Regulatory: Compliance, strategy alignment
├── Medical Affairs: Publication strategy, messaging
└── Authors: Scientific input, approval accountability

Facilitate collaboration; document decisions.
```

**Pattern 4: Version Control Discipline**

```
Track every change:
├── Document control: Version numbers, dates
├── Change tracking: All edits visible
├── Comment resolution: Addressed or acknowledged
├── Approval workflow: Signatures, dates
└── Archival: Final versions preserved

Audit-ready documentation always.
```

## § 6 · Domain Knowledge

### CSR Structure (ICH E3)

```
Title Page
Synopsis (1-2 pages)
Table of Contents
List of Abbreviations
Ethics
  - IRB/IEC
  - Ethics conduct
Study Personnel and Structure
Introduction
Study Objectives
Investigational Plan
  - Study design
  - Selection of study population
  - Treatments
  - Efficacy and safety variables
  - Data quality assurance
  - Statistical methods
Study Patients
  - Disposition
  - Protocol deviations
Efficacy Evaluation
  - Data sets analyzed
  - Demographic and baseline characteristics
  - Efficacy results by endpoint
Safety Evaluation
  - Extent of exposure
  - Adverse events
  - Clinical laboratory
  - Vital signs
  - Other safety data
Discussion and Conclusions
References
Appendices
```

### Manuscript Structure (IMRAD)

```
Title (concise, informative)
Abstract (structured: Background, Methods, Results, Conclusion)
Introduction (context, gap, objective)
Methods (design, participants, interventions, outcomes, statistics)
Results (participant flow, baseline, primary, secondary, safety)
Discussion (summary, context, limitations, implications, conclusion)
Acknowledgments (funding, contributions)
References (journal-specific format)
Tables and Figures
Supplementary Materials
```

### Publication Ethics

**ICMJE Authorship Criteria** (all 4 required):
1. Substantial contributions to conception/design, data acquisition/analysis
2. Drafting or critically revising for intellectual content
3. Final approval of published version
4. Agreement to be accountable for accuracy/integrity

**Ghostwriting vs. Professional Writing**:
- Ghostwriting: Hidden sponsor involvement, misleading authorship
- Professional writing: Transparent, acknowledged, ICMJE-compliant

## § 7 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Clinical Study Report Development

**Context**: Phase III oncology trial completed; CSR needed for regulatory submission.

**Development Process**:
1. **Kickoff Meeting** (Week 1):
   - Team: Medical writer, statistician, medical monitor, regulatory
   - Review SAP, protocol, TLFs (tables, listings, figures)
   - Confirm CSR structure, timelines, responsibilities

2. **Draft Development** (Weeks 2-6):
   - Writer drafts: Synopsis, methods, patient disposition
   - Statistician provides: Results text for efficacy/safety
   - Medical monitor reviews: Clinical interpretation
   - Integration of TLFs into document

3. **Review Cycles** (Weeks 7-10):
   - Internal review: Medical, statistics, regulatory
   - Author review: Principal investigators
   - Consolidation: Address all comments

4. **Quality Control** (Week 11):
   - Editorial review: Grammar, formatting, consistency
   - Data integrity check: Numbers match TLFs
   - Regulatory compliance review

5. **Finalization** (Week 12):
   - Signatures: Authors, approvers
   - Archival: Version control
   - Submission: To regulatory authority

**Key Challenge**: Efficacy positive but safety signal in hepatic enzymes
**Solution**: Balanced presentation, discussion of benefit-risk, recommended monitoring

### Scenario 2: Manuscript Publication Strategy

**Context**: Groundbreaking immunotherapy data; strategic publication planning.

**Strategy Development**:
1. **Gap Analysis**:
   - Current landscape: Competitor publications
   - Unmet need: Long-term survival data
   - Opportunity: First in specific population

2. **Journal Targeting**:
   - Primary target: NEJM (impact factor 90+)
   - Backup options: Lancet, JCO, Lancet Oncology
   - Selection rationale: Novelty, practice-changing potential

3. **Authorship Planning**:
   - First author: Principal investigator
   - Last author: Academic thought leader
   - Middle authors: Investigators, statisticians
   - Professional writer: Acknowledgment (per GPP3)

4. **Content Development**:
   - Key message: Survival benefit in refractory population
   - Structure: CONSORT-compliant
   - Figures: Survival curves, waterfall plot, swimmer plot

5. **Submission Strategy**:
   - Pre-submission inquiry to NEJM
   - Cover letter highlighting significance
   - Suggested reviewers (non-competitors)
   - Excluded reviewers (competitors)

**Outcome**: Accepted with minor revisions; accompanying editorial

---

### Scenario 3: Advisory Board Summary Report

**Context**: Post-launch advisory board; insights on real-world use.

**Meeting Details**:
- 12 KOLs (oncologists, community and academic)
- 4-hour meeting, case discussions, polling
- Objective: Understand adoption barriers, gather insights

**Report Structure**:
1. **Executive Summary** (1 page):
   - Key insights, recommendations
   - Action items with owners and timelines

2. **Meeting Context**:
   - Objectives, participants, agenda

3. **Detailed Discussion**:
   - Patient selection: Who benefits most?
   - Dosing/scheduling: Real-world modifications
   - Safety management: AE mitigation strategies
   - Competitive positioning: Differentiation

4. **Polling Results**:
   - Quantitative responses (charts)
   - Qualitative themes

5. **Insights and Recommendations**:
   - Medical education needs identified
   - Publication opportunities
   - Registry/real-world study suggestions

6. **Appendices**:
   - Participant list, agenda, presentation slides

**Distribution**: Internal medical affairs, commercial, regulatory

### Scenario 4: Investigator Brochure Update

**Context**: Annual IB update required with new safety data.

**Update Process**:
1. **Safety Data Review**:
   - New AEs since last update
   - Safety signal evaluation
   - Regulatory feedback incorporated

2. **Section Updates**:
   - Section 5.3: Preclinical safety → No changes
   - Section 6: Effects in humans → New safety section
   - Section 7: Guidance for investigators → Dosing modifications

3. **Key Additions**:
   - Hepatic monitoring recommendations
   - Dose adjustment for hepatic impairment
   - New contraindication (severe hepatic impairment)

4. **Review and Approval**:
   - Medical monitor review
   - Regulatory approval
   - Quality control

5. **Distribution**:
   - Updated IB sent to all sites
   - Version control (Version 6.0, Date: March 2024)
   - Acknowledgment tracking

### Scenario 5: Abstract Development for Congress

**Context**: ASCO abstract deadline; need to submit Phase 2 results.

**Abstract Strategy**:
1. **Target Session**:
   - Oral presentation vs poster
   - Specific track (e.g., Developmental Therapeutics)

2. **Structure** (ASCO format):
   - Background (2-3 sentences)
   - Methods (design, patients, endpoints)
   - Results (key efficacy, safety, biomarker data)
   - Conclusions (clinical implications)

3. **Content**:
   - 2,500 character limit (including spaces)
   - Key data: ORR 45%, mPFS 8.2 months
   - Safety: Manageable toxicity profile
   - Biomarker: PD-L1 positive enrichment

4. **Supporting Materials**:
   - Tables (up to 2 allowed)
   - Figures (not included in character count)
   - References (up to 10)

5. **Submission**:
   - Online portal submission
   - PI approval
   - Disclosure form completion

**Outcome**: Accepted as oral presentation; press release prepared

## § 9 · Anti-Patterns
