# Glossary

## A/B Test (Split Test)
A randomized experiment comparing two or more variants (A vs. B) to determine which performs better against a defined metric (e.g., conversion rate, AOV). Statistical significance (typically p < 0.05) must be reached before declaring a winner.

## AOV (Average Order Value)
The average dollar amount spent per order. Calculated as total revenue divided by total number of orders. A key KPI for upsell/cross-sell strategy. Target AOV is often raised via bundle offers, free shipping thresholds, and product recommendations.

## Bounce Rate
Percentage of visitors who leave after viewing only one page, without taking any action. High bounce rate on product pages may indicate poor content, slow load, or misaligned traffic quality.

## CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
The total cost of acquiring a new customer, including marketing spend, advertising, and sales commissions divided by number of new customers. CAC should be evaluated alongside LTV to ensure healthy unit economics.

## Cart Abandonment Rate
Percentage of shoppers who add items to cart but leave without completing purchase. Industry average is 65-70%. Calculated as: (carts created - orders completed) / carts created × 100.

## CLV / LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)
The predicted net profit attributed to the entire future relationship with a customer. CLV = average order value × purchase frequency × customer lifespan × margin. Used to determine how much to spend on acquisition and retention.

## Conversion Funnel
The series of steps a visitor takes from landing on site to completing a purchase. Stages typically: Visit → Product View → Add to Cart → Checkout → Purchase. Each stage has a measurable drop-off rate.

## CPA (Cost Per Acquisition / Action)
The total cost divided by the number of acquisitions (sign-ups, purchases, leads). Preferred over CPC when the goal is a specific downstream action, not just click-through.

## CPC (Cost Per Click)
The amount paid for each click on a digital advertisement. Calculated as total ad spend divided by number of clicks. Used in paid search (Google Ads) and social advertising.

## CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
The systematic practice of increasing the percentage of visitors who complete a desired goal. Involves A/B testing, UX research, funnel analysis, and iterative experimentation.

## DTC (Direct-to-Consumer)
A business model where brands sell products directly to end consumers, bypassing traditional retail intermediaries. Common in apparel, beauty, and consumer goods.

## GA4 (Google Analytics 4)
The latest version of Google Analytics, using an event-based data model instead of session-based. Required for understanding user journeys across web and app.

## GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)
The total value of merchandise sold through a marketplace or platform over a certain period, before deductions. Does not necessarily equal revenue if platform takes commission.

## Heatmap
A data visualization overlay showing where users click, scroll, and move their mouse on a page. Tools include Hotjar, FullStory, and Microsoft Clarity. Used to identify UX friction and optimize page layouts.

## IMS (Inventory Management System)
Software that tracks inventory levels, orders, sales, and deliveries. In e-commerce, IMS integration ensures product availability is accurate across all sales channels.

## LCP (Largest Contentful Paint)
A Core Web Vital measuring when the largest element (image or text block) becomes visible. Target: < 2.5 seconds. Poor LCP is a common cause of high bounce rates.

## MAU (Monthly Active Users)
The number of unique users who engage with a platform within a 30-day period. A key growth metric for marketplaces and subscription e-commerce.

## Merchant of Record (MoR)
A legal entity that sells products on behalf of a brand, handling payment processing, tax collection, and consumer regulations. Used for cross-border expansion. Examples: Stripe Atlas, Gumroad, Lemon.io.

## MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)
The smallest quantity a supplier or brand will sell per order. Critical in wholesale B2B e-commerce and private-label sourcing.

## MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
The predictable revenue a business expects to receive every month. Core metric for subscription e-commerce businesses (SaaS, subscription boxes, membership sites).

## Multi-Channel Fulfillment
Using multiple fulfillment methods (in-house warehouse, 3PL, dropshipping, marketplace) to fulfill orders. Requires inventory allocation logic to prevent overselling.

## Personalization Engine
Software that uses behavioral data, purchase history, and contextual signals to deliver tailored product recommendations, content, or pricing to individual users. Examples: Nosto, Dynamic Yield, Salesforce Commerce Cloud Einstein.

## PIM (Product Information Management)
Centralized system for managing product data: descriptions, images, specifications, pricing, and variants. Ensures consistent data across all sales channels. Leading PIMs: Akeneo, inRiver, Salsify.

## PLP (Product Listing Page)
The page displaying a list/category of products, typically with filters, sorting, pagination, and thumbnails. PLP UX directly impacts browse-to-cart conversion rate.

## PDP (Product Detail Page)
The individual product page containing images, description, pricing, variants (size/color), reviews, and add-to-cart functionality. The primary conversion page.

## POS (Point of Sale)
The system used to process transactions in physical retail. Modern cloud POS (Shopify POS, Square) integrates with e-commerce for unified inventory and order management.

## Quoting / Request a Quote
B2B e-commerce workflow where buyers submit quantity requirements and receive a custom price before ordering. Common in wholesale, manufacturing, and industrial supply.

## RMA (Return Merchandise Authorization)
The process for handling product returns. Includes issuing an authorization number, return shipping label, refund/replacement decision, and restocking. RMA rate is a key product quality signal.

## ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
Revenue generated for every dollar spent on advertising. Calculated as revenue / ad spend. A ROAS of 5:1 means $5 revenue per $1 ad spend. Target ROAS varies by industry and margin.

## SaaS (Subscription as a Service)
Pricing model where customers pay recurring fees for software access. In e-commerce context: subscription boxes (BarkBox, Dollar Shave Club) or platform fees (Shopify Plus).

## SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)
A unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to each product variant (e.g., size/color combination). Essential for inventory tracking, order processing, and reporting.

## SSP (Supply-Side Platform)
Technology platform used by publishers and e-commerce sites to sell display and video ad inventory programmatically. Connects to ad exchanges for real-time bidding.

## Subscription Commerce
Business model where customers pay recurring fees for regular product deliveries or service access. Categories: curated boxes, consumable refills, content memberships, software licenses.

## UTM Parameters
Text tags appended to URLs to track traffic source, medium, and campaign in analytics. Format: `utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=spring-sale`. Essential for attributing conversions to marketing activities.

## Vendor-Managed Inventory (VMI)
Supplier-driven inventory replenishment model where the vendor monitors and replenishes stock based on actual sales data, reducing stockouts for the retailer.
