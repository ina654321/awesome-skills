# Real-World Examples

## 10.1 Shrink Reduction in a 50-Store Retail Chain

**Scenario:** A specialty apparel retailer discovers shrink has increased from 1.2% to 2.1% over 6 months, representing $340K in losses. Loss Prevention investigation suggests both external theft and internal collusion.

**Approach:**
1. **Diagnosis:** Conduct a shrink audit across all 50 stores. Category analysis shows 65% of shrink concentrated in denim, outerwear, and activewear — all high-theft categories. Store-level analysis identifies 3 stores with disproportionately high shrink.

2. **Intervention - Physical Security:**
   - Upgrade to RFID EAS tags in high-shrink categories (was RF tags only)
   - Install concealed cameras at high-risk areas identified via footage review
   - Add locked peg fixtures for small high-value items (fleece jackets)

3. **Intervention - Operational Controls:**
   - Implement mandatory bag check policy for all associates leaving the building
   - Require dual authorization for all RTVs (returns to vendor) over $500
   - Add daily exception reporting review by store manager and district LP
   - Randomize closing cash procedures weekly to prevent predictable patterns

4. **Intervention - Staffing & Culture:**
   - Partner with local law enforcement for quarterly training on organized retail crime (ORC)
   - Train all associates on "see something, say something" protocol
   - Establish anonymous tip line with incentive program ($50-$200 per actionable tip)

5. **Results:** Shrink reduced to 1.4% within 4 months. Four internal theft cases were identified and prosecuted. Total annual savings: approximately $250K. Shrink remained below 1.5% for the following 18 months.

## 10.2 Launching BOPIS in 30 Days

**Scenario:** A regional grocery chain (22 stores) wants to launch Buy Online Pick-Up In-Store to compete with Walmart and Target. Currently no omnichannel capability. Must be live within 30 days.

**Approach:**
1. **Week 1 — Assessment:**
   - Audit each store's readiness: receiving area size, staff availability, POS system capability
   - Select 5 pilot stores with best operational performance
   - Evaluate order management system (OMS) options — chose Shopify POS integration for speed

2. **Week 2 — Setup:**
   - Configure BOPIS in OMS: store-level inventory visibility, hold duration (4 hours), notification triggers
   - Define fulfillment SLA: orders ready within 2 hours during store hours
   - Create "BOPIS Zone" in each pilot store: dedicated pickup area with signage and staging shelves
   - Train store managers and designated fulfillment associates (2 per store)

3. **Week 3 — Testing:**
   - Internal testing: place test orders, pick in-store, evaluate accuracy and timing
   - Test communication flows: SMS notification on order ready, "what to bring" messaging
   - Test exception handling: what if item is damaged, out of stock, or order not found?

4. **Week 4 — Launch & Monitor:**
   - Soft launch with 50% of online SKUs (excluding alcohol, age-restricted items)
   - Monitor fulfillment time-to-ready hourly; target <90 minutes average
   - Set up daily standup with operations, IT, and store managers
   - Capture customer feedback via post-pickup SMS survey

5. **Results:** BOPIS launched in 5 pilot stores. Average fulfillment time: 67 minutes. Customer satisfaction score: 4.6/5.0. Expanded to all 22 stores within 60 days of original 30-day target.

## 10.3 Inventory Crisis: DC Fire Recovery

**Scenario:** A DC fire destroys 40% of inventory for the holiday season (mid-September). Holiday orders have already been placed by stores. Normal lead time is 8 weeks — too long for seasonal merchandise.

**Approach:**
1. **Immediate (Days 1-3):**
   - Activate emergency PO process with top 10 suppliers for immediate replacement
   - Negotiate air freight vs. ocean: accept 3x cost increase to get goods in 2 weeks
   - Freeze all non-essential orders to redirect vendor capacity to priority items
   - Contact insurance carrier and begin loss documentation

2. **Short-term (Days 4-21):**
   - Redirect in-transit shipments from other DCs to affected stores
   - Negotiate with suppliers to share excess inventory from other retailer's orders
   - Extend holiday season timeline by reducing promotional cadence (less markdown)
   - Communicate with stores: reduce order quantities per store to match available supply

3. **Mid-term (Days 22-60):**
   - Work with merchants to substitute unavailable SKUs with similar in-stock items
   - Source from secondary vendors with faster production (domestic vs. overseas)
   - Implement packaway strategy: store excess seasonal inventory for Q1

4. **Recovery Results:** 78% of lost holiday inventory replaced within 6 weeks. Holiday season revenue within 8% of original plan. Insurance claim filed for $2.1M replacement cost. Root cause analysis led to new DC fire suppression system installation.

## 10.4 Store Opening: New 15,000 sq ft Flagship

**Scenario:** A beauty brand is opening its first flagship store in a major mall. Grand opening is scheduled for a major shopping holiday. Must open on time with fully trained staff and complete merchandising.

**Approach:**
1. **12 Weeks Out — Planning:**
   - Finalize floor plan and fixture order (14-week lead time for custom fixtures)
   - Begin hiring: 25 associates needed across cashier, beauty advisor, stock, and management roles
   - Create training curriculum: POS system, product knowledge, customer service standards, loss prevention

2. **8 Weeks Out — Construction:**
   - Weekly site visits to monitor build-out progress
   - Coordinate with mall on HVAC, electrical, and signage approvals
   - Order inventory: initial floor stock + 4-week back stock
   - Begin associate onboarding: first 2 weeks classroom training, last 2 weeks in existing store

3. **2 Weeks Out — Merchandising:**
   - Install planograms with brand visual standards team
   - Merchandise all fixtures, set feature walls, install testers
   - Program POS system with all SKUs, pricing, and promotional codes
   - Stock back room with 4-week supply per category
   - Conduct walk-through with all department managers, create punch list

4. **Opening Week:**
   - Soft opening to loyalty members only (1 day) to stress-test operations
   - Staff all shifts with double coverage for first week
   - Hourly inventory checks on top 20 SKUs to prevent OOS
   - Evening recovery meetings: what sold, what needs replenishment, customer feedback

5. **Results:** Store opened on schedule. Grand opening weekend revenue: $127K (exceeded $100K target). Customer NPS: 72. On-shelf availability maintained above 96% through opening month.

## 10.5 Implementing Cycle Counting Across 80 Stores

**Scenario:** A home goods retailer discovers system inventory accuracy is only 87% due to years of unchecked receiving errors and damage write-offs. This causes chronic OOS and overstock situations, costing an estimated $500K annually in missed sales and excess markdowns.

**Approach:**
1. **Phase 1 — Categorization (Weeks 1-4):**
   - Classify all SKUs into A/B/C categories using velocity and cost data
     - A-class: top 20% of SKUs by revenue — count weekly
     - B-class: middle 30% — count monthly
     - C-class: bottom 50% — count quarterly
   - Identify which SKUs have the largest cumulative variance historically

2. **Phase 2 — Process Redesign (Weeks 5-8):**
   - Mandatory barcode scanning at receiving for all incoming POs
   - Two-person receiving check: one scans, one verifies physical count
   - Damage write-off must be logged within 24 hours with photo evidence
   - Standardize stockroom: ABC-labeled bins, perpetual location labels

3. **Phase 3 — Training & Accountability (Weeks 9-12):**
   - Train all store managers and stock leads on cycle count procedures
   - Assign inventory accuracy KPI to store manager performance reviews
   - Create weekly exception report: stores with variance >2% require written corrective action plan
   - Regional manager reviews inventory accuracy metrics monthly

4. **Phase 4 — Continuous Improvement (Ongoing):**
   - Monthly root cause analysis on variances >$500
   - Quarterly recalibration of ABC category thresholds
   - Annual full-store wall-to-wall count

5. **Results:** Inventory accuracy improved from 87% to 97.3% within 6 months. OOS rate decreased 40%. Excess inventory write-offs decreased 35%. Annual savings estimated at $380K in recovered sales and reduced markdowns.
