# Glossary

## ADT (Average Daily Transaction)
The average number of transactions processed per day. Used for labor scheduling, cash handling, and sales trend analysis.

## ASN (Advanced Shipping Notice)
A document sent by a supplier to a retailer before shipment, containing details of the incoming shipment (items, quantities, PO numbers). Enables receiving efficiency and dock scheduling.

## BOPIS (Buy Online Pick-Up In-Store)
An omnichannel fulfillment model where customers order online and pick up at a physical store location. Also called click-and-collect. Reduces last-mile delivery cost and drives store traffic.

## Cycle Count
A partial inventory count of a subset of SKUs, performed on a scheduled basis (daily, weekly, monthly) to verify system inventory accuracy without shutting down the store.

## DIFOT (Delivery In Full, On Time)
A supply chain metric measuring the percentage of orders delivered completely and on the promised date. Target: >95%. DIFOT directly impacts in-stock rates and customer satisfaction.

## Distribution Center (DC)
A centralized warehouse facility that receives bulk inventory from suppliers and redistributes to retail locations or directly to consumers. Also called fulfillment center or fulfillment warehouse.

## EAS (Electronic Article Surveillance)
A loss prevention system using RF (radio frequency) or AM (acoustic magnetic) tags attached to merchandise. Tags are deactivated at POS; any active tag passing through detection antennas triggers an alarm.

## EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)
The computer-to-computer exchange of business documents (purchase orders, invoices, shipping notices) in a standardized electronic format. Retail EDI standards include ANSI X12 and EDIFACT.

## Fill Rate
The percentage of customer demand for a product that is met from available inventory without stockouts. A 97% fill rate means 3% of demand went unfilled.

## FIS (Floor Inventory System)
The total sellable inventory physically located on the sales floor (as opposed to back stock). Managing FIS accuracy ensures items are findable by customers and associates.

## Floor Ready
Merchandise that is received, ticketed, and ready for the sales floor without additional handling. Floor-ready compliance measures how quickly received goods can be placed on shelves.

## GPI (Goods Processing Index)
A measure of receiving and processing efficiency. GPI = time to make goods floor-ready / standard time allowed. Values below 1.0 indicate above-standard productivity.

## Gross Margin Return on Inventory Investment (GMROI)
An inventory profitability metric: GMROI = Gross Margin $ / Average Inventory Cost. Shows how much gross profit each dollar of inventory generates. Target varies by category (apparel: 2.0+, grocery: 0.8-1.2).

## Hard Tags / Soft Tags
Hard tags are physical EAS tags requiring a detacher for removal (used on apparel, electronics). Soft tags are adhesive labels embedded in packaging (used on cosmetics, media). Both must be deactivated or removed at POS.

## Hold (Customer Order Hold)
Inventory allocated to a specific customer order (BOPIS, special order, SFS) that cannot be sold or used for other purposes until the hold expires or is released.

## IPI (Inventory Performance Index)
A composite metric combining inventory turn, in-stock rate, and fill rate to assess overall inventory management effectiveness. Used by brands to evaluate retailer partners.

## Inventory Turnover
The number of times inventory is sold and replaced over a period. Calculated as Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory Value. Higher turns indicate efficient inventory management.

## Lost Time Injury (LTI)
A workplace injury that results in an employee being unable to work the next scheduled shift. Tracked as LTIR (Lost Time Injury Rate) = LTIs × 200,000 / hours worked. A key safety KPI.

## Markdown
A permanent price reduction applied to merchandise that is not selling at full price. Types: permanent markdown (clearance), temporary markdown (promotional). Markdown planning is critical to margin management.

## Merchandise Flow
The end-to-end movement of goods: from supplier → DC → store receiving → back stock → floor → customer purchase → stock room processing.

## Min/Max Inventory
A replenishment method setting minimum (reorder point) and maximum (target stock level) quantities for each SKU. When inventory hits min, a reorder is triggered to bring it to max.

## Mosaic / Retail Mosaic
A visual merchandising approach using product groupings, signage, and fixture placement to tell a brand story and guide the customer journey through the store.

## MRP (Materials Requirements Planning)
A system that calculates the materials and components needed to manufacture a product based on the master production schedule. In retail context, similar logic applies to replenishment planning.

## On-Shelf Availability (OSA)
The percentage of time a product is available on the shelf for purchase when a customer wants it. OSA target is typically 95%+. Monitored via shelf audits and RFID/sensor data.

## OOS (Out of Stock)
A condition where a product has no available inventory and cannot be fulfilled. OOS events directly reduce sales and can drive customers to competitors.

## P&D (Put-and-Display)
A receiving and merchandising process where received goods are immediately priced, tagged, and placed on the sales floor rather than stored in back stock.

## Packaway
Merchandise strategy where excess inventory is stored in back stock or off-site until needed, as opposed to immediately placing on floor. Used to manage overflow from strong sell-through.

## PDA (Personal Digital Assistant)
A handheld device used by retail associates for inventory counts, price checks, receiving, and stock room management. Modern equivalents: Zebra scanners, mobile POS.

## Planogram (POG)
A visual diagram showing the placement of products on retail shelves. Planograms dictate SKU facings, shelf positions, and fixture layout. Governed by merchandising standards for brand consistency.

## POR (Purchase Order Request)
An internal requisition form requesting authorization to order merchandise from a vendor. Reviewed by merchandising and planning teams before PO issuance.

## Price Integrity
The accuracy and consistency of pricing across all channels and touchpoints. Price integrity failures (wrong shelf tag, mismatched online price) create customer trust issues and regulatory risk.

## Put-to-Light
A fulfillment technology using light indicators and digital displays to guide pickers to the correct items and quantities in a warehouse or stock room. Common in BOPIS and SFS operations.

## Receiving
The process of accepting incoming shipments, verifying quantities against PO, checking for damage, and entering goods into the inventory system. The first step in merchandise flow.

## Retail Loss Prevention (LP)
The department and practices focused on preventing theft (external and internal), fraud, and operational losses. Combines technology (EAS, CCTV), processes (audits), and people (LP agents).

## RTV (Return to Vendor)
The process of returning unsold or defective merchandise to the supplier. RTV authorization is required; products must be in original condition. RTVs affect supplier relationships and chargeback exposure.

## SFS (Ship From Store)
An omnichannel fulfillment model where online orders are picked and shipped directly from a retail store location, rather than from a DC. Extends store inventory to online channels.

## Shrink / Shrinkage
The difference between recorded inventory and actual physical inventory, expressed as a percentage of total retail value. Causes: theft (external/internal), administrative errors, supplier fraud, damage. Target shrink: <1.5%.

## SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)
A unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to each product variant (size/color/style). The fundamental unit for inventory tracking, purchasing, and sales analysis.

## SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)
A documented step-by-step process for performing a retail operation (receiving, opening, closing, customer returns). SOPs ensure consistency and compliance across shifts and locations.

## Sourcing Lead Time
The total time from placing a PO to receiving merchandise in the store/DC. Includes production time, transit time, and receiving/processing time. Critical for seasonal and trend merchandise.

## SSP (Shelf Share Percentage)
The percentage of shelf space a brand or category occupies relative to total shelf space. Used in category management negotiations with retailers.

## Stock Room
The back-of-house storage area holding excess inventory not currently on the sales floor. Also called stockroom, back room, or reserve stock area.

## Stock Transfer
The movement of merchandise from one retail location to another, or between DC and store. Transfer requests are generated to balance inventory based on sales patterns.

## Ticketing
The process of attaching price labels, EAS tags, and any required promotional signage to merchandise after receiving. Often done at the receiving dock before items go to floor or stock room.

## Till / Cash Drawer
The physical cash container in a POS register, containing the starting cash, transaction cash received, and ending cash. Reconciled at shift change and store close.

## TOC (Theory of Constraints)
A management approach applied to retail: identify the bottleneck in the fulfillment process (e.g., receiving, picking, shipping) and focus improvement efforts there to maximize throughput.

## Unit Control
The management of individual item quantities and their movement through the supply chain. Unit control systems track every unit from PO through sale.

## Virtual Inventory
The total available inventory across all channels and locations that can theoretically be sold or allocated, before physical constraints are applied. Used in omnichannel allocation algorithms.

## Visual Merchandising
The practice of optimizing the physical presentation of products in-store to maximize sales. Includes window displays, mannequins, product grouping, signage, and fixture placement.
