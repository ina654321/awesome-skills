# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Supplier Disruption Response

**Customer:** "Our primary supplier for electronic components just notified us of a 4-week production shutdown. We have 3 weeks of inventory. What's our best course of action?"

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> This is a critical supply chain disruption requiring immediate action:
> 
> 1. **Immediate Actions (0-48 hours):**
>    - Activate emergency inventory buffer
>    - Contact alternate qualified suppliers
>    - Assess customer order priority
>    - Calculate exact exposure window
> 2. **Short-term Response (Week 1):**
>    - Expedite orders with alternate suppliers
>    - Negotiate partial shipments
>    - Implement allocation for customers
>    - Consider air freight for critical needs
> 3. **Medium-term Strategy:**
>    - Qualify secondary suppliers
>    - Negotiate longer contracts with backup
>    - Increase safety stock permanently
>    - Design alternate products
> 
> **Risk Mitigation for Future:**
> - Dual-source critical components
> - Maintain 4-6 weeks safety stock for single-source
> - Develop supplier scorecard tracking
> - Quarterly supply chain risk reviews

## 9.2 Demand Volatility Management

**Customer:** "Our Q4 orders exceeded forecast by 340%. We missed $2M in revenue and damaged customer relationships. How do we prevent this?"

**Expert:**
> **Root Cause Analysis:**
> 
> This typically stems from multiple factors:
> 
> 1. **Forecasting Issues:**
>    - Not incorporating market signals
>    - Siloed planning (no S&OP)
>    - Reliance on historical data only
> 2. **Supply Chain Responsiveness:**
>    - Long lead times limiting flexibility
>    - Inflexible supplier contracts
>    - Capacity constraints
> 3. **Communication Gaps:**
>    - Sales not communicating pipeline
>    - Marketing not sharing campaign plans
>    - Operations not providing feedback
> 
> **Improvement Plan:**
> - Implement rolling monthly forecasts
> - Establish weekly S&OP meetings
> - Create demand sensing process
> - Develop scenario planning capability
> - Increase supply chain flexibility
> 
> **Metrics to Track:**
> - Forecast accuracy (MAPE)
> - Fill rate performance
> - Inventory turnover
> - Revenue vs. forecast variance

## 9.3 International Logistics Challenge

**Customer:** "We're expanding to European markets. What do we need to know about customs, tariffs, and compliance?"

**Expert:**
> **International Expansion Requirements:**
> 
> 1. **Customs and Tariffs:**
>    - HS code classification accuracy
>    - FTA eligibility (USMCA, EU-Korea, etc.)
>    - duty calculation and optimization
>    - Bonded warehouse options
> 2. **Compliance Requirements:**
>    - CE marking for EU
>    - UKCA marking for UK
>    - REACH compliance (chemicals)
>    - GDPR for data handling
> 3. **Logistics Considerations:**
>    - Incoterms selection (DDP vs DAP)
>    - Carrier capabilities
>    - Transit times and reliability
>    - Last-mile delivery in EU markets
> 
> **Recommended Approach:**
> - Engage customs broker early
> - Set up VAT registration in key markets
> - Use authorized economic operator (AEO) status
> - Implement multi-currency pricing
> - Local compliance review for each country

## 9.4 E-commerce Fulfillment Optimization

**Customer:** "Our e-commerce business is growing 80% YoY but fulfillment costs are eating profits. How do we scale efficiently?"

**Expert:**
> **Fulfillment Optimization Strategy:**
> 
> 1. **Network Optimization:**
>    - Evaluate distributed fulfillment model
>    - Strategic positioning near customers
>    - 3PL vs. in-house analysis
>    - Automation ROI assessment
> 2. **Cost Reduction Levers:**
>    - Negotiate carrier contracts
>    - Optimize packaging (dimensional weight)
>    - Batch shipments efficiently
>    - Implement order batching
> 3. **Technology Investment:**
>    - WMS with wave planning
>    - Real-time inventory visibility
>    - Automated picking systems
>    - Returns management automation
> 
> **Key Metrics:**
> - Cost per order
> - Order cycle time
> - Shipping accuracy
> - Return rate and processing cost
> - Inventory accuracy
> 
> **Scalability Checkpoints:**
> - Test systems at 2x current volume
> - Have surge capacity agreements
> - Plan for peak seasons

## 9.5 Sustainability in Supply Chain

**Customer:** "Our board wants us to achieve carbon-neutral supply chain by 2030. Where do we start?"

**Expert:**
> **Sustainability Roadmap:**
> 
> 1. **Baseline Measurement:**
>    - Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions inventory
>    - Life cycle assessment (LCA)
>    - Supplier emissions data collection
>    - Identify major emission sources
> 2. **Quick Wins (Year 1):**
>    - Warehouse LED lighting
>    - EV fleet transition
>    - Route optimization
>    - Packaging reduction
> 3. **Strategic Initiatives:**
>    - Supplier sustainability requirements
>    - Circular economy programs
>    - Renewable energy procurement
>    - Low-carbon transportation modes
> 
> **Certification and Reporting:**
> - ISO 14001 certification
> - Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi)
> - CDP reporting
> - EcoVadis assessment
> 
> **Stakeholder Engagement:**
> - Customer requirements alignment
> - Investor ESG expectations
> - Employee engagement
> - Regulatory compliance monitoring
