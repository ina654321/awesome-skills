# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Procedural Pitfalls

| # | Pitfall | Severity | Prevention |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | **Failing to disclose conflicts** | 🔴 High | Complete thorough disclosure checklist |
| 2 | **Exceeding arbitral authority** | 🔴 High | Stay within four corners of submission |
| 3 | **Not scheduling adequate discovery** | 🔴 High | Allow sufficient time per complexity |
| 4 | **Rushing the hearing** | 🟡 Medium | Allow full presentation, no time pressure |
| 5 | **Losing neutrality (showing favoritism)** | 🔴 High | Equal treatment, equal time for both sides |

## 10.2 Legal Errors

⚠️ **Awards Subject to Vacatur:**
- Rendering beyond submission (award more than requested)
- Failure to consider dispositive issues
- Evident partiality (e.g., undisclosed relationship with counsel)
- Corruption or fraud
- Refusing to hear material evidence

## 10.3 Award Drafting Errors

⚠️ **Common Mistakes:**
- Awarding more than prayed for
- Failing to address all claims
- Ambiguous language causing enforcement disputes
- Not reserving jurisdiction for post-award matters
- Late award without extension (grounds to vacate)

## 10.4 Ethical Violations

| Standard | Rule | Requirement |
|----------|------|-------------|
| **Neutrality** | Canon I | Remain neutral throughout |
| **Disclosure** | Canon II | All potential conflicts |
| **Competence** | Canon III | Adequate subject matter expertise |
| **Ex Parte** | Canon IV | No unilateral communications |
| **Fees** | Canon V | Reasonable, not contingency |

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

- [ ] All conflicts disclosed and waived
- [ ] Procedural orders issued timely
- [ ] Parties given equal opportunity to present
- [ ] All claims addressed in award
- [ ] Award rendered within timeline or extended
- [ ] Award format meets institutional requirements
- [ ] Confidentiality provisions followed
- [ ] Fee allocation addressed if disputed
