# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Complex Commercial Dispute

**User:** "Two LLC members have a 50/50 deadlock. One claims breach of fiduciary duty. The other says the first is blocking operations intentionally. How do we structure the arbitration?"

**Expert:**
> **Commercial Deadlock Arbitration:**
>
> | Issue | Party A Claim | Party B Response |
> |-------|---------------|------------------|
> | Deadlock | B blocking decisions | A refusing to approve budget |
> | Breach of fiduciary | B misappropriated funds | A authorized all payments |
> | Valuation | Company worth $5M | Company worth $1.5M |
>
> **Procedural Approach:**
> 1. Bifurcate: Resolve fiduciary duty claim first (affects valuation)
> 2. Appoint single expert to value company if buyout is remedy
> 3. Consider: Is this really deadlock, or one party's breach?
>
> **Key Evidence:**
> - Bank statements and cancelled checks
> - Meeting minutes showing pattern of obstruction
> - Communications between members
> - Financial projections and budget disputes
>
> **Possible Outcomes:**
> - Buyout at fair market value
> - Company dissolution and asset distribution
> - Injunction against obstructionist behavior
> - Damages for breach of fiduciary duty

## 9.2 Employment Discrimination Case

**User:** "An employee claims wrongful termination based on age (60 years old, 25 years tenure). Employer says performance issues. How does the arbitration proceed?"

**Expert:**
> **Employment Arbitration Setup:**
>
> | Element | Consideration |
> |---------|---------------|
> | **ADA/ADEA claims** | Arbitrator must understand protected classes |
> | **Burden of proof** | Employee shows prima facie case; employer articulates legitimate reason |
> | **Documentation** | Performance reviews, PIPs, termination memo |
> | **Remedies** | Back pay, front pay, punitive damages (check arbitration agreement) |
>
> **Arbitration Protocol:**
> 1. **Discovery**: Full personnel file, HR communications, comparator evidence
> 2. **Expert witnesses**: Vocational expert (mitigation), statistical expert (pattern evidence)
> 3. **Damages cap**: Check FAA limits vs. employment arbitration carve-outs
>
> **Decision Framework:**
> - Was termination based on age, not performance?
> - Were similarly-situated younger employees treated differently?
> - Did employer follow its own procedures?
> - Would injunction be appropriate vs. monetary relief?

## 9.3 Consumer Arbitration Issue

**User:** "A financial advisor arbitration has a class action waiver. The consumer class has 500 members. How does this work?"

**Expert:**
> **Class Arbitration Challenges:**
>
> 1. **Is class arbitration allowed?**
>    - Check arbitration agreement language
>    - *Stolt-Nielsen S.A. v. AnimalFeeds Int'l Corp.* (2010): Courts, not arbitrators, decide class
>    - *Green Tree Financial Corp. v. Bazzle* (2003) (overruled): Contract controls
>
> 2. **If class is allowed:**
>    - Bellwether proceedings (arbitrate 5-10 cases first)
>    - Common issue vs. individual damages
>    - Use statistical sampling for damages
>
> 3. **If class is not allowed:**
>    - Each consumer must arbitrate individually
>    - Ethically advise client of class waiver implications
>    - Consider opt-out provisions if any exist
>
> **Ethical Concern:** Individual arbitration may be cost-prohibitive — this is a known criticism of class waivers.
