# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Pre-Hearing Phase

```
Phase 1: Appointment & Engagement
├── Accept appointment from institution or parties
├── Execute neutrality agreement
├── Issue disclosure checklist to both parties
├── Review disclosures and assess challenges
└── Confirm jurisdiction and scope

Phase 2: Preliminary Procedures
├── Issue Procedural Order No. 1 (administrative)
│   ├── Hearing date, location, format
│   ├── Submission deadlines
│   ├── Discovery scope
│   └── Confidentiality requirements
├── Receive position statements
├── Conduct preliminary conference call
└── Issue Procedural Order No. 2 (substantive)

Phase 3: Discovery & Submissions
├── Exchange documents per agreed scope
├── Conduct depositions if permitted
├── Exchange expert reports (if applicable)
├── Joint exhibit list preparation
└── Pre-hearing brief submission
```

## 8.2 Hearing Phase

```
Day of Hearing Protocol:
├── 30 min before: Review room setup, materials
├── 15 min before: Parties arrive, introductions
│
├── Opening
│   ├── Arbitrator introductions and disclosures
│   ├── Confirm procedural matters
│   └── Party opening statements
│
├── Party A Case-in-Chief
│   ├── Direct examination of witnesses
│   ├── Cross-examination by Party B
│   └── Redirect/re-recross as needed
│
├── Party B Case-in-Chief
│   ├── Direct examination of witnesses
│   ├── Cross-examination by Party A
│   └── Redirect/re-recross as needed
│
├── Closing Arguments
│   ├── Party A closing
│   ├── Party B closing
│   └── Party A rebuttal
│
└── Post-Hearing
    ├── Post-hearing brief schedule
    ├── Jurisdiction to reopen?
    └── Adjourn — award within agreed time
```

## 8.3 Award Phase

1. **Draft Award**: Write within agreed timeline (typically 30 days)
2. **Circulate Draft**: Check for clerical errors (most rules require this)
3. **Final Award**: Sign and issue to parties and institution
4. **Interpretation**: Address any requests for clarification
5. **Post-Award**: Retain jurisdiction only if reserved

## 8.4 Award Enforcement

- File petition to confirm in appropriate district court
- Opposing party has 3 months to challenge under FAA §10
- Grounds for vacatur: corruption, fraud, evident partiality, exceeding powers
- New York Convention: Enforcement in 170+ signatory nations
