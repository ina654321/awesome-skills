# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Regulatory Violations

| # | Pitfall | Severity | Prevention |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | **Missing regulatory changes** | 🔴 High | Monitor regulatory agencies weekly |
| 2 | **Inadequate training** | 🔴 High | Annual refresher, role-specific content |
| 3 | **Third-party non-compliance** | 🔴 High | Contractual requirements, audit rights |
| 4 | **Incomplete incident response** | 🔴 High | Documented procedures, test annually |
| 5 | **Poor documentation** | 🟡 Medium | Contemporaneous records, audit trail |

## 10.2 Program Design Failures

⚠️ **FSGG §8B2.1 Violations:**
- Compliance program not tailored to risk profile
- Insufficient authority and resources
- Inadequate screening of employees
- Failure to respond to detected violations
- Inadequate communication throughout organization

## 10.3 Reporting Failures

⚠️ **Hotline/Whistleblower Issues:**
- Anonymous reporting channel not available
- Retaliation against reporters (SOX §806, Dodd-Frank)
- Inadequate follow-up on reported issues
- Failure to escalate to board level when needed

## 10.4 Data Privacy Gaps

⚠️ **Common GDPR/CCPA Failures:**
- Missing lawful basis documentation
- Inadequate consent mechanisms
- No data subject rights process
- Missing privacy notices or policies
- Cross-border transfer issues (SCCs not in place)
- Data retention: keeping data too long

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Risk assessment updated annually
- [ ] All applicable regulations mapped
- [ ] Policies reviewed and updated within 90 days of regulatory change
- [ ] Training completion tracked (100%)
- [ ] Third-party due diligence documented
- [ ] Incident response plan tested
- [ ] Hotline reporting tracked and resolved
- [ ] Board reporting on compliance metrics
- [ ] Disciplinary actions documented
- [ ] Control testing evidence retained
