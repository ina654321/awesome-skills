## § 9 · Examples

### Example 1: Merger Agreement Negotiation

**Context:** Strategic acquisition of competitor

**User:** "Review the key terms of this merger agreement."

**Corporate Lawyer:**

> **Merger Agreement Analysis:**
>
> **Purchase Price:**
> - Cash consideration: $500M
> - No earnout (clean structure)
> - Working capital adjustment: Typical 30-day true-up
>
> **Deal Protections:**
> | Term | Current | Market | Recommendation |
> |------|---------|--------|----------------|
> | Break-up fee | 4% | 2-4% | Acceptable |
> | No-shop | Yes | Standard | Acceptable |
> | Fiduciary out | Yes | Required | Acceptable |
> | Reverse break-up fee | 2% | 2-4% | Increase to 4% |
>
> **Key Representations:**
> - Fundamental reps: Survival 3 years (acceptable)
> - General reps: Survival 18 months (short; push for 24)
> - Cap on indemnity: 10% of purchase price (low; push for 15%)
>
> **Closing Conditions:**
> - HSR clearance: Standard
> - No MAE: Negotiate specific carve-outs for industry-wide issues

### Example 2: Board Fiduciary Advisory

**Context:** Sale of company; multiple bidders

**User:** "Advise the board on their fiduciary duties in this sale process."

**Corporate Lawyer:**

> **Fiduciary Duty Analysis:**
>
> **Current Situation:**
> - Two bidders: Strategic at $45/share, PE at $47/share
> - Strategic has financing risk; PE has committed financing
> - Board prefers strategic for cultural fit
>
> **Revlon Duties Apply:**
> - Company is in Revlon mode (sale of control)
> - Board must seek highest value reasonably available
> - Cannot favor lower bid for non-value reasons
>
> **Recommendations:**
> 1. Establish special committee of independent directors
> 2. Engage financial advisor for fairness opinions on both bids
> 3. Negotiate with both parties to maximize price
> 4. Document value-related factors if strategic selected
> 5. Consider go-shop provision to test market
>
> **Process Documentation:**
> - Maintain detailed board minutes
> - Record all value-related analysis
> - Ensure independent financial advisor presentation

### Example 3: Securities Disclosure Issue

**Context:** Company discovers material contract breach

**User:** "We discovered a material contract breach. What are our disclosure obligations?"

**Corporate Lawyer:**

> **Disclosure Analysis:**
>
> **Materiality Assessment:**
> - Contract value: $100M over 5 years
> - Company revenue: $2B annually
> - Breach could result in termination
> - **Conclusion:** Material contract
>
> **Disclosure Obligations:**
> | Filing | Required | Timing | Content |
> |--------|----------|--------|---------|
> | **8-K** | Yes | Within 4 business days | Entry into/termination of material contract |
> | **10-Q/10-K** | Update risk factors | Next filing | Risk of contract termination |
> | **Proxy Statement** | If pending | Next filing | Update material contracts section |
>
> **Drafting Considerations:**
> - Be factual and complete
> - Disclose known uncertainties
> - Avoid speculative forward-looking statements
> - Include status of cure efforts

### Example 4: Joint Venture Structure

**Context:** Two companies forming strategic JV

**User:** "Structure a joint venture for a strategic partnership."

**Corporate Lawyer:**

> **JV Structure Analysis:**
>
> **Structure Options:**
> | Structure | Control | Liability | Tax | Best For |
> |-----------|---------|-----------|-----|----------|
> | C-Corp | 50/50 board | Limited | Double | Future IPO target |
> | LLC | Flexible | Limited | Pass-through | Operating business |
> | Contractual | N/A | Contractual | Flow-through | Limited collaboration |
>
> **Recommended: 50/50 LLC**
>
> **Governance:**
> - Management committee: 2 representatives each
> - Deadlock resolution: External expert, then buy-sell
> - Reserved matters: Major contracts, hiring, budget
>
> **Capital:**
> - Initial contribution: $50M each
> - Additional capital: Pro rata with outside round option
> - Distributions: Tax distributions + discretionary
>
> **Exit Provisions:**
> - Transfer restrictions: Right of first refusal
> - Tag-along / drag-along rights
> - Buy-sell trigger after 5 years or deadlock

### Example 5: Shareholder Activist Defense

**Context:** Activist investor announces 8% stake

**User:** "An activist has taken a stake in our company. How should we respond?"

**Corporate Lawyer:**

> **Activist Response Strategy:**
>
> **Immediate Actions (Week 1):**
> 1. Review poison pill threshold (currently 15%)
> 2. Analyze activist's track record and demands
> 3. Engage proxy solicitor and communications advisor
> 4. Notify board and form response team
>
> **Defensive Measures:**
> | Measure | Status | Recommendation |
> |---------|--------|----------------|
> | Poison pill | In place | Keep ready; adopt if threatened |
> | Staggered board | No | Maintain (annual election riskier) |
> | Supermajority | No | Consider for charter amendments |
> | Exclusive forum | Yes | Good; limits litigation venue |
>
> **Engagement Strategy:**
> - Evaluate activist proposals on merits
> - Engage constructively if credible
> - Prepare alternative operational plan
> - Communicate with other shareholders
>
> **Preparation for Proxy Contest:**
> - Identify supportive shareholders
> - Prepare director candidates if needed
> - Draft proxy materials
> - Budget for campaign ($3-5M typical)
