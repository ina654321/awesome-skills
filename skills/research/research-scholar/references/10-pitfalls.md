# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns in Academic Research

| # | Anti-Pattern| Severity| Quick Fix|
|---|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Scope Creep** | 🔴 High | Limit proposals to 3-5 specific aims; avoid "kitchen sink" approaches |
| 2 | **Significance Neglect** | 🔴 High | Lead with importance, not methodology — reviewers need motivation first |
| 3 | **Underpowered Studies** | 🔴 High | Always run power analysis before finalizing sample size |
| 4 | **Predatory Journal Targeting** | 🟡 Medium | Check indexing, impact factor legitimacy before submission |
| 5 | **Generic Cover Letters** | 🟡 Medium | Customize each cover letter — explain why THIS journal for THIS paper |

## 10.2 Problematic Statements

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead |
|----------|----------------|
| "We believe this paper is important for the field" | "This study is the first to demonstrate X mechanism in Y disease" |
| "The novelty is insufficient" | "The contribution would be enhanced if authors demonstrated X" |
| "This is a breakthrough" | Reserve for paradigm-shifting findings; use "new," "significant" |

## 10.3 Common Research Mistakes

### Writing Issues
- Passive voice throughout
- Results not clearly linked to claims
- Discussion repeats results instead of interpreting
- References incomplete or outdated

### Methodology Issues
- No power analysis
- Inappropriate statistical tests
- Missing controls
- Insufficient detail for replication

### Submission Issues
- Not following journal guidelines
- Cover letter generic or missing
- Response to reviewers combative
- Missing required forms

## 10.4 Grant Writing Pitfalls

### Common Problems
- Aims that depend on each other (if Aim 1 fails, whole grant fails)
- No preliminary data
- Unclear hypothesis
- Unrealistic budget
- No significance statement

### Red Flags in Proposals
- No innovation statement
- Weak experimental design
- No statistical plan
- Vague timeline

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

Before manuscript submission:
- [ ] Abstract accurately reflects paper
- [ ] Introduction clearly states gap
- [ ] Methods detailed enough to replicate
- [ ] Results support conclusions
- [ ] Discussion interprets, not just repeats
- [ ] All citations present and relevant
- [ ] Figures clear and properly labeled
- [ ] Grammar and style consistent
