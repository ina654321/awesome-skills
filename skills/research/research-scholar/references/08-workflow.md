# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Paper Publication Process

### Phase 1: Research Design & Execution (Ongoing)

**Step 1.1 - Literature Review**
- Search PubMed, Web of Science, Scopus
- Read 50+ relevant papers
- Identify gaps in knowledge

**Step 1.2 - Hypothesis Formation**
- Develop specific, testable hypotheses
- Ensure novelty and significance
- Get feedback from colleagues

**Step 1.3 - Study Design**
- Determine sample size via power analysis
- Select appropriate controls
- Plan statistical analyses

**Step 1.4 - Execution**
- Conduct experiments with proper controls
- Document everything
- Analyze data regularly

### Phase 2: Manuscript Drafting

**Step 2.1 - Methods Section (First)**
- Write while work is fresh
- Be detailed enough for replication
- Include all necessary details

**Step 2.2 - Results Section**
- Present data objectively
- Create clear figures and tables
- Include statistical results

**Step 2.3 - Introduction**
- Motivate the problem
- Cite prior work properly
- State hypothesis clearly

**Step 2.4 - Discussion**
- Interpret results
- Acknowledge limitations
- Future directions

### Phase 3: Submission & Revision

**Step 3.1 - Journal Selection**
- Match scope and impact factor
- Consider review speed
- Check acceptance rates

**Step 3.2 - Submission**
- Format according to guidelines
- Write compelling cover letter
- Submit with all required forms

**Step 3.3 - Review Response**
- Receive reviews (typically 2-4 weeks)
- Plan major/minor revision strategy
- Respond to each comment systematically

**Step 3.4 - Revision**
- Make all required changes
- Write detailed response letter
- Resubmit within deadline

## 8.2 Grant Application Process

### Phase 1: Funding Discovery (6-12 months before deadline)

**Step 1.1 - Identify Opportunities**
- Search Grants.gov, agency databases
- Review program priorities
- Align with your research area

**Step 1.2 - Assess Fit**
- Does your research match the FOA?
- What's the success rate?
- Do you have preliminary data?

### Phase 2: Proposal Development (4-6 months before)

**Step 2.1 - Specific Aims**
- Draft 1-page document
- Get feedback from colleagues
- Align with program priorities

**Step 2.2 - Research Strategy**
- Significance: Why does this matter?
- Innovation: What's new?
- Approach: How will you do it?

**Step 2.3 - Budget**
- Personnel costs
- Equipment and supplies
- F&A calculation

### Phase 3: Submission

**Step 3.1 - Internal Review**
- Have colleagues review
- Check compliance requirements
- Submit to sponsored programs

**Step 3.2 - Post-Submission**
- Respond to post-submission inquiries
- Prepare for panel review
- Wait for funding decision
