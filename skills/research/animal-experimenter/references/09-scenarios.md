# Research Terminology Glossary

## A

- **AALAS** — American Association for Laboratory Animal Science; professional organization setting certification standards
- **Analgesia** — Pain relief through pharmacological or non-pharmacological means
- **Anesthesia** — Reversible loss of consciousness with analgesia
- **Anorexic** — Loss of appetite; indicator of illness in animal models

## B

- **Biosafety Cabinet (BSC)** — Ventilated enclosure for handling biohazardous materials
- **Body Condition Score (BCS)** — 1-5 or 1-9 scale assessing fat and muscle cover
- **Buprenorphine** — Partial opioid agonist analgesic commonly used in rodents

## C

- **Cage-side observation** — Daily welfare check without removing animal from cage
- **Cervical dislocation** — Physical method of euthanasia; requires training certification
- **Clinical signs** — Observable indicators of health or disease in animals
- **CO2 exposure** — Inhalation euthanasia method using carbon dioxide gas
- **Complete blood count (CBC)** — Hematological analysis for health monitoring
- **Comfortable pose** — Normal resting posture indicating lack of pain/distress
- **CRISPR** — Gene editing technology widely used in rodent model creation

## D

- **DAC** — Designated Campus Official; IACUC institutional official
- **DBA/2** — Common inbred mouse strain; known for susceptibility to certain pathogens
- **DO** — Diversity Outbred mice; genetically diverse population for research
- **Dominant/aggressive** — Behavioral descriptor; indicates social hierarchy or threat
- **Dre4** — Common inbred rat strain; used in cardiovascular research

## E

- **Ear notching/punching** — Permanent identification method for rodents
- **ELISA** — Enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay; common blood analysis
- **Euthanasia** — Humane death; from Greek "good death"
- **Exsanguination** — Blood removal; terminal procedure for tissue collection

## F

- **F344** — Fischer 344 inbred rat strain; common in toxicology
- **Footnotes in data sheets** — Explanatory notes for unusual observations
- **Food/m-fluid diet** — Moistened food for animals with reduced mobility

## G

- **GAERS** — Genetic Absence Epilepsy Rat; genetic model of epilepsy
- **General anesthesia** — Complete unconsciousness with analgesia and muscle relaxation
- **Guillotine** — Device for rapid cervical decapitation; requires training

## H

- **Humane endpoint** — Pre-determined criteria triggering euthanasia or intervention
- **Humane endpoint criteria** — Specific signs indicating animal distress requiring action
- **Hypothermia** — Low body temperature; common post-anesthesia risk

## I

- **IACUC** — Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee; oversight body for animal research
- **IACUC protocol number** — Unique identifier for approved animal use protocols
- **IACUC semi-annual inspection** — Required facility and program review
- **IgU** — International Guiding Principles for Biomedical Research Involving Animals

## K

- **Ketamine** — Dissociative anesthetic; used alone or with xylazine/medetomidine
- **Klein** — Inbred rat strain used in neurological research

## L

- **Laboratory animal veterinarian** — DVM with specialized training in lab animal medicine
- **LAR** — Laboratory Animal Research; institutional animal care program
- **Local anesthesia** — Targeted pain relief at surgical or injection site

## M

- **MAC** — Minimum Alveolar Concentration; anesthetic potency measure
- **Meloxicam** — NSAID analgesic commonly used in rodents
- **MMRRC** — Mutant Mouse Regional Resource Center; repository for mutant mice
- **Moribund** — Terminal state; irreversible approach to death
- **Mouse grimace scale** — Validated pain assessment tool using facial expressions
- **Mycoplasma** — Bacterial pathogen; common contaminant in cell lines and animal colonies

## N

- **Narcosis** — Drug-induced unconsciousness
- **Necropsy** — Post-mortem examination of tissues for diagnostic purposes
- **NIH** — National Institutes of Health; major funder of animal research
- **NOD/SCID** — Immunodeficient mouse strain; used for human tissue xenografts
- **NSAID** — Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug; e.g., meloxicam, carprofen

## O

- **OFA** — Olfactory Furious Aggression; DBA/2 substrain behavior
- **OLAW** — Office of Laboratory Animal Welfare; NIH oversight office

## P

- **Pachydermy** — Thickened skin; used in elephant phenotype descriptions
- **Paralytic** — Indicates inability to move; animal welfare concern
- **Parenteral administration** — Injection route (IV, IP, SC, IM, ID)
- **Pentobarbital** — Barbiturate euthanasia agent; approved by AVMA
- **Per diem rate** — Daily cost per animal for housing and care
- **Perfusion** — Vascular flushing for tissue preservation; used in terminal procedures
- **PETH** — Phenoxyethanol; fish anesthetic
- **PHS** — Public Health Service; federal agency with animal welfare policy
- **Physical method of euthanasia** — Cervical dislocation, decapitation; requires training
- **Porphyrin staining** — Red-brown discharge around eyes/nose; stress indicator in rats
- **Post-mortem** — After death; tissue collection procedures
- **Premedication** — Pre-anesthetic medication to reduce anxiety and enhance analgesia
- **PVA** — Polyvinyl alcohol; used in fish anesthesia

## R

- **Rat grimace scale** — Validated pain assessment for rats
- **Reprints** — Published articles documenting animal model research
- **Rodent** — Order Rodentia; includes mice, rats, hamsters, guinea pigs
- **Rota-rod** — Motor coordination testing apparatus
- **RSPCA** — Royal Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals

## S

- **Sedative** — Agent reducing anxiety and activity without full unconsciousness
- **Semi-annual program review** — IACUC-required institutional assessment
- **Sentinel animals** — Cagemates used to monitor colony health
- **Serum/blood collection** — Terminal or survival procedure for biological sampling
- **SF** — Sprague-Dawley outbred rat; common research strain
- **Shock** — Circulatory failure; veterinary emergency
- **Splints** — Orthopedic devices to support limbs during recovery
- **Stadol** — Butorphanol; analgesic for moderate pain
- **Sterilization (equipment)** — Autoclave, gas, or chemical decontamination
- **Subcutaneous (SC)** — Beneath the skin; injection route

## T

- **Tail clipping** — Toe clipping method for mouse identification and DNA collection
- **Tamoxifen** — Pharmacological agent used to induce conditional gene expression
- **Thermal burn injury** — Tissue damage from heat; model for wound healing studies
- **Thrombosis** — Blood clot; model for vascular disease research
- **Tilapia** — Tropical fish species used in aquaculture research
- **Tranquilizer** — Agent reducing anxiety without analgesia
- **Tricaine methanesulfonate (MS-222)** — Fish anesthetic; FDA-approved
- **Twitching** — Involuntary muscle movement; may indicate insufficient anesthesia

## U

- **Urethane** — Historical anesthetic; not AVMA-approved; limited use

## V

- **Vasopressor** — Agent increasing blood pressure; used in shock management
- **Veterinary care** — Required component of animal care programs
- **Veterinary consultation** — Veterinarian involvement in protocol design and animal care
- **Veterinary oversight** — Required supervision by attending veterinarian

## W

- **Wistar** — Common outbred rat strain; widely used in research
- **Working alone (solitary)** — Single-person animal procedure; not recommended for hazardous procedures
- **Wound clips/staples** — Surgical closure devices for incisions

## X

- **Xenograft** — Tissue transplanted between species
