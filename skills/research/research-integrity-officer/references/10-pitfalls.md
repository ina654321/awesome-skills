# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns in Research Integrity

| # | Anti-Pattern| Severity| Quick Fix|
|---|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Proceeding without documentation** | 🔴 High | Every step must be documented contemporaneously — post-hoc rationalization is inadmissible |
| 2 | **Failing to preserve evidence** | 🔴 High | Issue immediate hold on relevant records; chain of custody begins at allegation |
| 3 | **Interviewing without protocol** | 🔴 High | Use structured interview guides; record or take detailed notes; inform of confidentiality limits |
| 4 | **Delayed response to allegation** | 🟡 Medium | 30-day inquiry deadline is firm — missing it invites ORI scrutiny |
| 5 | **Inadequate committee composition** | 🟡 Medium | Investigators must have relevant expertise and no conflicts of interest |

## 10.2 Problematic Practices

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead |
|----------|----------------|
| "We've looked into it informally and think there's nothing to the allegation." | "Following preliminary assessment on [date], we determined sufficient evidence warrants formal inquiry per 42 CFR 93.305." |
| "We don't need to preserve the evidence — we'll just review it." | "Immediate evidence hold issued; chain of custody initiated per institutional policy." |
| "The respondent seems credible — we can trust their explanation." | "Document respondent's explanation and any corroborating evidence; assess credibility systematically." |
| "This is just an authorship dispute, not misconduct." | "Assess whether this involves potential FFP; if unclear, proceed to inquiry." |

## 10.3 Common Investigation Mistakes

### Assessment Phase
- **Dismissal without review:** Not examining specific allegations
- **Over-reliance on respondent explanation:** Not verifying claims independently
- **Delayed assessment:** Allowing evidence to be lost

### Inquiry Phase
- **Insufficient documentation:** Not recording basis for decisions
- **Biased committee:** Including individuals with conflicts
- **Incomplete review:** Not examining all relevant evidence

### Investigation Phase
- **Inadequate interviews:** Not following up on discrepancies
- **Pre-determined outcome:** Looking for evidence to support initial hypothesis
- **Insufficient evidence:** Making findings without preponderance

### Reporting Phase
- **Missing ORI report:** Failing to report when required
- **Incomplete documentation:** Not retaining records properly
- **Delayed sanctions:** Not implementing in timely manner

## 10.4 Due Process Checklist

For every investigation, verify:
- [ ] Respondent received written notice of allegations
- [ ] Respondent had opportunity to respond before adverse finding
- [ ] Committee members had no conflicts of interest
- [ ] Decision based on preponderance of evidence
- [ ] All evidence documented and preserved
- [ ] Timeline requirements met (30-day inquiry, etc.)
- [ ] Appeal process available to respondent

## 10.5 Best Practices

1. **Document Everything:** Every step, every decision, every basis
2. **Preserve Evidence Early:** Act immediately when allegation received
3. **Follow the Process:** 42 CFR 93 timelines and procedures matter
4. **Protect Respondent:** Due process is essential and non-negotiable
5. **Seek Expert Help:** Complex cases need specialized expertise
