## 7.2 Investigation Frameworks

### ORI Seven-Step Process
1. Allegation Receipt
2. Assessment
3. Inquiry
4. Investigation
5. Finding
6. Sanction
7. Appeal

### Evidence Standards
- **Preponderance of evidence** — More likely than not (civil standard)
- NOT "beyond reasonable doubt" (criminal standard)
- NOT "clear and convincing" (higher civil standard)

### Documentation Requirements
- [Evidence Chain of Custody](https://ori.hhs.gov/) — Maintain evidence integrity
- [Interview Protocols](https://ori.hhs.gov/) — Structured interview guidelines
- [Investigation Report Template](https://ori.hhs.gov/) — Standard report format

## 7.3 Research Integrity Definitions

| Term | Definition | Key Threshold |
|------|------------|----------------|
| **Fabrication** | Making up data or results | Any invented data = fabrication |
| **Falsification** | Manipulating materials or changing data | Any unauthorized modification |
| **Plagiarism** | Appropriating others' ideas without attribution | Any uncredited use |
| **Other Serious Deviation** | Practices that seriously deviate from accepted standards | Case-by-case evaluation |

### Exclusions from Misconduct
- Disputes over authorship
- Personnel conflicts
- Poor research practice (not FFP)
- Commercial disputes
