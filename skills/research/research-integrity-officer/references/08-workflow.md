# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Misconduct Investigation Protocol

### Phase 1: Allegation Receipt & Assessment (Days 1-30)

**Step 1.1 - Receipt and Logging**
- Receive written allegation with specific details
- Assign case number
- Date stamp all documents

**Step 1.2 - Preliminary Assessment**
- Determine if allegation falls within definition of research misconduct
- Identify applicable federal regulations and funding sources
- Assess urgency and potential for evidence destruction

**Step 1.3 - Decision Point: Inquiry Warranted?**
- Document decision with rationale
- If YES → proceed to Phase 2
- If NO → close with documentation (no further action)

### Phase 2: Inquiry (Days 31-120)

**Step 2.1 - Committee Appointment**
- Appoint inquiry committee (1-3 qualified individuals)
- Ensure no conflicts of interest
- Document committee qualifications

**Step 2.2 - Evidence Review**
- Review all relevant documentation
- Conduct initial interviews (if appropriate)
- Assess allegation specificity and credibility

**Step 2.3 - Determination**
- Prepare written inquiry report
- Decide: sufficient evidence to warrant formal investigation?
- Document decision with evidence basis

**Step 2.4 - Decision Point: Investigation Warranted?**
- YES → proceed to Phase 3
- NO → close with documentation; notify respondent

### Phase 3: Investigation (Days 121-210)

**Step 3.1 - Committee Expansion**
- Appoint investigation committee (3+ qualified individuals)
- Include technical expertise relevant to case
- Ensure independence from inquiry committee

**Step 3.2 - Comprehensive Evidence Review**
- Full document review
- Interview complainant, respondent, witnesses
- Obtain expert opinions if needed

**Step 3.3 - Investigation Report**
- Prepare detailed report with findings
- Include evidence supporting conclusions
- Provide respondent opportunity to respond

**Step 3.4 - Decision Point: Finding of Misconduct?**
- YES → proceed to Phase 4
- NO → close with documentation; notify all parties

### Phase 4: Resolution (Days 211-270)

**Step 4.1 - Respondent Response**
- Present findings to respondent
- Allow response period (typically 30 days)
- Consider response in final determination

**Step 4.2 - Final Determination**
- Institutional official makes final decision
- Consider all evidence and responses
- Document rationale

**Step 4.3 - Sanctions (if applicable)**
- Implement sanctions proportionate to findings
- Consider Mitigating/Aggravating factors
- Document sanctions with justification

**Step 4.4 - Reporting**
- Report to ORI if required (federally-funded research)
- Close case with documentation archived
- Minimum 3-year record retention

## 8.2 Policy Development Workflow

### Step 1: Regulatory Analysis
- Map applicable federal, state, and funding agency requirements
- Review 42 CFR 93 requirements
- Identify state-specific requirements

### Step 2: Gap Assessment
- Compare current policies against regulatory requirements
- Identify missing elements
- Prioritize gaps by risk

### Step 3: Stakeholder Input
- Consult with researchers, legal, administration
- Survey faculty on pain points
- Review peer institution policies

### Step 4: Draft Policy
- Develop policy language with clear definitions and procedures
- Include: misconduct definition, inquiry process, investigation process, appeals, record retention

### Step 5: Legal Review
- Ensure enforceability and compliance
- Review for due process requirements
- Confirm regulatory alignment

### Step 6: Implementation
- Train community on new procedures
- Deploy procedures with forms and templates
- Communicate to all research personnel

### Step 7: Periodic Review
- Annual assessment of policy effectiveness
- Updates based on regulatory changes
- Lessons learned from cases
