# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Research Translation Workflow

### Phase 1: Understanding (15-30 minutes)

**Step 1.1 - Read Primary Paper**
- Abstract, introduction, results, conclusions
- Skip method details unless critical

**Step 1.2 - Identify Core Finding**
- One sentence: What did they discover?
- What's surprising or new?

**Step 1.3 - Note Limitations**
- What didn't they study?
- What's uncertain?

### Phase 2: Angle Development (10-15 minutes)

**Step 2.1 - Identify Audience**
- Who cares about this?
- What do they need to know?

**Step 2.2 - Determine Hook**
- Why is this interesting NOW?
- What's the news value?

**Step 2.3 - Frame Story**
- Problem → Discovery → Implication
- Not "What they did" but "What it means"

### Phase 3: Drafting (20-45 minutes)

**Step 3.1 - Write Lead**
- Conclusion first
- Most important info in first paragraph
- Answer: Who, What, Why important

**Step 3.2 - Explain Mechanism**
- Plain language
- One analogy
- Connect to familiar concepts

**Step 3.3 - Include Expert Quote**
- Get researcher quote
- Or quote another expert
- Adds credibility

**Step 3.4 - Add Context**
- How does this compare to prior work?
- What's the broader significance?

### Phase 4: Review (10-15 minutes)

**Step 4.1 - Fact-Check**
- Verify against source
- Check numbers
- Confirm quotes

**Step 4.2 - Read Aloud**
- Check flow
- Fix awkward phrases

**Step 4.3 - Final Polish**
- Headline
- Formatting
- Links

## 8.2 Press Release Workflow

### Step 1: Confirm News Value
- Is this genuinely newsworthy?
- Would a journalist care?

### Step 2: Interview Researcher
- Get context beyond paper
- Personal story?
- Future plans?

### Step 3: Draft Headline
- Punchy
- Accurate
- Specific

### Step 4: Write Lead Paragraph
- The "what" in 30 words
- Answer most important questions

### Step 5: Add Context
- Why does this matter?
- To whom?

### Step 6: Include Quote
- Researcher's enthusiasm
- Appropriately measured

### Step 7: Add Boilerplate
- Institutional background
- About the research team

### Step 8: Prepare Q&A
- Anticipate journalist questions
- Have answers ready
