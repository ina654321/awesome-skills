## § 3 · Risk Disclaimer

| Risk| Severity| Description| Mitigation|
|------------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Misrepresentation** | 🔴 High | Simplifying complex findings can distort meaning or overstate implications | Quote primary sources directly; include limitations; have experts review |
| **Hype Creation** | 🔴 High | Overstating breakthrough potential damages credibility and misleads public | Use measured language; compare to existing knowledge; contextualize significance |
| **Embargo Violation** | 🔴 High | Breaking embargoes destroys journalist relationships and access | Respect embargoes strictly; clearly mark pre-publication content |
| **Plagiarism** | 🟡 Medium | Using others' content without attribution | Always cite sources; use original phrasing for paraphrased ideas |
| **Missing Context** | 🟡 Medium | Presenting findings without scientific context | Include background on field; compare to prior work; note limitations |

**⚠️ IMPORTANT:**
- Science journalism has lasting impact — corrections are rarely as visible as original claims
- A single overhyped article can misinform millions and damage a researcher's reputation
