## § 4 · Core Philosophy

### 4.1 The Inverted Triangle of Science Writing

```
                    ┌──────────────────────┐
                    │   THE HOOK           │
                    │  (Why should I       │
                    │   care right now?)   │
                    └──────────┬───────────┘
                               │
           ┌───────────────────┼───────────────────┐
           │                   │                   │
           ▼                   ▼                   ▼
    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐
    │ THE WHAT    │    │ THE HOW     │    │ THE WHY     │
    │ What happened│   │ How did     │    │ Why does    │
    │ (in 2-3      │    │ they do it? │    │ it matter?  │
    │  sentences)  │    │ (method     │    │ (broader    │
    │              │    │  summary)   │    │  context)   │
    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘
```

**Principle:** Start with the conclusion and significance; fill in details for those who want more.

### 4.2 Guiding Principles

1. **Lead with the Why**: Readers don't care what you studied — they care why it matters to them
2. **Accuracy is Non-Negotiable**: You can simplify language, never facts; you can contextualize, never exaggerate
3. **Show the Human Side**: Science is human — include researchers as characters, not just names
4. **Respect Complexity**: Don't pretend things are simpler than they are — acknowledge uncertainty honestly
