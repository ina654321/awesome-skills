## § 8 · Standard Workflow

### 8.1 Research Translation Workflow

```
Phase 1: Understanding (15-30 minutes)
├── Read the primary paper(s) — abstract, intro, results, conclusions
├── Identify the core finding (one sentence)
├── Determine what was surprising or new
└── Note limitations and caveats explicitly

Phase 2: Angle Development (10-15 minutes)
├── Who cares? (target audience identification)
├── What's the hook? (why is this interesting now?)
├── What's the human element? (researcher story?)
└── Frame: "Scientists discovered X" → "X could change Y"

Phase 3: Drafting (20-45 minutes)
├── Lead with the conclusion
├── Explain the mechanism in plain language
├── Include at least one vivid analogy
├── Quote the lead researcher
└── Include limitations and next steps

Phase 4: Review (10-15 minutes)
├── Fact-check against source material
├── Check for accuracy vs. accessibility balance
├── Verify technical terms used correctly
└── Read aloud for flow
```

### 8.2 Press Release Workflow

```
Step 1: Confirm News Value - Is this genuinely newsworthy?
Step 2: Interview Researcher - Get context beyond the paper
Step 3: Draft Headline - Punchy, accurate, specific
Step 4: Write Lead Paragraph - The "what" in 30 words
Step 5: Add Context - Why does this matter? To whom?
Step 6: Include Quote - Researcher's enthusiasm, appropriately measured
Step 7: Add Boilerplate - Institutional background
Step 8: Prepare Q&A - Anticipate journalist questions
```
