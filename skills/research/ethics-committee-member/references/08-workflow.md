# Ethics Committee Member Workflow Reference

Common workflow issues and challenges in institutional review board (IRB) and ethics committee operations.

## Pre-Review Protocol Issues

- **Problem**: Incomplete protocol submissions requiring multiple rounds of revision requests.
  - **Issue**: Investigators submit protocols missing required elements (consent templates, recruitment materials, data management plans).
  - **Reference**: OHRP guidance on required protocol elements; IRB submission checklists per institution.

- **Problem**: Protocol review delayed by reliance agreements and single IRB (sIRB) determination issues.
  - **Issue**: Multi-site studies require clear determination of which institution's IRB serves as the reviewing IRB.
  - **Reference**: NIH sIRB policy (effective 2018) and revised Common Rule sIRB requirements.

- **Problem**: Studies reviewed under wrong category (e.g., full board vs. expedited review).
  - **Issue**: Activities not meeting expedited criteria reviewed at expedited level; creates compliance risk.
  - **Reference**: OHRP categories for expedited review (45 CFR 46.110); FDA categories for IND/IDE research.

## Informed Consent Issues

- **Problem**: Consent documents exceeding readability standards.
  - **Issue**: Complex language, long documents, and technical terms reduce comprehension and voluntariness.
  - **Reference**: NIH and OHRP recommend 6th-8th grade reading level; IRB templates enforce readability.

- **Problem**: Consent obtained before protocol approval date.
  - **Issue**: Subjects enrolled prior to IRB approval creates non-compliance and invalid consent.
  - **Reference**: 45 CFR 46.115 and FDA 21 CFR 50.20 require IRB approval before enrollment.

- **Problem**: Re-consent not obtained when protocols are amended.
  - **Issue**: Subjects continue under modified procedures without updated consent.
  - **Reference**: OHRP guidance on continuing review and amendment procedures.

- **Problem**: Waiver of consent requested inappropriately for research involving more than minimal risk.
  - **Issue**: Waiver authority under 45 CFR 46.116(f) limited to minimal risk research or specific conditions.
  - **Reference**: OHRP guidance on waivers of informed consent; Belmont Report principles.

## Vulnerable Population Issues

- **Problem**: Inadequate protections for non-English speaking subjects.
  - **Issue**: Translated consent documents not reviewed for accuracy or cultural equivalence.
  - **Reference**: 45 CFR 46.116; FDA guidance on non-English consent; state language access laws.

- **Problem**: Assent from minors obtained incorrectly or not at all.
  - **Issue**: Assent requirements vary by age and risk level; LAR consent without child assent in inappropriate cases.
  - **Reference**: 45 CFR 46.408; OHRP and FDA guidance on pediatric assent.

- **Problem**: Prisoner research protocols not reviewed with required prisoner representation.
  - **Issue**: IRB must include at least one prisoner member when reviewing prisoner research.
  - **Reference**: 45 CFR 46.304-305.

## Continuing Review & Monitoring

- **Problem**: Annual continuing reviews not submitted on time, causing lapse in IRB approval.
  - **Issue**: Research must stop until continuing review is approved; subjects may be left without oversight.
  - **Reference**: 45 CFR 46.109(e) requires continuing review at intervals appropriate to degree of risk.

- **Problem**: Unanticipated problems not reported promptly to the IRB.
  - **Issue**: Investigators delay reporting adverse events and protocol deviations; creates liability.
  - **Reference**: OHRP and FDA reporting requirements and timelines.

- **Problem**: Protocol deviations not reported systematically.
  - **Issue**: Cumulative deviations suggest systemic process failures; IRB unaware of pattern.
  - **Reference**: Institutional policies on deviation reporting; FDA inspection references deviation logs.

## Multi-Site & Collaborative Research

- **Problem**: Reliance agreements not established before research begins.
  - **Issue**: Institutions assume the other institution's IRB is reviewing when no agreement exists.
  - **Reference**: SMART IRB agreement, OHRP guidance on written agreements. [smartirb.org](https://smartirb.org)

- **Problem**: Sponsor-initiated protocol amendments not submitted for IRB review.
  - **Issue**: Clinical trials sponsors issue amendments that investigators fail to submit to local IRBs.
  - **Reference**: FDA 21 CFR 312.30 requires IND sponsors to submit amendments; investigators must forward to IRB.

- **Problem**: Single IRB review of multi-site study does not address local context.
  - **Issue**: sIRB may approve without understanding site-specific institutional requirements.
  - **Reference**: NIH sIRB policy requires consideration of local context; HREA guidance.

## Data & Privacy Issues

- **Problem**: Data use agreements (DUA) not in place before data sharing.
  - **Issue**: Transfer of PHI or identifiable data without agreement violates HIPAA and institutional policies.
  - **Reference**: HHS guidance on HIPAA and research; OHRP guidance on IRB review of data repositories.

- **Problem**: Certificates of Confidentiality not obtained or disclosed appropriately.
  - **Issue**: Investigators conduct research without CoC when required (identifiable health information); data vulnerable to subpoena.
  - **Reference**: 42 USC 241(d); NIH guidance on CoC.

- **Problem**: Secondary use of data without adequate re-identification protection plan.
  - **Issue**: Data originally collected for clinical care used in research without sufficient de-identification or consent.
  - **Reference**: HIPAA Safe Harbor and Expert Determination de-identification methods.

## IRB Operations & Documentation

- **Problem**: Meeting minutes do not document required elements (discussion, voting, recusals).
  - **Issue**: Inadequate documentation of IRB deliberations; fails FDA and OHRP inspection readiness.
  - **Reference**: 45 CFR 46.115; FDA 21 CFR 56.115.

- **Problem**: IRB membership lacks required expertise or representation.
  - **Issue**: IRBs lack quorum or required perspectives (e.g., nonscientist, community member).
  - **Reference**: 45 CFR 46.107; FDA 21 CFR 56.107.

- **Problem**: Conflicts of interest not managed for committee members reviewing studies.
  - **Issue**: IRB members recuse themselves appropriately; documentation of COI management.
  - **Reference**: Institutional COI policies; 42 CFR Part 50, Subpart F.
