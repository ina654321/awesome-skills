# Ethics Committee Member Templates, Checklists & Protocols

Practical tools for IRB operations, protocol review, and compliance management.

## Initial Protocol Submission Checklist

For investigators submitting to the IRB:

- [ ] Complete protocol application with all required sections
- [ ] Attach current, date-stamped informed consent document(s)
- [ ] Attach assent form(s) if pediatric population is involved
- [ ] Attach recruitment materials (flyers, emails, scripts, social media posts)
- [ ] Attach data collection instruments (surveys, interview guides, case report forms)
- [ ] Attach data management and security plan
- [ ] Attach investigator brochure or device manual (if applicable)
- [ ] Attach protocol from sponsor (for industry-sponsored trials)
- [ ] Attach HIPAA authorization or waiver request
- [ ] Attach funding proposal or award notice (identifying funder)
- [ ] List all co-investigators and key personnel with credentials
- [ ] Complete financial conflict of interest disclosure for all personnel
- [ ] Attach certificate of confidentiality (if applicable)
- [ ] Attach site-specific information for multi-site studies
- [ ] Confirm that all key personnel have completed human subjects protection training (e.g., CITI Program)
- [ ] Confirm that consent documents are written at appropriate reading level (6th-8th grade)
- [ ] Verify protocol does not begin enrollment before IRB approval

## IRB Meeting Preparation Protocol

For IRB chair and members:

**Two Weeks Before Meeting:**
- [ ] Distribute protocols and supporting documents to members
- [ ] Assign primary and secondary reviewers for each protocol
- [ ] Verify quorum is achievable for the meeting
- [ ] Identify any protocols requiring full board vs. expedited review
- [ ] Flag potential conflicts of interest for recusal

**One Week Before Meeting:**
- [ ] Reviewers complete preliminary review forms
- [ ] Chair reviews any complex or problematic protocols
- [ ] Determine whether external experts are needed for specialized review
- [ ] Confirm all required institutional representatives will attend

**During the Meeting:**
- [ ] Call roll to confirm quorum is maintained throughout
- [ ] Document attendance including names and roles of all members
- [ ] Recuse members with conflicts before each discussion and vote
- [ ] Allow primary reviewer to present summary and concerns
- [ ] Open floor for questions and deliberation
- [ ] Vote on each protocol (approve, disapprove, tabled, modifications required)
- [ ] Document discussion points, required modifications, and rationale for decisions

**After the Meeting:**
- [ ] Draft and distribute minutes within 30 days per 45 CFR 46.115
- [ ] Notify investigators of decisions within 5 business days
- [ ] Track required modifications and response deadlines
- [ ] Verify continuing review dates are entered in tracking system

## Expedited Review Checklist

For designated reviewers conducting expedited review:

- [ ] Confirm the protocol falls within OHRP expedited categories (45 CFR 46.110) or FDA categories
- [ ] Verify the research presents no more than minimal risk (or fits the permitted greater-than-minimal-risk expedited categories)
- [ ] Check that the consent document includes all required elements (45 CFR 46.116)
- [ ] Verify vulnerable population protections are addressed (if applicable)
- [ ] Review data safety monitoring plan (if applicable)
- [ ] Check for completeness of investigator qualifications
- [ ] Assess adequacy of privacy and confidentiality protections
- [ ] Document the review and determination in writing
- [ ] Forward any questionable cases to full board review

## Continuing Review Protocol

For tracking and reviewing ongoing research:

**Tracking:**
- [ ] IRB staff maintains database of all active protocols with approval and expiration dates
- [ ] Send reminder notices at 60, 30, and 14 days before expiration
- [ ] Flag protocols with overdue adverse event reports or deviations
- [ ] Identify protocols that should be closed (enrollment complete, no data collection active)

**Review Process:**
- [ ] Request continuing review application from investigator
- [ ] Verify all modifications and amendments have been submitted and approved
- [ ] Review adverse event and deviation summary reports
- [ ] Assess enrollment status and any protocol deviations
- [ ] Verify that consent forms remain current (no new risks discovered)
- [ ] Determine if consent process remains appropriate
- [ ] Make determination: approve, require modifications, suspend, or terminate
- [ ] Update approval period (maximum 12 months for greater than minimal risk)

**Closure:**
- [ ] If research is complete, close protocol in tracking system
- [ ] Verify final data collection is complete
- [ ] Confirm data analysis and publication plans are documented
- [ ] Confirm specimen disposition (destruction or retention with consent)
- [ ] Verify records will be retained per institutional policy

## Unanticipated Problem Reporting Protocol

When a UP is identified:

1. **Initial Report (Investigator to IRB):**
   - Contact IRB office within 24-48 hours for serious events
   - Submit written UP report form within 5 business days
   - Include description, date, relationship to study, and corrective actions
   - Reference 45 CFR 46.108(a)(4) and OHRP guidance

2. **IRB Triage:**
   - IRB chair or designated reviewer triages within 24 hours
   - Determine if immediate action is needed to protect subjects
   - Assess whether full board review is required

3. **Assessment (for FDA-regulated research):**
   - Is it unexpected? (not described in consent or protocol)
   - Is it related or possibly related to the study intervention?
   - Does it suggest increased risk of harm?
   - Does it affect rights, safety, or welfare of subjects?

4. **Reporting (as required):**
   - OHRP: Report to OHRP within 30 days for UPs involving risks to subjects or others
   - FDA: Report unexpected and related serious adverse events per 21 CFR 312.32
   - Sponsor: Notify sponsor per protocol requirements
   - Institutional officials: Notify per institutional policy

5. **Documentation:**
   - Maintain complete record of UP, review, and actions taken
   - Update protocol and consent if warranted
   - Monitor for recurrence

## Protocol Deviation Management Checklist

When a deviation occurs:

- [ ] Assess whether deviation meets criteria for serious or continuing noncompliance
- [ ] Determine if subjects were harmed or at risk of harm
- [ ] Report serious deviations to IRB within 5 business days
- [ ] Submit protocol amendment if systemic change is needed
- [ ] Notify sponsor if deviation affects protocol compliance (industry trials)
- [ ] Document deviation in research records
- [ ] Implement corrective and preventive actions (CAPA)
- [ ] Update consent if new risks are identified
- [ ] Report to FDA if IND/IDE requirements are affected
- [ ] Include in continuing review summary

## Multi-Site Study Reliance Checklist

When establishing sIRB reliance:

- [ ] Identify all performance sites involved in the research
- [ ] Confirm which institution's IRB will serve as the reviewing IRB
- [ ] Execute reliance/authorization agreement before research begins
- [ ] Verify reviewing IRB is registered with OHRP and/or FDA
- [ ] Obtain FWA coverage for all sites under the reviewing IRB's assurance
- [ ] Develop process for local context issues (consent of state law, institutional requirements)
- [ ] Establish communication protocol between sites and reviewing IRB
- [ ] Define process for site-specific modifications
- [ ] Ensure each site has a designated contact for IRB communications
- [ ] Clarify which institution is responsible for DSMB/safety monitoring
- [ ] Confirm NIH sIRB policy requirements are met (for NIH-funded studies)
- [ ] Use SMART IRB online system to execute agreement where applicable [smartirb.org](https://smartirb.org)

## IRB Inspection Readiness Checklist

Preparing for OHRP or FDA inspection:

- [ ] Maintain all required records per 45 CFR 46.115 / 21 CFR 56.115
- [ ] Verify IRB membership complies with regulatory composition requirements
- [ ] Confirm FWA is current and covers all active research
- [ ] Verify IRB registration with OHRP is current
- [ ] Ensure all protocols have current approved consent documents
- [ ] Review any deferred or tabled protocols for completion of IRB action
- [ ] Verify continuing reviews are current and documented
- [ ] Confirm UP and deviation reports are complete and properly reported
- [ ] Verify training records for all IRB members and key personnel
- [ ] Review COI policies and documentation
- [ ] Ensure meeting minutes document required elements (quorum, discussion, votes, recusals)
- [ ] Review sIRB agreements are in place and current for multi-site studies
