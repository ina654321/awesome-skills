# Ethics Committee Member Glossary & Key Terms

Essential terminology for IRB and research ethics committee operations.

## Core Concepts

- **Institutional Review Board (IRB)**: A formally designated committee that reviews and monitors research involving human subjects to ensure compliance with ethical standards and regulatory requirements.

- **Research Ethics Committee (REC)**: The international term for a committee that reviews the ethics of research proposals, commonly used in Europe, Canada, and other regions.

- **Independent Ethics Committee (IEC)**: An ethics committee not affiliated with the research institution, often used in clinical trials.

- **Common Rule (45 CFR 46)**: The U.S. federal policy for the protection of human subjects in research, adopted by 15 federal departments and agencies.

- **Human Subject**: A living individual about whom an investigator conducting research obtains data through intervention or interaction, or identifiable private information.

- **Research**: A systematic investigation designed to develop or contribute to generalizable knowledge.

- **Minimal Risk**: The probability and magnitude of harm anticipated in the research are not greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or routine examinations.

## Review Categories

- **Full Board Review**: Review of research by the convened IRB quorum, required for greater than minimal risk research and certain categories defined in regulations.

- **Expedited Review**: Review by IRB chair or designated experienced reviewer, limited to specific categories of minimal risk research (45 CFR 46.110).

- **Exempt Review**: Categories of research that are exempt from the Common Rule requirements, determined by IRB officials (45 CFR 46.104). Exempt does not mean unethical — it means the activity does not require ongoing IRB oversight.

- **Limited IRB Review**: A form of expedited or exempt review for specific consent requirements (e.g., waiver of consent for secondary research with identifiable data).

## Consent Terms

- **Informed Consent**: A process by which a subject voluntarily confirms willingness to participate after being informed of all relevant aspects of the research.

- **Legally Authorized Representative (LAR)**: An individual authorized under applicable law to consent on behalf of a prospective subject (e.g., parent of a minor, legal guardian).

- **Assent**: Agreement by a minor or cognitively impaired individual to participate, distinct from parental/guardian consent.

- **Waiver of Informed Consent**: IRB authorization permitting research without requiring informed consent under specific regulatory conditions.

- **Waiver of Documentation of Consent**: IRB authorization permitting oral consent in place of a signed consent document.

- **Broad Consent**: A consent format under the 2018 Common Rule for storage, maintenance, and secondary research with identifiable data and biospecimens.

## Vulnerable Populations

- **Vulnerable Subjects**: Individuals who may have diminished autonomy and require additional protections, including children, prisoners, pregnant women, cognitively impaired individuals, economically disadvantaged, and educationally disadvantaged.

- **45 CFR 46 Subparts**: Additional protections for specific populations:
  - Subpart B: Pregnant women, human fetuses, and neonates
  - Subpart C: Prisoners
  - Subpart D: Children

- **Wards**: Children who are wards of the state or any agency, institution, or entity, requiring additional safeguards.

## Privacy & Data Terms

- **Protected Health Information (PHI)**: Individually identifiable health information held or transmitted by a covered entity under HIPAA.

- **De-identification**: Removal of identifying information from PHI. Two methods: Safe Harbor (18 identifiers removed) or Expert Determination.

- **Certificate of Confidentiality (CoC)**: Protects identifiable research data from compelled disclosure, obtained for sensitive research involving identifiable information.

- **Data Use Agreement (DUA)**: Contract governing the transfer of identifiable data between institutions or to external parties.

- **Limited Data Set**: Data from which direct identifiers have been removed, but may include geographic and dates; requires a DUA.

- **Secondary Research**: Use of previously collected data or biospecimens for research different from the original purpose.

## Adverse Events & Safety

- **Unanticipated Problem Involving Risks to Subjects or Others (UP)**: Any incident, experience, or outcome that meets OHRP's criteria for unanticipated problems.

- **Adverse Event (AE)**: Any untoward medical occurrence in a subject; classified as serious (SAE) or non-serious.

- **Serious Adverse Event (SAE)**: Adverse event resulting in death, life-threatening condition, hospitalization, persistent disability, or other significant hazard.

- **Protocol Deviation / Violation**: Departure from the approved study protocol. Deviations are unplanned; violations are intentional or systemic.

- **Data and Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB)**: Independent committee monitoring accumulating data for safety and efficacy signals in clinical trials.

- **Data Safety Monitoring Plan (DSMP)**: Documented plan for ongoing safety monitoring, required for higher-risk studies.

## Multi-Site Research

- **Single IRB (sIRB) Review**: One IRB serves as the reviewing IRB for all sites in a multi-site study. NIH mandate for domestic studies (effective 2018).

- **Reliance Agreement / Authorization Agreement**: Written agreement between institutions specifying which IRB serves as the reviewing IRB and the responsibilities of each party.

- **SMART IRB**: A standardized online reliance agreement system developed by NIH to streamline sIRB arrangements. [smartirb.org](https://smartirb.org)

- **Coordinating Center**: Entity managing logistics, data collection, and communications for multi-site studies.

## FDA-Regulated Research

- **Investigational New Drug (IND)**: FDA approval to test an experimental drug in humans; 21 CFR 312.

- **Investigational Device Exemption (IDE)**: FDA approval to test a significant risk device in humans; 21 CFR 812.

- **IND Safety Reports**: Sponsor must report serious and unexpected suspected adverse reactions to FDA within 15 days (21 CFR 312.32).

- **IDE Progress Reports**: Investigators must submit progress reports to FDA and IRBs at regular intervals.

## IRB Operations

- **Quorum**: Minimum number of IRB members required for a valid meeting, including at least one non-scientist and one member not affiliated with the institution.

- **IRB Chair / Vice Chair**: Senior official designated to oversee IRB operations and sign approvals.

- **Designated Reviewer**: Individual authorized to conduct expedited reviews under 45 CFR 46.110.

- **Recusal**: IRB member's formal removal from review due to conflict of interest or lack of expertise.

- **OHRP Assurance (FWA)**: Federalwide Assurance agreement binding an institution to comply with the Common Rule.

- **IRB Registration**: OHRP registration number assigned to an institution's IRB(s); renewed every 3 years.

- **Continuing Review**: Periodic re-evaluation of approved research by the IRB, required at intervals determined by degree of risk (maximum 12 months for greater than minimal risk).
