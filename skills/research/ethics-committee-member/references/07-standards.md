# Ethics Committee Member Reference Standards

Standards, regulations, and institutional policies governing research ethics committees (IRBs, IECs, RECs).

## Core Regulatory Frameworks

- **U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)**: The Common Rule (45 CFR 46) governs federally funded human subjects research. [hhs.gov/ohrp](https://hhs.gov/ohrp)
- **Office for Human Research Protections (OHRP)**: Oversees compliance with the Common Rule, provides guidance, and determines IRB registration. [hhs.gov/ohrp](https://hhs.gov/ohrp)
- **Food and Drug Administration (FDA)**: 21 CFR 50 (protection of human subjects) and 21 CFR 56 (IRB regulations) govern FDA-regulated research. [fda.gov](https://fda.gov)
- **International Council for Harmonisation (ICH)**: ICH E6(R2) Good Clinical Practice guidelines for clinical trials. [ich.org](https://ich.org)
- **European Union**: Clinical Trials Regulation (EU 536/2014) and GDPR (EU 2016/679) governing research in EU member states.
- **United Kingdom**: Health Research Authority (HRA) and the UK Policy Framework for Health and Social Care Research. [hra.nhs.uk](https://hra.nhs.uk)

## International Guidelines

- **World Health Organization (WHO)**: Council for International Organizations of Medical Sciences (CIOMS) International Ethical Guidelines for Health-related Research. [who.int](https://who.int)
- **Declaration of Helsinki**: World Medical Association's ethical principles for medical research involving human subjects. [wma.net](https://wma.net)
- **Belmont Report**: Foundational U.S. document establishing ethical principles (respect for persons, beneficence, justice) for human subjects research. [hhs.gov/ohrp](https://hhs.gov/ohrp)
- **UNESCO**: Universal Declaration on Bioethics and Human Rights. [unesco.org](https://unesco.org)

## IRB Structure & Operations

- **IRB Registration**: OHRP requires IRB registration (45 CFR 46) for institutions conducting federally funded human subjects research. [hhs.gov/ohrp](https://hhs.gov/ohrp)
- **OHRP Assurance (FWA)**: Federalwide Assurance agreements institutions must hold to receive HHS funding for human subjects research.
- **FDA-Recognized IRB**: For FDA-regulated device and drug studies, IRBs must be registered with FDA under 21 CFR 56.
- **AAHRPP Accreditation**: The Association for the Accreditation of Human Research Protection Programs provides voluntary accreditation. [aahrpp.org](https://aahrpp.org)
- **Sentinel Initiative**: FDA's system for monitoring safety of marketed medical products. [fda.gov](https://fda.gov)

## Informed Consent Standards

- **21 CFR 50.25 / 45 CFR 46.116**: Required elements of informed consent.
- **FDA Informed Consent Requirements**: 21 CFR Part 50 for informed consent in clinical investigations.
- **Legally Authorized Representatives (LAR)**: Regulations for consent on behalf of persons who cannot consent (children, cognitively impaired). State laws vary significantly.
- **Waiver of Informed Consent**: Conditions under which IRB may waive consent requirements for minimal risk research (45 CFR 46.116(f)).
- **Waiver of Documentation of Consent**: 45 CFR 46.117 allows oral consent with a written summary for specific minimal risk research.

## Vulnerable Populations

- **Children (45 CFR 46, Subpart D)**: Additional protections requiring direct benefits justification for greater than minimal risk research.
- **Prisoners (45 CFR 46, Subpart C)**: Special protections including prisoner representation on IRB.
- **Pregnant Women (45 CFR 46, Subpart B)**: Research risk-benefit standards for pregnant women and fetuses.
- **Cognitively Impaired Individuals**: Decision-making capacity assessment, use of LARs, and assent requirements.
- **International Research**: CIOMS guidelines address research in low-resource settings and among vulnerable populations.

## Privacy & Data Protection

- **HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR 164)**: Governs protected health information (PHI) in research contexts. [hhs.gov/hipaa](https://hhs.gov/hipaa)
- **Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA)**: Protects against discrimination based on genetic information in health insurance and employment.
- **GDPR (EU 2016/679)**: Data protection requirements for research in EU; legal bases for processing, data minimization, and subject rights.
- **Certificates of Confidentiality**: HHS authority to protect identifiable research data from compelled disclosure (42 USC 241(d)). [hhs.gov/ohrp](https://hhs.gov/ohrp)

## Conflict of Interest

- **HHS Financial Conflict of Interest Regulations (42 CFR Part 50, Subpart F)**: Requires institutions to manage financial conflicts of interest in federally funded research. [hhs.gov](https://hhs.gov)
- **FDA Financial Disclosure (21 CFR 54)**: Requires clinical investigators to disclose financial interests to sponsors.
- **Institutional COI Policies**: Most research institutions maintain their own COI policies beyond federal requirements.

## Adverse Event Reporting

- **OHRP Unanticipated Problems Policy**: Guidance on reporting unanticipated problems involving risks to subjects or others. [hhs.gov/ohrp](https://hhs.gov/ohrp)
- **FDA Safety Reporting**: 21 CFR 312.32 (IND safety reports), 21 CFR 803 (medical device reporting), 21 CFR 20.
- **Data and Safety Monitoring Boards (DSMBs)**: Required for multi-site clinical trials and high-risk interventions.

## Record Keeping & Reporting

- **IRB Records (45 CFR 46.115)**: IRB must maintain protocols, consent documents, correspondence, and meeting minutes.
- **FDA Records Requirements**: 21 CFR 812 (IDE) and 21 CFR 312 (IND) require records for clinical investigations.
- **ClinicalTrials.gov Registration & Results Reporting**: FDAAA 801 requires registration and results reporting (42 USC 282).
- **OHRP Assurance Reporting**: Institutions must report unanticipated problems, suspensions, and terminations to OHRP.
