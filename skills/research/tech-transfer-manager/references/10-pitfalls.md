# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns in Tech Transfer

| # | Anti-Pattern| Severity| Quick Fix|
|---|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Premature Licensing** | 🔴 High | Don't license technology before it has demonstrated some reproducibility |
| 2 | **Overly Broad Claims** | 🔴 High | Narrow claims survive prosecution; broad claims get rejected |
| 3 | **Royalty-Only Focus** | 🔴 High | Most licenses never earn royalties — ensure meaningful upfront and milestones |
| 4 | **Ignoring Trade Secrets** | 🟡 Medium | Not all tech is patentable — some is better protected as trade secret |
| 5 | **Fighting Over Equity** | 🟡 Medium | Focus on deal economics, not equity percentages — success matters more |

## 10.2 Problematic Deal Terms

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead |
|----------|----------------|
| "$0 upfront, 3% royalty, no milestones" | "$100K upfront, 4% royalty, $2M milestones" |
| "Best efforts" | "Commercially reasonable efforts" with specific timelines |
| Exclusive, all fields | Exclusive by field-of-use |

## 10.3 Common Mistakes

### Patent Issues
- Filing after public disclosure
- Claims too broad
- Not filing internationally
- Missing inventor assignments

### Licensing Issues
- Licensing too early
- No field-of-use restrictions
- Ignoring due diligence
- Accepting poor terms for "prestige"

### Startup Issues
- Unrealistic valuations
- Equity disputes with founders
- No IP assignment agreements
- Ignoring university policies

## 10.4 Quality Checklist

Before licensing:
- [ ] Patent protection secured
- [ ] Market assessment complete
- [ ] Due diligence on licensee
- [ ] Field-of-use defined
- [ ] Meaningful upfront payment
- [ ] Milestones structured
- [ ] IP assignment clear

Before startup:
- [ ] IP assigned to startup
- [ ] Founder agreements in place
- [ ] Board structure defined
- [ ] Milestones and funding tied
- [ ] University policies followed
