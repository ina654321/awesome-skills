# Lab Technician Glossary & Key Terms

Essential terminology for laboratory technical practice.

## Safety & Regulatory Terms

- **OSHA Laboratory Standard (29 CFR 1910.1450)**: Federal regulation mandating Chemical Hygiene Plans and worker protection in laboratories.

- **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)**: 16-section standardized document providing chemical hazard, handling, storage, and emergency information per OSHA HazCom.

- **Chemical Hygiene Plan (CHP)**: Written plan describing how a laboratory minimizes chemical exposure and complies with the OSHA Laboratory Standard.

- **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)**: Equipment worn to minimize exposure to hazards — gloves, lab coats, safety glasses, face shields, respirators.

- **Engineering Controls**: Equipment and design features that isolate workers from hazards (fume hoods, biosafety cabinets, shielding).

- **Administrative Controls**: Work practices, SOPs, and training that reduce hazard exposure (signage, procedures, training).

- **Biosafety Level (BSL-1 to BSL-4)**: CDC/NIH classification of containment requirements for handling biological agents. [cdc.gov](https://cdc.gov)

- **Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC)**: Institutional committee that reviews and approves recombinant DNA and biohazardous research per NIH Guidelines.

- **Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC)**: Institutional committee overseeing animal research protocols and welfare.

- **Institutional Chemical Safety Committee**: Committee reviewing hazardous chemical use and approving particularly hazardous substance protocols.

## Chemical & Reagent Terms

- **Primary Standard**: Highly pure reagent used to standardize solutions; traceable to NIST.

- **Secondary Standard**: Solution standardized against a primary standard.

- **Standard Solution**: Solution of known concentration used for quantitative analysis.

- **Stock Solution**: Concentrated solution from which working solutions are prepared.

- **Serial Dilution**: Stepwise dilution where each dilution is the stock for the next.

- **Molarity (M)**: Moles of solute per liter of solution.

- **Molality (m)**: Moles of solute per kilogram of solvent.

- **Percent Solution**: Weight/volume (% w/v) or volume/volume (% v/v); commonly used in laboratory settings.

- **Hazardous Waste**: Chemical exhibiting RCRA characteristics (ignitability, corrosivity, reactivity, toxicity) or listed hazardous waste.

- **Saturation**: Condition where a solution contains the maximum amount of dissolved solute at a given temperature.

- **Precipitation**: Formation of an insoluble solid from a solution (e.g., AgCl precipitate).

## Biological Terms

- **Cell Line**: Cultured cells derived from a specific tissue that can be maintained and propagated in vitro.

- **Primary Culture**: Cells cultured directly from tissue; finite lifespan; not immortalized.

- **Passage / Subculture**: Transferring cells from one culture vessel to another to maintain growth.

- **Confluence**: Percentage of culture surface covered by cells.

- **Cryopreservation**: Freezing cells in liquid nitrogen with cryoprotectant (DMSO) for long-term storage.

- **Mycoplasma**: Small bacteria lacking cell walls; common cell culture contaminant causing experimental artifacts.

- **Aseptic Technique**: Practices preventing microbial contamination of cell cultures and sterile work.

- **Biohazardous Material**: Biological material posing risk to human health (pathogens, toxins, recombinant DNA, human/animal specimens).

- **Decontamination**: Reduction of microbial contamination to safe levels; methods include autoclaving, chemical disinfection, and UV radiation.

## Instrumentation Terms

- **Pipette**: Precision instrument for measuring and transferring specific volumes of liquid.

- **Micropipette**: Pipette for volumes in the microliter range; air-displacement and positive-displacement types.

- **Burette**: Long glass tube with a stopcock for delivering variable, measured volumes of liquid; used in titration.

- **Volumetric Flask**: Precision glassware for preparing solutions of exact volume.

- **Centrifuge**: Instrument separating components by centrifugal force; types include microcentrifuge, clinical, refrigerated, and ultracentrifuge.

- **Spectrophotometer**: Instrument measuring light absorption (UV-Vis) to determine concentration of analytes.

- **HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography)**: Separates, identifies, and quantifies chemical compounds in liquid samples.

- **pH Meter**: Instrument measuring hydrogen ion concentration (acidity/alkalinity) of solutions.

- **Balance / Analytical Balance**: Precision instrument for measuring mass; analytical balances read to 0.0001 g.

- **Biosafety Cabinet (BSC)**: Enclosed workspace with HEPA-filtered airflow providing personnel and product protection from biohazardous materials.

- **Fume Hood**: ventilated enclosure capturing and exhausting hazardous vapors away from the worker.

## Quality Control Terms

- **Internal Quality Control (IQC)**: Ongoing testing of control materials to verify instrument and method performance.

- **External Quality Assessment / Proficiency Testing (PT)**: Testing of unknown samples by an external provider to assess laboratory performance.

- **Westgard Rules**: Multi-rule QC system for detecting out-of-control conditions in clinical laboratories.

- **Calibration Verification**: Testing to confirm that a method's performance characteristics meet specifications for patient results.

- **Method Validation**: Process confirming that a method performs as intended for its intended use.

- **Limit of Detection (LOD)**: Smallest amount of analyte reliably distinguished from blank.

- **Limit of Quantitation (LOQ)**: Lowest amount of analyte that can be quantitatively measured with acceptable precision.

- **Linearity**: Ability of a method to produce results proportional to analyte concentration across the measuring range.

- **Accuracy / Bias**: Closeness of agreement between a measured value and the true value.

- **Precision / Reproducibility**: Closeness of agreement between independent measurement results.

- **Coefficient of Variation (CV)**: Standard deviation expressed as a percentage of the mean.

## Laboratory Mathematics Terms

- **Significant Figures**: Digits in a measured quantity that carry meaning regarding precision.

- **Dilution Factor**: Ratio of initial to final concentration (or volume relationship in dilution).

- **Molar Mass / Molecular Weight**: Mass of one mole of a substance (g/mol).

- **Avogadro's Number**: 6.022 × 10²³ entities per mole.

- **Density**: Mass per unit volume (g/mL or g/cm³).

- **Specific Gravity**: Ratio of a substance's density to that of water.

- **Beer-Lambert Law**: Relationship between light absorption and concentration (A = εbc) in spectrophotometry.

## Animal Research Terms

- **Species**: The kind of animal (e.g., Mus musculus, Rattus norvegicus, Macaca mulatta).

- **Strain**: Genetic variant within a species (e.g., C57BL/6, Sprague-Dawley, Long-Evans).

- **Genotype**: Genetic makeup of an individual (specific alleles present).

- **Phenotype**: Observable characteristics resulting from genotype and environment.

- **Husbandry**: Day-to-day care of animals including feeding, housing, and health monitoring.

- **Environmental Enrichment**: Additions to animal housing (toys, nesting material, social housing) to promote species-typical behavior.

- **Anesthesia**: Loss of sensation with or without loss of consciousness; induced for surgical procedures.

- **Analgesia**: Relief from pain; provided as part of pain management in animal protocols.

- **Euthanasia**: Humane death of an animal; must follow AVMA Guidelines or institutional policy.

- **Post-Operative Care**: Monitoring and care following surgical procedures including pain management.

- **IACUC (Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee)**: Committee reviewing and approving animal research protocols.

- **AWA (Animal Welfare Act)**: Federal law establishing minimum standards for animal care in research. [usda.gov](https://usda.gov)

- **ILAR Guide**: Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals; 8th edition (2011); definitive reference for animal care. [nap.edu](https://nap.edu)

- **Three Rs**: Replacement (alternative methods), Reduction (minimize numbers), Refinement (minimize suffering) — principles guiding humane animal research.

## Record Keeping Terms

- **Laboratory Notebook**: Primary record of experimental methods, observations, and results.

- **Chain of Custody**: Documented history of evidence handling from collection through analysis.

- **ALCOA+ Principles**: Data integrity standards: Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate + Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available.

- **Audit Trail**: Chronological record of all changes to electronic data; required in regulated environments.

- **GLP (Good Laboratory Practice)**: Quality system for non-clinical laboratory studies; 21 CFR Part 58. [fda.gov](https://fda.gov)

- **SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)**: Written procedure describing how to perform a specific task safely and consistently.
