# Lab Technician Reference Standards

Standards, regulations, and institutional policies governing laboratory science practice.

## OSHA & Workplace Safety

- **Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA)**: Federal regulations for laboratory safety under the Occupational Exposure to Hazardous Chemicals in Laboratories Standard (29 CFR 1910.1450), known as the OSHA Laboratory Standard. [osha.gov](https://osha.gov)
- **Hazard Communication Standard (HCS / 29 CFR 1910.1200)**: Requires chemical labels, Safety Data Sheets (SDS), and employee training on hazardous chemicals. [osha.gov](https://osha.gov)
- **Bloodborne Pathogens Standard (29 CFR 1910.1030)**: Requirements for protecting workers from exposure to bloodborne pathogens (HIV, Hepatitis B). [osha.gov](https://osha.gov)
- **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Standards (29 CFR 1910 Subpart I)**: Requirements for protective equipment in laboratories. [osha.gov](https://osha.gov)

## CDC & Biosafety

- **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)**: Biosafety in Microbiological and Biomedical Laboratories (BMBL) 6th edition — the gold standard for biosafety practices. [cdc.gov](https://cdc.gov)
- **Biosafety Levels (BSL-1 through BSL-4)**: Containment levels defining safety requirements for handling biological agents. [cdc.gov](https://cdc.gov)
- **NIH Guidelines for Research Involving Recombinant or Synthetic Nucleic Acid Molecules**: Govern recombinant DNA work; oversight by Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC). [nih.gov](https://nih.gov)
- **Select Agents and Toxins (42 CFR Part 73, 7 CFR Part 331, 9 CFR Part 121)**: Regulations for possession, use, and transfer of hazardous biological agents. [cdc.gov](https://cdc.gov)

## EPA & Environmental

- **Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)**: Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA) for hazardous waste management; defines characteristics of hazardous waste (ignitability, corrosivity, reactivity, toxicity). [epa.gov](https://epa.gov)
- **EPAtoxics Release Inventory (TRI)**: Reporting requirements for releases of listed chemicals. [epa.gov](https://epa.gov)
- **State Hazardous Waste Programs**: States may have regulations more stringent than federal RCRA.

## Radiation Safety

- **Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC)**: 10 CFR Part 20 (Standards for Protection Against Radiation), 10 CFR Part 30-36 (Byproduct materials). [nrc.gov](https://nrc.gov)
- **Agreement States**: States with authority to regulate radioactive material use; state regulations may differ from NRC.
- **NRC License Requirements**: Institutional license required for possession of radioactive materials.
- **FDA**: Regulates radiation-producing equipment (X-ray machines, accelerators).

## Fire & Chemical Safety

- **National Fire Protection Association (NFPA)**: NFPA 45 (Fire Protection for Laboratories Using Chemicals), NFPA 704 (Safety Standard for Identification of Hazards). [nfpa.org](https://nfpa.org)
- **National Fire Protection Association (NFPA)**: NFPA 70 (National Electrical Code) — governs electrical installations. [nfpa.org](https://nfpa.org)
- **Compressed Gas Association (CGA)**: Standards for safe handling of compressed gas cylinders (CGA P-1, P-18). [cganet.com](https://cganet.com)

## Animal Research

- **Animal Welfare Act (AWA / 7 U.S.C. § 2131)**: Federal law governing the care and use of animals in research. [usda.gov](https://usda.gov)
- **Animal Welfare Regulations (9 CFR Chapter 1, Subchapter A)**: Detailed requirements for animal care. [ecfr.gov](https://ecfr.gov)
- **Public Health Service (PHS) Policy on Humane Care and Use of Laboratory Animals**: Policy applying to all PHS-funded research using animals. [nih.gov](https://nih.gov)
- **Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals (NRC/ILAR)**: The definitive guide for animal care and use; 8th edition. [nap.edu](https://nap.edu)
- **Association for Assessment and Accreditation of Laboratory Animal Care International (AAALAC International)**: Voluntary accreditation for animal care and use programs. [aaalac.org](https://aaalac.org)

## Clinical Laboratory Standards

- **Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI)**: Guidelines for clinical laboratory testing, quality control, and method verification. [clsi.org](https://clsi.org)
- **College of American Pathologists (CAP)**: Laboratory accreditation checklists including regulations, QC, and safety. [cap.org](https://cap.org)
- **The Joint Commission (TJC)**: Accreditation standards for hospital and clinical laboratories. [jointcommission.org](https://jointcommission.org)
- **Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments (CLIA / 42 CFR Part 493)**: Federal standards for clinical laboratory testing. [cms.gov](https://cms.gov)

## Chemical & Hazardous Materials

- **DOT Hazardous Materials Regulations (49 CFR Parts 171-180)**: Requirements for shipping hazardous materials. [dot.gov](https://dot.gov)
- **IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations**: International air transport requirements for hazardous materials. [iata.org](https://iata.org)
- **National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)**: CAMEO (Computer-Aided Management of Emergency Operations) software suite. [noaa.gov](https://noaa.gov)

## Quality Management

- **Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI)**: CLSI C24 (Statistical Quality Control for Quantitative Measurements), EP05 (Evaluation of Precision), EP07 (Interference Testing). [clsi.org](https://clsi.org)
- **ISO 17025**: General requirements for the competence of testing and calibration laboratories. [iso.org](https://iso.org)
- **Good Laboratory Practice (GLP / 21 CFR Part 58)**: FDA standards for non-clinical laboratory studies. [fda.gov](https://fda.gov)
- **Good Clinical Practice (GCP / ICH E6)**: Standards for designing, conducting, and reporting clinical trials. [ich.org](https://ich.org)

## Professional Organizations

- **American Chemical Society (ACS)**: Division of Chemical Health and Safety; safety resources and guidelines. [acs.org](https://acs.org)
- **American Biological Safety Association (ABSA)**: Biosafety professional organization and guidance. [absa.org](https://absa.org)
- **American Society for Clinical Pathology (ASCP)**: Board of Certification for laboratory professionals. [ascp.org](https://ascp.org)
- **American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE)**: Laboratory ventilation guidelines (ASHRAE 62.1). [ashrae.org](https://ashrae.org)
