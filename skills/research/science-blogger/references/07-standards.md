# Standards & Reference

## 7.1 Science Communication Standards

### Accuracy Guidelines
- [National Association of Science Writers](https://nasw.org/) — Science writing ethics
- [Society of Environmental Journalists](https://www.sej.org/) — Environmental reporting standards
- [AAAS Science Communication](https://www.aaas.org/) — Best practices

### Fact-Checking Resources
- [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) — Primary source verification
- [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/) — Citation checking
- [Retraction Watch](https://retractionwatch.com/) — Paper corrections

### Press Ethics
- [EASE Guidelines](https://www.ease.org.uk/) — Science editing standards
- [Journal embargo policies](https://www.scientific.org/) — Pre-publication handling

## 7.2 Platform Guidelines

### Twitter/X Best Practices
- [Twitter Science Community](https://twitter.com/) — Engagement strategies
- Character limits: 280 per tweet
- Thread structure: 7-10 tweets max

### LinkedIn Best Practices
- [LinkedIn Algorithm](https://www.linkedin.com/) — Feed optimization
- Article length: 800-1500 words
- Professional tone appropriate

### YouTube Best Practices
- [YouTube Creator Academy](https://creatoracademy.youtube.com/) — Video best practices
- Video length: 8-15 minutes optimal
- Engagement: comments, community posts

## 7.3 Content Quality Metrics

### Readability Standards
| Platform | Recommended Reading Level | Tools |
|----------|--------------------------|-------|
| Blog | 8th-10th grade | Hemingway App |
| Twitter | 6th-8th grade | Plain language |
| LinkedIn | 10th-12th grade | Professional tone |

### Engagement Benchmarks
| Metric | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Engagement rate | 3-5% | >8% |
| Click-through | 1-2% | >3% |
| Share ratio | 5-10% | >15% |
