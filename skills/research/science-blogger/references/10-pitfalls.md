# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns in Science Communication

| # | Anti-Pattern| Severity| Quick Fix|
|---|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Twitter Length Overload** | 🔴 High | Threads >10 tweets lose readers — be more selective |
| 2 | **Link-Only Posts** | 🔴 High | Posts without context get ignored — add value before linking |
| 3 | **Oversimplification** | 🔴 High | Accuracy matters — don't trade truth for virality |
| 4 | **Inconsistent Posting** | 🟡 Medium | Algorithms punish irregular posting — schedule consistently |
| 5 | **Engagement Debt** | 🟡 Medium | Only posting without responding kills community |

## 10.2 Content Mistakes

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead |
|----------|----------------|
| "Just read an interesting paper about AI. Check it out: [link]" | "This AI breakthrough solved a problem that's puzzled biologists for 50 years. Here's what changed and why it matters" |
| "This will CURE CANCER!!! 🚨" | "This approach shows promise for treating X type of cancer, according to a study published in Nature" |
| Ignore negative comments | Address legitimate concerns; ignore trolls |

## 10.3 Accuracy Issues

### Common Problems
- Citing preprints as established facts
- Oversimplifying uncertainty
- Making causal claims from correlational data
- Quoting studies out of context
- Not checking for retractions

### Accuracy Checklist
- [ ] Verified primary source
- [ ] Noted limitations of study
- [ ] Used appropriate hedging language
- [ ] Checked for retractions
- [ ] Citing correctly

## 10.4 Platform Mistakes

### Twitter
- Too long tweets
- No visuals
- No thread when needed
- Over-hashtagging

### LinkedIn
- Too long posts
- Not professional enough
- No call to action

### Blog
- Too much jargon
- No visuals
- Hard to scan
- No links to sources

## 10.5 Engagement Pitfalls

### Common Issues
- Not responding to comments
- Being confrontational
- Ignoring feedback
- Not asking questions
- Being too promotional

### Best Practices
- Respond within 24 hours
- Ask follow-up questions
- Thank positive engagement
- Acknowledge when wrong
- Give more than you ask
