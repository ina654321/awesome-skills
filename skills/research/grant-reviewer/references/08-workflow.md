# Grant Reviewer Workflow Reference

Common workflow issues and challenges in peer review of grant applications.

## Pre-Review Preparation Issues

- **Problem**: Reviewers receive inadequate context about the funding mechanism.
  - **Issue**: R01, R21, R03, P01, and training grants have different expectations; reviewers apply uniform criteria inappropriately.
  - **Reference**: Each NIH institute publishes specific review criteria for its programs; NSF program descriptions specify expectations.

- **Problem**: Reviewers unfamiliar with the field receive applications outside their expertise.
  - **Issue**: Panel composition may not perfectly match all applications; review quality suffers.
  - **Reference**: SROs match reviewers to applications; reviewers should flag expertise gaps.

- **Problem**: Conflict of interest not identified until late in the process.
  - **Issue**: COI identified after discussion begins wastes panel time and creates bias risk.
  - **Reference**: COI must be declared before the meeting; early disclosure protocols.

## Review Quality Issues

- **Problem**: Inconsistent scoring across criterion ratings.
  - **Issue**: Reviewers apply scales differently; some use only 3-5 of 9 scores; calibration lacking.
  - **Reference**: NIH and NSF provide scoring guidance and calibration sessions.

- **Problem**: Global score does not align with individual criterion scores.
  - **Issue**: Reviewers inflate or deflate overall scores inconsistent with specific ratings.
  - **Reference**: Criterion score reviews reduce halo effects and improve score validity.

- **Problem**: Reviewer critiques lack specificity or actionable feedback.
  - **Issue**: Generic feedback ("methodology needs improvement") does not help PI or program.
  - **Reference**: Structured review templates (NIH SRO-provided templates) improve critique quality.

- **Problem**: Critique written for revision application applies to original submission.
  - **Issue**: Reviewers critique prior iterations rather than current application.
  - **Reference**: Reviewers should evaluate the current application on its merits.

## Panel Discussion Issues

- **Problem**: Dominant reviewers steer discussion disproportionately.
  - **Issue**: Others defer to senior or more vocal members; less experienced investigators disadvantaged.
  - **Reference**: SRO facilitation guidelines; structured discussion per criterion.

- **Problem**: Scientific background of applicant inflates scores (halo effect).
  - **Issue**: Well-known investigators receive benefit of the doubt; new investigators penalized.
  - **Reference**: NIH/NSF reviewer training addresses halo effects and implicit bias.

- **Problem**: Personal biases affect evaluation (institutional prestige, geography, gender).
  - **Issue**: Systematic bias in scoring across demographic groups; documented disparities.
  - **Reference**: Implicit bias training (NIH, NSF, HHMI); structured evaluation rubrics.

- **Problem**: Reviewers not present for full meeting participate in votes.
  - **Issue**: Members who miss discussions cast votes on unvetted applications.
  - **Reference**: SRO protocols; teleconference and virtual meeting participation policies.

## Mechanism-Specific Issues

- **Problem**: Career development awards (K awards) reviewed with R01 criteria.
  - **Issue**: Mentorship quality and training plan overlooked; emphasis on preliminary data inappropriate.
  - **Reference**: K-award review criteria emphasize candidate, mentor, and career development plan.

- **Problem**: Program project grants (P01) reviewed as multiple independent R01s.
  - **Issue**: Integration and synergy across projects not evaluated; overall program value missed.
  - **Reference**: P01 review criteria include synergy, leadership, and overall program quality.

- **Problem**: Training grants reviewed without sufficient attention to trainee outcomes.
  - **Issue**: Institutional reputation overshadows actual training record and plan quality.
  - **Reference**: T32 review emphasizes trainee publication and career outcomes.

## Budget & Feasibility Issues

- **Problem**: Reviewers recommend budget cuts without scientific basis.
  - **Issue**: Budget decisions made outside expertise; affect feasibility and research quality.
  - **Reference**: Budget justification review is program staff responsibility; reviewers assess scientific merit.

- **Problem**: Modular budget applications reviewed with detailed budget scrutiny.
  - **Issue**: Reviewers request budget breakdowns beyond what is required at application stage.
  - **Reference**: NIH modular budget policy; indirect cost limitations apply.

## Institutional & Compliance Issues

- **Problem**: Vertebrate animal and human subjects sections reviewed by non-experts.
  - **Issue**: Protocol adequacy assessments require specific expertise; generalists miss key issues.
  - **Reference**: Special emphasis panels include expertise;兽人 and human subjects protocols reviewed separately.

- **Problem**: Recombinant DNA and biosafety sections reviewed superficially.
  - **Issue**: IBC and institutional biosafety committee approvals are post-award; reviewers note concerns.
  - **Reference**: Reviewers identify potential concerns; IBC approval is institutional pre-award requirement.

## Post-Review Issues

- **Problem**: Summary statements contain conflicting information from reviewers vs. panel.
  - **Issue**: SRO summary does not accurately reflect discussion; PI receives misleading feedback.
  - **Reference**: SROs document discussion; applicants may request clarification.

- **Problem**: Appeals or reconsideration requests based on review quality issues.
  - **Issue**: PI disputes critiques they consider unfair or inaccurate; appeals process limited.
  - **Reference**: NIH reconsideration policy (NOT-OD-11-064); grounds for appeal are narrow.

- **Problem**: Reviewer identity disclosure requests (both ways) causing tension.
  - **Issue**: PIs want to respond to specific critiques; reviewers reluctant to be identified.
  - **Reference**: NIH allows optional open review (Public Access to Evaluations); anonymous review standard.
