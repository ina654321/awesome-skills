# Grant Reviewer Glossary & Key Terms

Essential terminology for peer review of research grant applications.

## Funding Mechanisms

- **R01 (Research Project Grant)**: The original and most common NIH research grant; supports discrete, specified research projects for up to 5 years.

- **R21 (Exploratory/Developmental Research Grant)**: NIH mechanism for exploratory, high-risk research; shorter duration and smaller budget than R01.

- **R03 (Small Research Grant)**: NIH mechanism for pilot studies and feasibility; limited to 2 years and $50,000 direct costs.

- **R41/R42 (Small Business Technology Transfer - STTR)**: Cooperative research between small business and research institution; phases I and II.

- **R43/R44 (Small Business Innovation Research - SBIR)**: Small business research and development funding; phase I feasibility, phase II expansion.

- **P01 (Program Project Grant)**: Multi-project grant combining multiple related research projects with shared resources and leadership.

- **U01 (Cooperative Agreement)**: Grant with substantial federal scientific/programmatic involvement; federal scientist participates in project.

- **U54 (Specialized Center)**: Large multi-project grant with research cores and often clinical/translational focus.

- **K Awards (Career Development)**: NIH mentored (K01, K08, K23) and independent (K05, K24, K26) career development awards.

- **K99/R00 (Pathway to Independence Award)**: Two-phase award supporting late postdoctoral to early faculty transition.

- **F30/F31 (Predoctoral Fellowship)**: NRSA training awards for predoctoral (F31) and combined MD/PhD training (F30).

- **F32 (Postdoctoral Fellowship)**: NRSA award for postdoctoral research training.

- **T32 (Institutional Training Grant)**: NRSA grant supporting predoctoral or postdoctoral training programs at institutions.

- **T35 (Short-Term Training)**: NRSA institutional grant for short-term research training (e.g., summer programs).

- **D43 (International Research Training)**: NIH Fogarty program for international research capacity building.

- **S21/S06 (Research Infrastructure Programs)**: Infrastructure and capacity-building grants for under-resourced institutions.

## Review Terminology

- **Study Section**: A peer review group organized by CSR or an institute that reviews grant applications within a scientific area.

- **Special Emphasis Panel (SEP)**: Ad hoc review group convened for specific initiatives outside standing study section scope.

- **Advisory Council/Board**: The second level of NIH review; provides advice on program relevance and balance.

- **Scientific Review Officer (SRO)**: The federal official responsible for managing the peer review process and ensuring fair and independent review.

- **Program Officer (PO)**: NIH staff who manage grants after award; provides programmatic oversight and guidance to PIs.

- **Grants Management Officer (GMO)**: NIH staff responsible for administrative and fiscal aspects of grant awards.

- **Priority Score**: Numeric rating (100-500 on NIH 1-9 scale multiplied by significance factor) indicating scientific merit.

- **Percentile**: Ranking of priority score within the pool of applications reviewed by the study section.

- **Payline**: The funding cutoff percentile established by each institute; applications below payline are not funded.

- **Impact Score**: The NIH's terminology for the overall score; derived from priority score analysis.

- **Summary Statement**: The official record of review, including reviewers' written critiques, criterion scores, and priority score.

- **Streamlined Review Process**: NIH process for identifying applications unlikely to be funded for skip-to discussion, reducing meeting time.

- **Streamlined Review Pane**: Reviewer preliminary scoring identifying applications unlikely to be discussed.

- **Not Discussed**: Applications not selected for full panel discussion; still receive written critiques.

## Review Criteria

- **Significance**: Does the project address an important problem or barrier? Will it advance the field?

- **Investigator(s)**: Are the PI and team qualified? Do they have appropriate experience and track record?

- **Innovation**: Does the project challenge paradigms? Does it employ novel concepts, methods, or technologies?

- **Approach**: Is the design and methodology sound? Are analyses and statistical methods appropriate?

- **Environment**: Does the institutional setting support the research? Are resources and facilities adequate?

- **Intellectual Merit (NSF)**: Potential to advance knowledge within the field.

- **Broader Impacts (NSF)**: Potential to benefit society through research, education, infrastructure, or diversity outcomes.

## Scoring System

- **NIH 1-9 Scoring Scale**: 1 = exceptional, 2 = outstanding, 3 = excellent, 4 = very good, 5 = good, 6 = satisfactory, 7 = fair, 8 = marginal, 9 = poor.

- **Criterion Scores**: Individual scores for each of the five NIH review criteria.

- **Overall Impact/Priority Score**: Reflects the overall influence of the proposed research; not an average of criterion scores.

- **Administrative Review**: Initial screening for completeness and compliance; not scientific merit review.

## Application Components

- **Specific Aims**: Concise statement of goals and objectives; typically 1 page; first impression of the application.

- **Research Strategy**: The main body of the application; includes significance, innovation, and approach sections.

- **Biosketch**: PI and key personnel CV formatted per NIH format (five pages); includes personal statement, contributions, and publications.

- **Budget and Budget Justification**: Detailed or modular budget with narrative justification of requested funds.

- **Resources and Environment**: Description of institutional resources, facilities, and equipment available.

- ** Vertebrate Animals Section**: Description of animal use, including justification, species, numbers, and welfare considerations.

- **Human Subjects Section**: Description of recruitment, consent, risks, and protections for human subjects research.

- **Biohazards / Biosafety**: Description of recombinant DNA, infectious agents, or other biohazardous materials.

- **Authentication of Key Resources**: Statement confirming verification of key biological and chemical reagents.

- **Data Management and Sharing Plan**: Plan for managing, sharing, and preserving research data (NIH required).

## Funding Decisions

- **Funded**: Application receives an award; begins negotiation of terms and start date.

- **Not Funded**: Application not selected for funding; summary statement provided for resubmission.

- **Disallowed**: Application excluded from competition for administrative reasons (e.g., non-compliance, duplicate submission).

- **Withdrawn**: Application removed from competition at PI's or institution's request.

- **Pending**: Funding decision deferred; additional information requested (Just-in-Time).

- **Modular Budget**: NIH budget format for direct costs up to $250,000 per year; no detailed line-item budget required.

## Post-Award Terms

- **Award Notice**: Official document authorizing grant and specifying terms and conditions.

- **No-Cost Extension (NCE)**: Extension of project period without additional funds; requires prior approval.

- **Rebudgeting**: Reallocation of funds between budget categories; governed by 2 CFR 200 rules.

- **Carryover**: Transfer of unobligated balances from one budget period to the next.

- **Progress Report (RPPR)**: Annual and final performance progress report submitted by PI.

- **Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI) Report**: Required disclosure and management of financial conflicts.

## Career Development Terms

- **Candidate Quality**: Assessment of the potential of the applicant for an independent research career.

- **Mentorship Plan**: Description of mentor's qualifications and proposed mentoring activities.

- **Career Development Activities**: Training, coursework, and experiences proposed to develop research independence.

- **Letter of Reference**: External letters of support evaluating candidate's qualifications (required for training grants).

- **Transition Probability**: Likelihood that the K99 will successfully transition to R00 independent phase.
