# Grant Reviewer Reference Standards

Standards, policies, and ethical guidelines governing peer review of research grant applications.

## U.S. Federal Grant Agencies

- **National Institutes of Health (NIH)**: The largest federal funder of biomedical and behavioral research; uses a dual peer review system (study sections and Advisory Councils/Boards). [nih.gov](https://nih.gov)
- **National Science Foundation (NSF)**: Funds fundamental research across all science and engineering fields; uses merit review process. [nsf.gov](https://nsf.gov)
- **Department of Energy (DOE)**: Office of Science funds research in basic energy sciences, biological and environmental sciences, advanced computing. [energy.gov](https://energy.gov)
- **National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)**: Science Mission Directorate and research programs. [nasa.gov](https://nasa.gov)
- **U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA)**: National Institute of Food and Agriculture (NIFA) funds agricultural research. [nifa.usda.gov](https://nifa.usda.gov)
- **Department of Defense (DoD)**: Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs (CDMRP), DARPA, basic research funding. [defense.gov](https://defense.gov)
- **Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)**: STAR grants and Science to Achieve Results program. [epa.gov](https://epa.gov)

## NIH Peer Review Standards

- **NIH Center for Scientific Review (CSR)**: Manages the initial peer review of most NIH grant applications through standing study sections. [csr.nih.gov](https://csr.nih.gov)
- **NIH Scoring Framework**: 1-9 scale (1 = exceptional, 9 = poor) on five core review criteria:
  1. Significance
  2. Investigator(s)
  3. Innovation
  4. Approach
  5. Environment
- **NIH Review Criteria**: Additional criteria for specific mechanisms (e.g., R03, R21, R01, P01). [grants.nih.gov](https://grants.nih.gov)
- **NIH Payline System**: Funding cutoffs based on priority score percentile, varies by institute and mechanism.
- **NIH Council/Board Review**: Second level of review by institute advisory councils.
- **Ruth L. Kirschstein National Research Service Award (NRSA)**: Training grant review criteria and stipend levels. [grants.nih.gov](https://grants.nih.gov)

## NSF Merit Review

- **NSF Merit Review Process**: Two criteria — Intellectual Merit and Broader Impacts. [nsf.gov](https://nsf.gov)
- **Broader Impacts**: Activities that benefit society through research outcomes, education, infrastructure, and diversity.
- **NSF Directorates**: Major research funding divisions (BIO, CISE, EDU, ENG, GEO, MPS, SBE, TIP).
- **NSF CAREER Award**: Faculty Early Career Development Program; requires integration of research and education. [nsf.gov](https://nsf.gov)
- **Collaborative Proposals**: Different submission windows and review processes than single-entity proposals.
- **NSF Proposal Certification**: PI certifications regarding proposer's institutional compliance.

## Federal Grant Regulations

- **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)**: Uniform administrative requirements for federal grants, including cost principles and audit requirements. [ecfr.gov](https://ecfr.gov)
- **Bayh-Dole Act**: Governs intellectual property rights from federally funded research. [commerce.gov](https://commerce.gov)
- **Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI)**: 42 CFR Part 50, Subpart F requires institutions to manage financial conflicts in federally funded research.
- **Research Misconduct Policy**: 42 CFR Parts 93 (Public Health Service) and similar policies for NSF-funded research.

## International & Foundation Funding

- **European Research Council (ERC)**: EU funding for frontier research. [erc.europa.eu](https://erc.europa.eu)
- **Wellcome Trust**: UK biomedical research funding; grant review processes. [wellcome.org](https://wellcome.org)
- **Howard Hughes Medical Institute (HHMI)**: Investigator Program and other research funding. [hhmi.org](https://hhmi.org)
- **Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation**: Science and environment funding. [moore.org](https://moore.org)
- **Alfred P. Sloan Foundation**: Research in science, technology, and economics. [sloan.org](https://sloan.org)
- **Canada Foundation for Innovation**: Research infrastructure funding. [innovation.ca](https://innovation.ca)
- **Canadian Institutes of Health Research (CIHR)**: Peer review processes for health research. [cihr-irsc.gc.ca](https://cihr-irsc.gc.ca)
- **Australian National Health and Medical Research Council (NHMRC)**: Research grant peer review. [nhmrc.gov.au](https://nhmrc.gov.au)

## Scientific Review Officers (SRO) Standards

- **SRO Responsibilities**: Manage study sections, ensure fair and timely review, maintain reviewer pool, comply with ethics policies.
- **Conflict of Interest Management**: SROs must recuse themselves from reviews where conflicts exist.
- **Score Desk Rejects**: Applications not recommended for funding without full discussion; SRO protocols for identification.
- **Summary Statement Quality**: Standardized format including critique templates and criterion scores.

## Reviewer Eligibility & Conduct

- **NIH Reviewer Guidelines**: Standards for reviewing NIH grant applications; confidentiality requirements. [csr.nih.gov](https://csr.nih.gov)
- **NSF Reviewer Guidelines**: Merit review principles; confidentiality and conflict of interest. [nsf.gov](https://nsf.gov)
- **Confidentiality**: All grant applications and reviewer identities are confidential. Distribution prohibited.
- **Recusal Requirements**: Reviewers must recuse themselves for personal, professional, or financial conflicts.
- **Bias Prevention**: Training on implicit bias and structured review processes.

## Career Development & Training Grants

- **NIH K-series Awards**: Mentored research career development awards (K01, K08, K23, K99/R00). [grants.nih.gov](https://grants.nih.gov)
- **T32 Training Grants**: Institutional training programs reviewed for training plan, mentor qualifications, trainee outcomes.
- **F30/F31 Pre-doctoral Fellowships**: Training potential, career development plan, mentor and institution evaluation.
- **Review Criteria for Training Grants**: Candidate, mentor, environment, research plan, and training potential.

## Post-Review & Award Management

- **Summary Statement**: Review outcome document including critiques, criterion scores, and priority score.
- **Just-in-Time Information**: Additional documentation requested after review but before funding decision.
- **Modular vs. Detailed Budgets**: NIH modular grants (direct costs up to $250K) vs. detailed budgets for larger amounts.
- **Scoring Change Process**: Procedures for appeal or reconsideration of review outcomes.
- **Resubmission Policies**: NIH A1 (first resubmission), A2 (second resubmission) policies; NSF revised budgets.

## Reproducibility & Rigor

- **NIH Rigor and Reproducibility**: Requirements for rigor, transparency, and robustness in grant applications (NOT-OD-15-103). [grants.nih.gov](https://grants.nih.gov)
- **Authentication of Key Biological and Chemical Resources**: Required for new reagents and cell lines.
- **Sex as a Biological Variable (SABV)**: NIH requirement to address sex/gender in research design.
