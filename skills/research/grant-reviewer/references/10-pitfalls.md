# Grant Reviewer Templates, Checklists & Protocols

Practical tools for reviewing grant applications efficiently and consistently.

## Pre-Review Preparation Checklist

Before beginning the review:

- [ ] Confirm you have no conflicts of interest with the application
- [ ] Download and review the funding opportunity announcement (FOA) and review criteria
- [ ] Familiarize yourself with the specific grant mechanism (R01, R21, K08, etc.)
- [ ] Download the complete application including all sections, appendices, and budgets
- [ ] Review the PI's biosketch and track record
- [ ] Note any mammalian subject, vertebrate animal, or biohazard components
- [ ] Check the specific institute/center and program contact if needed
- [ ] Note the submission date and your deadline for the review

## Initial Read-Through Checklist

During first review of the application:

- [ ] Read the abstract and specific aims first to understand the big picture
- [ ] Note whether the project is hypothesis-driven, exploratory, or methodological
- [ ] Assess whether the proposed work is within the reviewer's field of expertise
- [ ] Flag any components requiring special expertise (biostatistics, animal model, clinical protocol)
- [ ] Check for completeness — are all required sections present?
- [ ] Note the overall organization and clarity of the writing
- [ ] Assess the preliminary data — is it sufficient to support the proposed aims?
- [ ] Form preliminary impressions on each of the five review criteria

## Criterion-by-Criterion Review Checklist

### Significance
- [ ] Does the project address an important problem or barrier in the field?
- [ ] Will the results advance the field if the aims are achieved?
- [ ] Does the application explain why this work is important now?
- [ ] Are the long-term goals well framed in the context of the field?
- [ ] Does the application justify the potential impact of the research?

### Investigator(s)
- [ ] Does the PI have the training and experience to conduct the proposed research?
- [ ] Is the PI's track record (publications, prior funding) appropriate for career stage?
- [ ] Are co-investigators and key personnel qualified for their roles?
- [ ] For career awards: is the mentorship plan credible?
- [ ] Does the team have complementary expertise for all aspects of the project?

### Innovation
- [ ] Does the application challenge existing paradigms or methods?
- [ ] Does it propose novel concepts, approaches, or technologies?
- [ ] Is innovation present in the research questions, methods, or tools?
- [ ] For established fields: is incremental work appropriately framed with novelty?
- [ ] Is the application merely applying known methods to new contexts?

### Approach
- [ ] Is the overall strategy logical, rigorous, and feasible?
- [ ] Are specific aims well-designed and testable?
- [ ] Is the methodology described in sufficient detail to assess feasibility?
- [ ] Are power calculations and statistical methods appropriate?
- [ ] Have potential pitfalls and alternative approaches been considered?
- [ ] Is the timeline realistic?
- [ ] For clinical research: are recruitment plans, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and outcomes measures appropriate?
- [ ] For animal research: is the species appropriate, and is the number justified?
- [ ] Are data analysis plans adequate?
- [ ] Are endpoints and success criteria clearly defined?

### Environment
- [ ] Are institutional resources, equipment, and facilities adequate?
- [ ] Does the environment support the proposed research?
- [ ] Are there institutional strengths that enhance the project?
- [ ] Is there adequate access to patient populations, animal models, or reagents?
- [ ] Does the PI have the necessary protected time for research?

## Compliance and Methodological Checkpoints

### Human Subjects
- [ ] Is the study population clearly defined?
- [ ] Are inclusion/exclusion criteria appropriate?
- [ ] Is the recruitment and consent process adequate?
- [ ] Are risks minimized and proportionate to benefits?
- [ ] Are privacy and confidentiality protections adequate?
- [ ] Is data security plan sufficient for identifiable data?
- [ ] Is sex as a biological variable addressed in design?
- [ ] Are underserved populations appropriately included or justified?

### Vertebrate Animals
- [ ] Is the species and number of animals clearly justified?
- [ ] Are procedures for minimizing pain and distress described?
- [ ] Is euthanasia method consistent with AVMA guidelines?
- [ ] Does the plan address the three Rs (Replacement, Reduction, Refinement)?

### Biohazards / Biosafety
- [ ] Are biohazardous materials identified and appropriate?
- [ ] Is the biosafety level adequate for the agents?
- [ ] Are institutional IBC requirements addressed?

### Data Management
- [ ] Is there a data management and sharing plan?
- [ ] Is the data storage and security plan adequate?
- [ ] Are software, tools, and code sharing plans appropriate?

### Rigor and Reproducibility
- [ ] Has the application addressed rigor of the prior literature?
- [ ] Are there authentication plans for key biological and chemical resources?
- [ ] Is there sufficient experimental detail to enable replication?
- [ ] Are blinding, randomization, and controls adequately described?

## Writing the Critique

### Structure for Each Criterion
1. **Strengths** — What works well in this application?
2. **Weaknesses / Concerns** — What are the deficiencies or areas of concern?
3. **Specific Feedback** — Be concrete: cite pages, figures, and aims
4. **Suggestions for Improvement** — What would make this stronger?
5. **Recommendation** — Does this criterion merit a specific score?

### Scoring Guidance
- **1 (Exceptional)**: Truly outstanding; near-perfect; no weaknesses
- **2 (Outstanding)**: Excellent; very few minor weaknesses
- **3 (Excellent)**: Strong application; some minor weaknesses only
- **4 (Very Good)**: Good application; some weaknesses that could be addressed
- **5 (Good)**: Adequate application; weaknesses are real but correctable
- **6 (Satisfactory)**: Acceptable but with notable weaknesses
- **7 (Fair)**: Significant weaknesses; concern about feasibility
- **8 (Marginal)**: Major weaknesses; unlikely to succeed without major revisions
- **9 (Poor)**: Fundamental problems; unlikely to be fundable

### Checklist for Critique Quality
- [ ] Critique is specific and references application content (pages, figures, sections)
- [ ] Feedback is actionable — PI can use it to strengthen the application
- [ ] Strengths are acknowledged alongside weaknesses
- [ ] Scores are consistent with written critiques
- [ ] Global assessment matches criterion-level ratings
- [ ] Comments are professional, constructive, and respectful
- [ ] Comments do not reveal reviewer identity or biases
- [ ] Reviewer has not been influenced by institutional reputation or prior knowledge of PI

## Panel Discussion Protocol

During the study section meeting:

**Before Your Application is Discussed:**
- [ ] Review summary statements for applications in your expertise area
- [ ] Identify key discussion points for your assigned applications
- [ ] Note any applications where scores may be inconsistent

**During Discussion:**
- [ ] Present your assigned critiques clearly and concisely
- [ ] Focus on substantive scientific and methodological issues
- [ ] Be prepared to answer questions from other panel members
- [ ] Recuse yourself from applications with conflicts
- [ ] Be open to different perspectives from panel colleagues
- [ ] Avoid dominating or deferring inappropriately

**After Discussion:**
- [ ] Ensure your final scores reflect the panel discussion
- [ ] Update written critiques to reflect discussion points if needed
- [ ] Confirm attendance record is accurate
