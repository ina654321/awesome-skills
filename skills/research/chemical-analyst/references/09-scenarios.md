# Research Terminology Glossary

## A

- **Accuracy** — Closeness of agreement between a measured value and true value; expressed as % recovery
- **Analyte** — The specific compound or element being measured
- **Audit trail** — Computerized record of all changes to raw data and analytical results

## B

- **Blank** — Sample processed without analyte to measure background signal or contamination
- **BPR (Back Pressure Regulator)** — Device maintaining pressure in HPLC systems
- **Breakthrough** — When column capacity is exceeded; analyte elutes early or unretained

## C

- **Calibration curve** — Relationship between instrument response and known analyte concentrations
- **CFR (Code of Federal Regulations)** — US federal regulations, e.g., 21 CFR Part 211 (pharmaceuticals)
- **CRM (Certified Reference Material)** — Reference material with certified values traceable to national standards
- **CV (Coefficient of Variation)** — Standard deviation expressed as percentage of mean

## D

- **Detection limit** — Lowest amount of analyte detectable above background noise
- **Dilution integrity** — Confirmation that diluted samples remain accurate and precise
- **Dwell time** — Time instrument spends measuring each mass or wavelength point
- **Dynamic range** — Range of concentrations over which response is linear

## E

- **EICP (Extracted Ion Current Profile)** — Total ion chromatogram from selected masses
- **EP (European Pharmacopeia)** — Regulatory reference for pharmaceutical analysis

## F

- **FID (Flame Ionization Detector)** — GC detector for organic compounds
- **FWHM (Full Width at Half Maximum)** — Peak width at 50% height; measure of chromatographic efficiency

## G

- **Gas Chromatography (GC)** — Separation technique for volatile compounds using gas mobile phase
- **GLP (Good Laboratory Practice)** — Quality system for non-clinical safety studies

## H

- **HPLC (High Performance Liquid Chromatography)** — Separation technique for non-volatile compounds using liquid mobile phase
- **ICP-MS (Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometry)** — Elemental analysis technique with ppt-ppb detection

## I

- **ICH (International Council for Harmonisation)** — Sets pharmaceutical quality guidelines
- **ICP-OES (ICP Optical Emission Spectrometry)** — Elemental analysis using optical detection
- **Ion suppression** — Reduction in analyte ionization in mass spec due to matrix components
- **Ion pairing** — HPLC technique using ion-pairing reagents to separate ionic compounds

## J

- **J-criteria** — Acceptance criteria in Japanese Pharmacopeia for analytical methods

## L

- **LCS (Laboratory Control Sample)** — Blank matrix spiked with known analyte amounts
- **LOD (Limit of Detection)** — Signal/noise ≥3:1; lowest detectable amount
- **LOQ (Limit of Quantitation)** — Lowest amount quantitated with acceptable precision and accuracy
- **Linear dynamic range** — Concentration range over which response is linear

## M

- **Matrix effect** — Influence of sample matrix on analyte response
- **Matrix-matched calibration** — Calibration standards prepared in same matrix as samples
- **Method detection limit (MDL)** — Statistical LOD determined by replicate analysis of low-level samples
- **Method validation** — Experimental confirmation that method meets requirements for intended use
- **MRM (Multiple Reaction Monitoring)** — Tandem MS mode for quantifying specific ion transitions
- **MS/MS (Tandem Mass Spectrometry)** — Two-stage mass analysis for selectivity

## N

- **NIST SRM (National Institute of Standards and Technology Standard Reference Material)** — Certified reference materials for calibration
- **Noise** — Random variation in instrument signal without analyte

## O

- **OCR (Overall Campaign Report)** — Complete analytical report for sample set
- **OOS (Out of Specification)** — Test result not meeting pre-established acceptance criteria
- **OOT (Out of Trend)** — Result consistent with specs but not following historical pattern

## P

- **Plasma** — Region of ionized gas used as ion source in ICP
- **Precision** — Degree of agreement among replicate measurements; expressed as RSD
- **Process blank** — Blank processed through entire sample preparation procedure
- **PT (Proficiency Testing)** — Inter-laboratory comparison to assess performance

## Q

- **QC (Quality Control)** — Procedures ensuring reliable analytical results
- **Quantitation ion** — Primary mass used for analyte quantification in MS
- **Quenching** — Stopping chemical reactions (e.g., enzymatic activity) during sample prep

## R

- **R² (Coefficient of Determination)** — Measure of linearity; ≥0.999 typical for quantitation
- **Recovery** — Percentage of analyte recovered through sample preparation
- **Residual** — Difference between observed and predicted values in calibration
- **Resolution (Rs)** — Measure of chromatographic separation; Rs ≥ 1.5 for baseline separation
- **RSD (Relative Standard Deviation)** — Standard deviation as percentage of mean
- **Ruggedness** — Method robustness under normal variation in environmental/operational conditions

## S

- **Sample preparation** — Physical/chemical treatment of sample before analysis
- **Selectivity** — Ability to measure analyte in presence of interferences
- **Signal-to-noise ratio (S/N)** — Analyte signal relative to background noise
- **S/N (Signal/Noise)** — Ratio used to determine detection and quantitation limits
- **SPE (Solid Phase Extraction)** — Sample cleanup/concentration using solid sorbent
- **Specificity** — Ability to unambiguously identify analyte; includes peak purity
- **Spike/spiking** — Adding known amount of analyte to sample to measure recovery
- **SRM (Standard Reference Material)** — NIST-certified reference material
- **SST (System Suitability Test)** — Pre-run checks ensuring system performance

## T

- **Tailing factor (T)** — Measure of peak symmetry; T = 1 is ideal Gaussian peak
- **TCD (Thermal Conductivity Detector)** — Universal GC detector for non-hydrocarbon compounds
- **Theoretical plates (N)** — Column efficiency measure; higher N = narrower peaks
- **Traceability** — Unbroken chain linking measurement to national/international standards
- **Transitions (MRM)** — Ion pair monitored in tandem MS quantitation

## U

- **UHPLC (Ultra High Performance Liquid Chromatography)** — HPLC operating at higher pressures (>600 bar)
- **USP (United States Pharmacopeia)** — Official compendium of drug standards

## V

- **Validation** — Confirmation through examination that method meets requirements for intended use
- **Verification** — Confirmation that method performs as expected in user's hands

## W

- **Wavelength accuracy** — Correctness of wavelength setting in spectrophotometry

## Y

- **Yield** — Amount of product obtained relative to theoretical maximum
