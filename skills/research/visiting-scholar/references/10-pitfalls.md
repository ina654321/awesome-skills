# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns in Visiting Scholar Applications

| # | Anti-Pattern| Severity| Quick Fix|
|---|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **"Armchair Visiting"** | 🔴 High | Don't propose research without verifying host has necessary infrastructure — contact host before applying |
| 2 | **Single-Institution Focus** | 🔴 High | Apply to 5+ institutions — acceptance rates for individual programs are 10-20% |
| 3 | **Ignoring the Two-Year Rule** | 🔴 High | J-1 visa requires 2-year home residency — factor into career planning |
| 4 | **Vague Research Proposals** | 🟡 Medium | Proposals without specific methodology and deliverables signal lack of preparation |
| 5 | **No Exit Strategy** | 🟡 Medium | Plan handoff from day one — leaving ongoing work reflects poorly on future applications |

## 10.2 Problematic Statements

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead |
|----------|----------------|
| "I want to do machine learning research at Stanford" | "I will develop a novel transformer architecture for climate model downscaling, leveraging Stanford's Earth M3 facility, targeting publication in Nature Climate Change" |
| "This will be a great learning experience" | "I will contribute X to the host lab while developing Y expertise" |

## 10.3 Common Mistakes

### Application Issues
- Not contacting host before applying
- Vague research plans
- No clear benefit to host
- Missing required documents
- Generic proposals

### Visa Issues
- Not starting visa process early enough
- Ignoring two-year rule implications
- Not having adequate insurance
- Missing financial requirements

### Integration Issues
- Not building relationships early
- Working only on your project
- Not engaging with host community
- No exit planning

## 10.4 Quality Checklist

Before Application:
- [ ] Host contacted and confirmed interest
- [ ] Research proposal specific and detailed
- [ ] Host benefit clearly articulated
- [ ] Timeline realistic
- [ ] Documents complete
- [ ] Multiple applications submitted

Before Departure:
- [ ] Visa approved
- [ ] Housing arranged
- [ ] Insurance obtained
- [ ] Workspace confirmed
- [ ] Home institution notified
- [ ] Emergency contacts shared

During Visit:
- [ ] Regular check-ins with host
- [ ] Relationships being built
- [ ] Research progressing
- [ ] Exit planning started

Before Departure:
- [ ] Work documented
- [ ] Handoffs complete
- [ ] Relationships maintained
- [ ] Follow-up planned
