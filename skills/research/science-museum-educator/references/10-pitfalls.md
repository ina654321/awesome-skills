# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns in Museum Education

| # | Anti-Pattern| Severity| Quick Fix|
|---|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Lecture in a museum** | 🔴 High | Museums are for experiencing, not listening — keep talking to <30% of time |
| 2 | **Yes/No questions only** | 🟡 Medium | Use open questions: "What do you think...?" "Why might that happen?" |
| 3 | **Ignoring visitor questions** | 🟡 Medium | If a visitor asks, it's a teachable moment — answer or explore together |
| 4 | **One-size-fits-all** | 🟡 Medium | Different ages, backgrounds, interests — offer choices |
| 5 | **Answer-focused** | 🟡 Medium | The question is as valuable as the answer — model curiosity |

## 10.2 Problematic Practices

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead |
|----------|----------------|
| "Watch this demonstration and I'll explain the science." | "What do you think will happen? Now let's see... What did you notice?" |
| "Are you having fun?" | "What did you discover?" or "What surprised you?" |
| Talk for 20 minutes straight | 2-3 minutes of talking max, then hands-on |

## 10.3 Common Mistakes

### Facilitation Issues
- Too much talking
- Questions not open
- Not listening to visitors
- Rushing through experiences

### Program Design
- Too much content
- Not age-appropriate
- No hands-on component
- No clear objectives

### Safety Issues
- Not monitoring hazards
- Inappropriate for age group
- Missing emergency protocols

## 10.4 Age-Appropriate Adaptation

### Young Children (Ages 3-7)
- Simple, concrete concepts
- Lots of touching/exploring
- Short activities (5-10 minutes)
- Focus on senses

### Children (Ages 8-12)
- More complex explanations
- Hands-on experiments
- Connect to everyday life
- Allow some independence

### Teenagers
- More sophisticated concepts
- Allow more choice
- Connect to real-world issues
- Respect their knowledge

### Adults
- Can handle more detail
- Appreciate context
- May have specific questions
- Want to learn for themselves

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

Before session:
- [ ] Clear learning objectives defined
- [ ] Activities appropriate for audience
- [ ] Safety considerations addressed
- [ ] Materials prepared
- [ ] Questions ready

During session:
- [ ] Visitors engaged hands-on
- [ ] Questions are open-ended
- [ ] Listening to visitors
- [ ] Adapting to interest
- [ ] Summarizing key points
