# Research Terminology Glossary

## A

- **Abstract** — A structured summary (250-300 words) summarizing the research question, methods, results, and conclusions
- **Aim/Hypothesis** — The specific research question or testable prediction the study addresses
- **Anonymization** — Removing identifying information from data or documents
- **Appendices** — Supplementary materials (methods details, raw data) attached to the main manuscript

## B

- **Biosketch** — A 5-page document describing an investigator's education, employment, publications, and research support (NIH format)
- **Boolean operators** — AND, OR, NOT used to refine database searches

## C

- **Cover letter** — A letter to the journal editor accompanying manuscript submission explaining the work's significance
- **CRediT taxonomy** — Contributor Roles Taxonomy defining 14 roles in scholarly contributions
- **Conflict of interest (COI)** — Financial or personal relationships that may bias research objectivity

## D

- **Data availability statement** — A section describing where supporting data can be accessed
- **doi/DOI** — Digital Object Identifier, a unique alphanumeric string assigned to scholarly articles
- **Draft** — An early version of a manuscript; different from preprint

## E

- **e-Print** — Electronic preprint versions posted on servers like arXiv or bioRxiv
- **Ethics approval** — Documentation that an IRB or ethics committee reviewed and approved the research protocol

## F

- **FAIR principles** — Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable guidelines for data management
- **First author** — The researcher who made the primary intellectual contribution; listed first

## G

- **Galley proofs** — Final formatted version of an article before publication for author review
- **Ghost authorship** — Undisclosed writing assistance that constitutes an ethical violation

## H

- **h-index** — Metric measuring both productivity and citation impact of a researcher's publications

## I

- **Impact factor** — Journal metric based on average citations per article over a two-year period
- **IMRaD** — Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion — standard structure for research papers
- **Intellectual property** — Creations of the mind protected by patents, copyrights, or trade secrets

## J

- **Journal Impact Factor (JIF)** — Bibliometric measure calculated by Clarivate (formerly Thomson Reuters)

## L

- **Lead author** — First author or corresponding author; primary contributor
- **Literature review** — Survey of existing scholarly sources on a topic

## M

- **Methodology** — The overall system of methods and principles used in research
- **Middle author** — Authors between first and last positions, typically contributing specific expertise
- **Mosaic/Parallel publication** — Publishing the same research in multiple journals without disclosure

## N

- **Corresponding author** — The author responsible for communication with the journal; listed with contact details
- **No-cost extension (NCE)** — An extension of grant period without additional funding

## O

- **ORCID** — Open Researcher and Contributor Identifier, a persistent digital identifier for researchers
- **Originality** — The requirement that submitted work must be original and not previously published

## P

- **Peer review** — Evaluation of scholarly work by experts in the same field
- **Postprint** — Author's accepted manuscript after peer review, before journal formatting
- **Preprint** — A draft version of a scholarly paper posted publicly before formal peer review
- **Preprint servers** — Platforms for posting preprints: arXiv (physics, math, CS), bioRxiv (biology), medRxiv (medicine), ChemRxiv (chemistry), PsyArXiv (psychology)
- **Corresponding author** — The author responsible for communication with the journal

## R

- **Reproducibility** — The ability to replicate results using the same methods and data
- **Research integrity** — Adherence to ethical principles in conducting and reporting research

## S

- **Scholarly communication** — The system through which research is evaluated, distributed, and preserved
- **Senior author** — Last author, typically the principal investigator or lab director
- **Salami slicing** — Dividing a single study into multiple publications inappropriately
- **Structured abstract** — An abstract with labeled sections (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions)

## T

- **Technical editing** — Language and formatting review for technical accuracy and consistency
- **Transparency** — Open reporting of methods, data, and potential conflicts of interest

## V

- **Vetting** — Initial editorial review to determine if a manuscript is appropriate for the journal
