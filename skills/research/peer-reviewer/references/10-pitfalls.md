# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns in Peer Review

| # | Anti-Pattern| Severity| Quick Fix|
|---|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **"The novelty is insufficient"** without defining what would be novel | 🔴 High | Specify what additional contribution would elevate the paper |
| 2 | **Comparing to your own work** rather than evaluating on its merits | 🔴 High | Remove references to your research; evaluate the submitted work |
| 3 | **Accepting or rejecting based on prestige** of authors or institution | 🔴 High | Strip identifying information; evaluate manuscript independently |
| 4 | **Minor issues raised as major** (e.g., "change figure colors") | 🟡 Medium | Reserve "Major" for fundamental methodological flaws |
| 5 | **Copy-paste from reviewer template** without addressing specific manuscript | 🟡 Medium | Every comment should reference specific sections or lines |

## 10.2 Problematic Review Phrases

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead |
|----------|----------------|
| "The novelty is insufficient" | "The contribution would be strengthened if the authors could demonstrate X" |
| "This is well-known" | "This approach is established in the literature (cite), but the application to Y is novel" |
| "The writing is poor" | "The manuscript would benefit from editing for clarity in Section X" |
| "This is outside my expertise" | "I cannot evaluate the methodological approach in Section Y due to its specialized nature" |
| "I would have done it differently" | "Alternative approaches the authors may consider include..." |

## 10.3 Major vs. Minor Issues Distinction

**Major Issues (require revision to accept):**
- Fundamental methodological flaws that invalidate conclusions
- Missing critical controls or data
- Statistical analyses that cannot support the claims
- Claims that exceed what the data can support
- Missing ethical approvals or data availability statements

**Minor Issues (can be addressed in revision):**
- Typos, grammatical errors
- Figure formatting issues
- Missing or incomplete references
- Abbreviations not defined
- Minor clarifications needed

## 10.4 Bias Detection Checklist

Before finalizing your review, verify:
- [ ] Did I evaluate the manuscript on its scientific merits alone?
- [ ] Did I check for any identifying information that might bias my review?
- [ ] Am I applying the same standards I would for any paper in this field?
- [ ] Did I avoid language that could be perceived as dismissive or condescending?
- [ ] Did I provide specific, actionable feedback rather than vague criticism?

## 10.5 Quality Assurance Steps

1. **Read the manuscript completely** before drafting any comments
2. **Check each figure and table** against claims in text
3. **Verify all statistical claims** are supported by reported statistics
4. **Confirm all citations** are relevant and accurate
5. **Test your suggestions** — would they actually improve the paper?
6. **Consider the authors' perspective** — is the feedback you'd want to receive?
