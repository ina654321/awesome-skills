# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Complete Manuscript Review Process

### Phase 1: Initial Assessment (15-30 minutes)

**Step 1.1 - Abstract and Title Review**
- Read the abstract carefully — it should summarize the entire study
- Evaluate if the title accurately reflects the content
- Note: A weak abstract often indicates weak methodology

**Step 1.2 - Structural Scan**
- Skim all sections: Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion
- Check for logical flow and completeness
- Identify the primary research question and hypothesis

**Step 1.3 - Initial Impression Formation**
- Note overall quality level: exceptional, acceptable, problematic
- Identify any immediately apparent major issues
- Decision: Proceed to detailed review OR return for revisions

### Phase 2: Detailed Section Review (60-120 minutes)

**Step 2.1 - Introduction Evaluation**
- Problem statement: Is the significance clearly established?
- Literature review: Is prior work properly cited and acknowledged?
- Hypothesis: Is it specific, testable, and well-justified?

**Step 2.2 - Methods Assessment**
- Study design: Appropriate for the research question?
- Sample size: Is power analysis provided and adequate?
- Controls: Are appropriate controls included?
- Statistical methods: Are tests appropriate for data types?
- Reproducibility: Are methods described in sufficient detail?

**Step 2.3 - Results Analysis**
- Data presentation: Are figures clear and properly labeled?
- Statistical reporting: Are p-values, confidence intervals included?
- Claims support: Do results actually support the conclusions?

**Step 2.4 - Discussion Evaluation**
- Interpretation: Are conclusions reasonable given the data?
- Limitations: Are weaknesses honestly acknowledged?
- Significance: Is impact clearly articulated?

### Phase 3: Feedback Drafting (30-60 minutes)

**Step 3.1 - Major Concerns Identification**
- List fundamental flaws that block publication
- Each major issue should include specific location (e.g., "page 5, paragraph 2")

**Step 3.2 - Minor Concerns Documentation**
- Polish items that would strengthen the manuscript
- Organize by section (Introduction, Methods, Results, etc.)

**Step 3.3 - Constructive Framing**
- Lead with strengths before detailed criticism
- Provide specific suggestions for improvement

### Phase 4: Final Review and Submission

**Step 4.1 - Tone Check**
- Is the feedback constructive, not destructive?
- Would you want to receive this review as an author?

**Step 4.2 - Completeness Verification**
- All sections reviewed? All figures and tables addressed?
- Recommendation clearly stated?

## 8.2 Quick Review Workflow (Conference Abstracts)

1. Read the research question
2. Assess novelty claim (vs. recent literature)
3. Check methodology described
4. Verify conclusions match results
5. Output: Summary + 3-5 specific suggestions

## 8.3 Post-Decision Revision Review

When evaluating a revised manuscript:
- Check if all reviewer concerns were addressed
- Look for new issues introduced in revision
- Verify authors didn't overreach beyond the data
- Assess whether revision improved the paper overall
