# Journal Editor Glossary & Key Terms

Essential terminology for associate editors and editorial roles in scientific journals.

## Editorial Roles

- **Editor-in-Chief (EIC)**: Final authority on all editorial decisions; responsible for journal's scientific quality and integrity.

- **Associate Editor (AE)**: Handles assigned manuscripts; manages peer review; makes editorial recommendations to EIC.

- **Statistical Editor**: Specialist editor for statistical methods review; assigned to manuscripts with complex quantitative analyses.

- **Editorial Board Member**: Recognized expert who reviews manuscripts and provides strategic guidance.

- **Guest Editor**: Expert invited to curate a special issue; manages reviews for that issue.

- **Managing Editor / Journal Coordinator**: Professional staff managing workflow, communications, and production.

## Manuscript Lifecycle

- **Submission**: Author submits manuscript through journal tracking system (ScholarOne, Editorial Manager, Open Journal Systems, etc.).

- **Administrative Pre-Screen**: Editorial office verifies completeness — required sections, disclosures, figures, compliance with author guidelines.

- **Manuscript Triage / Desk Rejection**: Editor-in-chief or associate editor rejects manuscript before peer review due to poor fit, quality, or scope.

- **Peer Review Assignment**: Associate editor selects and invites qualified reviewers.

- **Reviewer Invitation**: Email to potential reviewers with abstract, deadline, and review criteria.

- **Under Review**: Manuscript is with reviewers; status tracked in manuscript system.

- **Review Complete**: All reviewer reports received; associate editor reviews reports.

- **Revision Requested**: Authors asked to respond to reviewer comments and revise manuscript.

- **Revised Manuscript Submitted**: Author resubmits with point-by-point response to reviewers.

- **Acceptance**: Final approval to publish; manuscript proceeds to production.

- **Rejection**: Manuscript not accepted; may be rejected after review or desk rejected.

- **Transfer**: Manuscript moved to another journal (often within the same publisher family).

## Peer Review Terms

- **Anonymized Review**: Reviewer identity hidden from authors (single-blind) or both parties hidden (double-blind).

- **Open Review**: Reviewer identity disclosed to authors; may include published reviewer reports.

- **Post-Publication Review**: Peer evaluation occurs after online publication; may be formal (PeerJ, F1000Research) or informal (PubPeer).

- **Structured Review Template**: Standardized review form requiring responses to specific criteria.

- **Reviewer Recommendation**: Reviewer's suggested editorial decision (accept, minor revision, major revision, reject).

- **Reviewer Conflict of Interest**: Personal, financial, or professional relationship requiring reviewer withdrawal.

## Article Types

- **Research Article**: Original empirical research; full description of methods, results, and conclusions.

- **Review Article**: Comprehensive summary of existing research; narrative or systematic approach.

- **Systematic Review**: Explicit, reproducible methodology for literature identification and synthesis; follows PRISMA.

- **Meta-Analysis**: Statistical combination of results from multiple studies addressing the same question.

- **Case Report**: Description of unusual or novel clinical cases; typically brief.

- **Letter / Correspondence**: Commentary on published articles; brief research findings; questions for authors.

- **Short Communication**: Preliminary but significant findings in condensed format.

- **Perspective / Commentary**: Expert opinion and analysis of scientific issues.

- **Study Protocol**: Detailed pre-specified methods for a planned study; peer-reviewed before data collection.

- **Registered Report**: Manuscript format where methods are peer-reviewed before data collection; publication guaranteed regardless of results.

## Publication Ethics Terms

- **Fabrication**: Inventing or making up data, results, or records.

- **Falsification**: Manipulating research materials, equipment, or processes; changing, omitting, or suppressing data.

- **Plagiarism**: Presenting others' work, ideas, or text as one's own without attribution.

- **Redundant Publication**: Publishing overlapping or substantially similar work without disclosure; self-plagiarism.

- **Image Manipulation**: Electronically altering images beyond standard adjustments; includes gel splicing and selective cropping.

- **Citation Manipulation**: Artificially inflating citations through coercive citation, self-citation rings, or agreement to cite.

- **Peer Review Manipulation**: Fabricating reviewer identities; reviewer "rings"; fake peer review accounts.

- **Competing Interest / Conflict of Interest**: Financial, professional, or personal interests that could bias judgment.

- **Ghostwriting / Ghost Authorship**: Professional writer contributes without acknowledgment; often industry-funded.

- **Gift Authorship / Honorary Authorship**: Listing individuals who did not make substantial contributions.

## Corrections & Retractions

- **Erratum / Correction**: Notice of publisher's or author's error in a published article.

- **Addendum**: Supplementary information published after the original article.

- **Expression of Concern**: Publisher's note indicating concerns about a published article's integrity or reliability.

- **Retraction**: Formal withdrawal of a published article; published notice explains reason (error, misconduct, unreliable).

- **Retraction Index**: List of retracted articles; used to track retraction rates and patterns.

## Open Access Terms

- **Open Access (OA)**: Free, unrestricted online access to scholarly articles.

- **Gold Open Access**: Article published in an open access journal; immediate access.

- **Green Open Access / Self-Archiving**: Author deposits accepted manuscript in repository; embargo may apply.

- **Hybrid Journal**: Subscription journal offering open access for individual articles (APC charged).

- **Diamond / Platinum OA**: Open access without author fees; funded by institutions or sponsors.

- **Article Processing Charge (APC)**: Fee for open access publication; varies by journal.

- **Plan S Compliance**: Journals must use CC-BY license and provide immediate open access.

## Data & Reproducibility

- **Data Availability Statement**: Author's declaration of where underlying data can be accessed.

- **Data Sharing Plan**: Pre-specified statement of how data will be shared (repository, on request, not shared).

- **FAIR Data Principles**: Data should be Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable.

- **Research Resource Identifiers (RRID)**: Unique identifiers for antibodies, cell lines, model organisms, and software tools.

- **Preregistration**: Registration of study design and analysis plan before data collection (OSF, ClinicalTrials.gov).

- **Replication**: Independent study designed to reproduce and verify prior findings.

## Metrics

- **Journal Impact Factor (JIF)**: Average citations per article in a 2-year window; use critically.

- **CiteScore**: Average citations per document; 4-year window; Scopus metric.

- **Article Influence Score**: Eigenfactor metric measuring article influence per citation.

- **Altmetric Score**: Weighted score of online attention from news, social media, policy documents, and blogs.

- **h-index (Journal)**: The h number of articles with at least h citations each.

- **Article-Level Metrics**: Citation and usage data at the individual article level.

## Submission Systems

- **ScholarOne**: Manuscript submission and peer review platform (Clarivate/Clarivate).

- **Editorial Manager**: Manuscript submission and peer review platform (Aries Systems/Elsevier).

- **Open Journal Systems (OJS)**: Open source journal management and publishing platform (Public Knowledge Project).

- **BenchPress / PeerJ**: Manuscript submission and peer review system (PeerJ).

- **EM (Elsevier)**: Elsevier's journal management system.
