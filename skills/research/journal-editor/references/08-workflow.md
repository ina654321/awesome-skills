# Journal Editor Workflow Reference

Common workflow issues in scientific journal editorial work.

## Reviewer Management Issues

- **Problem**: Reviewers agree to review but do not submit within the agreed timeframe.
  - **Issue**: Editorial delays; authors frustrated; manuscript aging.
  - **Reference**: COPE flowchart for reviewers who do not respond; send reminders at appropriate intervals.

- **Problem**: Authors suggest reviewers who are not qualified or have undisclosed conflicts.
  - **Issue**: Inadequate review quality; compromised peer review; reviewer bias.
  - **Reference**: COPE guidance on author-suggested reviewers; verify reviewer credentials independently.

- **Problem**: Insufficient reviewer diversity (same reviewers repeatedly used for related manuscripts).
  - **Issue**: Review fatigue; reviewer burnout; limited methodological diversity in reviews.
  - **Reference**: Journal diversity initiatives; expand reviewer database; engage new reviewers.

## Conflict of Interest Issues

- **Problem**: Associate editor has a competing interest with the authors and does not recuse.
  - **Issue**: Appearance of bias; unfair editorial decisions; damaged trust.
  - **Reference**: ICMJE conflict of interest standards; journal COI policy; COPE flowcharts.

- **Problem**: Reviewer identifies a conflict after accepting the review but does not withdraw.
  - **Issue**: Contaminated review process; potential bias.
  - **Reference**: Journal COI policy; clear reviewer instructions to withdraw on conflict identification.

- **Problem**: Authors fail to disclose significant competing interests.
  - **Issue**: Post-publication discovery damages journal credibility; potential retraction.
  - **Reference**: ICMJE disclosure requirements; systematic disclosure checking.

## Manuscript Quality Issues

- **Problem**: Manuscripts with severe methodological flaws sent for external review.
  - **Issue**: Wasted reviewer time; inconsistent editorial quality; author frustration.
  - **Reference**: Clear internal assessment criteria; associate editor training; editorial triage checklists.

- **Problem**: Language quality so poor that reviewers cannot evaluate the science.
  - **Issue**: Reviewers refuse to review; wasted reviewer time; unfair to authors.
  - **Reference**: Journal English language requirements; pre-screening for language; professional editing services.

- **Problem**: Statistical methods inadequate; reviewers miss critical statistical errors.
  - **Issue**: Methodologically flawed studies published; reproducibility concerns.
  - **Reference**: Statistical review requirement for complex studies; training in statistical peer review.

## Decision Consistency Issues

- **Problem**: Similar manuscripts receive different decisions (accept vs. reject).
  - **Issue**: Perceived unfairness; author complaints; inconsistent journal standards.
  - **Reference**: Standardized decision criteria; calibration among associate editors; periodic quality reviews.

- **Problem**: Revision requests excessive; manuscripts bounce back repeatedly for minor issues.
  - **Issue**: Publication delays; author frustration; reviewer fatigue.
  - **Reference**: ICMJE guidelines on revision scope; clear guidance on what constitutes minor vs. major revision.

## Integrity & Misconduct Issues

- **Problem**: Plagiarism detected after review but before publication.
  - **Issue**: Publication of plagiarized work; time lost; potential legal exposure.
  - **Reference**: COPE flowchart on plagiarism; similarity report analysis; COPE guidelines on text recycling.

- **Problem**: Image manipulation suspected but not investigated before acceptance.
  - **Issue**: Manipulated images published; post-publication corrections or retractions.
  - **Reference**: Journal image integrity policy; screening tools; COPE flowchart on image concerns.

- **Problem**: Data not available upon request during review.
  - **Issue**: Reviewers unable to evaluate claims; concerns about reproducibility.
  - **Reference**: Data availability request during review; journal data sharing policy.

## Review Quality Issues

- **Problem**: Reviewers provide generic feedback that does not address the manuscript.
  - **Issue**: Unhelpful to authors; editorial decisions lack evidentiary basis.
  - **Reference**: Structured review templates; editor feedback on review quality; reviewer training.

- **Problem**: Reviewer recommends accept or reject without adequate justification.
  - **Issue**: Difficult to make editorial decision; author receives unhelpful feedback.
  - **Reference**: Minimum review requirements; editor solicitation of detailed feedback.

## Revision & Resubmission Issues

- **Problem**: Authors address reviewer comments superficially without making substantive changes.
  - **Issue**: Reviewer frustration; potential for rejection; publication of inadequate work.
  - **Reference**: Clear revision requirements; point-by-point response review; re-review when warranted.

- **Problem**: Authors submit revision with new data not requested by reviewers.
  - **Issue**: Reviewers must re-evaluate scope beyond original request; delays.
  - **Reference**: Clear guidance on revision scope; editor determination of whether new data requires new review.

## Special Issues & Collections Issues

- **Problem**: Guest editors for special issues not screened for conflicts or qualifications.
  - **Issue**: Guest editors with competing interests or inadequate expertise; reduced quality.
  - **Reference**: Guest editor qualification criteria; oversight of special issue quality.

- **Problem**: Special issue timelines not met; manuscripts accumulate without resolution.
  - **Issue**: Delays for authors; inconsistent review standards; journal reputation damage.
  - **Reference**: Clear special issue timelines; EIC oversight of guest editor performance.

## Appeals & Complaints Issues

- **Problem**: Authors appeal editorial decisions with inadequate evidence or argumentation.
  - **Issue**: Editorial time spent on unfounded appeals; no clear framework for appeals.
  - **Reference**: Journal appeals procedure; COPE guidance on appeals; criteria for appeal acceptance.
