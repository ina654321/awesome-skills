# Engineering Consultant Templates, Checklists & Protocols

Practical tools for managing engineering consulting projects from proposal through closeout.

## Pre-Proposal Checklist

Before submitting a proposal or fee estimate:

- [ ] Obtain and review all available project documents (architectural drawings, existing surveys, geotechnical reports, prior studies)
- [ ] Visit site or review recent site documentation
- [ ] Identify all applicable codes and standards for the jurisdiction
- [ ] Confirm occupancy classification, use, and risk category
- [ ] Identify all disciplines required and determine if subconsultants are needed
- [ ] Define scope of services in detail, including specific deliverables
- [ ] List all exclusions and assumptions clearly
- [ ] Review client references and assess project risk
- [ ] Verify professional liability insurance coverage is adequate for project
- [ ] Check for conflicts of interest with current and past clients
- [ ] Confirm licensing requirements for the project jurisdiction
- [ ] Define fee structure (lump sum, time-and-materials, hourly, percentage)
- [ ] Establish project schedule milestones and deliverable dates
- [ ] Review contract terms before signing (payment terms, liability cap, IP ownership)

## Contract Review Protocol

Reviewing engineering services contracts:

**Key Clauses to Evaluate:**
- Scope definition — is it clear, or does it invite scope creep?
- Deliverable specifications — are they objective and achievable?
- Fee and payment terms — are milestones aligned with deliverables?
- Limitation of liability — is it capped at an appropriate level?
- Indemnification — does it cover professional negligence only, or broader liability?
- Insurance requirements — do they match your coverage?
- Termination provisions — is there a fair exit for both parties?
- Change order process — is there a formal process before extra work begins?
- Ownership of work product — who owns the design documents?
- Dispute resolution — is arbitration or mediation preferred over litigation?

**Reference**: NSPE contracts and ACEC contract negotiation guidance. [nspe.org](https://nspe.org), [acec.org](https://acec.org)

## Design Phase QA/QC Checklist

Before stamping and issuing design documents:

- [ ] Verify all applicable loads per ASCE 7 (dead, live, snow, wind, seismic, rain, flood)
- [ ] Confirm load combinations match the specified design methodology (LRFD or ASD)
- [ ] Check that materials meet code-specified strength requirements
- [ ] Verify deflection criteria are satisfied for all members
- [ ] Confirm structural system is continuous and complete load path exists
- [ ] Check drift limits per IBC for the applicable risk category
- [ ] Verify all referenced standards and specifications are current editions
- [ ] Confirm fire and life safety requirements (egress, fire resistance ratings)
- [ ] Check coordination with architectural, MEP, and subconsultant drawings
- [ ] Verify foundation design is consistent with geotechnical report recommendations
- [ ] Confirm delegated design elements are clearly identified with design criteria
- [ ] Review calculations for arithmetic errors and logical consistency
- [ ] Independent peer review of structural calculations by second PE
- [ ] Check that seismic detailing requirements are met (high ductility, SDC B-F) per ASCE 7
- [ ] Confirm connection designs are consistent with analysis assumptions
- [ ] Verify accessibility (ADA) requirements are addressed

## Site Observation Protocol

Performing site visits during construction:

**Before the Visit:**
- [ ] Review approved drawings, specifications, and applicable codes
- [ ] Identify items requiring observation based on project schedule
- [ ] Bring approved drawings, specifications, and site visit report forms
- [ ] Know the special inspection requirements for the visit

**During the Visit:**
- [ ] Document the date, time, weather, and personnel present
- [ ] Photograph progress and any observed conditions
- [ ] Compare observed work to approved documents
- [ ] Note any deviations from approved design
- [ ] Review contractor's quality control measures
- [ ] Verify materials stored on-site match approved submittals
- [ ] Document any RFIs issued and their status
- [ ] Note any work that should not proceed until issues are resolved

**After the Visit:**
- [ ] Prepare written site observation report within 24-48 hours
- [ ] Distribute report to owner, contractor, architect, and relevant parties
- [ ] Follow up on critical issues with immediate written notification
- [ ] Track unresolved issues through project closeout

## Change Order Protocol

Managing scope changes and additional services:

1. **Identify** the work that is beyond the current scope of services
2. **Document** the basis for the change (RFI response, site condition, client request, code change)
3. **Quantify** the additional fee and schedule impact
4. **Prepare** a written change order proposal with scope description, fee, and schedule
5. **Obtain** written client authorization before proceeding with additional work
6. **Execute** the work per the authorized change order
7. **Track** change order status throughout the project
8. **Invoice** for completed additional services per change order terms

**Reference**: EJCDC C-940 for engineering services contracts; standard change order forms available from NSPE and EJCDC.

## Project Closeout Checklist

At project substantial completion:

- [ ] Confirm all deliverables have been submitted and accepted
- [ ] Prepare and transmit record drawings reflecting as-built conditions
- [ ] Compile project correspondence and documentation
- [ ] Confirm all RFIs have been resolved and documented
- [ ] Confirm all submittals have been reviewed and approved
- [ ] Verify special inspection and testing reports are complete
- [ ] Issue certificate of completion or final inspection report
- [ ] Submit final invoice with all supporting documentation
- [ ] Archive all project records per firm's retention policy
- [ ] Conduct project closeout meeting with client
- [ ] Document lessons learned for future project improvements

## Professional Liability Risk Checklist

Ongoing risk management practices:

- [ ] Maintain current professional liability (E&O) insurance
- [ ] Use written contracts for all engagements
- [ ] Document all client communications (email, meeting minutes)
- [ ] Obtain written change orders before extra work
- [ ] Keep project files for statutory period (recommend 10+ years)
- [ ] Provide written disclaimers for preliminary or conceptual work
- [ ] Never stamp documents not personally prepared and reviewed
- [ ] Stay current with applicable codes and standards
- [ ] Complete required continuing education for license renewal
- [ ] Report potential claims to your insurance carrier promptly
