# Engineering Consultant Glossary & Key Terms

Essential terminology for engineering consulting practice.

## Professional Practice Terms

- **PE (Professional Engineer)**: Licensed engineer who has passed the NCEES PE exam and met state experience requirements, qualified to offer engineering services directly to the public and seal engineering documents.

- **SE (Structural Engineer)**: Advanced licensure or certification indicating competence in structural engineering; NCEES offers the SE exam covering vertical and lateral loads.

- **Engineer of Record (EOR)**: The licensed engineer who takes responsibility for the engineering design, signs and seals the documents, and is accountable to the authority having jurisdiction.

- **Prime Consultant / Engineer of Record**: The lead engineering firm contracted directly by the client, responsible for overall project coordination and deliverables.

- **Subconsultant**: A specialist engineer contracted by the prime consultant to provide design services for specific disciplines (e.g., geotechnical, electrical, mechanical).

- **Delegated Design**: Elements for which the contractor is responsible for detailed design, subject to the prime engineer's criteria and approval (e.g., metal decking, open-web steel joists).

- **Peer Review**: Independent review of design calculations and drawings by a qualified engineer not involved in the original design, common for complex or high-profile projects.

- **Independent Technical Review (ITR)**: Formal QA process for major projects, typically involving multiple reviewers checking calculations, drawings, and specifications.

## Project Delivery Methods

- **Design-Bid-Build**: Traditional sequential delivery where the designer completes design before contractor selection.

- **Design-Build**: Single entity provides both design and construction services; consultant may be a subconsultant to the design-build team.

- **Construction Manager at Risk (CMAR)**: Construction manager provides preconstruction services and takes on construction risk; often involved during design.

- **Integrated Project Delivery (IPD)**: Collaborative delivery method with shared risk/reward among owner, designer, and contractor.

## Codes & Standards Terms

- **Occupancy Classification**: IBC classification determining occupancy type (A, B, E, H, I, M, R, S, U) that drives code requirements.

- **Use and Occupancy Load**: The maximum expected occupancy load used for egress and structural design, determined per IBC Chapter 10 and ASCE 7.

- **Strength Design (LRFD)**: Load and Resistance Factor Design — design methodology using load factors and resistance factors for ultimate strength design.

- **Allowable Stress Design (ASD)**: Design methodology using working stresses and service loads for member sizing.

- **Load Combinations**: Combinations of dead, live, wind, seismic, and other loads per ASCE 7 for strength design verification.

- **Risk Category**: IBC classification (I through IV) determining seismic, wind, and snow load importance factors based on building occupancy and risk.

- **Prescriptive vs. Performance-Based Design**: Code compliance via specified methods vs. alternative approaches meeting stated performance objectives.

## Engineering Analysis Terms

- **Load Path**: The continuous path through structural elements transferring loads from point of application to foundation.

- **Limit State**: Condition beyond which a structure or element no longer longer satisfies prescribed performance criteria (strength limit state, serviceability limit state).

- **Deflection Criteria**: Maximum allowable deflection limits for structural elements to prevent cracking, occupant discomfort, or visual distress.

- **Drift**: Lateral displacement of a structure under wind or seismic loads; limited by IBC drift limits to protect non-structural elements.

- **P-Delta Effect**: Second-order effect where axial loads interact with lateral displacements, amplifying moments in columns and walls.

- **Yield Line Theory**: Plastic analysis method for reinforced concrete slabs and footings based on yield line failure mechanisms.

- **Soil-Structure Interaction (SSI)**: Effects of flexible foundation behavior on structural response, particularly important for seismic design.

## Contract & Business Terms

- **Change Order**: Formal document authorizing a modification to the contract scope, schedule, or fee.

- **Work Change Directive**: Directive to proceed with changes before agreement on cost/schedule impact, typically used when urgency requires proceeding before a change order is executed.

- **Request for Proposal (RFP)**: Solicitation document requesting detailed proposals from consultants for a specific project.

- **Request for Qualifications (RFQ)**: Solicitation document requesting qualifications, experience, and approach; fee may be requested separately or later.

- **Prime Rate / Market Rate**: Hourly billing rates for professional engineering services, ranging widely by discipline, experience, and market.

- **Reimbursable Expenses**: Non-labor costs directly related to the project (travel, printing, subconsultant fees, laboratory testing).

- **Not-to-Exceed (NTE) Fee**: Maximum fee cap for a defined scope; changes beyond scope require amendment.

- **Retainer**: Advance payment securing consultant availability and establishing credit terms.

## Quality & Documentation Terms

- **As-Built Drawings**: Drawings reflecting the constructed condition of the project, updated during construction and provided at project closeout.

- **Record Drawings**: Official as-built documents sealed by the engineer of record, provided to the owner.

- **Shop Drawings**: Detailed fabrication and installation drawings prepared by the contractor or supplier, reviewed and approved by the engineer of record.

- **Special Inspection**: Inspection required by IBC Chapter 17, performed by qualified individuals independent of the contractor, to verify compliance with approved documents.

- **Seal (Professional Stamp)**: Attestation by a licensed engineer that documents were prepared in accordance with applicable standards of care and professional licensure requirements.

- **Standard of Care**: The degree of care, skill, and diligence ordinarily exercised by competent professionals in the same field under similar conditions.

## Project Management Terms

- **Critical Path Method (CPM)**: Scheduling technique identifying the longest sequence of dependent activities determining minimum project duration.

- **Request for Information (RFI)**: Formal question from contractor to design team requesting clarification or information not in the contract documents.

- **Substantial Completion**: Point at which the project is sufficiently complete for its intended use; triggers inspections, punch list creation, and occupancy permit applications.

- **Notice to Proceed**: Formal authorization from the owner to the consultant to commence work per the contract.
