# Instrument Manager Glossary & Key Terms

Essential terminology for scientific instrumentation management.

## Calibration & Metrology Terms

- **Calibration**: Determination of the relationship between instrument output and known reference values; establishes accuracy.

- **Verification**: Confirmation that an instrument meets specified requirements; less formal than calibration.

- **Validation**: Confirmation that an instrument or process meets its intended use requirements.

- **Standard Reference Material (SRM)**: NIST-certified reference material with known properties; used for calibration and QC. [nist.gov](https://nist.gov)

- **Traceability**: Unbroken chain of comparisons linking a measurement to national or international standards (typically NIST).

- **Accuracy**: Closeness of agreement between a measured value and the true value.

- **Precision / Repeatability**: Closeness of agreement between independent measurements under same conditions.

- **Uncertainty**: Quantified doubt about a measurement result; expressed as a confidence interval.

- **IQ / OQ / PQ**: Installation Qualification, Operational Qualification, Performance Qualification — formal qualification stages for regulated environments.

- **Limit of Detection (LOD)**: Smallest signal distinguishable from noise; typically 3× standard deviation of blank.

- **Limit of Quantitation (LOQ)**: Lowest concentration reliably quantifiable with acceptable precision and accuracy.

- **Linearity**: Ability of an instrument to produce results proportional to analyte concentration across the measuring range.

## Quality Control Terms

- **Internal Quality Control (IQC)**: Ongoing testing of control materials to verify instrument performance between patient/research samples.

- **External Quality Assessment (EQA) / Proficiency Testing (PT)**: Comparison of laboratory results against external reference values or peer group results.

- **Westgard Rules**: Multirule QC system for detecting out-of-control conditions; includes 1:2s, 1:3s, 2:2s, R:4s, 4:2s, 8:x rules.

- **Control Material**: Specimen with known analyte concentration used to verify instrument performance.

- **Mean (X̄)**: Average of QC control values; used to calculate control limits.

- **Standard Deviation (SD)**: Measure of variability in control values; used to establish control limits (typically ±2 SD, ±3 SD).

- **Coefficient of Variation (CV)**: Standard deviation expressed as a percentage of the mean; measure of relative precision.

- **Bias**: Systematic deviation of measured values from the true value; difference between observed mean and target.

- **Correlation Coefficient (r)**: Measure of linear relationship between two measurement methods.

## Instrument Types & Components

- **HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography)**: Separates, identifies, and quantifies chemical compounds in liquid samples.

- **GC-MS (Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry)**: Separates and identifies volatile compounds; provides mass spectral data for compound identification.

- **LC-MS/MS (Liquid Chromatography-Tandem Mass Spectrometry)**: Highly sensitive analytical technique for quantifying trace-level compounds.

- **NMR (Nuclear Magnetic Resonance)**: Determines molecular structure and dynamics through NMR-active nuclei (1H, 13C, 31P).

- **MALDI-TOF (Matrix-Assisted Laser Desorption/Ionization Time-of-Flight)**: Mass spectrometry for protein, peptide, and polymer analysis.

- **ICP-MS (Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometry)**: Multi-element trace metal analysis at parts-per-trillion levels.

- **Flow Cytometer**: Analyzes physical and chemical characteristics of cells or particles in a fluid stream using laser light.

- **Confocal Microscope**: Fluorescence imaging with optical sectioning; eliminates out-of-focus blur.

- **Cryo-Electron Microscope (Cryo-EM)**: Electron microscopy of frozen-hydrated samples for high-resolution structural biology.

- **Confocal Raman Microscope**: Combines Raman spectroscopy with confocal microscopy for chemical imaging.

- **qPCR / Real-Time PCR**: Quantitative polymerase chain reaction for nucleic acid amplification and quantification.

- **DNA Sequencer**: Instruments for determining nucleotide sequences (Sanger sequencing, next-generation sequencing platforms).

- **Flow Cytometer / Cell Sorter**: Analyzes and sorts cells based on light scattering and fluorescence.

- **Centrifuge (Ultracentrifuge)**: Separates components by centrifugal force; ultracentrifuges reach >100,000 rpm.

- **Spectrophotometer / Microplate Reader**: Measures light absorption (UV-Vis, fluorescence, luminescence) for sample analysis.

- **Biosafety Cabinet (Class II / Class III)**: Enclosed workspace providing personnel, product, and environmental protection from biohazardous materials.

- **Autoclave (Steam Sterilizer)**: High-pressure steam sterilization equipment for laboratory materials and waste.

## Safety & Compliance Terms

- **Biosafety Level (BSL-1 through BSL-4)**: CDC/NIH classification of containment requirements for microbiological work. [cdc.gov](https://cdc.gov)

- **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)**: Gloves, lab coats, safety glasses, face shields, respirators used to minimize exposure.

- **Safety Data Sheet (SDS)**: Standardized chemical hazard information; 16-section format per OSHA HazCom.

- **Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC)**: Institutional committee that reviews and approves recombinant DNA and biohazardous research.

- **Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC)**: Committee overseeing animal research protocols and welfare.

- **Laser Safety Officer (LSO)**: Individual designated to oversee laser safety program for Class 3B and Class 4 lasers.

- **Radiation Safety Officer (RSO)**: Individual responsible for the institutional radiation safety program.

- **Institutional Chemical Hygiene Officer (CHO)**: Individual responsible for the Chemical Hygiene Plan under OSHA Lab Standard.

- **Lock-Out Tag-Out (LOTO)**: Safety procedure ensuring dangerous machines are properly shut off and cannot be started during maintenance.

- **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)**: Written procedure describing how to safely perform a specific task or operation.

## Data & Computer Systems

- **LIMS (Laboratory Information Management System)**: Software for managing laboratory data, workflows, and sample tracking.

- **ELN (Electronic Laboratory Notebook)**: Digital replacement for paper notebooks; manages experimental data and protocols.

- **CDS (Chromatography Data System)**: Software for acquiring and processing chromatography data (e.g., Empower, OpenLab).

- **Audit Trail**: Chronological record of all actions and changes to electronic data; required in regulated environments.

- **Electronic Signature**: Computer-based evidence of identity authentication linked to electronic records (21 CFR Part 11).

- **GAMP (Good Automated Manufacturing Practice)**: ISPE guidelines for computerized system validation in pharmaceutical environments. [ispe.org](https://ispe.org)

- **CSV (Computer System Validation)**: Documented process ensuring computer systems perform as intended and produce reliable data.

## Maintenance & Service Terms

- **Preventive Maintenance (PM)**: Scheduled maintenance activities to prevent equipment failure (cleaning, calibration, parts replacement).

- **Predictive Maintenance (PdM)**: Maintenance based on monitoring actual equipment condition (vibration analysis, oil analysis).

- **Corrective Maintenance**: Unscheduled repairs performed after a failure occurs.

- **Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF)**: Average time a piece of equipment operates between failures.

- **Mean Time to Repair (MTTR)**: Average time required to repair a piece of equipment after failure.

- **Uptime**: Percentage of time equipment is operational and available for use.

- **Service Contract**: Annual or multi-year agreement with manufacturer or third-party provider for maintenance and support.

- **Parts Life Cycle**: Manufacturer support timeline for spare parts, consumables, and technical support for a given instrument model.

## Facility & Infrastructure Terms

- **Cleanroom**: Environment with controlled contamination levels (particles, microbes, temperature, humidity).

- **HEPA Filter**: High-Efficiency Particulate Air filter; removes ≥99.97% of particles ≥0.3 μm; used in biosafety cabinets and cleanrooms.

- **Laboratory Fume Hood (Biosafety Cabinet vs. Chemical Fume Hood)**: Chemical fume hoods exhaust vapors; BSC recirculates (Class II) or exhausts (Class III) air with HEPA filtration.

- **Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS)**: Backup power system protecting instruments from power fluctuations and outages.

- **Compressed Air / Lab Air**: Compressed air systems for instrument operation; lab air (oil-free) for breathing apparatus connections.

- **Process Chiller**: Cooling system for analytical instruments (HPLC, lasers, NMR magnets).

- **Liquid Nitrogen (LN2) Dewar**: Cryogenic storage container for liquid nitrogen used in sample preservation and cryo-work.

- **Environmental Chamber**: Controlled environment (temperature, humidity, light) for stability testing and controlled experiments.
