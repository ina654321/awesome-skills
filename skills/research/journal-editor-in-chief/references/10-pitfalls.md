# Journal Editor-in-Chief Templates, Checklists & Protocols

Practical tools for journal editorial leadership and quality management.

## Manuscript Triage / Desk Rejection Protocol

Standard criteria for desk rejection without peer review:

**Scope and Fit:**
- [ ] Is the topic within the journal's stated scope?
- [ ] Does the manuscript advance knowledge in the journal's subject area?
- [ ] Is the work of sufficient general interest to the journal's readership?

**Technical Quality:**
- [ ] Is the English language adequate for publication (or has translation been attempted)?
- [ ] Is the manuscript format compliant with journal guidelines?
- [ ] Are required sections (abstract, methods, results, discussion) present?
- [ ] Are figures and tables readable and appropriately formatted?

**Scientific Quality Indicators:**
- [ ] Does the abstract clearly describe the research question, methods, and conclusions?
- [ ] Is the sample size adequate for the stated conclusions?
- [ ] Are statistical methods appropriate and adequately described?
- [ ] Are references complete and from appropriate sources?

**Ethical Compliance:**
- [ ] Are conflict of interest disclosures present?
- [ ] Is ethics committee/IRB approval documented for human or animal studies?
- [ ] Is clinical trial registration documented if applicable?
- [ ] Is data availability addressed?

**Decision:**
- [ ] If ANY criterion is NOT met: desk reject with constructive feedback
- [ ] If ALL criteria are met: send for peer review
- [ ] Document rejection reason for consistency tracking

## Peer Reviewer Selection Protocol

Assigning the right reviewers:

**Reviewer Identification:**
- [ ] Search manuscript references for potential reviewers in the specific topic area
- [ ] Use journal's established reviewer database for the subject area
- [ ] Check recent publications in the field for active researchers
- [ ] Consider reviewers recommended by authors (verify credentials independently)
- [ ] Check for reviewers who have published in related topics in the journal

**Avoid:**
- [ ] Reviewers from the same institution as the authors
- [ ] Reviewers with documented conflicts of interest (collaborators, competitors, personal relationships)
- [ ] Reviewers who have been over-used (reviewer fatigue)
- [ ] Reviewers with recent disputes with the authors

**Invitation Process:**
- [ ] Send personalized invitation with manuscript abstract
- [ ] Provide clear deadline (typically 2-4 weeks for research articles)
- [ ] Offer reviewer recognition options (ORCID credit, naming in annual acknowledgment)
- [ ] Send reminder at 50% of deadline if no response
- [ ] If reviewer declines, ask for alternative reviewer recommendations

**Minimum Review Requirements:**
- [ ] Obtain at least 2 independent reviews for most articles
- [ ] For special issues, confirm sufficient reviewer coverage across all manuscripts
- [ ] Use senior reviewers for high-risk or highly technical manuscripts

## Editorial Decision Protocol

Making and documenting decisions:

**Review Analysis:**
- [ ] Read the manuscript independently before reading reviews
- [ ] Assess whether reviews are thorough and constructive
- [ ] Identify areas of reviewer agreement and disagreement
- [ ] Determine if reviewer expertise matches the manuscript content
- [ ] Check for potential reviewer bias or conflicts

**Decision Categories:**
- [ ] **Accept**: Paper meets all standards; ready for production
- [ ] **Minor Revision**: Small changes needed; verify completion without re-review
- [ ] **Major Revision**: Significant changes needed; re-review required
- [ ] **Reject**: Paper not suitable for this journal; may suggest alternatives
- [ ] **Reject (Post-Review)**: Despite review, paper does not meet standards
- [ ] **Transfer**: Appropriate for a different journal in the same publisher family

**Decision Letter Template Elements:**
1. Summary statement (1-2 sentences on the overall assessment)
2. Major concerns requiring resolution
3. Minor concerns requiring attention
4. Specific reviewer comments (direct or summarized)
5. Required changes for revision
6. Suggested but not required improvements
7. Decision with clear next steps
8. Timeline for revision (typically 4-8 weeks)
9. Revision submission instructions

**Documentation:**
- [ ] Record decision in manuscript tracking system
- [ ] Document reviewer recommendations vs. final decision if different
- [ ] Note any COI management for editors involved in the decision
- [ ] Archive reviewer reports securely

## Handling Suspected Misconduct Protocol

Following COPE flowcharts when concerns arise:

**Step 1: Initial Assessment**
- [ ] Receive allegation or notice of concern (anonymous tip, PubPeer post, reviewer concern)
- [ ] Do NOT contact the author immediately — preserve the allegation
- [ ] Gather all available information (manuscript, reviews, supporting data)
- [ ] Determine if the concern falls within editorial scope or requires institutional investigation

**Step 2: Triage**
- [ ] Can the concern be explained by honest error or editorial oversight?
- [ ] Is the concern substantial enough to warrant investigation?
- [ ] Should the editor handle internally or refer to the author's institution?
- [ ] Consult COPE flowchart appropriate to the allegation type:
  - [ ] Image concerns: compare with original data; request raw data
  - [ ] Text overlap/plagiarism: use plagiarism detection software
  - [ ] Data concerns: request original data and protocols
  - [ ] Authorship disputes: request contribution information from all parties

**Step 3: Initial Inquiry**
- [ ] Contact author with specific, non-accusatory questions
- [ ] Request original/unprocessed data, raw images, and methodology details
- [ ] Set clear but reasonable deadline for response
- [ ] Inform author of confidentiality expectations during inquiry

**Step 4: Resolution Options**
- [ ] **No Action**: Concern was unfounded or adequately explained
- [ ] **Editorial Correction**: Erratum or correction needed; author cooperative
- [ ] **Expression of Concern**: Concerns warrant further investigation; publication held in limbo
- [ ] **Retraction**: Clear evidence of misconduct; publication must be withdrawn
- [ ] **Referral to Institution**: Matter exceeds editorial scope; involve ORI or institutional integrity office
- [ ] **Referral to Publisher**: Major misconduct; publisher-level investigation needed

**Step 5: Communication**
- [ ] Notify author of decision with explanation
- [ ] Update article record in Crossref (retraction notice, expression of concern)
- [ ] If retraction: publish retraction notice with reason (error, misconduct, redundant)
- [ ] Document all actions and timeline
- [ ] If criminal activity suspected: consult legal counsel

## Editorial Board Management Protocol

**Annual Editorial Board Review:**
- [ ] Assess each board member's review performance (number of reviews, timeliness, quality)
- [ ] Evaluate geographic and demographic diversity
- [ ] Assess expertise coverage for journal scope
- [ ] Identify inactive members for removal
- [ ] Recruit new members to address gaps
- [ ] Document decisions and communicate with all members

**Board Recruitment:**
- [ ] Identify gaps in expertise, geography, and demographics
- [ ] Seek recommendations from existing board members and editorial colleagues
- [ ] Invite candidates with explicit expectations (number of reviews, term length)
- [ ] Provide new member orientation (journal policies, reviewer database, COI procedures)

**Board Meeting Agenda (Annual):**
1. Journal performance metrics (submissions, acceptance rate, time to decision, impact)
2. Review of editorial policies and any needed updates
3. Strategic direction and new initiatives (special issues, collections, scope changes)
4. Board composition and potential new appointments
5. Reviewer recognition and retention strategies
6. Industry developments (open access, preprint policies, funder mandates)
7. Publisher relationship and contract review

## Journal Policy Development Protocol

**When to Update Policies:**
- [ ] New ICMJE, COPE, or CSE recommendations issued
- [ ] Publisher or society requirements change
- [ ] Regulatory requirements change (e.g., data sharing mandates)
- [ ] Post-incident review identifies policy gaps
- [ ] Field standards evolve (e.g., preprint practices, open data)

**Policy Update Process:**
1. Identify the issue and gap in current policy
2. Draft proposed policy language
3. Consult editorial board for input
4. Review against COPE/ICMJE/CSE guidelines
5. Consult publisher legal/policy team
6. If society journal: consult society governance
7. Finalize policy with implementation date
8. Communicate changes to authors and reviewers
9. Archive previous policy version

**Standard Policies to Maintain:**
- [ ] Authorship policy (who qualifies, contribution requirements)
- [ ] Conflict of interest policy (disclosure requirements, management)
- [ ] Data sharing policy (mandates, repositories, exceptions)
- [ ] Peer review policy (model, blinding, timelines)
- [ ] Preprint policy (allowed, preprint servers, citation)
- [ ] Duplicate submission policy
- [ ] Plagiarism and text similarity policy
- [ ] Image integrity policy
- [ ] Corrections, expressions of concern, and retraction policy
- [ ] Appeals and complaints procedure
- [ ] Post-publication discussion policy

## Journal Metrics Review Protocol

Quarterly review of journal performance:

**Submission & Throughput:**
- [ ] Total submissions (monthly and trend)
- [ ] Acceptance rate
- [ ] Average time to first decision
- [ ] Average time to publication (submission to online)
- [ ] Backlog of papers in review
- [ ] Desk rejection rate and trends

**Peer Review:**
- [ ] Average number of reviewers per manuscript
- [ ] Reviewer acceptance rate
- [ ] Average time from invitation to review completion
- [ ] Number of active reviewers
- [ ] Reviewer retention rate
- [ ] Quality of reviews (editor assessment)

**Publication:**
- [ ] Articles published per issue/volume
- [ ] APC revenue and waivers granted
- [ ] Open access percentage
- [ ] Supplementary material usage

**Impact & Visibility:**
- [ ] Journal Impact Factor (JIF) and trend
- [ ] CiteScore and other metrics
- [ ] Submission from new vs. returning authors
- [ ] Geographic distribution of submissions
- [ ] Media coverage and altmetric scores

**Action Items:**
- [ ] Identify bottlenecks in editorial process
- [ ] Recruit reviewers in underrepresented areas
- [ ] Address declining metrics
- [ ] Plan special issues or collections
- [ ] Review APC pricing and waiver policy
