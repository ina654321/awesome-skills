# Journal Editor-in-Chief Reference Standards

Standards, best practices, and ethical guidelines for scientific journal editorial leadership.

## Publication Ethics Frameworks

- **Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE)**: International organization providing guidelines and flowcharts for handling research and publication misconduct. [cope.org](https://cope.org)
- **International Committee of Medical Journal Editors (ICMJE)**: Recommendations for the Conduct, Reporting, Editing, and Publication of Scholarly Work (Uniform Requirements). [icmje.org](https://icmje.org)
- **World Association of Medical Editors (WAME)**: Recommendations on Best Practices for Peer Reviewing Biomedical Journals. [wame.org](https://wame.org)
- **Council of Science Editors (CSE)**: White Paper on Promoting Integrity in Scientific Journal Publications. [cse members.org](https://cse members.org)
- **National Information Standards Organization (NISO)**: Recommended Practices for Online Journal Article Versions (NISO RP-8). [niso.org](https://niso.org)

## Industry Standards

- **Crossref**: DOI registration agency; guidelines for metadata, Crossmark, and funder identifiers. [crossref.org](https://crossref.org)
- **Open Access Scholarly Publishers Association (OASPA)**: Standards of conduct for open access publishers. [oaspa.org](https://oaspa.org)
- **Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ)**: Criteria for indexing open access journals. [doaj.org](https://doaj.org)
- **Committee of Publication Ethics (COPE) Core Practices**: Comprehensive guidelines for journal editors. [cope.org](https://cope.org)
- **STM Association**: Science, Technical, and Medical publishing standards; Best Practices for Secure Researcher IDs. [stm-assoc.com](https://stm-assoc.com)

## Legal & Regulatory Standards

- **International Association of Scientific, Technical and Medical Publishers (STM)**: Copyright guidelines and industry standards. [stm-assoc.com](https://stm-assoc.com)
- **U.S. Copyright Office**: Copyright law principles; work-made-for-hire doctrine. [copyright.gov](https://copyright.gov)
- **GDPR (EU 2016/679)**: Data protection requirements affecting journals publishing EU subjects' data; consent for publication. [gdpr.eu](https://gdpr.eu)
- **NIH Public Access Policy (Public Law 111-148)**: Mandates NIH-funded publications be deposited in PubMed Central. [nih.gov](https://nih.gov)

## Editorial Office Operations

- **Office of Research Integrity (ORI)**: Handles research misconduct investigations in HHS-funded research; publication ethics guidance. [ori.hhs.gov](https://ori.hhs.gov)
- **PubMed Central (PMC)**: NIH repository for biomedical literature; PMCID assignment for NIH-funded articles. [ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://ncbi.nlm.nih.gov)
- **ORCID**: Unique researcher identifier system; integration with manuscript submission. [orcid.org](https://orcid.org)
- **CAS Registry**: Chemical compound registry supporting accurate manuscript nomenclature.

## Peer Review Standards

- **Single-Anonymized Review**: Reviewer knows author identity; author does not know reviewer identity (traditional model).
- **Double-Anonymized Review**: Neither author nor reviewer identity is disclosed to the other party.
- **Open Review**: Reviewer and author identities are disclosed to each other; review reports may be published.
- **Post-Publication Peer Review**: Review occurs after publication; may be formal or informal.
- **Registered Reports**: Peer-reviewed publication format where methods are reviewed before data collection.

## Editorial Governance

- **Scientific Society Journals**: Owned by professional societies (e.g., ACS, IEEE, ASCE); governed by society bylaws and editorial boards.
- **Publisher-Owned Journals**: Owned by commercial publishers (Elsevier, Wiley, Springer Nature, Taylor & Francis, SAGE); governed by publishing agreements.
- **University Press Journals**: Owned by universities; often open access; governed by academic standards.
- **Community Journals**: Founded by researchers; often diamond/platinum open access.

## Editorial Board Structures

- **Editor-in-Chief (EIC)**: Senior editorial authority; sets editorial policy; responsible for journal integrity.
- **Associate Editors**: Handle day-to-day manuscript decisions; supervise peer review.
- **Editorial Board**: Advisory body; provides strategic guidance; may assist with peer review.
- **Guest Editors**: Handle special issues; selected for specific expertise.
- **Managing Editor / Editorial Office**: Professional staff managing submission systems, communications, and production.

## Manuscript Tracking & Metrics

- **Journal Impact Factor (JIF)**: Clarivate metric; average citations to recent articles. Use critically. [clarivate.com](https://clarivate.com)
- **CiteScore**: Scopus metric; average citations per document over a 4-year window.
- **Scimago Journal Rank (SJR)**: Elsevier metric; journal prestige weighted by citation source.
- **Altmetric**: Alternative metric tracking online attention (news, social media, policy documents). [altmetric.com](https://altmetric.com)
- **h-index**: Journal's h-number; h articles with at least h citations.

## Research Integrity & Misconduct

- **Research Misconduct Categories**: Fabrication, falsification, and plagiarism (FFP) in proposing, performing, or reporting research.
- **Redundant Publication**: Publishing overlapping or duplicate work; includes self-plagiarism.
- **Image Manipulation**: Altering scientific images beyond adjustments for brightness/contrast; includes gel splicing.
- **Data Falsification**: Manipulating research data to support desired conclusions.
- **Citation Manipulation**: Coercive citation practices; excessive self-citation to inflate metrics.
- **Peer Review Fraud**: Reviewer rigging; fake peer review; ghost reviewers; reviewer impersonation.
