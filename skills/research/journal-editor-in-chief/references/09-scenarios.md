# Journal Editor-in-Chief Glossary & Key Terms

Essential terminology for journal editorial leadership and publication ethics.

## Editorial Roles & Governance

- **Editor-in-Chief (EIC)**: The senior editorial leader responsible for a journal's overall direction, quality, and integrity. Sets editorial policy and has final authority on all editorial decisions.

- **Associate Editor**: Handles day-to-day manuscript management, assigns reviewers, and makes initial recommendations; reports to EIC.

- **Editorial Board**: A group of recognized experts providing strategic guidance, peer reviewing, and representing the journal's scientific scope.

- **Guest Editor**: An expert invited to manage a special issue or themed collection on a specific topic.

- **Managing Editor**: Professional editorial staff member responsible for day-to-day journal operations, production, and workflow management.

- **Publisher Representative**: Liaison between the journal and the publishing house (Elsevier, Wiley, Springer Nature, etc.).

- **Editorial Office**: The professional staff supporting journal operations including manuscript tracking, author communication, and production.

## Manuscript Types & Categories

- **Research Article**: Original empirical research; full account of methods and results; longest article type.

- **Review Article**: Systematic synthesis of existing literature; may be narrative or systematic review.

- **Systematic Review**: Protocol-driven literature review following explicit methods (PRISMA guidelines).

- **Meta-Analysis**: Statistical synthesis of quantitative data from multiple studies on the same topic.

- **Case Report**: Description of a single case or small series; clinical observations.

- **Letter to the Editor**: Short commentary on a published article or brief research communication.

- **Short Communication / Letter**: Brief report of significant new findings.

- **Perspective / Commentary**: Expert opinion or viewpoint on a scientific topic.

- **Hypothesis**: A brief article presenting a testable hypothesis with supporting evidence.

- **Study Protocol**: Detailed description of methods for a planned study; often peer-reviewed.

- **Registered Report**: Manuscript format where methods are peer-reviewed before data collection.

- **Replication Study**: Study designed to replicate and verify findings of a prior publication.

- **Correction / Erratum**: Notice of error in a published article; author's or publisher's correction.

- **Expression of Concern**: Publisher's note indicating concerns about a published article's integrity.

- **Retraction**: Formal withdrawal of a published article due to error, misconduct, or invalid conclusions.

## Peer Review Models

- **Single-Anonymized Review (Traditional)**: Reviewer knows author identity; author does not know reviewer identity.

- **Double-Anonymized Review (Double-Blind)**: Neither author nor reviewer identity is disclosed to the other party.

- **Open Peer Review**: Both author and reviewer identities are disclosed; reviews may be published alongside the article.

- **Transparent Peer Review**: Reviewer identity is disclosed to authors; published review reports may be optional.

- **Post-Publication Peer Review**: Review occurs after publication; often facilitated by platforms (PubPeer, preprint servers).

- **Collaborative Review**: Multiple reviewers work together to produce a consolidated report.

- **Sequential Review**: Expert reviewers review sequentially, each building on the previous review.

## Publication Ethics Terms

- **Research Misconduct**: Fabrication (making up data), Falsification (manipulating data), and Plagiarism (FFP) in proposing, performing, or reporting research.

- **Redundant Publication**: Publishing overlapping or substantially similar work without appropriate citation or disclosure; includes self-plagiarism.

- **Duplicate Submission**: Submitting the same manuscript to multiple journals simultaneously.

- **Image Manipulation**: Altering scientific images beyond standard adjustments for brightness, contrast, or color balance; prohibited without disclosure.

- **Data Falsification**: Manipulation of research data, images, or results to support predetermined conclusions.

- **Plagiarism**: Presenting others' work, ideas, or text as one's own without attribution. Includes self-plagiarism.

- **Citation Manipulation**: Artificially inflating citation counts through coordinated self-citation or coercive citation practices.

- **Peer Review Fraud**: Fabrication of reviewer identities; reviewer "ring" arrangements; fake peer review accounts.

- **Competing Interest / Conflict of Interest**: Financial, professional, or personal interests that could inappropriately influence editorial judgment.

- **Ghost Authorship**: Omitting authors who made significant contributions; often involves ghostwriters paid by industry.

- **Gift Authorship / Honorary Authorship**: Listing individuals as authors who did not make significant contributions.

## Manuscript Processing

- **Submission**: Author's initial manuscript entry into the journal's manuscript tracking system.

- **Pre-Screen / Triage / Desk Rejection**: Initial assessment to determine if a manuscript fits journal scope and quality threshold; rejected without peer review.

- **Initial Quality Check**: Editorial office verifies completeness of submission (format, disclosures, signatures, figures).

- **Editorial Assessment**: EIC or associate editor evaluates manuscript for suitability, novelty, and fit.

- **Peer Review Assignment**: Associate editor invites qualified reviewers matched to manuscript content.

- **Review Invitation**: Email to potential reviewers requesting their participation; includes abstract and timeline.

- **Major Revision**: Manuscript requires substantial changes; review required after revision.

- **Minor Revision**: Manuscript requires small changes; may be verified by editor without re-review.

- **Acceptance**: Final approval to publish; manuscript proceeds to production.

- **Rejection**: Manuscript not accepted for publication; may be desk rejection, post-review rejection, or outright rejection.

## Article Processing & Production

- **Article Processing Charge (APC)**: Fee charged by open access journals for publishing; varies widely.

- **Page Charges**: Fee per published page; less common; primarily for society journals.

- **Production**: Process of preparing accepted manuscript for publication (copyediting, typesetting, proofreading).

- **Galley Proof**: Final typeset version of article sent to author for final corrections before publication.

- **DOI (Digital Object Identifier)**: Unique, persistent identifier assigned to each article; registered with Crossref.

- **Crossref**: DOI registration agency; also provides Crossmark (correction/update metadata) and funder identification.

- **PubMed Central ID (PMCID)**: Identifier for articles deposited in PubMed Central; required for NIH-funded research.

- **Preprint**: Version of article posted to a preprint server before or during journal peer review; not peer reviewed.

- **Preprint Server**: Platform for sharing preprint versions (bioRxiv, medRxiv, arXiv, ChemRxiv).

## Journal Metrics & Performance

- **Journal Impact Factor (JIF)**: Average citations per article in a 2-year window; published by Clarivate. Use critically.

- **CiteScore**: Average citations per document; 4-year window; published by Scopus.

- **Scimago Journal Rank (SJR)**: Journal prestige indicator using cite-weighted citations; Elsevier product.

- **h-index (Journal)**: The h number of articles that have been cited at least h times.

- **Altmetric Score**: Weighted score of online attention; includes news, social media, policy documents, and patents.

- **Article-Level Metrics (ALM)**: Citation and usage metrics at the individual article level.

- **Acceptance Rate**: Percentage of submitted manuscripts accepted; varies widely; declining to publish may signal prestige.

- **Time to First Decision**: Average time from submission to first editorial decision; key author experience metric.

- **Time to Publication**: Total time from submission to online publication; varies by journal and field.

## Data & Reproducibility Terms

- **Data Availability Statement**: Author's declaration of where underlying data are stored and how to access them.

- **Data Sharing**: Practice of making research data publicly available; increasingly required by journals and funders.

- **FAIR Principles**: Data should be Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable.

- **Supplementary Materials**: Additional data, figures, videos, or methods published alongside the main article.

- **Preregistration**: Registration of study design and analysis plan before data collection; supports transparency.

- **Replication Study**: Study designed specifically to reproduce and verify findings of a prior study.
