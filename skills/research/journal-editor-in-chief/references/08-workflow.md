# Journal Editor-in-Chief Workflow Reference

Common workflow issues and challenges in scientific journal editorial leadership.

## Editorial Board Management Issues

- **Problem**: Editorial board members not actively reviewing or contributing to journal quality.
  - **Issue**: Board members appointed for prestige but not engaged; reduced reviewer pool.
  - **Reference**: COPE guidelines on editorial board responsibilities; annual board performance review.

- **Problem**: Editorial board appointments driven by cronyism or geographic representation rather than expertise.
  - **Issue**: Review quality and editorial decisions suffer; appearance of bias.
  - **Reference**: COPE best practice guidelines; WAME recommendations on editorial board composition.

- **Problem**: Board members with conflicts of interest not recusing themselves from decisions.
  - **Issue**: Unfair editorial decisions favoring friends or competitors.
  - **Reference**: ICMJE conflict of interest standards; COPE flowcharts on editor conflicts.

## Peer Review Process Issues

- **Problem**: Reviewers not responding to invitations or declining without providing alternative reviewers.
  - **Issue**: Delayed review process; increased editorial burden; reviewer fatigue.
  - **Reference**: COPE flowcharts for reviewers not responding; reviewer recognition programs.

- **Problem**: Review quality highly variable; reviewers providing superficial or inappropriate feedback.
  - **Issue**: Poor editorial decisions; authors frustrated; reputation damage.
  - **Reference**: COPE guidelines on peer review quality; reviewer training programs; structured review templates.

- **Problem**: Peer review fraud (reviewer rigging, fake reviews) not detected.
  - **Issue**: Fabricated reviews accepted; fraudulent papers published; severe reputational damage.
  - **Reference**: COPE guidance on peer review manipulation; publisher detection tools; PubPeer concerns.

- **Problem**: Authors manipulating reviewer suggestions (suggesting reviewers who are co-authors or themselves).
  - **Issue**: Compromised peer review; biased acceptance decisions.
  - **Reference**: COPE guidance on author-suggested reviewers; verification procedures; email domain checks.

## Manuscript Decision Issues

- **Problem**: Acceptance decisions made without adequate peer review (desk rejection without clear criteria).
  - **Issue**: Inconsistent desk rejections; authors frustrated; unfair filtering.
  - **Reference**: WAME recommendations on manuscript triage; transparent rejection criteria.

- **Problem**: Revision requests to authors creating excessive rounds of review.
  - **Issue**: Delays in publication; author frustration; reviewer burnout.
  - **Reference**: ICMJE recommendations; COPE guidelines on revision requests.

- **Problem**: Editor-in-chief overriding associate editor decisions without clear justification.
  - **Issue**: Reduced associate editor autonomy; morale issues; inconsistent decisions.
  - **Reference**: Clear decision authority guidelines; editorial governance documents.

## Conflict of Interest Issues

- **Problem**: Editor handling a manuscript from a competing lab without disclosure.
  - **Issue**: Appearance of bias; unfair review; potential legal liability.
  - **Reference**: ICMJE conflict of interest disclosure; COPE flowcharts; journal COI policy.

- **Problem**: Authors failing to disclose competing interests; editors not requiring disclosure.
  - **Issue**: Trust damage; potential retraction if discovered post-publication.
  - **Reference**: ICMJE disclosure form; journal conflict of interest policy; post-publication review.

## Integrity & Misconduct Issues

- **Problem**: Suspected misconduct (image manipulation, data fabrication) not escalated appropriately.
  - **Issue**: Fraudulent research published; retraction causes reputational damage; journal integrity questioned.
  - **Reference**: COPE flowcharts for handling suspected misconduct; ORI guidelines.

- **Problem**: Post-publication concerns raised (on PubPeer, by whistleblowers) not investigated promptly.
  - **Issue**: Unaddressed concerns undermine journal credibility; authors suffer from unresolved allegations.
  - **Reference**: COPE post-publication guidelines; response timeline expectations.

- **Problem**: Image integrity screening not performed at submission or before publication.
  - **Issue**: Manipulated images published; post-publication corrections or retractions.
  - **Reference**: Journal of Cell Biology image integrity screening; Nature image manipulation policies.

## Publication Ethics Issues

- **Problem**: Handling cases of redundant publication (self-plagiarism, duplicate submission) inconsistently.
  - **Issue**: Some redundant work retracted; other similar cases ignored; perceived unfairness.
  - **Reference**: COPE flowcharts on redundant publication; ICMJE definition of duplicate submission.

- **Problem**: Editors soliciting papers from authors and then using these invitations to pressure reviews.
  - **Issue**: Ethical concerns about coercive citation or review practices.
  - **Reference**: COPE ethical guidelines for editors; STM association best practices.

- **Problem**: Corrections, expressions of concern, and retractions handled inconsistently.
  - **Issue**: Similar issues treated differently; authors and readers confused.
  - **Reference**: COPE retraction guidelines; ICMJE recommendations on corrections and retractions.

## Diversity & Inclusion Issues

- **Problem**: Editorial board lacks demographic diversity (gender, geography, career stage).
  - **Issue**: Perception of bias; reduced global representation; limited perspectives.
  - **Reference**: CSE recommendations on editorial diversity; WAME recommendations.

- **Problem**: Reviewer pools not diverse; same reviewers repeatedly used.
  - **Issue**: Review fatigue; limited perspectives; perpetuation of geographic/gender bias.
  - **Reference**: Elsevier reviewer diversity initiatives; ORCID integration for reviewer identification.

## Operational & Business Issues

- **Problem**: Article processing charges (APCs) creating barriers to open access publication.
  - **Issue**: Authors from low-resource institutions cannot afford to publish; equity concerns.
  - **Reference**: DOAJ APC guidance; institutional agreements; fee waiver policies.

- **Problem**: Turnaround times exceeding competitive standards; journal reputation damaged.
  - **Issue**: Authors submit elsewhere; rejection rates perceived as unfair; impact factor affected.
  - **Reference**: Author survey data; competitor journal timelines; WAME turnaround time recommendations.

- **Problem**: Transfer agreements between journals not working smoothly for rejected manuscripts.
  - **Issue**: Authors frustrated with re-submission process; loss of reviewer effort.
  - **Reference**: COPE guidelines on manuscript transfer; Crossref manuscript exchange.

## Post-Publication Issues

- **Problem**: Letters to the editor and post-publication comments not handled consistently.
  - **Issue**: Some concerns addressed; others ignored; inconsistent standards.
  - **Reference**: COPE post-publication guidelines; journal comment policies.

- **Problem**: Preprint concerns raised affecting journal decision-making.
  - **Issue**: Journals must decide whether to consider preprint servers; preprint precedence issues.
  - **Reference**: ICMJE statement on preprints; individual journal preprint policies.

- **Problem**: Data availability statements not enforced; underlying data not shared.
  - **Issue**: Irreproducible research; post-publication concerns about data unavailability.
  - **Reference**: FAIR data principles; journal data sharing policies; FORCE11 data citation principles.
