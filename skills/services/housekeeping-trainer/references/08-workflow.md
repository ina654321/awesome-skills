# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Room Cleaning Workflow

```
Room Cleaning Process
├── Preparation
│   ├── Gather supplies and equipment
│   ├── Check room status (vacant/dirty/clean)
│   └── Inspect for special instructions
├── Initial Assessment
│   ├── Check for damages or issues
│   ├── Remove trash and recyclables
│   └── Strip beds if applicable
├── Deep Cleaning
│   ├── Dust all surfaces (top to bottom)
│   ├── Clean and sanitize bathroom
│   ├── Vacuum and mop floors
│   └── Clean windows and mirrors
└── Final Quality Check
    ├── Inspect all areas
    ├── Restock amenities
    └── Final presentation
```

## 8.2 Deep Cleaning Procedure

1. **Preparation (15 minutes)**
   - Ventilate room by opening windows
   - Turn on all lights for inspection
   - Remove all visible trash and debris
   - Disconnect and unplug any electronics

2. **Dusting Phase (20 minutes)**
   - High dusting (vents, fans, fixtures)
   - Wall corners and ceiling edges
   - Light fixtures and bulbs
   - Baseboards and trim work
   - Furniture tops and underneath

3. **Sanitization Phase (25 minutes)**
   - Bathroom: toilet, sink, tub/shower, floor
   - Kitchen: counters, stove, refrigerator exterior
   - High-touch surfaces: doorknobs, switches, remotes
   - Electronics: screens, keyboards, phones

4. **Floor Care (15 minutes)**
   - Vacuum all carpeted areas (under furniture)
   - Spot clean stains
   - Mop hard floors
   - Allow proper drying time

5. **Final Touches (10 minutes)**
   - Make beds with fresh linens
   - Arrange furniture properly
   - Add amenities and decor
   - Final walkthrough inspection

## 8.3 Commercial Cleaning Workflow

1. **After-Hours Protocol**
   - Coordinate with building management
   - Set up proper signage
   - Establish work zones
   - Maintain secure access procedures

2. **High-Traffic Areas**
   - Focus on entryways and lobbies
   - Elevator banks and stairs
   - Restrooms (multiple times daily)
   - Break rooms and kitchens

3. **Specialized Areas**
   - Medical facilities (biohazard protocols)
   - Food service areas (HACCP compliance)
   - Industrial spaces (safety requirements)
   - Educational facilities (non-toxic products)

4. **Quality Control**
   - Inspection checklists
   - UV light testing (sanitization)
   - ATP testing (cleanliness)
   - Documentation requirements

## 8.4 Infection Control Cleaning

1. **PPE Requirements**
   - Gloves (nitrile for chemicals)
   - Eye protection
   - Mask (N95 for biohazard)
   - Gown or apron when needed

2. **High-Touch Surface Focus**
   - Light switches
   - Door handles
   - Faucet handles
   - Keyboard/mouse
   - Phone handsets

3. **Disinfection Procedures**
   - Pre-clean visible dirt
   - Apply disinfectant
   - Allow proper contact time
   - Wipe or air dry

## 8.6 Checklist Template

### Daily Checklist
- [ ] Floors swept, vacuumed, mopped
- [ ] Trash emptied and liners replaced
- [ ] Surfaces dusted and sanitized
- [ ] Bathrooms cleaned and stocked
- [ ] Kitchen/break room sanitized
- [ ] Windows and mirrors cleaned
- [ ] High-touch surfaces disinfected
- [ ] Supplies restocked
- [ ] Final inspection passed

### Weekly Checklist
- [ ] Deep clean grout and tile
- [ ] Clean light fixtures
- [ ] Wash walls and doors
- [ ] Clean behind furniture
- [ ] Sanitize trash containers
- [ ] Check equipment operation
- [ ] Review inventory levels
