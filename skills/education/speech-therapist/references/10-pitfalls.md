# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Over-relying on standardized tests** | High | Always supplement with language sampling and functional observation |
| 2 | **Using "resting" face when a child is struggling** | High | Therapeutic affect matters — match or slightly exceed child's emotional energy |
| 3 | **Treating the wrong level** | High | Address phonology/articulation separately from language; don't confuse them |
| 4 | **Not collecting data every session** | High | Without data, you can't prove progress or justify continued services |
| 5 | **Treating in isolation only** | Medium | Work toward carryover — collaborate with teachers, train parents |

## 10.2 Assessment Errors

1. **Testing to a point of fatigue**: Children can't perform well when tired; split assessment across sessions if needed
2. **Ignoring cultural variables**: Eye contact, turn-taking, and eye gaze norms vary culturally
3. **Not assessing bilingual students in both languages**: English-only assessment is insufficient
4. **Confusing language difference with language disorder**: L2 acquisition vs. SLI — requires bilingual norms
5. **Relying on age-equivalent scores**: Use standard scores for eligibility; age equivalents are misleading

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Referral reviewed and case history obtained before assessment
- [ ] Hearing and vision screening completed or referral provided
- [ ] Oral mechanism examination documented
- [ ] Standardized assessment administered with proper protocol
- [ ] Language sample collected and analyzed
- [ ] Assessment report completed within 30 days
- [ ] Diagnostic impression clearly stated with supporting data
- [ ] Goals are measurable with baseline data documented
- [ ] Treatment plan includes evidence-based approach justification
- [ ] Parent feedback conference held within 10 days of assessment
- [ ] IEP or service plan meeting scheduled within required timeline
- [ ] Data collected on every treatment session
- [ ] Progress data graphed and reviewed at least monthly
- [ ] Parent/caregiver coaching provided at least monthly
- [ ] Discharge criteria met before ending services
