# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Student at Risk of Not Graduating

**User:** "A 12th-grade student has failed two required courses and has 18 credits; they need 24 to graduate. The student says they want to walk at graduation. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Actions:**
>
> 1. **Verify credit audit** against exact state/campus graduation requirements — determine if the courses can be retaken or substituted
> 2. **Check seat-time vs. credit recovery options**: Many states allow credit recovery through approved online courses (e.g., Edgenuity, Apex Learning)
> 3. **Engage the family**: Schedule a conference with student and parent/guardian; document all communication
> 4. **Explore alternative graduation pathways**: Exit-level exams, portfolio demonstration, night school
> 5. **Emotional support**: A student close to graduation who is at risk experiences significant stress — connect with counselor
>
> **Key consideration**: Know your state-specific graduation requirements and the window for credit recovery completion. Many districts have mid-year or summer credit recovery deadlines.

## 9.2 Transfer Student with Incomplete Records

**User:** "A transfer student arrives mid-semester with no transcript from their previous school. The family insists they were passing all classes. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Procedure:**
>
> 1. **Do not guess**: Never assign grades or credits without documentation
> 2. **Temporary placement**: Enroll student in grade-level courses; assign provisional schedule
> 3. **Escalate records request**: Contact previous school via formal request; follow up within 5 business days
> 4. **Interim grading**: Use teacher-assigned grades during the waiting period; update permanently once transcript received
> 5. **Check for special services**: Inquire about IEP, 504, EL status — request records separately if flagged
> 6. **FERPA compliance**: Document all attempts to obtain records; include family in the process

## 9.3 Student Experiencing Homelessness

**User:** "A student is identified as homeless under McKinney-Vento. How do I handle enrollment and what supports should I connect them to?"

**Expert:**
> **McKinney-Vento Compliance:**
>
> - **Immediate enrollment** regardless of missing documents (enrollment cannot be delayed)
> - **School of origin rights**: Student may remain at original school with transportation provided
> - Connect to **school liaison** and **community housing resources**
> - **Fee waivers**: Automatically eligible for SAT/ACT fee waivers, AP exam fee reductions
> - **Academic accommodations**: Extended time on assignments, access to counseling
> - **Privacy**: Do not stigmatize or segregate; treat as any other enrolled student
>
> **Critical documentation**: Complete McKinney-Vento designation form; update student info system enrollment status code

## 9.4 Academic Dishonesty Incident

**User:** "A teacher reports a student cheated on a major exam. The student denies it. What's the protocol?"

**Expert:**
> **Due Process Steps:**
>
> 1. **Teacher documents**: Collect evidence (answer patterns, witness statements, digital forensics if applicable)
> 2. **Administrator review**: Determine if evidence meets academic dishonesty threshold
> 3. **Student conference**: Give student opportunity to explain; document the conversation
> 4. **Parent notification**: Communicate finding, consequence, and appeal rights
> 5. **Consequence range**: First offense typically ranges from re-do assignment to 0 on the assessment; repeated offenses escalate
> 6. **No grade reduction for unproven allegations**: If evidence is inconclusive, do not penalize
> 7. **Record keeping**: Log in student conduct file; report to post-secondary institutions per local policy
