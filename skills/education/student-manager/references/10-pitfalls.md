# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Record-Keeping Pitfalls

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Entering grades before term end | 🔴 High | Lock gradebook at term close; follow grading period deadlines |
| 2 | Deleting/discarding disciplinary records | 🔴 High | Archive all records per retention policy; FERPA requires documentation |
| 3 | Sharing student data with unauthorized persons | 🔴 High | Verify identity and authorization before any data disclosure |
| 4 | Inconsistent attendance coding | 🟡 Medium | Train all office staff on standardized attendance codes |
| 5 | Failing to update emergency contacts | 🟡 Medium | Verify contact info at each enrollment and annually |

## 10.2 Communication Anti-Patterns

- **Emailing grades to the entire class** — FERPA violation; send individual grades only
- **Discussing student issues in hallways** — Confidential conversations belong in private offices
- **Sending report cards to the wrong address** — Double-check recipient before sending
- **Using personal email for official communication** — Always use district/school email systems
- **Posting student work/photos without consent** — Obtain media release annually

## 10.3 Enrollment & Scheduling Anti-Patterns

- Placing student in course without prerequisite verification → credit deficiency cascade
- Overriding master schedule constraints without documentation → impossible schedule situations
- Assigning same counselor to all students in a caseload → conflict of interest risks
- Delaying enrollment for missing documents (homeless, foster care) → McKinney-Vento violation

## 10.4 Best Practice Checklist

- [ ] All student records updated within 48 hours of enrollment
- [ ] FERPA training completed annually
- [ ] Attendance data reconciled daily
- [ ] Parent contact log maintained for at-risk students
- [ ] Credit audit run at start of each semester for all grade levels
- [ ] 504/IEP accommodations verified before scheduling
- [ ] Graduation requirements reviewed with every 11th-grader
- [ ] Student data shared only with verified authorization
- [ ] Incident reports filed within 24 hours
- [ ] Backup procedures tested for SIS data integrity
