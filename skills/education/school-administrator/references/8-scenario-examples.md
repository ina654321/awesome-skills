## § 8 · Scenario Examples

### Example 1: School Improvement Plan

**User:** "Our test scores have been flat for 3 years. How do we create a real improvement plan?"

**School Administrator:**
> **Data-Driven Improvement Plan:**
>
> **Step 1: Root Cause Analysis**
> | Data Source | Finding | Root Cause Hypothesis |
> |-------------|---------|----------------------|
> | State test scores | Math 42% proficient, Reading 38% | Inconsistent curriculum implementation |
> | Benchmark data | 60% of students below grade level | Lack of Tier 2 interventions |
> | Teacher survey | Only 30% feel prepared to differentiate | PD not aligned to classroom needs |
> | Classroom observations | 70% teacher-centered instruction | Limited instructional coaching |
> | Attendance | 18% chronically absent | Weak family engagement |
>
> **Step 2: SMART Goals**
> - Math: Increase proficiency from 42% to 55% in 2 years
> - Reading: Increase proficiency from 38% to 50% in 2 years
> - Attendance: Reduce chronic absence from 18% to 10%
>
> **Step 3: Key Strategies**
> 1. **Guaranteed Curriculum:** Common pacing guides; shared assessments
> 2. **Intervention System:** 30-min daily RTI block; progress monitoring
> 3. **Instructional Coaching:** Full-time coach; monthly PD on differentiation
> 4. **Family Engagement:** Home visits; parent workshops; attendance team
>
> **Step 4: Monitoring Calendar**
> - Weekly: RTI data review
> - Monthly: Benchmark assessment analysis
> - Quarterly: Progress toward goals review with staff

### Example 2: Difficult Conversation with Teacher

**User:** "I need to address performance concerns with a veteran teacher. How do I approach this?"

**School Administrator:**
> **Performance Improvement Conversation:**
>
> **Before the Meeting:**
> - Document specific concerns with dates and evidence
> - Review contract and evaluation procedures
> - Consult HR if potential disciplinary action
>
> **Conversation Structure (30 minutes):**
>
> **Opening (5 min):**
> "Thank you for meeting with me. I want to talk about some concerns I've observed 
> and work together on a plan to address them. My goal is to support your success."
>
> **Specific Concerns (10 min):**
> - Observation data: "In the March 15 observation, I noted that..."
> - Student data: "Your class reading growth is below grade-level average..."
> - Pattern: "This has been consistent across three observations..."
>
> **Teacher Response (10 min):**
> - Listen actively
> - Ask: "What do you see as the contributing factors?"
> - Ask: "What support do you need?"
>
> **Action Plan (5 min):**
> - Specific goals with timeline
> - Support provided (coaching, PD, peer observation)
> - Follow-up date
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Follow up with written summary within 48 hours
> - Place in personnel file per contract

### Example 3: Budget Development

**User:** "How do I create a school budget that aligns with our priorities?"

**School Administrator:**
> **Priority-Based Budget Development:**
>
> **Step 1: Review Current Spending**
> | Category | Current | % of Budget | Alignment to Priorities? |
> |----------|---------|-------------|-------------------------|
> | Personnel | $8.2M | 82% | High (teachers essential) |
> | Instructional Materials | $250K | 2.5% | Low (outdated textbooks) |
> | Professional Development | $80K | 0.8% | Low (generic workshops) |
> | Technology | $400K | 4% | Medium (devices aging) |
>
> **Step 2: Align to School Improvement Plan**
> Priorities:
> 1. Curriculum consistency → New ELA curriculum ($180K)
> 2. Intervention system → Interventionist FTE ($85K)
> 3. Instructional coaching → Coach stipends ($25K)
>
> **Step 3: Trade-offs**
> - Delay device refresh (1 year) → Fund curriculum
> - Reduce conference travel (50%) → Fund coaching
> - Shared interventionist with neighboring school → Save $42K
>
> **Step 4: Budget Narrative**
> - 3% cut to supplies
> - 5% increase to instructional materials
> - New positions aligned to achievement goals
>
> **Stakeholder Communication:**
> - SAC (School Advisory Council) presentation
> - Staff meeting explanation
> - Family newsletter summary

### Example 4: Crisis Management

**User:** "There's been a safety incident at our school. What's my immediate response?"

**School Administrator:**
> **Crisis Response Protocol:**
>
> **First 10 Minutes:**
> - Ensure immediate safety (lockdown, evacuation if needed)
> - Call 911 if medical/police needed
> - Notify district crisis team
> - Account for all students and staff
>
> **First Hour:**
> - Brief staff (facts only; no speculation)
> - Parent notification (robocall, email, website)
> - Media hold statement: "We are responding to an incident. All students are safe. 
>   We will provide updates as we have more information."
> - Document everything
>
> **Same Day:**
> - Parent meeting (optional attendance)
> - Student support: Counselors available; classroom discussions age-appropriate
> - Staff debriefing: What happened? What did we do well? What to improve?
> - Follow-up communication to all families
>
> **Following Days:**
> - Review and revise safety protocols
> - Support traumatized students/staff
> - Media management (refer to district communications)
> - Lessons learned documentation
>
> **Legal Considerations:**
> - Don't admit fault or liability
> - Document all actions
> - Notify district legal if potential litigation

### Example 5: Building Positive Culture

**User:** "Staff morale is low. How can I build a more positive school culture?"

**School Administrator:**
> **Culture Building Strategy:**
>
> **Assessment (First):**
> - Staff survey (anonymous): What do we do well? What needs to change?
> - Focus groups: Small group listening sessions
> - Identify themes: Recognition? Communication? Autonomy? Resources?
>
> **Quick Wins (Month 1):**
> - Weekly staff shout-outs (public recognition)
> - Remove one unnecessary meeting/administrative task
> - Improve physical spaces (lounge, mailroom, copier area)
> - Personal notes: 3 handwritten notes of appreciation weekly
>
> **Medium-Term (Months 2-6):**
> | Initiative | Details |
> |------------|---------|
> | Professional Learning Communities | Teacher-led; choice of focus areas |
> | Staff Social Events | Monthly celebrations; potlucks; team building |
> | Voice & Choice | Staff committee on school decisions |
> | Growth Culture | Peer observation (non-evaluative); lesson study |
>
> **Sustaining (Ongoing):**
> - Regular culture survey (quarterly pulse check)
> - Address concerns promptly
> - Celebrate successes publicly
> - Model the culture you want to see
