# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Co-Teaching Models

```
Model 1: One Teach, One Observe
├── Lead teacher instructs; TA observes student responses
├── Observer collects data: who's answering, who's disengaged, error patterns
└── Post-lesson debrief: share observation data with lead teacher

Model 2: Parallel Teaching
├── Split class in half; both teach same content simultaneously
├── Lower student-to-teacher ratio enables more individual attention
└── Coordinate timing and content to ensure parity

Model 3: Station Teaching
├── Class divided into stations; content and materials vary by station
├── Lead teacher circulates; TA manages 1-2 stations
└── Each station should be self-explanatory with clear directions

Model 4: Lead and Support
├── Lead teacher presents new content; TA supports specific students
├── TA may work with small group, provide scaffolds, or manage materials
└── Pre-planned roles: don't ad-lib during instruction

Model 5: Team Teaching
├── Both teach simultaneously; share instruction fluidly
├── Requires highest level of coordination and trust
└── Effective for complex content requiring real-time expertise
```

## 8.2 Small Group Instruction Workflow

1. **Pre-session coordination** (before class): Review lesson objective with lead teacher; clarify your group's specific target
2. **Prep materials**: Ensure all materials are ready and organized before pulling the group
3. **Signal the pull**: Use a consistent, respectful signal (e.g., "Group 1, please come to the back table")
4. **Instruction**: Deliver content in chunks — 5 minutes max before checking for understanding
5. **Active engagement**: Every student should respond at least once every 3 minutes
6. **Data collection**: Note which students grasp the concept and which need reteaching
7. **Closure**: Summarize key point; link back to whole-class lesson
8. **Debrief with lead teacher**: Share which students need follow-up support

## 8.3 Supporting Students with Special Needs

1. **Know the student's IEP/504**: Read the accommodation and modification sections before the lesson
2. **Preview content**: Briefly pre-teach vocabulary or concepts to students with processing difficulties
3. **Seating arrangement**: Place student near you for proximity support; away from distractions
4. **Checklist for self-monitoring**: Help the student track their own progress on a simple checklist
5. **Break tasks into steps**: Students with executive function challenges need step-by-step guidance
6. **Scaffold language**: Provide sentence starters, word banks, and graphic organizers
7. **Behavior intervention**: Implement the BIP consistently — document incidents and responses
8. **Communicate with lead teacher**: Daily or end-of-day check-in on student progress
