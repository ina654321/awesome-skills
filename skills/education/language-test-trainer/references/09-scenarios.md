# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Common Scenario: Strong Grammar, Weak Speaking

**User:** "My student consistently scores 90+ in grammar sections but only 60 in speaking. How do I bridge this gap?"

**Expert:**
> **Active Production Bridge:**
>
> 1. **The problem**: Grammar knowledge does not automatically transfer to real-time production. This is called "passive competence."
> 2. **Time-pressured repetition**: Have student do timed grammar-to-speech drills. "Translate this sentence into spoken English in 10 seconds." Speed forces automaticity.
> 3. **Shadowing**: Play native speaker model response; have student repeat immediately. Focus on rhythm, intonation, and pace — not just accuracy.
> 4. **Conversation scaffolds**: Give sentence starters, connectors, and opinion phrases. Gradually remove scaffolds as fluency improves.
> 5. **Speaking journal**: 5-minute daily speaking diary recorded via phone. Listen back, self-assess, and note one thing to improve.
> 6. **Error fossilization prevention**: When errors are repeated, address immediately with targeted reformulation drills.
> 7. **Target: 20 hours of speaking practice before next mock test.** Grammar knowledge is the foundation — now we build the bridge to production.

## 9.2 Complex Scenario: Student Fails Test by 0.5 Band — Retake?

**User:** "A student's IELTS score was 6.0 but their university requirement is 6.5. Should they retake? How long should they wait?"

**Expert:**
> **Retake Decision Framework:**
>
> 1. **Analyze the score report**: Was the 6.0 consistent across all sections (L:6, R:6, W:6, S:6) or was it lopsided? A 5.5 in Writing + 6.5 in others requires a different strategy than across-the-board 6.0.
> 2. **Timeline pressure**: If university deadline is within 3 weeks — yes, retake. If within 1 week — consider asking university for conditional offer + later score.
> 3. **Targeted remediation**: Before retake, address the weakest sub-skill intensively. 20-30 hours of focused practice can shift 0.5 band.
> 4. **Wait time**: Minimum 2-3 weeks to show improvement. Waiting 6+ weeks without practice is wasteful.
> 5. **Cost-benefit**: IELTS retake fee vs. cost of a pre-sessional language course. Sometimes the course is better value.
> 6. **Probability**: A 0.5 band improvement is achievable but not guaranteed. Set realistic expectations. Prepare for possibility of needing to take TWO retakes.
> 7. **Alternative proof**: Some universities accept other tests (PTE Academic, Duolingo) as substitutes. Check if this applies.

## 9.3 Edge Case: Student Has Test Anxiety

**User:** "Every practice test my student scores 7.0, but they panic on the real test and drop to 6.0. What can we do?"

**Expert:**
> **Test Anxiety Management Program:**
>
> 1. **Simulate real conditions**: Practice with strict timed conditions, no notes, unfamiliar room setup. Normalize the stress response.
> 2. **Physiological regulation**: Teach 4-7-8 breathing technique. Practice before every practice test. Deep breathing reduces cortisol.
> 3. **Desensitization**: Visit the test center before the exam date. Familiarity reduces novelty anxiety.
> 4. **Mindset reframe**: "Nervous = energized." Anxiety and excitement have identical physiological signatures — coach them to reframe.
> 5. **Strategic section-order**: Let student choose which section to start with (if test allows flexibility). Starting with their strongest section builds confidence.
> 6. **Time-buffers**: Teach time-boxing with a "move on" rule. No question is worth panicking over — flag and return if time allows.
> 7. **Post-test ritual**: Establish a calming routine to do in the 10 minutes before the test starts (e.g., deep breathing, visualization of success).
> 8. **Professional support**: If anxiety is severe, consider brief CBT intervention from a counselor specializing in performance anxiety.
