# Standards & Reference

## 7.1 Academic Calendar & Scheduling Standards

- **State-mandated instructional days**: Minimum seat time requirements per state education code
- **District academic calendar frameworks**: Semester, trimester, block scheduling models
- **Credit hour definitions**: Carnegie Unit standards (120 contact hours = 1 high school credit)
- **Bell schedule templates**: Traditional, block, hybrid rotating schedules
- **Final exam/assessment windows**: Semester-end assessment scheduling protocols
- **Professional development day allocation**: State-required PD hours integration

## 7.2 Curriculum Alignment Standards

- **Understanding by Design (UbD)**: Backward design from standards to assessments to instruction
- **State curriculum frameworks**: Common Core, NGSS, C3 Social Studies standards alignment
- **Scope and sequence documents**: Vertical alignment across grade levels
- **Course catalog standards**: NCAA/UC a-g compliance verification
- **Textbook adoption cycles**: State and district adoption review processes

## 7.3 Master Schedule Standards

- **Student-to-course allocation**: Balancing section sizes with enrollment demands
- **Teacher certification matching**: Course assignments must align with credentialing
- **Prerequisite chains**: Course sequencing to prevent scheduling conflicts
- **Co-teaching model scheduling**: Inclusion and collaborative teaching arrangements
- **Intervention/enrichment block scheduling**: WIN (What I Need) time allocation

## 7.4 Data-Driven Planning Standards

- **Enrollment projections**: Demographic-based student population forecasting
- **Course demand analysis**: Elective and core course enrollment trend tracking
- **Staffing ratio standards**: Student-to-teacher ratios by subject area
- **Facility utilization metrics**: Classroom and lab usage optimization
- **Section fill rate targets**: Minimum enrollment thresholds per section
