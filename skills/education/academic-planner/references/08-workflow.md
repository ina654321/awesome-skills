# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Master Schedule Development Workflow

```
Schedule Development Cycle (Oct–Jun)
├── Phase 1: Data Review (Oct–Nov)
│   ├── Review student enrollment projections
│   ├── Analyze course request data from current year
│   ├── Assess building and staffing capacity
│   └── Align with state and district requirements
├── Phase 2: Structural Design (Dec–Jan)
│   ├── Determine bell schedule and calendar structure
│   ├── Define course priority tiers
│   ├── Map teacher certifications to course needs
│   └── Identify scheduling constraints
├── Phase 3: Course Placement (Jan–Mar)
│   ├── Build prerequisite chains
│   ├── Assign courses to periods
│   ├── Schedule intervention/enrichment blocks
│   └── Balance section loads
├── Phase 4: Validation & Adjustment (Apr–May)
│   ├── Verify all students can complete graduation requirements
│   ├── Identify and resolve conflicts
│   ├── Adjust section sizes as needed
│   └── Communicate schedule changes to families
└── Phase 5: Finalization (May–Jun)
    ├── Publish master schedule
    ├── Train teachers on schedule structure
    └── Prepare for first-day logistics
```

## 8.2 Course Request Processing Workflow

1. Collect student course requests (January window)
2. Verify prerequisite completion for all requests
3. Flag conflicts with graduation requirements
4. Run course demand report; adjust section numbers
5. Identify courses at risk of cancellation (low enrollment)
6. Notify students of alternate placements
7. Final enrollment lock before schedule building

## 8.3 Schedule Change Request Workflow

1. Student submits request during designated window (typically first 5 days of semester)
2. Review reason for change; verify space availability
3. Check prerequisite completion and graduation impact
4. Confirm teacher certification for course
5. Evaluate room and period capacity
6. Approve or deny with documented rationale
7. Notify student, parent, teacher, and registrar
8. Update SIS and generate new schedule if approved

## 8.4 Semester Transition Workflow

1. Pre-semester: Finalize section rosters; distribute to teachers
2. Week 1: Monitor add/drop period; process schedule changes
3. Week 2: Finalize all rosters; close schedule changes
4. Weeks 3-4: Monitor course performance; identify students needing schedule changes
5. Ongoing: Process schedule changes only for documented exceptional circumstances
