## 8. Standard Workflow

### 8.1 New Program Development

```
Phase 1: Market Research (Weeks 1-4)
├── Conduct labor market analysis: O*NET, Burning Glass, state employment data
├── Survey 10+ potential employer partners: skill gaps, hiring volume, willingness to pay
├── Identify funding sources: WIOA, state funds, employer contracts
└── [✓ Done]: Demand validated: 3+ signed letters of intent + funding source identified
    [✗ FAIL]: No employer commitment → STOP, validate demand before proceeding

Phase 2: Curriculum Design (Weeks 5-10)
├── Map to industry competencies: NCMM, vendor certifications, internal standards
├── Design assessment framework: written + practical + portfolio
├── Identify instructional capacity: internal instructors vs. subcontracted
└── [✓ Done]: Curriculum aligned with 3+ recognized credentials
    [✗ FAIL]: No credential alignment → redesign; pure internal certs have low value

Phase 3: Partnership Formalization (Weeks 11-14)
├── Draft MOU with employer partners: roles, commitments, financial terms
├── Submit grant applications if government-funded
├── Register apprenticeship if applicable
└── [✓ Done]: Signed MOUs + funding secured + compliance approved
    [✗ FAIL]: No signed partners → delay launch; never start without committed employers

Phase 4: Launch & Iteration (Weeks 15+)
├── Recruit first cohort: marketing, enrollment, onboarding
├── Deliver program with continuous feedback loops
├── Track outcomes: completion, employment, salary
├── Iterate based on employer feedback
└── [✓ Done]: 80%+ completion, 75%+ employment, employer satisfaction >85%
```

### 8.2 Employer Partnership Development

```
Step 1: Identify Target Employers
  → Companies with documented skill gaps, recent growth, or turnover issues
  → Industry sectors with regional demand (check local workforce data)

Step 2: Initial Outreach
  → Value proposition: "We reduce your hiring cost by 40% with pre-trained candidates"
  → Request: 30-min call to understand their workforce challenges

Step 3: Needs Assessment
  → Interview hiring managers: What skills do you need? What's the pain?
  → Analyze job postings: What credentials do you require?
  → Benchmark salaries: What do you pay for trained vs. untrained?

Step 4: Program Co-Design
  → Propose curriculum based on their needs
  → Request advisory involvement: review curriculum, mentor students, hire graduates
  → Discuss financial commitment: direct funding, hiring commitment, or both

Step 5: Formalize Partnership
  → Draft MOU: deliverables, timeline, financial terms, exit clauses
  → Launch pilot cohort with clear success metrics

[✓ Done]: Employer signs MOU + commits to hiring 3+ graduates per cohort
```
