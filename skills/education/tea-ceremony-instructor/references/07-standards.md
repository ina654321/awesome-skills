## 7.2 Skill Progression Framework (Urasenke)

| Level | Name | Description | Typical Time |
|-------|------|-------------|:---:|
|入门 (Nyūmon) | Introduction | Basic posture, how to enter/tatami etiquette | 3-6 months |
| Basic (Kihon) | Fundamentals | Folding fukusa, temae basics | 1-2 years |
| Intermediate (Chūkyū) | Advanced basics | Standing procedures, guests of honor protocols | 2-4 years |
| Upper intermediate | Extended procedures | Urasenke-specific temae variations | 3-5 years |
| Advanced (Jōkyū) | Teacher preparation | Supervised teaching, complex protocols | 5-8 years |
| Master (Kōshō) | Full transmission | Complete mastery, lineage transmission | 10+ years |

## 7.3 Tea Ceremony Etiquette Reference

| Element | Correct Practice |
|---------|-----------------|
| Bowing | From the waist, 15° casual, 30° formal, 45° deep |
| Entering the tea room | Left foot first; step over the tatami seam |
| Seating | Formal kneeling (seiza) or half-seiza; never full seiza for long periods |
| Handling chakin | Fold in thirds, wipe in one direction only |
| Whisking matcha | Fast back-and-forth in mame or wide W in gojyū byōki |
| Receiving the bowl | Turn clockwise twice before drinking; compliment host |
| Host's bow timing | Bow when guest bows; match guest's bow depth |

## 7.4 Historical Periods and Their Impact

- Muromachi period (1336-1573): Origins of wabi-cha; Sen no Rikyū influences
- Momoyama period (1573-1615): Architectural development of chashitsu (tea room)
- Edo period (1615-1868): Formalization of procedure; full expansion of lineages
- Meiji period onward: Preservation and transmission of tradition
- Contemporary: Adaptation to global audiences while maintaining core protocols
