# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Rushing through temae to "finish"** | High | Emphasize process over product; one full bow is more meaningful than three rushed ones |
| 2 | **Teaching the form without the meaning** | High | Every movement has history and significance — explain it |
| 3 | **Correcting too many things at once** | Medium | Focus on one correction per session; layering corrections causes frustration |
| 4 | **Comparing students to each other** | Medium | Each student's journey is individual; traditional arts require patience |
| 5 | **Neglecting the student's cultural background** | Medium | Western students need different framings than Japanese students |

## 10.2 Teaching Practice Errors

1. **Skipping the theory**: Students who don't know why they fold the fukusa a certain way will forget the form quickly
2. **Not practicing before teaching**: Instructors must rehearse — teaching imperfect form propagates imperfect form
3. **Overcorrecting physical posture**: Excessive posture correction without explaining why kills the joy of learning
4. **Ignoring the emotional experience**: Tea ceremony is supposed to be calming — if a student leaves stressed, the teaching failed
5. **Not adapting for non-Japanese students**: Some concepts (wa, kei, waabi) require extensive explanation for non-Japanese learners

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Lesson plan prepared before each session
- [ ] All utensils cleaned and properly arranged before students arrive
- [ ] Each student's progress documented after each lesson
- [ ] Balance of theoretical explanation and hands-on practice in each lesson
- [ ] Seasonal elements (scroll, flowers, sweets) updated appropriately
- [ ] Home practice assigned and reviewed at subsequent lesson
- [ ] Instructor maintains own practice (at minimum monthly observation of higher-level practitioner)
- [ ] Safety considerations met (hot water, sharp implements, tatami care)
- [ ] Assessment of student's readiness before advancing to next level
- [ ] Cultural sensitivity and historical accuracy maintained in all teaching
- [ ] Documentation of student progression maintained (portfolios, belt/kakemono advancement)
