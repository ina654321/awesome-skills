# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Lesson Planning Workflow

```
Pre-Lesson
├── Review student's progress from previous lesson
├── Prepare the topic and specific technique for this session
├── Set up the chashitsu: tatami orientation, flowers, scroll
├── Prepare all utensils (kama, chakin, hishaku, etc.) — laid out in order of use
├── Practice the temae being taught (instructors must rehearse)
└── Consider student age, attention span, cultural background

Lesson Structure (60-90 minutes)
├── 10 min: Greeting, tea, conversation — build rapport; inquire about student's week
├── 10 min: Theory/background — explain the meaning behind what they will practice
├── 20 min: Demonstration — instructor performs temae slowly; narrates each movement
├── 30 min: Guided practice — student mirrors instructor step by step; instructor corrects
├── 15 min: Student performs full sequence independently while instructor observes
└── 5 min: Debrief — what felt difficult? Preview next lesson; bow out

Post-Lesson
├── Document progress notes for next lesson plan
├── Clean and store utensils properly
├── Reflect on what teaching approach worked/didn't work
└── Prepare home practice assignments
```

## 8.2 Progression Workflow for Students

### Beginner Path
1. **Etiquette first**: How to enter the room, bow, sit, hold the fan
2. **Host's role introduction**: Watch instructor perform a full thick tea (koicha) ceremony
3. **Hands-on with thick tea**: Make and drink koicha first (it's forgiving; taste covers mistakes)
4. **Thin tea (usucha) fundamentals**: Folding fukusa, using chakin, hishaku
5. **Full basic temae**: Light usucha ceremony from beginning to end

### Intermediate Path
1. **Guest protocols**: Learning to be a guest before being a formal guest of honor
2. **Kōgū (light greeting):** The abbreviated ceremony for frequent guests
3. **Seasonal variations**: How scrolls, flowers, and sweets change with seasons
4. **Handling utensils with confidence**: Grace, economy of movement, quietness

### Advanced Path
1. **Nakadachi**: Standing to receive guests
2. **Multiple guests of honor protocols**
3. **Interpretation**: Learning to explain the ceremony to English-speaking guests
4. **Teaching observation**: Watch your instructor teach beginners
5. **Begin supervised teaching**: Start with simple corrections; progress to full instruction

## 8.3 Teaching Demonstration Protocol

1. Instructor demonstrates full ceremony at normal pace
2. Instructor repeats at half-speed, narrating each movement and its meaning
3. Instructor demonstrates key transition points 3 times at slow speed
4. Student attempts while instructor provides physical guidance (hand-over-hand for posture corrections)
5. Student performs independently while instructor notes errors for post-performance feedback
6. Correct errors one at a time — do not overwhelm with multiple corrections
