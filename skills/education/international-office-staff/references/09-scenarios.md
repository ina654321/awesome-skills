# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Common Scenario: Student Visa Denied After Admission

**User:** "An international student just received a visa denial 2 weeks before the program starts. What can we do?"

**Expert:**
> **Response Protocol:**
>
> 1. **Immediate**: Contact the student to understand the denial reason (checklist vs. section 214b)
> 2. **If administrative processing**: Assure student; provide updated enrollment letter; write support letter highlighting institutional ties
> 3. **If denial for finances**: Help student identify additional funding sources; provide detailed cost-of-attendance breakdown; explore scholarship options
> 4. **If 214b (immigrant intent)**: Advise on reapplication strategy; address what evidence of home-country ties can be strengthened
> 5. **Deferral**: Offer one-semester or one-year deferral at no additional cost; reissue I-20 for new start date
> 6. **Online option**: If available, offer initial semester(s) online while visa is reprocessed
> 7. **Document**: Record denial reason and actions taken; flag student record for future admissions cycles
> 8. **Appeal**: Most visa denials cannot be appealed; reapplication with stronger documentation is the path

## 9.2 Complex Scenario: Student Arrested in Host Country

**User:** "We received word that one of our international students was arrested. What are our obligations and limitations?"

**Expert:**
> **Crisis Response Protocol:**
>
> 1. **Privacy first**: Do not disclose the student's identity to others. FERBA/GDPR applies — only need-to-know staff may be informed.
> 2. **Notify DSO/RO**: The Designated School Official or Responsible Officer must be informed immediately.
> 3. **Do not intervene in legal process**: The institution cannot provide legal representation or intervene with police. Provide a list of immigration attorneys instead.
> 4. **Contact home country's embassy/consulate**: International students have the right to communicate with their embassy. Provide this contact information to the student.
> 5. **Visa implications**: Arrest does NOT automatically mean visa cancellation. The DSO must assess whether the student remains in status. Consult with immigration attorney.
> 6. **Academic support**: The student's spot in classes should be held pending resolution.
> 7. **Mental health**: Connect student with counseling services immediately.
> 8. **Family contact**: Do NOT contact family without student consent (except if student is a minor).

## 9.3 Edge Case: Student Requests Religious Accommodation Conflict with Academic Calendar

**User:** "A student requests to miss all Friday afternoon classes for religious observances, but some required courses only meet on Fridays at that time."

**Expert:**
> **Accommodation Negotiation:**
>
> 1. **Engage the student**: Document their specific religious obligation and the duration/pattern
> 2. **Engage the faculty**: Explain the accommodation request; do not disclose the specific religion without consent
> 3. **Explore alternatives**:
>    - Independent study or directed reading for one course
>    - Alternative assessment (exam taken at a different time)
>    - Course substitution if an equivalent course is available at a different time
>    - Late enrollment in a later semester for the specific course
> 4. **Document the agreement**: Written record signed by student, faculty, and international office
> 5. **Visa implications**: Missed classes do not automatically affect visa status if student still completes the program within normal duration. Monitor to ensure minimum enrollment is maintained.
> 6. **No denial without alternatives**: A required course cannot be refused accommodation without offering a viable alternative pathway to meet the requirement.
