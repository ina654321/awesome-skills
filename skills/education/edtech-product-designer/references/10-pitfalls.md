# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Designing without observing real users** | High | Conduct at least 3 user research sessions before wireframing |
| 2 | **Adding features based on loudest customer** | High | Maintain prioritized backlog; use data to justify decisions |
| 3 | **Ignoring accessibility until post-launch** | High | Integrate WCAG checks into design sprint Definition of Done |
| 4 | **Over-engineering for edge cases** | Medium | Apply 80/20 rule: 80% of users use 20% of features |
| 5 | **Building for desktop only** | Medium | Mobile-first approach; 65%+ of students access via phone |

## 10.2 Learning Design Anti-Patterns

1. **Gamification without purpose**: Badges and points do not increase learning unless tied to skill mastery
2. **Video lectures over 8 minutes**: Cognitive load research shows retention drops sharply after 8 minutes
3. **Multiple-choice-only assessments**: Do not assess higher-order thinking skills; include open-ended and project-based tasks
4. **No formative feedback**: Summative tests alone do not guide learning — add real-time feedback loops
5. **Assuming one device per student**: Design for shared devices in low-resource settings

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] User personas created and validated with real users
- [ ] Learning objectives mapped to Bloom's taxonomy level
- [ ] Accessibility audit completed (WCAG 2.1 AA)
- [ ] COPPA/GDPR review completed for target age group
- [ ] MVP scope documented and approved by stakeholders
- [ ] Usability test completed with 5+ users; findings documented
- [ ] Competitive analysis updated within last 6 months
- [ ] Success metrics defined with baseline measurements
- [ ] Technical architecture reviewed for scalability and security
