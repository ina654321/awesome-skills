## 8. Standard Workflow

### 8.1 Daily Childcare Planning

```
Phase 1: Preparation (Before children arrive)
├── Review individual child needs and any special circumstances
├── Prepare environment: materials, safety check, setup activities
├── Review daily schedule and any deviations needed
└── [✓ Done]: Environment ready and materials prepared

Phase 2: Care Routine (Throughout the day)
├── Arrival: Greet families, exchange information, note concerns
├── Meals/Snacks: Nutritious food, social experience, self-feeding support
├── Sleep: Consistent routines, safe sleep practices
├── Diapering/Toileting: Individual routines, hygiene
└── [✓ Done]: All basic needs met with warmth and consistency

Phase 3: Learning Activities
├── Choose activities based on: developmental goals, child interests, weather
├── Lead with open-ended materials: blocks, art, dramatic play
├── Follow child's lead: extend their play, ask questions
├── Document learning: photos, notes for parent communication
└── [✓ Done]: At least 2-3 purposeful learning experiences per day

Phase 4: End of Day
├── Complete daily reports: meals, naps, activities, highlights, concerns
├── Share with parents: verbal handoff, answer questions
├── Document: incident reports, developmental observations
└── [✓ Done]: Smooth transition; parents informed and confident
```

### 8.2 Responding to Behavior Challenges

```
Step 1: Observe and Understand
  → What's happening? Context, triggers, child's state
  → Is this developmentally normal? (tantrums are normal at 2)
  → What does the child need? (attention, food, rest, space?)

Step 2: Respond with Guidance
  → Acknowledge feelings: "I see you're frustrated"
  → Set limit: "We don't hit, but you can say 'no thank you'"
  → Offer alternative: "Let's use our words
  → Stay calm: Model self-regulation

Step 3: Follow Through
  → If behavior continues: redirect to different activity
  → If harmful: remove from situation safely
  → If persistent: document and discuss with parents

Step 4: Reflect and Adjust
  → What worked? What didn't?
  → Pattern? (time of day, transitions, specific children?)
  → Environment or routine change needed?

[✓ Done]: Child returns to regulated state; no escalation
```
