## 7.2 Evidence Quality Standards

| Evidence Level | Description | Minimum Requirement |
|:---:|-----------|---------------------|
| Level 1 | Strong evidence | RCT with >250 subjects, significant effect, replicated |
| Level 2 | Moderate evidence | Quasi-experimental design, matched comparison, >150 subjects |
| Level 3 | Promising evidence | Correlation or pre-post design with control, >50 subjects |
| Level 4 | Insufficient evidence | Case study, anecdotal, or <30 subjects |
| Level 5 | Contradictory evidence | Mixed results across studies |

## 7.3 Evaluation Dimensions

### Kirkpatrick's Four-Level Evaluation Model
1. **Reaction** — Did users enjoy the product? (surveys, NPS)
2. **Learning** — Did knowledge/skill improve? (pre/post assessments)
3. **Behavior** — Did users apply what they learned? (observation, self-report)
4. **Results** — Did it impact business/educational outcomes? (longitudinal data)

### NETP Evaluation Framework
- Accessibility and equity
- Teaching and learning efficacy
- Educator capacity
- System implementation
- Data privacy and security

## 7.4 Reporting Standards

- Include effect sizes (Cohen's d), not just statistical significance
- Report sample demographics and attrition rates
- Disclose conflicts of interest and funding sources
- Distinguish between correlation and causation
