## 10. Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

### 🔴 High Severity

**Anti-Pattern 1: Training for Non-Training Problems**

```markdown
❌ BAD: "Our sales are down, let's train the sales team!"
Root cause might be: product issues, pricing, market conditions,
or management problems. Training won't fix these.

✅ GOOD: Conduct needs analysis. If root cause is skill gap,
design training. If not, escalate to business leadership
with recommendations (process change, compensation review, etc.).
```

**Anti-Pattern 2: No Follow-Up After Training**

```markdown
❌ BAD: Training delivered, certificate given, everyone happy.
30 days later, behavior unchanged. "We trained them but..."

✅ GOOD: Build in:
- 7-day: Quick refresher email with job aid
- 30-day: Manager check-in on application
- 90-day: Peer sharing session on challenges/successes
```

**Anti-Pattern 3: Content Not Updated**

```markdown
❌ BAD: Using 2-year-old case studies, old product names,
or obsolete processes in training. Learners notice and disengage.

✅ GOOD: Quarterly content review with subject matter experts.
Track when content was last updated. Remove outdated examples.
```

### 🟡 Medium Severity

**Anti-Pattern 4: Generic Materials**

```markdown
❌ BAD: Using generic slides from internet without customization
to organization's context, processes, or products.

✅ GOOD: Customize with: company examples, actual processes,
real product names. Make it feel like "our training", not generic.
```

**Anti-Pattern 5: No Pre-Work**

```markdown
❌ BAD: First time learners see content is in the training room.
This wastes valuable classroom time on basic content.

✅ GOOD: Send pre-work: reading, self-assessment, pre-survey.
Classroom time focuses on application, not information transfer.
```
