# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 IEP Development Workflow

```
Step 1: Referral and Consent (2 weeks)
├── Teacher or parent submits written referral
├── Obtain parent consent for evaluation
└── Review existing records and prior interventions

Step 2: Evaluation (60 days per IDEA)
├── Cognitive assessment (WPPSI, WISC, or KABC)
├── Achievement assessment (WIAT, KTEA, Woodcock-Johnson)
├── Adaptive behavior rating (Vineland or ABAS)
├── Functional behavior assessment (FBA) if behavior is concern
├── Speech-language evaluation if applicable
├── Classroom observation (minimum 2)
└── Teacher and parent input via standardized checklists

Step 3: Eligibility Determination (within 60 days)
├── Review all data as team
├── Determine if student meets eligibility criteria for any disability category
├── If eligible: proceed to IEP development
├── If not eligible: provide intervention plan (RTI/MTSS) and re-evaluate later
└── Document eligibility decision with data-based rationale

Step 4: IEP Development (2-3 hours meeting)
├── Present levels of achievement and functional performance
├── Annual goals (SMART: specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound)
├── Services, accommodations, modifications, and LRE determination
├── Transition goals (age 16+)
└── Parent rights and procedural safeguards reviewed

Step 5: Implementation
├── Schedule services per IEP
├── Distribute IEP to all teachers working with student
├── Implement accommodations and modifications
└── Begin data collection on baseline and goals

Step 6: Progress Monitoring
├── Collect data weekly or bi-weekly on each goal
├── Graph data to visualize progress
├── Review progress quarterly (minimum) with team
└── Annual IEP review and revision
```

## 8.2 Classroom Instruction Workflow

### Daily Lesson Planning (UDL Framework)
1. **Multiple means of engagement**: Offer choice in activity; connect to student interests; ensure appropriate challenge level
2. **Multiple means of representation**: Present content visually AND verbally; provide graphic organizers; pre-teach vocabulary
3. **Multiple means of action/expression**: Allow verbal, written, or project-based demonstration; offer assistive technology

### Small Group Instruction
1. Pre-assess to form flexible groups (not fixed)
2. 3-5 students per group maximum
3. Direct instruction: 10 minutes max before guided practice
4. Scaffold heavily: models → prompts → fade support
5. Check for understanding every 3 minutes

## 8.3 Behavior Intervention Workflow

1. **ABC data collection**: Antecedent, Behavior, Consequence — for 3-5 occurrences before hypothesis
2. **Function of behavior**: Identify if escape, attention, sensory, or tangible motivated
3. **Replacement behavior**: Teach a socially appropriate behavior that serves the same function
4. **Teaching replacement behavior**: Explicit instruction + reinforcement schedule
5. **FBA to BIP**: Functional Behavior Assessment → Behavioral Intervention Plan
6. **Monitor and adjust**: Data-driven decision making on BIP effectiveness
