# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **IEP goals too vague ("improve reading skills")** | High | Goals must be measurable: "Will read grade-level text at 90 wcpm with 90% accuracy" |
| 2 | **Copi ng IEP goals from year to year without updating** | High | Review baseline data; goals should show progress, not repetition |
| 3 | **Relying solely on grades to measure IEP progress** | High | Use standardized data, rubrics, and curriculum-based measurement |
| 4 | **Making major IEP changes without a meeting** | High | Any substantive change to services, placement, or goals requires a team meeting |
| 5 | **Not training paraprofessionals on their role** | Medium | Paraprofessionals must have clear directives in the IEP or a separate behavior plan |

## 10.2 IEP Process Errors

1. **Missing timelines**: 60-day evaluation timeline, annual review, 3-year reevaluation — all are legal deadlines
2. **Parent no-show**: If parent doesn't attend IEP meeting, offer alternative dates; document all attempts; meeting can proceed with 3-day notice
3. **FAPE violation by under-serving**: Students must receive ALL services listed in their IEP — partial implementation is a violation
4. **No progress data**: If a student isn't making progress on goals, document it, analyze why, and revise the plan — don't wait for annual review
5. **LRE violation**: Do not remove students from general education unless their needs genuinely cannot be met there

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] IEP meeting held within 60 days of referral
- [ ] All required team members present or documented as excused
- [ ] Present levels are specific and include data
- [ ] Goals are SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- [ ] Baseline data documented for each goal
- [ ] Accommodations are specific and implementable in general education
- [ ] Progress reports sent to parents per schedule (minimum quarterly)
- [ ] Data collected on each goal at least bi-weekly
- [ ] BIP developed if behavior interferes with learning
- [ ] Annual IEP review held before the anniversary date
- [ ] 3-year reevaluation completed on schedule
- [ ] Transition services started by age 16 (or younger if appropriate)
- [ ] Parent provided procedural safeguards at least annually
