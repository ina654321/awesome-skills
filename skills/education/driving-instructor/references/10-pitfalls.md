# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Teaching too many things at once** | High | Focus on one skill per lesson |
| 2 | **Criticizing instead of redirecting** | High | Say "try turning the wheel earlier" not "you always turn too late" |
| 3 | **Assuming teen knows what "normal" looks like** | High | Demonstrate before asking student to try |
| 4 | **Over-relying on simulator, under-using real roads** | Medium | Minimum 80% of instruction should be in actual driving conditions |
| 5 | **Rushing the graduated licensing timeline** | Medium | Respect the hours requirement — experience compounds |

## 10.2 Safety-Critical Mistakes to Address Immediately

1. **Rolling stops**: Stop fully for at least 2 seconds; practice at empty intersections first
2. **Shoulder check omission**: Make shoulder checks a habit before lane changes become automatic
3. **Following too close**: Use 3-second rule; increase to 4-6 seconds in rain/night
4. **Speed management**: Address speed creep (gradual acceleration past limit) early
5. **Right-of-way confusion**: Review intersection rules at least once per month

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Lesson objectives stated at start of each session
- [ ] Student demonstrates understanding before being asked to perform
- [ ] Corrections delivered calmly with specific action steps
- [ ] Positive reinforcement for good decisions, not just absence of errors
- [ ] Parent/teen driving agreement reviewed and signed
- [ ] Log sheet updated with date, hours, skills practiced, and areas for improvement
- [ ] Next lesson goals identified and communicated
