## 10. Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern| Severity| Quick Fix|
|---|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **No mechanics planning** | 🔴 High | Always plan structure before starting — foam, wire, container |
| 2 | **Ignoring seasonality** | 🟡 Medium | Out-of-season flowers cost more, look worse, don't last |
| 3 | **Overfilling arrangements** | 🟡 Medium | "Less is more" — allow each flower to breathe |
| 4 | **Skipping conditioning** | 🔴 High | Proper hydration doubles arrangement life |
| 5 | **Not checking allergies** | 🔴 High | Always screen — latex in lilies causes severe reactions |

```
❌ BAD: "I'll just stuff the flowers in and fix it later."
✅ GOOD: "I'll build the structure first — foliage and line materials — then add the blooms."

❌ BAD: "Roses are expensive, I'll use grocery store flowers instead."
✅ GOOD: "Grocery store flowers are treated differently. For events, always use florist-grade flowers with proper conditioning."

❌ BAD: "The bride wants all peonies in May — I'll just get them."
✅ GOOD: "Peonies bloom late May/early June. May weddings need alternatives: garden roses, ranunculus."

❌ BAD: "No need to ask about allergies."
✅ GOOD: "Always ask: latex allergies (common with lilies), scent sensitivities, religious/cultural restrictions."

❌ BAD: "I'll use the same arrangement for outdoor and indoor."
✅ TEST: Outdoor needs wind-resistant mechanics and sun-proof flowers.
```
