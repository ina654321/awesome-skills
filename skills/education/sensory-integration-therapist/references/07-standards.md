## 7.2 Assessment Standards

| Assessment Tool | Age Range | Admin Time | Constructs Measured |
|-----------------|:---------:|:----------:|---------------------|
| Sensory Profile 2 (SP-2) | Birth-65+ | 20-30 min | Sensory processing patterns |
| Sensory Processing Measure (SPM) | 5-12 | 15-20 min | School environment sensory functions |
| Infant Toddler Sensory Profile (ITSP) | 0-6 months | 15-20 min | Early sensory processing |
| DeGangi-Berk Test of Sensory Integration | 3-5 | 20-30 min | Vestibular, proprioceptive, tactile |
| SIPT (full battery) | 4-11 | 2+ hours | 17 subtests for SI certification |

## 7.3 Ayres Sensory Integration Fidelity Criteria

1. Physical setting provides controlled sensory environment (swing, brush, tactile materials)
2. Therapy includes active therapeutic movement (proprioceptive and vestibular input)
3. Therapy provides tactile, proprioceptive, and vestibular stimulation in combination
4. Sessions are play-based and child-directed with therapist-facilitated challenges
5. Therapist ensures adequate arousal regulation throughout session
6. Therapy is provided 2-3 times per week for minimum 16-20 weeks
7. Therapist maintainsASI certification (through USC or other accredited program)

## 7.4 Evidence Quality

- Ayres Sensory Integration: Strong evidence (multiple RCTs) for autism, ADHD, developmental coordination disorder
- Wilbarger Protocol: Limited evidence; use with caution; not as standalone treatment
- Sensory diets: Moderate evidence; outcomes vary significantly by implementation fidelity
