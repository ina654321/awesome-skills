# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Sensory Integration Evaluation Workflow

```
Initial Interview (60-90 min)
├── Parent/caregiver interview: developmental history, concerns, sensory patterns at home
├── Review prior assessments, medical history, school reports
├── Establish therapy goals with family (SMART goals)
└── Screen for comorbidities (ASD, ADHD, DCD, anxiety)

Standardized Assessment (2-3 sessions)
├── Sensory Profile 2 or Sensory Processing Measure
├── Clinical observation of sensory reactivity (tactile, auditory, visual, olfactory, gustatory)
├── Postural-ocular-respiratory assessment
├── Bilateral motor coordination (BIDE subtest)
├── Praxis assessment (imitation of postures, bilateral integration)
└── Sedation-free vestibular testing (rotations, linear movement response)

Integration and Interpretation
├── Score all standardized measures
├── Identify patterns: sensory modulation disorder vs. discrimination vs. praxis
├── Determine severity: mild/moderate/severe impact on participation
├── Rule out other conditions (ATAxia, visual impairment, auditory processing)
└── Write comprehensive evaluation report with recommendations

Family Feedback Session
├── Share findings with family in accessible language
├── Prioritize goals together
├── Explain treatment approach and expected timeline
├── Demonstrate 1-2 therapeutic activities
└── Schedule treatment sessions
```

## 8.2 Treatment Session Workflow

### Standard 60-Minute ASI Session
1. **Pre-session parent check-in** (5 min): Brief parent interview on home progress, concerns
2. **Activity setting** (5 min): Arrange equipment; establish child-therapist alliance
3. **Active treatment** (40 min): Child-led play with therapist-facilitated sensory input; therapist grades challenge
4. **Post-session cleanup** (5 min): Child helps clean — proprioceptive input
5. **Parent consultation** (5 min): Teach 1-2 home strategies; answer questions

### Session Progression Framework
- Weeks 1-4: Build trust, assess child's arousal regulation capacity, identify sensory preferences/aversions
- Weeks 5-8: Increase therapeutic challenge; introduce praxis tasks; monitor arousal
- Weeks 9-16: Complex motor planning; social participation in sensory-rich environments
- Weeks 17+: Generalization — school observations, home carryover, discharge planning

## 8.3 Discharge Planning Workflow

1. **Achievement of goals**: All or majority of IEP/goals met
2. **Generalization evidence**: Skills observed in at least 2 settings (clinic, school, home)
3. **Parent confidence**: Caregiver can implement sensory strategies independently
4. **School collaboration**: Teacher can support sensory needs without OT present
5. **Gradual fade**: Reduce frequency to biweekly, then monthly before discharge
6. **Reassessment**: SP-2 or SPM readministered at discharge for outcome documentation
