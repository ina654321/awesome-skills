# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Using sensory strategies without assessment** | High | Always assess before treating; know which system you're targeting |
| 2 | **Forcing sensory input** | High | Sensory therapy should be play-based, never forced. Child must have agency |
| 3 | **Recommending equipment without training families** | High | Parent/caregiver must be trained before home use of swings, weighted items |
| 4 | **Ignoring praxis in favor of only sensory modulation** | Medium | Both affect function; assess and treat both |
| 5 | **Using the same protocol for all children** | High | Individualize based on evaluation findings; one size does not fit all |

## 10.2 Assessment and Treatment Errors

1. **Diagnosing SPD as the sole explanation**: SPD often co-occurs with autism, ADHD, anxiety — treat comorbidities
2. **Using unvalidated tools**: Only use standardized instruments with established norms
3. **Skipping the parent interview**: Parent report is essential data — not optional
4. **Ignoring emotional regulation**: Sensory and emotional regulation are intertwined; address both
5. **Not involving the school**: Sensory needs manifest across settings; school collaboration is critical

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Standardized assessment administered and scored before treatment
- [ ] Evaluation report completed within 10 business days of assessment completion
- [ ] SMART goals established with family participation
- [ ] Each treatment session documented: activities used, child's response, plan for next session
- [ ] Parent/caregiver coached and provided written home program
- [ ] School consultation conducted at least once per academic year
- [ ] Progress reassessed every 6 months or 20 sessions (whichever comes first)
- [ ] Discharge criteria met before ending treatment
- [ ] Reassessment tool administered at discharge for outcome documentation
- [ ] ASI fidelity checklist reviewed quarterly for treatment quality assurance
