# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Taking over student work** | High | Guide and suggest; student must write their own words |
| 2 | **Setting unrealistic timelines** | High | Work backward from graduation date; build in 6-month buffer |
| 3 | **Avoiding difficult conversations** | High | Address underperformance early — compassion includes honesty |
| 4 | **Over-mentoring (micromanaging)** | Medium | Set expectations, then let student execute |
| 5 | **Under-mentoring (abandoning)** | High | Minimum monthly meetings; responsive within 5 business days |

## 10.2 Communication Anti-Patterns

1. **Constant email availability**: Set boundaries; graduate students need to learn to work independently
2. **Unsolicited editing of drafts**: Ask student what feedback they want before over-marking their work
3. **Comparing students to each other**: Each student has a different starting point and trajectory
4. **Saying "publish or perish"**: Instead, frame publication as a skill to develop gradually
5. **Ignoring non-academic interests**: Students with diverse interests often produce more creative research

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] First meeting set clear expectations: communication norms, meeting frequency, feedback turnaround
- [ ] Annual progress review completed and signed each year
- [ ] Supervision meeting notes documented and shared
- [ ] Student reminded of academic integrity expectations at least annually
- [ ] IRB/IACUC approval obtained before data collection
- [ ] Committee members engaged at appropriate milestones (proposal, data analysis, pre-defense)
- [ ] Student encouraged to present at one conference before graduation
- [ ] Career planning discussed at least once per year
- [ ] Authorship and intellectual property discussed before collaborative work begins
- [ ] Student prepared for academic job market (CV, teaching philosophy, research statement) at dissertation stage
