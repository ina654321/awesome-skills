# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Ethical & Confidentiality Pitfalls

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Disclosing student disclosures to parents without processing with student first | 🔴 High | Always debrief with student before involving parents (except mandatory reporting) |
| 2 | Making college recommendations based on personal bias or relationships | 🔴 High | Use data-driven criteria; document rationale for all recommendations |
| 3 | Overstepping scope of practice (diagnosing mental health conditions) | 🔴 High | Refer to licensed mental health professionals; do not diagnose |
| 4 | Using one-size-fits-all graduation plans for all students | 🟡 Medium | Develop individualized ICAPs with student agency |
| 5 | Failing to document counseling sessions | 🟡 Medium | Maintain contemporaneous case notes |

## 10.2 Academic Advising Anti-Patterns

- **Scheduling students into courses without transcript review** → credit deficiency, delayed graduation
- **Pressuring students toward specific colleges or careers** → violation of student autonomy rights
- **Ignoring cultural factors in career/college discussions** → ineffective guidance for diverse populations
- **Delaying scholarship and financial aid discussions to senior year** → missed opportunities
- **Recommending course levels without considering prerequisite skills** → student failure risk

## 10.3 Communication Anti-Patterns

- Communicating college application outcomes without considering student's emotional readiness
- Sending mass emails about college acceptances (FERPA violation)
- Discussing student cases with colleagues outside the counseling team
- Using acronyms or jargon with students/families unfamiliar with education systems

## 10.4 Best Practice Checklist

- [ ] All student information kept confidential; FERPA training current
- [ ] Individual counseling sessions documented in case notes
- [ ] ICAP updated with student at least once per year
- [ ] 9th-grade transition meetings completed by end of first semester
- [ ] Post-secondary outcome data tracked (college enrollment, military, workforce)
- [ ] Parent permission obtained for small-group counseling
- [ ] Mandatory reporting obligations understood and followed
- [ ] Crisis procedures reviewed and accessible
- [ ] Referral resources (community mental health, crisis lines) current
- [ ] ASCA Ethical Standards reviewed annually
