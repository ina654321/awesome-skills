# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Solving all problems yourself** | High | Develop leadership capacity in others; distributed leadership is more sustainable |
| 2 | **Avoiding difficult conversations** | High | Address underperformance early — a 5-minute conversation now prevents a 5-hour crisis later |
| 3 | **Making decisions without data** | High | Use achievement data, climate surveys, and observational data — not instinct alone |
| 4 | **Treating compliance as optional** | High | IDEA, FERPA, Title IX — ignorance is not a defense |
| 5 | **Managing up vs. leading down** | Medium | Your primary obligation is to students and teachers, not to looking good to supervisors |

## 10.2 Leadership Practice Errors

1. **Micromanaging instruction**: "Walkthroughs" that feel punitive will shut down teacher risk-taking
2. **Implementing reform without professional development**: New initiatives without training fail and breed resentment
3. **Ignoring the school culture**: Data shows that school culture is a stronger predictor of student achievement than any specific program
4. **Equity as a slogan**: All decisions — from hiring to discipline to resource allocation — should be filtered through an equity lens
5. **Celebrating everything equally**: High expectations require acknowledging when things go wrong, not just when things go right

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] School Improvement Plan developed with stakeholder input and submitted on time
- [ ] Teacher evaluations completed per state requirements (frequency, documentation)
- [ ] All staff have current contracts, job descriptions, and evaluations on file
- [ ] Budget aligned to school improvement priorities
- [ ] Special education compliance review completed; no uncorrected violations
- [ ] Student discipline data reviewed monthly; disproportionality analyzed
- [ ] Parent engagement events planned and documented
- [ ] Safety drills completed and documented per schedule
- [ ] Professional development calendar aligned to school improvement goals
- [ ] Attendance data reviewed weekly; intervention for chronic absentees
- [ ] Crisis response plan reviewed and updated annually
- [ ] School council/board reports prepared with accurate data
- [ ] Exit interviews conducted with departing staff; themes analyzed
- [ ] Self-assessment conducted against ISLLC or state leadership standards
