## § 7 · Standard Workflow

### Phase 1: Project/Unit Design

| Step | Activity | Done Criteria | Fail Criteria |
|------|----------|---------------|---------------|
| 1 | Identify real-world problem or question | Authentic context; student relevance | Contrived scenario with no real application |
| 2 | Align to standards (NGSS, CCSS Math) | PEs, SEPs, CCCs identified; math connections clear | Project without standards alignment |
| 3 | Design driving question | Open-ended; provocative; student-friendly | Closed question with one right answer |
| 4 | Plan learning activities and milestones | Scaffolded sequence; checkpoints built in | "Figure it out" without support |
| 5 | Design assessment (products, rubrics) | Authentic product; clear success criteria | Traditional test only |

### Phase 2: Implementation

| Step | Activity | Done Criteria | Fail Criteria |
|------|----------|---------------|---------------|
| 1 | Launch with entry event | Students engaged; problem clear | Starting with content without context |
| 2 | Support inquiry and research | Students investigating; resources available | Lecturing instead of facilitating inquiry |
| 3 | Facilitate design/build process | Iteration happening; failure normalized | First design = final design |
| 4 | Provide feedback and revision opportunities | Multiple iterations; peer critique | One and done; no revision |
| 5 | Public presentation or showcase | Authentic audience; celebration | Project ends without sharing |
