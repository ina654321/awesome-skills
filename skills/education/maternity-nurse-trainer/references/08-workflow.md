# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Maternity Nurse Competency Development

```
Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
├── Orientation to maternal-newborn unit
├── BUBBLEHE assessment framework
├── Vital signs and newborn assessment
├── Documentation standards and EMR training
└── Supervised observation of 10+ births (if applicable)

Phase 2: Core Skills (Weeks 5-12)
├── Breastfeeding support and LATCH scoring
├── Postpartum care procedures
├── Newborn feeding (breast and formula)
├── Medication administration
└── Competency checklist signed off by preceptor

Phase 3: Intermediate Practice (Weeks 13-24)
├── Independent care of 2-3 postpartum patients
├── Recognizing and escalating complications
├── Patient education delivery (postpartum care, contraception)
├── Family planning counseling skills
└── Post-partum mood disorder screening (Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale)

Phase 4: Advanced/Precepting (Weeks 25+)
├── Full patient load management
├── Precepting junior staff and students
├── Quality improvement project participation
└── Final competency validation by charge nurse
```

## 8.2 Teaching Session Workflow (for maternity nurse trainees)

### Skills Workshop Structure (90 minutes)
1. **Briefing (10 min)**: Explain the skill, why it matters, and safety considerations
2. **Demonstration (15 min)**: Trainer performs skill with live model or manikin; narrates each step
3. **Guided practice (30 min)**: Trainee performs skill with trainer observing; trainer corrects in real time
4. **Independent practice (20 min)**: Trainee performs skill without assistance; self-checks against checklist
5. **Debrief (15 min)**: What went well? What needs improvement? How does this connect to clinical practice?

### Bedside Teaching Moment
1. **See one**: Trainer demonstrates on a patient (with consent)
2. **Do one**: Trainee attempts with direct supervision
3. **Teach one**: Trainee explains the skill to a junior student — teaching reinforces learning

## 8.3 Competency Assessment Workflow

1. **Direct observation**: Trainer watches trainee perform skill in clinical setting
2. **Checklist completion**: Each skill has a validated checklist with critical steps marked
3. **Clinical scenario questions**: Trainee verbalizes response to "What would you do if..." scenarios
4. **Objective structured clinical examination (OSCE)**: For high-stakes skills (e.g., postpartum hemorrhage management)
5. **Documentation audit**: Review 5 patient charts written by trainee for completeness and accuracy
