# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Skipping newborn assessment at handover** | High | Use structured handoff (SBAR or I-PASS); cross-check at least 2 identifiers |
| 2 | **Assuming mother knows how to breastfeed** | High | Every mother needs hands-on latch support until competent |
| 3 | **Documenting only what's abnormal** | High | Document normal findings — protects against liability |
| 4 | **Delaying escalation for "quick fixes"** | High | Escalate per protocol — don't try >2 interventions before calling |
| 5 | **Ignoring mother's emotional state** | Medium | Assess mood at every encounter; Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale at 2 weeks |

## 10.2 Clinical Safety Errors

1. **Wrong medication dose**: Always double-check maternal medications against order; never assume
2. **Failing to monitor lochia**: Offensive odor or return to bright red bleeding = report immediately
3. **Inadequate pain assessment**: Pain is undertreated in postpartum patients; use numeric scale
4. **Missing a deteriorating patient**: Trust your gut — if something seems wrong, assess and escalate
5. **Privacy violations**: Maternal health information is sensitive; avoid discussing in hallways

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Patient identification verified before any intervention (minimum 2 identifiers)
- [ ] LATCH score documented at every breastfeeding session
- [ ] BUBBLEHE assessment completed and documented per shift
- [ ] Vital signs within acceptable range or escalated appropriately
- [ ] Medication rights checked (5 rights) with barcode verification when available
- [ ] Newborn security protocol followed (ID banding checked against mother's ID)
- [ ] Postpartum depression screening scheduled at appropriate intervals
- [ ] Patient education documented (breastfeeding, danger signs, contraception)
- [ ] Structured handoff completed at every care transition
- [ ] Emergency drills (postpartum hemorrhage, shoulder dystocia) completed annually
