# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Teaching Methodology Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Teaching debit/credit as memorization without conceptual grounding | 🔴 High | Use T-accounts and visual flow diagrams; connect to accounting equation |
| 2 | Moving too fast through the accounting cycle | 🔴 High | Ensure mastery at each step before advancing; diagnostic assessments |
| 3 | Using only textbook problems instead of real-world scenarios | 🔴 High | Integrate actual financial statements from public companies |
| 4 | Neglecting Excel/spreadsheet skills in accounting curriculum | 🟡 Medium | Incorporate spreadsheet assignments from day one |
| 5 | Over-relying on multiple-choice assessments | 🟡 Medium | Add constructed-response problems requiring journal entries and calculations |

## 10.2 Assessment Anti-Patterns

- **Allowing students to pass without completing the full accounting cycle** → gaps in competency
- **Exams that don't reflect real-world accounting tasks** → poor preparation for the profession
- **No formative assessment between major exams** → errors go uncorrected for weeks
- **Curving grades instead of addressing instructional effectiveness** → masks learning problems
- **Not aligning CPA exam prep with course curriculum** → missed exam readiness opportunities

## 10.3 Professional Preparation Anti-Patterns

- Failing to teach ethical standards alongside technical skills
- Not exposing students to accounting software (QuickBooks, SAP, Excel advanced)
- Overlooking soft skills: professional communication, business writing, presentation
- Neglecting tax law updates in the curriculum (tax law changes every year)
- Not connecting accounting concepts to current business news and events

## 10.4 Best Practice Checklist

- [ ] All students can complete the full accounting cycle independently by course end
- [ ] Debit/credit competency assessed before moving to adjusting entries
- [ ] Real-world financial statements (from annual reports) used in at least 50% of examples
- [ ] CPA exam content integrated into upper-division courses
- [ ] Spreadsheet competency assessed each semester
- [ ] Academic integrity policy reviewed and enforced consistently
- [ ] Post-course survey collects student feedback on pacing and clarity
- [ ] Faculty stay current with FASB/IFRS changes through CPE requirements
- [ ] Industry advisory board reviews curriculum annually
- [ ] Capstone project requires students to analyze real company financials
