# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Baking Lesson Planning Workflow

```
Lesson Structure (90–180 min class)
├── Pre-Lab Preparation (Before students arrive)
│   ├── Recipe review and demonstration planning
│   ├── Ingredient mise en place (weighed and prepped)
│   ├── Equipment check and station assignment
│   └── Safety briefing and sanitation review
├── Opening (10 min)
│   ├── Learning objectives for the day (posted visibly)
│   ├── Demo of key technique (2-3 minutes max)
│   └── Sanitation and safety reminders
├── Production Phase (60–120 min)
│   ├── Students work through recipe independently
│   ├── Instructor circulates: observing, correcting, questioning
│   ├── Mid-production check: dough temperature, mixing endpoint
│   └── Continuous sanitation monitoring
├── Baking & Cooling (Variable)
│   ├── Load oven with proper spacing; note time
│   ├── Monitor baking; rotate pans at halfway point
│   └── Cool completely before finishing or tasting
└── Closure (15 min)
    ├── Product evaluation: appearance, texture, taste
    ├── Self-assessment against rubric
    ├── Cleanup and station inspection
    └── Assignment of next session's prep homework
```

## 8.2 Skills Progression Workflow

1. **Week 1-4**: Mise en place, knife skills, measuring techniques, basic doughs (biscuits, quick breads)
2. **Week 5-8**: Laminated doughs (puff pastry, croissants), fermentation science, shaping
3. **Week 9-12**: Yeast breads (lean doughs, enriched doughs), sourdough basics, scoring techniques
4. **Week 13-16**: Laminated pastries, choux pastry, piped decorations
5. **Week 17-20**: Advanced cakes and tortes, buttercream variations, assembly
6. **Week 21-24**: Chocolate work (tempering, molding, ganache), sugar work basics
7. **Week 25-28**: Restaurant-style plated desserts, menu development
8. **Week 29-32**: Final practical exam preparation; certification review

## 8.3 Practical Exam Workflow

1. Pre-exam: Student draws assigned item from exam basket
2. 5-minute read: Student reviews recipe, identifies ingredients, plans timing
3. Production: Timed practical (typically 2-4 hours depending on complexity)
4. Instructor observation: Notes technique errors, timing management, sanitation
5. Finished product evaluation: Taste, texture, appearance, portioning, plating
6. Oral exam: Student explains technique, identifies potential errors, discusses alternatives
7. Sanitation inspection: Station breakdown and sanitation compliance

## 8.4 Sanitation & Safety Workflow

1. **Pre-class**: Sanitize surfaces; verify handwashing stations stocked; check refrigeration temps
2. **During production**: Handwashing every cross-contamination event; color-coded cutting boards; glove use
3. **Temperature monitoring**: Document food temperatures every 30 minutes during production
4. **Post-production**: Proper labeling and dating of leftover products; storage at correct temperatures
5. **Cleanup**: Three-compartment sink procedure; sanitize surfaces; equipment storage
