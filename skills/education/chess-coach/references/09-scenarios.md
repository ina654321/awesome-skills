# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Disciplinary Issue

**User:** "During a tournament, one student accuses another of cheating after losing. There's no evidence, but emotions are high. How do you handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Immediate Response:**
> ```
> 1. Separate the students
> 2. Speak with each privately
> 3. Maintain calm demeanor
> 4. Focus on sportsmanship
> ```
>
> **Conversation with Accuser:**
> ```
> "I understand you're upset about losing. It can feel unfair when we lose.
> What specifically made you think they were cheating?"
>
> If no evidence: "I understand it felt unfair, but we need to trust that
> everyone plays honestly. What can we learn from this game?"
>
> If evidence exists: "Thank you for telling me. Let's look at the game together."
> ```
>
> **Conversation with Accused:**
> ```
> "I wanted to talk with you about your game. The other player felt
> something unusual happened. Can you explain your thinking on some moves?"
>
> If innocent: "Thank you for being patient. Good games are about
> playing honestly. I'm glad you're here."
>
> If guilty: Appropriate consequences per tournament rules.
> ```
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Review rules and ethics before tournaments
> - Emphasize growth mindset
> - Model good sportsmanship
> - Use anti-cheating measures if needed

## 9.2 Struggling Student

**User:** "A student has been taking lessons for 6 months but is still making the same beginner mistakes - hanging pieces, not looking at opponent's replies. Rating is stuck at 800. How do you help them?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Diagnostic Assessment:**
> ```
> Common Beginner Mistakes:
> ├── Hanging pieces (one-move threats)
> ├── Ignoring opponent's last move
> ├── No developing plan
> ├── Panic moves under time pressure
> └── Poor king safety
> ```
>
> **Intervention Plan:**
> ```
> 1. Stop and Check Technique:
>    - Before moving, ask: "Can my opponent take my piece?"
>    - After opponent's move, ask: "What did they threaten?"
>
> 2. Piece Protection Drills:
>    - Find moves that attack and defend
>    - Calculate exchanges
>
> 3. One-Move Threat Puzzles:
>    - Focus on tactical awareness
>    - "Find all checks, captures, and threats"
>
> 4. Slower Time Controls:
>    - Play 15|10 instead of blitz
>    - Time pressure is masking skill gaps
>
> 5. Game Review:
>    - Analyze own games
>    - Find where pieces were lost
>    - Create checklist for future games
> ```
>
> **Parent Communication:**
> - Explain that 6 months is early
> - Rating plateau is normal
> - Focus on habits, not rating
> - Practice 20 min daily on tactics
