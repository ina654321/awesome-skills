# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Strategic Planning Workflow

```
Phase 1: Environmental Scan (Year 1 of 5-year cycle)
├── Demographic analysis: enrollment projections, regional high school graduation trends
├── Competitive analysis: peer institution strategies, market positioning
├── Internal assessment: SWOT analysis by division
├── Student success data review: retention, completion, time-to-degree
├── Financial sustainability assessment: tuition dependency, endowment, grant revenue
└── External stakeholder input: alumni, employers, board of trustees

Phase 2: Goal Setting (Year 1 continued)
├── President and cabinet alignment on 3-5 strategic priorities
├── Board of trustees approval of strategic framework
├── Provost-academic senate collaboration on academic priorities
└── Institutional research validates feasibility of goals

Phase 3: Operational Planning (Year 2)
├── Division-level operational plans aligned to strategic goals
├── Budget allocation tied to strategic priorities
├── Key performance indicators (KPIs) established per goal
├── Dashboard created for ongoing monitoring
└── Communication plan: town halls, web updates, annual reports

Phase 4: Implementation and Monitoring (Years 2-5)
├── Quarterly progress review with cabinet
├── Annual report to board and accreditation bodies
├── Mid-cycle review and adjustment if needed
└── Environmental scan refresh every 2 years

Phase 5: Comprehensive Review (Year 5)
├── Full strategic plan evaluation
├── New plan development begins
└── Lessons learned incorporated into next cycle
```

## 8.2 Budget Development Workflow

1. **Baseline preparation** (October): Finance office provides current-year budget and projected enrollments
2. **Enrollment projection** (October-November): Institutional research projects enrollment by college/program
3. **Revenue modeling** (November): Tuition revenue, state appropriation, grant projections
4. **Resource requests** (November): Divisions submit new funding requests with strategic justification
5. **Budget committee review** (December): Prioritize requests; balance competing needs
6. **President/cabinet decision** (January): Final budget proposal
7. **Board approval** (February/March): Board of trustees adopts final budget
8. **Communication** (April): Deans and division heads briefed on final allocations
9. **Implementation** (July 1): New fiscal year begins with approved budget

## 8.3 Accreditation Workflow

1. **Self-study preparation** (12-18 months before visit): Institutional self-study against accreditation standards
2. **Data compilation**: Achievement data, governance documentation, faculty qualifications, financial stability evidence
3. **Draft review**: Campus-wide review of self-study draft
4. **Peer review team nomination**: Commission assigns team
5. **Site visit**: 3-5 day visit by peer reviewers
6. **Team report**: Reviewers submit findings to accrediting body
7. **Institution response**: Institution may respond to team report
8. **Commission action**: Accreditation status granted, reaffirmed, or sanctions issued
9. **Follow-up reporting**: Mid-cycle reports and focused visiting teams as required
