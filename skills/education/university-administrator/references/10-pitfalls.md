# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Strategic planning without faculty input** | High | Academic mission must be faculty-driven; admin enables, not dictates |
| 2 | **Reactive rather than proactive communication** | High | Anticipate issues; brief stakeholders before crises become public |
| 3 | **Avoiding shared governance processes** | High | Cutting corners on shared governance creates long-term institutional damage |
| 4 | **Making budget cuts without strategic review** | High | Cut what doesn't align with mission — not what's politically easiest |
| 5 | **Viewing compliance as optional** | High | FERPA, Title IX, ADA, Clery Act — violations carry legal and financial consequences |

## 10.2 Institutional Leadership Errors

1. **Collegiality over accountability**: Protecting poor performers to maintain "peace" serves no one
2. **Politicizing shared governance**: All stakeholders should feel heard — but decisions ultimately rest on evidence and mission
3. **Data avoidance**: Using data selectively to support predetermined conclusions undermines institutional trust
4. **Deferred maintenance metaphor**: Neglecting infrastructure (physical, academic, human) until crisis costs more than prevention
5. **Siloed decision-making**: Major decisions have cross-divisional implications — coordinate before announcing

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Strategic plan developed with broad stakeholder input; approved by board
- [ ] Annual operating budget aligned to strategic priorities; board approved
- [ ] Enrollment projections reviewed quarterly; contingency plans in place for variance
- [ ] Retention and completion data reviewed monthly; interventions funded
- [ ] Accreditation self-study on schedule; no uncorrected compliance findings
- [ ] Title IX coordinator designated; annual training completed by all staff
- [ ] Financial aid packaging meets federal requirements; no material errors in verification
- [ ] Student conduct processes follow institutional policy and due process
- [ ] Annual campus safety and security report (Clery Act) filed accurately
- [ ] ADA compliance: facilities and programs accessible; accommodations documented
- [ ] Collective bargaining agreements followed; labor relations monitored
- [ ] Board reports prepared with accurate and complete data
- [ ] Institutional effectiveness reviews completed per accreditation cycle
- [ ] Emergency response plan tested annually; crisis communication plan current
- [ ] Presidential evaluation completed by board; feedback shared constructively
