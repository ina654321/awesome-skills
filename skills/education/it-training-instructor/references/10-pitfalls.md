# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Reading slides verbatim** | High | Use slides for visuals only; speak conversationally |
| 2 | **No hands-on practice** | High | Minimum 50% of training time must be hands-on for IT skills |
| 3 | **Using outdated software versions** | High | Match production environment versions; state version in materials |
| 4 | **Teaching to the test** | Medium | Focus on understanding, not just passing the certification exam |
| 5 | **No post-training follow-up** | High | Schedule 30-day follow-up; track on-the-job application |

## 10.2 Technical Teaching Mistakes

1. **Skipping fundamentals**: Trainees who don't understand why a process works will forget it quickly
2. **No real-world context**: Every concept should be connected to the trainee's actual job tasks
3. **One-size-fits-all**: Beginners and experienced users need different tracks
4. **Lab environment too different from production**: Trainees will say "it worked in training but not in my real environment"
5. **Ignoring keyboard shortcuts**: Power users who know shortcuts adopt skills 30% faster

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Pre-assessment administered to all trainees before training
- [ ] Training objectives aligned to job tasks (task analysis completed)
- [ ] Hands-on lab exercises match at least 80% of production environment
- [ ] Post-training assessment administered within 1 week
- [ ] 30-day follow-up scheduled to check skill transfer
- [ ] Training materials version-controlled and updated at least annually
- [ ] Trainee feedback collected (end-of-training survey + 90-day pulse)
- [ ] Training environment uptime monitored — below 95% uptime is unacceptable
- [ ] Accessibility reviewed for all e-learning content (captions, alt text, keyboard nav)
- [ ] Instructor CPE/continuing education verified annually
