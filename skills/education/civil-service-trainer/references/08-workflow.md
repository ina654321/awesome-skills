## 8. Standard Workflow

### 8.1 Comprehensive Exam Preparation

```
Phase 1: Assessment & Strategy (Weeks 1-2)
├── Take diagnostic test: identify current level across all sections
├── Analyze exam pattern: section weights, question types, time limits
├── Set realistic target: based on historical cut-offs and own baseline
├── Create study plan: allocate time based on priority matrix
└── [✓ Done]: Assessment complete with 12-week preparation plan
    [✗ FAIL]: No baseline assessment → generic plan may misallocate time

Phase 2: Content Foundation (Weeks 3-8)
├── Cover high-frequency topics first (60% of questions from 30% of syllabus)
├── Build problem-solving frameworks (not just solutions)
├── Practice with previous years' papers (last 5 years minimum)
├── Identify and drill weak areas
└── [✓ Done]: Complete syllabus coverage with >75% accuracy target
    [✗ FAIL]: Gaps in any high-weight section → predictably lower score

Phase 3: Speed & Strategy (Weeks 9-11)
├── Timed practice: build test stamina
├── Section-specific strategies: optimize time allocation
├── Mock tests: simulate exam conditions
├── Error analysis: fix systematic mistakes
└── [✓ Done]: Achieve target accuracy at target speed in mock tests
    [✗ FAIL]: Speed below target → will underperform in actual exam

Phase 4: Revision & Exam Day (Week 12)
├── Light revision: focus on weak areas
├── Exam day strategy: last-minute checklist
├── Mental preparation: stress management techniques
└── [✓ Done]: Ready for exam with confidence and clear strategy
```

### 8.2 Interview Preparation

```
Step 1: Position Analysis
  → Research the position: job description, required competencies
  → Identify key skills and experiences needed
  → Understand the selection criteria

Step 2: Content Development
  → List 10-15 key experiences that demonstrate competencies
  → Structure each using STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result
  → Prepare 3-5 stories that can be adapted to multiple questions

Step 3: Mock Interview Practice
  → Practice out loud: record and review
  → Get feedback: use peer practice or coaching
  → Refine: adjust tone, pacing, body language

Step 4: Scenario Preparation
  → Prepare for common scenarios: conflict, leadership, ethical dilemmas
  → Develop decision framework for unexpected questions
  → Prepare questions to ask interviewer (shows engagement)

[✓ Done]: Can articulate 5+ competency stories with confidence
```
