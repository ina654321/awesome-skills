## 10. Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

### 🔴 High Severity

**Anti-Pattern 1: Neglecting Time Management Practice**

```markdown
❌ BAD: Studying content for 3 hours daily but never timing practice.
In the exam, realize too late that you spent 20 minutes on 5 difficult questions,
leaving 30 easy questions unattempted.

✅ GOOD: Every practice session is timed. Build internal clock for question pacing.
At 90 seconds without solution, move on. Track timing in mock tests.
```

**Anti-Pattern 2: Guessing Without Strategy**

```markdown
❌ BAD: "I'll just attempt all questions, guessing the ones I don't know."
With -0.25 negative marking, random guessing actually lowers your score.
Expected value of random guess: -0.0625 per question.

✅ GOOD: Eliminate at least 2 wrong options, then guess (positive expected value).
Skip questions where you can't eliminate any options. Strategic guessing only.
```

**Anti-Pattern 3: Cramming at the End**

```markdown
❌ BAD: "I have 3 months, I'll start serious preparation in the last month."
Foundation building takes time. Last-month cramming increases anxiety,
not competency. Scores plateau in the last 2 weeks.

✅ GOOD: Front-load content coverage. Last month is for speed building,
mock tests, and light revision. Quality sleep is more valuable than
last-minute cramming.
```

### 🟡 Medium Severity

**Anti-Pattern 4: Ignoring Negative Marking**

```markdown
❌ BAD: "I'll just mark all answers, at least I attempted everything."
Without negative marking strategy, this approach reduces scores by 10-15%.
Random guessing on 50 questions could cost 15+ marks.

✅ GOOD: Track attempted questions vs. accuracy rate. Adjust strategy
based on negative marking impact. In difficult sections, skip more.
```

**Anti-Pattern 5: Over-Studying Easy Topics**

```markdown
❌ BAD: Spending equal time on all topics because "everything is important."
20% of topics generate 60% of questions. Over-studying easy topics
means under-studying high-weight topics.

✅ GOOD: Use topic priority matrix. Allocate time by frequency, not comfort.
```
