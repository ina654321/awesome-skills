# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|---------------|:---:|-----------|
| 1 | **Schedule-first advising** | High | Build relationship first; schedule is a tool, not the purpose |
| 2 | **Not documenting interactions** | High | Always log advising notes; absence of documentation = absence of accountability |
| 3 | **Assuming all students know how college works** | High | First-gen, transfer, and international students may need explicit system navigation |
| 4 | **Over-enrolling struggling students** | High | 12 credits is full-time; 15 is not required every semester — sometimes 12 is the right load |
| 5 | **Advising students toward YOUR preferred major** | Medium | Student-centered advising means the student's goals drive decisions |

## 10.2 Advising Communication Errors

1. **Using unexplained acronyms**: Always spell out abbreviations (SAP, GPA, ESL) for first-time students
2. **Emailing too formally**: Students ignore emails that sound like legal documents; be warm and clear
3. **Missing office hours**: Post them widely; respond within 48 hours
4. **Dismissing student concerns**: "That's not a big deal" shuts students down — validate first, then reframe
5. **Disclosing confidential information**: You know things about students; keep them confidential

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Degree audit reviewed before every advising session
- [ ] Student ID verified at start of every session
- [ ] Academic Standing status confirmed (good standing vs. warning vs. probation)
- [ ] Degree plan reviewed and updated at least once per year
- [ ] Mid-term grade alerts submitted for all at-risk students
- [ ] Advising notes documented in SIS within 24 hours
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled for all students with academic concerns
- [ ] Campus resource referrals made appropriately (tutoring, counseling, financial aid)
- [ ] Transfer credit evaluated against degree plan for incoming transfers
- [ ] Graduation audit submitted 30 days before final semester
- [ ] NACADA professional development completed every 3 years
- [ ] Student course evaluations collected and reflected upon
- [ ] Disclosed accommodations verified and implemented per DSS documentation
