# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Initial Family Contact

### Step 1: Intake Call
- Express condolences immediately
- Gather basic information: pet name, species, breed, age
- Determine current situation: at home, at vet, need transport
- Identify family needs: cremation, burial, memorial

### Step 2: Needs Assessment
- Ask about wishes: service, viewing, urn selection
- Inquire about special requests: toys, blanket, items to include
- Determine timeline: urgency, scheduled service
- Note any cultural or religious considerations

### Step 3: Arrangements
- Explain service options clearly and compassionately
- Provide pricing transparently
- Schedule transport or drop-off appointment
- Confirm next steps and timeline for ashes/return

## 8.2 Remains Transfer

### Transport Protocol
- Arrive on time or communicate any delays
- Handle with dignity: speak softly, move carefully
- Provide comfort items to family if present
- Document transfer: photos if family wishes

### At Facility
- Secure identification throughout process
- Proper storage per regulations
- Maintain chain of custody documentation
- Prepare for family's planned service

## 8.3 Memorial Services

### Planning
- Work with family on service details
- Recommend timing (typically 3-7 days after passing)
- Suggest officiant or family-led service
- Prepare space: seating, photos, music

### Service Elements
- Memorial folder with pet's photo
- Guest book for memories and condolences
- Viewing time if family requests
- Eulogy or tribute reading
- Candle lighting or balloon release
- Final farewell at cremation/burial site

## 8.4 Return of Ashes

### Preparation
- Verify identification before returning
- Place in selected urn or container
- Include certificate of cremation
- Add memorial items if requested

### Handoff
- Schedule private appointment for return
- Allow ample time for family to grieve
- Share memories of pet if appropriate
- Provide grief support resources
- Follow up in 2-4 weeks with check-in

## 8.5 Follow-Up Care

### Ongoing Support
- Check in with family at appropriate intervals
- Offer grief counseling referrals
- Provide anniversary support (remembrance cards)
- Invite to memorial events or candle-lighting ceremonies
- Share pet loss support resources
