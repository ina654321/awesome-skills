# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 High-Severity Anti-Patterns

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 1: Making Decisions Without Consulting Bride
```
❌ BAD: Deciding to move the ceremony time to avoid rain
✅ GOOD: Present options with pros/cons; let bride/family decide
```
**Why it matters**: This is HER day; you support, not direct.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 2: Taking Family Sides
```
❌ BAD: Agreeing with bride's complaint about her mother-in-law
✅ GOOD: Listen, validate feelings, redirect to solutions
```
**Why it matters**: You're there for the bride, but neutrality preserves relationships.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 3: Drinking Too Much at Events
```
❌ BAD: Having "a few glasses" to celebrate during duties
✅ GOOD: Limit to one drink maximum; stay sharp for emergencies
```
**Why it matters**: You're the sober support; the couple needs you alert.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 4: Sharing Unpublished Details
```
❌ BAD: Posting sneak peeks or details before the couple does
✅ GOOD: Wait for couple's approval on all social media
```
**Why it matters**: Wedding details and photos are theirs to share first.

## 10.2 Medium-Severity Anti-Patterns

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 5: Over-Managing the Wedding Party
```
❌ BAD: Constantly instructing bridesmaids on what to do
✅ GOOD: Coordinate key moments; let people enjoy themselves otherwise
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 6: Neglecting Your Own Attire/Grooming
```
❌ BAD: Arriving disheveled; dress not properly prepared
✅ GOOD: Have your own logistics handled so you're fully present
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 7: Forgetting to Eat/Hydrate
```
❌ BAD: Running on adrenaline all day; getting lightheaded
✅ GOOD: Eat breakfast; keep snacks in emergency kit; hydrate
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 8: Complaining About Workload
```
❌ BAD: "This is so stressful" or "I can't believe how much we have to do"
✅ GOOD: Stay positive; the couple's stress is higher
```

## 10.3 Best Practices Checklist

### Pre-Wedding
- [ ] Clear on your responsibilities and boundaries
- [ ] Emergency kit assembled and accessible
- [ ] All vendors and contacts in your phone
- [ ] Timeline distributed to relevant parties
- [ ] Hotel/travel arranged for yourself

### Wedding Day
- [ ] Arrive early with buffer time
- [ ] Phone on silent (but accessible for emergencies)
- [ ] Stay calm under pressure
- [ ] Keep bride calm and hydrated
- [ ] Be where you need to be when you need to be there
- [ ] Help others enjoy themselves

### Emotional Support
- [ ] Check in on bride regularly
- [ ] Read the room for who's struggling
- [ ] Redirect stress to productive action
- [ ] Be the calm presence when chaos arises

## 10.4 Red Flags to Watch For

| Red Flag | Risk Level | Action |
|----------|------------|--------|
| Bride having panic attack | High | Find quiet space; breathing exercises; stay with her |
| Family members drunk/disruptive | Medium | Coordinate with groom/best man; venue security if needed |
| Vendor no-show | High | Contact immediately; activate backup plan; notify couple |
| Bride or groom second thoughts | High | Take aside; listen; don't minimize; suggest professional support |
