# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Initial Assessment Phase

### Step 1: Client Consultation
- Schedule initial meeting (in-person preferred for estate work)
- Gather basic information: property size, timeline, family members involved
- Identify urgency factors: property sale deadline, lease end, health concerns
- Determine emotional context: recent loss, downsizing, relocation, estate settlement

### Step 2: Property Walkthrough
- Conduct non-committal walkthrough of all spaces
- Identify scope: rooms, closets, attic, basement, garage, storage units
- Note large items: furniture, appliances, vehicles
- Observe general condition and organization level

### Step 3: Family Dynamics Assessment
- Identify all stakeholders and their roles (executor, beneficiaries, family members)
- Determine decision-making structure
- Note any existing tensions or disagreements
- Establish communication preferences (single point of contact vs. group)

## 8.2 Planning Phase

### Step 4: Develop Sorting Framework
- Choose categorization method based on estate type:
  - **Bereavement**: Sort by relationship to deceased, then by item type
  - **Downsizing**: Sort by priority (keep/must-sell/donate/discard)
  - **Estate Settlement**: Sort by value tier and legal requirements
- Create timeline with realistic milestones
- Identify resources needed: truck, storage, appraisal, estate sale

### Step 5: Resource Planning
- Estimate boxes/tape/markers needed (rule of thumb: 1 box per 10 sq ft of living space)
- Arrange temporary storage if needed
- Coordinate with estate sale companies or auctioneers
- Schedule professional appraisals for valuable collections

## 8.3 Execution Phase

### Step 6: Room-by-Room Process
**Phase A: High-Value Areas First**
- Master bedroom, home office, storage with valuables
- Complete before emotional exhaustion sets in

**Phase B: Living and Common Areas**
- Kitchen, living room, dining room
- Often most items; allocate extra time

**Phase C: Personal and Sentimental Spaces**
- Children's rooms, hobby areas
- Most emotionally charged; schedule with support available

**Phase D: Out-of-Sight Areas**
- Attic, basement, garage, shed
- Often forgotten items; can yield surprises

### Step 8.4 Disposition Phase

### Step 7: Distribution
- Family distribution: Create clear system for claiming items
- Document all transfers with signatures
- Use lottery system for contested items if family agrees
- Consider charitable donation for unclaimed items

### Step 8: Disposal Methods
- **Estate Sale**: Best for multiple items of moderate value; hire professional
- **Auction**: Better for single high-value items or collections
- **Consignment**: Quality furniture, designer items
- **Donation**: Goodwill, Salvation Army, local charities (get receipts)
- **Discard**: Responsible disposal; furniture donation pickup available from many charities

## 8.5 Completion Phase

### Step 9: Final Walkthrough
- Check all closets, cabinets, drawers
- Verify all items removed or accounted for
- Clean property if required by sale agreement
- Take final photos for records

### Step 10: Documentation Handoff
- Complete inventory with final disposition
- Provide digital copies to executor/family
- Summarize any unfinished items or decisions needed
- Offer follow-up support for questions
