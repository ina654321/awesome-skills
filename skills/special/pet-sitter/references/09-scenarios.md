# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scenario: New Dog Aggression

**User:** "The dog I've been sitting for bit me unprovoked this morning. There's no blood but it scared me. What should I do?"

**Pet Sitter:**
> **This is a serious situation — let's handle it properly:**
> 
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 1. **Stop interaction immediately** — give the dog space
> 2. **Do not punish** — this will make it worse
> 3. **Secure the dog safely** — in a room or crate if possible
> 4. **Contact the owner right away** — this needs to be disclosed
> 
> **Assess What Happened:**
> - Did you approach while eating or with a treat?
> - Did you corner or corner the dog?
> - Any warning signs (growling, lip lifting) before the bite?
> - Has this happened before? (Ask owner)
> 
> **Document Everything:**
> - Date, time, exact circumstances
> - Location of bite (even minor)
> - Circumstances before the incident
> - Dog's body language before and during
> 
> **Questions to Ask Owner:**
> - Has this happened before? With other sitters?
> - Does the dog have triggers you've noticed?
> - What did the vet say when this happened before?
> 
> **For Future Sits:**
> - Do not approach this dog while eating
> - Let the dog come to you for pets
> - Watch for warning signs: whale eye, stiff body, lip lifting
> - Consider not continuing if no improvement
> 
> **Note:** Bites should be documented; consult local regulations.

## 9.2 Scenario: Medical Emergency

**User:** "The cat I'm watching started having trouble breathing and is making weird noises. The owner is unreachable on their flight. What do I do?"

**Pet Sitter:**
> **This is a medical emergency — act immediately:**
> 
> **Step 1: Assess Quickly**
> - Is the cat actually struggling or just making noises?
> - Can you see the gums? (Blue/pale = emergency)
> - Is the cat conscious and responsive?
> 
> **Step 2: Call Emergency Vet NOW**
> - Don't wait for owner — call emergency vet immediately
> - Explain symptoms and get guidance
> - Be prepared to transport if they advise it
> 
> **Step 3: Contact Backup**
> - Call any emergency contact owner provided
> - Leave messages for owner (they may be in air)
> - Keep detailed notes of all attempts
> 
> **During Transport:**
> - Keep cat calm and quiet
> - Don't let cat overexert
> - Bring medical records if you have them
> 
> **What to Tell Vet:**
> - Full history: breed, age, known conditions
> - Any medications
> - What you've observed
> - When owner returns, they must follow up
> 
> **Authorization:** Hopefully owner left emergency authorization; if not, explain you're pet sitter and describe situation.

## 9.3 Scenario: Pet Won't Eat

**User:** "The dog has barely touched their food for two days. The owner said they usually eat eagerly. Should I be concerned?"

**Pet Sitter:**
> **This warrants monitoring but isn't immediately alarming:**
> 
> **Possible Causes:**
> - Stress from owner's absence (common first 1-2 days)
> - Change in environment or routine
> - Not hungry (overfed by owner before departure?)
> - Sensitive stomach or minor illness
> - Change in food from home
> 
> **What to Do:**
> 1. **Check temperature** — fever indicates illness
> 2. **Monitor water intake** — dehydration is more concerning than not eating
> 3. **Check for other symptoms** — vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy?
> 4. **Try warming food slightly** — enhances aroma
> 5. **Hand feeding** — sometimes helps build trust
> 
> **Timeline:**
> - Day 1-2: Monitor closely, try different approaches
> - Day 3: Contact vet for guidance
> - Any vomiting/lethargy: Contact vet immediately
> 
> **Contact Owner:**
> - Let them know what's happening
> - Ask if any recent changes (new food, stress)
> - Get vet contact authorized if not done
> 
> **Red Flags:**
> - Not eating + not drinking = emergency
> - Vomiting or diarrhea
> - Lethargy
> - Weight loss visible
