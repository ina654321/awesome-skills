### First Aid Priorities
1. **A**irway: Is breathing passage clear?
2. **B**reathing: Is pet breathing normally?
3. **C**irculation: Pink gums, capillary refill <2 seconds
4. **D**isability: Alert, responsive, moving?
5. **E**xposure: Control bleeding, prevent shock

## 7.2 Species-Specific Care

### Dogs
| Need | Standard |
|------|----------|
| **Walking** | 30-60 min daily minimum |
| **Water** | Fresh daily, 1oz per pound body weight |
| **Feeding** | Twice daily typical; follow owner's schedule |
| **Alone time** | Max 6-8 hours typical |

### Cats
| Need | Standard |
|------|----------|
| **Litter box** | 1 per cat + 1 extra; clean daily |
| **Water** | Multiple sources; fountain preferred |
| **Feeding** | 2 meals daily typical |
| **Play** | 15-20 min interactive daily |

### Small Mammals (Rabbits, Guinea Pigs, Hamsters)
| Need | Standard |
|------|----------|
| **Housing** | Minimum cage size per species |
| **Diet** | Hay daily, fresh vegetables, limited pellets |
| **Exercise** | Supervised playtime outside cage daily |

## 7.4 Health Monitoring Standards

### Daily Health Checks
- [ ] Appetite and water intake normal
- [ ] Urination/defecation normal
- [ ] Energy level typical for pet
- [ ] Eyes clear, alert, no discharge
- [ ] Nose cool and moist (dogs/cats)
- [ ] Coat condition normal
- [ ] No limping or mobility issues
- [ ] Medication given if prescribed

### Warning Signs (Contact Vet Immediately)
- Vomiting or diarrhea >24 hours
- Lethargy or weakness
- Difficulty breathing
- Not eating/drinking >24 hours
- Collapse or inability to stand
- Seizures
- Bleeding that won't stop
- Signs of pain (whimpering, hiding)
