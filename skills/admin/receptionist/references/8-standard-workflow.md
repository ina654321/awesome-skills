## § 8 · Standard Workflow

### 8.1 Managing a Walk-In Visitor

```
Phase 1: Warm Welcome
├── Stand and make eye contact (shows respect)
├── Smile and greet: "Good morning/afternoon, welcome to [Company]"
├── Ask: "How may I help you today?"
└── Note: If on phone, acknowledge the walk-in with a nod

Phase 2: Needs Assessment
├── Request name and company (if not provided)
├── Ask who they're meeting
├── Verify appointment if applicable
└── Determine if they need immediate assistance or need to wait

Phase 3: Action
├── Contact the host: "[Name], you have a visitor: [Visitor Name] from [Company]"
├── If host available: Direct or escort to location
├── If host unavailable: Offer waiting area, refreshments, estimated wait time
└── If no appointment: Confirm if they should wait, offer to call for guidance

Phase 4: Departure
├── Provide directions if self-navigating
├── Set expectations for host's arrival time
├── Thank them for visiting
└── Note in system if needed
```

### 8.2 Handling an Angry or Difficult Caller

```
Step 1: Stay Calm
├── Don't take it personally — they're upset at the situation
├── Take a breath before responding
├── Keep your voice steady and professional

Step 2: Acknowledge and Validate
├── "I understand this is frustrating, and I want to help resolve this"
├── Let them finish without interrupting
├── Paraphrase to show you understand: "So if I understand correctly..."

Step 3: Take Ownership
├── "Let me see what I can do" (not "that's not my department")
├── If you can't help, find someone who can
├── Don't transfer unless necessary — it frustrates callers more

Step 4: Resolve or Escalate
├── Offer solutions within your authority
├── If you need to escalate, explain why and get their permission
├── If escalating, warm transfer with full context (not "here's your call")

Step 5: Close Positively
├── Confirm what action will be taken
├── Give timeline for resolution
├── Thank them for their patience
```
