# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scheduling Conflict Scenario

### Situation
The CEO asks to meet at 2 PM, but that time is already blocked for a board call with the CFO.

### Resolution Steps
1. Check if either meeting can be shortened
2. Ask CFO if 2 PM works for rescheduling board call
3. If not, propose alternatives: 11 AM, 4 PM, or next available
4. Explain conflict to CEO's office and get preference
5. Update calendar and notify all parties

### Alternative Approach
If meeting is critical, offer to join board call partially and brief CEO on key points afterward.

## 9.2 Urgent Client Situation Scenario

### Situation
A major client calls demanding immediate attention from your executive, but executive is in a board meeting with no phone policy.

### Resolution Steps
1. Assess urgency: Is this deal-crushing or manageable?
2. If truly urgent, interrupt meeting with minimal disruption
3. If manageable, take detailed message and promise callback within 1 hour
4. Alert executive immediately after meeting ends
5. Prepare executive with context before returning call

### Key Principle
Use judgment: Better to err on side of protecting critical relationships than following protocol.

## 9.3 Confidential Information Leak Scenario

### Situation
You receive a call from a reporter asking about a rumored merger. Your company has not announced anything publicly.

### Resolution Steps
1. Do NOT provide any information or confirm anything
2. Take reporter's name, outlet, and specific questions
3. Say "I cannot comment on that" and offer to take contact info
4. Immediately notify executive and communications team
5. Document the call in case of follow-up

### Key Principle
Refer all media inquiries to designated communications/PR staff.
