# Common Pitfalls

## 10.1 Process Pitfalls

### Purchasing Without Approved PO
**Risk**: Unauthorized spending, audit findings, personal liability
**Solution**: Always use formal PO process, no verbal commitments

### Splitting Purchases to Bypass Approval
**Risk**: Policy violation, fraud perception, termination
**Solution**: Aggregate requirements, follow true needs

### Using Non-Approved Vendors
**Risk**: Quality issues, no contract protection, payment delays
**Solution**: Order only from approved vendor list

## 10.2 Documentation Pitfalls

### Incomplete Purchase Documentation
**Risk**: Cannot audit, cannot deduct expenses
**Solution**: Maintain complete file (requisition, PO, receipt, invoice)

### Missing Competitive Bids
**Risk**: Audit findings, perceived favoritism
**Solution**: Document sourcing for all purchases >$10,000

### Not Matching Documents
**Risk**: Payment delays, duplicate payments
**Solution**: Three-way match before payment: PO = Receipt = Invoice

## 10.3 Relationship Pitfalls

### Accepting Personal Gifts from Vendors
**Risk**: Conflict of interest, policy violation, termination
**Solution**: Decline gifts over $50, report any offers to manager

### Sharing Pricing with Other Vendors
**Risk**: Anti-competitive behavior, legal issues
**Solution**: Keep competitive information confidential

### Verbal Commitments Without Documentation
**Risk**: Disputes, no audit trail, personal liability
**Solution**: Confirm all agreements in writing, email is sufficient

## 10.4 Strategic Pitfalls

### Not Developing Supplier Relationships
**Risk**: No leverage, reactive only, missed savings
**Solution**: Quarterly business reviews with key suppliers

### Ignoring Contract Expiration
**Risk**: Price increases, missed renegotiation, supply gaps
**Solution**: Calendar contract end dates, start renegotiation 90 days early

### Over-Reliance on Single Supplier
**Risk**: Supply disruption, no leverage, monopoly pricing
**Solution**: Qualify at least 2 suppliers for critical items
