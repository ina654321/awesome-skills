# Jewelry Design — Standards & Reference

## Official Standards & Organizations

### Gemological Standards

- [GIA (Gemological Institute of America)](https://www.gia.edu/) — The leading authority on gemology. Publishes the 4Cs grading system (Cut, Color, Clarity, Carat). All major gem laboratories reference GIA standards.
- [GIA Diamond Grading Reports](https://www.gia.edu/gemlab-reporting-jewelry) — The international standard for diamond certification. Reports include 4Cs, fluorescence, and proportions.
- [AGS (American Gem Society)](https://www.americangemsociety.org/) — Alternative grading laboratory using the AGS 0-10 grading scale (lower = better). Known for cut grade analysis.
- [IGI (International Gemological Institute)](https://www.igi.org/) — Major international gem lab. Widely used for colored stones and lab-grown diamonds.
- [Gübelin Gem Lab](https://www.gubelingemlab.com/) — Swiss gem lab. Publishes detailed reports including country of origin for colored stones.

### Metallurgy Standards

- [Karat Mark System](https://www.fairjewelry.org/) — Gold purity: 24K (99.9%), 18K (75%), 14K (58.3%), 10K (41.7%). Below 10K is not considered fine jewelry in the US.
- **Platinum Standards**: PT950 (95% platinum, 5% ruthenium/iridium) is standard. PT900 is used for pavé settings.
- **Sterling Silver**: 925 (92.5% silver, 7.5% copper). The global standard for silver jewelry.
- [ASTM F2999](https://www.astm.org/) — Standard consumer safety specification for precious metal jewelry.

### Industry Organizations

- [Jewelers of America (JA)](https://www.jewelers.org/) — Largest US trade association. Sets ethical standards and best practices.
- [CIBJO (World Jewellery Confederation)](https://www.cibjo.org/) — International confederation setting standards for gem and jewelry trade.
- [RJC (Responsible Jewellery Council)](https://www.responsiblejewellery.com/) — Certification standard for ethical, social, and environmental responsibility in the jewelry supply chain.
- [DMCC (Dubai Multi Commodities Centre)](https://www.dmcc.ae/) — Major diamond and gemstone trading hub with standards for trade documentation.

## Metal & Material Standards

### Gold Alloys

| Karat | Gold Content | Common Colors | Best Use | Notes |
|-------|-------------|--------------|----------|-------|
| 24K | 99.9% | Yellow only | Investment, heritage pieces | Too soft for daily wear |
| 22K | 91.7% | Yellow | High-end jewelry | Slightly soft for settings |
| 18K | 75% | Yellow, white, rose | Fine jewelry, bridal | Industry standard for quality |
| 14K | 58.3% | Yellow, white, rose | Daily wear, bridal | US market standard |
| 10K | 41.7% | Yellow, white, rose | Budget daily wear | Minimum for US "gold" |
| 18K White | 75% + palladium | White | Fine jewelry | Nickel's controversial |

### Platinum Alloys

| Alloy | Composition | Best Use |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| PT950 | 95% Pt + 5% Ru | Fine jewelry, daily wear |
| PT900 | 90% Pt + 10% Pd | Pavé settings, prongs |
| PT950 Co | 95% Pt + 5% Co | Casting, more malleable |

### Prong & Setting Standards

- **Minimum prong thickness**: 1.5mm for center stones >1ct; 1.0mm for <1ct
- **Prong height**: ≥65% of stone height
- **Basket depth**: Minimum 20% deeper than stone girdle
- **Shank thickness**: 1.8-2.2mm for daily wear; comfort fit adds 0.3mm

## Gemstone Grading Standards

### Diamond (4Cs)

**Cut (Most Important):**
- Ideal/Excellent: Maximum brilliance and fire
- Very Good: Excellent light performance
- Good: Standard quality
- Fair/Poor: Noticeable light leakage

**Color (D-Z Scale):**
- D-F: Colorless (premium)
- G-J: Near-colorless (best value)
- K-M: Faint yellow
- N-Z: Noticeable color

**Clarity (FL to I3):**
- FL/IF: Flawless/Internally Flawless (rare, expensive)
- VVS1/VVS2: Very Very Slightly included (very difficult to see under 10x)
- VS1/VS2: Very Slightly included (easy to see under 10x)
- SI1/SI2: Slightly Included (visible under 10x)
- I1/I2/I3: Included (visible to naked eye)

**Carat Weight**: 1 carat = 0.2 grams. Price jumps at 1ct, 1.5ct, 2ct thresholds.

### Colored Gemstone Grading

**Ruby:** Primary: Pigeon Blood (Myanmar) > Vivid Red (Sri Lanka). Heat treatment is common and accepted. Unheated stones command premium.

**Sapphire:** Kashmir (cornflower blue) > Burmese > Ceylon. Heat treatment standard. Padparadscha (pink-orange) is rare.

**Emerald:** Colombian > Zambian > Brazilian. Jardin (inclusions) is characteristic and acceptable. Oil/resin treatment is standard — must be disclosed.

**Colored Stone Enhancement Codes (GIA):**
- N: No treatment
- H: Heat treatment
- E: External treatment
- F: Filling (fracture filling, glass-filled rubies — must disclose)

## Responsible Sourcing Standards

- [Kimberley Process](https://www.kimberleyprocess.com/) — Certification for rough diamonds. Prevents trade in conflict diamonds.
- [System of Warranties (SoW)](https://www.wto.org/) — Post-Kimberley documentation for polished diamond trade.
- [Fairmined Gold](https://fairmined.org/) — Certification for artisanal and small-scale gold mining.
- [Fairtrade Gold](https://www.fairtrade.net/) — Certification ensuring fair prices and safe conditions for miners.
- [The Goldsmiths' Company Assay Office](https://www.theassayoffice.co.uk/) — UK hallmarking authority for precious metals.
- [AGSO (Alliance for Responsible Mining)](https://www.responsiblemines.org/) — Standards for ASM (Artisanal and Small-scale Mining) gold.

## Safety & Regulatory Standards

- [CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission)](https://www.cpsc.gov/) — US safety regulations for jewelry, especially regarding lead and cadmium in children's jewelry.
- [Proposition 65 (California)](https://www.p65warnings.ca.gov/) — Warning requirements for jewelry containing lead, cadmium, or nickel above threshold levels.
- [Nickel Directive (EU)](https://www.ec.europa.eu/) — Limits nickel release from jewelry in EU market.
- [Hallmarking Act (UK)](https://www.assayofficelondon.co.uk/) — Mandatory third-party hallmarking for gold, silver, platinum above certain weights.
- **ASTM F2923** — Standard specification for children's jewelry safety.
