# Jewelry Design — Troubleshooting

## Material Selection Issues

### Gold Color Inconsistency Across Pieces

**Symptom:** Multiple pieces of gold jewelry have different shades of yellow/white/rose.

**Cause:** Different alloy compositions between manufacturers or lots.

**Fix:**
```yaml
Yellow Gold:
  - Different gold content (14K vs 18K = different base color)
  - Different copper/silver ratios affect warmth
  - Solution: Source all pieces from same casting house/lot

White Gold:
  - Rhodium plating thickness varies
  - Base white gold can be slightly different
  - Solution: Specify rhodium re-plating after finishing

Rose Gold:
  - Copper percentage drives color intensity
  - Standard: 18K rose = 75% gold + 22.25% copper + 2.75% silver
  - Variations: 18K rose can range from pale pink to deep rose
```

### Gemstone Color Doesn't Match Sample

**Symptom:** Set gemstone looks different from the sample seen before purchase.

**Fix:**
```yaml
Natural Variation:
  - No two natural gems are identical
  - Always approve final stone before setting
  - Solution: See stones in person, not just photos

Lighting Environment:
  - Gemstone color shifts under different light sources
  - Solution: View in daylight (D65), incandescent, and LED
  - A GIA report confirms consistency grade

Origin Effect:
  - Colombian emerald ≠ Zambian emerald in color
  - Kashmir sapphire ≠ Ceylon sapphire
  - Solution: Specify origin requirements in writing

Lab-Grown vs Natural:
  - Lab-grown stones may have different color perception
  - Solution: Disclose lab-grown clearly — prices differ significantly
```

## Manufacturing Issues

### Casting Porosity / Bubbles

**Symptom:** Finished piece has small holes or rough areas from gas bubbles during casting.

**Fix:**
```yaml
For minor porosity:
  1. Locate void with loupe
  2. Drill small access hole if needed
  3. Fill with matching karat gold solder
  4. Polish and refinish

For significant porosity:
  1. Re-cast may be necessary
  2. Review casting process:
     - Increase vacuum pressure
     - Check metal temperature
     - Use centrifugal casting instead of steam
     - Verify metal is clean (no recycled metal contamination)

Prevention:
  - Use fresh metal (not overly recycled)
  - Proper burnout cycle for investment
  - Controlled cooling
  - Cast with adequate metal amount
```

### Stone Setting Failures

**Symptom:** Stones are loose, tilted, or have been damaged during setting.

**Fix:**
```yaml
Loose Prongs After Setting:
  1. Retighten prongs with prong pusher
  2. Check prong tips — if worn, re-tip (add gold)
  3. Verify stone security with prong meter

Tilted Stone:
  1. Remove stone carefully
  2. Re-seat in seat or create new seat
  3. Re-set with proper pressure
  4. Check that basket is level

Damaged During Setting:
  - Cracked stone: Replace (cuts by manufacturer warranty)
  - Chipped girdle: Re-cut if minor
  - Scratched facet: Polish if very minor; replace if visible

Prevention:
  - Always use proper setting tools
  - Support stone from below during setting
  - Don't over-tighten prongs
  - Use appropriate pressure for stone hardness
```

### Soldering Problems — Firescale / Pitting

**Symptom:** Dark spots and pitting on silver or gold surface after soldering.

**Fix:**
```yaml
Firescale (Silver):
  1. Causes: Oxidation during heating; copper in alloy reacts
  2. Remove with pickle (sodium bisulfate solution)
  3. For deep firescale: sand and refinish
  4. Prevention: Use firecoat (boric acid + alcohol) before heating

Solder Pitting:
  1. Caused by flux residue or overheating solder
  2. Polish out minor pitting
  3. Re-solder with cleaner joints if severe
  4. Prevention: Use adequate flux; don't overheat

Gold Solder Flow Issues:
  1. Clean surfaces thoroughly before joining
  2. Ensure proper heat application (heat the metal, not the solder)
  3. Use appropriate solder flow temperature
  4. Match solder karat to base metal karat
```

## Quality Control Issues

### Prong Failure in Finished Piece

**Symptom:** Prongs are bent, worn thin, or breaking after short wear.

**Fix:**
```yaml
For Worn Prongs:
  1. Assess remaining metal — measure prong thickness
  2. If <0.5mm remaining, re-tip prongs
  3. Add gold: pre-formed prong tips + laser welding or soldering
  4. Re-finish and polish

For Broken Prongs:
  1. Remove stone (protect with transfer tool)
  2. Repair or replace prong
  3. Re-set stone
  4. Re-finish

Prevention:
  - Specify minimum prong thickness: 1.5mm for >1ct center stone
  - Use quality metal (no recycled gold with impurities)
  - Educate client on care (remove during physical activity)
  - Offer annual inspection service
```

### Rhodium Plating Fading

**Symptom:** White gold turns yellow after a few months.

**Fix:**
```yaml
For Faded Plating:
  1. Strip old rhodium
  2. Re-polish to remove base metal showing through
  3. Re-plate with fresh rhodium
  4. Typical cost: $50-150 per piece

Duration Expectations:
  - Ring with daily wear: 6-12 months
  - Pendant/bracelet: 12-18 months
  - Earrings: 18-24 months (minimal contact)

To Extend Life:
  - Remove jewelry during physical activity
  - Apply perfume/lotion before wearing
  - Store separately to avoid scratching plating
  - Clean with mild soap and water only
```

## Gemstone Issues

### Included Stone Cracking After Setting

**Symptom:** Gemstone with inclusions develops a crack shortly after being set.

**Cause:** Pressure from setting or thermal shock during soldering near stone.

**Fix:**
```yaml
If Stone is Cracked:
  1. Remove stone from setting
  2. Assess if repairable (some cracks can be stabilized)
  3. Most cracked stones must be replaced
  4. Claim with gem dealer if fracture was pre-existing

Prevention:
  - Pre-examine all stones under loupe before setting
  - Identify inclusion locations (stress concentration points)
  - Don't apply heat directly near heat-sensitive stones
  - Diamonds rarely crack; emeralds and opals are most vulnerable
  - Match setting pressure to stone hardness (emerald needs gentle handling)
```

### Diamond Symmetry Issues After Setting

**Symptom:** Set diamond appears off-center or tilted.

**Fix:**
```yaml
For Tilted Stone:
  1. Check if prong pressure is even
  2. Re-level the stone seat
  3. Re-set evenly

For Off-Center Stone:
  1. Remove stone
  2. Adjust basket or prong positions
  3. Re-seat stone centered in setting

For Crown Not Parallel to Table:
  1. Verify stone wasn't cut with crown tilt
  2. If cut issue, cannot fix without re-cutting
  3. If setting issue, re-center and level
```

## Design & Custom Issues

### Ring Too Tight/Loose After Completion

**Symptom:** Client cannot wear ring or ring spins freely.

**Fix:**
```yaml
For Too Tight:
  - Stretching: Up to 1 size (straight gold bands only)
  - Sizing beads: Add on inside (small adjustment)
  - Cutting and adding metal: Add sizing bar with bridge
  - Complete remake: Cut and add/remove metal

For Too Loose:
  - Sizing beads: Add 1-2 on inside (reduce 1/4-1/2 size)
  - Spring insert: Thin comfort insert inside band
  - Complete remake: Cut and add/remove metal

Prevention:
  - Always verify ring size at multiple times of day
  - Measure with client standing (gravity effect)
  - Account for knuckle size (may need to size for knuckle, not base)
```

### Custom Design Doesn't Match Client Expectation

**Symptom:** Finished piece looks different from the CAD rendering or sketch.

**Fix:**
```yaml
Prevention (Best Strategy):
  1. Show CAD render before production
  2. Create wax model for physical preview
  3. Get written approval of design

If Mismatch Occurred:
  1. Assess if rework is possible
  2. If minor: modify and refinish
  3. If major: remake from scratch
  4. Clear communication with client about what went wrong
  5. Typically: maker absorbs cost if design error; client pays if expectation mismatch

CAD-to-Wax Fidelity:
  - Modern wax printing is very accurate
  - Metal casting can introduce ±0.3mm variation
  - Complex geometries may not cast perfectly
  - Build tolerance into design
```
