# Jewelry Design — Glossary

## Metals & Alloys

**Karat (K)** — Measure of gold purity. 24K = pure gold. 18K = 75% gold. 14K = 58.3% gold.

**Karat vs. Carat** — Karat measures gold purity. Carat (ct) measures gemstone weight. Karat = K, Carat = ct.

**Platinum (PT)** — Precious metal (Pt950, PT900). Heavier than gold, hypoallergenic, durable for daily wear.

**Sterling Silver** — 925 sterling = 92.5% silver, 7.5% copper. The standard for silver jewelry.

**Rose Gold** — Gold alloy with higher copper content, producing pink/red color. 18K rose = 75% gold + copper.

**White Gold** — Gold alloyed with white metals (palladium, nickel, manganese). Always rhodium-plated for bright white appearance.

**Palladium** — Platinum group metal. Lighter than platinum, naturally white. Used as alloy and as standalone metal (PD950).

**Vermeil** — Sterling silver with gold plating (minimum 2.5 microns in the US).

**Gold Filled** — Sterling silver with mechanically bonded gold layer (5% gold by weight). Not fine jewelry; costume category.

**Rolled Gold** — Thinner gold layer than gold filled. Not fine jewelry.

## Gemstone Terms

**4Cs** — GIA grading system: Cut, Color, Clarity, Carat weight. The standard for diamond quality assessment.

**Clarity Grade** — Measure of internal inclusions (FL, IF, VVS1-VVS2, VS1-VS2, SI1-SI2, I1-I3).

**Color Grade** — Measure of body color in diamond (D = colorless, Z = light yellow/brown).

**Cut Grade** — Assessment of diamond's light performance based on proportions, symmetry, and polish.

**Fluorescence** — Visible light emitted by diamond when exposed to UV. Can enhance or diminish appearance depending on grade.

**Lab-Grown Diamond (LGD)** — Diamond created in controlled environment. Chemically identical to natural. GIA certified with "Laboratory-Grown" notation.

**Conflict Diamond / Blood Diamond** — Diamond mined in war zones and sold to finance armed conflict. Regulated by Kimberley Process.

**Jardin** — Characteristic inclusions in emeralds (garden-like appearance). Visible inclusions are expected and acceptable.

**Figure / Flame** — Patterns in wood and gemstone that create unique visual effects (birdseye maple, flame in sapphire).

**Chatoyancy** — Cat's eye effect in gemstones. Cabochon-cut gems showing a single bright line.

**Asterism** — Star effect in rubies, sapphires. Convex-cut gems showing multi-rayed star.

**Labradorescence** — Flash of color from within gemstone (labradorite).

## Setting Types

**Prong Setting** — Metal claws hold stone in place. Most common. 4-prong (most secure), 6-prong (maximum sparkle).

**Pavé Setting** — Small stones set close together with minimal metal visible. French pavé has beaded metal; micro-pavé uses tiny stones and laser-set prongs.

**Bezel Setting** — Metal collar surrounds stone's girdle. Most protective setting. Stone appears to float in metal.

**Half-Bezel / Rub-Over** — Metal partially surrounds stone. Shows more of stone while still protecting.

**Channel Setting** — Stones set between two parallel walls with no metal between them. Common for wedding bands.

**Bar Setting** — Similar to channel but individual bars separate each stone.

**Gypsy Setting** — Stone set flush with band surface. Modern and low-profile.

**Tension Setting** — Stone held by pressure from split band. Shows maximum stone.

**Cluster Setting** — Center stone surrounded by smaller stones.

**Halo Setting** — Center stone surrounded by circle of smaller stones.

**Illusion Setting** — Stone set in a metal basket that appears to make it larger.

**Invisible Setting** — Stones set with no visible metal between them. Used for mosaic-like patterns.

## Manufacturing Terms

**Casting** — Creating jewelry by pouring molten metal into a mold. Lost wax casting (cire perdue) is most common.

**Lost Wax / Investment Casting** — Wax model is burned out, leaving cavity for metal. Cast in gold, platinum, silver.

**Forging** — Shaping metal by hammering or pressing. Creates denser, stronger metal.

**Fabrication** — Building piece from scratch with sheet, wire, and hand tools. Most labor-intensive.

**Die Strucking** — Stamping metal under extreme pressure in a die. Produces consistent, dense pieces. Used for wedding bands and chains.

**Electroforming** — Building up metal using electrolytic deposition. Creates lightweight, hollow pieces.

**3D Printing / Additive Manufacturing** — Direct metal printing (DMP). Growing pieces layer by layer from CAD files.

**Wax Model** — Physical model created from wax or printed from CAD. Used to make rubber mold for casting.

**CAD (Computer-Aided Design)** — Digital design software for jewelry (RhinoGold, Matrix, JewelCAD).

**CAM (Computer-Aided Manufacturing)** — CNC milling or printing of wax models and metal.

**Finished Casting** — After casting, piece must be: devested (cleaned), polished, assembled, set, re-polished.

## Quality & Grading

**Hallmark** — Small stamp indicating metal purity. Required in UK, Switzerland, France for precious metals above weight thresholds.

**Assay** — Testing and certifying metal purity. Done by assay offices.

**GIA Report** — Gemological Institute of America certificate. Includes 4Cs grading and diagram of inclusions.

**Laser Inscription** — GIA report number lasered on diamond girdle. Visible under 10x magnification.

**AGS Grade** — American Gem Society 0-10 scale (lower = better). Known for precise cut grading.

**Proportion Scope** — Instrument measuring light performance of diamond based on proportions.

**Inclusion Map** — Diagram showing location and type of inclusions in a gemstone.

## Design & Style

**Bridal Set** — Engagement ring + wedding band designed to fit together.

**Tension Set** — Modern setting where stone appears held by pressure from split band.

**Art Deco** — 1920s-1930s style. Geometric patterns, bold colors, symmetry.

**Art Nouveau** — Late 19th century. Nature-inspired, flowing lines, romantic motifs.

**Edwardian** — Early 20th century. Delicate, feminine. Filigree, pearls, platinum.

**Victorian** — 19th century. Sentimental motifs. Flowers, hearts, serpents, mourning jewelry.

**Mid-Century Modern** — 1950s-60s. Clean lines, yellow gold, abstract forms.

**Contemporary** — Current design era. Eclectic, often mixing metals, textures, and styles.

## Sizing & Fitting

**Ring Size** — Circumference of finger. US sizes 1-17 (quarter sizes available). Measured in mm.

**Sizing** — Adjusting ring to fit. Can be: stretched, cut and added/removed, sizing beads, spring insert.

**Sizing Bar** — Metal bar added inside ring to make slightly larger.

**Sizing Beads** — Small metal balls added to inside of shank to make slightly smaller.

**Comfort Fit** — Rounded inside of band for easier sliding and comfort.

**Standard Fit** — Flat inside of band.

**Knuckle Clearance** — Extra ring size needed to accommodate knuckle during removal.

## Repair & Maintenance

**Re-tipping** — Adding new prong tip when original has worn thin.

**Re-plating** — Re-applying rhodium plating to white gold.

**Re-polishing** — Restoring surface finish. Options: high-polish, satin, matte, sandblast.

**Refinishing** — Complete restoration of surface: polish, rhodium, repair scratches.

**Soldering** — Joining two pieces of metal with lower-melting-point alloy.

**Laser Welding** — Precision joining using concentrated heat. Allows repairs without affecting nearby stones.

**Pickle** — Acid solution (usually sodium bisulfate) used to clean metal after soldering.

**Firescale** — Dark oxidation on silver surface caused by heating.

## Business Terms

**FF&E (Fixtures, Fittings, and Equipment)** — In jewelry: stones, settings, findings.

**Findings** — Pre-made components: clasps, catches, pins, earring wires.

**Bezel Cup** — Pre-made metal tube for setting stones in bezels.

**Mounting** — The metal framework that holds stones. A setting without stones.

**CAD Fee** — Design fee for custom computer-aided design work.

**Setting Fee** — Labor cost for stone setting.

**Mark-up** — Industry standard mark-up on stones: 30-50%. Mark-up on jewelry: 2-3x gold weight + labor.

**Consignment** — Supplier provides stones or jewelry for sale, payment on sale.

**Memo** — Document showing goods on consignment. Not payment until goods are sold.

**Weight Variance** — Acceptable weight difference between design and finished piece (±5% typical).
