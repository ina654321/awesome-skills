# Traditional Instrument Making — Standards & Reference

## Official Documentation & Standards

### Chinese Instrument Standards

- [National Standard GB/T 23519-2009](https://www.gbstandards.org/) — Chinese national standard for traditional musical instruments. Covers guqin, guzheng, pipa, erhu, and other recognized traditional instruments. Defines grading requirements, dimensional tolerances, and material specifications.
- [Zhejiang Conservatory Instrument Research](https://www.zjcm.edu.cn/) — Academic research on Chinese instrument acoustics, materials, and construction. Publishes comparative studies on tonal characteristics by wood species.
- [Shanghai Conservatory Instrument Workshop](https://www.shcmusic.edu.cn/) — One of the premier institutions for traditional instrument making. Research on acoustic properties and restoration methodology.

### International Instrument Standards

- [ISO 18156:2004](https://www.iso.org/standard/35525.html) — International standard for acoustic properties of string instruments. While focused on Western orchestral instruments, the measurement methodologies apply to acoustic testing.
- [UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage Convention](https://ich.unesco.org/) — Traditional instrument making is recognized as intangible cultural heritage in China, Japan, and Korea. UNESCO designations affect export regulations for antique instruments.

## Wood Science & Material Standards

|---------|-------------|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Paulownia** | 泡桐 | Light (0.27-0.31 g/cm³), fast drying, excellent acoustic resonance | Guqin, guzheng, sanxian soundboards | Traditional preference for aged paulownia |
| **Catalpa** | 梓木 | Medium density (0.40-0.50 g/cm³), warm tone, excellent workability | Erhu body, guqin back board | Traditional pairing with paulownia |
| **Phoebe zhennan** | 紫檀 | Dense (0.80-1.00 g/cm³), deep reddish-brown, excellent durability | Pipa, erhu bodies, bridges | CITES Appendix II — restricted |

| **Aged Cedar** | 老杉木 | Old-growth cedar, superior resonance | Soundboards for bowed instruments | Very difficult to source ethically |
| **Walnut** | 胡桃木 | Medium density, warm tone | Bow sticks, fittings | Sustainable alternative to rosewood |

### Wood Drying & Treatment

- **Natural Air Drying**: 1 year per 1" thickness minimum. For soundboards, 3-10 years preferred. Old wood (50+ years) produces superior instruments.
- **Kiln Drying**: Accelerated drying at controlled temperature/humidity. Faster but can stress wood if done too aggressively.
- **Thermal Modification**: Heat treatment (160-230°C) improves dimensional stability and modifies acoustic properties.
- **Moisture Content Standards**: Soundboards: 6-8% MC. Back/sides: 8-12% MC. Fittings: 10-14% MC.
- **Resonance Testing**: Tap testing with piezoelectric transducer or microphone. Frequency response analysis for modal frequencies.

## Acoustic Engineering Standards

### Vibration Analysis

- **Modal Analysis**: Identifying natural frequencies of soundboard and body. Top resonances typically: 200-400 Hz (main), 400-800 Hz (secondary).
- **Radiation Ratio**: Measure of how efficiently soundboard converts vibration to sound. Higher ratio = more efficient.
- **Damping Factor**: Rate at which oscillations decay. Too high = dead tone; too low = harsh/sustaining.
- **Young's Modulus (Elasticity)**: Determines stiffness-to-weight ratio. Higher E/w ratio = better for soundboards.

## Professional Associations

- [China National Orchestra Society](https://www.cnos.org/) — Professional body for traditional music. Organizes instrument quality assessments.
- [Chinese Traditional Instrument Research Association](https://www.ctira.cn/) — Academic society publishing standards and research on traditional instruments.
- [MUSIADES](https://www.musiades.org/) — International association of bowed string instrument makers. Good reference for acoustic principles.
- [Violin Society of America (VSA)](https://www.violinsociety.org/) — Though focused on Western instruments, their acoustic research and grading standards are relevant for understanding instrument quality evaluation.

## Craftsmanship & Quality Standards

### Grading for Professional Instruments

| Grade | Criteria | Price Range |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Student** | New wood, standard construction, basic setup | ¥800-3,000 |
| **Semi-Professional** | Aged wood (3-5 years), better craftsmanship, refined setup | ¥3,000-8,000 |
| **Professional** | Old wood (10+ years), master craftsmanship, custom setup | ¥8,000-30,000 |
| **Concert/Museum** | Antique or master-made, exhibition quality | ¥30,000+ |

### Finish Standards

- **French Polish (Shellac)**: Traditional finish for plucked instruments. 2-3% shellac in denatured alcohol.
- **Oil Varnish**: For bowed instruments. Linseed oil + rosin varnish. Allows more vibration than lacquer.
- **Chinese Lacquer**: Traditional instrument finish. Nitrocellulose or acrylic. Durable but changes tone.
- **Thickness Standards**: Finish should not exceed 0.3mm total on soundboards. Thicker finish dampens vibration.

## Safety Standards

### Hazardous Materials

- **Rosewood (Dalbergia spp.)**: All Dalbergia species are CITES Appendix II (some Appendix I). Export restrictions apply.
- **Wood Dust**: All wood dust is hazardous. Rosewood/ebony are particularly dangerous sensitizers. Always use N95 minimum.
- **Shellac**: Alcohol-based finish. Flammable. Use adequate ventilation.
- **Linseed Oil**: Can spontaneously combust if rags are not properly disposed. Lay flat to dry.

## Historical Reference

### Classical Instrument Texts

- **《齐民要术》(Qimin Yaoshu)** — 6th-century text by Jia Sixie. Contains earliest known written instructions for instrument making.
- **《梦溪笔谈》(Dream Pool Essays)** — 11th-century text by Shen Kuo. Contains detailed descriptions of guqin acoustics and making.
- **《斫琴》(Zhuo Qin)** — Classical treatise on guqin making. Modern edition available from China Conservatory Press.
