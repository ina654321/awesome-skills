# Traditional Instrument Making — Real-World Scenarios

## Scenario 1: Commissioning a Professional-Grade Guqin

**Context:** A guqin student (intermediate level) wants to commission a professional-grade instrument. Budget: ¥25,000.

**Specification:**
```yaml
Wood Selection:
  Soundboard: Aged paulownia, minimum 8 years air-dried
    - Thickness: 1.0-1.2cm at 7th mark position
    - Tap test: Clear, sustained tone at G2 (~196 Hz)
  Back Board: Aged catalpa, 8-10 years dried
  Strings: Silk strings initially (¥800-1,200)

Expected Characteristics:
  - Full, warm tone with good sustain
  - Clear mark positions
  - Responsive to subtle vibrato technique
  - Comfortable string height
```

**Timeline:** 14-18 months total (wood preparation 4-6 months, construction 3-4 months, lacquer 3-6 months, setup 2-4 weeks).

## Scenario 2: Restoring a Qing Dynasty Guqin

**Context:** A collector owns a Qing Dynasty (~1850) guqin with cracks on the back board, missing inlay, and worn varnish. Value: ¥150,000+.

**Assessment:**
```yaml
Condition:
  Soundboard: Intact, minor wear
  Back board: Three longitudinal cracks (stable)
  Lacquer: Worn, thin in some areas
  Inlays: One mark position missing

Conservative Approach:
  1. Clean surface gently (no aggressive cleaning)
  2. Stabilize cracks: Inject thin hide glue
  3. DO NOT remove old varnish (patina is valuable)
  4. Commission new inlay from specialist
  5. Document everything with photos

NOT to do:
  - Strip and refinish (destroys patina)
  - Fill cracks with epoxy
  - Replace soundboard or back board
```

## Scenario 3: Building a Pipa for a Student

**Context:** A 12-year-old student beginning pipa study. Parents want a good-quality student instrument. Budget: ¥2,500.

**Recommendation:**
```yaml
Materials:
  Body: New phoenix wood (3+ years air-dried)
  Neck: White wax wood
  Frets: Brass (durable for beginners)
  Bridge: Rosewood or alternative
  Tuning Pegs: Machine heads (easier for children)

Setup Requirements:
  - String height: Lower than traditional (easier for small hands)
  - Action: Low-medium
  - Weight: Medium-light
```

## Scenario 4: Diagnosing Why an Erhu Has Weak Projection

**Context:** A professional erhu player says their instrument "doesn't carry" in ensemble settings.

**Systematic Diagnosis:**
```yaml
Possible causes:
  1. Soundpost displaced or wrong diameter
  2. Body not properly joined (air leak)
  3. String height too low
  4. Bow is old (horsehair needs replacing)
  5. Rosin is low quality
  6. Soundboard has aged/thickened finish

Most Likely Culprit:
  - Old bow hair (2+ years)
  - Low-quality strings
  - Professional erhu hair replacement: ¥200-400
```

## Scenario 5: Sourcing Ethical Alternatives to Rosewood

**Context:** A maker wants Dalbergia (rosewood) for pipa but faces CITES restrictions.

**Ethical Alternatives:**
```yaml
Structural Elements:
  - Wenge (鸡翅木): Dense, dark, close grain. Excellent. Not CITES-listed.
  - Ebonized maple: Sustainable, excellent workability
  - Sheesham: Dalbergia sissoo, not Appendix I

Tonal Considerations:
  - Wenge: Very close match to rosewood
  - Ebonized maple: Brighter, good sustain
  - For ethical sourcing: Request FSC certification
```

## Scenario 6: Emergency Bridge Repair at Performance

**Context:** During a guqin performance, the bridge starts to lift.

**Emergency Fix:**
```yaml
Quick Fix (if lifting but not detached):
  1. Slide thin tracing paper under bridge foot
  2. Apply small amount of white glue (PVA) to paper only
  3. Press bridge down gently
  4. Let dry 5-10 minutes

NOT Recommended:
  - Super glue (damages finish)
  - Hot glue (not strong enough)
  - Epoxy (not reversible)

Proper Fix After Performance:
  1. Remove string tension from that area
  2. Remove bridge completely
  3. Clean old glue from both surfaces
  4. Apply thin hide glue
  5. Clamp minimum 4 hours
```

## Scenario 7: Building 10 Guzheng for a Music School

**Context:** A music school needs 10 guzheng. Budget: ¥80,000 total (¥8,000 each).

**Production Plan:**
```yaml
Quality: Semi-professional
  - Soundboard: Paulownia, 5-7 year aged
  - Frame: Plywood with solid wood veneer
  - Bridges: Machine-turned
  - Strings: Quality steel-core with nylon winding

Consistency Strategy:
  - All soundboards from same maker
  - Match instruments in pairs for ensemble

Timeline: 7 months total
Warranty: 2-year structural
Maintenance: ¥5,000/year
```
