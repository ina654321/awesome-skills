# Traditional Instrument Making — Glossary

## Instrument Types

**Guqin (古琴)** — Seven-string zither. One of the oldest Chinese instruments. UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. Made primarily of paulownia (soundboard) and catalpa (back).

**Guzheng (古筝)** — Twenty-one string zither with movable bridges. Made of paulownia. Played with fingerpicks. Larger than guqin.

**Pipa (琵琶)** — Four-string lute with curved neck and fretted fingerboard. Traditionally phoenix wood body. Played with fingerpicks.

**Erhu (二胡)** — Two-string fiddle. Bow hairs placed between the two strings. No fingerboard — strings pressed directly against the neck.

**Zhonghu (中胡)** — Lower-pitched version of erhu with larger body.

**Gaohu (高胡)** — Higher-pitched erhu variant, tuned a fourth higher.

**Sanxian (三弦)** — Three-stringed lute with long neck and small body. Traditionally strung with silk.

**Dizi (笛子)** — Transverse bamboo flute with a membrane (笛膜). One of the oldest Chinese instruments.

**Xiao (箫)** — End-blown bamboo flute. Typically made from purple bamboo (紫竹).

## Wood & Materials

**Paulownia (泡桐)** — Primary soundboard wood. Light, fast-growing, excellent acoustic properties.

**Catalpa (梓木)** — Traditional back-board wood for guqin. Medium density, warm tone.

**Phoenix Wood (梧桐木)** — Primary wood for pipa body. Fine grain, excellent for bending.

**Rosewood (紫檀)** — Dense hardwood from Dalbergia genus. Used for pipa bridges, erhu bodies. CITES-listed.

**Walnut (胡桃木)** — Sustainable alternative to rosewood for fittings and bridges.

## Construction Terms

**Soundboard (面板)** — The top plate that vibrates to produce sound. Most critical acoustic element.

**Hide Glue (鱼胶)** — Traditional adhesive. Reversible with heat and moisture.

**French Polish** — Finish made from shellac dissolved in alcohol. Traditional for plucked instruments.

**Voicing (调音)** — Adjusting soundboard thickness to achieve desired tonal characteristics.

**Inlay (镶嵌)** — Decorative materials set into surface (nacre, bone, metal).

## Acoustic Terms

**Resonance Frequency** — The frequency at which a soundboard naturally vibrates most strongly.

**Harmonics (泛音)** — Higher-frequency vibrations that occur simultaneously with the fundamental.

**Projection** — How well an instrument's sound carries in a room.

**Tonal Balance** — The evenness of response across the instrument's range.

**Attack** — The initial transient when a string is plucked or bowed.

## Restoration Terms

**Patina (包浆)** — The aged surface character. Should be preserved, not removed.

**Reversibility (可逆性)** — The principle that a restoration treatment should be undoable without damage.

**Consolidation (加固)** — Reinforcing fragile wood with adhesive or synthetic resin.
