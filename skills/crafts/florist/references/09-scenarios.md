# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scenario: Budget Wedding Flowers

**User:** "I'm getting married in June with a budget of $1,500 for flowers. I want centerpieces, bouquet, and ceremony decor. Is this realistic?"

**Florist:**
> **June wedding with $1,500 is tight but possible:**
> 
> **Budget breakdown suggestion:**
> - Bridal bouquet: $150-200
> - 8 bridesmaid bouquets: $400-500
> - 10 centerpieces: $400-500
> - Ceremony arrangements: $200-300
> - Delivery/setup: $100-200
> 
> **How to maximize impact:**
> 
> **1. Focus on statement pieces:**
> - One stunning arch vs. many small arrangements
> - High-impact ceremony backdrop
> 
> **2. Use seasonal flowers:**
> - June: Garden roses, peonies, dahlias
> - Less expensive than out-of-season imports
> - More variety for same price
> 
> **3. Simplify design:**
> - Elegant bud vases vs. elaborate arrangements
> - Smaller centerpieces with candles
> - Potted plants as accent (can be taken home)
> 
> **4. Reduce quantities:**
> - 8 vs. 10 centerpieces
> - Simple bouquets with statement flowers
> - Mix DIY and professional elements
> 
> **Recommendations:**
> - Prioritize: bouquet > ceremony > reception
> - Use floral foam mechanics to stretch stems
> - Consider dried flowers for part of decor
> - Ask about local/seasonal alternatives

## 9.2 Scenario: Flowers Wilting Before Event

**User:** "The centerpieces I made yesterday look droopy today. It's a corporate event tomorrow. How do I fix this?"

**Florist:**
> **This is recoverable — here's what to do:**
> 
> **Immediate assessment:**
> - Are stems still in water?
> - Is water fresh?
> - How droopy — slight or severe?
> 
> **Recovery steps:**
> 
> **1. Re-cut and hydrate:**
> - Remove from arrangement
> - Re-cut stems at angle
> - Submerge in warm water (not hot)
> - Let sit 2-4 hours
> 
> **2. Use hydration solution:**
> - Clear conservator or Hydra+
> - Spray stems if wilting is mild
> - Wrapping loosely can help straighten
> 
> **3. Cold shock (for tulips):**
> - Submerge in ice water
> - Store in cooler overnight
> - Can recover significantly
> 
> **Prevention for future:**
> - Don't arrange too early (max 1 day before)
> - Use fresh, conditioned flowers
> - Keep in cooler until delivery
> - Add hydration packets to arrangements
> - Mist and cover during transport

## 9.3 Scenario: Customer Request for Out-of-Season Flowers

**User:** "My customer wants peonies for a December wedding but they're not in season. How do I handle this?"

**Florist:**
> **Out-of-season requests require creative solutions:**
> 
> **First, assess:**
> - How attached are they to peonies specifically?
> - What's their budget?
> - Is there flexibility on look vs. specific flower?
> 
> **Options to present:**
> 
> **1. Domestic grower's greenhouse:**
> - Some specialty growers force peonies
> - Higher cost (2-3x normal)
> - Limited availability
> 
> **2. Imported:**
> - South American or Israeli growing
> - Available but expensive
> - Quality can be inconsistent
> - Order far in advance
> 
> **3. Substitute flowers:**
> - Garden roses (similar look)
> - Double tulips (similar shape)
> - Ranunculus (romantic, full)
> - Show examples of alternatives
> 
> **4. Dried/seated alternatives:**
> - Preserved peonies (real, dried)
> - Silk/studio flowers (if budget allows)
> - Mix of fresh and preserved
> 
> **Communication approach:**
> - Be honest about challenges and costs
> - Present options with pricing
> - Let customer decide
> - If they insist on fresh peonies: order and price accordingly
