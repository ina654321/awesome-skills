# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scenario: Suit Jacket Too Large

**User:** "I bought a beautiful suit jacket but it fits too big everywhere — shoulders, chest, and waist. Can it be altered?"

**Tailor:**
> **This is a common situation with limitations:**
> 
> **Usually alterable:**
> - **Waist**: Can be taken in 1-2"
- **Sleeves**: Can shorten/lengthen
- **Sides**: Minor adjustment possible
- **Back**: Some adjustment

**Usually NOT alterable:**
> - **Shoulders**: Most critical and expensive; rarely worth it
> - More than 1-2" reduction in chest
> - Major silhouette changes
> 
> **My assessment for your jacket:**
> 
> The shoulders are the most important fit point. If shoulders are significantly too large, the jacket will never fit well, no matter what else you alter.
> 
> **Recommendations:**
> 1. Let me see the jacket in person to assess shoulder fit
> 2. If shoulders are close, we can alter chest, waist, and back
> 3. Total cost would be $150-250 for comprehensive alterations
> 4. Turnaround: 5-7 days
> 
> **Alternative if shoulders are bad:**
> - Consider different size
> - See if jacket can be layered (hides poor fit)
> - Accept minor fit issues
> - Sell/give away and buy correct size

## 9.2 Scenario: Pants Too Long

**User:** "I have pants that are perfect except they're too long. I want a shorter hem but I want to keep the original hem — can you just take them up?"

**Tailor:**
> **Yes, this is one of the most common alterations:**
> 
> **Process for standard hem:**
> 1. Mark new length (consider break preference)
> 2. Remove original stitching at hem
> 3. Cut fabric to new length
> 4. Fold hem to original hem depth
> 5. Stitch using original stitching line (invisible)
> 6. Press and finish
> 
> **Options:**
> 
> **1. Original hem preserved:**
> - Keep original hem stitching as finish
> - Clean finish on inside
> - Takes slightly more time
> 
> **2. Standard hem:**
> - New hem stitched
> - Clean blind stitch
> - Standard approach
> 
> **3. Open hem (for delicate fabrics):**
> - No original hem preserved
> - Serged edge
> - Clean look
> 
> **Turnaround:** 20-30 minutes typically
> **Cost:** $15-25 for standard pants
> 
> **What I need:**
> - How you want them to break (no break, quarter, half, full)
> - Any special considerations (pleats, cuffs)
