# Art Conservation & Restoration — Standards & Reference

## Official Standards & Organizations

### Conservation Standards

- [AIC (American Institute for Conservation)](https://www.culturalheritage.org/) — The leading professional organization for art conservators in the US. Publishes the Code of Ethics and Guidelines for Practice.
- [ICOM-CC (International Council of Museums — Committee for Conservation)](https://www.icom-cc.org/) — The primary international organization for conservation science and practice. Publishes triennial conference proceedings with current research.
- [CCI (Canadian Conservation Institute)](https://www.canada.ca/en/conservation-institute.html) — Publishes technical bulletins (Notas) and condition reporting standards. Widely used internationally.
- [British Museum Conservation Department](https://www.britishmuseum.org/) — Publishes research and guidelines on object conservation, especially archaeological materials.

### UNESCO & Cultural Heritage

- [UNESCO 1970 Convention](https://en.unesco.org/1970-convention) — International agreement on preventing illicit trade in cultural property.
- [UNESCO World Heritage Centre](https://whc.unesco.org/) — Standards for preserving cultural heritage sites.
- [UNIDROIT Convention 1995](https://www.unidroit.org/) — Convention on stolen or illegally exported cultural objects.

### ISO Standards for Cultural Heritage

- [ISO 18976:2018](https://www.iso.org/) — Imaging materials — Photographic activity test for color photographic materials.
- [ISO 18920:2011](https://www.iso.org/) — Imaging materials — Storage of audio recordings.
- [ISO 18924:2013](https://www.iso.org/) — Imaging materials — Arrangements for possible unfavourable storage conditions.

## Conservation Materials Standards

### Adhesives

| Adhesive | Reversibility | Use | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-----|-------|
| **Paraloid B-72** | Reversible with acetone/ethanol | Consolidation, attachment | Gold standard; soluble years later |
| **Klucel G (HPC)** | Reversible with water/alcohol | Paper sizing, adhesive | Non-yellowing |
| **Wheat Starch Paste** | Reversible with water | Paper repairs | Traditional, archival |
| **Isinglass** | Reversible with water | Lightweight repairs | Traditional fish collagen |
| **BEVA 371** | Reversible with mineral spirits | Lining, heat-set repairs | Stable, heat-activated film |
| **PVA (Polyvinyl Acetate)** | NOT fully reversible | NOT recommended for fine art | Yellows, becomes insoluble |

### Archival Materials

- **Glass Fiber Screen (Methylene Chloride free)** — Support for fragile works. Inert, strong, archival.
- **Japanese Tissue (Kozo/Mitsumata/Edano)** — Acid-free, long-fiber tissue. Weights: 9gsm to 45gsm.
- **Mat Board (Conservation Grade)** — pH 8.5, alpha cellulose. 4-ply to 8-ply. Use 4-ply minimum for window mats.
- **Archival Boxes (Hollinger Metal Edge)](https://www.hollingergroup.com/)** — Metal-edge boxes, phase boxes.
- **Silica Gel** — Indicating silica gel for humidity control. 200-400 mesh.

## Environmental Standards

### Temperature & Humidity Ranges

| Material Type | Relative Humidity | Temperature | Notes |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|-------|
| **General works on paper** | 40-60% RH | 18-24°C | Stable is key |
| **Oil paintings** | 45-55% RH | 16-21°C | Prevents craquelure progression |
| **Photographs** | 20-50% RH | 2-8°C (cold storage for color) | Color photos degrade above 25°C |
| **Textiles** | 45-55% RH | 18-21°C | Higher RH for natural fibers |
| **Metals** | <45% RH | Ambient | Low RH prevents corrosion |
| **Bone/Ivory** | 45-55% RH | 18-21°C | Similar to human comfort zone |

### Light Standards

- **Lux Level**: <150 lux for light-sensitive works (textiles, watercolors)
- **UV Radiation**: <75 μW/lumen. Remove UV from all light sources.
- **Annual Exposure**: <50,000 lux-hours per year for sensitive works.
- **ASHRAE 62.1** — Ventilation for acceptable indoor air quality.

## Documentation Standards

### Condition Reports

| Level | Description | Components |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Preliminary** | Quick assessment | Dimensions, medium, condition summary, photos |
| **Detailed** | Full documentation | Damage mapping, material analysis, treatment options |
| **Comprehensive** | Museum standard | All above + scientific analysis, provenance research |

### Photographic Documentation

- **Overall**: Captured under raking light, normal light, UV fluorescence
- **Detail**: Macro photography of damage areas
- **UV Fluorescence**: 365nm UV lamp reveals overpainting, restorations, varnish layers
- **IR Reflectography**: Reveals underdrawings in paintings
- **X-radiography**: Reveals construction, pentimenti, repairs

## Scientific Analysis Methods

| Method | What It Detects | Non-Destructive? |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **XRF (X-Ray Fluorescence)** | Elemental composition | Yes |
| **FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared)** | Organic compounds, binding media | Yes |
| **Polarized Light Microscopy (PLM)** | Fiber identification, pigments | Yes |
| **Cross-Section Analysis** | Layer structure | No (micro-sample) |
| **GC-MS (Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry)** | Complex organic identification | No |
| **Dendrochronology** | Wood dating | No (core sample) |
| **Carbon-14 Dating** | Absolute dating of organic material | No |

## Regulatory Frameworks

- **National Register of Historic Places (US)** — Standards for preservation of historic properties and cultural artifacts.
- [NARA (National Archives and Records Administration)](https://www.archives.gov/) — Standards for archival storage and preservation of paper and film.
- **Museum Accreditation Standards** — Each country has accreditation requirements. US: AAM (American Alliance of Museums).
