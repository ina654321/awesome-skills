# Wood Carving — Standards & Reference

## Industry Standards & Certification Bodies

### Wood Carving Associations

**The Caricature Carvers of America (CCA)** — Annual competitions, workshops, and educational resources for caricature and decorative carving. Website: woodcarver.org

**National Wood Carvers Association (NWCA)** — Founded 1933, publishes "Wood Carvers' Gazette." Resources for chip carving, relief carving, and wood sculpture. Website: woodcarvers.org

**Society of Wood Anglers (UK)** — Field sports and decoy carving. Traditional wildfowl carving techniques. Website: societyofwoodanglers.co.uk

**Woodcraft League of America** — Educational programs, local chapters, and carving competitions.

**The Carvers' Association of Hong Kong** — Traditional Chinese carving techniques, apprenticeships, and heritage preservation.

### Technical Standards

**Janka Hardness Scale (ASTM D143)** — Standardized method for measuring wood hardness. Essential for selecting appropriate species for carving depth and detail. Reference: ASTM International, West Conshohocken, PA.

**Moisture Content Standards (ASTM D4442)** — Wood moisture content directly affects carving quality. Optimal carving range: 6-12% MC. Reference: ASTM D4442-07.

**Carving Tool Specifications** — AISI/SAE tool steel grades (O1, A2, W1) and their heat treatment requirements for carving chisels.

### Health & Safety Standards

**NIOSH Wood Dust Exposure Limits** — Respirable crystalline silica exposure limits. Wood dust from certain species (oak, walnut, mahogany, yew) classified as carcinogenic. Reference: NIOSH Pocket Guide to Chemical Hazards.

**ANSI Z87.1 Eye Protection** — Safety glasses with side shields mandatory for all carving operations. High-velocity wood chips cause serious eye injuries.

**OSHA General Duty Clause (Section 5)** — Employer responsibility to provide safe workplace, including wood dust ventilation and PPE.

## Professional Development

### Master Craftsman Certification

**Old Growth Guild (UK)** — Traditional green woodworking certification and apprenticeship programs.

**Lie-Nielsen Toolworks Workshop Certification** — Hand tool woodworking mastery programs.

### Tool Manufacturers & Reference

**Ashley Iles (UK)** — Premium hand-forged carving tools. Reference for proper bevel angles and steel quality.

**Two Cherries (Germany)** — Pfeil brand carving tools. Industry standard for detail work.

**Flexcut** — American-made carving skews and gouges. Reference for beginners and professionals.

**Narex (Czech Republic)** — Premium edge tools at accessible price points.

### Wood Carving Publications

**"The Complete Book of Wood Carving" by E.J. Tangerman** — Classic reference for carving techniques.

**"An Introduction to Wood Carving" by Chris Pye** — Fundamentals of wood grain, tool selection, and technique.

**"Carving Architectural Detail in Wood" by Francis C. Wood** — Advanced relief and architectural carving.

**"The Woodworker's Guide to Wood" by R. Bruce Hoadley** — Essential reference on wood properties, moisture, and grain.

### Museum & Historical Reference

**Victoria & Albert Museum Wood Collection** — Historical wood carving examples across cultures.

**Museo del Legno (Cantù, Italy)** — International woodworking and carving museum.

**Yuanmingyuan Imperial Wood Carving Museum (Beijing)** — Chinese classical wood carving traditions.
