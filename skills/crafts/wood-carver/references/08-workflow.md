# Wood Carving — Troubleshooting Guide

## Common Problems & Solutions

### Tear-Out & Grain Issues

**Problem: Severe tear-out when carving across end grain**
- Cause: Cutting against the grain direction
- Solution: Identify grain direction on end grain; carve from inside toward edge, or flip piece to carve with grain
- Prevention: Study the wood's grain pattern before cutting; use a skew chisel for end grain work

**Problem: Fuzzy or shredded surface after carving**
- Cause: Dull tools, wood too moist, cutting against grain
- Solution: Resharpen immediately; let wood acclimate to controlled humidity (6-12% MC); adjust cutting direction
- Prevention: Maintain razor-sharp edges; store wood in controlled environment

**Problem: Cracking or splitting along grain lines**
- Cause: Wood dried too quickly, internal stress release, or carving into weak zones
- Solution: Stop carving in affected area; seal with diluted glue or thick varnish; allow to rest between sessions
- Prevention: Kiln-dry or air-dry wood properly (1 year per inch); carve symmetric pieces in matched pairs; seal ends immediately after cutting

### Tool Problems

**Problem: Chisel slips unexpectedly during push cut**
- Cause: Dull edge, improper hand position, wet wood, or inadequate support
- Solution: Sharpen immediately; move trailing hand behind tool, never in front; ensure work is secured
- Prevention: Keep tools razor-sharp; use push stroke with dominant hand guiding; keep wood at proper moisture content

**Problem: Chisel producing burn marks (dark discoloration)**
- Cause: Friction from dull tool, excessive pressure, or incorrect tool for species
- Solution: Resharpen; reduce pressure; increase cutting fluid (water or salive for relief work)
- Prevention: Sharp tools and appropriate speed prevent burning; hardwoods require sharper tools

**Problem: Chisel edge chips or rolls over**
- Cause: Hitting hard knot, improper steel for task, or impact damage
- Solution: Re-grind the affected bevel; inspect for micro-chipping under magnification
- Prevention: Avoid knots; use appropriate steel hardness for wood species; never use chisel as pry bar

**Problem: Gouge producing facet marks instead of smooth curve**
- Cause: Twisting wrist instead of letting tool sweep, wrong sweep angle for the cut
- Solution: Practice continuous sweeping motion; select correct sweep (e.g., #5 for medium curves, #9 for shallow)
- Prevention: Match sweep to the intended radius; practice on scrap wood before final cuts

### Structural Problems

**Problem: Relief carving cracking at thin areas**
- Cause: Wood movement due to humidity change; too much material removed; grain direction perpendicular to thin section
- Solution: Stop work; apply diluted wood sealer to grain end; reinforce with basswood backing if necessary
- Prevention: Leave 50% wood mass in high-relief areas; avoid thin sections under 3mm; seal work progressively

**Problem: Project warping after completion**
- Cause: Uneven drying, internal stress release, or asymmetric design
- Solution: Lightly dampen concave side and clamp to shape; or resaw and re-glue if severe
- Prevention: Balance design symmetrically; carve in controlled humidity; use kiln-dried wood

**Problem: Basswood turning black in carved details**
- Cause: Oxidation from bare hands (skin oils), moisture, or iron contamination
- Solution: Clean with 3% hydrogen peroxide; avoid touching carved surfaces with bare hands
- Prevention: Handle only by edges; apply shellac sealer between carving sessions; use talc on hands

### Finishing Problems

**Problem: Finish appears blotchy or uneven**
- Cause: Inconsistent absorption due to varying grain density; wood not properly de-whisked
- Solution: Light sand with 320-grit; apply additional coat with long-brush strokes; thin first coat 50%
- Prevention: De-whisk with sharp card scraper before finishing; raise grain with damp cloth and sand when dry

**Problem: Oil finish remains tacky**
- Cause: Excessive application, insufficient cure time, or cool temperature
- Solution: Wipe off excess with lint-free cloth; warm with hair dryer; wait 48-72 hours between coats
- Prevention: Apply thin coats; wipe off excess within 30 minutes; maintain temperature above 18°C

### Safety Problems

**Problem: Wood splinter embedded in skin**
- Cause: Rough handling, dry wood splinters more easily
- Solution: Clean with antiseptic; if deep, use sterilized tweezers; monitor for infection
- Prevention: Use push stick for narrow work; handle green wood with gloves; keep first aid kit nearby

**Problem: Respiratory issues from wood dust**
- Cause: Inadequate dust collection, improper respirator
- Solution: Stop work immediately; move to fresh air; use N95 or P100 respirator
- Prevention: Always use dust collection; wear P100 respirator when working oak, walnut, mahogany, or exotic species; work in ventilated space
