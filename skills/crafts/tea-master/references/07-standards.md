# Tea Mastery — Standards & Reference

## Official Standards & Organizations

### Tea Organizations

- [International Tea Masters Association (ITMA)](https://www.iteausa.org/) — Professional association for tea masters and educators in the US.
- [Tea Association of USA](https://www.usatea.org/) — Industry trade organization for US tea businesses.
- [Specialty Tea Institute (STI)](https://www.specialtytea.org/) — Education and certification for tea professionals.
- [UK Tea Academy](https://www.uktaacademy.co.uk/) — British tea education and training programs.
- [World Tea Organization (WTO)](https://worldtea.org/) — Global tea industry organization.

### Tea Standards

- [ISO 3720:2011](https://www.iso.org/) — Minimum requirements for black tea. Defines categories (fine, medium, ordinary).
- [ISO 20715:2022](https://www.iso.org/) — Classification of tea based on production method.
- [GB/T 14456](https://www.gbstandards.org/) — Chinese green tea standards. Multiple parts covering different varieties.
- [JAS (Japanese Agricultural Standards)](https://www.maff.go.jp/) — Japanese tea quality standards.

### Certification Bodies

- [Rainforest Alliance](https://www.rainforest-alliance.org/) — Sustainable farming certification for tea estates.
- [Fair Trade USA](https://www.fairtradecertified.org/) — Fair trade certification for tea workers and communities.
- [USDA Organic](https://www.usda.gov/) — US organic certification for tea.
- [EU Organic](https://ec.europa.eu/) — European organic certification.

## Tea Evaluation (Gaiwan Method)

### Tea Evaluation Scoring

| Category | Points | Criteria |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Appearance | 10 | Color, uniformity, plucking standard |
| Aroma (dry) | 10 | Fragrance, intensity, character |
| Aroma (wet) | 15 | Complexity, evolution through steeps |
| Liquor color | 10 | Hue, clarity, brilliance |
| Taste | 25 | Sweetness, umami, bitterness, astringency |
| Mouthfeel | 20 | Body, thickness, smoothness, huigan |
| Aftertaste | 10 | Persistence, returning sweetness |

### Sensory Vocabulary

- **Hui Gan (回甘)** — Sweetness returning after swallowing, especially with bitter teas.
- **Yan Yun (韵味)** — The "rhythm" or lingering character of a tea.
- **Sheng Jin (生津)** — Salivation effect, especially under the tongue.
- **Xiang Yun (香韵)** — The aromatic character unique to the tea.
- **Chen Xiang (陈香)** — Aged character in pu-erh and aged oolongs.
- **Guang (甘)** — Sweetness on the tongue.
- **Mian (绵)** — Long-lasting, silky mouthfeel.
- **Hou (厚)** — Thick, substantial body.
- **Wu (无)** — Void of bitterness or astringency.

## Brewing Standards

### Water Quality

| Parameter | Ideal | Acceptable | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|-----------|-------|
| **pH** | 6.5-7.0 | 6.0-7.5 | <5.5, >8.0 |
| **TDS** | 30-100 mg/L | 20-150 mg/L | <10, >200 |
| **Hardness** | Soft to moderate | — | Very hard |
| **Chlorine** | None | — | Any detectable |

**Best water:** Filtered spring water. Avoid distilled (flat taste) and tap water (chlorine).

### Brewing Parameters

| Tea Type | Temperature | Leaf Ratio | Steep Time | Steeps |
|----------|-----------|-----------|------------|--------|
| **Gyokuro** | 60-65°C | 5-6g/100ml | 2 min | 2-3 |
| **Sencha** | 70-75°C | 4-5g/100ml | 45s | 2-3 |
| **Dragon Well** | 75-80°C | 4g/100ml | 2 min | 3-4 |
| **Silver Needle** | 75-80°C | 3-4g/100ml | 3 min | 3-4 |
| **Da Hong Pao** | 95-100°C | 5g/100ml | 20s | 8-12 |
| **Tie Guan Yin** | 90-95°C | 5g/100ml | 30s | 8-12 |
| **Aged Pu-erh** | 95-100°C | 8-10g/100ml | 10s | 15+ |

## Tea Storage Standards

### Storage Requirements by Type

| Tea | Temperature | Humidity | Light | Airing |
|-----|-----------|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Green** | 0-4°C (freezer ok) | Sealed, dry | None | Minimal |
| **White** | Cool, dry | 50-60% | None | Occasional |
| **Oolong** | Room temp | 60-70% | Dark | Occasional |
| **Pu-erh (raw)** | 15-25°C | 60-75% | Dark | Regular |
| **Pu-erh (ripe)** | Room temp | 50-65% | Dark | Occasional |
| **Black** | Cool, dry | 50-60% | None | Occasional |

### Pu-erh Aging Conditions

- **Relative humidity**: 60-75% (higher promotes aging)
- **Temperature**: 20-30°C (accelerated aging)
- **Air circulation**: Minimal but present
- **Light**: Completely dark
- **Odors**: No exposure to strong smells

## Tea & Health

### Caffeine Content (per 240ml cup)

| Tea Type | Caffeine | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Black** | 40-70mg | Highest caffeine |
| **Oolong** | 30-50mg | Medium |
| **Green** | 20-40mg | Moderate |
| **White** | 10-25mg | Lowest |
| **Pu-erh** | 30-60mg | Varies by age |

### Caffeine Reduction Methods

- Use fewer leaves
- Lower water temperature
- Shorter steeps
- Use older/aged leaves (lower caffeine)
- Decaffeinated options available
