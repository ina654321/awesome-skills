# Tea Mastery — Glossary

## Tea Types & Categories

**Green Tea (绿茶)** — Unoxidized tea. Pan-fired (Chinese) or steamed (Japanese). Fresh, vegetal, grassy flavors.

**White Tea (白茶)** — Minimally processed. Withered and dried only. Delicate, sweet, light.

**Yellow Tea (黄茶)** — Light oxidized. Similar to green with a "yellowing" step. Rarer than green tea.

**Oolong Tea (乌龙茶)** — Partially oxidized (15-85%). Wide range from green-ish to black-ish. Floral, fruity, toasty profiles.

**Red/Black Tea (红茶)** — Fully oxidized. Malty, robust, full-bodied. Called "black tea" in Western classification.

**Dark/Pu-erh Tea (普洱茶)** — Post-fermented. Aged to develop complex, earthy flavors.

**Pu-erh (普洱)** — Dark tea from Yunnan province. Can be raw (sheng) or ripened (shou). Improves with age.

## Tea Processing Terms

**Withering** — Leaving fresh leaves to dry and soften before oxidation.

**Oxidation / Fermentation** — Chemical reaction when tea leaves are bruised or exposed to air. Creates flavor compounds.

**Fixing (Pan-firing)** — Heating to stop oxidation. Key step in green tea production.

**Rolling** — Shaping leaves and breaking cell walls to release juices and facilitate oxidation.

**Drying / Baking** — Final step removing moisture. Stabilizes the tea.

**Sheng (Raw) Pu-erh** — Natural aging. Tea ferments slowly over years.

**Shou (Ripe) Pu-erh** — Accelerated fermentation through controlled moisture and piling.

## Tea Evaluation Terms

**Gaiwan (盖碗)** — Lidded bowl for tea evaluation and brewing. Standard for tea tasting.

**Cha Hai (茶海)** — Fairness pitcher. Used to blend infusions before serving.

**Gongfu Cha (功夫茶)** — Skilled or "with effort" tea ceremony. Traditional Chinese brewing with small vessels and multiple steepings.

**Gongfu (功夫)** — Skill, effort,功夫. In tea context: skilled, attentive brewing.

**Hui Gan (回甘)** — Sweetness returning after swallowing. Characteristic of good teas.

**Yan Yun (韵味)** — The lingering character or "rhyme" of a tea.

**Sheng Jin (生津)** — Salivation effect, especially under the tongue.

**Chen Xiang (陈香)** — Aged character in pu-erh and aged oolongs.

**Mian (绵)** — Long-lasting, silky mouthfeel.

**Hou (厚)** — Thick, substantial body in the liquor.

**Xiang Yun (香韵)** — The aromatic character unique to the tea and terroir.

## Tea Sensory Terms

**Liquor** — The brewed tea liquid. Evaluated for color, clarity, and body.

**Aroma** — The scent of the dry leaf, wet leaf, and brewed liquor.

**Bouquet** — The aromatic complexity in aged teas.

**Terroir** — The environmental factors (soil, altitude, climate) that affect tea character.

**Tip / Bud** — The young leaf tip. More tips = higher quality.

**Mao (毛)** — Fine white down/hairs on tea leaves. Indicator of quality.

**Fannai (茶毫)** — The fine hairs on tea leaves.

## Brewing Terms

**Gaiwan Method** — Brewing with a lidded bowl. Allows full appreciation of aroma.

**Chaoyong (潮功夫)** — Gongfu Cha from Chaozhou tradition. Minimal water, multiple steepings.

**Long Jing (龙井)** — Dragon Well style. Famous Chinese green tea.

**Wash / Awakening Steep** — First steep is poured out to wake up the leaves.

**Fo Pour** — Pouring with force to create bubbles and oxidation.

## Tea Storage Terms

**Gan (干)** — Dry storage. Dry conditions for long-term aging.

**Wet Storage** — Higher humidity aging (60-80% RH). Accelerates fermentation.

**Aged Tea** — Tea that has improved through controlled aging.

**Caked Tea** — Compressed tea (brick, cake, tuo).

**Loose Tea** — Uncompressed, loose leaf tea.

## Tea Ceremony

**Sadō / Chanoyu (茶道)** — Japanese tea ceremony. Based on Zen Buddhism and harmony.

**Matcha (抹茶)** — Stone-ground green tea powder. Whisked in hot water.

**Temomi (手揉み)** — Hand-rolled Japanese tea process.

**Ippodo (一般)** — Japanese tea wholesale house.

## Tea Quality Terms

**First Flush / Spring Harvest** — First spring picking. Most prized, most expensive.

**Qingming (清明)** — Early spring, before Qingming festival. Very light, delicate teas.

**Gui Fei (贵妃)** — "Imperial concubine" — Taiwan high-mountain oolong term for special quality.

**Alpine Tea (高山茶)** — Tea grown at high altitude (>1,000m). Sweeter, more aromatic.

**Orthodox Tea** — Traditional leaf style tea (as opposed to CTC).

**CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl)** — Machine-processed tea. Small particles. Used for tea bags and black tea.
