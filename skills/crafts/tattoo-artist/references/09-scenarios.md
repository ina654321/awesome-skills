# Tattoo Artistry — Glossary

## Tattoo Styles

**Traditional / American Traditional** — Bold outlines (3-5RL or 5RL), limited color palette (red, yellow, green, blue, black), classic imagery (anchor, eagle, rose). Most durable style.

**Neo-Traditional** — Traditional aesthetic but with more detail, smoother shading, and broader color palette. More shading than bold outlines.

**Japanese / Irezumi** — Full traditional Japanese iconography: dragons, koi, cherry blossoms, waves, samurai. Large scale. Placement follows body contours.

**Realism / Portrait** — Photographic-quality images, people, animals. Requires exceptional skill. Limited skin type suitability.

**Blackwork** — Solid black areas, tribal patterns, geometric, or decorative. Very durable. Works on all skin types.

**Fine Line** — Single-needle or small groupings (1RL, 3RL). Delicate, subtle designs. Best on light skin. Fades fastest.

**Watercolor** — Painterly application of color without outlines. Looks like watercolor paintings. Least durable style.

**Dotwork** — Images built from individual dots. Used for shading, geometric patterns, mandalas. Very time-intensive.

**Geometric** — Mandalas, sacred geometry, precise lines. Requires mathematical precision.

**Script / Lettering** — Hand-lettered text, quotes, names. Font choice is everything. Placement affects legibility.

**Chicano** — Black and gray, religious and cultural imagery. Strong outlines, photorealistic shading.

**Polynesian / Tribal** — Traditional Pacific Island patterns. Geometric, band-based. Solid black dominant.

**Ornamental** — Fine line with decorative elements, nature motifs. Mix of styles.

## Body Areas & Anatomy

**Y-Axis / Vertical Stretch** — Areas that stretch when body bends (elbows, knees, fingers). Tattoos distort with movement.

**Pressure Points** — Areas with thin skin (inner wrist, ankle, tops of feet). More painful, heals differently.

**High-Friction Areas** — Areas that rub against clothing or body parts. Fades faster (waistband, thighs, armpits).

**Placement Zones** — Upper arm, forearm, bicep, inner bicep, chest, back, thigh, calf, ankle, foot, hand, finger, neck, behind ear.

## Equipment

**Coil Machine** — Electromagnetic tattoo machine. Standard for most tattooing. Adjustable for lining, shading, coloring.

**Rotary Machine** — Motor-driven machine. Quieter, less vibration. Good for shading and some lining.

**Pen Machine** — Rotary machine shaped like a pen. Growing in popularity. Disposable grip systems.

**Cartridge Needle** — Pre-sterilized needle in a cartridge. Single-use. Quick change between needle types.

**Tube / Grip** — The hand-held part holding the needle. Autoclaved between clients.

**Shader Tube** — Wider needle grouping for filling in color and soft shading.

**Liner Needle** — Tight grouping for clean outlines and fine lines.

**Magnum Needle** — Tapered grouping for shading. Stippling or whip shading.

## Tattoo Process

**Stencil** — Carbon paper transfer of the design onto skin. Temporary, guides tattooing.

**Stencil Solution** — Liquid applied to skin before stenciling to make it transfer better.

**Patch Test** — Testing for allergic reactions by tattooing small area. Used for clients with sensitive skin.

**Blowout** — Ink spreading under skin, causing blurry area. Caused by going too deep.

**Keloid** — Raised scar beyond tattoo boundaries. Genetic predisposition. Cannot be fully prevented.

**Hypertrophic Scar** — Raised but contained within tattoo area. More common than keloids.

## Healing & Aftercare

**Oozing** — Clear or slightly blood-tinged fluid from new tattoo. Normal in first 24-48 hours.

**Peeling** — Skin peeling off new tattoo. Normal days 4-14. Do NOT pick.

**Touch-Up / Retouch** — Additional session to fill in patchy or faded areas after initial healing.

**Fading** — Gradual loss of saturation over time. Normal. Rate varies by style, placement, and skin type.

**Banding** — Visible lines of healed tattoo where passes overlap. Skill issue or healing problem.

## Ink & Pigments

**Carbon Black** — Primary black pigment. Most stable ink ingredient. From carbon or iron oxide.

**Iron Oxide** — Used in browns, reds, yellows. Can oxidize over time (turn orange/rusty).

**Heavy Metals** — Some inks contain cadmium, mercury, cobalt. Can cause allergic reactions.

**Organic Pigments** — Plant-based or synthetic. Can fade faster than mineral pigments.

**UV / Blacklight Reactive** — Special inks that glow under blacklight. Limited data on long-term safety.

**White Ink** — Titanium dioxide. Settles over time, turns yellow. Almost never recommended.

## Health & Safety

**Bloodborne Pathogen** — Disease-causing organism transmitted through blood (HIV, hepatitis B/C).

**Cross-contamination** — Transfer of pathogens from one surface/client to another.

**Sterile** — Free from all living microorganisms. Requires autoclave or single-use.

**Autoclave** — Machine that sterilizes equipment using steam and pressure. Must be tested regularly.

**Barrier Protection** — Plastic film covering equipment (machine, cords, tray). Changed between clients.

**Single-Use** — Items used once and discarded. Mandatory for needles, ink caps, razors, gloves.

**OSHA** — Occupational Safety and Health Administration. Federal agency setting workplace safety standards.

**MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet)** — Information about ink and chemical ingredients. Must be available.

## Client Communication

**Consultation** — Pre-tattoo meeting to discuss design, placement, expectations, and concerns.

**Consent Form** — Legal document acknowledging risks, aftercare instructions, and consent.

**Aftercare Instructions** — Written instructions for healing. Should be provided to every client.

**Healed Photo** — Photo taken 4+ weeks after tattoo. Shows true final result.

**Portfolio** — Collection of artist's healed work. Used for client consultation and marketing.

## Legal & Business

**Licensing** — Most jurisdictions require tattoo artist and/or studio license. Verify local requirements.

**Liability Insurance** — Coverage for injury, infection, or allergic reaction claims.

**Age Verification** — Mandatory for clients under 18. Some jurisdictions require parental consent.

**Informed Consent** — Client understanding and agreeing to risks before tattooing.

**Flash** — Pre-designed tattoo designs available for immediate tattooing. Artist owns flash copyright.
