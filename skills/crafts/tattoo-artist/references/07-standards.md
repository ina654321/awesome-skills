# Tattoo Artistry — Standards & Reference

## Official Standards & Health Regulations

### Health & Safety

- [APSI (Alliance of Professional Piercers)](https://www.safepiercing.org/) — Standards for safe piercing and tattoo practices. Though focused on piercing, their bloodborne pathogen protocols apply to tattooing.
- [OSHA Bloodborne Pathogen Standard (29 CFR 1910.1030)](https://www.osha.gov/) — Federal standard for occupational exposure to blood. Mandatory for all tattoo studios.
- [EPA Standards for Medical Waste](https://www.epa.gov/) — Regulations for disposing of sharps, contaminated supplies, and ink.
- [State/Local Health Departments](https://www.cdc.gov/) — Each state/city has tattoo regulations. Verify local requirements (most US states require studio licensing).

### Industry Organizations

- [APT (Association of Professional Tattoodists)](https://www.aptmember.org/) — Professional organization for tattoo artists.
- [NIOSH (National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health)](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/) — Publishes guidelines on occupational exposure in tattooing.
- [ASTM International](https://www.astm.org/) — Standards for tattoo ink ingredients and sterility.

## Tattoo Styles Reference

| Style | Best For | Skin Type | Longevity | Key Characteristics |
|-------|----------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Traditional (American)** | Bold statement pieces | All | Excellent | Thick outlines, limited palette, classic imagery |
| **Neo-Traditional** | Detailed, colorful pieces | All | Very Good | More detail than traditional, vibrant colors |
| **Japanese (Irezumi)** | Large scale, traditional imagery | All | Excellent | Full sleeve/back pieces, wave/cherry blossom/koi |
| **Realism / Portrait** | Portraits, detailed subjects | Light-Medium | Good | Photographic detail, requires touch-up |
| **Blackwork** | Heavy coverage, solid | All | Excellent | Solid black, geometric, tribal |
| **Fine Line** | Minimalist, delicate | Light only | Moderate | Thin lines, subtle, less bold |
| **Watercolor** | Artistic, painterly | Light only | Poor | Color splashes, fades quickly |
| **Dotwork** | Geometric, stippling | All | Very Good | Individual dots create shading/images |
| **Geometric** | Mandala, sacred geometry | All | Very Good | Precise lines and shapes |
| **Script / Lettering** | Names, quotes, dates | All | Varies | Font choice critical |

## Skin & Healing Reference

### Skin Types (Fitzpatrick Scale)

| Type | Skin Color | Sun Reaction | Tattoo Suitability |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **I** | Very fair | Always burns, never tans | Excellent for all styles |
| **II** | Fair | Burns easily, tans minimally | Excellent for most styles |
| **III** | Medium | Burns sometimes, tans uniformly | Good for most styles |
| **IV** | Olive | Burns rarely, tans easily | Moderate — avoid fine line, white |
| **V** | Brown | Very rarely burns, tans darkly | Limited — blackwork, traditional best |
| **VI** | Dark | Almost never burns, deeply pigmented | Traditional, blackwork; avoid light colors |

### Healing Stages

| Stage | Days | Characteristics | Care |
|-------|------|----------------|------|
| **Initial** | 1-3 | Oozing, redness, tenderness | Leave covered 2-4 hours, wash gently |
| **Peeling** | 4-14 | Flaking, itching (don't scratch!) | Apply thin layer of aftercare ointment |
| **Settling** | 14-30 | New skin forms, color settles | Moisturize, avoid sun |
| **Healed** | 30+ | Fully healed surface | Protect from sun, touch-up if needed |

## Ink & Equipment Standards

### Ink Composition

- [EPA Regulation on Tattoo Inks](https://www.epa.gov/) — Some states (California, New York) regulate ink ingredients.
- **Black Inks**: Carbon-based (most stable). Avoid vegetable-based (fades quickly).
- **White Inks**: Titanium dioxide or zinc oxide. Settles over time, turns yellow.
- **Red Inks**: Mercury sulfide (cinnabar) or iron oxide. Mercury-based red can cause allergic reactions.
- **Yellow Inks**: Cadmium sulfide. Can cause reactions, fades quickly.
- **Avoid**: Neon pigments, glow-in-the-dark (unproven safety).

### Needle & Equipment Standards

- **Sterile, Single-Use Needles**: Mandatory in most jurisdictions. Cartridge needles must be sterile from manufacturer.
- **Ink Caps**: Single-use only.
- **Glove Standards**: Nitrile (not latex — allergy risk).
- **Barrier Film**: Plastic film covering equipment, changed between clients.

## Safety & Contraindications

### Absolute Contraindications (Do Not Tattoo)

- Active skin infection (bacterial, viral, fungal)
- Open wounds or burns in tattoo area
- Keloid scarring history in tattoo area (relative — assess risk)
- Blood disorders (hemophilia, platelet disorders)
- Immunosuppression (HIV, chemotherapy — consult physician)
- Pregnancy (especially first trimester)
- Under 18 without parental consent (check local law)

### Relative Contraindications (Proceed with Caution)

- Psoriasis in tattoo area (can trigger flare)
- Eczema in tattoo area
- Allergies to ink ingredients (test patch required)
- Heavy scar tissue in area
- Diabetes (healing may be impaired)
- Active acne in tattoo area
- Recent vaccination (wait 2 weeks)
- Menstruation (increased sensitivity)

### Medication Interactions

| Medication | Effect | Action |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Blood thinners** (warfarin, aspirin) | Increased bleeding | Consult physician; may need to pause |
| **Accutane** | Slowed healing | Wait 6-12 months after ending treatment |
| **Topical steroids** | Thinned skin | Wait until cleared |
| **Antibiotics** | Varies | Generally fine, check specific drug |
