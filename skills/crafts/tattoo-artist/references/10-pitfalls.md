# Tattoo Artistry — Real-World Scenarios

## Scenario 1: Fine Line Tattoo on Dark Skin

**Context:** A client with Fitzpatrick Type IV skin wants a delicate fine line floral tattoo on the forearm. They were told by another artist it would work.

**Assessment:**
```yaml
Reality:
  - Fine line tattoos rely on clear contrast
  - Darker skin has less contrast with ink
  - Fine detail becomes muddied as skin regenerates
  - This combination has high fade risk

Recommendation:
  "Fine line work on your skin tone will look great initially,
   but the fine detail will soften over 2-3 years. You may see
   significant blurring. I can do a bold/traditional style that
   will look sharp for decades, or we can do fine line with the
   understanding that touch-ups every 2-3 years will be needed."

Alternative approach:
  - Thicker line work (at least 2RL) maintains visibility
  - Blackwork elements provide structure
  - Minimal detail, bold outlines
```

## Scenario 2: Cover-Up of Large Tribal Tattoo

**Context:** Client has a large tribal tattoo (12" × 8") covering the entire upper arm. They want it replaced with a realistic wolf portrait.

**Assessment:**
```yaml
Reality check:
  1. Wolf portrait requires LIGHT areas for fur detail
  2. Dark tribal tattoo shows through dark design
  3. A realistic portrait CANNOT be done over this — black shows through

Design strategy:
  1. Use dark design elements that incorporate the existing tattoo
  2. Geometric/blackwork/neo-traditional style works
  3. A wolf could be stylized (dark, bold) OR
  4. Full cover-up requires laser removal first (expensive, time-consuming)

Honest conversation:
  "A photorealistic wolf is not possible over this tattoo.
   I can create a stunning wolf in a traditional or neo-traditional
   style that will incorporate the black elements of your existing
   tattoo, or we can discuss laser removal to lighten it for a
   lighter design."

Recommended design:
  - Full sleeve Japanese piece (dragon, koi, samurai) incorporating old tattoo
  - Neo-traditional wolf with strong black shading (shows less through)
  - Abstract/decorative design
```

## Scenario 3: First Tattoo — Nervous Client

**Context:** First-time client getting a 4" traditional rose on the outer forearm. Extremely nervous about pain and aftercare.

**Approach:**
```yaml
Pain management:
  1. Explain: Outer forearm is moderate pain (3-5/10 for most)
  2. Well-fed and hydrated helps
  3. Avoid alcohol the night before
  4. Arrive rested
  5. We take breaks if needed

During tattoo:
  1. Explain each step as you go
  2. Breaks are available
  3. Provide stress ball
  4. Talk to distract

Aftercare education:
  Written instructions: Give and explain
  Key points:
  - Leave wrap on 2-4 hours
  - Wash with unscented soap
  - Pat dry, don't rub
  - Apply thin layer of unscented lotion 2-3x daily
  - NO picking, scratching, soaking
  - No swimming, hot tubs for 2 weeks
  - Wear loose clothing

Follow-up:
  - Touch-up policy explained
  - Check in after 3-4 days if concerned
```

## Scenario 4: Custom Design — Client Can't Decide

**Context:** Client wants custom mandala but can't decide on size, placement, or specific elements. Has been revising for 3 months.

**Approach:**
```yaml
Problem: Analysis paralysis on custom design

Solution — Move to action:
  1. "A mandala is a great choice. Let's narrow it down.
      Would you prefer: a) 6 inches on your shoulder blade, or 
      b) 8 inches on your upper back?"
  
  2. "For the mandala center, do you want: a) floral, b) geometric,
      or c) an animal motif?"

  3. "Let's schedule a design session. I'll draw 2-3 rough concepts
      in 30 minutes. We pick one and refine together."

Decision framework:
  - 3 max rounds of revisions (before final)
  - Set deadline: "Design needs to be finalized 1 week before session"
  - Deposit secures time slot
  - Design changes after deposit: Additional fee

End result:
  - Move from indefinite planning to action
  - Client commits with deposit
  - Clear expectations set
```

## Scenario 5: Laser Removal vs. Cover-Up Decision

**Context:** Client has a small tattoo (2") on wrist they hate. Cover-up would be difficult due to placement and size.

**Comparison:**
```yaml
Cover-up pros:
  - Immediate result
  - Less expensive than removal
  - One session

Cover-up cons:
  - Must be larger and darker
  - Limited design options
  - May still show through over time

Laser removal pros:
  - Can completely remove (6-12 sessions, 6+ months)
  - No new tattoo needed
  - Clean slate

Laser removal cons:
  - Expensive ($100-300/session × 8-12 sessions)
  - Time-consuming
  - Can be painful
  - Risk of scarring or incomplete removal
  - Certain colors harder to remove

Recommendation:
  "For a small wrist tattoo, laser removal is often the better 
   choice. A cover-up would need to be much larger and very dark 
   for your skin tone. Let's discuss: Would you prefer to wait 
   12 months for a clean removal, or accept a larger cover-up 
   that can be done now?"
```

## Scenario 6: Client Arrives With Active Skin Condition

**Context:** Client shows up for appointment with visible eczema flare-up in the tattoo area (inner elbow).

**Decision:**
```yaml
Assessment:
  - Active eczema = contraindication
  - Tattooing inflamed skin risks:
    1. Infection
    2. Poor ink retention
    3. Triggering widespread flare-up
    4. Permanent scarring

Action:
  "I can see your elbow is flaring. I can't tattoo over 
   active eczema — it increases infection risk and could make
   your eczema worse. Once it clears up, we'd love to work
   with you. Let's reschedule."

Offer:
  - No rebooking fee
  - Reschedule when clear for 2+ weeks
  - If taking medication: Confirm with doctor it's safe

Documentation:
  - Note in system: Eczema in tattoo area
  - Ask at future bookings if still clear
  - Have them confirm no active outbreak day-of
```

## Scenario 7: Custom Japanese Sleeve — Multi-Session Planning

**Context:** Client wants a full Japanese sleeve (traditional: dragon, koi, cherry blossoms, wave pattern). Large project spanning 10-15 sessions.

**Planning:**
```yaml
Design concept:
  - Main focal piece: Dragon on upper arm
  - Supporting: Koi fish, waves, cherry blossoms, bamboo
  - Flow: Elements follow arm anatomy, wave pattern as connector

Session plan:
  Session 1: Outline and initial color on focal dragon
  Sessions 2-4: Dragon coloring and detail
  Sessions 5-7: Koi and background wave pattern
  Sessions 8-10: Cherry blossoms, clouds, background elements
  Sessions 11-12: Final detail, touch-ups, finishing
  Session 13-15: Any touch-ups, adjustments

Each session: 4-6 hours, every 4-6 weeks

Deposit structure:
  - Design fee: $300-500 (credited to sleeve total)
  - Per session deposit: $100-200 (refundable with 2-week notice)
  - Total sleeve estimate: $4,000-8,000

Care between sessions:
  - Full aftercare for each session
  - UV protection on completed areas
  - Schedule adjustments as needed
```

## Scenario 8: Finger Tattoo Touch-Up Cycle

**Context:** A repeat client has had their finger script tattoo (boyfriend's name) for 3 years. They return every 6-12 months for touch-up.

**Discussion:**
```yaml
Honest assessment:
  "Finger tattoos are the fastest-fading area on the body.
   Your script is very fine, which makes it even more
   challenging. The fact that you wash your hands constantly
   and use sanitizer speeds up fading.

   We can keep touching it up, but the results will always
   fade faster than anywhere else. The realistic expectation
   is annual touch-ups forever."

Options:
  1. Annual touch-ups: $50-100 each time
  2. Bold redesign: Thicker letters, larger, would last longer
  3. Consider removing if it becomes unwanted
  4. Accept the maintenance cycle

For long-term relationship:
  - Set up touch-up reminder
  - Touch-up costs less than new tattoo
  - Keep client informed of condition each time
  - Document touch-up history
```
