# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Engine Fire During Start

**Situation:** "During engine start, you observe fire at the engine inlet. The fire detection system has not yet activated."

**Expert:**
> **IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED:**
> 
> This is a time-critical emergency:
> 
> 1. **Engine Start Aborted:**
>    - Fuel cut-off immediately
>    - Continue cranking if fire persists
>    - Do NOT open cowling (oxygen feeds fire)
> 2. **Fire Extinguishing:**
>    - Discharge engine fire bottle
>    - Wait minimum 60 seconds
>    - Have fire extinguisher ready
> 3. **Post-Fire Actions:**
>    - Do NOT attempt restart
>    - Evacuate aircraft
>    - Notify emergency services
>    - Secure area for investigation
> 
> **Root Cause Investigation:**
> - Fuel controller malfunction
> - Igniter probe failure
> - Combustion chamber failure
> - Oil/fluid leak into hot section
> 
> **Documentation:**
> - All symptoms and actions
> - Time of occurrence
> - Engine hours since new/overhaul
> - Parts replaced

## 9.2 Hydraulic System Failure in Flight

**Situation:** "The flight crew reports hydraulic system #1 has failed. The aircraft is 150 miles from destination."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Hydraulic failure reduces landing capability:
> 
> **Flight Crew Actions:**
> 1. **Immediate:**
>    - Declare emergency
>    - Land at nearest suitable airport
>    - Anticipate manual brakes
>    - Verify emergency extension
> 
> **Maintenance Response:**
> 1. **Pre-Landing:**
>    - Position maintenance at runway end
>    - Have emergency equipment ready
>    - Fire department on standby
> 2. **Post-Landing:**
>    - Assess aircraft from safe distance
>    - Check for hydraulic fluid leaks
>    - Evaluate fire/smoke condition
>    - Provide access for crew
> 
> **Investigation Focus:**
> - Hydraulic pump failure
> - Reservoir depletion
> - Line rupture
> - Component failure
> 
> **Safety Note:** Multiple redundant systems typically prevent catastrophic loss

## 9.3 Unscheduled Landing Due to Bird Strike

**Situation:** "A bird strike has shattered the #2 engine cowling. The engine is still running but making unusual sounds. The crew has diverted."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Assessment:**
> 
> **Pre-Landing:**
> - Brief fire department on standby
> - Position maintenance supervisor
> - Prepare tow vehicle
> - Have parts available
> 
> **Landing Inspection Required:**
> 1. **Engine Cowling:**
>    - Extent of damage
>    - Fan blade condition
>    - Inlet guide vane damage
>    - Foreign Object Damage (FOD)
> 2. **Engine Performance:**
>    - Vibration levels
>    - EGT margins
>    - Oil consumption
>    - Power asymmetry
> 3. **Airframe:**
>    - Wing leading edges
>    - Cockpit windows
>    - Radome and antennas
>    - Pitot tubes
> 
> **Disposition:**
> - Engine likely requires teardown inspection
> - Cowling repair/replacement needed
> - Full airframe damage assessment
> - Test flight required before return to service

## 9.4 Cabin Pressurization Warning

**Situation:** "The crew reports cabin altitude warning at FL350. They are descending to FL250."

**Expert:**
> **Technical Assessment:**
> 
> This indicates loss of cabin pressure integrity:
> 
> **Possible Causes:**
 1. **Outflow valve malfunction**
 2. **Cabin pressure controller failure**
 3. **Door seal leak**
 4. **Emergency release inadvertently activated**
 5. **Structural damage
> 
> **Crew Actions:**
> - Don oxygen masks
> - Descend below 10,000 ft
> - Notify company
> - Prepare for landing
> 
> **Maintenance Response:**
> 1. **Document symptoms:**
>    - Cabin altitude and differential
>    - Temperature
>    - When warning occurred
> 2. **Ground Testing:**
>    - Pressurization system test
>    - Check outflow valve operation
>    - Test controllers
>    - Inspect door seals
> 3. **Repair and Verification:**
>    - Repair/replace faulty components
>    - Full pressurization test
>    - Leak check
>    - Preflight check

## 9.5 Landing Gear Anomaly at Night

**Situation:** "The landing gear did not retract after takeoff. The crew reports the gear down but not locked indication."

**Expert:**
> **Nighttime Procedure:**
> 
> This requires careful ground observation:
> 
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 1. **Visual Confirmation:**
>    - Have trained personnel observe gear
>    - Use flashlights, not high intensity
>    - Note which position uncertain
> 2. **Aircraft Positioning:**
>    - Taxi to well-lit area
>    - Position for easy access
>    - Clear approach path
> 
> **Troubleshooting:**
> 1. **Gear Door Issues:**
>    - Door may be blocking gear lock
>    - Door actuator failure
>    - Linkage problem
> 2. **Locking Mechanism:**
>    - Check uplocks and downlocks
>    - Verify position sensors
>    - Test alternate release
> 
> **Resolution:**
> - Manual locking if needed
> - Proper gear door rigging
> - Full operational test
> - Functional check flight may be required

## 9.6 EGT Exceedance During Climb

**Situation:** "During climb, engine EGT is exceeding limits. The crew has reduced power and is requesting maintenance advice."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Assessment:**
> 
> EGT exceedance can cause engine damage:
> 
> **Crew Actions:**
> 1. **Reduce power** to maximum continuous
> 2. **Climb at lower thrust** if terrain allows
> 3. **Note exact exceedance** values
> 4. **Prepare for possible diversion**
> 
> **Maintenance Response:**
> 1. **Collect Data:**
>    - EGT values and duration
>    - N1, N2, fuel flow
>    - Altitude and ambient temp
>    - Time of occurrence
> 2. **Engine Assessment:**
>    - Review trend data
>    - Check for other parameters
>    - Evaluate hot section condition
> 
> **Disposition Options:**
> - Continue to destination (minor exceedance)
> - Divert for inspection (significant)
> - Land immediately (severe exceedance)
> 
> **Post-Flight:**
> - Borescope inspection
> - Performance test
> - Possible hot section repair
