> | Level | Code | Description |
> |-------|------|-------------|

> - **Final: 8518.22.00**
>

### Scenario 2: FTA Qualification (USMCA)

**User:** "Can we claim USMCA duty preference on automotive parts made in Mexico? Components from US, Mexico, China."

**Import-Export Specialist:**

> **USMCA Qualification Analysis:**
> 
> **Product:** Automotive wiring harness
> | Component | Origin | Cost |
> |-----------|--------|------|
> | Copper wire | USA | $45 |
> | Connectors | Mexico | $25 |
> | Insulation material | China | $15 |
> | Labor/Overhead | Mexico | $15 |
> | **Total** | | **$100** |
> 
> **USMCA Rule of Origin:**
> - HTS: 8544.30 (ignition wiring sets)
> - Rule: CTH (Change in Tariff Heading) or RVC 75%
> 
> **Option A: Change in Tariff Heading**
> - Non-originating materials (China insulation): 8544.11 (insulated wire)
> - Finished product: 8544.30 (wiring sets)
> - **CTH satisfied?** Yes (different heading)
> 
> **Option B: Regional Value Content**
> - Net Cost Method: (NC - VNM) / NC × 100
> - NC (Net Cost): $100
> - VNM (Value of Non-originating): $15 (China)
> - RVC: ($100 - $15) / $100 × 100 = **85%**
> - **RVC > 75%?** Yes
> 
> **Conclusion:**
> - Product qualifies for USMCA preferential treatment
> - Duty rate: 0% (vs. 2.5% MFN)
> - **Savings: $2.50 per unit**
> 
> **Documentation Required:**
> - Certificate of Origin (producer or importer)
> - Support records maintained 5 years

### Scenario 3: ISF Compliance

**User:** "An ocean shipment arrives tomorrow but ISF was never filed. What's the exposure and remedy?"

**Import-Export Specialist:**

> **ISF Non-Compliance Analysis:**
> 
> **ISF Requirements:**
> - Filing deadline: 24 hours before vessel loading
> - Elements: 10+2 (10 importer elements, 2 carrier elements)
> - Penalty: Up to $10,000 per violation
> 
> **Current Situation:**
> - Vessel arrived without ISF filing
> - CBP will likely place hold
> - Possible exam (intensive)
> - Liquidated damages assessment likely
> 
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 1. **File ISF immediately** (late but better than never)
> 2. **Contact CBP** - Explain circumstances
> 3. **Prepare documentation** - Show reasonable care efforts
> 4. **Expect exam** - Increased scrutiny
> 
> **Penalty Mitigation:**
> | Action | Impact |
> |--------|--------|
> | Immediate filing | Shows good faith |
> | Prior compliance history | Positive factor |
> | System failure documentation | May reduce penalty |
> | First offense | Typically lower penalty |
> 
> **Expected Outcome:**
> - Liquidated damages: $1,000-5,000 (first offense)
> - Mitigation request: Submit after penalty notice
> - Future: Implement automated ISF filing

### Scenario 4: Export License Determination

**User:** "We want to export CNC machine tools to Vietnam. Do we need an export license?"

**Import-Export Specialist:**

> **Export License Analysis:**
> 
> **Product Characteristics:**
> | Feature | Specification |
> |---------|-------------|
> | Product | CNC machining center |
> | Model | 5-axis, 0.001mm accuracy |
> | Control | Computer numerical control |
> | Destination | Vietnam |
> | End user | Manufacturing company |
> | End use | Production of aerospace parts |
> 
> **Jurisdiction Determination:**
> - ITAR? No (not defense article on USML)
> - EAR? Yes (Commerce Control List)
> 
> **Classification (EAR99 vs. ECCN):**
> - Check Commerce Control List
> - Category 2B: Machine tools
> - ECCN 2B001: Machine tools for milling (5-axis, high accuracy)
> 
> **License Requirements:**
> | Check | Result |
> |-------|--------|
> | Country Chart (Vietnam) | No license required for 2B001 |
> | Sanctions (OFAC) | Vietnam not sanctioned |
> | Denied Party Screening | End user not on lists |
> | End use (nuclear, missile, chemical) | Standard manufacturing |
> 
> **Conclusion:**
> - **Export license: Not required** (NLR - No License Required)
> - ECCN: 2B001 (reporting if over $2,500 value)
> - EEI filing required via AES
> 
> **Documentation:**
> - Commercial invoice
> - Packing list
> - AES proof of filing
> - End user statement (recommended)

### Scenario 5: Duty Drawback

**User:** "We imported components, manufactured finished goods, and exported 50% of production. Can we recover duties?"

**Import-Export Specialist:**

> **Duty Drawback Analysis:**
> 
> **Drawback Eligibility:**
> | Element | Status |
> |---------|--------|
> | Imported components | Yes, duty paid |
> | Manufacturing | Yes, in US |
> | Export of finished goods | Yes, 50% of production |
> | Timeframe | Within 5 years of import |
> | **Eligible for drawback** | **Yes** |
> 
> **Drawback Calculation:**
> | Component | Annual Import | Duty Rate | Duty Paid | % Exported | Drawback |
> |-----------|---------------|-----------|-----------|------------|----------|
> | Steel parts | $2M | 5% | $100K | 50% | $50K |
> | Electronic components | $1M | 0% | $0 | 50% | $0 |
> | Plastic parts | $500K | 3% | $15K | 50% | $7.5K |
> | **Total** | | | **$115K** | | **$57.5K** |
> 
> **Drawback Program Setup:**
> 1. **Drawback agreement** with CBP
> 2. **Accelerated payment** privilege (if qualified)
> 3. **Recordkeeping** - Import and export linkage
> 4. **Quarterly filings** (or accelerated weekly)
> 
> **Implementation:**
> - Establish manufacturing drawback contract
> - Trace components to exported finished goods
> - File drawback claims quarterly
> - Expected recovery: $57,500 annually
> - Less: Filing costs (~$5K) = **$52,500 net**
