# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 E-commerce Distribution Network Redesign

**Customer:** "Our e-commerce business is growing 50% annually. Current network cannot support. Help us design the next 5-year network."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> E-commerce has unique distribution requirements:
> 
> **Key Challenges:**
> 1. **Speed Requirements:**
>    - Next-day delivery expectations
>    - Same-day in major metros
>    - Real-time inventory visibility
> 2. **Inventory Positioning:**
>    - SKU proliferation
>    - Demand volatility
>    - Return handling
> 3. **Cost Pressure:**
>    - Last-mile cost
>    - Returns processing
>    - Packaging complexity
> 
> **Network Design Options:**
> - **Option A: Distributed Micro-fulfillment**
>   - Small automated facilities near customers
>   - High fixed cost, low shipping cost
>   - Best for high-density metro areas
> - **Option B: Regional Hubs**
>   - Larger facilities with automation
>   - Balance of speed and cost
>   - Good for broad geographic coverage
> - **Option C: Hybrid Approach**
>   - Combine micro-fulfillment + regional
>   - Optimal for varied demand patterns
> 
> **Recommendation:** Hybrid approach with:
> - 2-3 regional fulfillment centers
> - 8-12 micro-fulfillment centers
> - Strategic 3PL partnerships for peak

## 9.2 Manufacturing Plant Relocation

**Customer:** "We're moving a major manufacturing plant from Ohio to Texas. How should we redesign our distribution network?"

**Expert:**
> **Impact Analysis:**
> 
> Plant relocations require comprehensive network redesign:
> 
> **Distance Impact:**
> - Previous: 400-mile average to customers
> - New: 800-1000 mile average to Eastern markets
> - Comparable: ~600 mile to Western markets
> 
> **Recommended Approach:**
> 1. **Maintain Eastern Market Service:**
>    - Add regional DC in Pennsylvania or Virginia
>    - Consider third-party logistics
>    - Evaluate rail transportation
> 2. **Leverage Texas Location:**
>    - Serve Southwest directly
>    - West Coast via intermodal
>    - Mexico cross-border opportunity
> 3. **Inventory Implications:**
>    - Increase safety stock 15-20%
>    - Longer replenishment cycles
>    - Higher transportation costs
> 
> **Cost-Benefit:**
> - Texas: Lower labor and real estate costs
> - Eastern DC: Additional $3-5M annual operating cost
> - Transportation: +$8-12M annually
> - Total net savings: ~$10-15M annually
> 
> **Timeline:** 18-24 months for full transition

## 9.3 Cold Chain Network Design

**Customer:** "We need to expand our fresh produce distribution network nationally. What infrastructure is needed?"

**Expert:**
> **Cold Chain Requirements:**
> 
> Fresh produce requires precise temperature control:
> 
> **Temperature Zones:**
> | Category | Temperature | Products |
> |----------|-------------|----------|
> | Frozen | -18°C | Frozen items |
> | Chill | 0-4°C | Meat, dairy |
> | Cool | 8-12°C | Produce |
> | Ambient | 15-20°C | Tropical |
> 
> **Infrastructure Requirements:**
> 1. **Distribution Centers:**
>    - Multi-temp facilities
>    - Refrigerated truck docks
>    - Pre-cooling capabilities
>    - Quick turnaround design
> 2. **Transportation:**
>    - Refrigerated trucks (reefers)
> - Temperature-controlled rail
> - Intermodal cold chain
> 3. **Last Mile:**
>    - Refrigerated vans
>    - Dark store model
>    - Insulated packaging
> 
> **Technology Requirements:**
> - Real-time temperature monitoring
> - IoT sensors throughout chain
> - Chain of custody documentation
> - Recall capability by lot

## 9.4 International Network Optimization

**Customer:** "We're expanding from US-only to serving Europe and Asia. What network structure should we use?"

**Expert:**
> **International Considerations:**
> 
> Global expansion requires different approach:
> 
> **Network Structure:**
> 1. **US Export:**
>    - Maintain US manufacturing base
>    - Consolidate at port cities
>    - Use freight forwarders
> 2. **European Distribution:**
>    - Hub in Netherlands (Port of Rotterdam)
>    - Distribution to EU-27
>    - VAT and customs compliance
> 3. **Asian Distribution:**
>    - Hub in Singapore or Hong Kong
>    - Regional manufacturing consideration
>    - Cross-docking at ports
> 
> **Key Decisions:**
> | Factor | Decision Points |
> |--------|------------------|
> | Make vs. Buy | Own facilities or 3PL |
> | Lead time | 4-6 weeks ocean transit |
> | Inventory | Deconsolidation strategy |
> | Customs | Bonded vs. duty-paid |
> | Currency | Pricing strategy |
> 
> **Incoterms Selection:**
> - For European customers: DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)
> - For own import: FOB origin + freight

## 9.5 Disaster Recovery Network

**Customer:** "A major hurricane disabled our Gulf Coast distribution center. How do we build resilience into our network?"

**Expert:**
> **Resilience Design:**
> 
> Natural disasters require redundancy planning:
> 
> **Risk Assessment:**
> - Identify geographic risk zones
> - Analyze single points of failure
> - Calculate exposure time
> - Define recovery objectives
> 
> **Resilience Strategies:**
> 1. **Geographic Diversification:**
>    - No single DC in hurricane zone
>    - Maintain alternate facilities
>    - Partner with 3PLs outside risk area
> 2. **Inventory Buffer:**
>    - Pre-position 3-4 weeks extra inventory
>    - Strategic reserves at customer sites
>    - Emergency stock agreements
> 3. **Transportation Alternatives:**
>    - Multiple carrier relationships
>    - Air freight capability
>    - Alternative routing plans
> 4. **Technology:**
>    - Cloud-based WMS
>    - Remote monitoring
>    - Communication backup
> 
> **Business Continuity Plan:**
> - Documented recovery procedures
> - Pre-established contracts
> - Regular disaster drills
> - Annual review and update
