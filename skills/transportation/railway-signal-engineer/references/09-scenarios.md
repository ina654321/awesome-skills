# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Signal Aspect Failure Investigation

**Situation:** "A wayside signal is displaying no light. The lamp tests good and there is voltage present."

**Expert:**
> **Diagnostic Approach:**
> 
> This requires systematic troubleshooting:
> 
> 1. **Initial Observations:**
>    - Check signal head lens for damage/obstruction
>    - Verify correct lamp wattage installed
>    - Measure actual voltage at lamp terminals
>    - Check for corrosion at connections
> 
> 2. **Component Testing:**
>    - Test transformer secondary output
>    - Verify optical system alignment
>    - Check for loose connections in housing
>    - Inspect wire insulation for damage
> 
> 3. **Root Cause:**
>    - Common causes: loose connections, corrosion, transformer failure
>    - Check for moisture intrusion
>    - Verify ground connections
> 
> **Resolution:**
> - Clean/replace corroded connections
> - Verify transformer output meets spec
> - Check optical lens alignment
> - Document and return to service

## 9.2 Highway Crossing Gate Malfunction

**Situation:** "Gate at milepost 142.3 is not lowering. The mechanism runs but the gate arm doesn't move."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Gate mechanism failures can be safety-critical:
> 
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 1. Notify dispatcher and suspend highway traffic
> 2. Post flaggers or barricades
> 3. Check for trains approaching
> 4. Verify track circuits are clear
> 
> **Diagnostic Steps:**
> - Listen for motor running
> - Check gear mechanism for binding
> - Verify limit switches position
> - Inspect drive chain/sprocket
> - Test motor windings
> 
> **Common Causes:**
> - Broken drive chain
> - Seized gear assembly
> - Failed limit switch
> - Motor failure
> - Broken torque spring
> 
> **Safety Note:** Never allow highway traffic while gate is inoperable

## 9.3 Positive Train Control Alert

**Situation:** "PTC system is displaying a 'Communication Loss' alert on locomotive 4521. The train is currently moving at 35 mph."

**Expert:**
> **Critical Protocol:**
> 
> PTC communication loss requires immediate response:
> 
> 1. **Immediate Actions:**
>    - Engineer notifies dispatcher
>    - Reduce speed per railroad rules
>    - Prepare for full stop if required
>    - Locate last known position
> 
> 2. **System Response:**
>    - Verify on-board system status
>    - Check radio/antenna functionality
>    - Attempt system reboot (per procedure)
>    - Document incident time and location
> 
> 3. **Regulatory Requirements:**
>    - FRA requires PTC to remain operative
>    - Communication loss may require stopping
>    - Detailed incident report required
>    - System must be restored before continuing
> 
> **Recovery:**
> - Typical resolution: system reboot restores comms
> - If persistent: await wayside tech response
> - Full PTC operational check before resuming

## 9.4 Interlocking System Fault

**Situation:** "The dispatcher reports that the dispatcher console shows 'Unauthorized Route' at the west leg of the yard."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Unauthorized route indications are serious:
> 
> 1. **Immediate Safety:**
>    - No trains should move through affected area
>    - Notify all crews in vicinity
>    - Establish physical blocking if needed
> 
> 2. **Diagnostic Approach:**
>    - Check controller status
>    - Verify switch position feedback
    - Check route locking circuits
>    - Review event logs
> 
> 3. **Common Causes:**
>    - Switch point detection failure
>    - Circuit controller malfunction
>    - Broken feed or return wiring
>    - Software/hardware fault
> 
> **Resolution Protocol:**
> - May require physical inspection of switch
> - Test control circuits individually
> - Replace faulty relay or module
> - Full test before return to service
> 
> **Documentation:**
> - All field observations
> - Test results
> - Parts replaced
> - FRA report if required

## 9.5 D.C. Traction Interference

**Situation:** "Signal at milepost 89 is showing false proceed indications during Electrified Territory operation."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> D.C. traction (third rail) causes unique interference:
> 
> **The Problem:**
> - Stray current affects track circuits
> - Can cause signals to display without train present
> - Creates safety hazard
> 
> **Diagnostic Steps:**
> 1. **Identify affected circuits:**
>    - Monitor during traction operations
>    - Document timing correlation
 2. **Measure interference:**
    - Check for stray voltage
    - Measure current on rails
    - Test insulators and bonds
> 
> 3. **Solutions:**
>    - Add impedance bonds
>    - Improve negative return system
>    - Adjust signal timing
    - Add interference protection
> 
> **Coordination:**
> - Work with traction power department
> - May require infrastructure upgrades
> - Test during both traction and non-traction periods
