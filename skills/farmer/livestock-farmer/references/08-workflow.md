# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Daily Herd Health Monitoring Workflow

```
Morning Inspection (7–8 AM)
├── Observe from distance: behavior, gait, appetite indicators
├── Check waterers: clean, flowing, temperature (not frozen in winter)
├── Check feed bunks: availability, bunk清洁, residual feed
├── Identify animals needing attention: limping, off-feed, respiratory signs
└── Record temperature of any sick animal (normal: cattle 101–102.5°F)

Midday Check (1–2 PM)
├── Monitor feeding behavior: crowding bunk, sorting behavior
├── Check water consumption in heat stress conditions
├── Assess respiratory rate in calves (heat stress indicator)
├── Verify calf huts/neonatal area: bedding condition, calf health
└── Review morning treatments: response to medication

Evening Inspection (5–6 PM)
├── Final water availability check
├── Move animals requiring treatment to hospital pen
├── Set up for evening feeding if applicable
├── Verify night security: perimeter check, predator threats
└── Log all observations in herd management software

Emergency Sick Animal Protocol
├── Isolate: move to designated hospital pen with clean water and hay
├── Diagnose: temperature, pulse, respiration, physical exam
├── Treat: per veterinarian protocol; record treatment date, drug, dose, withdrawal
├── Re-evaluate: 24h and 48h post-treatment
└── Cull if chronic or unrecoverable: humane euthanasia per AVMA guidelines
```

## 8.2 Calving & Neonatal Management Workflow

```
Pre-Calving (2–4 weeks before due date)
├── Move cows to calving area: clean, well-bedded, low-stress
├── Provide adequate space: 100–150 sq ft/cow
├── Check udder development: bagging up indicates calving within 2 weeks
├── Monitor behavior: nesting behavior, separation from herd
└── Ensure clean, dry calving environment: straw bedding 6+ inches

Active Calving (Stage 2)
├── Observe from distance: do not disturb unless assistance needed
├── Normal delivery: front feet first, head between; 30–60 min
├── Assistance needed if: no progress after 30 min, malpresentation, dystocia
├── If malpresentation: clean, lubricate, reposition per veterinary training
└── Pull only with gentle, steady traction — never jerk

Post-Calving (0–24 hours)
├── Ensure calf is breathing: clear airways, stimulate if needed
├── Navel dip: 7% iodine tincture within 30 minutes of birth
├── Colostrum: 3–4 quarts within 2 hours of birth (navel not optional)
├── Record: birth weight, dam ID, time, presentation, assistance needed
├── Move to calf hutch within 12 hours
└── Monitor calf for scours: check hydration, treat early
```

## 8.3 Pasture & Feed Management

1. **Rotational grazing:** Move cattle every 3–7 days; rest period 21–30 days
2. **Stocking rate:** Match to forage production; monitor pasture condition monthly
3. **Water access:** Clean water within 800 feet of all grazing areas
4. **Fence check:** Inspect weekly; remove dangerous wire, repair posts
5. **Mineral supplementation:** Provide free-choice trace mineral salt year-round
6. **Forage testing:** Sample pastures monthly during growing season
