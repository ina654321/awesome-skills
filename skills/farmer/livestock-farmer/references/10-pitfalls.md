# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Administering antibiotics without veterinary guidance** | 🔴 High | Establish VCPR; follow veterinarian protocol |
| 2 | **Not following drug withdrawal periods** | 🔴 High | Record every treatment; check labels; pay attention to slaughter hold |
| 3 | **Skipping colostrum for newborn calves** | 🔴 High | 3–4 quarts within 2 hours; colostrum is non-negotiable |
| 4 | **Overcrowding pastures or facilities** | 🟡 Medium | Match stocking rate to carrying capacity; monitor forage |
| 5 | **Not monitoring water intake** | 🟡 Medium | Animals can survive weeks without feed but only days without water |
| 6 | **Ignoring foot health** | 🟡 Medium | Check hooves weekly; trim before issues become severe |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Establish a valid VCPR** (Veterinary Client Patient Relationship) — required for drug use
2. **Record every treatment:** date, animal ID, drug, dose, route, withdrawal period
3. **Cull chronic animals** — repeated illness animals are cost centers and disease risks
4. **Vaccination program** — work with vet to design based on herd health status
5. **Biosecurity protocols** — visitor logs, quarantine new arrivals, clean equipment
6. **Humane handling always** — Temple Grandin principles; low-stress handling reduces injury

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Daily animal observation logs completed
- [ ] All treatments recorded with proper drug withdrawal
- [ ] Fresh water available at all times
- [ ] Calving records: birth weight, dam, dystocia score, colostrum given
- [ ] Vaccination program current per veterinarian plan
- [ ] Mortality composting/burial/disposal compliant with state law
- [ ] Pasture rotation schedule maintained
- [ ] Foot health checked weekly; trim schedule maintained
- [ ] Biosecurity log maintained: visitor records, new animal quarantine
- [ ] Herd health meeting with veterinarian quarterly
