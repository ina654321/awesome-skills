# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Annual Crop Planning & Execution Workflow

```
Phase 1: Pre-Season Planning (Winter/Early Spring)
├── Review previous year's yield data and soil tests
├── Update soil nutrient management plan based on soil tests
├── Select varieties: maturity group, pest resistance, yield data
├── Plan crop rotation: corn-soybean-alfalfa cycle benefits
├── Arrange input purchases: seed, fertilizer, chemicals
├── Calibrate equipment: planters, sprayers, combines
└── File crop insurance: buy-up coverage by sales closing date

Phase 2: Planting Operations (Spring)
├── Check soil temperature: corn > 50°F, soybean > 55°F
├── Check soil moisture: plant into adequate moisture, not wet
├── Calibrate seeding rate: seeds/acre per variety recommendation
├── Plant depth: corn 1.5–2.0", soybean 1.0–1.5"
├── Row spacing: corn 30", soybean 15–30" (narrow > conventional)
└── Monitor emergence: target 80%+ emergence within 7–10 days

Phase 3: In-Season Management (Summer)
├── Scout weekly: weeds, insects, disease, nutrient deficiencies
├── Apply post-emergence herbicide: V2–V4 corn, V2–V3 soybean
├── Side-dress nitrogen (corn): V6–V8 growth stage
├── Monitor for disease: scouting, fungicide decision (feekes 10.5 for wheat)
├── Irrigation scheduling: check soil moisture, evapotranspiration data
└── Monitor market prices: forward contract or hedge as appropriate

Phase 4: Harvest Operations (Fall)
├── Monitor grain moisture: corn 15–25%, harvest at 15–17% (dryer)
├── Check test weight: indicator of grain fill and maturity
├── Set combine properly: concave clearance, rotor speed, sieve
├── Calibrate yield monitor: weigh wagon or field verification
└── Complete harvest before weather damage or lodging
```

## 8.2 Soil Testing Workflow

1. **Sample timing:** Collect soil samples in fall after harvest (most accurate) or early spring
2. **Sampling method:** Grid sampling (2.5-acre grid) or zone sampling based on management zones
3. **Sample depth:** 0–6" for N, P, K; 0–24" for deeper sampling in special cases
4. **Submission:** Air-dry samples; submit to state soil testing lab or commercial lab
5. **Interpretation:** Use land-grant university recommendations (e.g., Tri-State Fertilizer Recommendations)
6. **Nutrient plan:** Develop variable-rate application map based on results
7. **Apply nutrients:** Incorporate P and K in fall or spring; sidedress N in-season for corn

## 8.3 Integrated Pest Management (IPM) Protocol

| Step | Action | Decision Point |
|------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Scout weekly; record pest levels | Economic threshold vs. treatment cost |
| 2 | Identify pest accurately | Weed, insect, disease ID guides |
| 3 | Assess damage | % infestation, yield impact estimate |
| 4 | Choose control method | Cultural, biological, chemical, integrated |
| 5 | Apply treatment | Follow label; calibrate equipment |
| 6 | Evaluate results | Post-treatment scouting; yield comparison |
