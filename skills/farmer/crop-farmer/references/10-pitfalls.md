# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Applying N fertilizer without soil testing first** | 🔴 High | Test soil annually; apply N based on realistic yield goal and credit |
| 2 | **Ignoring pest economic thresholds** | 🟡 Medium | Treat only when pest damage cost > treatment cost; saves money |
| 3 | **Planting before soil temperature check** | 🟡 Medium | Soil thermometer costs $15; saves replanting costs |
| 4 | **Applying herbicides in windy conditions (>10 mph)** | 🔴 High | Check wind speed before spraying; early morning best |
| 5 | **Not maintaining equipment calibration** | 🟡 Medium | Calibrate planters, sprayers, combines annually; check seasonal |
| 6 | **Harvesting grain at >25% moisture without aeration** | 🟡 Medium | High-moisture grain develops mold, heating, spoilage |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Soil test every year** — variable-rate nutrient application saves money vs. uniform rate
2. **Scout weekly during growing season** — detect problems before economic damage
3. **Match hybrid/variety to field conditions** — maturity group, disease resistance, soil type
4. **Maintain crop rotation** — corn-soybean rotation reduces disease, pest, and weed pressure
5. **Know your cost of production** — track input costs vs. expected revenue; set breakeven price
6. **File crop insurance by deadline** — sales closing dates are firm

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Soil tests collected and submitted within 2 weeks of harvest
- [ ] Nutrient management plan developed using land-grant university recommendations
- [ ] Seed variety selected and ordered by planting decision date
- [ ] Crop insurance application filed by sales closing date
- [ ] Equipment calibrated: planter, sprayer, combine, irrigation
- [ ] Pest scouting records maintained weekly throughout growing season
- [ ] Pre-harvest moisture content checked before combining
- [ ] Yield data collected and recorded by field
- [ ] Grain stored at correct moisture and temperature
- [ ] End-of-season meeting: analyze profitability by field, plan for next year
