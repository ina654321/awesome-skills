# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Bypassing or removing PTO master shield** | 🔴 High | Required safety device — reinstall immediately |
| 2 | **Operating without ROPS and seatbelt** | 🔴 High | OSHA requires ROPS on tractors >20HP; wear belt always |
| 3 | **Skipping daily pre-operation inspection** | 🟡 Medium | Use checklist; catch problems before field failure |
| 4 | **Fueling hot engine** | 🔴 High | Let engine cool 5+ minutes before refueling |
| 5 | **Wearing loose clothing near PTO** | 🔴 High | Tuck in shirts, no dangling objects, tie back long hair |
| 6 | **Leaving engine running when dismounting** | 🟡 Medium | Shut down engine; engage parking brake |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Complete daily pre-op checklist** — document in operator log
2. **Know the safe slope limits** — 15° for 2WD tractors; 25°+ for 4WD on firm ground
3. **Clean chaff and debris** from engine bay daily during harvest — fire prevention
4. **Check and maintain fire extinguishers** — charge annually, recharge after use
5. **Lubricate per schedule** — reduces wear, prevents breakdown
6. **Never exceed rated PTO speed** — 540 or 1000 RPM per implement requirement
7. **Match implement to tractor horsepower** — PTO HP, not engine HP is the limit

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Pre-operation inspection completed and logged
- [ ] All safety shields in place: PTO, belts, pulleys
- [ ] ROPS installed and seatbelt functional
- [ ] Fire extinguisher mounted and charged
- [ ] Emergency shut-off location known and accessible
- [ ] First aid kit stocked and accessible
- [ ] Lights and SMV sign clean and visible
- [ ] Tire pressure verified
- [ ] Brake pedal lock engaged for road transport
- [ ] Hitch pin rating matches implement weight
