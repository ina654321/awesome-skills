# Boeing Safety & Quality Plan

## Background: The Crisis

### Alaska Airlines Flight 1282

**January 5, 2024**
- Boeing 737-9 MAX (new aircraft, delivered October 2023)
- Left mid-cabin door plug blew out at 16,000 feet
- Rapid decompression; no serious injuries
- Emergency landing in Portland, Oregon

**Root Cause (NTSB Investigation):**
- Four bolts securing door plug were **missing**
- Plug had been removed at Spirit AeroSystems for rivet rework
- Bolts not reinstalled; no documentation of removal
- Quality inspection failed to detect missing hardware

### FAA Response

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| Jan 6, 2024 | Grounded 171 Boeing 737-9 MAX aircraft |
| Jan 2024 | Halted 737 MAX production expansion |
| Feb 2024 | Administrator Whitaker directed comprehensive action plan |
| Feb 2024 | Increased inspector presence at Renton and Wichita |
| Mar 2024 | Audit found 33 of 89 product tests non-compliant |
| May 2024 | Boeing submitted Safety & Quality Plan |
| Jun 2024 | Whitaker visited Charleston factory |
| Dec 2024 | Follow-up visit; progress noted but more work needed |

### 1. Invest in Workforce Training

**Actions Taken:**

| Initiative | Status | Details |
|------------|--------|---------|
| **Training Enhancement** | ✅ Implemented | ~300 hours of new training material deployed |
| **Workplace Coaches** | ✅ Deployed | Peer trainers embedded on production lines |
| **Manufacturing Training Pause** | ✅ Completed | 6-week assessment and program redesign |
| **Proficiency Metrics** | 📊 Tracking | Share of employees proficient in core skills |

**Employee Proficiency KPI:**
- Measures: Core skill competency
- Target: 100% proficiency
- Review: Monthly

---

### 2. Simplify Plans and Processes

**Actions Taken:**

| Initiative | Status | Details |
|------------|--------|---------|
| **Build Plan Access** | ✅ Implemented | Reduced steps to access work instructions |
| **Quality Media Simplification** | 🔄 In Progress | Simplifying 400+ quality-related documents |
| **Management Floor Time** | ✅ Implemented | Fewer meetings; more time with mechanics |
| **FOD Prevention** | ✅ Deployed | Embedded management and metrics in critical areas |

**Process Improvements:**
- Removing redundancies and contradictions in command media
- Streamlining work instruction access
- Empowering frontline decision-making

---

### 3. Eliminate Defects

**Actions Taken:**

| Initiative | Status | Details |
|------------|--------|---------|
| **737 Fuselage Inspection** | ✅ Implemented | Quality inspection before shipment from Spirit |
| **Daily Compliance Sweeps** | ✅ Re-established | Regular factory floor compliance checks |
| **Work in Process System** | ✅ Deployed | New WIP system tracks and secures incomplete parts |
| **Work Instruction Adherence** | ✅ Implemented | Standard checks for mechanic/inspector proficiency |

**Key Performance Indicators:**

| KPI | Description | Target |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| **Notice of Escape (NoE) Rework** | Hours fixing non-conforming work | Reduce |
| **Supplier Shortages** | Daily shortages from suppliers | Reduce |
| **Rework Hours per Airplane** | Time spent on rework | Reduce |
| **Travelers at Rollout** | Unfinished jobs at factory exit | Reduce 75% |
| **Ticketing Performance** | Quality escapes before delivery | Reduce |

**Traveled Work Reduction:**
- 737 program: **75% reduction** at rollout since March 2024
- All programs: **60% average reduction**

## Spirit AeroSystems Acquisition

### Strategic Context

**June 30, 2024**: Boeing announced agreement to acquire Spirit AeroSystems

**Rationale:**
- Vertical integration of critical fuselage manufacturing
- Control over quality at source
- Reduction of supplier coordination complexity

**Key Facts:**
| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Acquisition Price** | $8.3B (including assumed debt) |
| **Spirit Revenue** | ~$6B annually |
| **Spirit Employees** | ~20,000 |
| **Key Products** | 737 fuselages, 787 forward fuselage, 777/767 components |
| **Expected Close** | Mid-2025 (pending regulatory approvals) |

**Regulatory Process:**
- FTC/DOJ antitrust review
- International regulatory approvals
- Customer notifications

## Cultural Transformation Under Ortberg

### CEO Kelly Ortberg's Priorities

**Statement on Safety Culture:**
> "Boeing has a tremendous and rich history as a leader and pioneer in our industry, and I'm committed to working together with the more than 170,000 dedicated employees of the company to continue that tradition, with safety and quality at the forefront."

### Key Changes

| Area | Before | After (Ortberg) |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| **CEO Location** | Chicago/Arlington | Seattle (factory proximity) |
| **Focus** | Financial returns | Safety & quality first |
| **Organization** | Decentralized | Operations-centered |
| **Union Relations** | Adversarial | New contract, collaboration |
| **Transparency** | Limited | Proactive regulator engagement |

### Bonuses Restructured

Ortberg implemented **uniform safety and quality metrics** for all employee bonuses:
- Replaced individual/business unit metrics
- Company-wide safety and quality KPIs
- Aligns all employees around common goals

## Long-Term Outlook

### Industry Context

Commercial aviation remains the **safest mode of transportation**, achieved through:
- Continuous learning from incidents
- Regulatory oversight evolution
- Industry-wide safety management systems

### Boeing's Path Forward

| Timeline | Milestone |
|----------|-----------|
| 2024-2025 | Safety plan execution; production stabilization |
| 2025-2026 | 737-7/737-10 certification; 777X entry into service |
| 2026-2028 | Cash flow positive; debt reduction |
| 2028+ | Next generation aircraft development |

✅ **Short-term (2025):**
- No quality escapes causing groundings
- Production rate targets achieved
- Employee engagement scores improved

✅ **Medium-term (2027):**
- All MAX variants certified and delivering
- 777X operational with customers
- Defense programs stabilized

✅ **Long-term (2030):**
- Safety culture restored to "Gold Standard"
- Market share recovered to 40-45%
- New aircraft program launched
