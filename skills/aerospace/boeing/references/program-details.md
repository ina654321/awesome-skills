# Boeing Program Details

## Commercial Airplanes

### 737 MAX Family

| Variant | Passengers | Range | Status | Notes |
|---------|------------|-------|--------|-------|
| **737-7** | 138-153 | 3,850 nm | Certification pending | Engine anti-ice system redesign required |
| **737-8** | 162-210 | 3,550 nm | In production | Most popular variant; 4,000+ orders |
| **737-9** | 178-220 | 3,550 nm | In production | Alaska Airlines door plug incident variant |
| **737-10** | 188-230 | 3,300 nm | Certification pending | Largest MAX; needs anti-ice fix |

**Production Status (2024-2025):**
- Target rate: 38 aircraft/month (reduced from pre-crisis 52/month)
- Backlog: 4,303 aircraft (firm orders)
- ~55 aircraft in inventory awaiting delivery (including ~40 for China)
- IAM 751 strike (Sept-Nov 2024) disrupted recovery

**Key Technical Specifications:**
- Engines: CFM International LEAP-1B
- Wingspan: 35.9m (117 ft 10 in) with split scimitar winglets
- Length: 35.6m (737-7) to 43.8m (737-10)
- Max Takeoff Weight: 80,286 kg (177,000 lb) for 737-10

### 787 Dreamliner Family

| Variant | Passengers | Range | Typical Use |
|---------|------------|-------|-------------|
| **787-8** | 242 | 7,305 nm | Long thin routes, 767 replacement |
| **787-9** | 290 | 7,530 nm | Core long-haul workhorse |
| **787-10** | 330 | 6,430 nm | High-density regional routes |

**Production Status:**
- Rate: 5/month (end 2024) → targeting 7/month (2025)
- ~25 pre-2023 aircraft in inventory requiring rework
- Supply chain constraint: Business class seats causing delivery delays
- Backlog: 726 aircraft

**Key Technical Specifications:**
- Engines: GEnx-1B or Rolls-Royce Trent 1000
- Fuselage: 50% composite materials (revolutionary at launch)
- Wingspan: 60.1m (197 ft) with raked wingtips
- Electrical system: 1.45 MW total generating capacity (no-bleed architecture)

### 777X Family

| Variant | Passengers | Range | Entry Into Service |
|---------|------------|-------|-------------------|
| **777-9** | 426 (2-class) | 7,285 nm | Delayed to 2026 |
| **777-8** | 395 (2-class) | 8,745 nm | Passenger: 2030+; Freighter: 2028 |

**Development Timeline:**
- Program launched: November 2013
- Original EIS: 2020
- Current EIS: 2026 (777-9)
- Flight testing: Resumed January 2025 (paused Aug-Dec 2024)

**Key Technical Specifications:**
- Engines: GE9X (world's most powerful jet engine: 105,000 lbf thrust)
- Wingspan: 71.8m (235 ft 5 in) extended; 64.8m (212 ft 9 in) folded
- **Folding wingtips**: First commercial application; enables Code E gate compatibility
- Composite wings: All-new carbon fiber design

**Program Losses:**
- $3.5B reach-forward loss recognized in 2024
- Additional $891M charge in Q4 2024 due to IAM 751 contract costs

---

### 767 Program

**Status: Sunset Phase**
- Commercial freighter production ends: Late 2026 or early 2027
- Remaining production: KC-46A Pegasus tanker (military derivative)
- Rate: ~3 aircraft/month combined

### F-15 Eagle II

**Status: Profitable Production**
- 2024 deliveries: 14 aircraft
- Key customers: Qatar (36), Saudi Arabia (84), USAF
- Capabilities: 4th++ generation fighter; advanced avionics
- Production: St. Louis, Missouri

---

### F/A-18 Super Hornet

**Status: End of Production**
- 2024 deliveries: 11 aircraft
- Production winding down
- US Navy transition to F-35C
- International customers: Australia, Kuwait

---

### P-8 Poseidon

**Status: Profitable Production**
- Based on 737-800 airframe
- Maritime patrol and anti-submarine warfare
- Customers: US Navy, UK, Australia, India, Norway, Germany, New Zealand, South Korea

---

### Space Programs

**Starliner (Commercial Crew):**
- Purpose: Transport astronauts to ISS
- Status: Development challenges; Boeing-funded improvements
- 2024 charge: $523M

**Space Launch System (SLS):**
- Boeing role: Core stage manufacturing
- Status: Production for Artemis program
- Challenge: NASA OIG concerns about cost/schedule performance

**Services Portfolio:**
- Parts supply and logistics
- Engineering and maintenance support
