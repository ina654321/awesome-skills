## § 8 Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: 737 MAX Status Inquiry

**User Input:**
"What's the current status of the 737 MAX program? Is it safe to fly now?"

**Expert Response:**

**Current Status Summary:**

The 737 MAX has returned to service in most jurisdictions following the 20-month grounding (March 2019 - November 2020). However, the program continues to navigate challenges:

**Safety Status:**
- ✅ **FAA re-certified** the MAX after comprehensive review
- ✅ **Software fixes** (MCAS redesign) and pilot training requirements implemented
- ✅ **Global regulators** (EASA, CAAC, others) have ungrounded the aircraft
- ⚠️ **Production quality** remains under intense FAA scrutiny after January 2024 Alaska Airlines door plug incident

**Current Challenges:**
- **Production capped** by FAA at levels below Boeing's targets
- **737-7 and 737-10 certification** delayed (engine anti-ice system issues)
- **~55 aircraft in inventory** (including ~40 for Chinese customers) awaiting delivery

**Production Recovery:**
- Target: 38 aircraft/month (vs. pre-crisis 52/month)
- IAM 751 strike (Sept-Nov 2024) disrupted production recovery
- New labor contract increases costs but provides stability

**The Bottom Line:**
The 737 MAX flying today has undergone the most scrutinized re-certification in aviation history. From a passenger perspective, it meets all regulatory safety standards. Boeing's challenge is rebuilding production quality and customer confidence while managing certification of the smaller 737-7 and larger 737-10 variants.

|--------|--------|--------|--------|

- Delivery delays frustrating airline customers

### Scenario 3: 787 Dreamliner Investment Analysis

**User Input:**
"Should an airline invest in the 787 Dreamliner for long-haul fleet renewal?"

**Expert Response:**

**787 Dreamliner Value Proposition (2024):**

**Aircraft Strengths:**
- **20% fuel efficiency** vs. 767/A330 it replaces
- **Composite fuselage** reduces corrosion, enables higher cabin humidity
- **Range flexibility**: 787-8 (7,305 nm) to 787-10 (6,430 nm)
- **Proven reliability**: 15+ years in service, mature systems
- **Strong resale value**: Market confidence in platform

**Current Production Status:**
- Rate: 5/month (end 2024) → 7/month (2025 target)
- **~25 pre-2023 aircraft in inventory** requiring rework before delivery
- Supply chain constraints on business class seats causing delays
- 726 aircraft backlog (strong demand)

**Investment Considerations:**

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Availability** | ⚠️ Delivery slots limited; expect delays |
| **Price** | Favorable due to Boeing's need for cash |
| **Support** | ✅ Global Services network mature |
| **Financing** | ✅ Strong lessor appetite for 787 |
| **Risk** | ⚠️ Boeing financial health adds counterparty risk |

**Recommendation:**

The 787 remains a **technically excellent aircraft** for long-haul operations. For airlines:

- ✅ **Proceed with orders** if delivery timeline acceptable
- ⚠️ **Negotiate protections** (performance guarantees, delay compensation)
- ✅ **Consider used market** — some 787-8/9 available from lessors
- ✅ **Evaluate A350 alternative** — Airbus competing aggressively

The aircraft is sound; the risk is Boeing's delivery execution, not the product itself.

### Scenario 4: Boeing Defense Programs Evaluation

**User Input:**
"What's happening with Boeing's defense business? Why are they losing money?"

**Expert Response:**

**Defense, Space & Security (BDS) Financials 2024:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Revenue | $23.9B |
| Operating Loss | ($5.4B) |
| Operating Margin | (22.6%) |

**Why the Losses? Fixed-Price Development Contracts**

BDS is hemorrhaging cash on **five major fixed-price development programs**:

| Program | 2024 Loss | Total Charges | Status |
|---------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| **KC-46A Pegasus** | $2.0B | $7B+ | Tanker delivered but deficiencies persist |
| **T-7A Red Hawk** | $1.8B | $2B+ | Trainer aircraft; development ongoing |
| **Commercial Crew (Starliner)** | $523M | $1.5B+ | Crewed flight test challenges |
| **VC-25B (Air Force One)** | $379M | $2B+ | Presidential aircraft; delayed |
| **MQ-25 Stingray** | $339M | $500M+ | UAV tanker for carriers |

**Root Cause:**
Boeing accepted **fixed-price contracts** during competitive bidding, underestimating development complexity. Unlike cost-plus contracts where government shares risk, Boeing bears all cost overruns.

**Stronger Programs:**
- **F-15 Eagle II**: Profitable, international demand
- **P-8 Poseidon**: Profitable, 737-based maritime patrol
- **Satellites**: Stable revenue but some challenges

**Strategic Response:**
- New CEO Ortberg has **restructured BDS leadership**
- Updated acquisition approaches with Air Force
- T-7A accelerated timeline to reduce carrying costs
- Future bids will reflect lessons learned

**Outlook:**
BDS remains strategically vital. The losses reflect **contract structure mistakes, not product quality**. Core production programs (F-15, P-8) remain profitable.

### Scenario 5: Boeing Stock Investment Analysis

**User Input:**
"Is Boeing stock a buy right now? What's the bull and bear case?"

**Expert Response:**

**Stock Overview (2024-2025):**
- Symbol: BA (NYSE)
- Market Cap: ~$140B
- 2024 Performance: Down ~30% from highs
- Dividend: Suspended during crisis

---

**🐂 BULL CASE:**

**1. Massive Backlog = Revenue Visibility**
- $521B total backlog (~7.8 years of revenue)
- 737 MAX and 787 sold out through decade
- Replacement cycle: 44,000 new aircraft needed by 2043

**2. Ortberg Turnaround Potential**
- Proven aerospace executive (Rockwell Collins → RTX)
- Engineering-first approach
- Based in Seattle — return to roots
- 2024 compensation: $18.4M (5 months) — aligned with performance

**3. Defense & Services Stability**
- Global Services: $3.6B operating profit (only profitable segment)
- Defense: $24B revenue base, stable demand
- Recurring revenue from 14,000+ aircraft in service

**4. Competitor Constraints**
- Airbus sold out; can't capture all Boeing share
- COMAC years from global competitiveness
- Duopoly pricing power intact long-term

**Price Target (Bulls):** $200-250/share

---

**🐻 BEAR CASE:**

**1. Cash Burn Continues**
- 2024 operating cash flow: -$12.1B
- Defense charges may continue
- Production recovery slower than hoped

**2. Certification Risk**
- 737-7/737-10 certification uncertain
- 777X delays extending
- FAA relationship still strained

**3. Labor Cost Inflation**
- IAM 751 contract: 25% raises over 4 years
- Non-union pay adjustments required
- Structural cost increase

**4. Competitive Erosion**
- Airbus gaining share; may not return
- COMAC in China (Boeing's ~25% of narrow-body market at risk)
- Airline customer frustration

**Price Target (Bears):** $120-140/share

---

**Investment Thesis:**

Boeing is a **turnaround story with binary outcomes**. The assets (backlog, technology, workforce) are world-class. The execution (production, quality, cash flow) has been poor.

**Risk Tolerance Required:** HIGH
**Time Horizon:** 3-5 years minimum
**Recommendation:** Speculative buy for patient investors; avoid for conservative portfolios
