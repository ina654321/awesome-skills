# General Dynamics Business Segment Details

## Aerospace (Gulfstream)

### Overview
Gulfstream Aerospace, a wholly-owned subsidiary of General Dynamics, is the world's leading manufacturer of large-cabin business jets. Based in Savannah, Georgia, Gulfstream has delivered more than 3,000 aircraft since 1958.

| **G650ER** | Ultra-long-range | 7,500 nm | Mach 0.925 | 19 | Production ended 2024 |
| **G600** | Long-range | 6,600 nm | Mach 0.925 | 19 | In production |

### Key Technologies

**Symmetry Flight Deck**
- Active sidestick controls (first in business aviation)
- Integrated touch-screen avionics
- Enhanced vision system (EVS)
- Predictive landing performance system
- Data concentration network

**Cabin Innovations**
- 100% fresh air (no recirculation)
- Low cabin altitude (2,840 ft at 41,000 ft)
- Panoramic oval windows (largest in industry)
- Quiet cabin technology
- High-speed Ka-band connectivity

### Jet Aviation

## Marine Systems

### Overview
General Dynamics is the U.S. Navy's premier builder of nuclear submarines and a major surface ship constructor. Marine Systems includes Electric Boat, Bath Iron Works, and NASSCO.

### Shipyards

**Electric Boat (Groton, CT & Quonset Point, RI)**
- Prime contractor for nuclear submarine construction
- 24,000+ employees
- 13% tonnage increase in 2025
- Virginia-class and Columbia-class programs

**Bath Iron Works (Bath, ME)**
- Builder of Arleigh Burke-class (DDG-51) destroyers
- Consistent ship-over-ship learning curve improvements
- Zumwalt-class (DDG-1000) lead yard

**NASSCO (San Diego, CA)**
- Auxiliary ship builder (T-AO, T-AKE, ESB)
- Commercial ship repair
- Positive schedule variance trends

### Major Programs

**Virginia-class Attack Submarines**
- Builder: Electric Boat (prime) + Newport News (teaming)
- Block V: Added Virginia Payload Module (+4 VLS tubes)
- Contract: $12.4B for 2 FY2024 submarines
- Status: Production ongoing

**Columbia-class SSBN**
- Mission: Replace Ohio-class ballistic missile submarines
- Ships: 12 planned (SSBN-826 through SSBN-837)
- First Boat: USS District of Columbia (SSBN-826)
  - 60% complete as of October 2025
  - Delivery: 2028
- Second Boat: USS Wisconsin (SSBN-827)
  - Delivery: End of decade
- Investment: $15.4B contract modification (March 2026)
- National Priority: #1 shipbuilding priority for U.S. Navy

**DDG-51 Arleigh Burke Destroyers**
- Builder: Bath Iron Works
- Flight III configuration with AN/SPY-6 radar
- Multi-year procurement contracts

**Auxiliary Ships (NASSCO)**
- John Lewis-class fleet oilers (T-AO)
- Expeditionary Sea Bases (ESB)
- Support vessels

### 2025 Performance
- **Revenue**: $16.7B (+16.6% YoY)
- **Operating Earnings**: $1.18B (+25.9% YoY)
- **Operating Margin**: 7.2% (improving)
- **Growth Driver**: Submarine production acceleration

## Combat Systems

### Overview
General Dynamics Combat Systems designs and manufactures land combat vehicles, weapons systems, and munitions for the U.S. military and allied nations.

### Major Business Units

**General Dynamics Land Systems (GDLS)**
- Headquarters: Sterling Heights, Michigan
- Production: Lima Army Tank Plant (Ohio)

**General Dynamics Ordnance and Tactical Systems (GD-OTS)**
- Munitions and advanced materials
- Armament systems

**European Land Systems**
- Piranha/Stryker vehicles
- Eagle tactical vehicles
- International programs

### Key Programs

**M1 Abrams Main Battle Tank**
- Current: M1A2 SEPv4 production
- Future: M1E3 next-generation tank
  - Hybrid-electric propulsion
  - 60-ton weight (down from 73+ tons)
  - Unmanned turret with autoloader
  - 3-person crew (vs. 4)
  - Integrated Active Protection System
  - 4 prototypes operational by summer 2026

**Stryker Family of Vehicles**
- 8x8 wheeled armored vehicle
- Multiple variants (ICV, MGS, MC, ATGM, etc.)
- Upgrades: 30mm Medium Caliber Weapon System (MCWS)
- International sales: Multiple NATO countries

**International Programs**
| Program | Customer | Value | Status |
|---------|----------|-------|--------|
| Eagle tactical vehicles | Germany | $4B+ | Awarded Q4 2025 |
| Bridges | Norway/UK | $600M | Awarded Q4 2025 |
| Light armored/logistics vehicles | Canada | $640M | Awarded Q4 2025 |
| Piranha vehicles | Multiple | Various | Ongoing |

### 2025 Performance
- **Revenue**: $9.2B (+2.8% YoY)
- **Operating Earnings**: $1.33B (+4.3% YoY)
