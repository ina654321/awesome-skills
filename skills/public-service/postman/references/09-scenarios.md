# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Threatening Dog

**User:** "A dog is loose in the yard and comes toward me aggressively. I've been bitten before. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Dog Encounter Protocol:**
>
> | Response | Action |
> |---------|--------|
> | **Immediate** | Stop — do not run, back away slowly |
> | **Do not pet** | Never attempt to pet a strange dog |
> | **Use vehicle** | Retreat to your vehicle if approached |
> | **Do not use mace** | USPS policy prohibits macing animals in most cases |
>
> **Documentation:**
> 1. Complete PS Form 1765 (Hazardous Condition Report)
> 2. Report to supervisor immediately
> 3. Note address and description of dog
> 4. Continue delivery if possible, different approach
>
> **If Bitten:**
> - Seek immediate medical attention
> - Report to supervisor and animal control
> - Complete incident report
> - Consider filing police report
>
> **Customer Notification:**
> - Leave PS Form 3849 explaining situation
> - Do not blame customer — say "animal present"
> - Supervisor will contact customer about securing animal

## 9.2 Mail for Deceased Person

**User:** "The person at this address has died. What do I do with their mail?"

**Expert:**
> **Deceased Resident Mail Handling:**
>
> | Situation | Action |
> |----------|--------|
> | **New mail for deceased** | Write "DECEASED" and return to sender |
> | **Personal mail to deceased** | Return with endorsement "DECEASED — NOT DELIVERABLE AS ADDRESSED" |
> | **Advertisements/junk mail** | Deliver — no endorsement needed |
> | **Priority/Certified** | Return with "DECEASED — NOT DELIVERABLE AS ADDRESSED" |
>
> **Process:**
> 1. Verify person is deceased through family or mail characteristics
> 2. Don't discuss with neighbors — confidentiality
> 3. Forward if forwarding order on file
> 4. If estate in process: deliver to estate representative
> 5. After 90 days with no forwarding: RTS
>
> **Do NOT:**
> - Open mail — federal crime
> - Discuss with neighbors
> - Assume mail belongs to new resident

## 9.3 Suspicious Package

**User:** "I'm delivering mail and notice a package that seems suspicious — unusual weight, no return address, possible odor. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Suspicious Package Protocol:**
>
> | Indicator | Action |
> |----------|--------|
> | **Unusual odor** | Do not open, do not shake |
> | **Excessive tape** | Could indicate tampering |
> | **Lopsided/uneven weight** | Suspicious |
> | **No return address** | Flag but may be legitimate |
> | **Leaking** | Do not handle, evacuate area |
>
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 1. **DO NOT DELIVER** — leave package at office
> 2. Do not open or smell contents
> 3. Call supervisor immediately
> 4. If opened inadvertently: leave area, wash hands
> 5. Call USPS Inspection Service: 1-877-876-2455
> 6. Call 911 if threat imminent
>
> **911 vs. Inspection Service:**
> - 911: If you believe there is an immediate threat
> - Inspection Service: Routine suspicious mail investigation
> - Supervisor: Internal reporting chain
