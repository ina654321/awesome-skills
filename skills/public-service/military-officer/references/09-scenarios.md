# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Fraternization Situation

**User:** "A junior enlisted soldier is in a relationship with a company commander. The commander's peer has reported it. What are the issues and options?"

**Expert:**
> **Fraternization Analysis:**
>
> | Issue | Analysis |
> |-------|----------|
> | **UCMJ Article 134** | Fraternization is prohibited under general article |
> | **Rank disparity** | Company commander (O-3) vs. junior enlisted (E-1 to E-4) |
> | **Command relationship** | Direct command relationship creates presumption of prejudicing good order |
>
> **Key Questions:**
> - Is the relationship actually fraternization or social?
> - Does commander have actual command authority over the soldier?
> - Are there塔 sexual assault or harassment undertones?
>
> **Options:**
> 1. **Administrative counseling**: Commander ends relationship, documented counseling
> 2. **Non-judicial punishment**: Article 15 for violation of regulation
> 3. **Court-martial**: If misconduct is serious enough
> 4. **Article 15 vs. Court-Martial**: Court-martial provides more rights
>
> **Commander's Rights:**
> - Must be properly advised of rights before questioning
> - Article 15 is not required — can elect court-martial
> - Command climate and unit readiness are command concerns
> - Equal application regardless of rank

## 9.2 Sexual Assault Response

**User:** "A soldier reports to you that she was assaulted by another soldier at a unit function. The alleged perpetrator is in your unit. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Sexual Assault Response Protocol:**
>
> | Step | Action | Timeline |
> |------|--------|----------|
> | 1 | **Immediate safety** | Ensure victim is safe, not in same unit as perpetrator |
> | 2 | **Victim advocacy** | Connect with SARC immediately (24/7) |
> | 3 | **Restricted reporting** | Explain option — victim's choice |
> | 4 | **Unrestricted reporting** | Explain — triggers investigation |
> | 5 | **Documentation** | Record only what victim voluntarily provides |
> | 6 | **Do NOT investigate yourself** | Refer to CID/NCIS immediately |
>
> **Key Rules:**
> - SHARP policy requires victim-centered response
> - Commander must notify SARC within 24 hours
> - Cannot take statement from victim as commander
> - Victim has right to advocate throughout process
> - Retaliation against victim is separate UCMJ violation

## 9.3 Article 15 Procedure

**User:** "A soldier was found asleep at his post. This is his first offense. The company commander wants to handle it with Article 15. Walk through the process."

**Expert:**
> **Article 15 Procedure:**
>
> | Step | Action |
> |------|--------|
> | 1 | **Notice to soldier**: Written notice of suspected violation |
> | 2 | **Rights advisement**: Right to demand trial by court-martial |
> | 3 | **Commander's determination**: Consider if NJP appropriate |
> | 4 | **Presentation of evidence**: Show soldier the evidence |
> | 5 | **Soldier's opportunity to respond**: Written statement or appearance |
> | 6 | **Commander's decision**: Impose NJP or refer/Dismiss |
> | 7 | **Written memorandum**: Document decision and punishment |
>
> **Limits on NJP:**
> - Forfeiture: Cannot exceed 1/2 pay for 2 months
> - Restriction: Cannot exceed 2 weeks
> - Confinement: Cannot exceed 1 week
> - Hard labor: Cannot exceed 2 weeks
> - Reduction: E-7+ cannot be reduced more than one grade
>
> **Appeals:** Soldier has 5 days to appeal to next commander
