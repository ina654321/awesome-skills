# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Multi-Unit Building

**User:** "I'm enumerating a large apartment building. Several residents won't answer their doors. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Multi-Unit Enumeration Protocol:**
>
> | Situation | Action |
> |-----------|--------|
> | **No answer at unit** | Leave NC-1 form, return at different times |
> | **Language barrier** | Use translated materials, call language hotline |
> | **Refusal** | Explain census purpose, call supervisor for refusal letter |
> | **Gate/buzzer blocked** | Try different times, ask manager for access |
> | **Vacant unit** | Check for signs of occupancy, mark appropriately |
>
> **Key Rules:**
> - Must attempt at least 6 times at different times/days
> - Can use proxy responses from neighbors or landlords
> - Never guess — if you can't determine, mark unknown
> - All attempts must be documented in system
>
> **Proxy Respondent Protocol:**
> - Ask neighbors: "Do you know who lives at [address]?"
> - Ask landlord/manager for tenant list
> - Document who provided proxy information
> - Do NOT accept landlord's statement as final without attempting contact

## 9.2 Group Quarters Enumeration

**User:** "How do I enumerate a college dormitory during the school year?"

**Expert:**
> **Group Quarters Enumeration:**
>
> | Step | Action |
> |------|--------|
> | 1 | Contact dormitory administrator for roster |
> | 2 | Verify against address listing |
> | 3 | Determine if students count at dorm or at home |
> | 4 | Interview each resident or obtain roster |
> | 5 | Handle students absent but with belongings as present |
>
> **Usual Residence Rule:**
> - College students count at their college dorm address
> - Exception: if living with parents during term, count with parents
> - Armed forces personnel on base = count at base
> - inmates at correctional facilities = count at facility
>
> **Special Cases:**
> - International students: count at dorm address
> - Commuter students: count at parents' home
> - Students in off-campus housing: count at off-campus address

## 9.3 Confidentiality Challenge

**User:** "A respondent asks why we need this information and whether it will be shared with ICE or the IRS. How do I respond?"

**Expert:**
> **Confidentiality Response Protocol:**
>
> **Required Response:**
> "Under Title 13 of the U.S. Code, your responses are protected by federal law. The Census Bureau cannot share your information with immigration enforcement agencies (ICE), tax enforcement agencies (IRS), or any other government agencies. Your individual responses cannot be used against you by any court or government agency. Only aggregate statistical data is released."
>
> | Source | Protection |
> |--------|-------------|
> | **13 U.S.C. §9** | Responses cannot be disclosed |
> | **13 U.S.C. §8** | False statements are a federal crime |
> | **13 U.S.C. §221** | Refusal to answer is a federal crime |
>
> **If Respondent Still Refuses:**
> 1. Remain professional and patient
> 2. Explain census importance for representation and funding
> 3. Offer to schedule another visit
> 4. Call supervisor if persistent refusal
> 5. Do NOT threaten or coerce — follow supervisor guidance
