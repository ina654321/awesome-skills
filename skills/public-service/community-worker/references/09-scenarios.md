# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Family Facing Eviction

**User:** "A single mother with two children is facing eviction in 5 days. She's working but can't afford the past-due rent. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Emergency Housing Response:**
>
> | Timeline | Action |
> |----------|--------|
> | **Day 1** | Screen for eviction cause — is it landlord or tenant issue? |
> | **Day 1** | Check for eviction court records — has she been to court yet? |
> | **Day 1** | Apply for emergency rental assistance (ERA) programs |
> | **Day 1** | Contact local legal aid for eviction defense |
> | **Day 2-3** | Emergency shelter as backup if assistance falls through |
> | **Day 2-3** | Coordinate with school social worker for children |
> | **Day 4-5** | Trial/hearing date — is there time for legal defense? |
>
> **Key Referrals:**
> - Emergency Rental Assistance (Treasury ERA1/ERA2)
> - Local legal aid organization
> - Domestic violence shelter if applicable
> - SNAP/food stamps for food budget relief
> - Medicaid/CHIP for children
>
> **Housing Court Process:**
> 1. Get copies of all court papers
> 2. Tenant has right to defend even after default judgment
> 3. If judgment entered, explore vacatur motion
> 4. If eviction order issued, seek emergency stay

## 9.2 Child Welfare Involvement

**User:** "A client reveals their children have been left home alone while they work. The kids are 8 and 10. What are your options?"

**Expert:**
> **Child Safety Assessment:**
>
> | Factor | Analysis |
> |--------|----------|
> | **Ages** | 8 and 10 — state laws vary on minimum age for self-care |
> | **Duration** | How many hours? During what times? |
> | **Safety** | Are there emergency contacts? Phone access? |
> | **Children's wishes** | How do the children feel about being alone? |
>
> **Possible Responses:**
> 1. **Support, not punish**: Connect to affordable childcare subsidies
> 2. **Mandatory reporting**: If child safety is at risk, reporting may be required
> 3. **Differential response**: Many states have alternative tracks for neglect vs. danger
>
> **Best Interest of Children:**
> - Keep family together when safe
> - Connect to childcare resources (CCDF subsidies)
> - Provide parent education on age-appropriate self-care
> - Follow state mandatory reporting laws (varies by state)

## 9.3 Veteran in Crisis

**User:** "A homeless veteran presents with mental health issues and substance use. He's resistant to treatment. What approach do you take?"

**Expert:**
> **Housing First Approach for Veterans:**
>
> | Step | Action |
> |------|--------|
> | 1 | **Stabilize housing first** | No preconditions for treatment |
> | 2 | **Connect to VA services** | VA Greater Los Angeles: (310) 478-3711 |
> | 3 | **Apply for HUD-VASH** | Permanent supportive housing voucher |
> | 4 | **Engage peer support** | Veteran peer specialists |
> | 5 | **SUD treatment on demand** | VA has same-day admission for SUD |
>
> **Key Principles:**
> - Do not require sobriety as condition of housing
> - Trauma-informed approach — meet the veteran where they are
> - Motivational interviewing over confrontation
> - Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH) model
> - Coordinated Entry system prioritization
>
> **Crisis Resources:**
> - Veterans Crisis Line: 988, then Press 1
> - VA Homeless Programs: va.gov/homeless
> - SSVF (Supportive Services for Veteran Families)
