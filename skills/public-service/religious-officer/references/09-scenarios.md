# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Conscientious Objector Request

**User:** "A soldier in your unit has come to you saying they want to file as a conscientious objector. They've never expressed this before. How do you help?"

**Expert:**
> **Conscientious Objector Support:**
>
> | Step | Action |
> |------|--------|
> | 1 | **Verify** | Is this a sincere change of belief, not avoidance of duty? |
> | 2 | **Explain process** | Application goes through chain of command |
> | 3 | **Document** | Write down what was said, date, your assessment |
> | 4 | **Support** | Can write character letter if asked |
> | 5 | **Remain neutral** | Cannot advocate for or against approval |
>
> **Key Legal Standards:**
> - Belief must be deeply held and sincere
> - Cannot be based solely on political disagreement with war
> - Can develop after enlistment (in some cases)
> - Application: DD Form 1860 (for Army)
> - Standard: OCR §75 (in-service CO applicants)
>
> **Chaplain's Role:**
> - Confidential pastoral counseling
> - Can be source statement writer
> - Cannot campaign for or against
> - Can inform of process
> - Should assess for mental health concerns

## 9.2 Diversity of Faith Request

**User:** "A Muslim service member asks for halal meal options. The base DFAC doesn't have them. A Christian soldier asks for a Lenten fasting accommodation. What's your role?"

**Expert:**
> **Religious Accommodation Request:**
>
> | Request | Chaplain's Role |
> |---------|----------------|
> | **Halal meals** | Coordinate with logistics, S4, DFAC manager |
> | **Kosher meals** | MRE kosher available through supply |
> | **Lenten fasting** | Work with leadership on meal timing |
> | **Prayer time** | Coordinate with unit for 5 daily prayers |
> | **Headscarf/hijab** | Coordinate uniform accommodation request |
>
> **DoD Directive 1300.17 Process:**
> 1. Soldier formally requests accommodation in writing
> 2. Commander evaluates military necessity
> 3. Least restrictive means must be considered
> 4. Negative determination can be appealed
> 5. Army: consult with chaplain, SJA
>
> **Chaplain Support:**
> - Can advocate for policy change
> - Can connect service members with religious resources
> - Can facilitate interfaith dialogue
> - Can help draft accommodation requests
> - Can educate leadership on religious diversity

## 9.3 Suicide Risk

**User:** "A service member comes to your office after a rough conversation with their spouse. They mention they 'don't see a way out.' What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Suicide Risk Response:**
>
> | Assessment | Response |
> |-----------|----------|
> | **Direct statements** | "I've been thinking about not being here anymore" |
> | **Behavioral changes** | Withdrawal, giving away possessions |
> | **Prior attempts** | History increases risk |
> | **Means access** | Firearms, medications |
> | **Protective factors** | Religion, family, future plans |
>
> **Immediate Steps:**
> 1. **Ask directly**: "Are you thinking about hurting yourself?"
> 2. **Assess means**: "Do you have access to weapons/medications?"
> 3. **Stay with them**: Don't leave alone
> 4. **Contact Behavioral Health**: Military OneSource 800-342-9647
> 5. **Notify chain of command**: Safety is paramount — confidentiality may be breached
>
> **Mandatory Reporting:**
> - Confidentiality does not apply if there is imminent danger
> - Must break confidentiality to save life
> - Document what was disclosed
> - Follow up after referral
