# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Contaminated Sharps Found

**User:** "While collecting garbage, you find a needle sticking out of a plastic bag. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Sharps Injury Prevention Protocol:**
>
> | Step | Action |
> |------|--------|
> | 1 | **Do NOT reach in** | Assume all sharps are contaminated |
> | 2 | **Use grabber/tongs** | Never touch directly |
> | 3 | **Place in sharps container** | Hard-sided, puncture-resistant |
> | 4 | **Report immediately** | Document exposure incident |
> | 5 | **Post-exposure protocol** | Supervisor must know immediately |
> | 6 | **Medical evaluation** | Within 2 hours if possible |
>
> **Post-Exposure Steps:**
> 1. Wash area thoroughly with soap and water
> 2. Report to supervisor immediately
> 3. Go to occupational health/ER for evaluation
> 4. Hepatitis B vaccination status check
> 5. HIV/Hepatitis C baseline testing
> 6. Follow-up testing schedule
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Never reach into containers
> - Always use mechanical lift equipment
> - Report recurring sharps in garbage problem areas

## 9.2 Illegal Dumping

**User:** "You discover a large pile of construction debris dumped illegally on a vacant lot. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Illegal Dumping Response:**
>
> | Action | Details |
> |--------|---------|
> | **Document** | Photo, location, date, description of materials |
> | **Report** | Contact supervisor and code enforcement |
> | **Preserve** | Don't disturb evidence |
> | **Trace** | Look for permits, bills, identifying information |
> | **Cleanup** | Only after investigator clears it |
>
> **Investigation Lead:**
> - Types of materials (contractor debris?)
> - Address or landmarks nearby
> - Tire tracks for vehicle identification
> - Overturned debris with date stamps
> - Flyers or business cards
>
> **Legal Framework:**
> - State environmental laws (illegal disposal)
> - Local ordinances (illegal dumping)
> - EPA Clean Water Act if near waterways
> - Cost recovery from responsible party
> - Fines and criminal penalties for willful violations

## 9.3 Blocked Street Due to Collection

**User:** "A resident has blocked the street with their cars and you can't get the truck through. The resident refuses to move their cars. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Blocked Route Resolution:**
>
> | Approach | Action |
> |---------|--------|
> | **First attempt** | Politely explain the situation, request cooperation |
> | **Second attempt** | Call supervisor to help communicate |
> | **Third attempt** | Code enforcement/non-emergency police |
> | **Final option** | Skip and RTS for those addresses |
>
> **Legal Authority:**
> - Municipal code gives collection vehicles right-of-way
> - Vehicles blocking sanitation trucks can be towed
> - Supervisor must be involved before enforcement
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Post notices before collection days
> - Work with HOA on parking restrictions
> - Alternate side parking during collection
> - Community outreach on collection schedules
>
> **If Escalated:**
> 1. Document all attempts to resolve
> 2. Have supervisor contact resident
> 3. Call non-emergency police line
> 4. Complete route without those addresses if necessary
> 5. Return after situation resolved
