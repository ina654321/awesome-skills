# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Resident Falls

**User:** "During morning care, an elderly resident stands up and starts to fall. You are alone. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Response:**
>
> ```
> Step 1: Stay with resident
> - Do NOT try to hold them up alone
> - Guide them down gently toward floor
> - Support their head if possible
>
> Step 2: Call for help
> - Call for assistance
> - Activate call light
> - Stay with resident
>
> Step 3: After resident is down
> - Assess for injury
> - Do NOT move resident
> - Wait for nurse assessment
> - Keep resident calm
> ```
>
> **After the Fall:**
> ```
> 1. Notify nurse immediately
> 2. Help with assessment as directed
> 3. Complete incident report
> 4. Document in chart
> 5. Help with repositioning
> ```
>
> **Prevention Next Time:**
> - Always have help when ambulating unsteady residents
> - Use gait belt
> - Ensure proper footwear
> - Call for assistance early

## 9.2 Resident with Confusion

**User:** "A resident with dementia is agitated and trying to leave the unit. They keep saying they need to 'go home to cook dinner for their family.' What is the appropriate response?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Understanding the Behavior:**
> ```
> Possible triggers:
> - Unmet need (hunger, thirst, toileting)
> - Fatigue
> - Overstimulation
> - Time disorientation
> - Unfamiliar environment
> ```
>
> **Response Approach:**
> ```
> DO:
> - Remain calm and patient
> - Use gentle touch
> - Validate their feelings
> - Distract with pleasant activity
> - Offer food/drink/toileting
> - Redirect to safe area
>
> DON'T:
> - Argue or correct
> - Use restraints
> - Yell or show frustration
> - Lock them in room
> - Physically restrain
> ```
>
> **Communication Example:**
> ```
> "It sounds like you have a lot to do at home. Let's sit here
> and have a snack first, then we can figure out the plan together."
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Note behavior and triggers
> - What interventions worked
> - Time and duration
> - Report to nurse
