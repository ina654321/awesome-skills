# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Informed Consent Deviation

**User:** "During a screening visit, the subject signed the consent form but we realized 2 days later that the wrong version was used (old version vs. current approved version). What should we do?"

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
>
> This is a protocol deviation involving the informed consent process.
>
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 1. Do NOT re-consent with correct version yet
> 2. Report to sponsor/CRO immediately
> 3. Assess if subject was already enrolled/randomized
> 4. Document the deviation with dates and circumstances
>
> **Subject Status:**
> - If subject not yet enrolled: Re-consent with correct version, proceed with screening
> - If subject already enrolled: Determine if they received any study procedures under wrong consent
>
> **Regulatory Consideration:**
> - IRB/IEC notification required
> - Protocol deviation form completed
> - Sponsor guidance on whether to continue subject
>
> **Prevention:**
> - Always verify consent version date against IRB approval letter
> - Use version control log at site
> - Double-check before any consent procedure

## 9.2 SAE Unblinding Request

**User:** "A subject in a blinded cardiovascular outcomes trial was hospitalized with chest pain. The local PI is asking whether to unblind the subject's study medication to guide treatment decisions in the ER. What is the process?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Key Principle:** Unblinding should only occur when necessary for subject safety and required by protocol.
>
> **Assessment Steps:**
> 1. Does the treating physician need to know the study drug for immediate treatment?
> 2. What does the protocol say about emergency unblinding?
> 3. Is the subject's condition life-threatening?
>
> **Process:**
> ```
> 1. Contact sponsor 24-hour hotline
> 2. Provide subject ID, site number, event description
> 3. Sponsor confirms unblinding is warranted
> 4. Unblinded treatment assignment disclosed
> 5. Document unblinding in source and CRFs
> 6. IRB/IEC notification within 24-48 hours
> ```
>
> **Note:** Many modern CV outcome trials have independent DSMB that can unblind treatment assignment without sponsor knowledge.
>
> **Post-Unblinding:**
> - Subject continues in study per protocol (unless contraindicated)
> - All endpoints collected regardless of unblinding
> - Statistical analysis pre-specified for unblinded subjects
