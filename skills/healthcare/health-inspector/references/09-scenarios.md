# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Foodborne Illness Investigation

**User:** "Multiple patrons from a restaurant are presenting to local ERs with vomiting and diarrhea. As a health inspector, what do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Step 1: Initial Response (Same Day)**
> ```
> 1. Coordinate with health department epidemiology
> 2. Interview initial cases
> 3. Establish case definition
> 4. Identify common restaurant exposure
> 5. Dispatch inspector to restaurant
> ```
>
> **Step 2: Restaurant Inspection**
> ```
> Priority Assessment:
> ├── Food handler illness history
> ├── Current illness among staff
> ├── Temperature control of foods
> ├── Cross-contamination risks
> ├── Sanitation status
> └── Kitchen hygiene practices
> ```
>
> **Step 3: Environmental Assessment**
> - Collect food samples for testing
> - Review temperature logs
> - Check pest control records
> - Assess refrigeration units
>
> **Step 4: Interventions**
> ```
> Immediate Actions:
> ├── Issue violation notices for critical items
> ├── Require exclusion of ill food handlers
> ├── Mandate proper food handling training
> ├── Consider temporary closure if high risk
> └── Environmental swab collection
> ```
>
> **Step 5: Follow-up**
> - Monitor lab results
> - Interview additional cases
> - Identify outbreak source
> - Issue public health advisory if needed

## 9.2 Swimming Pool Closure

**User:** "A hotel pool fails inspection with chlorine at 0.2 ppm (below 1.0 ppm minimum) and pH at 8.5 (above 8.2 maximum). What enforcement action is appropriate?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Violations Identified:**
> ```
> Critical Violations:
> ├── Free chlorine below minimum (0.2 vs 1.0 ppm)
> ├── pH above maximum (8.5 vs 8.2)
> └── Combined chlorine concern
> ```
>
> **Risk Assessment:**
> - Low chlorine + high pH = poor disinfection
> - Risk of waterborne illness transmission
> - Immediate public health concern
>
> **Recommended Action:**
> ```
> Immediate:
> ├── Close pool until corrected
> ├── Issue Notice of Violation
> ├── Require superchlorination before reopening
> └── Re-inspection required before reopening
> ```
>
> **Reopening Requirements:**
> ```
> 1. Chlorine: ≥1.0 ppm (or 7.5 with combined chlorine)
> 2. pH: 7.2-7.8
> 3. Water clarity: Main drain visible
> 4. Filtration system functioning
> 5. Staff re-trained on chemical handling
> 6. Documented maintenance log
> ```
