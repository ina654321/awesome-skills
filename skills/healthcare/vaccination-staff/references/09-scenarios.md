# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Immunocompromised Patient

**User:** "A 45-year-old on methotrexate for rheumatoid arthritis comes for flu shot. Can you give the vaccine? What recommendations?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Methotrexate and Vaccination:**
> ```
> Immunosuppressive Therapy Considerations:
> ├── Can give inactivated vaccines
> ├── Live vaccines contraindicated
> ├── Timing of vaccine around immunosuppression
> └── May have reduced response
>
> Flu Shot Decision:
> └── INACTIVATED influenza vaccine: YES
>     - Give at any time during immunosuppression
>     - Consider higher dose or adjuvanted flu vaccine
>     - Response may be blunted
>
> Live Vaccines: NO
> └── MMR, Varicella, LAIV, Yellow fever: Contraindicated
> ```
>
> **Recommendations:**
> ```
> 1. Administer inactivated flu vaccine
> 2. Document current medications
> 3. Consider PPSV23 if not given
> 4. Discuss timing with rheumatologist
> 5. Recommend household contacts get vaccinated
> 6. Counsel about reduced response
> 7. Consider revaccination after immunosuppression
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Document medication (methotrexate)
> - Note immunosuppression status
> - Document counseling provided
> - Note any coordination with specialist

## 9.2 Allergy to Vaccine Component

**User:** "A patient states they are allergic to eggs and asks if they can receive the flu vaccine. What is the appropriate response?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Egg Allergy and Flu Vaccine:**
> ```
> Current ACIP Guidance:
> ├── Severe egg allergy (anaphylaxis): Can receive any licensed flu vaccine
> ├── Mild egg allergy: Any flu vaccine
> └── Observation: 30 minutes for severe allergy
>
> Misconception:
> └── Modern flu vaccines contain minimal egg protein
>     - Recombinant (RIV) and cell-based (ccIIV4): No egg
>     - Egg-based: Trace amounts only
> ```
>
> **Assessment Steps:**
> ```
> 1. Determine severity of egg allergy:
>    ├── Anaphylaxis (severe): Documented, requires epinephrine
>    ├── Mild reaction: Hives only, no systemic
>    └── Tolerates baked egg: Can receive any vaccine
>
> 2. Select appropriate vaccine:
>    ├── Egg allergy (any severity): RIV4 or ccIIV4 preferred
>    └── No preference for those with no egg allergy
> ```
>
> **Appropriate Response:**
> ```
> "Egg allergy does not prevent you from getting the flu vaccine.
> We can give you a vaccine that is safe for people with egg
> allergies. You will be observed for 30 minutes after
> the vaccination as a precaution."
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Document allergy history
> - Note vaccine selected and rationale
> - Document observation time
