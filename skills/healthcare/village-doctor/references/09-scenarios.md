# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Pediatric Fever

**User:** "A mother brings in her 3-year-old with fever of 102°F for 2 days. No localizing symptoms, slightly fussy, drinking well. What is your approach?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Assessment:**
> ```
> Fever without Source (0-36 months):
> ├── Likely viral URI
> ├── Possible UTI
> ├── Consider pneumonia
> └── Meningitis less likely with no meningeal signs
>
> Red Flags Absent:
> ├── No lethargy
> ├── No petechiae
> ├── No bulging fontanelle
> ├── No respiratory distress
> └── Drinking well
> ```
>
> **Approach:**
> ```
> Step 1: Further Assessment
> ├── Complete exam including:
> │   ├── Ears, nose, throat
> │   ├── Lungs
> │   ├── Skin for source
> │   └── Neck for adenopathy
> ├── Consider:
> │   ├── Urinalysis (especially if <2 years)
> │   └── Rapid strep/flu if indicated
>
> Step 2: Management
> ├── Viral syndrome most likely
> ├── Supportive care
> ├── Antipyretics for comfort
> ├── Encourage fluids
>
> Step 3: Return Precautions
> ├── Persistent fever >3 days
> ├── Not improving
> ├── New symptoms
> ├── Dehydration signs
> └── Change in behavior
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Comprehensive exam documented
> - Red flags absent
> - Parental education provided
> - Return precautions clear

## 9.2 Chest Pain in Rural Setting

**User:** "55-year-old male farmer presents with chest pain, diaphoresis, and shortness of breath. You are 2 hours from the nearest hospital. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Emergency Assessment:**
> ```
> Likely ACS/MI:
> ├── Chest pain, diaphoresis, SOB
> ├── Risk factors likely (farmer, rural)
> └── Time-critical emergency
> ```
>
> **Immediate Actions:**
> ```
> 1. Call 911/Emergency Transport
> ├── Do not wait
> ├── Activate regional EMS
> ├── Consider air transport if available
> └── Distance matters - get help moving
>
> 2. While Waiting:
> ├── ABCs
> ├── Position comfortable
> ├── Oxygen if SpO2 <94%
> ├── Aspirin 324mg (if no allergy/contraindication)
> ├── Nitroglycerin if BP >90 systolic
> ├── IV access if possible
> ├── Monitor if available
> ├── Obtain history quickly
> └── Keep patient calm
>
> 3. Prepare for Transfer:
> ├── Document all findings
> ├── Call receiving ED
> ├── Provide report
> ├── Send records with patient
> └── Notify family
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Time of symptom onset
> - Initial presentation
> - Interventions given
> - Transport activated
> - Report to receiving facility
