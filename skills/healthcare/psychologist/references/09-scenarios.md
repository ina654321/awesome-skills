# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Depression Assessment Scenario

**User:** "A 35-year-old female presents with 6 weeks of low mood, anhedonia, sleep disturbance, and difficulty concentrating at work. PHQ-9 score is 15. What approach should I take?"

**Expert:**
> **Clinical Analysis:**
>
> Based on the presentation and PHQ-9 score (moderately severe depression):
>
> 1. **Safety Assessment First**: Inquire about suicidal ideation, plan, intent, and access to means. Use Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) for standardization.
>
> 2. **Medical Comorbidity**: Order CBC, CMP, TSH, vitamin B12, folate to rule out organic causes. Review current medications for depression-inducing agents (steroids, interferons).
>
> 3. **Treatment Planning**: PHQ-9 of 15 indicates moderately severe depression. First-line options:
>    - CBT (12-16 sessions) with Grade A evidence
>    - SSRI (sertraline, escitalopram) with psychiatric consultation
>    - Combination therapy for moderate-severe cases
>
> 4. **Follow-up**: Reassess PHQ-9 at 4-6 weeks. If <50% improvement, consider augmentation or specialist referral.

## 9.2 Anxiety Disorder with Comorbid Substance Use

**User:** "I have a patient with GAD-7 of 18 and positive AUDIT screen (score 14). How do I approach this?"

**Expert:**
> **Dual Diagnosis Approach:**
>
> This presentation requires integrated treatment addressing both conditions:
>
> 1. **Stage-wise Treatment**: Address acute withdrawal/safety first, then stabilization, then rehabilitation. Anxiety symptoms often improve with sobriety.
>
> 2. **Pharmacotherapy Caution**: SSRIs are first-line for GAD but require cautious initiation given alcohol use. Avoid benzodiazepines due to abuse potential.
>
> 3. **Referral**: Connect to substance use disorder (SUD) specialist for contingency management, motivational interviewing, and potential AUD medication (naltrexone, acamprosate).
>
> 4. **Integrated Monitoring**: Use AUDIT quarterly; GAD-7 monthly; coordinate with addiction medicine.

## 9.3 PTSD with Treatment-Resistant Symptoms

**User:** "Patient with PTSD (PCL-5 score 55) has failed sertraline and 12 sessions of CBT. What are the options?"

**Expert:**
> **Specialized PTSD Interventions:**
>
> 1. **Trauma-Focused Therapy Alternatives**:
>    - **EMDR** (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing): 8-12 sessions
>    - **Prolonged Exposure (PE)**: Modified version if prior CBT failure
>    - **Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)**: 12 sessions, written assignments
>
> 2. **Medication Augmentation**: Prazosin for nightmares (2-6 mg qHS); consider atypical antipsychotic augmentation.
>
> 3. **Specialist Referral**: Refer to trauma specialist or PTSD specialty clinic.
>
> 4. **Adjunctive Services**: Case management for functional impairment; vocational rehabilitation; peer support.
>
> 5. **Safety Planning**: Ensure crisis resources documented; assess for self-medication with substances.
