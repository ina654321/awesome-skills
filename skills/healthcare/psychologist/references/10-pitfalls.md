# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Clinical Assessment Pitfalls

| # | Pitfall | Severity | Prevention |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | Skipping suicide risk assessment | 🔴 Critical | Use C-SSRS at every encounter; document explicitly |
| 2 | Anchoring on initial diagnosis | 🔴 High | Reconsider at each session; watch for evolution |
| 3 | Missing medical causes of psychiatric symptoms | 🔴 High | Rule out thyroid, anemia, medications, substances |
| 4 | Cultural bias in assessment | 🟡 Medium | Use cultural formulation; consult interpreter services |
| 5 | Over-reliance on medication | 🟡 Medium | Combine with psychotherapy when possible |
| 6 | Premature closure (single diagnosis) | 🟡 Medium | Always consider comorbidities; PTSD often missed |
| 7 | Ignoring trauma history | 🔴 High | Screen systematically; trauma-informed care required |

## 10.2 Treatment Planning Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Description | Correct Approach |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|
| Cookbook therapy | One-size-fits-all manual application | Adapt to patient needs and learning style |
| Premature termination | Ending treatment before goals met | Establish clear termination criteria; plan ahead |
| Dual relationship | Social/financial entanglement with patient | Maintain professional boundaries strictly |
| Power imbalance abuse | Exploiting therapeutic relationship | Adhere to ethical guidelines; supervision/consultation |
| Unvalidated techniques | Using non-evidence-based methods | Use empirically supported treatments |

## 10.3 Documentation Pitfalls

- **Using vague language**: "Patient appears sad" → "Affect is constricted; reports feeling 'down' for 3 weeks"
- **Failing to document risk assessment**: Liability risk; must be explicit
- **Incomplete MSE**: Must address appearance, behavior, speech, mood, affect, thought, perception, cognition, insight, judgment
- **Not updating diagnoses**: Treatment plan must reflect current clinical picture

## 10.4 Best Practice Checklist

- [ ] Suicide risk assessed and documented at every encounter
- [ ] Medical causes considered before psychiatric diagnosis
- [ ] Evidence-based treatment selected based on presenting problem
- [ ] Cultural factors incorporated into assessment and treatment
- [ ] Informed consent obtained and documented
- [ ] Treatment plan updated at least annually
- [ ] Confidentiality limits (mandated reporting) communicated
- [ ] Appropriate referral made when scope exceeded

## 10.5 Ethical Considerations

1. **Informed Consent**: Explain treatment options, risks, benefits, alternatives, confidentiality limits
2. **Mandatory Reporting**: Child abuse, elder abuse, suicidal/homicidal ideation with plan/intent
3. **Scope of Practice**: Refer when specialized care needed
4. **Documentation**: Protect confidentiality; follow HIPAA guidelines
5. **Dual Relationships**: Avoid non-professional relationships with current/former patients
