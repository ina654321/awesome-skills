# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Chest Pain with Diaphoresis

**User:** "65-year-old male with crushing chest pain radiating to left arm, diaphoretic, nauseated. Onset 30 minutes ago. BP 150/90, HR 88, SpO2 97%. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Acute Coronary Syndrome Management:**
>
> 1. **Immediate Assessment**: Focused on life threats; prepare for arrest
>
> 2. **Treatment**:
>    - Aspirin 324mg chewed immediately
>    - Nitroglycerin 0.4mg SL if BP >90 systolic (repeat q5min x3)
>    - Morphine 2-4mg IV if pain unrelieved (caution: hypotension)
>    - Oxygen if SpO2 <94%
>    - P2Y12 inhibitor: clopidogrel 600mg or ticagrelor 180mg
>
> 3. **Diagnostic Priority**:
>    - Obtain 12-lead ECG within 5 minutes
>    - Look for STEMI criteria: ST elevation ≥1mm in 2 contiguous leads
>
> 4. **If STEMI**:
>    - Activate cath lab immediately
>    - Consider paramedic receipt for direct PCI
>    - Target door-to-balloon <90 minutes
>
> 5. **Transport**: ALS unit to PCI-capable facility; continuous monitoring
>
> 6. **Communication**: Early notification to ED; prehospital STEMI alert

## 9.2 Unconscious Diabetic Patient

**User:** "45-year-old female found unresponsive at grocery store. Blood glucose reads 'LOW' on glucometer. What are the priorities?"

**Expert:**
> **Hypoglycemic Emergency:**
>
> 1. **Primary Assessment**: ABCs; GCS; blood glucose confirmed
>
> 2. **Critical Intervention**:
>    - Dextrose 50% (D50W): 25g (50mL) IV push
>    - If no IV access: Glucagon 1mg IM/SQ/IN
>    - If no D50: Consider 10% dextrose infusion
>
> 3. **Expected Response**:
>    - Patient typically improves within 2-5 minutes of glucose
>    - Reassess level of consciousness
>    - Check glucose again: target >70 mg/dL
>
> 4. **Transport Decision**:
>    - If responsive and glucose >100: May refuse transport if competent
>    - Consider transport if on oral hypoglycemics (risk of rebound)
>    - Evaluate for cause: missed meal, insulin error, alcoholism
>
> 5. **Documentation**: Fingerstick value, response to treatment, refusals

## 9.3 Multi-System Trauma

**User:** "MVA rollover, unrestrained driver, found 50 feet from vehicle. GCS 11, BP 88/60, HR 110. Pupils equal but sluggish. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Trauma Resuscitation:**
>
> 1. **TRIAGE**: Meets trauma criteria → direct to Level I Trauma Center
>
> 2. **C-spine**: Manual stabilization; collar if needed
>
> 3. **Airway**: GCS 11 = borderline; prepare for definitive airway (GCS ≤8)
>
> 4. **Breathing**: High-flow O2; look for asymmetrical breath sounds
>
> 5. **Circulation**:
>    - Hypotensive with tachycardia = hemorrhagic shock
>    - Two large bore IVs; normal saline bolus
>    - TXA: 1g if <3 hours from injury
>    - Control external bleeding; consider pelvic binder
>
> 6. **Disability**: GCS 11 = moderate brain injury; monitor for decline
>
> 7. **Transport**: Lights and sirens; least time to definitive care
>
> 8. **Notification**: Trauma team activation; early surgical consult
