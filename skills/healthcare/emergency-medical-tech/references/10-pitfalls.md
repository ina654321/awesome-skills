# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Assessment Pitfalls

| # | Pitfall | Severity | Prevention |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | Missing C-spine injury | 🔴 High | High-risk mechanism = assume injury until cleared |
| 2 | Inadequate airway assessment | 🔴 High | GCS ≤8 needs definitive airway |
| 3 | Missing tension pneumothorax | 🔴 High | JVD, absent breath sounds, hypotension |
| 4 | Under-triage | 🔴 High | When in doubt, transport to higher level |
| 5 | Hyperventilating patient | 🟡 Medium | Target 10-12 breaths/min; excessive ventilation worsens outcomes |

## 10.2 Treatment Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Correct Approach |
|--------------|------------------|
| Delayed defibrillation | Charge during CPR; minimize interruptions |
| Nitro without BP check | Hold if SBP <90; avoid in inferior STEMI |
| Morphine before aspirin | Give aspirin first; morphine may mask pain |
| Prolonged scene time | Load-and-go for time-sensitive conditions |
| Too much IV fluid | 1-2L NS for trauma; reassess for pulmonary edema |

## 10.3 Communication Failures

| Pitfall | Consequence | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| No radio notification | Unprepared receiving team | Early prehospital notification |
| Vague report | Missing resources | Use structured format (MIVT, VOMIT) |
| Not calling for backup | Overwhelmed by scene | Request additional units early |
| Patient refused transport | Adverse outcome | Document capacity, risks explained |

## 10.4 Documentation Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Correct Approach |
|---------|------------------|
| Vague descriptions | "Severe, crushing substernal chest pain" not "patient complains of chest pain" |
| Missing times | Document exact times for interventions, vitals |
| Not documenting refusals | Capacity assessment, risks explained, signed forms |
| Incomplete assessment | Full set of vitals, GCS, O2 sat documented |

## 10.5 Quality Assurance Checklist

- [ ] Scene safety confirmed
- [ ] BSI precautions followed
- [ ] Primary assessment completed
- [ ] Time-sensitive conditions identified
- [ ] Appropriate interventions performed
- [ ] Vital signs documented (before/after interventions)
- [ ] Patient response to treatment assessed
- [ ] Transport decision appropriate
- [ ] Receiving facility notified
- [ ] Complete patient care report submitted

## 10.6 Safety Priorities

1. **Scene Safety First**: Never become a patient yourself
2. **Airway Always**: Cannot survive without oxygen
3. **Defibrillation Priority**: VF/pVT = immediate shock
4. **Transport for Time-Sensitive Conditions**: STEMI, stroke, major trauma
5. **Request Help Early**: No shame in calling for backup
