# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Contrast Reaction During Procedure

**User:** "During a hepatic artery embolization, the patient develops facial flushing, itching, and mild hypotension. What is your response?"

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> - Symptoms suggest allergic reaction (likely moderate)
>
> **Immediate Actions:**
> ```
> Step 1: Stop procedure
> - Pause contrast injection
> - Alert physician
>
> Step 2: Assess severity
> - Mild: flushing, itching, urticaria
> - Moderate: hypotension, dyspnea, wheezing
> - Severe: anaphylaxis, cardiovascular collapse
> ```
>
> **If Moderate Reaction:**
> ```
> 1. Position patient (supine, legs elevated)
> 2. 100% O2 via mask
> 3. IV normal saline wide open
> 4. Diphenhydramine 50mg IV
> 5. Methylprednisolone 125mg IV
> 6. Reassess vitals
> ```
>
> **Post-Reaction:**
> - Document reaction timing and symptoms
> - Update allergy list
> - Schedule recovery monitoring
> - Document premedication for future studies

## 9.2 High Radiation Dose Alert

**User:** "During a complex EVAR procedure, the fluoro time has exceeded 45 minutes and dose indicators are showing high values. What actions should be taken?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Dose Concerns:**
> ```
> - Cumulative fluoro time high
> - Skin dose approaching threshold
> - Patient size may affect dose
> ```
>
> **Actions:**
> ```
> Immediate:
> ├── Notify attending physician of dose
> ├── Document dose in chart
> └── Continue with caution
>
> Optimization Strategies:
> ├── Use last-image-hold extensively
> ├── Minimize cine runs
> ├── Collimation maximal
> ├── Consider alternate imaging
> └── Take breaks if possible
>
> Post-Procedure:
> ├── Document skin dose estimate
> ├── Note on requisition for future
> ├── Consider follow-up skin exam
> └── QC review for technique improvement
> ```
