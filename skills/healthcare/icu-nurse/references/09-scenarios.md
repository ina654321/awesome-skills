# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Ventilator Deterioration

**User:** "Patient on AC ventilator suddenly becomes agitated, high-pressure alarms, and desaturating to 85%. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Rapid Assessment and Intervention:**
>
> 1. **Immediate Response**:
>    - Check patient: is they in distress, diaphoretic, altered?
>    - Suction airway: check for secretions, obstruction
>    - Hand-bag patient: assess ease of ventilation
>
> 2. **Common Causes**:
>    - Disconnection or kink in circuit
>    - Mucus plug or secretion retention
>    - Patient-ventilator dyssynchrony
>    - Pneumothorax
>    - Equipment failure
>
> 3. **Interventions**:
>    - Verify ETT placement (listen bilaterally)
>    - Increase FiO2 to 100%; hand-ventilate
>    - Suction with inline suction catheter
>    - Check for spontaneous breaths; consider sedation bolus
>    - Obtain STAT chest X-ray
>
> 4. **Communication**: Notify RT and MD immediately with SBAR
>
> 5. **Documentation**: Times, vital signs, interventions, response

## 9.2 Vasopressor Weaning

**User:** "Patient on norepinephrine 0.15 mcg/kg/min for septic shock. BP stable at 110/65. How do I approach weaning?"

**Expert:**
> **Vasopressor Weaning Protocol:**
>
> 1. **Stability Criteria**:
>    - MAP ≥65 mmHg for ≥6 hours
>    - Adequate perfusion (lactate trending down, urine output >0.5 mL/kg/hr)
>    - No ongoing shock state
>    - No acute cardiac ischemia
>
> 2. **Weaning Approach**:
>    - Decrease by 0.02-0.05 mcg/kg/min every 1-2 hours
>    - Maintain MAP ≥65 throughout
>    - If BP drops >10%, hold and reassess
>
> 3. **Wean to Off**:
>    - When at 0.05 mcg/kg/min, assess for readiness to discontinue
>    - May transition to vasopressin if prolonged support needed
>    - Ensure adequate volume status before stopping
>
> 4. **Monitoring**: q5-15 min vitals during wean; watch for rebound hypotension

## 9.3 ICU Delirium Management

**User:** "ICU patient now positive for delirium (CAM-ICU positive). How do I manage this?"

**Expert:**
> **Delirium Management Protocol:**
>
> 1. **Identify Type**: Hyperactive (agitated, pulling lines) vs. Hypoactive (lethargic, withdrawn)
>
> 2. **Remove Reversible Causes** (ESCAPE):
>    - **E**lectrolytes: Check Na, Ca, Mg, glucose
>    - **S**leep: Implement sleep protocol; minimize nighttime interventions
>    - **C**hange: Avoidant polypharmacy; review deliriogenic meds (anticholinergics, benzodiazepines)
>    - **A**cute illness: Rule out infection, ischemia
>    - **P**ain: Assess with CPOT; treat adequately
>    - **E**nvironment: Reorient patient; glasses/hearing aids; family presence
>
> 3. **Pharmacologic** (if severe agitation/aggression):
>    - First-line: Dexmedetomidine or low-dose haloperidol
>    - Avoid benzodiazepines (worsen delirium except alcohol withdrawal)
>
> 4. **Family Engagement**: Encourage family presence; reorientation techniques
>
> 5. **Daily Reassessment**: CAM-ICU qshift; track trajectory
