## § 7 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Operating Margin Improvement

**Context**: Hospital operating margin declining to 1.5%; need improvement to 4%.

**Analysis**:
1. **Revenue Opportunities** (+2%):
   - Coding accuracy improvement: $2M
   - Denial reduction: $1.5M
   - Charge capture optimization: $1M

2. **Cost Reduction** (-3%):
   - Supply chain standardization: $3M
   - Labor productivity improvement: $4M
   - Pharmacy formulary optimization: $1M

3. **Implementation**:
   - Revenue cycle: CDI program expansion
   - Supply chain: Value analysis committee
   - Labor: acuity-based staffing, overtime reduction

**Timeline**: 12-month plan
**Projected Outcome**: 4.2% operating margin
**Risk**: Staff turnover, service disruption
**Mitigation**: Phased implementation, change management

### Scenario 2: Patient Safety Initiative

**Context**: Hospital-acquired infections (CLABSI) above national benchmark.

**Intervention**: Central Line Bundle Implementation
1. **Bundle Components**:
   - Hand hygiene compliance
   - Maximal barrier precautions
   - Chlorhexidine skin antisepsis
   - Optimal catheter site selection
   - Daily review of line necessity

2. **Implementation**:
   - Staff education (100% RNs, MDs)
   - Audit tool development
   - Unit-based champions
   - Dashboard for compliance

3. **Measurement**:
   - Process: Bundle compliance (target > 95%)
   - Outcome: CLABSI rate per 1,000 line-days
   - Balance: Staff satisfaction, time burden

**Results**:
- Month 0: 3.5 CLABSIs per 1,000 line-days
- Month 6: 1.2 CLABSIs per 1,000 line-days
- Bundle compliance: 96%
- Cost savings: $500K (estimated)

### Scenario 3: Service Line Expansion

**Context**: Market analysis indicates opportunity for cardiovascular service line.

**Strategic Assessment**:
1. **Market Analysis**:
   - Service area: 500,000 population
   - CV disease prevalence: 8%
   - Current market share: 15%
   - Competitor capabilities: Limited PCI, no TAVR

2. **Investment Plan**:
   - Cath lab expansion: $5M
   - TAVR program: $3M
   - Electrophysiology lab: $4M
   - Recruitment: 2 interventionalists, 1 EP

3. **Financial Projection**:
   - Year 1 revenue: $15M
   - Year 3 revenue: $35M
   - Break-even: Month 18
   - 5-year NPV: $25M

4. **Implementation**:
   - Certificate of need application
   - Physician recruitment
   - Facility construction
   - Program launch

**Outcome**: Market share increased to 35%; regional CV referral center

### Scenario 4: Labor Management During Crisis

**Context**: Pandemic surge requires immediate staffing adjustments.

**Immediate Response**:
1. **Surge Capacity**:
   - Cancel elective procedures
   - Convert units to ICU
   - Deploy float pool, agency staff
   - Implement crisis standards of care

2. **Staffing Strategies**:
   - Cross-training: Medical-surgical to ICU
   - Retired nurse recall
   - Student nurse deployment (appropriate scope)
   - Regional resource sharing

3. **Support Measures**:
   - Childcare support
   - Hotel accommodations
   - Mental health resources
   - Hazard pay consideration

4. **Sustainability**:
   - Weekly staffing assessments
   - Staff well-being monitoring
   - Burnout prevention programs
   - Exit interview analysis

**Outcome**: Maintained patient care; 85% staff retention post-surge

### Scenario 5: Quality Measure Improvement

**Context**: CMS Star Rating at 2 stars; need improvement to 4 stars.

**Gap Analysis**:
1. **Mortality**: Worse than national rate
2. **Safety**: Falls with injury above benchmark
3. **Readmissions**: 30-day readmit rate 16%
4. **Patient Experience**: HCAHPS below 50th percentile
5. **Timeliness**: ED wait times > 90th percentile

**Intervention Strategy**:
1. **Mortality**: Code sepsis protocol, rapid response enhancement
2. **Safety**: Hourly rounding, fall prevention bundle
3. **Readmissions**: Care transitions program, post-discharge calls
4. **Experience**: Communication training, quiet at night initiative
5. **Timeliness": Fast track for low-acuity, provider in triage

**Results (12 months)**:
- Mortality: Improved to better than national rate
- Safety: Falls with injury reduced 40%
- Readmissions: Reduced to 13%
- Experience: 65th percentile HCAHPS
- Star rating: 4 stars
