# Johnson & Johnson Engineering Pitfalls

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Regulatory Missteps

#### ❌ Inadequate Design Controls
**Mistake:** Starting development without comprehensive design inputs.

**Consequences:**
- Costly redesign late in development
- Regulatory rejection
- Schedule delays
- Budget overruns

**Prevention:**
- Complete design planning before development
- Document all user needs and requirements
- Establish traceability matrix early

#### ❌ Underestimating Regulatory Timelines
**Mistake:** Assuming FDA review will be quick based on competitor experience.

**Consequences:**
- Launch delays
- Revenue shortfall
- Competitive disadvantage

**Prevention:**
- Use FDA Q-Submission program for feedback
- Build conservative timelines
- Prepare comprehensive submissions

### 2. Quality Issues

#### ❌ Insufficient Risk Management
**Mistake:** Treating risk management as a checkbox exercise.

**Consequences:**
- Unidentified hazards
- Post-market issues
- Recalls
- Patient harm

**Prevention:**
- Integrate risk management throughout development
- Regular risk review meetings
- Update risk file with new information

#### ❌ Validation Shortcuts
**Mistake:** Reducing validation scope to meet deadlines.

**Consequences:**
- Regulatory findings
- Product quality issues
- Customer complaints

**Prevention:**
- Follow GAMP 5 methodology
- Document all validation activities
- Independent quality review

### 3. Manufacturing Challenges

#### ❌ Technology Transfer Issues
**Mistake:** Insufficient planning for scale-up from R&D to manufacturing.

**Consequences:**
- Process failures
- Batch rejections
- Supply shortages

**Prevention:**
- Early manufacturing involvement
- Process characterization studies
- Formal technology transfer protocol

#### ❌ Supplier Management
**Mistake:** Single-source critical components without qualification backup.

**Consequences:**
- Supply disruptions
- Quality variability
- Regulatory scrutiny

**Prevention:**
- Dual-source critical materials
- Regular supplier audits
- Continuous monitoring

### 4. Cultural Pitfalls

#### ❌ Ignoring Our Credo
**Mistake:** Prioritizing financial targets over patient safety.

**Consequences:**
- Reputational damage
- Regulatory action
- Employee disengagement

**Prevention:**
- Credo-based decision making
- Speak-up culture
- Ethics & compliance training

#### ❌ Siloed Decision Making
**Mistake:** Engineering decisions without cross-functional input.

**Consequences:**
- Manufacturing issues
- Commercial challenges
- Regulatory rejection

**Prevention:**
- Integrated product teams
- Stage-gate reviews
- Cross-functional collaboration

## Warning Signs

| Red Flag | Action Required |
|----------|-----------------|
| "We can fix it in the next version" | Stop and address now |
| "The regulator won't notice" | Ethical violation - escalate |
| "We've always done it this way" | Challenge assumptions |
| "Testing is just paperwork" | Quality culture issue |
| "Schedule is more important than quality" | Credo violation |
