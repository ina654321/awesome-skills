## 7.2 Drug Interaction Severity Classification

| Severity | Definition | Action Required |
|----------|------------|-----------------|
| Contraindicated | Do not use together | Avoid combination |
| Major | Potentially dangerous | Usually avoid; monitor closely if necessary |
| Moderate | May interact; adjust dose/timing | May need to separate administration |
| Minor | Minimal clinical effect | Monitor only |

## 7.3 CYP450 Enzyme Reference

| Enzyme | Substrates | Inhibitors | Inducers |
|--------|------------|------------|----------|
| CYP3A4 | Midazolam, simvastatin, clarithromycin | Erythromycin, ketoconazole, grapefruit | Rifampin, carbamazepine, phenytoin |
| CYP2C9 | Warfarin, phenytoin, NSAIDs | Fluconazole, amiodarone | Rifampin |
| CYP2C19 | Omeprazole, clopidogrel, diazepam | Omeprazole, fluoxetine | Rifampin, carbamazepine |
| CYP2D6 | Codeine, tramadol, metoprolol, fluoxetine | Bupropion, quinidine | None significant |
| CYP1A2 | Theophylline, caffeine, olanzapine | Fluvoxamine, ciprofloxacin | Smoking, rifampin |

## 7.4 Therapeutic Drug Monitoring Reference

| Drug | Target Level | Timing | Toxicity |
|------|-------------|--------|----------|
| Vancomycin | AUC 400-600 mg·h/L (trough 10-15 for serious infection) | Peak/trough or AUC | Nephrotoxicity |
| Aminoglycosides | Trough <1 mg/L; peak varies by drug | Trough before dose 3+ | Nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity |
| Digoxin | 0.5-2 ng/mL | 6-12h post-dose | Arrhythmias, toxicity |
| Phenytoin | 10-20 mcg/mL | Trough | Nystagmus, ataxia |
| Lithium | 0.6-1.2 mEq/L | 12h post-dose | Tremor, confusion |
