# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Multiple Drug Interactions

**User:** "Patient on warfarin is being started on fluconazole for candidemia. What are the considerations?"

**Expert:**
> **Drug Interaction Analysis:**
>
> 1. **Interaction Mechanism**: Fluconazole is a strong CYP2C9 inhibitor → increases warfarin levels → increased bleeding risk
>
> 2. **Clinical Significance**: Major interaction; INR can increase 2-4 fold
>
> 3. **Management Options**:
>    - Reduce warfarin dose by 25-50% and monitor INR every 2-3 days
>    - Alternatively, switch to LMWH bridge if high thrombotic risk
>    - Consider alternative antifungal (echinocandin) if feasible
>
> 4. **Monitoring Plan**:
>    - Check INR within 2-3 days of starting fluconazole
>    - Target INR may need to be temporarily lowered (e.g., 2-2.5 vs. 2.5-3.5)
>    - Watch for signs of bleeding (hematuria, melena, ecchymosis)
>
> 5. **Duration**: Effect persists 5-7 days after fluconazole discontinued

## 9.2 Renal Dose Adjustment

**User:** "72-year-old female with eGFR 25 mL/min needs pain management. She's on metformin for diabetes."

**Expert:**
> **Renal Dose Adjustment:**
>
> 1. **Immediate Concerns**:
>    - Hold metformin (contraindicated at eGFR <30 in many guidelines)
>    - Risk of lactic acidosis in renal impairment
>
> 2. **Analgesic Selection**:
>    - **Preferred**: Acetaminophen up to 3g/day (avoid if liver disease)
>    - **Second-line**: Tramadol (reduce dose; active metabolite accumulates)
>    - **Avoid NSAIDs**: High risk of nephrotoxicity, GI bleeding
>    - **Opioids**: Use lowest effective dose; fentanyl, hydromorphone safer than morphine (active metabolites)
>
> 3. **Gabapentin/Pregabalin**: Requires dose reduction at eGFR <60
>
> 4. **Diabetes Management**: Consider SGLT2 inhibitor hold; may need insulin adjustment
>
> 5. **Follow-up**: Monitor renal function weekly; adjust as needed

## 9.3 High-Alert Medication Management

**User:** "Patient on insulin pump being admitted for hip surgery. How should we manage her insulin?"

**Expert:**
> **Insulin Management in Hospital:**
>
> 1. **Transition**: Discontinue pump; convert to scheduled basal-bolus regimen
>    - Basal: Glargine at patient's total daily dose (TDD) minus prandial
>    - Prandial: Rapid-acting insulin (aspart, lispro) 10-15% of TDD per meal
>
> 2. **Correction Doses**: Use sensitivity factor to calculate correction insulin
>    - Correction = (blood glucose - target) / correction factor
>
> 3. **Monitoring**:
>    - Check blood glucose before meals and at bedtime
>    - Target: 140-180 mg/dL for most inpatients
>    - Tighter control only for select patients with endocrinology consult
>
> 4. **Sick Day Rules**: Hold pump if altered mental status; use IV insulin protocol if DKA
>
> 5. **Discharge Planning**: Ensure patient knows how to resume/manage pump; endocrinology consult if needed
