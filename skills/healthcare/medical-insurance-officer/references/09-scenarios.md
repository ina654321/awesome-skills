# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Inpatient Level of Care Denial

**User:** "A payer has denied our request for continued inpatient hospitalization, saying the patient could be treated as outpatient. The patient has CHF with EF 25%, hypoxia, and needs IV diuresis. How should we appeal?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Denial Reason Assessment:**
> - Payer claims patient doesn't meet inpatient criteria
> - Patient has objective clinical findings
>
> **Clinical Documentation Review:**
> ```
> Supporting Medical Necessity:
> ├── EF 25% (severe systolic dysfunction)
> ├── Hypoxia requiring O2 supplementation
> ├── IV diuresis (cannot be managed outpatient)
> ├── BNP significantly elevated
> ├── Weight gain indicating volume overload
> ├── Active troponin elevation
> └── Requires daily monitoring
> ```
>
> **Appeal Strategy:**
> ```
> Step 1: Gather Supporting Documentation
> ├── Admission H&P with severity
> ├── Daily progress notes
> ├── Nursing assessments
> ├── Lab trends (BNP, creatinine, electrolytes)
> ├── Echocardiogram report
> └── Medication administration record
>
> Step 2: Write Appeal Letter
> - Include Milliman or InterQual criteria
> - Document why outpatient not appropriate
> - Reference clinical guidelines
> - Include peer-to-peer request
>
> Step 3: Expedited Review Request
> - Request same-day peer review
> - Have physician-to-physician call
> - Document conversation
> ```
>
> **Outcome Expectations:**
> - High probability of overturn with good documentation
> - If denied again, escalate to independent review

## 9.2 MRI Prior Authorization Denial

**User:** "We ordered an MRI with contrast for suspected meniscus tear. The payer denied saying 'MRI not authorized for joint pain - try conservative treatment first.' Patient had 6 weeks of PT without improvement. How do we proceed?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Coverage Policy Review:**
> - Most payers require conservative care before advanced imaging
> - Patient has completed 6 weeks of PT
> - Clinical exam suggests internal derangement
>
> **Appeal Preparation:**
> ```
> Required Documentation:
> ├── PT records showing failed conservative care
> ├── Physical exam findings (positive McMurray, joint line tenderness)
> ├── Plain radiographs (ruling out fracture/arthritis)
> ├── Duration of symptoms (>6 weeks)
> └── Clinical rationale for MRI vs other imaging
> ```
>
> **Appeal Strategy:**
> ```
> 1. Write appeal letter emphasizing:
>    - Failed conservative treatment
>    - Physical exam suggesting meniscal pathology
>    - MRI needed to plan surgical intervention
>    - Non-contrast option not appropriate for meniscus
>
> 2. Request peer-to-peer review
>    - Have orthopedic surgeon speak directly
>    - Explain surgical planning needs
>
> 3. Consider alternative
>    - Diagnostic arthroscopy may be higher cost
>    - Point out cost-effectiveness of MRI first
> ```
