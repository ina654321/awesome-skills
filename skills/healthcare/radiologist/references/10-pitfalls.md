# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Interpretation Pitfalls

| # | Pitfall | Severity | Prevention |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | Satisfaction of search | 🔴 High | Systematic review; force yourself to consider alternatives |
| 2 | Anchoring on clinical history | 🔴 High | Review images without clinical info first |
| 3 | Missing subtle findings | 🟡 Medium | Structured search pattern every time |
| 4 | Over-calling artifacts as pathology | 🟡 Medium | Know common artifact patterns (beam hardening, motion) |
| 5 | Ignoring incidental findings | 🟡 Medium | Use incidental finding management frameworks |
| 6 | Contrast extravasation missed | 🔴 High | Check injection site on post-contrast images |
| 7 | Wrong imaging protocol comparison | 🟡 Medium | Verify same protocol/phase when comparing |

## 10.2 Reporting Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Correct Approach |
|--------------|------------------|
| "No acute cardiopulmonary abnormality" | Be specific: "No acute pulmonary embolism, pneumothorax, or consolidation" |
| Vague descriptors | Use standardized lexicon: "hypoechoic" not "darker than expected" |
| No recommendations | Always include actionable next steps |
| "Clinical correlation recommended" without specifics | Specify what clinical correlation is needed |
| Forgetting priors | Always check PACS for prior studies |

## 10.3 Communication Failures

| Failure Mode | Consequence | Mitigation |
|--------------|-------------|------------|
| Verbal order not documented | Critical finding delay | Document time/recipient for all verbal communications |
| "Call if abnormal" without specifics | Communication breakdown | Use structured handoff (SBAR) |
| Assuming results reviewed | Delayed treatment | Follow-up system for critical results |
| Wrong provider contacted | Wrong person acting on results | Verify identity and role |

## 10.4 Contrast Safety Pitfalls

- **Not checking eGFR**: Always verify renal function before iodinated contrast
- **Missing allergy premedication**: Document protocol for prior contrast reactions
- **Extravasation misdiagnosis**: Extravasation can mimic hemorrhage on CT
- **Gadolinium NSF risk**: Avoid linear gadolinium agents in eGFR <30

## 10.5 Quality Assurance Checklist

- [ ] Patient identification verified (2 identifiers)
- [ ] Clinical indication reviewed
- [ ] Prior imaging compared
- [ ] All series/images reviewed
- [ ] Measurements documented
- [ ] Standardized lexicon used
- [ ] Impression clear and actionable
- [ ] Recommendations time-specific
- [ ] Critical findings communicated properly
- [ ] Report signed and attested

## 10.6 Radiation Safety Principles

- **ALARA**: As Low As Reasonably Achievable
- **Justification**: Every exam must have clinical indication
- **Optimization**: Use lowest dose for diagnostic quality
- **Pediatric adjustment**: Use weight-based protocols; reduce kVp
