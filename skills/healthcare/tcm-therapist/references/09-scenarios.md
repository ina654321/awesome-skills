## 9.1 Chronic Pain Patient

**User:** "55-year-old female with chronic low back pain for 3 years. MRI shows mild degenerative changes. NSAIDs provide partial relief. What TCM approach would you recommend?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **TCM Pattern Differentiation:**
> ```
> Likely Patterns:
> ├── Kidney deficiency (chronic, low back, fatigue)
> ├── Liver qi stagnation (emotional stress)
> ├── Blood stasis (fixed, sharp pain)
> └── Damp-cold accumulation (weather sensitivity)
>
> Contributing Factors:
> ├── Sedentary lifestyle
> ├── Emotional stress
> ├── Cold environment
> └── Aging
> ```
>
> **Comprehensive Treatment Plan:**
> ```
> Acupuncture:
> ├── Primary points: UB23, UB40, KD3, DU4
> ├── Local: Ahshi points, UB25
> ├── Adjunctive: ST36, SP6
> ├── Reducing technique for stagnation
> ├── Tonifying for kidney deficiency
> └── Weekly x 8-12 treatments
>
> Herbal Formula:
> ├── Base: Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang
> ├── Modifications: +桃仁, 红花 for blood stasis
> ├── Consider: 金匮肾气丸 for kidney yang deficiency
> └── Granules or decoction
>
> Lifestyle Recommendations:
> ├── Gentle exercise (tai chi, walking)
> ├── Warm applications
> ├── Avoid cold foods
> ├── Stress management
> └── Proper posture
> ```

## 9.2 Insomnia Case

**User:** "42-year-old male with difficulty sleeping for 6 months. Trouble falling asleep and waking at 3am. Works stressful job. Tongue is red with thin coating, pulse is thin and rapid. What is your TCM assessment and treatment?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Pattern Identification:**
> ```
> Primary Pattern: Heart and Kidney Yin Deficiency
> ├── Heart Shen disturbed (3am waking)
> ├── Kidney yin deficiency (thin, rapid pulse)
> ├── Red tongue (yin deficiency heat)
> ├── Thin coating (deficiency pattern)
>
> Contributing Patterns:
> ├── Liver qi stagnation (stress, work)
> └── Heart fire (red tongue, rapid pulse)
> ```
>
> **Treatment Plan:**
> ```
> Acupuncture:
> ├── Heart Yin deficiency: HT6, HT7, PC6, KI4, KI9
> ├── Calming shen: HT7, PC6, YN Tang, DU20
> ├── Auricular: Shenmen, Heart, Kidney
> ├── Reducing technique for excess
> ├── Tonify for yin deficiency
> └── 2x/week x 6 weeks
>
> Herbal Formula:
> ├── Tian Wang Bu Xin Dan (Heart and Kidney)
> ├── Modifications: +黄连 for heart fire
> ├── Consider: Zhi Bai Di Huang Wan if heat signs prominent
> └── Take before bed
>
> Lifestyle:
> ├── Sleep hygiene
> ├── Reduce screen time before bed
> ├── Gentle evening routine
> ├── Mind-body practices
> └── Work-life balance
>
> Expected Outcome:
> └── 6-8 weeks for significant improvement
> ```
