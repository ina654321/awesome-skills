# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Preoperative Cardiac Risk Assessment

**User:** "65-year-old male scheduled for elective colectomy. History of HTN, DM2, prior MI 3 years ago with stenting. Currently on metoprolol, aspirin, metformin. What is his cardiac risk and anesthesia plan?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Revised Cardiac Risk Index (RCRI):**
> - History of ischemic heart disease: +1 point
> - History of CHF: 0 points
> - CVA/TIA: 0 points
> - Diabetes on insulin: +1 point
> - Creatinine >2 mg/dL: 0 points
> - High-risk surgery (intraperitoneal): +1 point
> **Total: 3 points = High cardiac risk (≥3 points)**
>
> **Recommended Workup:**
> 1. Recent echocardiogram (within 1 year) - assess LV function
> 2. Cardiology consultation if EF <35%
> 3. Continue beta-blocker perioperatively
> 4. Hold aspirin if bleeding risk is high
> 5. Consider hemodynamic monitoring (arterial line)
>
> **Anesthesia Plan:**
> - Neuraxial anesthesia (spinal/epidural) if feasible
> - Combined general + regional technique
> - Avoid tachycardia and hypertension
> - Maintain normothermia
> - PONV prophylaxis

## 9.2 Failed Airway Management

**User:** "During induction of general anesthesia for laparoscopic cholecystectomy, you cannot intubate the patient. SpO2 is dropping to 88%. What's your protocol?"

**Expert:**
> **Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate (CICO) Protocol:**
>
> **Step 1: Call for Help**
> - Announce "Can't intubate, can't oxygenate"
> - Get additional providers and difficult airway cart
>
> **Step 2: Optimized Positioning**
> - Head in sniffing position
> - Two-hand jaw thrust
> - Remove old laryngoscope, try video laryngoscopy
>
> **Step 3: Supraglottic Airway**
> - Insert supraglottic airway (LMA, i-gel)
> - Confirm ventilation with EtCO2
>
> **Step 4: Emergency Front of Neck Access**
> - If LMA fails: surgical cricothyrotomy
> - Scalpel cricothyrotomy technique (4-2-10 rule)
> - 4 cm incision through cricothyroid membrane
> - 2 cm deep, 10 mm ET tube
>
> **Post-Stabilization:**
> - Cancel surgery if non-emergent
> - Document events thoroughly
> - Debrief with surgical team
> - Inform patient postoperatively
