# Standards & Reference

- [ASA Standards](https://www.asahq.org/standards-and-guidelines) - American Society of Anesthesiologists practice parameters
- [ACR Contrast Manual](https://www.acr.org/Clinical-Resources/Contrast-Manual) - Contrast media guidelines
- [APAGBI Guidelines](https://www.apagbi.org.uk/publications/guidelines) - Association of Paediatric Anaesthetists
- [Stanford Anesthesia](https://anesthesia.stanford.edu) - Protocol references
- [Closed Claims Database](https://www.asaabstracts.com) - Liability analysis

## 7.2 ASA Physical Status Classification

| Class | Definition | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| ASA I | Normal healthy patient | Healthy, non-smoking |
| ASA II | Mild systemic disease | Well-controlled HTN, BMI <35 |
| ASA III | Severe systemic disease | Multiple comorbidities |
| ASA IV | Severe systemic threat to life | Unstable angina, sepsis |
| ASA V | Moribund patient | AAA rupture, massive trauma |
| ASA VI | Brain-dead organ donor | |

## 7.3 Monitoring Standards

```
Required Monitoring (ASA):
├── Pulse oximetry (SpO2)
├── Electrocardiography (ECG)
├── Non-invasive blood pressure (NIBP)
├── End-tidal CO2 (EtCO2) - required for intubation
├── Temperature - required when clinically indicated
└── Depth of anesthesia (EEG-based) - recommended

Advanced Monitoring:
├── Arterial line (invasive BP)
├── Central venous pressure (CVP)
├── Pulmonary artery catheterization
├── Transesophageal echocardiography (TEE)
└── Bispectral index (BIS)
```

## 7.4 Informed Consent Elements

1. Nature of procedure and alternatives
2. Risks and benefits (anesthesia-specific risks)
3. Anesthesia options and associated risks
4. Fasting requirements (NPO status)
5. Questions answered
6. Documentation of consent
