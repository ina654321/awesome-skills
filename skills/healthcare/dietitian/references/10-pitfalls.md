# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Assessment Pitfalls

| # | Pitfall | Severity | Prevention |
|---|---------|----------|------------|
| 1 | Relying on albumin alone | 🔴 High | Use multiple indicators; albumin is affected by inflammation |
| 2 | Ignoring fluid status | 🔴 High | Edema/obesity mask malnutrition; use dry weight |
| 3 | Skipping nutrition-focused physical exam | 🟡 Medium | Include muscle/fat stores assessment |
| 4 | Assuming weight is stable | 🟡 Medium | Check weight trends; unintentional loss/gain |
| 5 | Not considering social factors | 🟡 Medium | Assess food security, cooking ability, transportation |
| 6 | Using actual body weight in obese patients | 🟡 Medium | Consider adjusted or ideal body weight for dosing |

## 10.2 Intervention Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Correct Approach |
|--------------|------------------|
| Overfeeding on initiation | Start at 50-75% of goal; advance over 3-5 days |
| Not adjusting for renal function | Calculate protein and electrolyte restrictions |
| Supplement with meals | Give between meals (doesn't replace meal intake) |
| Assuming patient can afford therapeutic diet | Assess socioeconomic factors; provide community resources |
| Prescribing without considering preferences | Incorporate cultural and personal food preferences |

## 10.3 Drug-Nutrient Interaction Pitfalls

| Interaction | Effect | Management |
|-------------|--------|------------|
| Warfarin + Vitamin K | Altered INR | Consistent vitamin K intake; avoid large variations |
| MAOIs + Tyramine | Hypertensive crisis | Avoid aged/fermented foods (cheese, wine, cured meats) |
| Fluoroquinolones + Calcium/Mg/Al | Reduced absorption | Separate by 2 hours before/after |
| Levothyroxine + Calcium/iron | Reduced absorption | Separate by 4 hours |
| Grapefruit + Statins/Cyclosporine | Increased drug levels | Avoid grapefruit |
| ACE inhibitors + Potassium | Hyperkalemia | Monitor K intake in renal patients |

## 10.4 Quality Assurance Checklist

- [ ] Patient identification verified
- [ ] Complete nutrition assessment documented
- [ ] Evidence-based calculations used
- [ ] Diet order appropriate for diagnosis
- [ ] Drug-nutrient interactions considered
- [ ] Patient/caregiver education provided
- [ ] Follow-up plan established
- [ ] Recommendations communicated to care team

## 10.5 Ethical Considerations

1. **Informed Consent**: Discuss nutrition recommendations, alternatives, patient preferences
2. **Scope of Practice**: RDN credentials required for MNT; CNAs assist with feeding
3. **Cultural Competence**: Respect religious, ethnic, and personal food preferences
4. **End-of-Life Nutrition**: Distinguish oral, enteral, and parenteral nutrition in terminal illness; patient-centered decision-making
