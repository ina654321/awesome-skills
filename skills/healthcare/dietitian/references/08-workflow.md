# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Nutrition Assessment Workflow

```
Phase 1: Patient Identification & Chart Review
├── Verify patient identity (2 identifiers)
├── Review medical history and diagnosis
├── Check relevant labs (albumin, prealbumin, CRP, electrolytes)
├── Review medications for drug-nutrient interactions
├── Review weight history and appetite
└── Confirm diet order and restrictions

Phase 2: Nutrition-Focused Physical Exam
├── Inspect for muscle wasting, fat stores, fluid status
├── Assess oral cavity (dentition, swallow function)
├── Check for edema, ascites
├── Evaluate skin integrity and pressure injuries
└── Assess functional status and independence

Phase 3: Dietary Analysis
├── 24-hour recall or food diary review
├── Calculate estimated energy and protein needs
├── Identify food preferences and cultural factors
├── Assess barriers to eating (social, physical, psychological)
└── Evaluate need for enteral/parenteral nutrition

Phase 4: Intervention & Monitoring
├── Develop nutrition care plan with goals
├── Provide education and counseling
├── Coordinate with food services
├── Monitor weight, intake, labs
└── Document in nutrition care process format
```

## 8.2 Common Nutrition Care Workflows

### Diabetes Medical Nutrition Therapy

1. **Initial Assessment**: HgbA1c, diet history, self-monitoring logs
2. **Carbohydrate Counting**: 45-60% of calories from carbs; 15-20g per snack
3. **Meal Planning**: Plate method (½ non-starchy veg, ¼ protein, ¼ starch)
4. **Monitoring**: Pre/post meal glucose patterns; adjust based on response
5. **Follow-up**: 3-6 month reassessment with HgbA1c

### Enteral Nutrition Progression

| Day | Rate | Monitoring |
|-----|------|------------|
| 1 | Start at 10-25 mL/h; advance by 10-20 mL q4-8h | Gastric residuals, nausea, diarrhea |
| 2-3 | Goal rate (full support) | Blood glucose q4-6h |
| 4+ | Wean as oral intake improves | Daily weights, I/O |

### Refeeding Syndrome Prevention

1. **Identify high-risk patients**: BMI <16, weight loss >15%, no nutrition >7 days
2. **Start low**: 10-20 kcal/kg/day for first 3-5 days
3. **Supplement thiamine**: 100-200 mg IV/PO before feeding
4. **Monitor electrolytes**: K, Mg, Phos q12h initially
5. **Go slow**: Advance calories by 5 kcal/kg every 2-3 days

## 8.3 Documentation Standards

| Note Type | Required Elements | Timing |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| Initial Nutrition Assessment | SGA/MUST score, labs, recommendations | Within 24-48h of consult |
| Follow-up Note | Weight trend, intake, labs, plan | Weekly or per policy |
| Discharge Summary | Home diet, equipment needs, follow-up | Day of discharge |
