# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Hospital Malnutrition Case

**User:** "62-year-old male with COPD admitted for exacerbation. BMI 19, weight loss of 10 lbs in 3 months, albumin 2.8 g/dL. What is the appropriate nutrition intervention?"

**Expert:**
> **Nutrition Care Plan:**
>
> 1. **Risk Assessment**: MUST score ≥2 (BMI <20 + weight loss >10%) = High risk
>
> 2. **Energy Needs**:
>    - REE (Mifflin-St Jeor): ~1500 kcal (assuming 70kg, 172cm, 62yo male)
>    - With COPD: 30-35 kcal/kg = 2100-2450 kcal
>    - Protein: 1.2-1.5 g/kg = 84-105g protein
>
> 3. **Intervention**:
>    - High-calorie, high-protein oral nutritional supplements (ONS) between meals
>    - Small, frequent meals to reduce dyspnea during eating
>    - Consider snacking on easy-to-eat foods (smoothies, puddings)
>
> 4. **Monitoring**:
>    - Daily weights
>    - I/O tracking
>    - Weekly albumin/prealbumin
>    - CRP to interpret inflammatory markers
>
> 5. **Discharge Planning**: Home ONS, home health nutrition visit

## 9.2 Diabetes Carb Counting

**User:** "New-onset Type 2 diabetic needs help understanding how to count carbohydrates for insulin dosing."

**Expert:**
> **Carbohydrate Counting Education:**
>
> 1. **Key Concept**: 1 carbohydrate choice = 15g carbs
>
> 2. **Resources**: Nutrition facts labels, carb counting books, smartphone apps
>
> 3. **Meal Planning**:
>    - Target: 45-60g carb per meal (3-4 choices)
>    - Consistent carb intake helps predict insulin needs
>
> 4. **Insulin-to-Carb Ratio**: Typically 1 unit covers 10-15g carbs (individualized)
>
> 5. **Example**:
>    - 1 slice bread (15g) = 1 carb choice
>    - 1 small apple = 1 carb choice
>    - 1 cup milk = 1 carb choice
>
> 6. **Follow-up**: Review glucose logs; adjust ratios based on patterns

## 9.3 Chronic Kidney Disease Nutrition

**User:** "Stage 3 CKD patient with eGFR 45 asking about dietary changes to slow progression."

**Expert:**
> **CKD Nutrition Management:**
>
> 1. **Protein**: Moderate restriction 0.8 g/kg/day (not severely restricted unless Stage 4-5)
>
> 2. **Sodium**: <2300 mg/day (often need <2000 mg for hypertension)
>
> 3. **Potassium**: Monitor levels; limit high-K foods if hyperkalemic (bananas, tomatoes, potatoes)
>
> 4. **Phosphorus**: Limit processed foods with phosphate additives; natural phosphorus (meat) more absorbable
>
> 5. **Fluids**: Usually not restricted unless Stage 5 or volume overload
>
> 6. **Monitoring**: Labs q3 months (BMP, phosphorus, calcium)
>
> 7. **Referral**: Coordinate with nephrology; advance to renal dietitian at Stage 4
