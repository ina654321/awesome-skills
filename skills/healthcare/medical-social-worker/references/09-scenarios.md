# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Homeless Patient Discharge

**User:** "A 65-year-old homeless male is ready for discharge after CHF exacerbation. He has no address, limited income, and needs daily weight monitoring. How do you approach discharge planning?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Barriers Assessment:**
> ```
> Medical Needs:
> ├── Daily weight monitoring
> ├── Low-sodium diet adherence
> ├── Medication compliance (diuretics)
> ├── Follow-up with cardiology
> └── Signs of decompensation awareness
>
> Social Barriers:
> ├── No stable housing
> ├── Limited income (likely SSI/Medicaid)
> ├── Substance use history
> ├── No primary care relationship
> └── Uninsured/underinsured
> ```
>
> **Intervention Plan:**
> ```
> Housing Options:
> ├── Medical respite program
> ├── Emergency shelter placement
> ├── Transitional housing (if eligible)
> └── Temporary placement while awaiting housing
>
> Medical Management:
> ├── Arrange visiting nurse for weight checks
> ├── Medication delivery program
> ├── Establish with FQHC for primary care
> └── Cardiology follow-up scheduled
>
> Financial/Insurance:
> ├── Medicaid application if not enrolled
> ├── SSI application assistance
> ├── Patient assistance programs for medications
> └── Community benefit programs
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Complex discharge plan documented
> - Medical necessity for extended coordination
> - Time spent on housing navigation
> - Interdisciplinary care conference if needed

## 9.2 End-of-Life Family Conflict

**User:** "The family of a terminally ill patient is split on goals of care. Some want aggressive treatment, others want comfort care only. The patient is unable to speak for themselves. How do you intervene?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Conflict Assessment:**
> ```
> Family Dynamics:
> ├── Different coping styles
> ├── Unresolved grief
> ├── Guilt and blame
> ├── Misunderstanding of prognosis
> └── Cultural/religious differences
> ```
>
> **Intervention Steps:**
> ```
> Step 1: Individual Conversations
> ├── Meet with each family member separately
> ├── Understand their perspective
> ├── Acknowledge emotions
> └── Identify shared goals
>
> Step 2: Family Meeting
> ├── Facilitate with physician
> ├── Review medical situation
> ├── Clarify prognosis honestly
> ├── Explore values and wishes
> └── Guide toward consensus
>
> Step 3: Decision Support
> ├── Introduce palliative care
> ├── Discuss hospice option
> ├── Honor patient advance directive if exists
> ├── Document family consensus
> └── Plan for ongoing communication
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Family dynamics documented
> - Goals of care meeting notes
> - Surrogate decision-maker identified
> - Shared decision documented
