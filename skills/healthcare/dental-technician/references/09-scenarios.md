# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Discolored Zirconia Crown

**User:** "We received a complaint that an anterior zirconia crown has a grayish discoloration. The shade was prescribed as A2. What could cause this and how do we prevent it?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Possible Causes:**
> 1. **Insufficient opaque layer** - Zirconia shows gray substructure
> 2. **Framework too thin** - Insufficient space for adequate ceramic
> 3. **Opaque firing temperature too high** - Poor bonding, transparency
> 4. **Zirconia quality** - Lower quality or contaminated materials
> 5. **Contamination during fabrication** - Improper handling
>
> **Prevention Protocol:**
> ```
> 1. Verify framework thickness (min 0.5mm for anterior)
> 2. Apply 2 coats opaque, bake at correct temp
> 3. Avoid contamination - use dedicated instruments
> 4. Check material expiration dates
> 5. Fire according to manufacturer specs
> 6. Photo document each stage for QC
> ```
>
> **Remake Assessment:**
> - Evaluate remaining space
> - May need to re-prep or add ceramic build-up
> - Communication with dentist regarding adjustment

## 9.2 Denture Processing Changes

**User:** "The patient's new complete dentures are clicking when speaking and there's looseness. The wax try-in was perfect. What went wrong?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Common Processing Errors:**
> 1. **Overheating during polymerization** - Causes dimensional change
> 2. **Insufficient flask pressure** - Causes porosity, warpage
> 3. **Wrong flasking technique** - Causes tooth movement
> 4. **Prolonged processing time** - Material degradation
> 5. ** Cooling too fast** - Internal stresses
>
> **Prevention Protocol:**
> ```
> Proper Processing Steps:
> 1. Follow manufacturer's polymerization cycle
> 2. Use proper flasking technique (2-stage pour)
> 3. Apply consistent flask pressure throughout
> 4. Allow controlled cooling (room temp to bench cool)
> 5. Trim and polish without overheating
> 6. Use processing dents or processed denture bases
> ```
>
> **Solutions:**
> - Soft reline for minor adjustments
> - Hard reline if significant change needed
> - Remake if gross dimensional change
> - Consider digital workflow (3D printed) for better accuracy
