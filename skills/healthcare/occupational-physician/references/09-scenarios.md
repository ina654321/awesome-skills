# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Low Back Injury Claim

**User:** "A warehouse worker presents with acute low back pain after lifting a heavy box. Claims adjuster wants to know if this is work-related and what restrictions apply. How do you assess?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Causation Assessment:**
> ```
> Work-Related Determination:
> ├── Mechanism: Lifting heavy box at work
> ├── Onset: During work activity
> ├── Employment activity was contributing factor
> ├── Pre-existing conditions (if any)
> └── Apportionment consideration
>
> Most states: Arising out of and in course of employment
> ```
>
> **Work Status Determination:**
> ```
> Physical Assessment:
> ├── Range of motion limitations
> ├── Neurological exam
> ├── Strength testing
> ├── Pain pattern
> └── Functional capacity
>
> Restrictions by Phase:
> ├── Acute (0-2 weeks): Avoid lifting >10 lbs, prolonged standing/sitting
> ├── Subacute (2-6 weeks): Light duty, lifting 10-25 lbs occasionally
> └── Recovery: Gradual return to full duty
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Diagnosis: Acute lumbar strain
> - Work-related: Yes
> - Current restrictions
> - Expected duration
> - Treatment plan

## 9.2 DOT Disqualification

**User:** "A truck driver has a blood pressure of 168/98 at his DOT physical. What is the protocol?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **DOT Standards:**
> ```
> Blood Pressure Categories:
> ├── <140/90: 2-year certification
> ├── 140-159/90-99: 1-year certification
> ├── ≥160/≥100: Cannot certify until controlled
> └── Hypertensive crisis: Disqualify until controlled
> ```
>
> **Protocol:**
> ```
> If BP ≥160/100 at examination:
> 1. Document finding
> 2. Inform driver of disqualification
> 3. Provide medical reason for disqualification
> 4. Recommend follow-up with PCP
> 5. Can recertify once BP controlled
>
> Certification After Control:
> - Must be <140/90
> - On stable medication regimen
> - Likely 1-year certification initially
> ```
>
> **Documentation:**
> - Specific BP readings
> - Medication recommendation
> - Follow-up plan
> - Next DOT physical date
