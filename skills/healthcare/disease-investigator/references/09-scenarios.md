# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Foodborne Illness Outbreak

**User:** "We received reports of 15 people with diarrhea and vomiting who all ate at the same restaurant within the past 48 hours. How do we investigate this foodborne outbreak?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> **Step 1: Confirm the Outbreak**
> - Verify cases meet case definition
> - Calculate expected vs observed rates
> - Establish outbreak threshold
>
> **Step 2: Generate Hypothesis**
> ```
> Interview cases about exposures:
> ├── What did you eat?
> ├── When did you eat?
> ├── Where did you eat?
> ├── Symptoms onset timing?
> └── Common dishes consumed?
> ```
>
> **Step 3: Analytical Study Design**
> - Design case-control study
> - Select controls (diners without illness)
> - Calculate food-specific attack rates
> - Identify statistically significant associations
>
> **Step 4: Environmental Investigation**
> - Restaurant inspection
> - Food handler interviews
> - Kitchen sanitation assessment
> - Food temperature logs review
> - Laboratory testing of food samples
>
> **Interventions:**
> 1. Close restaurant if imminent hazard
> 2. Issue public health advisory
> 3. Continue surveillance for additional cases
> 4. Implement control measures

## 9.2 COVID-19 Cluster Investigation

**User:** "A nursing home reports 3 residents testing positive for COVID-19 within 48 hours. What immediate actions should we take?"

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Response:**
>
> **Step 1: Containment**
> ```
> 1. Isolate confirmed cases (private room, dedicated bathroom)
> 2. Implement universal masking for staff
> 3. Restrict visitor access
> 4. Cancel communal activities and dining
> 5. Implement enhanced hand hygiene
> ```
>
> **Step 2: Investigation**
> - Identify outbreak onset and transmission chain
> - Determine affected units
> - Assess staff exposure
> - Review infection control practices
>
> **Step 3: Testing Strategy**
> - Universal testing of all residents and staff
> - Serial testing every 3-7 days
> - Continue until no new cases for 14 days
>
> **Step 4: Control Measures**
> - Cohorting positive residents
> - Dedicated staff for COVID units
> - Enhanced PPE (N95, eye protection, gown, gloves)
> - Enhanced environmental cleaning
> - Review and reinforce donning/doffing
>
> **Communication:**
> - Notify families of affected residents
> - Report to state health department
> - Update infection control policies
