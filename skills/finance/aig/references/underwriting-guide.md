# AIG Underwriting Guidelines

## Core Underwriting Principles

### Risk Selection Framework

AIG's underwriting approach follows five key principles:

1. **Underwriting Discipline**
   - Price must reflect risk
   - Walk away from inadequately priced business
   - No premium growth at expense of profit

2. **Portfolio Diversification**
   - Geographic spread
   - Industry vertical balance
   - Peril distribution

3. **Risk Quality Focus**
   - Management assessment
   - Loss control evaluation
   - Safety culture measurement

4. **Capital Efficiency**
   - Risk-adjusted return measurement
   - Capacity optimization
   - Reinsurance utilization

5. **Relationship Value**
   - Total account profitability
   - Strategic importance
   - Long-term partnership potential

## Property Underwriting

### Risk Assessment Factors

**Physical Characteristics:**
- Construction type (fire-resistive vs. combustible)
- Age and condition
- Square footage and height
- Sprinkler protection (OHSA/NFSA grades)
- Special hazards (chemicals, dust, hot work)

**Catastrophe Exposure:**
- Wind/hail zones
- Earthquake seismicity
- Flood zones (FEMA mapping)
- Wildfire risk scoring

**Business Interruption:**
- Business income worksheets
- Contingent BI exposure
- Extra expense needs
- Recovery timeline estimates

### Property Pricing

**Rate Components:**
```
Base Rate (per $100 of TIV)
+ Catastrophe Loading (wind, EQ, flood)
+ Special Hazard Loading
- Risk Quality Credits (sprinklers, construction)
= Final Rate
```

**Deductible Optimization:**
| Account Size | Typical Deductible |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Small (<$10M TIV) | $5,000-25,000 |
| Medium ($10-100M) | $25,000-100,000 |
| Large ($100M-1B) | $100,000-500,000 |
| Jumbo (>$1B) | $500,000-5,000,000+ |

### Catastrophe Modeling

AIG utilizes RMS, AIR, and internal models to evaluate:

**Key Metrics:**
- **AAL (Average Annual Loss):** Expected loss per year
- **PML (Probable Maximum Loss):** Loss at specific return periods
  - 1-in-100 year (rating agency benchmark)
  - 1-in-250 year (solvency regulation)
- **OEP (Occurrence Exceedance Probability):** Single event
- **AEP (Aggregate Exceedance Probability):** Annual accumulation

**Exposure Management:**
- Real-time catastrophe accumulation monitoring
- Risk transfer optimization
- Capital allocation limits

## Casualty Underwriting

### General Liability

**Risk Classification:**
- Premises/operations
- Products/completed operations
- Professional liability exposure
- Pollution liability

**Pricing Methodology:**
- Exposure base (revenue, payroll, units)
- Industry loss cost
- Experience modification
- Risk quality adjustment

**Key Underwriting Information:**
- Operations description
- Subcontractor usage
- Contracts review
- Claims history (5-year)
- Risk management program

### Workers Compensation

**State-by-State Considerations:**
- Monopolistic states (ND, OH, WA, WY)
- NCCI vs. independent bureau states
- Assigned risk implications

**Experience Rating:**
- EMR (Experience Modification Rate)
- Interstate vs. intrastate modifiers
- Merit rating for smaller risks

**Classification:**
- NCCI manual codes
- Payroll assignment accuracy
- Multi-state operations

### Excess Casualty

**Attachment Point Selection:**
| Layer | Typical Attachment |
|-------|-------------------|
| Primary | $1-5M |
| First Excess | $5-15M |
| Second Excess | $15-50M |
| Umbrella | $1-10M primary |

**Nuclear Verdict Considerations:**
- Jurisdiction analysis (social inflation impact)
- Punitive damages exposure
- Reputational risk cases
- Litigation funding trends

## Financial Lines

### Directors & Officers (D&O)

**Coverage Sides:**
- **Side A:** Individual directors/officers (non-rescindable)
- **Side B:** Company reimbursement
- **Side C:** Entity securities coverage

**Public vs. Private:**
| Factor | Public Company | Private Company |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Premium (base) | Higher | Lower |
| Securities exposure | High | Low |
| Regulatory exposure | SEC | Private equity |
| Claims frequency | Higher | Lower |

**Key Underwriting Data:**
- Market capitalization/revenue
- Industry sector
- Corporate governance
- Financial results trends
- Litigation history
- Regulatory investigations
- Board composition

### Professional Liability (E&O)

**Industry Variations:**
- Technology E&O (cyber/systems failure)
- Accountants E&O
- Architects/Engineers
- Lawyers
- Medical professionals (via Healthcare division)

**Common Exclusions:**
- Fraud/dishonesty
- Bodily injury/property damage
- Known claims
- Prior acts (unless full prior acts)

### Cyber Insurance

**Risk Assessment Framework:**

1. **Security Controls (Critical)**
   - Multi-factor authentication (MFA) adoption
   - Endpoint detection and response (EDR)
   - Network segmentation
   - Patch management program
   - Security awareness training

2. **Incident Response Readiness**
   - Documented IR plan
   - Tabletop exercises completed
   - Retained forensics firm
   - Legal counsel identified

3. **Data Governance**
   - Data classification
   - Encryption (at rest, in transit)
   - Retention/destruction policies
   - Access controls

4. **Third-Party Risk**
   - Cloud provider security
   - Vendor management program
   - Cyber insurance requirements flow-down

**Coverage Components:**
- First-party: Business interruption, data restoration, forensics
- Third-party: Regulatory defense, liability, PCI-DSS
- Crisis management: PR, notification, credit monitoring
- Cybercrime: Social engineering, funds transfer fraud

**Pricing Trends (2024-2025):**
- Market softened after 2020-2022 hardening
- Rates down 5-15% on renewals
- Stricter underwriting requirements
- Ransomware sub-limits common

## Claims Considerations in Underwriting

### Loss History Analysis

**Key Metrics:**
- Loss ratio (3-5 year)
- Frequency vs. severity trends
- Open reserve adequacy
- Prior year development

**Red Flags:**
- Increasing frequency
- Large case reserve changes
- Litigation rate increases
- Emerging hazard patterns

### Claims Advocacy

AIG differentiates through:
- Dedicated claims professionals
- Early claim engagement
- Specialized adjusters (construction, cyber, etc.)
- Legal panel management
- Alternative dispute resolution
