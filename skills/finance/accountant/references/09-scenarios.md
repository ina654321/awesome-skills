# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Revenue Recognition Compliance

**User:** "We signed a $2M software contract with implementation. How do we recognize revenue?"

**Expert:**
> **Revenue Recognition Analysis:**
>
> 1. **Identify the contract** — Single contract, $2M fixed fee. Customer approvals obtained, commercial substance confirmed.
> 2. **Identify performance obligations** — Software license (distinct), Implementation services (distinct if customer can benefit alone), Annual support (distinct).
> 3. **Determine transaction price** — $2M fixed; no variable consideration identified.
> 4. **Allocate to POBs** — Based on standalone selling prices: License ($1.2M), Implementation ($500K), Support ($300K).
> 5. **Recognize when satisfied** — License recognized at point-in-time; Implementation over-time; Support ratably.
>
> **Key consideration:** Document SSP methodology per ASC 606-10-32-31.

## 9.2 Lease Accounting Transition

**User:** "We have 50 operating leases for office space. How do we account for them under ASC 842?"

**Expert:**
> **ASC 842 Implementation:**
>
> 1. **Identify all leases** — Review all contracts for lease component; 50 operating leases identified.
> 2. **Determine discount rate** — Use incremental borrowing rate (IBR) based on term and currency; obtained from lender.
> 3. **Calculate ROU asset and liability** — PV of remaining payments at IBR for each lease.
> 4. **Record initial entry:**
>    - Dr. ROU Asset $X
>    - Cr. Lease Liability $X
> 5. **Ongoing recognition** — Operating leases: straight-line expense; Finance leases: separate interest and amortization.
>
> **Common error:** Failing to include renewal options reasonably certain to be exercised.

## 9.3 Inventory Obsolescence Reserve

**User:** "Our inventory has grown 40% while sales declined. How much reserve do we need?"

**Expert:**
> **Inventory Reserve Analysis:**
>
> 1. **Age inventory by category** — Categorize by SKU age: 0-30d, 31-90d, 91-180d, 180d+.
> 2. **Apply reserve rates by category** — 0-30d: 0%, 31-90d: 10%, 91-180d: 25%, 180d+: 50%.
> 3. **Apply specific reserves** — Identify damaged, returned, or obsolete items at 100%.
> 4. **Calculate total reserve** — $120K based on aging analysis.
> 5. **Validate against historical losses** — Prior 3-year average obsolescence: $95K. Current reserve is conservative but justified by sales decline.
>
> **Recommendation:** Increase reserve from $50K to $120K; disclose in footnotes per ASC 330-10-50.
