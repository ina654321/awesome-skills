# Edward Jones Glossary

## Firm-Specific Terms

### Partnership Structure

**Limited Partnership (LP)**
Edward Jones' ownership structure. As a private partnership, the firm is not publicly traded and has no public shareholders. This enables long-term strategic focus without quarterly earnings pressure.

**Managing Partner**
The CEO and highest leadership position at Edward Jones. The sixth managing partner is Penny Pennington (2019-present), the first woman to hold this role.

**Principal**
A senior leadership designation at Edward Jones. Principals hold significant responsibility in the firm's governance and operations.

**Class B Limited Partnership**
A new ownership tier announced in 2025, allowing financial advisors to participate in the firm's partnership structure and share in company wealth.

### Service Model

**Branch Office Administrator (BOA)**
The support staff member in each Edward Jones branch who handles administrative tasks, client service, and office operations, allowing the advisor to focus on client relationships.

**Financial Advisor**
The primary client-facing role at Edward Jones. Each advisor typically operates from a single branch office and manages their own book of business.

**Book of Business**
The collection of client relationships and assets managed by an individual financial advisor.

**Door-Knocking**
The traditional Edward Jones practice of new advisors introducing themselves to prospective clients by going door-to-door in their communities. While less common today, it represents the firm's grassroots relationship-building philosophy.

**Edward Jones Generations™**
The firm's private wealth offering launched in 2024 for high-net-worth clients (typically $10M+), providing specialized services and alternative investments.

**Enterprise Reimagined**
A 2025 initiative to streamline home office operations, remove redundancies, and adopt new technology to improve advisor support and client experience.

### Investment Philosophy

**Buy and Hold**
The core Edward Jones investment strategy of purchasing quality investments and maintaining positions through market cycles rather than trading frequently.

**"One Out of Four Years"**
The Edward Jones framework for setting client expectations: historically, significant market declines occur approximately one year out of every four. This normalizes volatility and discourages panic selling.

**Rebalancing**
The disciplined practice of adjusting portfolio allocations back to target percentages, typically by selling appreciated assets and buying underperforming ones.

**Sleep-Well Test**
An informal Edward Jones criterion: if a client would worry about an investment during a market downturn, it's too risky for their portfolio regardless of potential returns.

**Time in the Market, Not Timing the Market**
The principle that consistent, long-term investment outperforms attempts to predict market movements and trade accordingly.

### Client Relationships

**Client Household**
Edward Jones' unit of measurement for clients, recognizing that advice often serves families rather than individuals. As of 2025, the firm serves approximately 9 million client households.

**Goals-Based Planning**
An approach that begins with understanding client life goals (retirement, education, legacy) and then designs financial strategies to achieve those goals.

**Comprehensive Planning**
Holistic financial planning that addresses investments, retirement, insurance, estate planning, and tax efficiency as an integrated whole.

**Life Plans into Financial Plans**
Edward Jones' framing of their advisory process—translating clients' life aspirations into concrete financial strategies.

### Business Metrics

**Assets Under Care (AUC)**
The total value of client assets that Edward Jones monitors and advises upon. As of 2025, approximately $2.5 trillion.

**Net New Assets (NNA)**
New client assets gathered minus assets lost from departing clients. A key growth metric; Edward Jones reported $74 billion in NNA for 2024-2025.

**Attrition Rate**
The percentage of financial advisors who leave the firm annually. Edward Jones reported approximately 5% attrition in recent years.

**Financial Fitness**
Edward Jones' financial education program with a goal of educating 1 million learners by end of 2025.

## Industry Terms

### Advisory Models

**Wirehouse**
Large Wall Street brokerage firms (Morgan Stanley, Merrill Lynch, Wells Fargo) characterized by high minimums, extensive resources, and often high-pressure sales environments.

**Independent Broker-Dealer (IBD)**
Firms like LPL Financial that provide regulatory and operational support to independent financial advisors who typically run their own practices.

**Registered Investment Advisor (RIA)**
Investment advisory firms registered with the SEC or state regulators, often fee-only and fiduciary.

**Fee-Based Advisor**
An advisor compensated through fees (typically percentage of assets) rather than commissions, potentially aligning interests more closely with clients.

**Commission-Based Advisor**
An advisor compensated through transaction commissions on products sold.

**Fiduciary**
A legal standard requiring advisors to act in clients' best interests. Edward Jones advisors in fee-based programs operate under fiduciary standards.

**Suitability**
A standard requiring recommendations be appropriate for the client's situation (lower than fiduciary).

### Investment Products

**Mutual Fund**
Pooled investment vehicle managed by professional portfolio managers. A core Edward Jones investment vehicle.

**ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)**
Index-tracking investment traded on exchanges like stocks. Edward Jones uses ETFs for cost-efficient diversification.

**Annuity**
Insurance product providing guaranteed income, often used in retirement planning for appropriate clients.

**Municipal Bond**
Bonds issued by state and local governments, typically offering tax-free interest for investors in higher tax brackets.

**Certificate of Deposit (CD)**
Bank product offering guaranteed interest for a fixed term; used for emergency funds and near-term needs.

**Alternative Investments**
Non-traditional investments (private equity, hedge funds, real estate) recently made available to high-net-worth Edward Jones clients.

### Planning Concepts

**Asset Allocation**
The distribution of investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) based on goals, time horizon, and risk tolerance.

**Diversification**
Spreading investments across different securities, sectors, and asset classes to reduce risk.

**Dollar-Cost Averaging**
Investing fixed amounts at regular intervals, reducing the impact of market timing.

**Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)**
Mandatory withdrawals from retirement accounts beginning at a certain age (currently 73-75 depending on birth year).

**Sequence of Returns Risk**
The danger that poor market returns early in retirement can permanently damage a portfolio's sustainability.

**Tax-Loss Harvesting**
Strategically selling investments at a loss to offset capital gains and reduce tax liability.

**Estate Equalization**
Ensuring fair distribution of assets among heirs when some receive operating businesses or illiquid assets.

### Retirement Planning

**401(k)**
Employer-sponsored retirement plan; Edward Jones advises clients on contribution strategies and rollovers.

**IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**
Tax-advantaged retirement account available to individuals. Edward Jones offers Traditional, Roth, and Rollover IRAs.

**Roth IRA**
After-tax retirement account offering tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

**Catch-Up Contribution**
Additional retirement contribution allowed for individuals age 50+, helping older workers save more as retirement approaches.

**Retirement Income Planning**
The shift from accumulation (saving) to distribution (withdrawal) strategies as clients approach and enter retirement.

**Social Security Optimization**
Strategic timing of Social Security benefit claims to maximize lifetime income.

### Business Succession Terms

**ESOP (Employee Stock Ownership Plan)**
A qualified retirement plan that invests primarily in employer stock, sometimes used for business succession.

**Installment Sale**
Selling a business over time with the buyer making payments; common in family succession.

**Buy-Sell Agreement**
Legal agreement defining how a business owner's interest will be transferred upon death, disability, or retirement.

**SCIN (Self-Cancelling Installment Note)**
An installment note that is cancelled if the seller dies before receiving all payments; used in estate planning.
