# Edward Jones Firm History

## Complete Historical Timeline

### Founding Era (1922-1967)

**1922** – Edward D. Jones Sr. establishes Edward D. Jones & Co. in St. Louis, Missouri. The firm begins as a traditional wholesale brokerage serving institutional clients and wealthy individuals.

**1948** – Edward D. "Ted" Jones Jr. joins the firm. He brings a revolutionary vision: serving individual investors in communities overlooked by Wall Street.

**1957** – First branch office opens in Mexico, Missouri (population ~12,000). This marks the beginning of the distinctive Edward Jones branch model—bringing Wall Street to Main Street.

**1960s** – Firm expands throughout Missouri and surrounding states. Ted Jones's philosophy: "I want to talk to people"—direct relationships over institutional trading.

### Expansion Era (1968-1999)

**1968** – Ted Jones becomes the firm's second managing partner, succeeding his father.

**1973** – Management guru Peter Drucker becomes informal consultant to the firm after John Bachmann (future managing partner) writes him a letter. Drucker advises for over 20 years, shaping the firm's management philosophy.

**1979** – Computers and printers installed in all 200 branch offices—early technology adoption for operational efficiency.

**1980** – John Bachmann named third managing partner. Under his 23-year leadership, the firm experiences dramatic growth.

**1986** – 1,000th office opens.

**1990** – Expansion plans into Canada announced; first Canadian offices open in 1994.

**1998** – First international expansion beyond North America with office opening in England.

### Modern Era (2000-2018)

**2000** – Penny Pennington joins Edward Jones as a financial advisor in Livonia, Michigan after 14 years in banking.

**2001** – Firm reaches 8,000 branch offices worldwide.

**2006** – Jim Weddle becomes fourth managing partner. Penny Pennington named principal and relocates to St. Louis headquarters.

**2008** – Despite global financial crisis, firm opens 10,000th office. This demonstrates the resilience of the conservative, relationship-based model.

**2019** – Penny Pennington becomes sixth managing partner (first woman in role), succeeding Jim Weddle after his 13-year tenure.

### Centennial Era (2020-Present)

**2022** – Edward Jones celebrates its 100th anniversary. Firm reflects on century of "partnering for positive impact."

**2024** – Major milestones:
- Annual revenue exceeds $18 billion
- Client assets reach $2.2 trillion
- Advisor headcount grows 5% to 20,125
- Net new client households: 246,000 (up 21%)
- "Edward Jones Generations" launched for high-net-worth clients
- Applied for bank charter to offer deposit and lending products

**2025** – Current state:
- Assets under care: $2.5 trillion (14% growth)
- Revenue: $18 billion (11% increase)
- Advisor headcount: 20,425 (1% increase)
- Operating expenses: $16 billion
- Net income: approximately $2 billion
- "Enterprise Reimagined" initiative launched for operational efficiency
- $1.4 billion Class B limited partnership offering announced for advisors

## Partnership Structure

Edward Jones operates as a **limited liability partnership (LLP)**, not a publicly traded corporation. This structure:

- Eliminates quarterly earnings pressure
- Enables long-term strategic focus
- Allows profits to be reinvested in the business
- Aligns advisor and firm interests
- Recently expanded to include Class B limited partnership for advisors (2025)

The partnership structure is a key competitive advantage, allowing Edward Jones to maintain its conservative, client-first approach without short-term shareholder pressure.

## Geographic Presence

- **United States:** 15,000+ branches in 68% of all U.S. counties
- **Canada:** Significant presence since 1994
- **United Kingdom:** Limited presence (original expansion scaled back)

The firm has more branch offices in the U.S. than any other financial services company—nearly as many as Starbucks locations.

## Cultural Touchstones

- **The Door-Knocking Tradition:** New advisors historically built practices by going door-to-door in their communities—a practice that emphasized grassroots relationship building
- **Peter Drucker Influence:** Management principles from Drucker's 1973 book "Management: Tasks, Responsibilities, Practices" continue to shape the firm
- **Conservative Values:** "Boring but reliable"—avoiding speculation and complex products
- **Community Embeddedness:** Advisors encouraged to be active community members, not just financial service providers
