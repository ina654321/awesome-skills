# Travelers Catastrophe Management Reference

## Overview

Travelers' catastrophe management program is designed to protect the company's financial strength while maintaining market presence in catastrophe-exposed regions. Despite record catastrophe losses of $3.7B in 2025, Travelers achieved an 89.9% combined ratio, demonstrating the effectiveness of its catastrophe risk management strategy.

---

## Catastrophe Risk Appetite

### Philosophy

> "We reinsure a portion of the risks we underwrite to further manage our exposure to losses and to protect our capital."
> — Travelers 2024 Annual Report

**Core Principles:**
1. **Maintain market presence** in catastrophe-prone areas with appropriate pricing
2. **Diversify exposure** across perils, geographies, and lines of business
3. **Use reinsurance** to protect capital from peak exposures
4. **Annual policy structure** enables rapid adjustment
5. **Proprietary modeling** informs underwriting decisions

### Risk Limits

| Dimension | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| **Geographic concentration** | State-level limits based on capital |
| **Peril exposure** | Modeled annual aggregate limits |
| **Line of business** | Property-centric cat load targets |
| **Single event** | Retention levels with reinsurance above |

## Peril-Specific Management

### Hurricane/Windstorm

**Exposure Management:**

| Action | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Rate adequacy** | Territory-specific wind rating |
| **Deductibles** | Percentage deductibles in high-risk zones |
| **Limits** | Maximum capacity per location |
| **Terms** | Named storm provisions |

**Geographic Focus:**
- Atlantic and Gulf coast states
- Hawaii
- International (UK, Ireland)

### Earthquake

**Exposure Management:**

| Action | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Sub-limits** | Separate earthquake limits |
| **Deductibles** | Higher percentage deductibles |
| **Risk selection** | Soil condition evaluation |
| **Pricing** | Zone-based rating |

**Primary Exposure:**
- California (San Andreas, Hayward faults)
- Pacific Northwest (Cascadia)
- New Madrid seismic zone
- International (Japan, New Zealand exposure via Lloyd's)

### Wildfire

**Proprietary Underwriting Model:**

Travelers has developed a sophisticated wildfire risk assessment model:

| Factor | Assessment Method |
|--------|------------------|
| **Terrain slope** | Digital elevation models |
| **Vegetation density** | Satellite imagery analysis |
| **Propensity to burn** | Historical fire data |
| **Road access** | Proximity to fire stations |

**Underwriting Actions:**
- Property-level risk scoring (not just ZIP code)
- Rate adjustments based on model output
- Defensible space requirements
- Construction/roofing type considerations

**2024-2025 Activity:**
- Selective non-renewal in highest-hazard zones
- Rate increases reflecting elevated risk
- Continued new business where risk-adjusted returns adequate

### Flood

**Proprietary Flood Underwriting:**

| Innovation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Building-level assessment** | Individual structure analysis vs. zone-based |
| **Elevation data** | Building footprint vs. flood zones |
| **Hydrology models** | Riverine and coastal flood risk |
| **Climate factors** | Changing precipitation patterns |

**Product Offerings:**
- Private flood insurance
- NFIP policies (Write Your Own)
- Commercial flood coverage
- Excess flood policies

### Severe Convective Storm

**Perils Included:**
- Tornado
- Hail
- Straight-line wind
- Lightning

**Management Approach:**
- Geographic diversification
- Roof type underwriting
- Attachment point management
- Reinsurance protection

### Catastrophe Bonds (Long Point Re)

**Program Details:**

| Attribute | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Coverage** | $575M |
| **Term** | Through May 2026 |
| **Perils** | Tropical cyclones, earthquakes, severe thunderstorms, winter storms |
| **Trigger** | Industry loss or company-specific loss |
| **Retention** | $2.8B (reset annually) |
| **Credit enhancement** | Reinsurance trust with AAA-rated money market funds |

### Traditional Reinsurance

**Coverage Types:**

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Quota share** | Proportional risk sharing |
| **Excess of loss** | Protection above retention |
| **Catastrophe excess** | Event-based protection |
| **Facultative** | Specific risk reinsurance |

### Reinsurance Costs

**Economic Considerations:**
- Reinsurance premiums charged to operating segments
- Cost allocation based on exposure
- Annual program renewal and optimization
- Balance between cost and protection

## Catastrophe Modeling

### Model Usage

**Primary Models:**
- RMS (Risk Management Solutions)
- AIR Worldwide
- Internal proprietary models

**Model Applications:**

| Use Case | Model Output |
|----------|--------------|
| **Pricing** | Expected catastrophe load |
| **Portfolio management** | PML (Probable Maximum Loss) estimates |
| **Reinsurance** | Attachment point optimization |
| **Capital** | Economic capital requirements |

### Model Validation

**Process:**
1. Compare model output to actual experience
2. Adjust for known model biases
3. Incorporate climate change factors
4. Stress test extreme scenarios

### Climate Change Integration

**Considerations:**
- Increasing frequency of severe weather
- Sea level rise impacts
- Changing precipitation patterns
- Temperature extremes

**Response:**
- Medium and long-term risk monitoring
- Pricing adjustments
- Portfolio mix optimization
- Terms and conditions modifications

## Strategic Considerations

### Market Presence vs. Exposure

**Balancing Act:**
- Maintain agent relationships in cat zones
- Support customer needs appropriately
- Avoid excessive concentration
- Price for risk-adjusted returns

### California Example

**Market Position:**
- Largest state exposure (~10.5% of premium)
- Earthquake, wildfire, and flood risk
- Regulatory constraints on rate adequacy

**Management Approach:**
- Selective underwriting
- Rate pursuit where permitted
- Risk control requirements
- Portfolio diversification

### Competitive Advantage

**Differentiation:**
- Superior modeling and pricing
- Rapid market adjustment capability
- Strong claims response
- Agent relationship preservation
