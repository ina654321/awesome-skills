## Scenario 6: Credit Card Portfolio Strategy

**Situation:** Wells Fargo wants to grow its credit card business while maintaining credit quality post-scandal.

**Analysis:**
- Current: 2.4M new accounts opened in 2024 (+20% YoY)
- Strategy: 11 new card products launched since 2021
- Risk: Must avoid pre-scandal aggressive tactics

**Wells Fargo Approach:**
1. **Product Innovation**: Cards tailored to specific segments (travel, cashback, business)
2. **Digital Acquisition**: Online and mobile-first application process
3. **Relationship Cross-Sell**: Target existing banking customers first
4. **Credit Discipline**: Maintain through-cycle underwriting standards

**Key Metrics:**
| Metric | Target | Actual |
|--------|--------|--------|
| New Accounts (Annual) | 2.0M+ | 2.4M |
| Credit Loss Rate | <5% | ~4% |
| Customer Acquisition Cost | <$200 | ~$180 |
| Activation Rate | >70% | ~75% |

## Scenario 7: Commercial Real Estate Workout

**Situation:** Downtown office building loan ($50M) showing stress with 40% occupancy.

**Wells Fargo Approach:**

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Downgrade risk rating to "Special Mention"
2. Require monthly financial reporting (vs. quarterly)
3. Property inspection and rent roll analysis
4. Sponsor guarantee enforcement review

**Restructuring Options:**
| Option | Structure | When Used |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Modification | Rate reduction, term extension | Temporary stress |
| Forbearance | Payment deferral | Short-term bridge |
| Equity Injection | Require sponsor capital | Long-term solution needed |
| Asset Sale | Facilitate property sale | No viable alternative |

**Expected Outcome:**
- 60% of cases: Successful modification and return to performing
- 25% of cases: Asset sale with minimal loss
- 15% of cases: Foreclosure with 30-40% loss severity
