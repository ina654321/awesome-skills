# Wells Fargo Transformation Details

## The Charlie Scharf Transformation (2019-Present)

### CEO Background

**Charlie Scharf:**
- Appointed CEO: October 2019
- Previous roles: CEO of BNY Mellon, CEO of Visa, CFO of JPMorgan Chase
- Education: Johns Hopkins University, NYU Stern MBA
- Compensation: $40M for 2025 (+28% YoY)
- Added role: Chairman of the Board (July 2025)

### Leadership Philosophy

**Three Principles (per Fortune analysis):**
1. **Discipline over flash** — Hard calls with steadiness, not showmanship
2. **Calm over charisma** — Quiet but firm leadership
3. **Accountability over optics** — Clear responsibility, measurable results

**Key Quotes:**
> "Building the right risk and control infrastructure remains our top priority."

> "We are much different today than we were five years ago."

> "We have worked hard to balance short-term performance and investing for long-term success."

## Transformation Pillars

### 1. Risk & Control Infrastructure

**Investment:**
- Billions spent on control enhancement
- Significant headcount increase in risk and compliance
- Technology investment for monitoring
- External expertise brought in

**Results:**
- 13 consent orders terminated
- Asset cap removed (June 2025)
- Control framework validated by regulators
- Risk culture embedded organization-wide

### 2. Business Simplification

**Divestitures (2019-2025):**
| Year | Divested Business | Rationale |
|------|-------------------|-----------|
| 2019 | Institutional Retirement & Trust | Non-core |
| 2021 | Asset Management | Strategic refocus |
| 2021 | Corporate Trust Services | Non-core |
| 2023 | Private equity fund investments | Capital efficiency |
| 2024 | Home Lending correspondent business | Simplification |
| 2025 | Commercial mortgage servicing | Complexity reduction |
| 2025 | Rail lease portfolio | Non-core |

**Strategy:** Focus on core banking franchises with sustainable competitive advantages

### 3. Efficiency Initiative

**Target:** $10B annual expense reduction

**Approach:**
- Process optimization
- Technology automation
- Workforce rationalization
- Real estate optimization

**Results:**
- $15B gross expense reductions achieved
- Enabled $15B investment in growth and controls
- Total expense base reduced
- ROTCE improvement from efficiency

### 4. Revenue Growth

**Focus Areas:**

| Segment | Strategy | Results |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Credit Cards | New product launches | +20% accounts YoY |
| Investment Banking | Talent acquisition | Ranking #8 to #8, fees +14% |
| Premier | Affluent focus | +14% balances |
| Auto Lending | Disciplined growth | +19% balances |
| Commercial | Relationship banking | +12% loans |

### 5. Technology Modernization

**Initiatives:**
- **Cloud Migration**: Hybrid multi-cloud (Azure, Google Cloud)
- **AI/ML**: Fargo™ assistant, Google Agentspace
- **Digital Banking**: Mobile enhancements, digital account opening
- **Core Systems**: Legacy modernization

**Investments:**
- Technology spend increased significantly
- Digital channel growth
- Automation of controls
- Customer experience enhancement

## Regulatory Transformation

### Consent Order Timeline

```
2016:
├── September: CFPB and OCC consent orders ($185M)
└── Scandal becomes public

2018:
├── February: Federal Reserve asset cap imposed
└── Additional consent orders

2019:
├── October: Charlie Scharf appointed CEO
└── Transformation begins

2020-2023:
├── Multiple consent orders terminated
├── Risk infrastructure investment
└── Regulatory engagement intensifies

2024:
├── February: OCC 2016 consent order terminated
├── Multiple others terminated
└── Progress accelerates

2025 (Transformational Year):
├── June: Asset cap removed (most significant milestone)
├── 7 consent orders terminated
├── Total terminated: 13 since 2019
└── Scharf appointed Chairman
```

### Regulatory Trust Rebuilding

**Key Actions:**
1. Transparent communication with regulators
2. Proactive issue identification and reporting
3. Substantial control investments
4. Culture change demonstration
5. Sustained execution over years

## Lessons from the Transformation

### For Banking Industry

1. **Culture matters** — Weak culture leads to bad outcomes
2. **Controls are foundational** — Growth without controls is dangerous
3. **Regulatory trust is hard to earn** — Takes years to rebuild
4. **Transparency is essential** — With regulators and stakeholders
5. **Sustained execution required** — Transformation takes time

### For Wells Fargo

1. **Risk-first is working** — Consent order closures validate approach
2. **Efficiency enables investment** — Cost savings fund growth
3. **Disciplined growth is sustainable** — Quality over quantity
4. **Customer trust is paramount** — Relationship banking over transactions
5. **Transformation continues** — Not done, ongoing journey
