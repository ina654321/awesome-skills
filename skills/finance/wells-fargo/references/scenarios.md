# Wells Fargo Additional Case Studies

## Case Study 1: Auto Lending Recovery

### Background
Wells Fargo's auto lending business faced challenges during the transformation period. With the asset cap removed and risk controls in place, the bank sought to return to growth.

### Situation
- Auto loan balances declined during asset cap period
- Market share loss to competitors
- Need to balance growth with credit quality

### Strategy
| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| Target Market | Prime and super-prime borrowers |
| Distribution | Dealer network expansion |
| Credit Standards | Maintain strict underwriting |
| Technology | Digital application process |

### Results (2025)
- Auto loan balances up 19% YoY
- Credit quality maintained
- Digital adoption increased
- Returns within target range

### Key Lessons
- Growth with discipline is possible
- Technology enables scale without compromising controls
- Prime focus protects credit quality

## Case Study 2: Corporate Real Estate Portfolio Management

### Background
Commercial Real Estate (CRE) is a core strength of Wells Fargo, but large office properties faced headwinds post-pandemic.

### Situation
- Office fundamentals challenged
- Concentration risk in portfolio
- Need for proactive management

### Strategy
```
Portfolio Actions:
├── Diversify across property types (office, retail, industrial, multifamily)
├── Reduce exposure to large urban office
├── Proactive borrower engagement
├── Frequent portfolio review
└── Active workout for troubled credits
```

### Approach
- Maintain relationships with strong sponsors
- Work with borrowers on modifications when appropriate
- Reserve adequately for potential losses
- Focus on cash-flowing properties

### Results
- Portfolio remains diversified
- Losses contained to expectations
- Office losses "lumpy" but manageable
- Strong performance in other CRE sectors

## Case Study 4: Branch Transformation

### Background
Wells Fargo operates one of the largest branch networks (~4,700 locations) and has been modernizing the physical footprint.

### Situation
- Changing customer preferences (digital adoption)
- Cost of physical infrastructure
- Need for enhanced customer experience
- Asset cap constrained investment

### Strategy
```
Branch Network Strategy:
├── Refurbishment (730 completed in 2024)
├── Format optimization
│   ├── Full-service branches (major markets)
│   ├── Mini-branches (secondary markets)
│   └── ATM centers (rural areas)
├── Digital integration
│   ├── Tablet-equipped bankers
│   ├── Digital account opening (40%+ of checking)
│   └── Video conferencing capabilities
└── Premier expansion
    ├── Dedicated Premier spaces
    └── Private banking suites
```

### Results
- Customer satisfaction improved
- Transaction migration to digital
- Cost per transaction reduced
- Premier channel growth enabled

## Case Study 5: Regulatory Remediation — Consent Order Closure

### Background
Wells Fargo has been working to terminate consent orders imposed after the 2016 scandal.

### The Process

**Step 1: Assessment**
- Understand order requirements
- Gap analysis vs. current state
- Remediation plan development

**Step 2: Implementation**
- Build required controls
- Enhance processes
- Train personnel
- Document compliance

**Step 3: Validation**
- Internal testing
- Independent validation
- Regulator examination
- Evidence compilation

**Step 4: Closure**
- Regulator review
- Termination order
- Ongoing monitoring

### Results (2019-2025)
| Year | Consent Orders Terminated |
|------|---------------------------|
| 2019-2023 | 6 |
| 2024 | Multiple (including OCC 2016 sales practices) |
| 2025 | 7 (including Federal Reserve asset cap) |
| **Total** | **13** |

### Key Success Factors
1. **CEO Commitment**: Scharf made this top priority
2. **Resource Investment**: Significant spending on controls
3. **Culture Change**: Risk-first mindset embedded
4. **Regulatory Engagement**: Transparent communication
5. **Sustained Execution**: Multi-year commitment

## Case Study 6: Zelle and Digital Payments Growth

### Background
Wells Fargo has been expanding its digital payment capabilities through Zelle and other platforms.

### Strategy

- Promote P2P payments for customer engagement
- Leverage for small business payments

## Case Study 7: Credit Card Portfolio Transformation

### Background
Wells Fargo significantly expanded its credit card offerings since 2021 as part of strategic repositioning.

### Product Launches
| Year | New Cards |
|------|-----------|
| 2021-2023 | 10 cards |
| 2024 | 4 consumer + 1 small business |
| Total | 15 new products |

### Strategy Elements
1. **Segment Targeting**: Travel, cashback, premium
2. **Customer Acquisition**: Digital and branch channels
3. **Credit Quality**: Maintain strict underwriting
4. **Rewards**: Competitive but sustainable economics

### Results
- 2.4 million new accounts opened in 2024
- Spend volume growth strong
- Credit quality maintained
- Revenue contribution increasing
