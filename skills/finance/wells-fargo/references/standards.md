# Wells Fargo Regulatory Standards & Frameworks

## Regulatory Background

### The 2016 Fake Accounts Scandal

**Timeline:**
- **2002-2016**: Hundreds of thousands of Community Bank employees opened millions of unauthorized accounts
- **September 8, 2016**: CFPB and OCC issue consent orders with $185M in penalties
- **2016-2018**: Multiple additional consent orders issued
- **February 2018**: Federal Reserve imposes $1.95T asset cap
- **October 2019**: Charlie Scharf appointed CEO
- **February 2024**: OCC terminates 2016 consent order
- **June 2025**: Federal Reserve removes asset cap

**Key Violations:**
- Opening unauthorized deposit accounts
- Submitting credit card applications without consent
- Enrolling customers in online banking without consent
- Transferring funds between accounts without authorization
- Creating fake email addresses to enroll customers

**Penalties:**
- CFPB: $100M civil penalty
- OCC: $35M civil penalty
- Los Angeles: $50M
- Total: $185M in regulatory penalties

## Regulatory Framework

### Three Lines of Defense

**First Line: Business Units**
- Own the risk in their operations
- Implement day-to-day controls
- Responsible for risk identification and management

**Second Line: Risk & Control Functions**
- Independent oversight of risk management
- Establish risk frameworks and policies
- Monitor and report on risk exposure

**Third Line: Internal Audit**
- Independent assurance on risk management
- Test control effectiveness
- Report findings to Board and regulators

### Key Regulatory Requirements

**Consumer Protection (CFPB)**
- Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (UDAAP)
- Truth in Lending Act (TILA)
- Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)
- Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)

**Safety and Soundness (OCC/Federal Reserve)**
- Capital requirements (Basel III/IV)
- Stress testing (CCAR/DFAST)
- Liquidity requirements (LCR/NSFR)
- Large Bank Standards

**Anti-Money Laundering (AML)**
- Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)
- Customer Identification Program (CIP)
- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)

### Operational Risk Framework

**Risk Categories:**
1. People risk
2. Process risk
3. Systems risk
4. External events risk

**Control Types:**
- Preventive controls
- Detective controls
- Corrective controls

**Key Metrics:**
- Operational Risk Event Rate
- Control Deficiency Rate
- Remediation Cycle Time

### Credit Risk Framework

**Rating System:**
- 21-grade internal rating scale
- Probability of Default (PD) estimates
- Loss Given Default (LGD) estimates
- Exposure at Default (EAD)

**Portfolio Management:**
- Concentration limits by industry, geography, product
- Stress testing scenarios
- Watchlist and classified asset monitoring

### Market Risk Framework

**Measurement:**
- Value at Risk (VaR)
- Stressed VaR
- Economic Capital
- Sensitivity analysis

**Limits:**
- Position limits
- Sensitivity limits
- VaR limits
- Stress loss limits
