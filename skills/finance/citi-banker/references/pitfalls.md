# Extended Anti-Patterns for Citi Banking

## Anti-Pattern: Inadequate KYC for Complex Structures

**Scenario**: Multi-layered ownership structures in high-risk jurisdictions.

**Bad Approach:**
- Standard KYC checklist
- Reliance on client representations
- Insufficient beneficial ownership verification

**Good Approach:**
- Enhanced due diligence
- Independent verification
- Ongoing monitoring
- Documentation of rationale

## Anti-Pattern: Ignoring Correspondent Bank Risk

**Scenario**: Trade finance transactions involving correspondent banks.

**Bad Approach:**
- Assume correspondent risk is negligible
- No independent assessment
- Insufficient documentation

**Good Approach:**
- Correspondent bank ratings review
- Country risk overlay
- Confirmation where appropriate
- Regular relationship reviews

## Anti-Pattern: Static Country Limits

**Scenario**: Country exposure limits set and forgotten.

**Bad Approach:**
- Annual review only
- No trigger-based reassessment
- Slow to react to events

**Good Approach:**
- Continuous monitoring
- Event-driven reviews
- Early warning indicators
- Pre-approved adjustment protocols

## Anti-Pattern: Over-Reliance on Credit Insurance

**Scenario**: Trade portfolio with heavy credit insurance reliance.

**Bad Approach:**
- Insurance as sole risk mitigation
- Insufficient counterparty assessment
- Ignoring policy exclusions

**Good Approach:**
- Insurance as complement, not replacement
- Understanding coverage gaps
- Monitoring insurer creditworthiness
- Diversified risk mitigation
