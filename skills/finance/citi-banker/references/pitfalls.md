# Extended Anti-Patterns for Citi Banking

> **Reference Document** — Version 2.0 | Updated March 2026

---

## Anti-Pattern: Inadequate KYC for Complex Structures

**Scenario**: Multi-layered ownership structures in high-risk jurisdictions.

**Bad Approach:**
- Standard KYC checklist
- Reliance on client representations
- Insufficient beneficial ownership verification
- No ongoing monitoring

**Good Approach:**
- Enhanced due diligence
- Independent verification
- Ongoing monitoring
- Documentation of rationale
- Senior approval for high-risk cases

---

## Anti-Pattern: Ignoring Correspondent Bank Risk

**Scenario**: Trade finance transactions involving correspondent banks.

**Bad Approach:**
- Assume correspondent risk is negligible
- No independent assessment
- Insufficient documentation
- No confirmation strategy

**Good Approach:**
- Correspondent bank ratings review
- Country risk overlay
- Confirmation where appropriate
- Regular relationship reviews
- Fallback bank identification

---

## Anti-Pattern: Static Country Limits

**Scenario**: Country exposure limits set and forgotten.

**Bad Approach:**
- Annual review only
- No trigger-based reassessment
- Slow to react to events
- No early warning system

**Good Approach:**
- Continuous monitoring
- Event-driven reviews
- Early warning indicators
- Pre-approved adjustment protocols
- Weekly dashboard review

---

## Anti-Pattern: Over-Reliance on Credit Insurance

**Scenario**: Trade portfolio with heavy credit insurance reliance.

**Bad Approach:**
- Insurance as sole risk mitigation
- Insufficient counterparty assessment
- Ignoring policy exclusions
- No insurer credit monitoring

**Good Approach:**
- Insurance as complement, not replacement
- Understanding coverage gaps
- Monitoring insurer creditworthiness
- Diversified risk mitigation
- Regular policy review

---

## Anti-Pattern: Underestimating Transformation Impact

**Scenario**: Client concerned about Citi's restructuring affecting service.

**Bad Approach:**
- Dismiss client concerns
- No contingency planning
- Ignore relationship manager changes
- Assume service continuity

**Good Approach:**
- Acknowledge transformation openly
- Provide transition plan
- Introduce backup contacts
- Service level agreement review
- Regular check-ins during transition

---

## Anti-Pattern: Not "Asking for the Business"

**Scenario**: Passive relationship management missing opportunities.

**Bad Approach:**
- Wait for clients to express needs
- Single-product focus
- No cross-sell initiative
- Reactive service model

**Good Approach:**
- Proactive needs assessment
- "Full wallet" analysis
- Regular opportunity reviews
- Commercial mindset: "Ask for the business"
- Fight for the complete relationship

---

## Anti-Pattern: Ignoring AI/Automation Changes

**Scenario**: Failing to leverage Citi's GenAI capabilities.

**Bad Approach:**
- Manual processes despite AI availability
- No technology roadmap
- Missed efficiency gains
- Competitive disadvantage

**Good Approach:**
- Evaluate GenAI use cases
- Implement document automation
- Fraud detection integration
- Continuous improvement
- Stay current with Citi tech stack

---

## Anti-Pattern: One-Size-Fits-All Pricing

**Scenario**: Applying standard pricing regardless of relationship value.

**Bad Approach:**
- Transactional pricing only
- No relationship consideration
- Missed bundling opportunities
- Price-driven client losses

**Good Approach:**
- Relationship-based pricing
- Multi-product bundling
- Value demonstration
- Competitive benchmarking
- "Full wallet" optimization

---

## Anti-Pattern: Neglecting Regulatory Changes

**Scenario**: Missed regulatory update affecting client operations.

**Bad Approach:**
- Reactive compliance
- No regulatory monitoring
- Client surprise notifications
- Last-minute scrambling

**Good Approach:**
- Proactive regulatory tracking
- Early client communication
- Transition planning support
- Thought leadership on changes
- Regulatory engagement

---

## Anti-Pattern: Insufficient Documentation

**Scenario**: Poor documentation leading to disputes or compliance issues.

**Bad Approach:**
- Verbal agreements
- Incomplete files
- No decision rationale
- Missing approvals

**Good Approach:**
- Comprehensive documentation
- Decision rationale recorded
- Proper approvals obtained
- Regular file reviews
- Audit-ready standards
