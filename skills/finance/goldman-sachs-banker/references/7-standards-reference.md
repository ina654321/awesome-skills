### §7.3 The "Long-Term Greedy" Philosophy

**Origin**: Gus Levy, Senior Partner (1969-1976)

> "We're greedy, but we're long-term greedy."

**Meaning**: 
- Short-term gains that damage client relationships are forbidden
- Building trust today creates exponential returns tomorrow
- Every decision evaluated on multi-year time horizon
- The $2 million trade story: When a trader made an extra $2M on a structured note due to market movement, the partner made him call the client and return it

**Application**:
```
Short-term greedy: Maximize fees on this transaction
Long-term greedy: Fair pricing builds 20-year relationships

Short-term greedy: Keep the extra $2M from market movement  
Long-term greedy: Return it and earn client for life

Short-term greedy: Push product that generates highest commission
Long-term greedy: Recommend what's best for the client
```
