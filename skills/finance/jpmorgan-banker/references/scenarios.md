### §1.1 Case Study: Salesforce Acquires Slack ($27.7B, 2020)

**Transaction Overview:**
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Acquirer | Salesforce (CRM) |
| Target | Slack Technologies |
| Transaction Value | $27.7 billion |
| Consideration | 100% stock |
| Premium to unaffected | 54% |
| Advisers | Goldman Sachs (Salesforce), Qatalyst (Slack) |

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Compete with Microsoft Teams in workplace collaboration
- Expand Salesforce's platform beyond CRM
- Cross-sell opportunity to existing Salesforce customers

**Valuation Analysis:**
| Metric | Value | Assessment |
|--------|-------|------------|
| EV/Revenue (NTM) | 25x | Premium multiple |
| EV/Revenue (run-rate) | 30x | Very high |
| Revenue growth (target) | 50%+ | Hypergrowth phase |
| Gross margin | 87% | SaaS-standard |

**Key Learning:**
Strategic acquisitions can justify premium multiples when they address existential competitive threats. Salesforce recognized Microsoft as a platform threat and paid aggressively to defend position.

## §3 · Trading Strategy Case Studies

### §3.1 Case Study: Basis Trade Collapse (March 2020)

**Strategy Overview:**
- Long Treasury bonds
- Short Treasury futures
- Capture small basis spread (~10-20bps)
- Highly leveraged (20-50x)

**What Went Wrong:**
- COVID panic caused massive flight to quality
- Treasury market liquidity evaporated
- Basis exploded to 50-100bps
- Hedge funds forced to unwind at losses

**JPMorgan Response:**
- Stepped in as market maker
- Provided liquidity when others withdrew
- Captured significant bid-ask spreads
- Demonstrated value of fortress balance sheet

**Lessons Learned:**
1. Basis trades carry liquidity risk, not just basis risk
2. Leverage amplifies tail risk
3. Market stress correlations approach 1.0
4. Balance sheet strength enables counter-cyclical profits

### §3.2 Case Study: Volatility Selling (2017-2018)

**Strategy:**
- Sell VIX futures and options
- Collect volatility risk premium
- Strategy worked consistently 2012-2017

**The Event (Feb 2018):**
- VIX spiked from 14 to 37 in one day
- XIV inverse VIX ETF terminated
- Short-vol funds lost 80%+ in hours

**Risk Management Takeaways:**
- Volatility selling is "picking up pennies in front of steamroller"
- Short gamma exposure requires dynamic hedging
- Position sizing must account for gap risk
- Correlation breakdown in stress (stocks/volatility)

## §4 · Risk Management Case Studies

### §4.1 Case Study: Archegos Capital Collapse (2021)

**Background:**
- Family office run by Bill Hwang
- $20B in assets under management
- Highly levered equity swaps
- Concentrated positions in media/Chinese tech stocks

**Failure Sequence:**
| Date | Event | Impact |
|------|-------|--------|
| March 22 | ViacomCBS announces stock offering | Stock -9% |
| March 23 | Additional block trades | Stocks -27% |
| March 25 | Margin calls triggered | Forced liquidation begins |
| March 26 | Banks unwind positions | $10B+ losses for prime brokers |

**Losses by Bank:**
| Bank | Loss | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Credit Suisse | $5.5B | Largest loss |
| Nomura | $2.9B | Significant hit |
| Morgan Stanley | $911M | Quick unwind minimized loss |
| Goldman Sachs | Minimal | Exited early |
| JPMorgan | Minimal | Limited exposure |

**Lessons Learned:**
1. Concentration limits must be enforced
2. Prime broker coordination failures
3. Hidden leverage in equity swaps
4. Due diligence on family office structures
5. JPMorgan's conservative approach paid off

## §5 · Regulatory Case Studies

### §5.1 Case Study: JPMorgan London Whale ($6B Loss, 2012)

**Background:**
- CIO (Chief Investment Office) in London
- Synthetic credit portfolio
- Bruno Iksil ("London Whale") accumulated massive position
- Hedging strategy became speculative

**Position Build-Up:**
| Timeframe | Position Size | Market Impact |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| 2011 | $10B notional | Normal market participant |
| Early 2012 | $50B notional | Moving markets |
| March 2012 | $100B+ notional | "Whale" nickname earned |

**Risk Management Failures:**
1. VaR model understated risk
2. Position limits not enforced
3. Independent risk oversight weak
4. Mark-to-market practices questioned

**Consequences:**
- $6.2 billion loss
- $920 million regulatory fine
- Dimon's "Tempest in a teapot" comment backfired
- Enhanced risk management frameworks implemented

**Legacy:**
- Accelerated Volcker Rule implementation
- Enhanced model risk management
- Strengthened independent risk oversight
- JPMorgan became more conservative

## §6 · Jamie Dimon Leadership Case Studies

### §6.1 Case Study: Bear Stearns Rescue (2008)

**Situation:**
- Bear Stearns facing liquidity crisis
- Fed facilitated acquisition
- JPMorgan acquired for $2/share (later $10/share)

**Dimon's Decision Framework:**
1. **Systemic Risk**: Bear failure would cascade
2. **Strategic Fit**: Prime brokerage, clearing capabilities
3. **Financial Terms**: Fed backstopped risky assets
4. **Execution Capability**: Confidence in integration

**Outcome:**
- Acquired $400B in assets for $1.2B
- Assumed significant liabilities
- Solidified JPMorgan's #1 position
- "Fortress balance sheet" tested and proven

**Dimon Quote:**
> "Buying a house on fire is different from buying a house."
