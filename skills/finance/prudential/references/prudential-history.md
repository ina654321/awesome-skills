# Prudential Financial: 150 Years of Strength and Stability

## Founding and Early Years (1873-1900)

### 1873 - The Beginning
- **Widows and Orphans Friendly Society** founded by John F. Dryden
- Inspired by British industrial insurance models
- Mission: Provide affordable life insurance to working-class families

### 1875 - The Prudential Friendly Society
- Official establishment date: **March 15, 1875**
- Location: Newark, New Jersey (still headquarters today)
- Initial capital: Approximately $25,000
- First policy issued: March 27, 1876

### 1877 - Incorporation
- Renamed **The Prudential Insurance Company of America**
- Began selling "industrial insurance"
- "Three Cents a Week" policy made coverage accessible

### 1896 - The Rock of Gibraltar
- Iconic logo adopted, symbolizing strength and stability
- Message: "The Prudential Has the Strength of Gibraltar"
- Still the corporate symbol today (130+ years)

### 1892 - Prudential Building
- Headquarters building opened in Newark
- Demonstrated company's growth and permanence

## Expansion and Innovation (1900-1966)

### 1909 - International Expansion
- First international offices: Toronto and Montreal, Canada
- Beginning of global footprint

### 1911 - $2 Billion Milestone
- $2 billion of insurance in force
- Significant growth from humble beginnings

### 1915 - Mutual Conversion
- Converted from stock to mutual insurer
- Policyholders became owners

### 1916 - Group Life Pioneer
- First group life insurance offered
- Expanded to workplace protection

### 1923 - Billion Dollar Assets
- Assets reached $1 billion
- Solid financial foundation established

### 1928 - Retirement Business
- First group pension contract
- Entry into retirement security

### 1948 - Regional Expansion
- First regional home office opened in Los Angeles
- Decentralized operations

### 1951 - Medical Insurance Pioneer
- Pioneered medical insurance in the U.S.
- Expanded protection offerings

### 1955 - Technology Leader
- One of the first U.S. corporations to install large-scale computers
- Early adopter of data processing

### 1964 - Variable Annuity Innovation
- Introduced first group variable annuity policy
- Product innovation leadership

### 1966 - Industry Leader
- Surpassed Metropolitan Life as largest U.S. insurance company
- Market leadership position

## Modern Era Transformation (1970-2001)

### 1970 - Variable Annuity First
- First insurance company to offer individual variable annuities
- Investment-linked retirement products

### 1981 - Bache Acquisition
- Acquired Bache Halsey Stuart Shields securities firm
- Entry into securities brokerage
- Later became Prudential Securities

### 1987 - Real Estate Expansion
- Entered residential realty business
- Diversification strategy

### 1994-1995 - Fortune 500 Recognition
- Added to Fortune 500 (ranked #5 in assets)
- Recognition as major financial services firm

### 1998-2001 - Demutualization
- Board announced demutualization plans (1998)
- Policyholders approved conversion (2001)
- **December 13, 2001:** Began trading on NYSE as PRU

## Public Company Era (2001-Present)

### 2001 - Public Debut
- Prudential Financial, Inc. (PRU) listed on NYSE
- Transition to publicly-traded company complete
- Opened new era of growth

### 2004 - American Skandia Acquisition
- Expanded retirement and investment markets
- Enhanced variable annuity business

### 2006 - Allstate Financial Acquisition
- Acquired Allstate Financial's annuity business
- Major expansion of retirement solutions

### 2012 - Pension Risk Transfer Leadership
- Landmark $25 billion General Motors buy-out
- $8 billion Verizon transaction
- Established market leadership in PRT

### 2014 - British Telecom Transaction
- $28 billion longevity reinsurance
- Expanded international PRT capabilities

### 2017 - Strategic Shift
- Focus on higher-growth, more capital-efficient businesses
- Reduction in market-sensitive product exposure

### 2024 - 150th Anniversary
- Celebrated 150 years since founding
- CEO Charles Lowrey's leadership transition announced
- Andy Sullivan named as next CEO (effective March 31, 2025)

### 2024 - Record Performance
- $26 billion pension liabilities safeguarded
- Ranked #1 in life and health insurance sector (Fortune World's Most Admired)
- Strong sales across retirement and insurance businesses

### 2025 - Leadership Transition
- Andy Sullivan becomes CEO (March 31, 2025)
- Charles Lowrey becomes Executive Chairman
- 17th consecutive year of dividend increase

## The Rock Symbol Legacy

### Evolution of the Logo

| Era | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| 1860s-1896 | Early concepts of Gibraltar imagery |
| 1896 | Official Rock of Gibraltar logo adopted |
| 1910s-1940s | Black and white variations, rectangular format |
| 1950s-1970s | Circular format standardized |
| 1977-1984 | Modern slanted logotype introduced |
| 1984-1996 | Blue and white color palette adopted |
| 1996-Present | Refined, modernized Rock symbol |

### Symbolism
- **Strength:** Financial stability and claims-paying ability
- **Stability:** 150 years of continuous operation
- **Trust:** Promise to be there when needed
- **Heritage:** Connection to founding principles

- Support through World Wars, Great Depression, pandemics
- Continuous philanthropic programs
- Financial wellness education initiatives

### Diversity and Inclusion
- Early hiring of women agents
- Commitment to diverse workforce
- Financial access initiatives
