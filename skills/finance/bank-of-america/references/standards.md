# Bank of America Regulatory Standards & Compliance

## Overview

Bank of America operates under a comprehensive regulatory framework as one of the largest systemically important financial institutions (SIFIs) in the United States. This document outlines the key regulatory standards, compliance frameworks, and risk management requirements.

---

## Primary Regulatory Bodies

### Federal Reserve (Fed)
- **Role**: Primary regulator for Bank of America Corporation (bank holding company)
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Capital adequacy supervision
  - Stress testing (CCAR)
  - Monetary policy implementation
  - Systemic risk monitoring

### Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC)
- **Role**: Primary regulator for national banks (Bank of America, N.A.)
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Safety and soundness examinations
  - Compliance with banking laws
  - Chartering and branching authority

### Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)
- **Role**: Consumer protection enforcement
- **Key Areas**:
  - Fair lending (ECOA, FHA)
  - Truth in Lending (TILA)
  - Real Estate Settlement Procedures (RESPA)
  - Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (UDAAP)

### Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
- **Role**: Securities regulation
- **Key Areas**:
  - Disclosure requirements
  - Merrill Lynch broker-dealer regulation
  - Investment advisory oversight

### Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA)
- **Role**: Self-regulatory organization for broker-dealers
- **Key Areas**:
  - Merrill Lynch supervision
  - Sales practice standards
  - Registration and licensing

## Capital Requirements

### Basel III Framework

| Ratio | Minimum | BofA Target (2024) |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) | 4.5% | 11.8% |
| Tier 1 Capital | 6% | ~13% |
| Total Capital | 8% | ~15% |
| Supplementary Leverage Ratio (SLR) | 3% | ~6% |

### Capital Buffers
- **Capital Conservation Buffer**: 2.5%
- **Global Systemically Important Bank (G-SIB) Surcharge**: ~3% (varies by year)
- **Countercyclical Capital Buffer**: 0-2.5% (as determined by Fed)

## Stress Testing (CCAR)

### Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review

**Process Timeline:**
1. **Data Submission**: January (FR Y-14 forms)
2. **Quantitative Results**: June
3. **Qualitative Assessment**: Ongoing
4. **Fed Response**: June (objection/non-objection)

**Stress Scenarios:**
- **Baseline**: Expected economic conditions
- **Adverse**: Moderate recession
- **Severely Adverse**: Severe global recession

**2024 Severely Adverse Scenario Key Variables:**
| Variable | Stress Level |
|----------|--------------|
| Unemployment | Peak at 10% |
| GDP Decline | -6.4% from pre-recession peak |
| Equity Prices | -40% decline |
| Corporate Credit Spreads | Widening to 550bps |
| Housing Prices | -36% decline |

**BofA 2024 CCAR Results:**
- Passed quantitative assessment
- No qualitative deficiencies noted
- Approved capital plan including dividends and buybacks

## Risk Management Framework

### Three Lines of Defense

**First Line: Business Units**
- Own risk management
- Day-to-day risk controls
- Self-assessment and monitoring

**Second Line: Risk Management Functions**
- Enterprise Risk Management (ERM)
- Compliance
- Financial crimes (AML, sanctions)

**Third Line: Internal Audit**
- Independent assurance
- Risk-based audit plan
- Reports to Board Audit Committee

### Risk Categories

**Credit Risk**
- Probability of Default (PD) models
- Loss Given Default (LGD) models
- Exposure at Default (EAD) models
- Credit portfolio management

**Market Risk**
- Value at Risk (VaR)
- Stressed VaR
- Incremental Risk Charge (IRC)
- Comprehensive Risk Measure (CRM)

**Operational Risk**
- Advanced Measurement Approach (AMA)
- Loss data collection
- Scenario analysis
- Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

**Liquidity Risk**
- Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR): >100%
- Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR): >100%
- Stress liquidity testing
- Contingency funding plan

**Compliance Risk**
- Regulatory change management
- Training and awareness
- Monitoring and testing
- Issue management

- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)

- Sanctions screening
- Prohibited transactions
- Blocked assets reporting
- SDN list monitoring

### BofA AML Program Components
1. Risk assessment
2. Policies and procedures
3. Training and awareness
4. Monitoring and surveillance
5. Investigation and reporting
6. Recordkeeping

## Consumer Protection

### Fair Lending Laws
- **Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)**: Prohibits discrimination in lending
- **Fair Housing Act (FHA)**: Prohibits discrimination in housing-related transactions
- **Community Reinvestment Act (CRA)**: Encourages meeting community credit needs

### Disclosure Requirements
- **Truth in Lending Act (TILA)**: Credit cost disclosure
- **Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA)**: Mortgage settlement disclosure
- **Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA)**: EFT rights and liabilities

### Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (UDAAP)
- Prohibition on unfair practices
- Prohibition on deceptive practices
- Prohibition on abusive practices (Dodd-Frank)
