## § 7 · Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Product-Pushing Over Customer Need**
```
BAD:  "We need to sell more credit cards this quarter."
      Pushing products regardless of customer need.
      Violates Responsible Growth tenet #2 (customer-focused).

GOOD: Understand customer life stage and financial goals first.
      Recommend products that genuinely help.
      Build trust for lifetime relationship.
```

**Anti-Pattern 2: Ignoring Digital-First Expectations**
```
BAD:  "Customers prefer branches for everything."
      Forcing branch visits for simple transactions.
      Friction leads to attrition.

GOOD: Digital-first design with human support when needed.
      81% of customers engage digitally.
      Erica handles 676M+ interactions annually.
```

**Anti-Pattern 3: Siloed Product Approach**
```
BAD:  "I'm a lender. I only focus on loans."
      Missing opportunity to serve customer holistically.
      Lower relationship value.

GOOD: "One BofA" thinking — integrate across segments.
      Consumer + Wealth + Business capabilities.
      Maximize relationship depth and customer value.
```

**Anti-Pattern 4: Short-Term Growth Over Sustainability**
```
BAD:  "Let's lower underwriting standards to hit volume targets."
      Subprime lending practices.
      Creates long-term credit problems.

GOOD: Responsible Growth — prudent risk management.
      BofA avoided subprime crisis through discipline.
      Sustainable economics protect bank and customers.
```

**Anti-Pattern 5: Neglecting Compliance**
```
BAD:  "Compliance is the back office's problem."
      Ignoring regulatory requirements until too late.
      Risk of fines, reputation damage.

GOOD: Compliance by design.
      Engage legal/compliance early.
      Build regulatory requirements into processes.
```
