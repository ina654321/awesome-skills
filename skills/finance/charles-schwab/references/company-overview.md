# Charles Schwab - Company Overview

## Corporate Profile

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Founded** | April 1971 (as First Commander Corporation) |
| **Founder** | Charles R. "Chuck" Schwab |
| **Headquarters** | Westlake, Texas (relocated from San Francisco in 2021) |
| **Legal Entity** | The Charles Schwab Corporation (NYSE: SCHW) |
| **Employees** | ~34,000 (as of 2024) |
| **Market Cap** | ~$132 billion |

## Scale & Reach

### Client Assets
- **Total Client Assets**: $10.31 trillion (November 2024)
- **Growth Under Bettinger**: From $1.14 trillion (2008) to $9.74 trillion (2024)
- **Milestone**: First exceeded $10 trillion in November 2024

### Account Statistics
- **Active Brokerage Accounts**: 36.5+ million
- **Banking Accounts**: 1.7+ million
- **Corporate Retirement Plan Participants**: 2.4+ million
- **RIA Advisory Accounts**: Custody for 15,000+ independent advisors
- **Total Accounts**: 43.2 million combined

### Geographic Presence
- **Branch Network**: 400+ locations across the United States
- **Expansion (2025)**: Adding 16 new branches, relocating/expanding 25 existing
- **Major Hubs**: Westlake TX, San Francisco CA, Denver CO, Orlando FL, Omaha NE
- **International**: Limited international presence primarily serving U.S. expats

## Business Model

### Revenue Streams
1. **Net Interest Income** (largest component): Earning spread on client cash
2. **Asset Management Fees**: ETF and mutual fund management fees
3. **Trading Revenue**: Payment for order flow (PFOF), though reduced post-commission elimination
4. **Advisory Fees**: Schwab Intelligent Portfolios Premium, wealth advisory services
5. **Banking Fees**: Lending, mortgage origination, credit cards

### Cost Structure
- **Operating Expense Ratio**: Targeting continued reduction through scale
- **Technology Investment**: ~3-4% of revenue annually
- **Competitive Advantage**: Scale enables lower costs per client than smaller competitors

## Competitive Position

### Market Rankings
| Segment | Position |
|---------|----------|
| RIA Custody | #1 (by AUM) |
| Retail Brokerage | Top 3 |
| ETF Issuer | #5 globally ($375B AUM) |
| Online Banking | Top 10 |

### Primary Competitors
- **Fidelity**: Closest competitor across all segments
- **Vanguard**: Competes on low-cost index funds and advisor services
- **Interactive Brokers**: Competes for active traders
- **E*TRADE** (Morgan Stanley): Competes for self-directed investors
- **Bank of America/Merrill**: Competes for affluent banking clients

### Competitive Differentiators
1. **Integrated Model**: Only major firm combining brokerage, banking, and advisory at scale
2. **Branch Network**: Physical presence for complex needs (Fidelity also has this)
3. **RIA Focus**: Built by and for independent advisors
4. **Pricing Leadership**: Consistently disrupted industry pricing (discount brokerage → free trades)

## Corporate Structure

### Major Subsidiaries
- **Charles Schwab & Co., Inc.**: Retail brokerage and advisory
- **Charles Schwab Bank, SSB**: Banking and lending operations
- **Charles Schwab Investment Management**: ETF and mutual fund management
- **Schwab Advisor Services**: RIA custody and support
- **TD Ameritrade, Inc.**: Former subsidiary (fully integrated 2023)

### Regulatory Oversight
- **SEC**: Securities brokerage and advisory
- **FINRA**: Broker-dealer regulation
- **FDIC**: Bank deposit insurance
- **CFTC/NFA**: Futures and options oversight
- **State Regulators**: Insurance and banking licenses

## Recent Strategic Developments

### TD Ameritrade Integration (2020-2023)
- **Acquisition Price**: $26 billion (all-stock)
- **Close Date**: October 6, 2020
- **Client Migration Complete**: September 2, 2023
- **Technology Retained**: thinkorswim, thinkpipes, iRebal
- **Cost Synergies**: $1.8-2.0 billion annual savings target

### 2023 Banking Crisis Response
- Faced significant deposit outflows due to "cash sorting"
- Utilized Federal Home Loan Bank advances ($45.6B peak)
- Paused share buybacks to conserve capital
- Demonstrated resilience of diversified business model
- Net interest margin pressure but no liquidity crisis

### Leadership Transition (2024-2025)
- Walt Bettinger announced retirement October 2024
- Rick Wurster assumed CEO role January 1, 2025
- Multi-year succession plan executed smoothly
- Bettinger continues as Executive Co-Chairman
