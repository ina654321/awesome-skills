# Active vs. Passive Investing at Fidelity

## The Debate

The investment world is divided between two philosophies:
- **Passive/Index Investing:** Match market returns at minimal cost
- **Active Investing:** Attempt to beat the market through research and selection

Fidelity is unique—they excel at both.

## Fidelity's Position

**On Index Investing:**
Fidelity offers some of the industry's lowest-cost index funds, including the revolutionary ZERO funds with 0.00% expense ratios. They acknowledge that in efficient markets (large-cap U.S. stocks), indexing is often the wisest choice.

**On Active Management:**
Fidelity built its reputation on active management. With 170+ global analysts, a rigorous research process, and legendary managers like Peter Lynch and Will Danoff, they believe skilled managers can add value in less efficient market segments.

### Investor Profiles for Indexing

- **Cost-conscious:** Every basis point matters
- **Tax-sensitive:** Index funds generate fewer capital gains
- **Hands-off:** Don't want to monitor manager performance
- **Believers in market efficiency:** Accept market returns

## When to Choose Active

### Less Efficient Markets (Active May Add Value)

| Market Segment | Inefficiency Source | Potential Active Advantage |
|----------------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Small-Cap U.S. | Limited analyst coverage | Information advantage |
| Emerging Markets | Information asymmetry, access | On-the-ground research |
| High-Yield Bonds | Credit research matters | Avoid defaults, find gems |
| Specialized Sectors | Requires deep expertise | Concentrated knowledge |
| International Small-Cap | Hard to access, research | Local presence |

### Investor Profiles for Active

- **Believers in manager skill:** Willing to pay for outperformance
- **Access to institutional share classes:** Lower fees through 401(k)
- **Long time horizons:** Can withstand periods of underperformance
- **Specific needs:** Income generation, downside protection

## Evaluating Active Funds

### Due Diligence Checklist

**Manager Tenure:**
- Minimum 5 years at fund
- Prefer 10+ years
- Watch for manager changes

**Expense Ratio:**
- Institutional/share class K: <0.75%
- Retail: <1.00%
- Every 0.10% matters

**Active Share:**
- Measures differentiation from benchmark
- >60% considered truly active
- <40% is "closet indexing" (expensive index fund)

**Turnover:**
- <50% annually preferred
- High turnover = tax inefficiency + transaction costs

**Track Record:**
- Outperformance vs. benchmark (net of fees)
- Consistency (not just one good year)
- Downside protection in bear markets

### Red Flags

- Manager change without succession plan
- Assets growing rapidly ($50B+ becoming unwieldy)
- Style drift (fund no longer doing what it says)
- Excessive marketing of recent performance
- High fees without consistent outperformance
