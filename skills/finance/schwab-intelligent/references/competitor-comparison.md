## Platform Deep Dives

### Schwab Intelligent Portfolios

**Target Investor:** Existing Schwab clients, fee-conscious investors, those wanting hybrid option

**Strengths:**
- Zero advisory fee (Standard tier)
- Seamless integration with full Schwab ecosystem
- Premium option with unlimited CFP access at flat fee
- Strong brand reputation and customer service
- Automatic rebalancing and TLH

**Weaknesses:**
- Mandatory cash allocation (6-30%) acts as hidden cost
- $5,000 minimum excludes small investors
- TLH requires $50,000 minimum
- Limited customization (can't fully exclude asset classes)
- 2022 SEC settlement over cash allocation disclosure

**Cost Analysis (Annual on $100,000):**
- Advisory fee: $0
- ETF expenses: ~$120 (0.12%)
- Cash drag opportunity cost: ~$300-500 (assuming 7-10% cash at 4-5% market return differential)
- **Effective cost: 0.42-0.62%**

---

### Betterment

**Target Investor:** Goal-focused planners, those wanting SRI options

**Strengths:**
- Industry-leading goal-based planning tools
- Excellent user interface and mobile experience
- Tax-loss harvesting on all taxable accounts
- Socially responsible investing (SRI) portfolios
- Tax-coordinated portfolio (optimizes account location)

**Weaknesses:**
- 0.25% advisory fee
- Premium requires $100,000 minimum
- No 401(k) management
- Higher fund expenses than some competitors

**Cost Analysis (Annual on $100,000):**
- Advisory fee: $250 (0.25%)
- Fund expenses: ~$90 (0.09%)
- Cash drag: Minimal (near-zero cash)
- **Effective cost: 0.34%**

---

### Wealthfront

**Target Investor:** Tech-savvy millennials, those wanting advanced features

**Strengths:**
- Direct indexing (US stocks) for accounts $100,000+
- Risk parity portfolio option ($100,000+)
- Portfolio line of credit (borrow against investments)
- Comprehensive financial planning tool ("Path")
- Daily tax-loss harvesting

**Weaknesses:**
- No human advisor access (pure robo)
- 0.25% advisory fee
- Direct indexing requires high minimum
- No joint accounts with direct indexing

**Cost Analysis (Annual on $100,000):**
- Advisory fee: $250 (0.25%)
- Fund expenses: ~$80 (0.08%)
- Cash drag: Minimal
- **Effective cost: 0.33%**

**Note:** Direct indexing can add 0.50-1.00% in after-tax returns through enhanced TLH

---

### Vanguard Digital Advisor

**Target Investor:** Cost-minimizing passive investors, Vanguard fund loyalists

**Strengths:**
- Lowest advisory fee (0.20%)
- Uses ultra-low-cost Vanguard ETFs (~0.05% expense ratio)
- Strong parent company reputation
- Tax-loss harvesting included
- Low $100 minimum

**Weaknesses:**
- Minimal customization options
- Basic digital interface
- Premium (Personal Advisor) requires $50,000 minimum
- Limited to Vanguard funds

**Cost Analysis (Annual on $100,000):**
- Advisory fee: $200 (0.20%)
- Fund expenses: ~$50 (0.05%)
- Cash drag: Minimal
- **Effective cost: 0.25% (industry low)**

---

### Fidelity Go

**Target Investor:** Beginning investors, small accounts, Fidelity clients

**Strengths:**
- $0 advisory fee for accounts under $25,000
- Uses zero-expense-ratio Fidelity Flex funds
- No minimum investment
- Strong parent company with physical branches
- Automatic rebalancing

**Weaknesses:**
- No tax-loss harvesting
- 0.35% fee above $25,000 (higher than competitors)
- Limited customization
- No human advisor option
- Conservative portfolio construction

**Cost Analysis (Annual on $100,000):**
- Advisory fee: $350 (0.35%)
- Fund expenses: $0 (Flex funds)
- Cash drag: Minimal
- **Effective cost: 0.35%**

## When to Choose Each Platform

### Choose Schwab Intelligent If:
- You already bank/broker with Schwab
- You value zero advisory fees
- You want option to upgrade to human advice
- You don't mind 6-30% cash allocation
- You have $5,000+ to invest

### Choose Betterment If:
- Goal-based planning is important
- You want socially responsible investing
- You value excellent mobile experience
- Tax-loss harvesting is priority
- You're comfortable paying 0.25% fee

### Choose Wealthfront If:
- You want advanced features (direct indexing, risk parity)
- You might want portfolio line of credit
- You prefer pure automation (no human advisors)
- You're tech-savvy and app-focused
- You have $500-100,000 to invest

### Choose Vanguard Digital If:
- Minimizing costs is top priority
- You're satisfied with Vanguard funds
- You want simple, proven approach
- You have $100+ to start
- You may upgrade to Personal Advisor later

### Choose Fidelity Go If:
- You have less than $25,000 to invest
- You want truly zero fees (under $25k)
- You already use Fidelity
- Tax-loss harvesting isn't important
- You want zero-expense-ratio funds
