# Acquisition History

## Overview

Raymond James has pursued a disciplined, culture-first acquisition strategy focused on strategic fit, cultural alignment, and long-term value creation. Unlike many competitors, Raymond James prioritizes organic growth supplemented by select strategic acquisitions.

**Acquisition Philosophy:**
> "We have used strategic mergers to grow throughout our history when the timing and pricing were right and, most importantly, when there was a strong cultural fit and clear path for integration."

## Major Acquisitions

### 1. Morgan Keegan (2012)

**Transaction Overview:**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Acquisition Date** | April 2, 2012 |
| **Seller** | Regions Financial Corporation |
| **Purchase Price** | $1.211 billion |
| **Financing** | Equity issuance, senior notes, credit facility |

**Assets Acquired:**
- Morgan Keegan & Company, Inc. (broker-dealer)
- MK Holding, Inc. and affiliates
- Headquarters in Memphis, Tennessee
- 57 full-service offices in 20 states
- ~3,100 employees
- ~900 financial advisors (892 retained as of Sept 2012)

**Strategic Rationale:**

1. **Expanded Private Client Business**
   - Significantly increased advisor count
   - Geographic expansion into new markets
   - Enhanced scale and capabilities

2. **Strengthened Capital Markets**
   - "One of the industry's top fixed income and public finance groups"
   - "Significant equity capital markets division"
   - Combined fixed income operations ~3x legacy size

3. **Cultural Fit**
   - Regional firm culture similar to Raymond James
   - Client-first orientation
   - Long-term relationship focus

**Integration Results:**

| Metric | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| Advisor Retention | 892 of 900+ (~99%) retained |
| Platform Migration | Completed Q2 FY2013 |
| Integration Costs | ~$99 million total |
| Client Assets | ~$85 billion added to AUA |

**Impact:**
> "With the addition of Morgan Keegan, we are one of the country's largest wealth management and investment banking firms, affording us even greater ability to support our financial advisors and retail and institutional clients."

### 2. Alex. Brown (2016)

**Historical Context:**

**Transaction Overview:**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|

1. **Prestigious Brand Heritage**

2. **High-Net-Worth Focus**
   - Complemented Raymond James's core affluent client base

   - Alex. Brown brand preserved
   - Boutique culture maintained
   - Integration approach minimized disruption

| Metric | Outcome |
|--------|---------|

**Key Quote:**
> "This advisor team is very attracted to the boutique partnership culture that Alex. Brown stood for. It harkens back to the boutique partnerships of the '80s and '90s and that client-focused approach to the business that many advisors and many clients are longing for."
> — G. Haig Ariyan, President, Alex. Brown

**Unique Structure:**
- Alex. Brown remains separate division within Raymond James
- Maintains distinct brand identity
- Continued distribution partnership with Deutsche Bank for certain products

## Acquisition Discipline

### Selection Criteria

Raymond James evaluates acquisitions against strict criteria:

1. **Cultural Fit (First and Foremost)**
   - Client-first values alignment
   - Advisor-centric culture
   - Long-term orientation
   - Ethical standards

2. **Strategic Fit**
   - Complements existing capabilities
   - Clear path to integration
   - Enhances competitive position
   - Geographic or client segment expansion

3. **Financial Discipline**
   - Appropriate pricing
   - Clear return on investment
   - Manageable integration costs
   - Accretive to shareholders

4. **Integration Feasibility**
   - Technology compatibility
   - Retention risk assessment
   - Operational integration plan
   - Timeline clarity

### Cultural Assessment

**Key Questions:**
- Do they put clients first?
- Do they value advisor independence?
- Is their focus long-term or short-term?
- Will they strengthen or dilute our culture?

**Paul Shoukry on M&A:**
> "We're really focused on quality over quantity. We can continue to be a leading grower in wealth management, which we have been on a pretty consistent basis. And doing it in a way that we feel sustainable."

**Key Differentiator:** Raymond James's culture-first approach and respect for advisor autonomy drive superior retention.

## Acquisition Strategy Evolution

### Historical Approach (1962-2010)
- Organic growth as primary driver
- Small, tuck-in acquisitions
- Individual advisor recruiting

### Strategic Expansion (2010-2020)
- Larger, transformative deals (Morgan Keegan)
- Brand acquisitions (Alex. Brown)
- International expansion

### Current Approach (2020-Present)
- Return to organic focus
- Selective strategic acquisitions
- Asset management expansion (Clark Capital)
- Technology-enabled capabilities

**Consistent Throughout:** Cultural fit as non-negotiable requirement.
