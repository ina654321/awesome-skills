# Advisor Affiliation Models (AdvisorChoice)

## Overview

Raymond James offers four distinct affiliation options through its **AdvisorChoice** program, allowing advisors to select the model that best fits their business philosophy, desired level of support, and entrepreneurial goals.

**Core Principle:** "Advisors are viewed as clients of the firm. We provide world-class resources and support to help run their business on their terms."

## 1. Raymond James & Associates (RJA) — Traditional Employee

**Model:** Traditional W2 Employee

### Description
The traditional employee model reflects how advisors have historically affiliated with broker-dealers. Advisors work in a traditional branch setting supported by local management and administrative staff.

### Key Characteristics

**Support Provided:**
- Branch office infrastructure
- Dedicated support staff
- Local branch manager supervision
- Human resources support
- Compliance oversight
- Technology platform
- Marketing resources

**Compensation:**
- Lower gross payout than independent models
- Comprehensive benefits package
- Retirement plans
- Deferred compensation

**Responsibilities:**
- Client service and acquisition
- Financial planning and investment management
- Limited administrative burden

### Best For
- Advisors who prefer comprehensive support
- Those transitioning from wirehouses
- Advisors focused on client relationships, not business operations

---

## 2. Raymond James Advisor Select (RJAS) — Independent Employee/Quasi-Independent

**Model:** Hybrid W2 Employee

### Description
This model combines features of traditional employee status with a level of autonomy similar to independent contractors. Advisors have more independence regarding office location, staffing, and day-to-day operations while still accessing firm administrative support.

### Key Characteristics

**Support Provided:**
- Administrative support (HR, compliance, payroll, technology)
- Higher gross payout than RJA
- More autonomy in office decisions
- Flexible staffing options

**Compensation:**
- Net profitability-based compensation
- Higher gross payout than traditional employee
- Benefits package available

**Responsibilities:**
- Office location selection
- Staffing decisions
- Day-to-day operations
- Client service and business development

### Best For
- Advisors wanting more control than RJA
- Those building their own practice identity
- Advisors transitioning toward independence

---

## 3. Raymond James Financial Services — Independent Contractor Division (RJFS-ICD)

**Model:** Independent Contractor

### Description
Independent contractors receive higher payouts than employee model advisors and have direct control over all resources, operations, and costs associated with running their businesses.

### Key Characteristics

**Support Model:**
- Access to full firm capabilities on à la carte basis
- Self-clearing broker-dealer backing
- Compliance support available
- Technology platform access
- Marketing resources optional

**Business Control:**
- Office leasing decisions
- Equipment and technology choices
- Staff hiring and management
- Full P&L responsibility
- Business ownership and equity

**Compensation:**
- Highest gross payout percentage
- Responsible for all business costs
- Net income = gross revenue minus expenses

**Flexibility:**
- With approval, can conduct approved business activities
- Insurance products
- Independent RIA services
- Accounting and tax services

### Best For
- Entrepreneurial advisors
- Those wanting maximum independence
- Advisors with established practices
- Business owners building equity

---

## 4. Corporate RIA — Fee-Based RIA Affiliation

**Model:** All-Inclusive RIA Support

### Description
Fee-based advisors can get the RIA experience while streamlining many of its burdens with Raymond James's all-inclusive Corporate RIA affiliation model.

### Key Characteristics

**RIA Benefits Without the Burden:**
- Fee-only advisory structure
- Streamlined operations
- Compliance support
- Technology platform
- Custody services

**Services Provided:**
- RIA operational infrastructure
- Compliance oversight
- Technology and reporting
- Investment platform access
- Business consulting support

### Best For
- Fee-only advisors
- Advisors transitioning to fee-based model
- Those wanting RIA structure with operational support

## Choosing the Right Model

### Key Considerations

1. **Support vs. Independence**
   - How much administrative support do you need?
   - Do you want to focus on clients or business operations?

2. **Compensation Structure**
   - Do you prefer higher gross payout with business costs?
   - Or lower payout with comprehensive support?

3. **Business Ownership**
   - Do you want to build equity in your practice?
   - Are you planning for succession?

4. **Client Service Model**
   - Fee-based vs. commission-based?
   - Comprehensive planning vs. investment management?

5. **Cultural Fit**
   - Do you value autonomy or collaboration?
   - What type of advisor community do you prefer?
