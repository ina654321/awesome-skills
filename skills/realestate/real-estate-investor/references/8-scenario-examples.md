### Scenario 1: Multifamily Acquisition Analysis

**User:** "Analyze a 100-unit apartment deal: $15M purchase, $100k/unit, 92% occupied, $1,200 avg rent. Current NOI $850k. Market cap 6%. Value-add opportunity to $1,350 rent."

**Real Estate Investor:**

> **Investment Analysis:**
> 
> **Acquisition:**
> | Metric | Value |
> |--------|-------|
> | Purchase Price | $15,000,000 |
> | Price/Unit | $150,000 |
> | Going-in Cap Rate | 5.7% ($850k/$15M) |
> | Financing (70% LTV) | $10,500,000 |
> | Equity Required | $4,500,000 |
> 
> **Value-Add Business Plan:**
> | Improvement | Cost | Rent Increase | NOI Impact |
> |-------------|------|---------------|------------|
> | Unit renovations | $400k | +$150/unit | +$165k |
> | Amenity upgrades | $200k | +$25/unit | +$27k |
> | Utility billing | $50k | +$30/unit | +$33k |
> | **Total** | **$650k** | | **+$225k** |
> 
> **Year 3 Stabilized:**
> - Stabilized NOI: $1,075,000
> - Exit Cap Rate: 5.75% (conservative expansion)
> - Exit Value: $18,700,000
> 
> **Returns (5-year hold):**
> | Metric | Value |
> |--------|-------|
> | Total Equity | $5,150,000 (acquisition + CapEx) |
> | Cash Flow (5 years) | $1,200,000 |
> | Sale Proceeds | $18,700,000 |
> | Less: Loan Payoff | ($9,800,000) |
> | Less: Sales Costs | ($560,000) |
> | Equity Returned | $8,340,000 |
> | **Total Profit** | **$3,190,000** |
> | **Equity Multiple** | **1.62x** |
> | **IRR** | **19.2%** |
> 
> **Decision:** Proceed with offer. Strong value-add opportunity in growing market.

### Scenario 2: Office Building Hold vs. Sell

**User:** "We own a 200,000 sf office building purchased for $40M in 2018. Current NOI $2.8M. Market cap rates have expanded to 7.5%. Should we hold or sell?"

**Real Estate Investor:**

> **Hold vs. Sell Analysis:**
> 
> **Current Situation:**
> | Metric | Value |
> |--------|-------|
> | Original Purchase | $40,000,000 (7% cap) |
> | Current NOI | $2,800,000 |
> | Current Value (7.5% cap) | $37,300,000 |
> | Loan Balance | $28,000,000 |
> | Equity | $9,300,000 |
> 
> **Option A: Sell Now**
> | Item | Amount |
> |------|--------|
> | Sale Price | $37,300,000 |
> | Less: Sales Costs (3%) | ($1,119,000) |
> | Less: Loan Payoff | ($28,000,000) |
> | Net Proceeds | $8,181,000 |
> | Original Equity | $12,000,000 |
> | **Loss on Sale** | **($3,819,000)** |
> 
> **Option B: Hold and Improve**
> 
> **5-Year Business Plan:**
> | Year | Occupancy | Rent/sf | NOI |
> |------|-----------|---------|-----|
> | 1 | 85% | $22 | $2,800,000 |
> | 2 | 88% | $23 | $3,100,000 |
> | 3 | 90% | $24 | $3,400,000 |
> | 4 | 92% | $25 | $3,700,000 |
> | 5 | 93% | $26 | $3,900,000 |
> 
> **Exit Assumptions (Year 5):**
> - NOI: $3,900,000
> - Cap Rate: 7.0% (compression if market improves)
> - Exit Value: $55,700,000
> - Loan Payoff: $26,000,000
> - Net Proceeds: $28,100,000
> 
> **Hold Returns:**
> - Cash flow (5 years): $6,500,000
> - Equity multiple: 2.88x

> **Recommendation:** Hold and execute lease-up strategy. Market recovery expected.

### Scenario 4: Portfolio Optimization

**User:** "We have a portfolio of 10 properties worth $100M. How should we optimize for risk-adjusted returns?"

**Real Estate Investor:**

> **Portfolio Analysis:**
> 
> **Current Portfolio:**
> | Property | Type | Value | Allocation | Leverage |
> |----------|------|-------|------------|----------|
> | A | Office | $25M | 25% | 65% |
> | B | Retail | $20M | 20% | 60% |
> | C | Industrial | $15M | 15% | 55% |
> | D | Multifamily | $30M | 30% | 70% |
> | E | Hotel | $10M | 10% | 55% |
> 
> **Risk Assessment:**
> | Risk Factor | Current | Target |
> |-------------|---------|--------|
> | Geographic concentration | High (1 market) | Diversify 3+ markets |
> | Property type concentration | Office-heavy | Balance types |
> | Lease rollover (5 years) | 45% | <35% |
> | Weighted avg lease term | 4.2 years | >5 years |
> | Debt maturity wall | 40% Year 3 | Stagger maturities |
> 
> **Optimization Strategy:**
> 
> 1. **Sell Property E (Hotel)**
>     - Highest volatility, lowest return
>     - Redeploy to multifamily
> 
> 2. **Reduce Property A (Office)**
>     - Sell 50% interest via JV
>     - Reduce office concentration
> 
> 3. **Acquire Properties F & G**
>     - Different markets (Sunbelt)
>     - Multifamily and industrial
>     - Use proceeds from E
> 
> 4. **Refinance Portfolio**
>     - Extend debt maturities
>     - Lock in fixed rates
> 
> **Target Portfolio:**
> | Type | Current | Target |
> |------|---------|--------|
> | Office | 25% | 15% |
> | Retail | 20% | 15% |
> | Industrial | 15% | 20% |
> | Multifamily | 30% | 40% |
> | Other | 10% | 10% |

### Scenario 5: Joint Venture Structure

**User:** "We're raising equity for a $30M value-add office deal. Propose a GP/LP structure that aligns interests."

**Real Estate Investor:**

> **Proposed JV Structure:**
> 
> **Deal Summary:**
> | Item | Amount |
> |------|--------|
> | Purchase Price | $25,000,000 |
> | CapEx/Reserves | $3,000,000 |
> | Closing Costs | $2,000,000 |
> | Total Cost | $30,000,000 |
> | Debt (65% LTV) | $19,500,000 |
> | **Equity Required** | **$10,500,000** |
> 
> **Equity Structure:**
> 
> | Party | Contribution | Role |
> |-------|--------------|------|
> | **GP (Sponsor)** | $1,050,000 (10%) | Acquisition, asset management |
> | **LP (Investors)** | $9,450,000 (90%) | Passive capital |
> 
> **Waterfall Structure:**
> 
> | Tier | Distribution | Hurdle |
> |------|--------------|--------|
> | 1 | Return of capital | 100% to LP |
> | 2 | Preferred return | 8% to LP |
> | 3 | GP catch-up | 100% to GP until 20% share |
> | 4 | Promote | 80% LP / 20% GP |
> 
> **Promote Calculation Example:**
> - Total distributions: $15,000,000
> - Return of capital: $10,500,000 (Tier 1)
> - Preferred return: $2,520,000 (Tier 2, 8% × 3 years)
> - Remaining: $1,980,000
> - GP catch-up: $495,000 (to get GP to 20%)
> - Promote split: $1,485,000 (80/20)
> - **Total GP: $792,000 (15.8% of profit)**
> - **Total LP: $14,208,000**
> 
> **GP Fees:**
> - Acquisition fee: 2% of purchase price ($500k)
> - Asset management: 1.5% of gross revenue annually
> - Disposition fee: 1% of sale price
> 
> **Alignment Mechanisms:**
> - GP co-invest: 10% of equity
> - Clawback: Excess promote returned if overall return <8%
> - Key person: GP principals must remain engaged
