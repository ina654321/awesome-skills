# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Lease Renewal Workflow

```
Phase 1: Renewal Assessment (90 days before expiration)
├── Review tenant payment history: on-time payment rate, late fees
├── Review property condition: maintenance requests, violations
├── Assess market rent: comparable units, demand levels
├── Calculate renewal economics: turnover cost vs. renewal rent
└── Draft renewal offer: rent adjustment, lease term, conditions

Phase 2: Tenant Communication (60–90 days before)
├── Deliver renewal notice per state law (30–60 day notice requirement)
├── Schedule in-person or phone conversation
├── Discuss any tenant concerns or maintenance issues
├── Present renewal terms clearly
└── Document all communications

Phase 3: Negotiation (as needed)
├── If tenant pushes back: consider rent hold or reduction
├── Factor in renewal vs. turnover cost: turnover = 1–2 months rent
├── If not renewing: provide proper notice per state law
└── Document decision and rationale

Phase 4: Execution (30 days before expiration)
├── Execute new lease or renewal addendum
├── Collect security deposit adjustment (if any rent change)
├── Update property management records
└── Schedule pre-move-out inspection for departing tenant

Phase 5: Post-Renewal Follow-Up
├── Confirm renter's insurance is current (if required)
├── Update HOA/owner on renewal status
└── Log renewal in property management system
```

## 8.2 Eviction Workflow

```
Step 1: Notice Served
├── Pay-or-Quit (3-day in most states) for non-payment
├── 30/60-day notice for month-to-month termination
├── Material lease violation notice
└── Document service: certified mail + posting

Step 2: Wait Period (per state law)
├── Most states: 3–5 days after notice before filing
├── Some states require court-approved notices
└── Document all tenant communication attempts

Step 3: Court Filing
├── File unlawful detainer (eviction) action
├── Pay filing fee (varies by jurisdiction)
├── Serve summons and complaint on tenant
└── File proof of service with court

Step 4: Court Hearing
├── Present evidence: lease, notice, payment records
├── Tenant may appear and contest
├── Judgment entered: eviction ordered or case dismissed
└── If judgment for landlord: writ of possession issued

Step 5: Enforcement
├── Sheriff posts lockout notice (3–5 days in most states)
├── Physical lockout on specified date
├── Return of personal property (state-mandated process)
└── Collect judgment (if applicable): wage garnishment, collections
```

## 8.3 Annual Budget Workflow

1. Review prior year actuals: income, expenses, variances
2. Project income: rent roll changes, planned increases, vacancy
3. Project expenses: fixed costs (taxes, insurance), variable (utilities, maintenance)
4. Reserve planning: capex, replacements, capital improvements
5. Owner approval: present draft budget for owner sign-off
6. Implement: communicate rent increases, vendor contracts
7. Quarterly review: compare actual vs. budget, adjust forecasts
