# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Investment Property Analysis Workflow

```
Phase 1: Market & Submarket Research
├── Macroeconomic analysis: population, employment, absorption
├── Submarket identification: supply/demand balance, rent growth trends
├── Comparable sales: cap rates, NOI multiples, price per unit/sf
├── Comparable rents: comparable properties, occupancy, rent growth
└── Regulatory review: rent control, zoning, eviction rules

Phase 2: Property-Level Due Diligence
├── Physical inspection: deferred maintenance, capex reserves needed
├── Rent roll analysis: lease terms, expirations, tenant credit
├── Financial statement review: T-12 income/expense history
├── Title/survey review: easements, encumbrances, zoning
└── Environmental: Phase I ESA, asbestos/lead paint if pre-1980

Phase 3: Financial Modeling
├── Purchase price negotiation range based on DCF and comps
├── Capital structure: debt/equity split, loan terms
├── Pro forma: stabilized NOI, vacancy allowance, expense growth
├── Exit scenario: refinance, sale, 1031 exchange
└── Sensitivity analysis: rent growth, cap rate, interest rate stress

Phase 4: Offer & Negotiation
├── Letter of Intent (LOI): non-binding term sheet
├── Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA): binding terms, contingencies
├── Due diligence period: 30–60 days for full investigation
└── Closing: title, escrow, lender coordination

Phase 5: Value-Add / Asset Management
├── Business plan: renovation scope, lease-up strategy
├── Property operations: PM selection, vendor contracts
├── Performance tracking: monthly NOI, variance to budget
└── Exit execution: marketing, buyer qualification, closing
```

## 8.2 DCF Model Framework

```
Step 1: Identify holding period (5–10 years typical)
Step 2: Project annual income: rents, other income
Step 3: Subtract operating expenses (escalate 2–3%/yr)
Step 4: Calculate NOI for each year
Step 5: Apply cap rate to terminal NOI → Terminal Value
Step 6: Discount all cash flows at target IRR (10–15%)
Step 7: NPV = Sum of PV of cash flows + PV of terminal value
Step 8: Compare to purchase price → Acquisition spread
```

## 8.3 Underwriting Checklist

- [ ] Rent roll: 12-month history, lease abstracts, security deposits
- [ ] T-12 income/expense statement: trailing 12 months actual
- [ ] Capital expenditure history: replaced items, upcoming capex
- [ ] Property tax assessment: challenge potential?
- [ ] Insurance: current premiums, coverage adequacy
- [ ] Utility history: landlord-paid vs. tenant-paid
- [ ] Management agreement: third-party or self-managed
- [ ] Tenant credit quality: credit scores, payment history
- [ ] Lease terms: CAM reconciliations, renewal options
- [ ] Zoning and land use: conforming use, variance status
