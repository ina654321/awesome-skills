# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Using in-place rents without vacancy adjustment** | 🔴 High | Apply market vacancy (5–8%) even if currently 100% occupied |
| 2 | **Ignoring capex reserves in NOI** | 🔴 High | Always deduct 5–10% of gross income for capex; don't treat NOI as cash flow |
| 3 | **Underwriting to current market rents on value-add deals** | 🔴 High | Use below-market rents during lease-up; time-value the rent growth |
| 4 | **Assuming fixed interest rates on variable-rate debt** | 🟡 Medium | Stress-test at +200–300 bps rate increase |
| 5 | **Not reviewing lease abstracts thoroughly** | 🟡 Medium |tenant improvement allowances, free rent periods, renewal options can significantly impact returns |
| 6 | **Overestimating exit cap rate compression** | 🟡 Medium | Exit at same or +25 bps wider than entry cap rate |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Always use a T-12 income/expense statement** — trailing 12 months from actual rent rolls
2. **Model three scenarios:** Base, bull, bear — stress-test rent growth and exit cap rate
3. **Verify DSCR and LTV requirements** — know lender constraints before bidding
4. **Include transaction costs:** Transfer taxes (1–2%), legal ($50K+), lender fees (1–2%), broker commissions
5. **Track equity multiple, not just IRR:** IRR can be manipulated by holding period; equity multiple is absolute
6. **Review HOA/POA documents:** Special assessments and underfunded reserves can destroy returns

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Rent roll reviewed: all units, lease terms, security deposits, concessions
- [ ] T-12 income/expense statement: actual historical data verified
- [ ] Capex reserve calculated: 5–10% of gross income
- [ ] Capital structure: debt/equity, interest rate, amortization, reserves
- [ ] Market analysis: rent comparables, vacancy trends, supply pipeline
- [ ] Physical inspection: deferred maintenance, remaining useful life
- [ ] Environmental review: Phase I ESA completed
- [ ] Title/survey: easements, encroachments, zoning confirmation
- [ ] Lease review: TI allowances, free rent, renewal options, CAM reconciliations
- [ ] Exit scenario modeled: refinance vs. sale at multiple cap rates
- [ ] Tax analysis: depreciation schedule, 1031 planning, capital gains exposure
