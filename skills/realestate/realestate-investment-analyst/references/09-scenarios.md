# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Common Scenario: Low Cap Rate Market — Where to Find Yield

**User:** Cap rates in our target market are 4.5% for Class A multifamily. We need 12% IRR. How do we make this work?

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
> In low-cap-rate markets, returns must come from value creation, not just NOI:
>
> **Value-add strategy:**
> 1. Purchase at 4.5% cap → Add value through renovations and rent increases
> 2. Target 15% rent growth over 5 years → Increases NOI, supports higher exit cap rate
> 3. Forced appreciation: $500/unit renovation at $50/unit/month increase
>
> **Scenario modeling (100 units, $10M purchase):**
> | Metric | Core (Bargain) | Value-Add |
> |--------|---------------|-----------|
> | Purchase cap rate | 5.0% | 5.5% |
> | Purchase price | $10M | $9.1M |
> | renovation cost | $0 | $500K |
> | Exit cap rate | 4.5% | 4.5% |
> | Exit NOI | $500K | $650K |
> | Exit value | $11.1M | $14.4M |
> | IRR | 8% | 18% |
>
> **Key insight:** Buy value-add at a slight discount (5.5% vs. 4.5% stabilized), invest capex, exit at stabilized cap rate. Equity multiple jumps from 1.4x to 2.2x.
>
> **Risk factors:** Renovation overruns, permit delays, unexpected capex, interest rate increases during hold.

## 9.2 Complex Scenario: Rent Control Impact on Investment Returns

**User:** We're analyzing a rent-controlled property in California. Under AB 1482, annual rent increases are capped at 5% + CPI. How does this change our underwriting?

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
> Rent control limits upside but also protects downside. Here's the analysis:
>
> **AB 1482 (California Tenant Protection Act) key limits:**
> - Annual rent increase: max 5% + local CPI (whichever is lower)
> - Just cause eviction required after 12 months
> - Applies to most pre-1978 multifamily buildings
>
> **Underwriting adjustments:**
> 1. **Rent growth assumption:** Lock to CPI (typically 2–3%/yr) rather than market rent growth
> 2. **Vacancy assumption:** Higher turnover (8–12%) due to move-in/move-out disputes
> 3. **Turnover capex:** Higher unit turnover costs in controlled environment
> 4. **Exit cap rate:** May be wider (lower) for rent-controlled assets due to suppressed rents
>
> **Value creation path:**
> 1. Costa-Hawkins exemption: newer construction or single-family may be exempt
> 2. Submarket rent differential: buy below-market rents, exit closer to market
> 3. Commercial unit conversion: convert residential to commercial (no rent control)
> 4. ADU development: add units, some jurisdictions exempt
>
> **Risk:** Proposition 21 and potential future rent control expansion — model for political risk.

## 9.3 Edge Case: 1031 Exchange — What If Replacement Property Falls Through

**User:** The seller's 1031 exchange property fell through 15 days into the 45-day identification period. What are the options?

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> This is a critical timing issue. The IRS rules are strict:
>
> **Identification period:** 45 days from sale of relinquished property
> **Exchange period:** 180 days (or tax due date, whichever is earlier)
>
> **Immediate options:**
> 1. **Identify additional properties:** Can identify up to 3 properties regardless of value. Write formal identification (signed, specific address) within 45 days.
> 2. **Reverse exchange:** Park funds in a qualified intermediary (QI) and acquire replacement property first — requires QI agreement in advance.
> 3. **Build-to-suit exchange:** Identify land and build, deferring completion until after sale.
>
> **Partial exchange:** If only a portion of proceeds are exchanged, remaining proceeds are taxable (partial 1031).
>
> **Identification requirements (per IRS Reg 1.1031(k)-1):**
> - Written, signed identification
> - Specific property description (street address or legal description)
> - Delivered to QI or other party (not just seller)
> - 3-property rule or 200% rule: max 3 properties OR properties worth up to 200% of sold property
>
> **Warning:** Failure to properly identify means full capital gains tax applies. Consult a 1031 exchange specialist or CPA immediately.
>
> **Prevention:** Always have backup replacement properties identified before closing on the relinquished property.
