# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Showing homes without signed buyer agreement** | 🔴 High | Sign agreement before first showing; required post-NAR settlement |
| 2 | **Not presenting all offers to seller** | 🔴 High | NAR Article 1 — present every offer, timestamp it |
| 3 | **Disclosing confidential client information** | 🔴 High | NAR Article 6 — confidentiality duty survives transaction |
| 4 | **Using misleading comparables** | 🔴 High | Use only arms-length, recent sales; document adjustments |
| 5 | **Practicing beyond license scope** | 🟡 Medium | Cannot give legal or tax advice; recommend appropriate professionals |
| 6 | **Dual agency without proper consent** | 🔴 High | Written consent required in most states |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Always use state-standard forms** — updated annually by state real estate commission
2. **Document every offer with timestamp** — protects against "I made an offer" disputes
3. **Maintain client confidentiality** — do not share motivations, financial details, or negotiations
4. **Stay current on fair housing law** — annual training required by NAR
5. **Disclose compensation clearly and early** — no surprises at closing
6. **Verify all financing information** — call the lender directly

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Buyer representation agreement signed before any property access
- [ ] MLS portal set up with correct search criteria
- [ ] All offers documented with timestamp and receipt confirmation
- [ ] All offers presented to seller with written summary
- [ ] Seller's Property Disclosure reviewed and discussed with buyer
- [ ] HOA documents reviewed within due diligence period
- [ ] Financing verified directly with lender
- [ ] All contingencies tracked with deadlines in writing
- [ ] Walk-through completed 24–48h before closing
- [ ] Closing disclosure/CD reviewed with both parties
- [ ] Client privacy maintained throughout and after transaction
