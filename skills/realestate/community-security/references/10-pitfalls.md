# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Tailgating through access-controlled doors** | 🔴 High | Enforce single-person entry; challenge all unescorted visitors |
| 2 | **Not documenting patrol rounds** | 🟡 Medium | GPS-tracked patrol app or physical log with timestamped photos |
| 3 | **Failure to report minor incidents** | 🟡 Medium | Even small incidents are data points for crime trends |
| 4 | **Not testing emergency equipment** | 🟡 Medium | Monthly fire alarm test, weekly CCTV verification |
| 5 | **Sharing access credentials** | 🔴 High | Zero tolerance; immediate credential revocation |
| 6 | **Not maintaining incident report confidentiality** | 🟡 Medium | Incident reports are legal documents — restrict access |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Conduct regular CPTED assessments** — natural surveillance, territorial reinforcement, access control
2. **Maintain CCTV coverage of all entry/exit points** — retention for minimum 30 days
3. **Random patrol patterns** — avoid predictable routes that allow criminals to time their actions
4. **Report and document every incident** — even near-misses inform prevention
5. **Know residents by face** — regular interaction builds community and deters outsiders
6. **Test gate intercoms and cameras** — monthly verification of functionality
7. **Coordinate with local law enforcement** — crime statistics, patrol requests, community programs

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] All security staff trained in DHS Run-Hide-Fight protocol
- [ ] Fire alarm system tested and documented monthly
- [ ] CCTV system verified operational with all cameras
- [ ] Access control credentials audited: remove terminated employees, old cards
- [ ] Patrol logs completed every shift with GPS verification
- [ ] Emergency contact list current: police, fire, ambulance, utility companies
- [ ] Incident reports filed for all security events
- [ ] Annual CPTED security assessment completed
- [ ] Emergency lighting tested monthly
- [ ] First aid/AED equipment verified current and operational
