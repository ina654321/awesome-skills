# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Common Scenario: Resident Locked Out — Suspected Unauthorized Entry

**User:** A resident claims someone entered their apartment while they were away. Security logs show the access card was used. How do we investigate?

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
> Unauthorized entry using a resident's credentials is a serious security breach:
>
> **Investigation steps:**
> 1. **Verify the claim:** Review CCTV at the time of access — who actually entered?
> 2. **Cross-reference:** Match card access time with CCTV footage
> 3. **Check resident's card status:** Is card reported lost/stolen? Is anyone else listed on the account?
> 4. **Interview surrounding residents:** Any suspicious activity observed?
>
> **Immediate actions:**
> 1. **Change the resident's access credentials** — prevent further unauthorized access
> 2. **Offer immediate accommodation** if resident feels unsafe — hotel, alternate unit
> 3. **Increase patrol frequency** in that area
> 4. **Notify property management** and legal counsel — potential civil/criminal matter
> 5. **File police report** if confirmed unauthorized entry
>
> **Possible explanations:**
> - Resident misremembered leaving
> - Family member with permission used the card
> - Card cloned (rare but possible with older mag-stripe systems)
> - Staff entry for maintenance (should be logged and communicated)
>
> **Prevention measures:**
> 1. Upgrade to smart card or mobile credential (harder to clone)
> 2. Implement two-factor: card + PIN for after-hours access
> 3. Automatic notification to resident on access card use

## 9.2 Complex Scenario: Active Shooter in Parking Structure

**User:** Security reports gunshots heard in the parking structure. What is the immediate response protocol?

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
> Lives are at immediate risk. Follow DHS Run-Hide-Fight protocol:
>
> **Immediate actions (first 60 seconds):**
> 1. **CALL 911 immediately** — provide: location (parking structure level B3), number of shots heard, any descriptions
> 2. **Activate emergency response protocol** — notify all security personnel
> 3. **LOCK DOWN the parking structure** — close gates, prevent additional vehicles entering
> 4. **BROADCAST shelter-in-place** — PA system, app notification: "Security emergency in parking structure B3. Do not enter. Shelter in place or evacuate if safe to do so."
>
> **If shooter is visible and you can evacuate:**
> - Leave belongings behind
> - Help others escape if possible
> - Call 911 when safe
> - Keep hands visible
> - Follow police instructions
>
> **If evacuation is not possible:**
> - Hide in locked office or vehicle
> - Silence phone, no vibration
> - Remain quiet
> - Call 911 if safe
>
> **DO NOT attempt to engage** — security personnel are not law enforcement
>
> **Post-incident:**
> 1. Secure scene for evidence
> 2. Account for all persons
> 3. Coordinate with police investigation
> 4. Critical incident debrief with all security staff
> 5. Notify property management and HOA board
> 6. Trauma support resources for affected residents and staff

## 9.3 Edge Case: Resident Files False Police Report Against Another Resident

**User:** Security received a 911 call from a unit claiming domestic violence and assault. Police arrived and found no evidence. The alleged victim is now refusing to cooperate. What do we do?

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> False police reports create legal liability for the accuser and require careful documentation:
>
> **Immediate actions:**
> 1. **Do not dismiss the report** — document everything as received
> 2. **Cooperate fully with law enforcement** — provide CCTV footage, access logs, incident history
> 3. **Do not confront either resident** — let law enforcement handle
> 4. **Notify property management** — potential civil liability
> 5. **Document all known facts** — what was reported, what was found, any contradictions
>
> **Known indicators of false report:**
> - History of false complaints by this resident
> - Vague or inconsistent descriptions
> - No physical evidence of assault
> - Resident refuses to cooperate with police
> - Prior disputes with alleged perpetrator (neighbor conflict)
>
> **Protective measures for the accused:**
> 1. Do NOT restrict accused's access to the building (potential Fair Housing retaliation claim)
> 2. Offer the accused any available resources
> 3. Document everything to protect against future false claims
>
> **What to avoid:**
> 1. Do not label the report as "false" without police determination
> 2. Do not retaliate against either party
> 3. Do not share details with other residents (privacy + liability)
>
> **Follow-up:**
> - Request police incident report number
> - Maintain documentation for 5+ years
> - Consider whether HOA needs legal counsel
