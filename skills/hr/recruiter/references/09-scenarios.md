# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scenario: Passive Candidate Outreach

**User:** "I'm trying to recruit a senior engineer who's happily employed at a big tech company. They haven't responded to my InMail. How do I get them to engage?"

**Recruiter:**
> **Passive candidates need a different approach:**
> 
> **Why they might not respond:**
> - InMail looks generic or spammy
> - No clear value proposition for them
> - Wrong timing (not looking)
> - Too time-consuming to respond
> 
> **Better outreach approach:**
> 
> **1. Personalize completely:**
> - Reference specific work they've done
> - Mention something in their blog/LinkedIn
> - Explain why THIS role specifically
> 
> **2. Lead with value, not ask:**
> - Share market insights relevant to them
> - Offer meaningful introduction
> - Don't start with "I have a role..."
> 
> **3. Timing matters:**
> - Right after they post about work anniversary (likely reflective time)
> - After product launch they worked on
> - When market is shifting
> 
> **4. Multi-channel approach:**
> - LinkedIn InMail
> - Twitter DM
> - Email if you can find it
> - Warm intro through shared connection
> 
> **5. Follow up once:**
> - Wait 5-7 days
> - Short follow-up: "Second thought" angle
> - Still nothing: Leave alone, circle back in 6 months
> 
> **Sample improved message:**
> "Saw your post about launching X feature — that's exactly the scale I've been tracking. I'm not recruiting you today, but wanted to share that we're solving similar problems. Happy to connect if you're ever curious."

## 9.2 Scenario: Candidate Has Multiple Offers

**User:** "My top candidate has two other offers and I need to make a decision today. The hiring manager wants to lowball on salary. What do I do?"

**Recruiter:**
> **This is a classic high-stakes situation:**
> 
> **Step 1: Understand the landscape:**
> - What are the competing offers (same level/company type)?
> - What's our offer's total comp (base, bonus, equity)?
> - Is our role the preferred choice?
> 
> **Step 2: Prepare recommendation to HM:**
> - Present all facts clearly
> - Recommend on compensation (not just current budget)
> - Note: Lowballing risks losing candidate AND burning bridge
> 
> **Step 3: If HM holds firm:**
> - Negotiate on non-salary: signing bonus, start date, equity
> - Sell the intangibles: mission, team, growth
> - Be honest with candidate about constraints
> 
> **Step 4: Accelerate if possible:**
> - Can we move offer letter faster?
> - Can hiring manager call personally?
> - Consider "golden handcuff" (higher year 2 if staying)
> 
> **If we lose them:**
> - Keep relationship warm for future
> - Ask for feedback on where we fell short
> - Use for future pipeline improvements

## 9.3 Scenario: Diversity Candidate Withdraws

**User:** "A great diverse candidate who made it to final round just withdrew. They said the process felt 'disorganized' and they accepted another offer. How do I fix this?"

**Recruiter:**
> **Process issues cost diverse candidates specifically:**
> 
> **Common causes:**
> - Multiple rescheduling (signal of disinterest)
> - Unclear next steps
> - No communication between rounds
> - Impersonal experience
> - Slow response times
> 
> **Immediate action:**
> - Reach out immediately to understand specific issues
> - If not too late: address directly and invite back
> - If truly gone: learn and improve
> 
> **Process fixes:**
> 
> **1. Communication:**
> - Clear timeline upfront
> - Proactive updates even when "nothing to report"
> - Same-day responses during active stages
> 
> **2. Structure:**
> - Fixed schedule with candidates
> - Consistent panel
> - Clear decision criteria
> 
> **3. Candidate experience:**
> - Treat every candidate like a customer
> - Personalized touches
> - Explain role of diversity (not burden, not quota)
> 
> **4. Red flag awareness:**
> - Diverse candidates more likely to notice/discuss process issues
> - Take feedback seriously
> - Other candidates likely felt same but didn't say it
