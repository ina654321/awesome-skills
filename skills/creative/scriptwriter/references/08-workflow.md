# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Development Process

```
Script Development Lifecycle
├── Phase 1: Concept (1-2 weeks)
│   ├── Generate premise/logline
│   ├── Identify protagonist and central conflict
│   ├── Research genre and market
│   └── Test logline with peers
│
├── Phase 2: Outline (2-4 weeks)
│   ├── Beat sheet (major story beats)
│   ├── Character arcs mapped
│   ├── Scene-by-scene outline
│   └── Test outline with trusted readers
│
├── Phase 3: First Draft (4-8 weeks)
│   ├── Write daily word/page targets
│   ├── First 10 pages are hardest - push through
│   ├── Don't edit while drafting
│   └── Complete full first draft before revising
│
├── Phase 4: Revision (4-12 weeks)
│   ├── Structural revision first
│   ├── Character consistency pass
│   ├── Dialogue polish pass
│   └── Read aloud and cut dead weight
│
└── Phase 5: Polish (1-2 weeks)
    ├── Format check
    ├── Spelling/grammar
    ├── Professional reader feedback
    └── Final draft
```

## 8.2 Scene Writing Workflow

```
Per-Scene Process
├── 1. Purpose
│   ├── What does this scene accomplish?
│   ├── Does it advance plot or reveal character?
│   └── Can this be combined or cut?
│
├── 2. Conflict
│   ├── What is the central tension?
│   ├── What does the character want vs. what's in the way?
│   └── How does this scene change the story?
│
├── 3. Beginning
│   ├── Enter scene as late as possible
│   ├── Establish who is there and what they want
│   └── Make an entrance worth watching
│
├── 4. Middle
│   ├── Escalate the conflict
│   ├── Add obstacles and complications
│   └── Make choices matter
│
├── 5. End
│   ├── Exit scene as early as possible
│   ├── Leave with new information or changed status
│   └── Set up next scene
│
└── 6. Test
    └── "If I cut this scene, would the story still work?" If yes, strengthen it.
```

## 8.3 TV Episode Structure

### One-Hour Drama (42-45 min runtime)

```
Act Structure
├── COLD OPEN (3-5 pages)
│   └── Hook, tease, problem introduced
├── ACT ONE (12-15 pages)
│   └── Setup, inciting incident, act out
├── ACT TWO (12-15 pages)
│   └── Complications, subplot weave, midpoint shift
├── ACT THREE (12-15 pages)
│   └── Consequences, crisis, cliffhanger
├── ACT FOUR (12-15 pages)
│   └── Climax, resolution, setup for next ep
└── COLD CLOSE (1 page)
    └── Final beat, final image
```

### Half-Hour Comedy (22-25 min runtime)

```
Structure
├── COLD OPEN (2-3 pages)
│   └── Standalone joke sequence
├── ACT ONE (8-10 pages)
│   └── A-story setup, B-story introduced
├── ACT TWO (8-10 pages)
│   └── Complication, B-story payoff
├── TAG (1-2 pages)
│   └── Final joke or beat
```

## 8.4 Dialogue Writing Standards

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Subtext | Characters don't say what they mean literally |
| Voice | Each character has distinct speech patterns |
| Efficiency | Cut dialogue that action or expression can convey |
| Rhythm | Vary sentence length; match character energy |
| Exposition | Reveal through conflict, not monologues |

## 8.5 Revision Checklist

- [ ] Does the protagonist have a clear want/need?
- [ ] Does each scene have conflict?
- [ ] Does each scene advance the story?
- [ ] Are character choices logical but surprising?
- [ ] Is exposition revealed through conflict?
- [ ] Does the dialogue sound distinct per character?
- [ ] Is every scene the best version of that scene?
- [ ] Would you watch the next page?
