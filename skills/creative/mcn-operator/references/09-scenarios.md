# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Creator Wants to Leave MCN Mid-Contract

**User:** "One of my top creators wants to terminate their contract 6 months early. They found a better offer. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Creator retention and contract enforcement:
>
> 1. **Review contract terms**: What are the termination clauses?
> 2. **Understand their concerns**: Schedule call to understand reasoning
> 3. **Evaluate retention offer**: Can you match or improve their offer?
> 4. **Calculate damages**: What's the MCN's actual loss?
> 5. **Negotiate solution**:
>    - Early termination fee payment
>    - Phased transition
>    - Continued brand deal collaboration
> 6. **Consider relationship value**: Litigation is expensive; sometimes parting well is best
>
> Prevention: Build relationships, not just contracts. Value creators beyond revenue extraction.

## 9.2 Creator Caught in Plagiarism Scandal

**User:** "A creator under management is accused of stealing another creator's content. They're innocent but the mob is against them. How do I handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> Crisis management for creator reputation:
>
> 1. **Gather facts immediately**: Timeline, evidence, original sources
> 2. **Suspend brand deals temporarily**: Protect brand partners
> 3. **Prepare statement**: "We're aware and investigating thoroughly"
> 4. **Support the creator**: Access to legal resources if needed
> 5. **Monitor platform**: Report false claims, flag stolen content
> 6. **Post-resolution**: If cleared, support rehabilitation
>
> False accusations happen. Help creators navigate with evidence, not emotion.

## 9.3 Brand Wants Unreasonable Revisions

**User:** "A brand is demanding 10+ revision rounds and threatening to withhold payment. Our contract only specified 'reasonable revisions.' What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Brand partnership disputes:
>
> 1. **Review the contract**: What does "reasonable revisions" mean?
> 2. **Document everything**: Screenshots of all communications
> 3. **Communicate professionally**: "Per our agreement, we're committed to X rounds of revisions"
> 4. **Offer alternatives**: "I'm happy to do one more round with these specific notes"
> 5. **Escalate within brand**: Request to speak with brand's manager
> 6. **Know when to escalate to legal**: Significant amounts warrant legal review
>
> Protect your creators from being exploited. Use the contract as your guide.

## 9.4 Platform Algorithm Changes Devastate Creators

**User:** "YouTube just changed the algorithm and several creators' revenue dropped 60%. How do I support them through this?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Algorithm volatility management:
>
> 1. **Rapid communication**: Inform creators immediately
> 2. **Data analysis**: Understand exactly what changed and why
> 3. **Strategy pivot**: Adapt content recommendations
> 4. **Diversification push**: Accelerate brand deal pipeline
> 5. **Platform alternatives**: Cross-platform audience building
> 6. **Emotional support**: Algorithm changes are stressful
> 7. **Long-term planning**: Build sustainable businesses, not algorithm dependency
>
> Algorithm changes are part of the industry. MCN value is in helping creators adapt.

## 9.5 Creator Burnout Request

**User:** "A creator I manage says they need to take 3 months off for mental health. Their contract requires 2 videos per week. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Creator wellbeing over contracts:
>
> 1. **Support the decision**: Mental health takes priority
> 2. **Review contract terms**: What are the obligations?
> 3. **Negotiate a pause**: Suspend contract terms temporarily
> 4. **Protect their brand**: Content schedule alternatives (reruns, backlog)
> 5. **Brand communication**: Notify partners of temporary hiatus
> 6. **Welcome them back**: Support their return when ready
>
> Creators are humans, not content machines. Supporting wellbeing builds long-term loyalty.
