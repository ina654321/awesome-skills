# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|--------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Feature-first copy** | High | Lead with benefits, support with features |
| 2 | **Passive voice** | Medium | Rewrite in active voice; subject does the action |
| 3 | **Weak headlines** | High | Test multiple; spend 50% of time on headline |
| 4 | **No clear CTA** | High | Every piece should have exactly one CTA |
| 5 | **Talking about yourself** | Medium | Use "you" 10x more than "we" |
| 6 | **Vague claims** | High | Quantify, specify, prove |
| 7 | **Too many messages** | High | One core message per piece |
| 8 | **Jargon and buzzwords** | Medium | Plain language speaks louder |
| 9 | **Walls of text** | High | Break into scannable chunks |
| 10 | **Forgetting the reader** | High | Write to one person, not a demographic |

## 10.2 Grammar & Style Mistakes

- **Inconsistent tense**: Pick one and stick with it
- **Sentence fragments**: Complete thoughts only
- **Excessive adjectives**: Let nouns and verbs carry weight
- **Hyphenation errors**: "Email" not "e-mail"; "online" not "on-line"
- **Missing serial commas**: Clarity > style preference
- **Apostrophe misuse**: "Its" (possessive) vs. "it's" (it is)

## 10.3 Conversion Mistakes

- **CTA too vague**: "Click here" vs. "Start my free trial"
- **CTA too demanding**: "Buy now" vs. "See pricing"
- **Buried offer**: Don't hide the ask at the bottom
- **No urgency**: Why buy today vs. tomorrow?
- **No risk reversal**: Guarantees, warranties, return policies
- **Missing social proof**: Testimonials, numbers, credentials

## 10.4 Client Relationship Mistakes

- **Taking feedback personally**: It's about the copy, not you
- **Not asking for examples**: Preferences are vague without samples
- **Not getting sign-off in writing**: Verbal approvals leave you exposed
- **Scope creep on revisions**: Track and bill extra rounds
- **Missing deadlines**: Build in buffer for feedback cycles

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Headline tested and strong
- [ ] Opening hook grabs attention
- [ ] Core message is singular and clear
- [ ] Benefits before features
- [ ] Active voice throughout
- [ ] Social proof included
- [ ] Objection handled
- [ ] Urgency or exclusivity created
- [ ] CTA is specific and singular
- [ ] Readable (short paragraphs, bullet points)
- [ ] Proofread for errors
- [ ] Read aloud for rhythm
- [ ] Client brief requirements met
- [ ] SEO requirements met (if applicable)
- [ ] Platform specifications met
