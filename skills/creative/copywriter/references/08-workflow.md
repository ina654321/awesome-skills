# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Project Intake

```
Discovery Process
├── 1. Client Brief
│   ├── Product/service being sold
│   ├── Target audience demographics and psychographics
│   ├── Desired action (buy, subscribe, click)
│   ├── Key messages and USPs
│   └── Competitors to differentiate from
│
├── 2. Competitor Analysis
│   ├── Review competitor messaging
│   ├── Identify differentiation opportunities
│   └── Note industry conventions to follow/buck
│
├── 3. Voice & Style Alignment
│   ├── Review existing brand materials
│   ├── Note brand voice guidelines (or create)
│   └── Confirm tone and vocabulary preferences
│
└── 4. Deliverable Definition
    ├── Format specifications
    ├── Word count/size requirements
    ├── Submission and revision schedule
    └── Approval workflow
```

## 8.2 Copywriting Process

```
Copy Creation Phases
├── Phase 1: Research (20% of time)
│   ├── Read existing materials and website
│   ├── Review product/service documentation
│   ├── Study target audience pain points
│   └── Collect testimonials and social proof
│
├── Phase 2: Outline (15% of time)
│   ├── Identify main hooks
│   ├── Map story arc or logical flow
│   ├── Draft subheadings as guideposts
│   └── Define call-to-action strategy
│
├── Phase 3: First Draft (40% of time)
│   ├── Write to one person, not a crowd
│   ├── Lead with benefit, not feature
│   ├── Use active voice throughout
│   └── Include social proof naturally
│
└── Phase 4: Refine (25% of time)
    ├── Read aloud for rhythm
    ├── Cut every unnecessary word
    ├── Strengthen headlines and CTAs
    └── Final proofread
```

## 8.3 Framework Library

### AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

```
Structure
├── Attention: Provocative headline or question
├── Interest: Relevant details that engage
├── Desire: Benefits that resonate with pain points
└── Action: Clear, specific CTA
```

### PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solve)

```
Structure
├── Problem: Identify the reader's pain point
├── Agitate: Make the problem feel urgent
└── Solve: Present your solution
```

### Feature-Benefit-Value

```
Structure
├── Feature: What it is (factual)
├── Benefit: What it does for the user (emotional)
└── Value: Why it matters (transformational)
```

## 8.4 Headline Testing Framework

| Headline Type | Use When | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| Question | Curiosity, self-reflection | "Are you making this common pricing mistake?" |
| Command | Clear action needed | "Download your free guide now" |
| Number | Lists, proven results | "7 Ways to Double Your Sales" |
| Benefit | Value-focused | "Get 50% more leads without more ads" |
| Mystery | Curiosity gap | "The one email tip top performers use" |
| Testimonial | Social proof | "'This changed my business' - Jane Smith" |
