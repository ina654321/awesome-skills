# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Getting Banned for Accidental TOS Violation

**User:** "I got a 24-hour ban for saying 'let's go' during a heated gaming moment. I didn't realize it was a banned phrase. How do I handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Twitch/YouTube ToS violations:
>
> 1. **Read the appeal guidelines**: Most platforms have specific appeal processes
> 2. **Submit appeal honestly**: "I was not intentionally targeting anyone with this phrase"
> 3. **Learn the rules**: Add automod for words you're unsure about
> 4. **Check channel settings**: Adjust automod sensitivity
> 5. **Wait it out**: Bans expire; don't build resentment
>
> Prevention: Read the full content policy. Most "accidental" bans fall in gray areas.

## 9.2 Community Splitting on Content Direction

**User:** "Half my community wants variety content, the other half wants just gaming. How do I handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> Community conflict requires careful navigation:
>
> 1. **Survey your community**: Data beats opinions
> 2. **Set a primary schedule**: Gaming on X days, variety on Y days
> 3. **Communicate the plan**: "After listening to feedback, here's how we're structuring things"
> 4. **Test the hybrid**: Some games satisfy both crowds
> 5. **Know your goal**: "My channel is about [core identity]"
>
> You can't please everyone. Choose the direction that excites YOU most.

## 9.3 Sponsor Demands Conflict with Community Values

**User:** "A sponsor wants me to read a promo that feels scammy and promotes gambling. My community hates gambling sponsors. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Sponsor conflicts require values-based decisions:
>
> 1. **Evaluate the offer**: Is this actually problematic or just uncomfortable?
> 2. **Negotiate terms**: "I'm happy to promote your product, but can we adjust the language to [something more honest]?"
> 3. **Know your leverage**: A good sponsor respects creator values
> 4. **Decline if necessary**: "This product doesn't align with my community. I'd love to work together on something else"
> 5. **Consider FTC compliance**: Gambling promotions have strict disclosure rules
>
> Your reputation with your community is worth more than one sponsorship.

## 9.4 Burnout from Constant Streaming

**User:** "I've been streaming 6 days a week for 2 years and I'm exhausted. My content is suffering. What do I do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Streaming burnout is real:
>
> 1. **Acknowledge the problem**: This isn't weakness, it's biology
> 2. **Set sustainable schedule**: 3-4 days is often healthier than 6-7
> 3. **Communicate with community**: They'll understand and appreciate honesty
> 4. **Plan content variety**: Quality > quantity
> 5. **Take breaks**: Real breaks, not "I'll stream lighter"
> 6. **Consider the long game**: A 10-year career beats 2-year burnout
>
> The streamers who last aren't the ones who stream most - they're the ones who stream sustainably.

## 9.5 Getting DoXXed or Swatted

**User:** "Someone in chat is threatening to doxx me or send a swat team. How do I handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> This is a serious safety threat:
>
> 1. **Contact platform immediately**: Report threat through official channels
> 2. **Enable delay stream**: 5-15 second delay prevents real-time swatting
> 3. **Do NOT share your location**: Even general area is too much
> 4. **File police report**: This is a serious crime in most jurisdictions
> 5. **Enable 2FA on all accounts**: Both streaming and personal
> 6. **Consult a lawyer**: Doxxing is illegal in many places
> 7. **Go offline if needed**: Your safety > streaming schedule
>
> Never dismiss threats. Better safe than sorry.
