# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Hydrogen Leak Detection

**Situation:** "Fixed hydrogen detector has alarmed. Portable detector confirms hydrogen presence near the fuel processor."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 
> 1. **Safety Protocol:**
>    - Eliminate ignition sources immediately
>    - Evacuate non-essential personnel
>    - Ventilate area
>    - Begin leak investigation
> 
> 2. **Detection:**
>    - Use soap solution or electronic detector
>    - Check all fittings systematically
>    - Identify leak source
>    - Document location
> 
> 3. **Repair:**
>    - De-energize system if safe
>    - Isolate leaking section
>    - Repair or replace fitting
>    - Pressure test after repair
> 
> 4. **Return to Service:**
>    - Verify no residual hydrogen
>    - Restart system per procedure
>    - Monitor for recurrence
>    - Document incident
> 
> **Prevention:**
> - Regular leak testing
> - Proper torque specifications
> - Quality fittings
> - Training on handling

## 9.2 Stack Performance Degradation

**Situation:** "After 30,000 hours of operation, stack voltage has dropped 12%. Power output below spec."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Performance degradation is expected:
> 
> 1. **Analysis:**
>    - Review voltage monitoring history
>    - Check operating conditions over life
>    - Assess operating profiles
>    - Compare to manufacturer predictions
> 
> 2. **Degradation Mechanisms:**
>    - Catalyst layer degradation
>    - Membrane thinning
>    - Carbon corrosion
>    - Contamination effects
> 
> 3. **Possible Actions:**
>    - Optimize operating parameters
>    - Adjust load profiles
>    - Plan for stack replacement
>    - Consider system upgrade
> 
> **End of Life Considerations:**
> - Typically 5-10% voltage loss
> - Economic replacement analysis
> - Maintenance contracts
> - Performance warranties

## 9.3 Coolant System Overheating

**Situation:** "Stack temperature approaching limits. Coolant pump is running but temperature rising."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Response:**
> 
> 1. **Reduce Load:**
>    - Reduce power output immediately
>    - Verify cooling request
> 
> 2. **Diagnose:**
>    - Check coolant level
>    - Verify pump operation
>    - Check heat exchanger
>    - Inspect for blockages
> 
> 3. **Common Causes:**
>    - Low coolant level
>    - Pump failure
>    - Heat exchanger fouling
>    - Stuck thermostat
>    - External temperature high
> 
> **Corrective Actions:**
> - Top off coolant if low
> - Verify pump operation
> - Clean heat exchanger
> - Replace thermostat
> - Emergency shutdown if uncontrolled
> 
> **Prevention:**
> - Regular coolant testing
> - Scheduled heat exchanger cleaning
> - Temperature monitoring

## 9.4 Compressor/Motor Failure

**Situation:** "Air compressor shows abnormal vibration and noise. Motor current elevated."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Compressor failure can cause shutdown:
> 
> 1. **Immediate Actions:**
>    - Monitor parameters
>    - Prepare for load reduction
>    - Plan for maintenance
>    - Order replacement parts
> 
> 2. **Investigation:**
    - Vibration analysis
> - Oil analysis (if lubricated)
    - Check bearings
    - Verify motor parameters
> 
> 3. **Possible Causes:**
    - Bearing wear
    - Motor winding failure
    - Lubrication issues
    - Foreign object damage
> 
> **Resolution:**
    - Spare compressor available
    - Planned replacement
    - Full system inspection
    - Performance verification
> 
> **Long-term:**
    - Predictive maintenance
    - Vibration monitoring
    - Spare parts inventory

## 9.5 Power Electronics Malfunction

**Situation:** "Inverter showing multiple fault codes. Power output erratic."

**Expert:**
> **Diagnosis:**
> 
> Power electronics require careful analysis:
> 
> 1. **Initial Response:**
    - Record all fault codes
    - Monitor grid interface
    - Prepare for shutdown
    - Contact manufacturer support
> 
> 2. **Possible Causes:**
    - Overheating
    - Grid disturbance
    - Firmware issue
    - Component failure
    - DC-DC converter issue
> 
> 3. **Troubleshooting:**
    - Review operating history
    - Check cooling
    - Verify grid conditions
    - Analyze fault logs
    - Update firmware
> 
> **Repair:**
    - May require vendor support
    - Field replaceable units
    - Factory repair
    - System restart procedure
> 
> **Documentation:**
    - All fault codes
    - Operating conditions
    - Corrective actions
    - Lessons learned

## 9.6 Emergency Response Scenario

**Situation:** "Fire alarm has activated in hydrogen storage area. No visible fire but detection active."

**Expert:**
> **Emergency Protocol:**
> 
> 1. **Immediate Actions:**
    - Sound alarm
    - Evacuate area
    - Call fire department
    - Do NOT attempt to fight fire
> 
> 2. **Hydrogen Considerations:**
    - Hydrogen burns with invisible flame
    - Rapid dispersal
    - Explosive range 18-59% in air
    - Thermal imaging needed
> 
> 3. **Fire Department Support:**
    - Provide hydrogen system info
    - Identify isolation points
    - Provide ventilation support
    - Monitor with thermal imaging
> 
> 4. **Recovery:**
    - Investigate cause
    - Repair or replace
    - Pressure test system
    - Restart per procedure
    - Incident report
> 
> **Prevention:**
    - Regular inspection
    - Training
    - Proper ventilation
    - Early detection
