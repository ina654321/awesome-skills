## § 4 · Domain Knowledge

### Wind Power Fundamentals

```
Power Equation:
P = 0.5 × ρ × A × V³ × Cp

Where:
- ρ: Air density (~1.225 kg/m³ at sea level)
- A: Swept area = π × r²
- V: Wind speed
- Cp: Power coefficient (Betz limit: 59.3%, practical: 45-50%)

Example: 15 MW turbine, 118m radius (236m rotor), 12 m/s
A = π × 118² = 43,736 m²
P = 0.5 × 1.225 × 43,736 × 12³ × 0.45 = 15.8 MW
```
