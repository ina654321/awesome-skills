# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Pipeline Leak Detection

**Situation:** "Odorant complaint from residential customer. Leak survey requested on 8-inch transmission line."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 
> 1. **Customer Response:**
>    - Dispatch technician immediately
>    - Verify odor complaint
>    - Check for other complaints in area
>    - Begin leak survey
> 
> 2. **Investigation:**
>    - Ground bar hole sampling
>    - Surface vegetation check
    - Pressure monitoring
>    - Identify leak source
> 
> 3. **Leak Classification:**
> | Grade | Response |
> |-------|----------|
> | Grade 1 | Immediate repair |
> | Grade 2 | Repair within 30 days |
> | Grade 3 | Repair within 1 year |
> 
> 4. **Repair:**
>    - Class 1: Emergency repair
>    - Excavate carefully
>    - Assess damage
>    - Full encirclement sleeve
>    - Pressure test after repair
> 
> **Regulatory Reporting:**
> - PHMSA reportable if ≥50 scfh
> - Document in records
> - Follow-up survey after repair

## 9.2 Compressor Station Emergency

**Situation:** "Compressor shutdown on high vibration. Gas burning at relief stack."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 
> 1. **Safety:**
    - Verify area evacuated
    - Eliminate ignition sources
    - Fire department response
    - Monitor perimeter
> 
> 2. **Assessment:**
    - Verify compressor shutdown
    - Check fuel gas isolation
    - Verify flare system operating
    - Monitor for fire
> 
> 3. **Investigation:**
    - Review vibration trend data
    - Check for mechanical failure
    - Inspect for foreign object damage
    - Check foundation and mounting
> 
> **Root Cause:**
    - Bearing failure
    - Piston rod failure
    - Foundation settlement
    - Resonance issue
> 
> **Recovery:**
    - Full mechanical inspection
    - Replace damaged components
    - Alignment check
    - Full testing before restart

## 9.3 Gas Quality Issue

**Situation:** "Downstream customer reporting flameout issues. Gas sample shows high nitrogen content."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Gas quality affects combustion:
> 
> 1. **Investigation:**
    - Check chromatograph data
    - Review supplier quality reports
    - Trace source of nitrogen
    - Check for cross-contamination
> 
> 2. **Possible Sources:**
    - Contaminated incoming gas
    - Tank venting
    - Air infiltration
    - Processing upset
> 
> 3. **Immediate Actions:**
    - Contact gas supplier
    - Blending if possible
    - Notify affected customers
    - Investigate processing units
> 
> **Resolution:**
    - Isolate source
    - Flush system
    - Resume normal operations
    - Prevent recurrence

## 9.4 Pressure Drop Investigation

**Situation:** "Significant pressure drop on 12-inch main serving industrial park. Multiple customer impacts."

**Expert:**
> **Initial Response:**
> 
> 1. **Verification:**
    - Confirm pressure readings
    - Check for demand increase
    - Verify instrumentation
    - Identify affected area
> 
> 2. **Isolation:**
    - Sectionalize system
    - Identify pressure change point
    - Isolate suspected section
    - Restore service to unaffected areas
> 
> 3. **Common Causes:**
    - Pipeline rupture
    - Major leak
    - Restriction in system
    - Regulator malfunction
    - High demand
> 
> **Investigation:**
    - Field survey
    - Pressure logging
    - SCADA data analysis
    - Inline inspection
> 
> **Resolution:**
    - Emergency repair if leak
    - Regulator adjustment
    - Additional supply if demand
    - System restart procedure

## 9.5 Acid Gas Removal Failure

**Situation:** "Amine regeneration column showing high CO2 residual. Treated gas exceeding spec."

**Expert:**
> **Diagnosis:**
> 
> Amine system problems:
> 
> 1. **Immediate Actions:**
    - Check treated gas quality
    - Reduce feed gas rate if needed
    - Notify downstream
    - Begin troubleshooting
> 
> 2. **Possible Causes:**
    - Amine degradation
    - Foaming in column
    - Heat exchanger fouling
    - Amine loss/leak
    - Contamination
> 
> 3. **Troubleshooting:**
    - Amine analysis
    - Filter inspection
    - Heat exchanger temperatures
    - Reboiler performance
> 
> **Corrective:**
    - Foam suppressant if foaming
    - Amine reclaiming
    - Heat exchanger cleaning
    - Amine replacement
> 
> **Prevention:**
    - Regular amine testing
    - Scheduled reclaiming
    - Filter maintenance
    - Feed gas quality monitoring

## 9.6 Emergency Valve Operation

**Situation:** "Valve repair required on main line valve. Requires isolation of 15-mile section."

**Expert:**
> **Planning:**
> 
> 1. **Impact Assessment:**
    - Customers affected
    - Flow reduction
    - Duration estimate
    - Alternative supply
> 
> 2. **Coordination:**
    - Notify customers
    - Coordinate with operations
    - Schedule during low demand
    - Line packing consideration
> 
> 3. **Execution:**
    - Isolate upstream
    - Reduce pressure in section
    - Vent safely
    - Repair valve
    - Pressure test
    - Restore operation
> 
> **Safety:**
    - Atmospheric monitoring
    - Hot work permit
    - Vent plan
    - Emergency response ready
> 
> **Communication:**
    - Customer notification
    - Regulatory notification
    - Documentation
    - Post-event review
