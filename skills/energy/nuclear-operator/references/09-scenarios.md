# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Reactor Scram Event

**Situation:** "Reactor has scrammed on high neutron flux rate. All control rods inserted. Plant is stable at hot standby."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Actions:**
> 
> 1. **Verify Reactor Trip:**
>    - Confirm source range detector in period mode
> - Confirm all control rods inserted
> - Verify power range indicators show subcritical
> 
> 2. **Stabilize Plant:**
>    - Verify adequate core decay heat removal
>    - Monitor coolant temperature and pressure
>    - Verify emergency core cooling not actuated
> 
> 3. **Assess Cause:**
>    - Review alarms and trends
>    - Identify initiating event
>    - Document all observations
> 
> **Key Parameters to Monitor:**
> - Criticality readings
> - Core temperature distribution
> - Containment isolation status
> - Safety system status
> 
> **Recovery Considerations:**
> - Identify and correct root cause
> - Complete required testing before restart
> - Notify NRC per reporting requirements
> - Brief operations management

## 9.2 Loss of Offsite Power

**Situation:** "Offsite power lost. EDGs started and loaded. Plant is in transition to emergency power."

**Expert:**
> **Critical Sequence:**
> 
> 1. **Immediate Response:**
>    - Confirm EDGs started and synchronized
>    - Verify emergency buses energized
>    - Monitor reactor protection system
> 
> 2. **Stabilization:**
>    - Reduce load to match EDG capacity
>    - Monitor fuel core cooling
>    - Status critical safety functions
> 
> 3. **Long-term Actions:**
>    - Assess cause of offsite power loss
>    - Coordinate with transmission operator
>    - Prepare for extended emergency operation
> 
> **Safety Function Status:**
> - **Reactor Coolant:** Hot shutdown capability
> - **Core Cooling:** Decay heat removal OK
> - **Containment:** Isolation possible
> - **Rad Monitoring:** Emergency systems powered
> 
> **Watchstander Focus:**
> - Continuous EDG monitoring
> - Fuel pool cooling verification
> - Battery condition monitoring
> - Communication with grid operations

## 9.3 Main Turbine Trip at Full Power

**Situation:** "Main turbine has tripped. Generator still connected. Reactor trip is not required at this time."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> 1. **Immediate Response:**
>    - Verify reactor trip NOT required
>    - Stabilize reactor coolant system
>    - Confirm steam dump system functioning
> 
> 2. **Plant Stabilization:**
>    - Check steam relief paths
>    - Monitor pressurizer level
>    - Verify letdown and makeup status
> 
> 3. **Cause Investigation:**
>    - Review turbine protection actuation
>    - Check for mechanical issues
>    - Assess generator stability
> 
> **Reactor Trip Not Required When:**
> - Steam generator levels controllable
> - Pressurizer can be stabilized
> - No safety limit exceeded
> - Negative reactivity not required
> 
> **Recovery Path:**
> - Clear turbine fault
> - Mechanical inspection if needed
> - Turbine roll and sync
> - Load up per procedure

## 9.4 High Pressure Safety Injection

**Situation:** "Safety injection has actuated. Containment isolation confirmed. Assessment in progress."

**Expert:**
> **Emergency Response Protocol:**
> 
> 1. **Initial Actions (First 15 Minutes):**
>    - Verify SI signal received
>    - Confirm all safety systems actuated
>    - Stabilize core cooling
>    - Status containment
> 
> 2. **Core Cooling Assessment:**
>    - Verify ECCS injection confirmed
> - Monitor core exit temperatures
> - Check accumulator status
> - Assess natural circulation capability
> 
> 3. **Accident Progression:**
> - Prepare for extended accident
> - Activate emergency operating center
> - Initiate emergency response procedures
> 
> **Key Monitoring:**
| Parameter | Alert Limit | Action |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Core exit T | > 1200°F | Maximize injection |
| Containment pressure | Rising | Isolation actions |
| Radiation | High | Protective actions |

## 9.5 Loss of Component Cooling Water

**Situation:** "Component cooling water system has tripped. EDG cooling is isolated. Equipment overheating concerns."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Assessment:**
> 
> 1. **Affected Equipment:**
>    - Safety system heat exchangers
>    - EDG jacket water coolers
>    - Motor-operated valve actuators
>    - Control rod drive cooling
> 
> 2. **Actions:**
>    - Transfer to backup CCW system
>    - Verify emergency cooling paths
>    - Reduce load on affected systems
>    - Monitor equipment temperatures
> 
> 3. **Mitigation:**
>    - Use portable cooling if available
>    - Reduce plant power if needed
>    - Prioritize critical systems
>    - Prepare for orderly shutdown if required
> 
> **Critical Path:**
> - EDG cooling is paramount
> - Core cooling equipment secondary
> - Containment isolation systems last

## 9.6 Security Event

**Situation:** "Unauthorized personnel detected in protected area. Security force has been notified and is responding."

**Expert:**
> **Operations Response:**
> 
> 1. **Immediate Actions:**
>    - Remain at control station
>    - Monitor plant status
>    - Do not compromise safety
> 
> 2. **Coordination:**
> - Follow security force instructions
> - Maintain safety system readiness
> - Protect critical functions
> 
> 3. **If Emergency Declared:**
> - Execute emergency procedures
> - Maintain reactor protection
> - Document all unusual events
> 
> **Watchstander Responsibilities:**
> - Focus on plant control
> - Avoid distraction
> - Support security as directed
> - Complete shift relief appropriately
