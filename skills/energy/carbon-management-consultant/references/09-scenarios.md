# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Manufacturing Emissions Reduction

**Situation:** "A mid-sized manufacturer wants to reach net-zero by 2040. Currently emitting 50,000 tCO2e annually."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Path to net-zero requires systematic approach:
> 
> **Emissions Breakdown:**
    - Scope 1: 25,000 tCO2e (natural gas, fleet)
    - Scope 2: 15,000 tCO2e (electricity)
    - Scope 3: 10,000 tCO2e (upstream materials)
> 
> **Recommended Roadmap:**
> 
> **Phase 1 (2024-2027): Quick Wins**
> 1. LED lighting upgrade: -500 tCO2e
> 2. Compressed air optimization: -800 tCO2e
> 3. HVAC efficiency: -400 tCO2e
> 4. Renewable energy contracts: -15,000 tCO2e
> 
> **Phase 2 (2027-2030): Major Projects**
> 1. Process electrification: -10,000 tCO2e
> 2. Heat recovery systems: -5,000 tCO2e
> 3. Fleet electrification: -2,000 tCO2e
> 4. Supplier engagement: -5,000 tCO2e
> 
> **Phase 3 (2030-2040): Transform**
> 1. On-site renewables: -5,000 tCO2e
> 2. Process redesign: -8,000 tCO2e
> 3. Residual offsets: ~5,000 tCO2e/year
> 
> **Investment:** ~$15-25M over 16 years
> **ROI:** Through energy savings and avoided costs

## 9.2 Scope 3 Supply Chain Assessment

**Situation:** "A retailer needs to address Scope 3 emissions from purchased goods. Category 1 represents 80% of emissions."

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Scope 3 requires supplier engagement:
> 
> **Quantification Approach:**
> 1. **Spend-based method:** Initial estimate using spend × emission factor
> 2. **Hybrid method:** Mix of spend and supplier-specific data
> 3. **Primary data:** Direct supplier reporting
> 
> **Engagement Strategy:**
    | Tier | Suppliers | Approach |
    |------|-----------|----------|
    | Critical | Top 20 by spend | Primary data request |
    | Strategic | Top 100 by spend | Template reporting |
    | Standard | All others | Sector average |

> **Supplier Questionnaire Requirements:**
> - Emissions by scope
> - Renewable energy % - Science-based targets
> - CDP reporting status
> - Product carbon footprint
> 
> **Timeline:** 12-18 months to meaningful coverage
> **Target:** 70% supplier coverage by weight

## 9.3 Carbon Offset Project Selection

**Situation:** "A company has 5,000 tCO2e unavoidable residual emissions. Wants to purchase high-quality offsets."

**Expert:**
> **Offset Selection Criteria:**
> 
> 1. **Quality Indicators:**
    | Criterion | Minimum | Recommended |
    |-----------|---------|--------------|
    | Additionality | Demonstrated | Conservative |
    | Permanence | Buffered | Insured |
    | Verification | Annual | Third-party |
    | Registry | Recognized | IC-VCM aligned |
> 
> 2. **Recommended Project Types:**
>    - Direct Air Capture (DAC) - emerging
>    - Biochar - durable, co-benefits
>    - Enhanced weathering - scalable
>    - Forestry (reforestation) - co-benefits
>    - Renewable energy - where additional
> 
> 3. **Red Flags:**
    - No independent verification
> - VVB conflict of interest
> - Overlapping claims
> - Lack of permanence buffer
> 
> **Pricing:**
> - Nature-based: $10-30/tCO2e
> - Tech-based: $150-600/tCO2e
> - DAC: $400-1000/tCO2e (emerging)
> 
> **Recommendation:** Blend of project types for portfolio

## 9.4 Science-Based Target Validation

**Situation:** "A logistics company wants SBTi validation. Currently at 100,000 tCO2e, growing 5% annually."

**Expert:**
> **SBTi Pathway:**
> 
> 1. **Choose Method:**
> - Absolute contraction: Reduce X% by Y year
> - Sectoral decarbonization approach (SDA)
> 
> 2. **Calculate Target:**
> 
> **Near-term (2030):**
> ```
> Target = Baseline × (1 - 0.042)^9
> = 100,000 × 0.68
> = 68,000 tCO2e
> ```
> 
> **Implications:**
> - 32% reduction by 2030
> - Efficiency improvements
> - Fleet electrification
> - Renewable energy
> 
> 3. **Long-term (2050):**
> - 90% reduction from baseline
> - Residual 10,000 tCO2e
> - Offset strategy for residual
> 
> **SBTi Submission:**
> - Letter of commitment
> - Internal letter of support
> - 24-month submission window
> - Public commitment required

## 9.6 Carbon Market Trading

**Situation:** "A heavy industry company needs to buy 10,000 allowances for compliance. Should buy now or wait?"

**Expert:**
> **Market Analysis:**
> 
> **Current Market Conditions:**
> - EU ETS: €80-90/tCO2e
> - California: $30-35/tCO2e
> - RGGI: $13-15/tCO2e
> 
> **Decision Framework:**
> 
> 1. **Price Expectations:**
>    - Historical volatility
>    - Regulatory outlook
>    - Supply/demand balance
>    - Market sentiment
> 
> 2. **Hedging Strategy:**
>    - Buy 50% now at current price
>    - Buy 25% in 6 months
>    - Buy 25% in 12 months
> 
> 3. **Compliance Timeline:**
>    - Must hold allowances by surrender date
>    - Banking allowed?
>    - Borrowing restrictions?
> 
> **Recommendation:**
> - Don't wait for lower prices
> - Diversify purchase timing
> - Consider futures/layered approach
> - Budget for $100+/tCO2e long-term
