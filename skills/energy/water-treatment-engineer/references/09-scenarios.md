# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Turbidity Spike in Source Water

**Situation:** "Following heavy rain, source water turbidity has increased from 2 NTU to 85 NTU. What adjustments are needed?"

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Source water turbidity spikes require immediate response:
> 
> 1. **Immediate Actions:**
>    - Notify supervisory staff
>    - Increase coagulation dose
>    - Reduce filtration rate if needed
>    - Increase monitoring frequency
> 
> 2. **Coagulation Adjustments:**
>    - Increase coagulant dose (alum or polymer)
>    - Consider dual coagulant approach
>    - Run jar tests every 2-4 hours
>    - Optimize pH for coagulation
> 
> 3. **Operational Changes:**
>    - Slow filtration rate
>    - Backwash filters more frequently
>    - Consider filter-to-waste for longer period
>    - Monitor all filters for breakthrough
> 
> **Compliance Considerations:**
> - Filtered water turbidity must stay < 0.3 NTU
> - May need to issue public notice if limits exceeded
> - Document all actions taken
> - Consider enhanced monitoring for pathogens
> 
> **Recovery:**
> - Expect 24-48 hours for stabilization
> - Continue heightened monitoring until settled
> - Review raw water intake protection

## 9.2 Chlorine Residual Loss

**Situation:** "Routine sampling shows chlorine residual has dropped from 1.2 mg/L to 0.3 mg/L at the far end of the distribution system."

**Expert:**
> **Diagnosis:**
> 
> Residual loss can indicate several issues:
> 
> 1. **Immediate Investigation:**
>    - Check chlorine feed equipment
>    - Verify analyzer calibration
>    - Review demand changes
>    - Check for main breaks or repairs
> 
> 2. **System Response:**
>    - Increase chlorine dose
>    - Flush dead ends
>    - Check for contamination sources
>    - Investigate low-flow zones
> 
> 3. **Common Causes:**
>    - Increased organic matter in water
>    - Nitrification in tank
>    - Reservoir turnover
>    - Main break allowing soil intrusion
>    - Equipment malfunction
> 
> **Corrective Actions:**
> - Booster chlorination may be needed
> - Flush affected areas
> - Consider system flushing program
> - Sample for bacteria at low-residual locations
> 
> **Prevention:**
> - Regular flushing program
> - Tank mixing studies
> - Continuous chlorine monitoring
> - Maintain minimum 0.2 mg/L at all points

## 9.3 Filter Backwash Waste Handling

**Situation:** "Backwash waste volume is exceeding permit limits. How do we address this?"

**Expert:**
> **Assessment:**
> 
> Backwash waste management is critical for compliance:
> 
> 1. **Source Identification:**
>    - Filter backwash is primary waste stream
>    - Sludge from settling tanks
>    - Liquid from clarification
> 
> 2. **Volume Reduction Options:**
>    - Optimize backwash frequency
>    - Reduce backwash rate and duration
>    - Install air scour for more efficient cleaning
>    - Use polyelectrolyte for improved settling
> 
> 3. **Treatment Options:**
>    - Recycle backwash water (with treatment)
>    - Thickening before disposal
>    - Dewatering equipment
>    - lagoons for settling
> 
> **Permit Compliance:**
> - Track daily volumes
> - Report any exceedances
> - Consider permit modification if needed
> - Document waste handling procedures
> 
> **Best Practice:**
> - Recycle up to 10% of plant influent
> - Provide treatment before recycle
> - Monitor for pathogen breakthrough

## 9.4 Distribution System Contamination

**Situation:** "A water main has been repaired without proper disinfection. Samples came back positive for total coliform."

**Expert:**
> **Immediate Response:**
> 
> This is a serious public health concern:
> 
> 1. **Initial Actions:**
>    - Re-chlorinate affected section
>    - Flush the repaired main
>    - Collect repeat samples
>    - Notify public health authorities
> 
> 2. **Repairs Assessment:**
>    - Was proper disinfection followed?
>    - Was pressure test conducted?
>    - Were fittings properly cleaned?
>    - Material quality verified?
> 
> 3. **Corrective Procedures:**
>    - Flush thoroughly
>    - Chlorinate to 50 mg/L initial
>    - Maintain for 24 hours
>    - Flush and test before return to service
> 
> **Regulatory Requirements:**
> - Level 1 or Level 2 assessment (per rule)
> - Public notification may be required
> - Corrective action plan to state
> - Follow-up sampling program
> 
> **Prevention:**
> - Mandatory cross-connection control
> - Proper repair procedures
> - Training on disinfection
> - Inspection of repairs

## 9.5 Membrane Fouling Event

**Situation:** "Membrane flux has dropped 25% over 2 weeks despite normal backwashes. What is the likely cause?"

**Expert:**
> **Diagnosis:**
> 
> Membrane fouling requires systematic investigation:
> 
> 1. **Fouling Type Identification:**
>    - **Biological:** Low chlorine, warm feedwater
>    - **Scaling:** High hardness, high pH
>    - **Organic:** Natural organic matter
>    - **Colloidal:** Turbidity, silica
> 
> 2. **Diagnostic Testing:**
>    - Silt density index (SDI)
>    - Membrane autopsy if available
>    - Feedwater quality review
>    - Cleaning efficiency test
> 
> 3. **Common Causes:**
>    - Insufficient pre-treatment
>    - Chemical dosing errors
>    - Biofilm development
>    - Scaling from hardness
> 
> **Corrective Action:**
> - Perform cleaning-in-place (CIP)
> - Adjust anti-scalant dosing
> - Optimize coagulation pre-treatment
> - Consider enhanced pretreatment
> 
> **Recovery Protocol:**
> - Acid cleaning for scaling
> - Chlorine/ caustic for biological
> - Enzyme treatment for organic
> - Verify cleaning effectiveness

## 9.6 Emergency Source Water Contamination

**Situation:** "Chemical spill detected upstream of our intake. The spill is 3 miles away and moving toward us."

**Expert:**
> **Emergency Response:**
> 
> This requires immediate protective action:
> 
> 1. **Immediate Actions:**
>    - **ACTIVELY CLOSE INTAKE** immediately
>    - Notify management and water authority
>    - Activate emergency response plan
       - Begin bottled water coordination
> 
> 2. **Assessment:**
>    - Identify chemical if possible
    - Estimate time to reach intake
>    - Evaluate system storage capacity
>    - Determine spill trajectory
> 
> 3. **Operational Response:**
>    - Operate on stored water
>    - Maintain service if possible
>    - Prepare for extended outage
>    - Coordinate with emergency management
> 
> **Key Considerations:**
> - Public health protection first
> - Notify downstream users
> - Environmental agency involvement
> - Long-term cleanup coordination
> 
> **Recovery:**
> - Monitor chemical levels
> - Resume intake only when safe
> - Flush system thoroughly
> - Test before returning to service
