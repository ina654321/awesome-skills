# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|--------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Skipping temperature logs** | 🔴 High | Make it the first task of every shift. Manager initials each log entry. |
| 2 | **Over-menuing** — 45+ item menu | 🔴 High | Cut to 25–30 items. More items = more waste, slower service, inconsistent quality. |
| 3 | **Understaffing to save labor** | 🔴 High | Understaffed = slow service, bad food, stressed staff, worse turnover. Staff to traffic. |
| 4 | **Not dating products** | 🔴 High | FIFO is non-negotiable. Date everything when it arrives and when you prep it. |
| 5 | **Ignoring guest feedback** | 🟡 Medium | Every complaint is free consulting. Respond within 4 hours on any platform. |
| 6 | **Training only at opening** | 🟡 Medium | Ongoing training required. Weekly 15-min skill refreshers per position. |
| 7 | **No recipe standardization** | 🟡 Medium | Every dish has a costing card. Every prep has a prep sheet. No tribal knowledge. |
| 8 | **Bypassing the pest control schedule** | 🟡 Medium | Monthly service minimum. Never skip — a pest sighting can shut you down. |
| 9 | **Cash handling inconsistencies** | 🟡 Medium | Same person counts, same procedure, every shift. No exceptions. |
| 10 | **Receiving without temp checks** | 🟡 Medium | Every incoming refrigerated item gets its temp logged. Reject anything >41°F. |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Write everything down** — Recipe cards for every dish. Prep sheets for every shift. Handoff notes for every transition. If it's not written down, it doesn't exist.
2. **Prep is everything** — 80% of successful service happens before the guest arrives. If your prep is done right, service takes care of itself.
3. **Labor scheduling is a science** — Use historical data (day of week, time of day, weather, events) to schedule. Never guess.
4. **Clean before service** — The restaurant should be cleaner at 11am than it was at 10am. Maintain during service.
5. **Guest allergies are life-or-death** — Ask every table about allergies at the start of service. No exceptions, no hesitation.
6. **Comp the meal, not the night** — A $50 comp on a bad dinner is cheaper than a viral 1-star review.
7. **Staff meals matter** — Feed your team well before service. Hungry staff are distracted staff.
8. **Manager visibility** — The GM should be on the floor at least 50% of every service. Presence prevents problems.
9. **Seasonal menu refresh** — Update the menu 4× per year minimum. Keep it fresh, keep margins sharp.
10. **Review your numbers weekly** — Food cost %, labor cost %, guest satisfaction scores. If you don't track it, you can't improve it.

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

**Opening Readiness:**
- [ ] All equipment installed, calibrated, and tested
- [ ] All staff trained on their position and cross-trained on adjacent positions
- [ ] All recipes costed and on recipe cards
- [ ] All systems tested: POS, inventory, scheduling, reporting
- [ ] All licenses and permits current (food handler certs, liquor license, health permit)
- [ ] Allergen matrix complete and staff trained
- [ ] Emergency procedures posted: fire, medical, active threat
- [ ] Supplier contracts signed and first delivery confirmed

**Daily Operations:**
- [ ] Temperature logs completed (opening and closing)
- [ ] Sanitizer concentration verified
- [ ] Date marking on all ready-to-eat TCS foods
- [ ] Staff food handler certifications on file and current
- [ ] Receiving log: all incoming product temps logged
- [ ] Prep sheets completed per station
- [ ] Daily special communicated and prepped
- [ ] Reservation list reviewed for VIPs, allergies, special occasions
- [ ] Shift huddle completed with full team
- [ ] Incident reports filed for any issues

**Financial Health:**
- [ ] Food cost % tracked weekly (target: 28–33%)
- [ ] Labor cost % tracked weekly (target: 28–35%)
- [ ] Prime cost % tracked weekly (target: 55–65%)
- [ ] Menu mix analyzed monthly (remove Dogs, promote Stars)
- [ ] Inventory par levels reviewed weekly
- [ ] Waste log reviewed weekly (target: 3–6% of food cost)
- [ ] Guest satisfaction scores reviewed (respond to all complaints within 4 hours)

## 10.4 Warning Signs That Demand Immediate Attention

| Warning Sign | What It Signals | Immediate Action |
|-------------|-----------------|------------------|
| 3+ health violations on one inspection | Imminent closure risk | Hire consultant; fix everything before re-inspection |
| Two consecutive weeks of declining covers | Reputation or quality issue | Mystery shop yourself; call 5 past guests personally |
| Key staff resigning in clusters | Culture or compensation problem | Exit interviews; fix root cause (pay, management, hours) |
| Food cost > 38% for 3+ weeks | Waste, theft, or pricing problem | Audit inventory; review portioning; check for theft |
| Labor cost > 38% for 3+ weeks | Overtime abuse or overstaffing | Review scheduling; check time clock compliance |
| Guest comp rate > 5% of revenue | Service or quality failure | Mystery shop; review top 10 complaints from last 30 days |
| No-show rate for reservations > 10% | Policy or reputation issue | Require credit card hold; enforce no-show fee |
