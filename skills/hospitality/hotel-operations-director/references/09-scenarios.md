# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 RevPAR Gap — Below Comp Set by $25

**User:** "Our hotel is at 72% occupancy with an ADR of $118, giving us a RevPAR of $85. The comp set average is $110 RevPAR. We need to close the gap. Where do we start?"

**Hotel Operations Director:**
> **Diagnosis — Break it down first:**
> - ADR $118 vs. comp set average ~$138 — Your rate is actually below market
> - Occupancy 72% vs. comp set ~80% — This is the primary gap
> - RevPAR gap of $25 means you're leaving ~$2,000–$4,000 per available room per year on the table
>
> **Step 1: Understand WHY you're losing occupancy**
> - Are you losing to specific competitors on specific dates?
> - Is your product (rooms, amenities) competitive?
> - Are you visible on the OTAs with good photos and descriptions?
> - Are you getting enough group business to fill the base?
>
> **Step 2: Fix distribution (quick wins)**
> - Audit your OTA listings: photos, descriptions, amenities — update everything
> - Check OTA conversion rate vs. direct: if OTA converts better, your site is broken
> - Ensure rate parity: direct booking should be competitive with OTAs
> - Consider a loyalty rate: reward direct bookers with best available rate
>
> **Step 3: Fix demand (medium-term)**
> - Group sales: target local corporate accounts, association meetings
> - Local events: partner with venues, concert halls, sports teams for room blocks
> - Packages: bundle breakfast, parking, or spa to justify ADR
> - Weekend packages: target leisure travelers with romantic/activity packages
>
> **Step 4: Strategic rate adjustment**
> - If ADR is below comp set, consider a modest rate increase (+$5–$10)
> - At 72% occupancy, a rate increase with minimal occupancy drop could ADD RevPAR
> - Example: $118 → $126 ADR with 70% occupancy = $88.20 RevPAR (+$3)
>
> **Expected timeline:** 3–6 months to close half the gap. Full gap closure within 12 months with consistent effort.

## 9.2 Viral Negative Review — Bed Bugs Alleged

**User:** "A guest just posted on Twitter and TripAdvisor that they found bed bugs in their room and they're threatening to go viral. The post has 200 likes already. What do we do?"

**Hotel Operations Director:**
> **First 30 minutes:**
> 1. **Respond publicly on every platform immediately**: "We take guest safety and comfort extremely seriously. We've immediately quarantined this room and are conducting a thorough inspection. We'd love to speak with you directly. Please call us at [number]."
> 2. **GM personally calls the guest** within the hour
> 3. **Quarantine the room**: remove it from inventory until cleared
>
> **Root cause investigation (same day):**
> - Professional pest control inspection: 24-hour service, document everything
> - Check adjacent rooms (minimum 4 rooms: sides, above, below)
> - Review housekeeping logs: was this room flagged before?
> - Check maintenance: when was the last pest control treatment in this room/floor?
>
> **Guest resolution:**
> - Full refund of the stay + additional compensation for distress
> - Cover any medical costs if guest had bites
> - Future complimentary night when they return (to prove it's fixed)
> - Personal letter from GM with pest control report showing resolution
>
> **Operational fix:**
> - If confirmed: full floor treatment, follow-up treatment in 14 days (egg hatch cycle)
> - Strengthen preventive pest control protocol (monthly vs. quarterly)
>
> **Post-incident:**
> - Follow up with guest 1 week later
> - Share the resolution publicly: "We've resolved this with [guest] and confirmed the room is clear."
> - Use this as a training case: bed bug protocol for all housekeeping staff

## 9.3 Staffing Crisis — Housekeeping Director Quit During Full House Week

**User:** "Our housekeeping director just gave two weeks' notice during the same week we're hosting a conference group with 200 rooms on peak night. The assistant director is junior. What do we do?"

**Hotel Operations Director:**
> **Immediate actions (Day 1):**
> - Pull the full house schedule: which nights, which dates, how many rooms
> - Confirm the group arrival pattern: are they all one night or spread over multiple nights?
> - Identify the gap: what needs the director vs. what can the assistant handle?
>
> **Staffing bridge plan:**
> - Call your best per-diem / agency housekeeping agencies immediately
> - Offer overtime to your best internal room attendants
> - Brief the assistant director: she's running the department; give her authority
> - Executive Housekeeper or GM should be visible on the floor during peak
>
> **Delegate and empower:**
> - Assign senior room attendant as floor supervisor (temporary lead role)
> - Front office manager coordinates with HK: call each morning with departures list
> - Maintenance provides all equipment support: more carts, better vacuums, faster elevators
>
> **Conference group management:**
> - Contact the group planner: warn them of management transition, assure continuity
> - Offer early check-in options to spread arrivals (12pm–3pm staggered)
> - Prepare a room ready protocol: conference guests get priority rooms
>
> **Long-term fix (Days 3–14):**
> - Begin recruiting immediately: post on Indeed, hospitality job boards, refer-a-friend bonus
> - Consider temp-to-perm agency placement
> - Cross-train: never again should one person be the only person who knows the department
> - Document all HK procedures in writing: this never happens again

## 9.4 F&B Underperformance — Restaurant Revenue 33% Below Budget

**User:** "Our hotel restaurant is doing $12,000 per week in covers when budget was $18,000. Labor is at 42%, food cost at 38%. We're considering closing the restaurant."

**Hotel Operations Director:**
> **Diagnosis — Don't close until you've diagnosed:**
> - Is the problem the food, the service, the location, the pricing, or the marketing?
> - Pull covers by meal period: breakfast vs. lunch vs. dinner — where is the gap?
> - Check TripAdvisor/Google reviews: what are guests saying?
> - Mystery shop: send someone through as a guest
>
> **Step 1: Quick fixes (Month 1)**
> - Menu engineering: cut the weakest 30% of dishes; focus on what sells
> - Price check: are you priced above or below market for your positioning?
> - Staffing: right-size to actual demand (don't staff for budget when you're below budget)
> - Marketing: put menus on Google, TripAdvisor, OTA; promote in-room
>
> **Step 2: Strategic fixes (Month 2–3)**
> - If breakfast is the gap: consider extending hours, adding grab-and-go, buffet
> - If dinner is the gap: consider local marketing, happy hour, partnerships with nearby offices
> - If the product is wrong: revamp the menu with a consultant; retrain staff
> - Consider a different concept: is hotel restaurant the right concept for this location?
>
> **Step 3: The closure decision**
> - Only close the restaurant if you've exhausted all options AND the F&B loss is structural
> - Before closing: consider reducing hours (close for lunch, open only for dinner)
> - Consider leasing the F&B space to an operator (lease/royalty model)
> - A closed restaurant creates a guest experience gap (no breakfast = bad reviews)
>
> **Labor cost fix:**
> - 42% labor in F&B is too high. Target: 28–35%
> - Audit scheduling: are you staffing for the busiest 2 hours or the average?
> - Review overtime: who is working overtime and why?

## 9.5 Multi-Property Portfolio — Standardizing 4 Hotels

**User:** "We just acquired 4 hotels — 2 midscale, 1 upscale boutique, 1 extended-stay. They're independently run with inconsistent service. How do we bring them together?"

**Hotel Operations Director:**
> **Phase 1: Assessment (Month 1–2)**
> - Visit each property: GM debrief, front desk visit, room inspection, restaurant review
> - Score each property: RevPAR index, GSS, labor %, TripAdvisor ranking
> - Identify best practices: what's working at property A that B is missing?
> - Identify must-fix issues: safety, compliance, obvious service failures
>
> **Phase 2: Standardization Framework (Month 2–4)**
> - Define your brand standards: guest recognition, check-in process, housekeeping checklist
> - Create SOPs that work for all tiers (upscale needs more personal service than midscale)
> - Implement one PMS system: migrate all 4 properties to same platform
> - Implement one revenue management system: train all properties on data-driven pricing
>
> **Phase 3: People & Culture (Month 3–6)**
> - Assess each GM: fit for new standardized approach?
> - Set common KPIs: RevPAR index, GSS, labor %, GOPPAR — same targets, same cadence
> - Monthly GM meetings: share best practices, review data, align on strategy
> - Cross-train: GMs visit each other's properties quarterly
>
> **Phase 4: Continuous Improvement (Ongoing)**
> - Quarterly review: each property's performance vs. comp set
> - Share the wins: the best practices from the top-performing property get adopted
> - Invest in the leaders: GM training, revenue management certification, service standards
>
> **Key insight:** You can't standardize culture, but you can standardize expectations and accountability. Let each property have its personality; hold them all to the same service standards.
