# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|--------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Chasing occupancy at the expense of ADR** | 🔴 High | Cost per occupied room is ~$25. Don't discount below that. Better to have unsold rooms than unprofitable ones. |
| 2 | **Ignoring online reviews** | 🔴 High | Respond to every review within 4 hours. Every review is free market intelligence. |
| 3 | **Understaffing during peak** | 🔴 High | Bad service at full house is more damaging than paying overtime at full house. |
| 4 | **Deferred maintenance** | 🔴 High | 1% of revenue in preventive maintenance prevents 5% in emergency repairs. |
| 5 | **No revenue management discipline** | 🔴 High | Dynamic pricing isn't optional. Manual rate setting misses demand shifts. |
| 6 | **Over-relying on OTAs** | 🟡 Medium | OTA commissions are 15–25%. Direct bookings are free. Balance the mix. |
| 7 | **Poor front desk training** | 🟡 Medium | Front desk creates the first and last impression. Train extensively; cross-train continuously. |
| 8 | **No guest recovery protocol** | 🟡 Medium | Empower staff to comp up to $100 without manager approval. Fast resolution wins guests. |
| 9 | **Neglecting preventive pest control** | 🟡 Medium | Monthly pest control. Bed bug incident costs $10,000+ and destroys reputation. |
| 10 | **Skipping labor compliance** | 🟡 Medium | Overtime violations, I-9 errors, tip credit mistakes = costly liability. Audit regularly. |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Revenue management is non-negotiable** — You don't set rates by gut. You set them by data, comp set analysis, and demand forecasting. Every $1 increase in ADR at 70% occupancy adds $255/available room/year.

2. **Every guest interaction is a retention opportunity** — A happy guest who has a problem solved becomes a loyal guest. Empower staff to recover.

3. **Direct bookings are your profit center** — OTA commission on a $150 room is $18–$30. Direct booking is free. Invest in your website, loyalty program, and direct booking incentives.

4. **Housekeeping is the product** — The room is the reason people stay. If it's not clean, nothing else matters. Inspect every room before it goes back on inventory.

5. **Preventive maintenance prevents crises** — A leaking faucet costs $50 to fix today and $5,000 in water damage + guest complaint if ignored.

6. **Comp the problem, not the process** — When something goes wrong, the comp cost is always less than the lifetime value of a lost guest and the cost of a bad review.

7. **Review your numbers weekly** — RevPAR, ADR, occupancy, GSS, labor %, food cost %. What you measure, you can improve.

8. **Staff retention is profitability** — Turnover costs 50–200% of annual salary per position. Invest in hiring, training, and retaining good people.

9. **Consistency beats brilliance** — A reliable 4.3 is better than alternating 5.0 and 3.0. Consistency is the brand.

10. **Train beyond the opening** — Ongoing training every quarter. Standards drift without reinforcement.

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

**Daily Operations:**
- [ ] Yesterday's metrics reviewed: RevPAR, ADR, occupancy, labor %
- [ ] Today's forecast confirmed: expected arrivals, departures, VIPs
- [ ] Room status audit: ready rooms vs. dirty vs. out-of-order
- [ ] Staffing confirmed: all shifts covered, overtime tracked
- [ ] Revenue management: next 7 days rates adjusted to demand
- [ ] Guest complaints from last 24h: all resolved and documented
- [ ] Special requests: VIP amenities, special occasions noted

**Weekly Operations:**
- [ ] Review STR / STAR report: RevPAR index vs. comp set
- [ ] 4-week rolling forecast updated
- [ ] Group pickup review: pace vs. historical
- [ ] Labor variance: actual vs. scheduled hours, overtime analyzed
- [ ] Review platform: TripAdvisor, Google, Yelp — respond to all reviews
- [ ] Housekeeping productivity: rooms per attendant, quality scores
- [ ] Maintenance work orders: open vs. closed, aging report

**Monthly Operations:**
- [ ] Financial review: rooms revenue, F&B revenue, GOP, labor %
- [ ] Competitive set analysis: rate shopping report
- [ ] Marketing performance: channel ROI, direct booking rate
- [ ] Capital expenditure review: deferred maintenance, upcoming projects
- [ ] Staffing plan: next month's schedule based on demand forecast
- [ ] Guest satisfaction trend: GSS score, review sentiment analysis

**Crisis Readiness:**
- [ ] Emergency protocols posted: fire, medical, active threat
- [ ] Key contact list: ownership, insurance, legal, PR
- [ ] Backup systems: PMS downtime procedures, reservation backup
- [ ] Security: night audit controls, camera systems functional
- [ ] Insurance current: property, liability, workers' comp

## 10.4 Warning Signs That Demand Immediate Action

| Warning Sign | Signals | Immediate Action |
|-------------|---------|-----------------|
| RevPAR index drops > 5 points for 2+ months | Product or pricing problem | Mystery shop; comp set audit; product inspection |
| GSS drops below 4.0 | Service failure | Read every review; interview front line; GM observations on floor |
| Labor % exceeds 38% for 3+ weeks | Overtime abuse or overstaffing | Audit scheduling; review time clock data; cross-train |
| > 3 bed bug complaints in 90 days | Infestation | Full pest control audit; professional treatment; PR response ready |
| Direct booking rate drops > 10% | Website or pricing problem | Audit direct vs. OTA parity; check website conversion |
| Group pace drops unexpectedly | Sales problem | Sales team audit; outreach to top accounts; rate/amenity adjustment |
| Staff turnover > 50% annual | Culture or compensation problem | Exit interviews; competitive wage analysis; management review |
| OTA conversion better than direct | Website or booking experience failure | UX audit; page speed test; booking flow analysis |
