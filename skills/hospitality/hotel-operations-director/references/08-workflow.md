# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Revenue Management Workflow

```
Daily (every morning, 8am):
├── Pull yesterday's performance: ADR, occupancy, RevPAR vs. comp set
├── Check next 7 days pickup vs. forecast
├── Adjust BAR rates based on:
│   ├── Demand level (high/medium/low by segment)
│   ├── Competitive set pricing (shop 3–5 key competitors)
│   ├── Group pickup vs. transient mix
│   └── OTA pace vs. direct pace
├── Manage inventory: close/discount low-demand dates; open/high-rate high-demand dates
├── Check distribution: OTA parity, GDS availability, brand.com optimization
└── Send daily flash report to ownership

Weekly (Monday):
├── Update 4-week rolling forecast
├── Review group pace: rebook or release unused group blocks
├── Analyze channel mix: direct vs. OTA vs. GDS vs. wholesale
├── Check local events calendar: adjust pricing for demand shifts
├── Review competitive set: any new openings or renovations?
└── Report to ownership: RevPAR outlook, opportunities, concerns

Monthly (by 5th business day):
├── Pull STR STAR report: hotel vs. comp set performance index
├── RevPAR index analysis: which segment is outperforming / underperforming?
├── Review guest satisfaction: GSS trend, review sentiment analysis
├── Budget vs. actual: rooms revenue, GOP, labor %
└── Set targets and strategy for next month: focus on weakness

Seasonal (quarterly):
├── Market assessment: competitive landscape changes
├── Marketing spend review: which channels deliver best ROI?
├── Pricing strategy refresh: are BAR rates aligned with market positioning?
├── Capital investment planning: deferred maintenance, renovation needs
└── Staffing model review: seasonal demand patterns, scheduling efficiency
```

## 8.2 Guest Experience Workflow

```
Pre-Arrival (48 hours before):
├── Send pre-arrival email: directions, parking, check-in time, amenities
├── Flag special occasions: birthday, anniversary, honeymoon (greet with surprise)
├── Review VIP list: assign recognition tier, prepare welcome amenity
├── Note room preferences: high floor, king vs. doubles, smoking vs. non
└── If suite upgrade available: pre-assign for VIP guests

Arrival (check-in process, < 3 minutes):
├── Warm greeting: "Welcome to [hotel]. It's great to have you."
├── Efficient verification: ID, payment, print folio
├── Rate confirmation: read back rate and dates
├── Room assignment: meets preferences if possible; proactively offer upgrade if available
├── Explain amenities: Wi-Fi, breakfast times, pool hours, gym
├── Hand over key / key card and say: "Enjoy your stay. Please reach out anytime."
└── If VIP: manager greeting or personalized note in room

Stay (daily service recovery):
├── Housekeeping: daily room refresh; twice daily for suites
├── Service requests: respond within 10 minutes
├── Maintenance issues: same-day resolution for guest-facing problems
├── F&B: proactive recommendations, dietary restrictions noted
├── Mid-stay check-in: for stays 3+ nights, a quick call or visit at Day 2–3
└── Problem resolution: empower staff to solve up to $100 without manager approval

Departure:
├── Express checkout available: itemized folio under door night before
├── Front desk: "How was your stay?" — listen for any last-minute issues
├── Ask for feedback: "We'd love a review on Google or TripAdvisor."
├── Arrange transportation: taxi, rideshare, airport shuttle
└── Personal thank you: "We hope to welcome you back soon."
```

## 8.3 Rooms Division Daily Operations

```
AM Shift (6am–2pm):
6:00 AM  — Night audit complete; review yesterday's revenue, occupancy, ADR
6:30 AM  — Housekeeping supervisor briefing: today's departures, check-outs, VIP arrivals
7:00 AM  — Rooms status review: what's ready, what's being cleaned, what's out-of-order
8:00 AM  — GM / Rooms Manager walk-through: lobby, restaurant, one guest floor
9:00 AM  — Front desk briefing: today's arrivals, departures, VIPs, special requests
10:00 AM — Revenue check: today's pickup vs. forecast; adjust if needed
11:00 AM — Maintenance review: open work orders; prioritize guest-facing issues
12:00 PM — Lunch coverage: cross-train front desk on PBX / reservations

PM Shift (2pm–10pm):
2:00 PM  — Shift handover: what's happening this afternoon/evening
3:00 PM  — Check-in rush begins: pre-assign rooms, have keys ready
5:00 PM  — Evening audit prep: revenue center reconciliation
6:00 PM  — VIP arrivals: welcome amenity delivery, manager acknowledgment
7:00 PM  — Service recovery check: any complaints logged in last 24h? Resolved?
9:00 PM  — Night audit begins: post all revenue, close day
10:00 PM — Security briefing: night manager on duty, incident log review
```

## 8.4 Hotel Opening Workflow

```
Phase 1: Pre-Opening (6–12 months before)
├── Hire core leadership team: GM, Director of Sales, Front Office Manager, Executive Housekeeper
├── Define brand standards: service levels, SOPs, operating procedures
├── Technology setup: PMS, POS, RMS, channel manager, Wi-Fi, phone system
├── Procurement: FF&E (furniture, fixtures, equipment), OS&E (operating supplies)
├── Recruit and hire: mass hiring event for all front-line positions
└── Training plan: brand training, position-specific, cross-training

Phase 2: Staffing & Training (8–12 weeks before)
├── Full team on board: all positions filled
├── Brand standards training: every position
├── Systems training: PMS (Opera/Mews/Cloudbeds), POS, RMS
├── Soft opening team: select 20–30 guests for practice runs
├── Practice service: friends & family nights (minimum 2)
└── Mystery shop: hire third-party to evaluate experience

Phase 3: Soft Opening (2–4 weeks before)
├── Limited room availability (50% of inventory)
├── Invite-only guests: industry peers, local press, influencers
├── Test all systems: PMS, POS, reservations, billing, key cards
├── Document every failure and fix it
├── GM and department heads on every shift
└── Gather feedback: surveys, personal calls, observation

Phase 4: Grand Opening & Stabilization
├── Full inventory available
├── Marketing campaign launch
├── Extra staff on hand for first 2 weeks
├── Daily debrief: every issue, every fix
├── Transition from "opening mode" by week 3
└── 30/60/90 day targets: RevPAR, GSS, labor %
```

## 8.5 Crisis & Reputation Management Workflow

```
Online Review Crisis (negative review, < 4 hours response required):
1. Acknowledge publicly: "Thank you for bringing this to our attention."
2. Apologize specifically: reference what went wrong
3. Take offline: "Please DM/call so we can resolve this directly."
4. Investigate internally: pull records, interview staff, inspect room
5. GM personally calls guest within 24 hours
6. Compensate fairly: refund, upgrade, credit — proportional to severity
7. Follow up publicly: "We resolved this and welcome them back."
8. Root cause fix: document what broke and how to prevent recurrence

Guest Safety Incident:
1. Ensure guest safety: call medical, security, law enforcement as needed
2. Preserve scene: do not move anything until authorities clear it
3. Document everything: incident report, photos, witness statements
4. Notify ownership and insurance immediately
5. Legal review before any guest communication
6. Cooperate fully with any investigation

Labor Compliance Issue (employee raises concern):
1. Listen without admission
2. Document: what, when, which shifts, which employees
3. Escalate to HR and legal immediately
4. Do not retaliate or promise resolution before facts known
5. Audit payroll records within 48 hours
6. Take corrective action — even partial — immediately
```

## 8.6 Staff Scheduling Workflow

```
4 Weeks Out — Forecast Demand:
├── Pull historical data: same week last year, same day-of-week patterns
├── Factor in: local events, holidays, comp set performance, marketing campaigns
├── Build preliminary schedule: right-size to demand, avoid overstaffing
└── Identify gaps: not enough coverage in any shift?

3 Weeks Out — Post Schedule:
├── Publish to team
├── Allow swap requests (48-hour minimum notice)
└── Confirm manager coverage: who is the duty manager each shift?

1 Week Out — Final Adjustments:
├── Adjust for confirmed changes (call-outs, late bookings)
├── Confirm maintenance coverage
├── Check labor budget vs. scheduled hours
└── Shift huddle: Friday afternoon — review next week's plan

Daily — Execute:
├── Shift handover: what's coming, what's depleted
├── Manager on floor during peak hours
├── End-of-day: actual vs. scheduled hours, variance explanation
└── Weekly: true up labor %, address any overtime issues
```
