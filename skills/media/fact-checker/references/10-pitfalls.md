# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|--------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Only checking one side** | High | Always seek multiple perspectives |
| 2 | **Taking statements at face value** | High | Verify independently |
| 3 | **Expert shopping** | High | Use expert consensus, not cherry-picked |
| 4 | **Relying on old fact-checks** | Medium | Re-verify when circumstances change |
| 5 | **Political correctness affecting ratings** | High | Rate based on evidence, not politics |
| 6 | **Giving false balance** | High | Science/evidence doesn't require "both sides" |
| 7 | **Not documenting methodology** | Medium | Audit trail protects credibility |
| 8 | **Rating without sufficient evidence** | High | More research or decline to rate |
| 9 | **Ignoring context** | High | Same facts can be presented misleadingly |
| 10 | **Avoiding controversial ratings** | High | Rate what evidence supports |

## 10.2 Verification Mistakes

- **Assuming official sources are correct**: Government can be wrong
- **Ignoring publication dates**: Old news in new context
- **Not checking images**: Reverse image search always
- **Taking statistics at face value**: Check methodology and source
- **Ignoring corrections**: Past corrections indicate problems
- **Not checking for parody/satire**: Check source before assuming

## 10.3 Rating Mistakes

- **Too binary**: "True" or "False" misses nuance
- **Confusing opinion with fact**: Is the underlying claim checkable?
- **Rating intent instead of facts**: What was said, not why
- **Over-reliance on quotes**: Context matters
- **Ignoring definitional disputes**: Some terms are legitimately contested

## 10.4 Ethical Mistakes

- **Ambushing sources**: Give chance to respond before publishing
- **Outing anonymous sources without permission**: Protect sources
- **Using unvetted experts**: Vetted credentials essential
- **Personal bias affecting ratings**: Separate opinion from evidence
- **Insufficient transparency**: Explain methodology to readers

## 10.5 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Claim clearly identified and documented
- [ ] All key factual elements checked
- [ ] Primary sources consulted
- [ ] Expert consensus identified (if relevant)
- [ ] Counter-evidence included
- [ ] Rating explained thoroughly
- [ ] Methodology documented
- [ ] All sources linked/cited
- [ ] Context provided
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled if developing story
