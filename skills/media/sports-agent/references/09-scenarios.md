# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Player Wants to Take Less to Join Contender

**User:** "A client wants to sign a below-market deal to join a championship contender. This significantly reduces your commission. How do you advise them?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Player choice vs. business reality:
>
> 1. **Understand the player's priorities**: Is this a legacy play?
> 2. **Explain the consequences**: Below-market deals affect future earnings
> 3. **Present alternatives**: Are there championship-caliber teams at market value?
> 4. **Model the math**: Show career earnings impact
> 5. **Respect the decision**: At the end of the day, it's their career
> 6. **Execute professionally**: Negotiate the best possible deal for their choice
>
> Your job is informed counsel, not control. Respect player autonomy.

## 9.2 Team Offers Less, Threatens to Move On

**User:** "A team offers 40% below market, saying 'take it or we'll go in another direction.' This is likely a negotiating tactic. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> Negotiation pressure tactics:
>
> 1. **Assess the situation**: Is the threat credible?
> 2. **Verify team's salary cap/flexibility**: Can they actually go elsewhere?
> 3. **Know comparable offers**: Are other teams genuinely interested?
> 4. **Counter confidently**: "My client is highly valued. Here's what we're looking at..."
> 5. **Use your leverage**: "We're happy to explore other options"
> 6. **Read the bluff**: If it's a real threat, you may need to negotiate
> 7. **Ultimately advise client**: Give them all information to decide
>
> Players are employees. Teams bluff. Don't fold unless you must.

## 9.3 Family Members Demanding Client Decisions

**User:** "A player's parents keep calling demanding you renegotiate deals. They're not the client. How do you handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Third-party pressure:
>
> 1. **Acknowledge respectfully**: "I understand your concern for your child"
> 2. **Clarify roles**: "My client is [player name]. I represent their interests."
> 3. **Set boundaries**: "I can share information with [player] to discuss with you"
> 4. **Don't badmouth**: Family relationships are complicated
> 5. **Empower the player**: "Have you spoken with your parents about this?"
> 6. **Document all conversations**: Protect yourself
>
> Family can be part of the team. But the player's decisions are final.

## 9.4 Endorsement Deal Falls Through Before Signing

**User:** "A major brand was ready to sign your player for $500K. They called this morning to back out due to an internal brand scandal. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Deal collapse:
>
> 1. **Get it in writing**: Official cancellation documentation
> 2. **Assess why**: Was it something about your player?
> 3. **Immediate pivot**: Begin outreach to backup brands
> 4. **Comfort the player**: This isn't their fault
> 5. **Reassess brand vetting**: Did you do sufficient due diligence?
> 6. **Negotiate if possible**: Could the deal be restructured or delayed?
>
> Brand deals fall through. It's not personal. Move forward.

## 9.5 Player's Social Media Causes Brand Partner Concern

**User:** "A brand is worried about a player's social media presence and is considering not renewing. The player thinks it's fine. How do you mediate?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Player vs. brand values:
>
> 1. **Review the content**: Is it actually problematic or overblown?
> 2. **Explain to player**: "This is affecting a $X renewal. Here's what's at stake"
> 3. **Educate on brand relations**: Social media affects real money
> 4. **Negotiate with brand**: "My player is willing to [compromise]. Can we keep this deal alive?"
> 5. **Find middle ground**: "What would you need to feel comfortable?"
> 6. **Establish guidelines**: Help player understand brand concerns
>
> Social media is currency. Help players understand the business reality.
