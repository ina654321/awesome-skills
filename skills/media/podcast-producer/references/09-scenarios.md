# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Guest Noshow or Cancellation

**User:** "A guest confirmed for tomorrow's episode hasn't responded to your recording link and isn't answering messages. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Guest no-shows require immediate pivot:
>
> 1. **Try all contact methods**: Email, phone, social media
> 2. **Consider personal emergency**: Give benefit of doubt
> 3. **Prepare solo episode**: Record intro/outro, fill with content
> 4. **Reschedule if possible**: Can you record later today?
> 5. **Cancel if necessary**: Better than forced interview
> 6. **Refund sponsor if ad-read affected**: Handle professionally
>
> Prevention: Record filler/backlog episodes; confirm morning of.

## 9.2 Audio Recording Lost

**User:** "Your recording software crashed mid-interview and the local recording is corrupted. You only have the guest's side. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> Data loss recovery:
>
> 1. **Check all sources**: Did guest record locally?
> 2. **Recover if possible**: Check temp files, auto-saves
> 3. **If unrecoverable**: Contact guest for their recording
> 4. **Interview alternative**: "Let's do a quick 10-minute version"
> 5. **Document for prevention**: What caused the crash?
> 6. **Prevent future loss**: Always record locally, not just in-browser
>
> Prevention: Local backup recording on both sides; cloud backup enabled.

## 9.3 Defamatory Content in Interview

**User:** "A guest says something clearly defamatory about a third party on your show. Do you publish it?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Legal and ethical concerns:
>
> 1. **Pause**: Do not publish immediately
> 2. **Review content**: Is it actually defamatory? (False statements of fact)
> 3. **Consult legal**: This is a serious liability
> 4. **Options**:
>    - Edit out the statement
>    - Require guest to provide evidence
>    - Contact the person being defamed for response
>    - Don't publish the episode
> 5. **Know your policies**: Does your host have legal protection?
>
> Your platform, your liability. Guests don't have the final say.

## 9.4 Sponsor Demands Unscripted Host Read

**User:** "A sponsor says your pre-recorded ad-read doesn't count for their deal; they want you to do a live read on the recording day. The pay bump is significant."

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Sponsor negotiation:
>
> 1. **Review contract**: Does it specify read type?
> 2. **Assess time commitment**: Can you accommodate?
> 3. **Negotiate terms**: "I can do a live read if we adjust the rate"
> 4. **Evaluate fit**: Will a live read feel natural to your audience?
> 5. **Be honest with audience**: FTC requires disclosure regardless
> 6. **Consider rejection**: Some sponsors aren't worth it
>
> Live reads often perform better. But don't sacrifice authenticity.

## 9.5 Copyright Music Dispute

**User:** "You've been using popular songs as your intro/outro for 2 years. A rights holder now claims infringement and demands $10,000."

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Copyright infringement:
>
> 1. **Don't panic**: Assess the claim validity
> 2. **Remove immediately**: Stop using the music now
> 3. **Negotiate if reasonable**: $10,000 is likely negotiable
> 4. **Consult entertainment attorney**: For significant claims
> 5. **Replace with licensed music**: Epidemic Sound, Artlist, etc.
> 6. **Document everything**: Screenshots of the claim
>
> Prevention: Never use copyrighted music without license. Ever.
