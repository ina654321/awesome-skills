# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Breaking News Catches Script Off Guard

**User:** "You're mid-broadcast and a major development breaks. You have no script and only have what's in your head. How do you handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
>
> Breaking news on air:
>
> 1. **Stay calm**: Viewers mirror your energy
> 2. **Lead with what you know**: "We are following breaking developments in..."
> 3. **Use natural language**: Not scripted, conversational
> 4. **Bring in reporters/experts**: Get them on quickly
> 5. **Don't speculate**: "We are working to confirm..."
> 6. **Control the room**: Alert EP, slow down
>
> Practice extemporaneous delivery. Natural authority builds trust.

## 9.2 Pronouncing Difficult Name or Word

**User:** "In the teleprompter, there's a name you haven't seen before. You have 30 seconds before going to air. What do you do?"

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
>
> Pronunciation uncertainty:
>
> 1. **Quick phonetic breakdown**: Sound it out
> 2. **Look for context clues**: In the script, other names
> 3. **Default to English pronunciation**: Most common approach
> 4. **If truly unsure**: Pause, use "said to be" construction
> 5. **Ad-lib correction**: "The mayor, [pronounced], said..."
> 6. **Note for future**: Mark pronunciation for next time
>
> Never be embarrassed to say it slowly and clearly.

## 9.3 Co-Anchor Drops Breaking Change

**User:** "Your co-anchor accidentally reveals a major story development during a transition before you expected to break it. How do you recover?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Unexpected revelation:
>
> 1. **Keep composure**: Don't show alarm
> 2. **Seamless transition**: Take the story naturally
> 3. **Fill context quickly**: Viewers may have missed intro
> 4. **Don't blame colleague**: Stay professional
> 5. **Control room will adjust**: Follow their lead
> 6. **Post-show debrief**: Address off-air
>
> Professional recovery is expected. Viewers rarely notice mistakes if you don't show them.

## 9.4 Guest Goes Off Script on Controversial Topic

**User:** "A guest veers into political controversy mid-interview. The EP signals to wrap. How do you handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Off-script guests:
>
> 1. **Don't panic**: Guests going off-topic is common
> 2. **Redirect professionally**: "I want to make sure our viewers understand the main point here..."
> 3. **Time awareness**: We're actually out of time on this segment
> 4. **Thank and close**: Graceful exit
> 5. **Post-interview**: Note for future bookings
> 6. **Editorial discussion**: Set clearer boundaries next time
>
> Guest management is an anchor skill. Guide without interrupting.

## 9.5 Technical Difficulties Mid-Show

**User:** "The teleprompter goes down 30 seconds before your lead story. The EP says 'stay on air.' How do you handle this?"

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Technical failure:
>
> 1. **Breathe**: You've done this before
> 2. **Natural pause**: Gather yourself
> 3. **Improvise from memory**: You reviewed this story
> 4. **Keep eye on camera**: Not looking for help
> 5. **Keep it brief**: Get to reporter/b-roll
> 6. **Thank technical team silently**: They're working
>
> Teleprompter is a tool. Your memory and training are the foundation.
