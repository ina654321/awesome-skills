# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Standing watch while fatigued** | 🔴 High | Call the officer if unable to stay alert; fatigue kills |
| 2 | **Bypassing lock-out/tag-out procedures** | 🔴 High | Always lock and tag machinery before maintenance |
| 3 | **Not wearing PFD near open deck in heavy weather** | 🔴 High | Personal flotation device saves lives; make it automatic |
| 4 | **Using alcohol or drugs on board** | 🔴 High | BUI limit is 0.04% for commercial vessels; zero tolerance on watch |
| 5 | **Skipping safety drill** | 🟡 Medium | Monthly lifeboat drill is required and practiced |
| 6 | **Leaving gangway unsecured** | 🟡 Medium | Gangway watch is mandatory when crew access is open |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Always brief before heavy weather** — know securing procedures for all equipment
2. **Maintain proper lookout at all times** — COLREGS Rule 5: look-out by sight and hearing
3. **Know your fire stations** — locate SCBA, fire extinguishers, fire dampers
4. **Report all near-misses** — data drives maritime safety improvements
5. **Follow the safety management system** — SMS is there for a reason
6. **Get adequate rest** — 10 hours off-duty per 24 hours minimum (STCW)

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] STCW basic safety training current (BST cert every 5 years)
- [ ] Medical certificate valid and current (MMEC or equivalent)
- [ ] Watch-standing qualification endorsed in MMC
- [ ] Ship security awareness training current (TWIC holders)
- [ ] Required watches attended and logged
- [ ] All equipment inspections completed
- [ ] Safety drills participated (lifeboat, fire, abandon ship, MOB)
- [ ] Ballast water log updated
- [ ] Oil record book updated
- [ ] Drug/alcohol policy acknowledgment signed
