# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 Anti-Patterns

| # | Anti-Pattern | Severity | Quick Fix |
|---|-------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Driving while fatigued (HOS violation)** | 🔴 High | Plan rest stops; never drive beyond 11 hours |
| 2 | **Not securing packages properly** | 🔴 High | Block, brace, and strap; prevent load shift |
| 3 | **Ignoring pre-trip vehicle inspection** | 🔴 High | Use checklist; catch problems before they cause breakdown |
| 4 | **Using cell phone while driving** | 🔴 High | Hands-free only; pull over for calls if needed |
| 5 | **Leaving vehicle unlocked with packages inside** | 🟡 Medium | Always lock vehicle at every stop |
| 6 | **Skipping meal breaks** | 🟡 Medium | 30-min meal break required by FMCSA HOS rules |

## 10.2 Best Practices

1. **Always complete pre-trip inspection** — document with signed checklist
2. **Follow FMCSA Hours of Service** — ELD records everything; violations are costly
3. **Secure every load** — even small packages can shift at 60 mph
4. **Maintain safe following distance** — 3–4 second gap in good conditions
5. **Report all near-misses** — data drives safety improvements
6. **Know hazmat emergency procedures** — ERG book in vehicle, call 911 first

## 10.3 Quality Checklist

- [ ] Pre-trip vehicle inspection completed and signed
- [ ] All packages loaded and secured; count verified against manifest
- [ ] Hazmat packages verified: placarding, shipping papers, PPE
- [ ] Route planned with time-window priorities identified
- [ ] ELD device operational and synced
- [ ] GPS and communication devices charged
- [ ] Safety equipment: triangles, fire extinguisher, first aid kit on board
- [ ] Emergency contacts and dispatch number accessible
- [ ] All deliveries confirmed in handheld system
- [ ] End-of-day vehicle check completed; maintenance needs reported
