# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Common Scenario: Vehicle Breaks Down Mid-Route

**User:** Your delivery vehicle breaks down on a highway shoulder. You have 50 packages on board and are 15 miles from the depot. What do you do?

**Expert:**
> **Analysis:**
> Vehicle breakdown on a highway is a high-risk situation that requires immediate safety response:
>
> **Immediate actions (0–5 minutes):**
> 1. **Safely move to shoulder** — as far right as possible, activate hazard lights
> 2. **Deploy warning triangles** — place at 50 feet, 150 feet, and 250 feet behind vehicle
> 3. **Call dispatch immediately** — provide: location, vehicle number, number of packages, estimated repair time
> 4. **Stay in vehicle if on active highway** — never walk on highway
>
> **Dispatch coordination:**
> 1. Dispatch will arrange recovery vehicle and package transfer
> 2. Keep your manifest/handheld device — packages need to be scanned
> 3. Provide delivery exception codes for each affected delivery
>
> **Protecting the cargo:**
> 1. Lock cab door if leaving vehicle
> 2. Packages are company property — secure as best as possible
> 3. Do not allow unauthorized persons to access cargo area
>
> **Time-sensitive deliveries:**
> 1. Notify dispatch of time-window deliveries on board
> 2. If customer needs package urgently: dispatch can arrange courier intercept
> 3. Document any spoiled or temperature-compromised items (food, medical)
>
> **After recovery:**
> 1. File vehicle breakdown report
> 2. Document any cargo damage or loss
> 3. Report maintenance issues that caused breakdown

## 9.2 Complex Scenario: Customer Claims Package Was Never Delivered

**User:** Customer says their package was marked as delivered but they never received it. The delivery photo shows the package at their front door. What do you do?

**Expert:**
> **Comprehensive Solution:**
> Delivered-but-not-received disputes are common. Here's how to handle them:
>
> **Immediate response:**
> 1. **Do not dismiss the customer's claim** — treat it as legitimate
> 2. **Pull delivery records:** Date, time, GPS location, photo
> 3. **Check if another delivery was made to same address** — sometimes neighbor delivery occurs
>
> **Investigation steps:**
> 1. **Review the delivery photo:** Does the house number match? Porch features visible?
> 2. **Verify GPS coordinates:** Are they within 50 feet of address on file?
> 3. **Check if signature was required:** If yes, was signature obtained?
> 4. **Interview the driver (if yourself):** Can you describe the delivery location? Weather/lighting?
>
> **Possible explanations:**
> - Package was stolen from porch (porch piracy)
> - Package delivered to wrong address (similar house number)
> - Package hidden by customer (behind plant, at side door)
> - Package delivered but customer forgot/unpacked without noticing
> - Neighbor picked up (mistaken delivery)
>
> **Resolution options:**
> 1. **File a tracer:** Investigation request to find package
> 2. **If confirmed delivered correctly:** Customer files theft claim with police
> 3. **If delivery error confirmed:** Resend or refund per policy
> 4. **Compensation decision:** Dispatch or customer service makes final determination
>
> **Prevention:**
> 1. Always take clear photos showing house number and package placement
> 2. Ring doorbell or knock (if safe) before leaving
> 3. Use delivery confirmation text to customer
> 4. For high-value items: recommend signature service

## 9.3 Edge Case: Attempting Delivery to Known Fraud Address

**User:** You recognize the delivery address as one previously associated with fraud (fake claims, package theft). You have a high-value electronics package for this address. What do you do?

**Expert:**
> **Special Handling:**
>
> Package fraud and porch theft are serious operational issues:
>
> **Protocol for suspected fraud addresses:**
> 1. **Follow company policy:** Most carriers have a fraud address flagging system
> 2. **If address is in your fraud database:** Contact dispatch before attempting delivery
> 3. **Do not make independent decisions** about refusing delivery — escalate to dispatch
>
> **Information to provide dispatch:**
> - Address and customer name
> - Reason you believe this is a fraud address
> - Package value and contents
> - Whether this matches a pattern of prior incidents
>
> **If dispatch approves delivery:**
> 1. Require signature from adult (21+)
> 2. Verify ID if company policy requires
> 3. Take photo with both package and signature in frame
> 4. Log any unusual behavior
>
> **Legal protection:**
> 1. Never accuse customer of fraud directly — defamation risk
> 2. Follow company protocol — document that you followed procedure
> 3. If you believe delivery would create personal safety risk: contact law enforcement non-emergency line before attempting
>
> **Note:** If you have a reasonable safety concern at any delivery address, you are not required to attempt delivery. Contact dispatch for re-routing.
