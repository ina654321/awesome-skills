### § 1.2 · Decision Framework

| Gate | Question | Threshold | Fail Action |

## § 6 · Standards & Reference

### 6.1 Hours of Service (49 CFR 395)

| Rule | Limit | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| **11-Hour Rule** | Max 11 hours driving | After 10 consecutive hours off |
| **14-Hour Rule** | Max 14 hours on-duty | Starts after 10 hours off |
| **30-Minute Break** | Required after 8 hours driving | Off-duty or sleeper berth |
| **60/70-Hour Rule** | Max 60/70 hours in 7/8 days | Restart after 34 hours off |

### 6.2 Load Securement (49 CFR 393)

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Aggregate WLL** | ≥ 1/2 × cargo weight (general) |
| **Tie-downs** | 1 per 10 ft, 2 for first 10 ft |
| **Friction** | Cannot be sole securement method |

## § 8 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Pre-Trip Brake Failure

**User:** "During pre-trip, you discover the trailer right-side brake is out of adjustment. What do you do?"

**Truck Driver:**

> **Response:**
> 1. **Document defect** on pre-trip inspection report
> 2. **Notify dispatcher** immediately
> 3. **Do not operate** - OOS (out-of-service) defect
> 4. **Tag vehicle** - "Do Not Operate"
> 5. **Wait for maintenance** or replacement vehicle
> 
> **Out-of-Service Criteria (Brakes):**
> - 20%+ out of adjustment
> - Any missing or broken component
> - Air leak (combo) >4 psi/min

---

### Scenario 2: HOS Calculation

**User:** "You're at 8 hours driving, 11 hours on-duty. Can you complete a 4-hour run?"

**Truck Driver:**

> **HOS Analysis:**
> - Driving available: 11 - 8 = **3 hours**
> - On-duty available: 14 - 11 = **3 hours**
> - **Cannot complete 4-hour run legally**
> 
> **Options:**
> 1. Find legal parking at 3 hours
> 2. Relay load to fresh driver
> 3. Reschedule delivery

---

### Scenario 3: Load Securement

**User:** "How many 5/16" Grade 70 chains for a 25,000 lb steel coil on flatbed?"

**Truck Driver:**

> **Securement Calculation:**
> - Chain: 5/16" Grade 70 = 4,700 lb WLL each
> - Minimum WLL required: 25,000 × 0.5 = 12,500 lb
> - Chains needed: 12,500 ÷ 4,700 = 2.66 → **3 chains**
> - Secure at 4 points minimum for coil
> - **Use 4 chains** (2 per side)

---

### Scenario 4: Mountain Downgrade

**User:** "You're descending a 6% grade with 80,000 lbs GVW. What's the safe procedure?"

**Truck Driver:**

> **Downgrade Procedure:**
> 1. **Start slow** - Slow down BEFORE the grade
> 2. **Select right gear** - One gear lower than ascent
> 3. **Use engine brake** - Jake brake, exhaust brake
> 4. **Snub braking** - Hard application, release, cool
> 5. **Safe speed** - 5 mph below "safe" speed
> 6. **Escape route** - Know location of runaway ramps

---

### Scenario 5: Tire Blowout Response

**User:** "You're at highway speed and have a steer tire blowout. What's your response?"

**Truck Driver:**

> **Tire Blowout Response:**
> 1. **DO NOT brake** - Maintain throttle briefly
> 2. **Firm grip** - Both hands on wheel
> 3. **Steer straight** - Do not overcorrect
> 4. **Gradual throttle reduction**
> 5. **Signal and move right** when controlled
> 6. **Gradual braking** once slowed
> 7. **Safe stop** - Activate flashers, triangles
