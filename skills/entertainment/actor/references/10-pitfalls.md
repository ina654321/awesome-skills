# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 High-Severity Anti-Patterns

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 1: "Acting" Instead of Doing
```
❌ BAD: Showing the audience you're feeling something (crying, looking emotional)
✅ GOOD: Doing what you want under the circumstances; the emotion is a byproduct
```
**Why it matters**: Audiences can tell the difference between genuine behavior and performed emotion. "Acting" is transparent.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 2: Choices Without Text
```
❌ BAD: Creating a character that contradicts what the playwright wrote
✅ GOOD: The text is your guide; choices should serve and enhance what's written
```
**Why it matters**: The playwright gave you words and actions — ignoring them creates a different play.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 3: Commenting Instead of Relating
```
❌ BAD: Playing "I'm being vulnerable" (thinking about the choice, not doing it)
✅ GOOD: Actually being vulnerable in the moment, with your scene partner
```
**Why it matters**: Meta-thinking kills spontaneity — audiences can feel you're working.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 4: Waiting to Feel It
```
❌ BAD: "I'll know I'm doing it right when I feel the emotion"
✅ GOOD: Make the choices and let the emotion follow; commit to the action
```
**Why it matters**: Waiting for inspiration is passive; acting is active.

## 10.2 Medium-Severity Anti-Patterns

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 5: Generic Choices
```
❌ BAD: Playing "angry" without specifics — angry how? At whom? For what?
✅ GOOD: Make specific, original choices that are different from other actors
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 6: Not Listening
```
❌ BAD: Planning your next line while your scene partner is speaking
✅ GOOD: Actually listen — react to what they say, not what you expect them to say
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 7: Technical Without Connection
```
❌ BAD: Perfect marks but no connection with scene partner
✅ GOOD: Let your scene partner's choices affect you; be in the moment together
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 8: Over-Practicing to Death
```
❌ BAD: Running the scene until there's no life left in it
✅ GOOD: Save some discovery for the performance; rehearse the problem, not the solution
```

## 10.3 Best Practices Checklist

### Before Audition
- [ ] Read the entire script or sides multiple times
- [ ] Made specific, active choices
- [ ] Memorized the lines (for prepared material)
- [ ] Researched the project
- [ ] Warmed up voice, body, imagination

### In Rehearsal
- [ ] Come prepared — lines memorized before rehearsal
- [ ] Take notes and try multiple choices
- [ ] Stay connected to scene partner
- [ ] Don't fixate on one choice too early
- [ ] Support fellow actors — you're a team

### In Performance
- [ ] Stay present — don't anticipate
- [ ] Listen and react to scene partner
- [ ] Commit fully to your choices
- [ ] Allow yourself to be surprised
- [ ] Have fun — this is the job you wanted

## 10.4 Red Flags to Watch For

| Red Flag | Risk Level | Action |
|----------|------------|--------|
| Memorization issues | High | More preparation time; work on understanding |
| Can't take direction | High | Practice being flexible; it's collaborative |
| Only one note works | Medium | Broaden your range; don't just do "your thing" |
| Not listening to partner | Medium | Focus on them, not yourself |
| Playing for laughs in drama | Medium | Check tone; don't undercut emotional weight |
