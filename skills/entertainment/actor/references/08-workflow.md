# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Character Development Process

### Phase 1: Text Analysis
1. **Read the entire script** (not just your scenes)
2. **Identify your character's arc** — how do they change?
3. **Map relationships** — who matters to this character?
4. **Find given circumstances** — what's the world of the play?

### Phase 2: Research & Preparation
1. **Research the time period** — how did people live, speak, move?
2. **Explore the character's profession** — what would they know?
3. **Consider physical life** — how do they walk, sit, gesture?
4. **Find personal connections** — what in your life relates to theirs?

### Phase 3: Making Choices
1. **Determine the super-objective** — what does your character want in the entire play?
2. **Identify scene objectives** — what do you want from the other person in each scene?
3. **Choose tactics** — what actions will you use to get what you want?
4. **Establish emotional range** — where can you go emotionally in this role?

## 8.2 Audition Preparation Workflow

### One Week Before
- **Memorize lines** so you can be fully present
- **Research the project** — director, writer, style, tone
- **Make specific choices** — don't wait to decide in the room
- **Practice out loud** — acting is doing, not thinking

### Day Before
- **Run full audition** with reader or on camera
- **Prepare materials** — headshot, resume, sides
- **Get rest** — performance requires energy

### Day Of
- **Arrive 15 minutes early** — no rushing
- **Warm up your instrument** — voice, body, imagination
- **Stay present** — don't let nerves take you out of the room
- **Take direction** — be flexible and collaborative

### After
- **Note what worked** — build on your successes
- **Let go** — you can't control the outcome, only the preparation

## 8.3 Rehearsal Process

### Week 1: Table Work
- Read through entire play
- Discuss given circumstances
- Explore character relationships
- Identify key beats and objectives

### Week 2-3: On Your Feet
- Work scenes on feet
- Begin blocking (stage movement)
- Try different choices
- Explore options, not final decisions

### Week 4+: Putting It Together
- Connect scenes
- Build stamina for full run-throughs
- Refine transitions
- Begin technical rehearsal integration

## 8.4 Performance Preparation

### Pre-Show Routine
- Arrive early enough for full warm-up
- Do physical and vocal exercises
- Review key objectives and choices
- Center yourself before entering

### During Performance
- Stay in the moment — listen and react truthfully
- Stay connected to your scene partner
- Trust your preparation
- Allow yourself to be surprised

### Post-Show
- Note what worked
- Celebrate progress with cast
- Prepare for next performance (if run)
