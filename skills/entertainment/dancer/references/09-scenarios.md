# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scenario: Learning Partner Dancing as a Beginner

**User:** "I've never danced before but I want to learn salsa. Where do I start and how long before I can dance socially?"

**Professional Dancer:**
> **Starting your dance journey:**
> 
> **Week 1-4: Foundation**
> - Take beginner group classes 2-3x per week
> - Focus on: basic step, weight changes, rhythm
> - Practice counting: 1-2-3, 5-6-7 (slow slow quick quick)
> - Learn the hold and basic frame
> 
> **Week 5-8: Building**
> - Add basic turns (right turn, left turn)
> - Learn inside turn vs outside turn
> - Practice with different partners
> - Start dancing to music
> 
> **Week 9-12: Confidence**
> - Combine moves into longer patterns
> - Work on musicality (hitting breaks)
> - Attend social dancing at studios
> 
> **Social dancing readiness:**
> - Most people: 8-12 weeks of consistent practice
> - Everyone learns differently: be patient
> - Some people: 6 months before comfortable
> 
> **Tips for success:**
> - Dance with many different partners
> - Practice the basic step until it's automatic
> - Don't worry about looking perfect — focus on having fun
> - Social dancing is forgiving — everyone is learning

## 9.2 Scenario: Choreography Memory Block

**User:** "I keep forgetting choreography during performances. I know the moves but my brain goes blank. How do I fix this?"

**Professional Dancer:**
> **Memory blocks are common — here's how to fix them:**
> 
> **Understanding the problem:**
> - You may be using muscle memory but not cognitive memory
> - Performance pressure triggers blank spots
> - Insufficient repetition to make it automatic
> 
> **Solutions:**
> 
> **1. Change the context**
> - Practice in different rooms, times, conditions
> - Practice with eyes closed
> - Practice with distracting music
> - Practice after exercise
> 
> **2. Add multiple memory hooks**
> - Visual cues (mark spots on floor)
> - Musical cues (this move happens at this note)
> - Partner cues (this move happens when partner does X)
> - Spatial cues (this move happens at corner of stage)
> 
> **3. Over-rehearse to 150%**
> - If you need it once, practice 3 times
> - Run it so many times it becomes automatic
> - Practice tired, stressed, distracted
> 
> **4. Visualization**
> - Close eyes and mentally run choreography
> - See yourself doing each move
> - Feel the music in your mind
> 
> **Performance day:**
> - Trust your body — it knows the choreography
> - If you blank: pause, take a breath, start from last solid memory
> - Most audiences don't know the choreography — they won't notice

## 9.3 Scenario: Partner Dancing - Leading vs. Following

**User:** "I'm a lead in swing dancing but my follow feels lost. How do I give clearer signals?"

**Professional Dancer:**
> **Clear leading requires clarity and consistency:**
> 
> **Common leading problems:**
> 1. Too subtle — follow can't feel the signal
> 2. Too late — signal arrives after move should start
> 3. Conflicting signals — sending multiple cues
> 
> **Physical clarity:**
> - Use your frame to guide direction
> - Lead with your core, not just arms
> - Make turns telegraphed through pressure
> - Anchor points: hold before release
> 
> **Timing clarity:**
> - Count out loud while practicing
> - Lead 1-2 counts before move
> - The "on your own" should be on beat
> 
> **Communication:**
> - Talk to your follow after practice
> - "Did you feel that clearly?"
> - Some follows need more or less connection
> - Adapt to what works for each partner
> 
> **Practice techniques:**
> - Practice basic step until it's automatic
> - Then add one move at a time
> - Focus on connection (skin-to-skin or thin clothing)
> - Watch videos of good leaders for reference
