# Common Pitfalls & Anti-Patterns

## 10.1 High-Severity Anti-Patterns

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 1: Skipping Warm-Up
```
❌ BAD: Going straight into complex choreography or intense movement
✅ GOOD: Always warm up 15-20 minutes minimum before dancing
```
**Why it matters**: Cold muscles lead to injury; warm-up prevents strains and sprains.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 2: Comparing to Others
```
❌ BAD: "I'll never be as good as that dancer"
✅ GOOD: Focus on your progress; every dancer has unique strengths
```
**Why it matters**: Comparison breeds frustration; your journey is different from others.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 3: Practicing Through Pain
```
❌ BAD: "I can work through this discomfort"
✅ GOOD: Stop and assess; pain is your body warning you
```
**Why it matters**: Dancing through injury leads to worse injuries and longer recovery.

### 🔴 Anti-Pattern 4: Neglecting Recovery
```
❌ BAD: Dancing every day without rest
✅ GOOD: Schedule rest days; muscles grow during recovery
```
**Why it matters**: Overtraining leads to injury and burnout; rest is essential for improvement.

## 10.2 Medium-Severity Anti-Patterns

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 5: Overcomplicating Choreography
```
❌ BAD: Adding too many moves, turns, and transitions
✅ GOOD: Dance to your skill level; simple done well beats complex done poorly
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 6: Ignoring Musicality
```
❌ BAD: Dancing the steps without connection to music
✅ GOOD: Feel the rhythm; movement should match the music
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 7: Poor Technique for Speed
```
❌ BAD: Speeding up before mastering technique
✅ GOOD: Master at slow tempo; speed comes naturally
```

### 🟡 Anti-Pattern 8: Neglecting Core Strength
```
❌ BAD: Only dancing, not cross-training
✅ GOOD: Strengthen core, flexibility, and conditioning
```

## 10.3 Best Practices Checklist

### Before Dancing
- [ ] Warm-up complete (15-20 minutes)
- [ ] Hydrated
- [ ] Proper footwear
- [ ] Clear space (no obstacles)
- [ ] Music at appropriate volume

### During Practice
- [ ] Taking breaks when tired
- [ ] Focusing on quality over quantity
- [ ] Staying present and mindful
- [ ] Recording for self-review

### After Dancing
- [ ] Cool-down and stretching
- [ ] Hydration
- [ ] Note what worked and what to improve
- [ ] Proper storage of dance shoes

## 10.4 Red Flags to Watch For

| Red Flag | Risk Level | Action |
|----------|------------|--------|
| Pain during movement | High | Stop immediately; assess; rest if needed |
| Dizziness or nausea | High | Stop; hydrate; may indicate over-exertion |
| Persistent soreness >48 hours | Medium | Rest; consider seeing professional |
| Consistent balance issues | Medium | Check footwear, core strength, alignment |
| Loss of flexibility | Medium | Focus on stretching; assess training load |
