# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Vocal Warm-Up Routine

### Phase 1: Body Preparation (5 minutes)
1. Gentle stretching: neck, shoulders, jaw
2. Jaw releases: gentle mouth opening, yawning sensation
3. Shoulder rolls: release tension
4. Gentle bouncing: activate breath support

### Phase 2: Breathing Exercises (5 minutes)
1. Diaphragmatic breathing: hands on stomach, feel expansion
2. Sustained exhale: hiss or "sss" for 30 seconds
3. Lip trills: gentle, low to high scale
4. Yawn-sigh: open throat, release tension

### Phase 3: Vocal Warm-Up (10 minutes)
1. Hum glides: comfortable low to high and back
2. Lip trills on five-note patterns
3. "Mi-Me-Ma-Mo-Moo" on scales
4. Gentle sirens: low to high to low
5. Light humming on arpeggios

### Phase 4: Repertoire Warm-Up (10 minutes)
1. Sing through songs you'll perform
2. Start slow, build to performance tempo
3. Focus on problem areas
4. Full voice only in final minutes

## 8.2 Song Learning Process

### Step 1: Listen
- Listen to original recording multiple times
- Study interpretation and arrangement
- Learn melody and lyrics
- Understand song structure

### Step 2: Analyze
- Identify vocal challenges and opportunities
- Determine appropriate key for your voice
- Note breathing points and phrase structure
- Mark emotional content

### Step 3: Practice
- Learn lyrics separately (recite them)
- Add melody slowly
- Focus on phrasing and expression
- Build to full performance

### Step 4: Personalize
- Make the song your own
- Adjust dynamics and tempo subtly
- Add personal interpretation
- Develop authentic emotional connection

## 8.3 Performance Preparation

### Day Before
- Light practice only (maintain, don't tire voice)
- Get extra sleep
- Avoid irritants (smoke, screaming)
- Prepare outfit and materials

### Day Of (Before Performance)
- Full warm-up 2-3 hours before
- Light meal 2 hours before
- No dairy, limit caffeine
- Stay hydrated throughout

### During Performance
- Start conservatively (don't belt immediately)
- Listen to yourself (monitor balance)
- Stay connected to lyrics and meaning
- Breathe — don't hold your breath under pressure

## 8.4 Vocal Recovery

### After Singing Heavily
- Gentle humming for 5 minutes
- Steam inhalation (10 minutes)
- Hydrate well
- Avoid whispering or clearing throat
- Get adequate rest

### If Voice is Tired
- Rest voice completely for 24-48 hours
- Warm tea with honey (not scalding)
- Avoid whispering (actually strains voice more)
- See specialist if hoarseness persists
