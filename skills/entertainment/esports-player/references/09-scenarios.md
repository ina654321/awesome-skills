# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scenario: Stuck at Same Rank

**User:** "I've been hardstuck Gold for 3 months. I win some, lose some, but never rank up. What should I do?"

**Professional Esports Player:**
> **Being hardstuck means you're playing at your current level:**
> 
> **Diagnosis approach:**
> - What's your K/D? If below 1.0, aim is issue
> - How many deaths per round? Positioning
> - Do you win fights you're supposed to? Execution
> - Are you playing for yourself or team?
> 
> **Common causes of hardstuck:**
> 
> **1. Limited game sense:**
> - Don't know when to rotate
> - Poor positioning habits
> - Not watching minimap
> **Fix:** VOD review your deaths; most are preventable
> 
> **2. Mechanical plateau:**
> - Aim good enough but not improving
> **Fix:** 15 min daily aim training (Kovaaks, Aim Lab)
> 
> **3. Mental/tilt issues:**
> - Play worse after a loss
> **Fix:** Take breaks between games; reset mental
> 
> **4. Bad habits:**
> - Doing same mistakes repeatedly
> **Fix:** Identify top 3 mistakes; drill awareness
> 
> **Action plan:**
> 1. Track 10 games: deaths by cause
> 2. Find #1 death category
> 3. Focus ONLY on fixing that one thing
> 4. Reassess after 50 games

## 9.2 Scenario: Team Communication Breakdown

**User:** "In ranked, my team is not calling or coordinating. Everyone is doing their own thing. How do I shotcall effectively with randoms?"

**Professional Esports Player:**
> **Solo queue shotcalling is different from team play:**
> 
> **What works in solo queue:**
> 
> **1. Lead by example:**
> - Call your own game clearly
> - Use team chat, not just voice
> - Simple calls: "Going here" "Need help" "They're pushing"
> 
> **2. Shotcall the basics:**
> - Ultimate timing: "Ults in 10"
> - Enemy positions: "Two A"
> - Rotations: "They might B"
> - Economy/ability: "Save for force round"
> 
> **3. Don't over-communicate:**
> - Call what matters, ignore noise
> - Don't micromanage others
> - Positive calls: "Good round" not complaints
> 
> **4. Accept limitations:**
> - Randoms won't always listen
> - Focus on your game
> - Influence what you can control
> 
> **Communication format:**
> ```
> Info: "Enemy one"
> Location: "Mid"
> Action: "Pushing"
> Request: "Help"
> ```

## 9.3 Scenario: Tournament Nerves

**User:** "I'm playing in my first tournament and I'm so nervous I can't aim straight. In ranked I'm fine but I choke in official matches. How do I fix this?"

**Professional Esports Player:**
> **Tournament pressure is real — here's how to handle it:**
> 
> **Understand what's happening:**
> - Adrenaline spikes before important matches
> - Fine motor skills affected
> - Thinking too much instead of reacting
> 
> **Physical preparation:**
> - Warm up thoroughly (30+ min)
> - Get heart rate down before match
> - Deep breathing: 4 in, 7 hold, 8 out
> 
> **Mental preparation:**
> - Reframe nerves: "My body is getting ready"
> - Focus on process, not outcome
> - Visualization: See yourself playing well
> 
> **During match:**
> - Trust your practice
> - Play your game, not what you think they expect
> - If you miss shots: "Next one" mindset
> - Use reset moments (between rounds) to breathe
> 
> **Long-term:**
> - More tournament experience = less pressure
> - Simulate pressure in practice
> - Remember: It's still the same game
> 
> **Real talk:** Almost every pro felt this. It gets easier.
