# Product Manager — Troubleshooting Guide

## Discovery & Validation Problems

**Problem: Feature built with no user research**
- Diagnosis: Pressure to ship, or research was skipped
- Assessment: Is this a core assumption or low-risk bet?
- Fix: If assumption-heavy: Pause and run 5 user interviews
- If low-risk: Ship and measure, then decide if research needed
- Prevention: Gate builds on discovery completion

**Problem: User research shows different needs than sales feedback**
- Diagnosis: Sales hears loudest, most frustrated customers
- Approach: Synthesize both — research is qualitative depth, sales is breadth
- Action: Weight research more heavily for product direction
- Action: Use sales as validation of themes, not source of features

**Problem: Multiple stakeholders requesting conflicting priorities**
- Diagnosis: Lack of strategic alignment at leadership level
- Approach: Escalate with data
- Present: Both requests with RICE scores and strategic rationale
- Decision: Ask leadership to choose based on strategy, not politics
- Document: The strategic rationale for the decision

## Prioritization Problems

**Problem: Roadmap is a feature list, not a strategy**
- Diagnosis: Pressure from stakeholders to commit to features
- Fix: Replace feature roadmap with theme-based or outcome-based roadmap
- Present: What outcomes we will achieve, not what we will build
- Negotiate: Stakeholder comfort with flexibility in solution

**Problem: High-priority item blocked by technical dependency**
- Diagnosis: Product didn't involve engineering early enough
- Options:
  1. Defer until dependency is unblocked
  2. Find alternative approach that doesn't need dependency
  3. Negotiate with engineering for faster dependency resolution
- Prevention: Include engineering in discovery, even informally

**Problem: Backlog has 200 items and nothing gets done**
- Diagnosis: No prioritization discipline; everything added
- Fix: Conduct backlog review quarterly
- Process: Score all items with RICE; bottom 50% archive
- Rule: Every item in backlog must have an estimated RICE score

## Roadmap Problems

**Problem: Roadmap date committed by sales without product input**
- Diagnosis: Sales pressure, revenue commitments
- Escalation: Present risk to leadership
- Options:
  1. Negotiate with customer: Flexible timeline or MVP approach
  2. Accept risk: Feature-date commitment with clear MVP scope
  3. Push back: Present product process and timeline honestly
- Prevention: Establish that roadmap commitments go through product

**Problem: Roadmap keeps changing every quarter**
- Diagnosis: Strategy not clear, or strategy changes frequently
- Assessment: Are changes reactive (responding to market) or chaotic?
- Fix: Establish clearer strategy; distinguish must-win battles from nice-to-haves
- Rule: Roadmap changes require explicit de-prioritization of something else

## Measurement Problems

**Problem: Don't know if shipped feature was successful**
- Diagnosis: Success metric not defined before shipping
- Action: Define now from available data
- Metric: Look for directional change in target metric
- Learn: Document what you'd measure next time
- Prevention: Define success metrics before shipping

**Problem: Metric improved but user feedback is negative**
- Diagnosis: Metric misspecified or proxy for something else
- Analysis: Dig into qualitative feedback
- Example: Conversion up, but NPS down — which matters more?
- Fix: Update metric definition; consider composite metrics

## Stakeholder Problems

**Problem: Executive wants to kill a feature you believe in**
- Approach: Present evidence-based case
- Data: Usage data, user feedback, competitive analysis
- Business case: Revenue impact, strategic value
- Don't: Argue from passion; argue from evidence
- Accept: If decision is made, execute professionally

**Problem: Engineering team doesn't agree with priorities**
- Diagnosis: Trust gap, or engineering has technical concerns
- Process: Include engineering in prioritization discussions
- Share: Business rationale, user evidence
- Listen: Engineering may have technical blockers or better approaches
- Outcome: Collaborative prioritization, not mandate

**Problem: Designer keeps redesigning instead of shipping**
- Diagnosis: Perfectionism, unclear scope, or missing acceptance criteria
- Fix: Clear definition of done and scope
- Process: Design review before design starts, not endless iterations
- Rule: Good design shipped beats perfect design never shipped
