# Product Manager — Glossary

## Product Management Terms

**PRD (Product Requirements Document)** — Detailed document describing product requirements, user needs, and success criteria.

**Sprint** — Time-boxed iteration (typically 1-4 weeks) in agile development.

**MVP (Minimum Viable Product)** — Smallest product that delivers core value and allows learning.

**Sprint Planning** — Meeting where team commits to sprint backlog items.

**Retrospective** — Team reflection on sprint to improve process.

**Kanban** — Visual workflow management method using boards and WIP limits.

**Scrum** — Agile framework with defined roles, events, and artifacts.

## Prioritization Terms

**RICE** — Scoring framework: Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort.

**MoSCoW** — Prioritization: Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have.

**Kano Model** — Customer satisfaction: Basic needs, Performance needs, Delighters.

**ICE** — Similar to RICE: Impact × Confidence / Effort (no Reach).

**Opportunity Scoring** — Matrix of importance vs. satisfaction to find opportunities.

**Weighted Scoring** — Custom criteria weighted by strategic importance.

## Discovery & Validation Terms

**Continuous Discovery** — Ongoing customer research (Teresa Torres framework).

**Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)** — Framework focusing on customer jobs, not features.

**Jobs-to-be-Done Canvas** — Tool for mapping the job, motivations, and obstacles.

**Lean UX Canvas** — Framework connecting business problem to hypotheses and experiments.

**User Interview** — Qualitative research method with individual users.

**Usability Test** — Watching users attempt tasks with product.

**A/B Test** — Experiment comparing two versions with users split randomly.

**Concierge MVP** — Manual process mimicking the product experience for validation.

**Wizard of Oz MVP** — Partial implementation that looks complete but uses human backend.

## Metrics Terms

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)** — Customer loyalty: % Promoters (9-10) minus % Detractors (0-6).

**DAU/MAU Ratio** — Daily active users / Monthly active users. Measures engagement depth.

**LTV (Lifetime Value)** — Total revenue expected from a customer over their relationship.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — Total sales and marketing cost per acquired customer.

**Churn Rate** — Percentage of customers who stop using product in a period.

**NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** — Revenue from existing customers including expansion minus churn.

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)** — Total revenue divided by number of active users.

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)** — Predictable revenue billed monthly.

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** — MRR × 12.

**North Star Metric** — Single metric that best captures product value delivered.

**OKR (Objectives and Key Results)** — Goal-setting framework linking objectives to measurable results.

## Roadmap Terms

**Theme** — Strategic category of work (e.g., "Improve activation")

**Epic** — Large body of work that can be broken into stories.

**Story / User Story** — Short requirement from user perspective: "As a user, I want..."

**Sprint Backlog** — Items committed to the current sprint.

**Product Roadmap** — Strategic plan showing planned product direction over time.

**Feature Roadmap** — Specific features mapped to dates (often problematic).

**Outcome Roadmap** — Outcomes/goals mapped to time periods (preferred).

## UX & Research Terms

**User Flow** — Path a user takes through the product to accomplish a task.

**Information Architecture** — Structure and organization of information.

**Wireframe** — Low-fidelity visual representation of product layout.

**Prototype** — Working model of product for testing and validation.

**Jobs-to-be-Done** — Framework asking what job the customer is hiring the product to do.

**Jobs Canvas** — Tool for mapping the functional, emotional, and social jobs.

**Success Metric** — How you'll know the product change was successful.

## Agile & Dev Terms

**User Story** — Brief description of a feature from user perspective.

**Story Point** — Relative estimate of effort/complexity (not hours).

**Velocity** — Average story points completed per sprint.

**Kanban Board** — Visual board showing work items and their status.

**WIP Limits** — Maximum number of items in a workflow stage.

**Stand-up** — Brief daily team sync meeting.

**Definition of Done** — Criteria that must be met for an item to be considered complete.

**Technical Debt** — Cost of rework from choosing easy solution now vs. better solution later.

## Go-to-Market Terms

**Launch** — Release of product or major feature to users.

**Beta** — Limited release to selected users for feedback before GA.

**GA (General Availability)** — Full public release.

**Feature Flag** — Toggle allowing features to be turned on/off without deploying code.

**Dark Launch** — Releasing feature to subset without visible change.

**Phased Rollout** — Gradually increasing percentage of users seeing new feature.

**Rollback** — Reverting to previous version if problems occur.
