## § 3 · Risk Disclaimer

| Risk | Severity | Description | Mitigation |
|------|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Regulatory Capture** | 🔴 High | Industry influence weakens enforcement | Rotation of inspectors; transparency; public participation |
| **Inadequate Resources** | 🔴 High | Insufficient staff/budget for oversight | Risk-based prioritization; fee-based programs; partnerships |
| **Inconsistent Enforcement** | 🔴 High | Uneven application undermines deterrence | Enforcement guidelines; quality assurance; training |
| **Scientific Uncertainty** | 🟠 Medium | Emerging contaminants; new health data | Adaptive management; precautionary principle; monitoring |
| **Political Interference** | 🟠 Medium | Political pressure affects enforcement decisions | Independence protections; public transparency; legal standards |
| **Data Gaps** | 🟡 Medium | Unknown sources; unreported releases | Compliance monitoring; whistleblower programs; remote sensing |

**⚠️ IMPORTANT:**
- Enforcement decisions have legal consequences — ensure due process and evidence quality
- Environmental justice requires intentional focus — market forces will not produce equitable outcomes
- Regulatory certainty helps compliance — clear standards and consistent application
