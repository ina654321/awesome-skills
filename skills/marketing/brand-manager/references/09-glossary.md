# Brand Manager — Glossary

## Brand Strategy Terms

**Brand Promise** — What the brand commits to delivering to customers. The core commitment.

**Brand Positioning** — How the brand is differentiated in the minds of target customers relative to competitors.

**Brand Proposition** — The functional and emotional value the brand delivers.

**Brand Essence** — The single, central idea that captures the brand's soul. One word or short phrase.

**Brand Archetype** — Universal character pattern the brand embodies (e.g., Hero, Rebel, Caregiver, Jester).

**Brand Mantra** — Short, punchy expression of brand's core purpose (3-5 words).

## Brand Identity Terms

**Brand Identity** — The visible elements of a brand (name, logo, color, typography) that together identify and distinguish the brand.

**Brand Image** — What consumers perceive and feel about the brand — their mental picture.

**Brand Personality** — Human character traits attributed to the brand.

**Brand Voice** — Consistent tone, style, and language the brand uses in communications.

**Visual Identity** — The graphical system: logo, color palette, typography, imagery style.

**Brand Guidelines** — Document specifying correct use of brand elements.

## Brand Equity Terms

**Brand Equity** — The commercial value derived from consumer perception of a brand name.

**Brand Value** — Financial value of the brand as an asset.

**Brand Loyalty** — Consumer's commitment to repurchase or continue using the brand.

**Brand Association** — Anything linked to the brand in memory (attributes, benefits, attitudes).

**Brand Awareness** — Consumer's ability to identify the brand under different conditions.

**Brand Recall** — Consumer's ability to retrieve the brand from memory when prompted with product category.

**Brand Recognition** — Consumer's ability to confirm prior exposure to the brand.

## Customer & Market Terms

**ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** — Description of the customer who gets the most value from your brand.

**Persona** — Fictional representation of an ideal customer type based on research.

**Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)** — What customers are trying to accomplish (functional, emotional, social).

**Target Segment** — Specific group of customers selected for marketing focus.

**Perceptual Map** — Visual representation of how customers perceive brands on key dimensions.

**Competitive Set** — Brands considered direct alternatives by consumers.

## Marketing Metrics Terms

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)** — Measure of customer loyalty and satisfaction.

**SOV (Share of Voice)** — Brand's share of total category advertising/expression.

**SOM (Share of Market)** — Brand's share of total category sales.

**SOV vs. SOM Correlation:** SOV > SOM typically indicates brand is growing; SOV < SOM indicates declining share.

**Share of Search** — Proportion of Google searches for a brand vs. competitors (Ehrenberg-Bass methodology).

**Mental Availability** — Likelihood of a brand being recalled in a buying situation.

**Double Jeopardy** — Statistical pattern where smaller brands have fewer buyers AND less loyalty.

## Communication Terms

**Above-the-Line (ATL)** — Mass media advertising (TV, print, online display).

**Below-the-Line (BTL)** — Targeted marketing (direct mail, email, events, digital).

**Through-the-Line (TTL)** — Integrated ATL and BTL campaigns.

**Integrated Campaign** — Unified message across all marketing channels and touchpoints.

**Creative Brief** — Document guiding creative development with objectives, audience, message, tone.

**Media Plan** — Strategy for allocating advertising budget across channels and timing.

**Reach** — Percentage of target audience exposed to an advertisement.

**Frequency** — Average number of times exposed to an advertisement.

## Brand Architecture Terms

**House of Brands** — Multiple unrelated brand names under corporate umbrella (Procter & Gamble model).

**Branded House** — One master brand with sub-brands (Apple, Google).

**Sub-brand** — Brand extending from master with its own identity (e.g., Toyota Camry).

**Brand Extension** — Using an existing brand name on a new product category.

**Brand Dilution** — Weakening of brand equity through poor extension or over-extension.

**Co-branding** — Partnership between two brands on a product or promotion.
