# Brand Manager — Standards & Reference

## Brand Strategy Frameworks

### Keller Brand Equity Model (CBBE)

The Customer-Based Brand Equity model by Kevin Lane Keller establishes four levels of brand equity building:

| Level | Component | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Level 1** | Brand Identity (Salience) | Customer awareness: unaided vs. aided recall, breadth and depth of brand associations |
| **Level 2** | Brand Meaning (Performance & Imagery) | What the brand delivers (performance) and what it represents (imagery) |
| **Level 3** | Brand Response (Judgments & Feelings) | Customer's rational judgments and emotional feelings about the brand |
| **Level 4** | Brand Resonance (Relationships) | Deep psychological bond: behavioral loyalty, attitudinal attachment, sense of community, active engagement |

### Kapferer Brand Identity Prism

Six facets of brand identity:

| Facet | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Physique** | Physical characteristics, product attributes |
| **Personality** | Human character traits the brand embodies |
| **Culture** | Values and principles underlying the brand |
| **Relationship** | Customer interaction and relationship quality |
| **Reflection** | Target customer archetype — who the brand images for |
| **Self-Image** | Internal self-image of the brand user |

### Ehrenberg-Bass Institute Principles

Scientific principles of marketing for brand growth:

- **Mental Availability**: Being top-of-mind in buying situations
- **Physical Availability**: Being easy to buy — accessible, prominent, permanent
- **Distinctive Brand Assets**: Brand elements that are easily recognized and always linked to the brand
- **Category Demand**: Growing the pie, not just stealing share
- **Learning & Memory**: Repetition and consistency build brand memory structures

## Brand Architecture

### Brand Architecture Models

| Model | Description | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Monolithic** | Single brand name across all products | When brand is powerful and products are closely related |
| **Endorsed** | House brands endorsed by corporate parent | When products are diverse; leverage parent credibility |
| **Freestanding** | Separate brand names with no visible link | When brand divorce is strategic |
| **Hybrid** | Combination of above | When context determines brand strategy |

## Brand Tracking & Research

**Brand Health Metrics:**

| Metric | Formula | Good Score |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **Unaided Awareness** | % naming brand without prompting | Category leaders: 40-60% |
| **Aided Awareness** | % recognizing brand when prompted | Category leaders: 80%+ |
| **Brand Consideration** | % considering brand for purchase | Should be 1.5-2× unaided |
| **Brand Preference** | % preferring brand vs. alternatives | Premium brands: 40%+ |
| **NPS** | % Promoters (9-10) minus % Detractors (0-6) | Tech SaaS: 30-50; FMCG: 20-40 |
