# Brand Manager — Troubleshooting Guide

## Brand Identity Problems

**Problem: Brand is losing differentiation from competitors**
- Diagnosis: Competitive convergence — brands are looking and sounding similar
- Analysis: Conduct perceptual mapping vs. competitors on key dimensions
- Check: Are you using category norms (premium/accessible, modern/traditional)?
- Fix: Identify white space on perceptual map; reposition to differentiate
- Reference: Ehrenberg-Bass "Same, Same" research — brands converge without intentional differentiation

**Problem: Brand is perceived as outdated or "for older customers"**
- Diagnosis: Brand refresh needed or positioning is genuinely dated
- Analysis: Age demographics of brand perception vs. target
- Check: Does product/formula need updating? Or is it just perception?
- Fix: Refresh visual identity if strategy is current; if strategy is dated, re-position first
- Warning: Logo change without strategy change is cosmetic only

**Problem: Brand extensions dilute core brand equity**
- Diagnosis: Extension doesn't reinforce core brand promise
- Check: Apply "extension fitness test": Does it fit with brand personality, expertise, values?
- Fix: Develop extensions that amplify brand associations, not contradict them
- Rule: If it requires explanation, it probably dilutes

## Brand Consistency Problems

**Problem: Different teams creating inconsistent brand expression**
- Diagnosis: Missing or outdated brand guidelines; no centralized review
- Fix: Update brand guidelines with specific do/don't examples
- Implement: Centralized brand review for all external-facing materials
- Tools: Use Frontify, Bynder, or Figma for centralized asset management
- Check: Does every team have access to current brand assets?

**Problem: Partner/co-brand uses brand incorrectly**
- Diagnosis: Co-brand guidelines not established or enforced
- Fix: Create explicit co-brand standards document
- Include: Logo placement rules, minimum sizes, color variations, language guidelines
- Enforce: Review and approve all co-brand executions before launch

## Brand Measurement Problems

**Problem: Brand metrics declining but don't know why**
- Diagnosis: Multi-factor causation — need to isolate variables
- Analysis:
  1. Check competitive activity (new entrants, increased spend)
  2. Check media coverage and sentiment
  3. Check product quality or service delivery issues
  4. Check social media for emerging complaints
  5. Review recent campaigns — any off-brand executions?
- Fix: Address root cause; if unknown, run tracking study more frequently

**Problem: Conflicting metrics between brand tracker and sales data**
- Diagnosis: Time lag between brand perception and purchase behavior
- Understanding: Brand tracker measures awareness/consideration; sales lag 3-12 months
- Fix: Use both metrics together; don't cherry-pick whichever looks better
- Reference: "Brand must be bought" (Ehrenberg-Bass) — purchase behavior is lagging indicator

## Crisis Management

**Problem: Social media crisis damaging brand equity**
- Response timeline:
  - Hour 0-2: Acknowledge — don't be silent
  - Hour 2-6: Assess — understand scope and root cause
  - Day 1: Act — specific response, not generic PR statement
  - Day 2-7: Monitor and adjust
  - Week 2+: Recovery communications
- Key: Authentic response beats perfect response; own the mistake

**Problem: Competitor launches attack on your brand**
- Diagnosis: Assess if attack is factually grounded or perception-based
- Response options:
  - If factual: Address quickly and honestly
  - If perception-based: Reframe the narrative
  - Avoid: Getting pulled into negative debate; rising to bait
- Strategy: Double down on your strengths rather than defending weaknesses

## Positioning Problems

**Problem: Positioning statement doesn't resonate with target audience**
- Diagnosis: Not validated with real customer input
- Check: Did you test positioning with target segment before launch?
- Fix: Conduct qualitative research (5-8 depth interviews) with target segment
- Iterate: Positioning is never final — test and refine

**Problem: Sales team not using brand positioning**
- Diagnosis: Positioning too abstract for sales to use practically
- Fix: Create "sales positioning cards" — one-pagers with key proof points
- Include: Positioning statement, 3 key benefits, proof points, competitive differentiators
- Train: Brief sales on how to use positioning in customer conversations
