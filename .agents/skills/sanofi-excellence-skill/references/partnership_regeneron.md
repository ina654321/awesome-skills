# Sanofi-Regeneron Partnership

## Partnership Overview

The Sanofi-Regeneron collaboration is one of the most successful pharmaceutical partnerships in history, centered on the development and commercialization of Dupixent (dupilumab) and other pipeline assets.

## Partnership History

### 2007: Agreement Signed
- Initial collaboration for monoclonal antibody development
- Broad discovery partnership
- Focus: inflammation and oncology

### Key Milestones

| Year | Milestone | Significance |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| 2007 | Partnership established | Long-term collaboration begins |
| 2010 | First patient dosed | Dupixent enters clinic |
| 2017 | FDA approval (AD) | First indication approved |
| 2018 | Asthma approval | Major expansion |
| 2019 | CRSwNP approval | ENT market entry |
| 2022 | EoE & PN approvals | Rare disease expansion |
| 2024 | COPD approval | First biologic in COPD |
| 2024 | Partnership lawsuit | Legal dispute emerges |

## Commercial Structure

### Dupixent Revenue Split

| Region | Sanofi Role | Regeneron Role | Economics |
|--------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| United States | Co-promotion | Co-promotion | 50/50 profit share |
| International | Commercial lead | Royalty recipient | Sanofi leads, REGN gets royalty |
| Manufacturing | Sanofi | - | Sanofi produces globally |

### 2024 Financials
- Global Dupixent: €13.1B (~$14.1B)
- Regeneron's share: ~$6B+ (significant portion of revenue)
- Sanofi's share: €13.1B (32% of company sales)

## Partnership Assets

### Commercialized Products
1. **Dupixent** (dupilumab) - Primary asset
2. **Libtayo** (cemiplimab) - Oncology (PD-1)
3. **Kevzara** (sarilumab) - Rheumatoid arthritis (discontinued)

### Pipeline Collaboration
- Multiple early-stage programs
- Shared discovery capabilities
- Platform technology sharing

## Recent Developments (2024-2025)

### November 2024: Regeneron Lawsuit

**Regeneron Allegations:**
- Failure to provide "full access" to PBM/payer contracts
- Blocking audit access to Dupixent books and records
- Potential underpayment of revenue share
- Claimed "significant monetary adjustment" owed

**Sanofi Response:**
- "Fully compliant with collaboration agreement terms"
- Information sharing within contract parameters
- Defending position vigorously

**Partnership Status:**
- Legal dispute ongoing
- Operational collaboration continues
- Dupixent commercialization unaffected
- Scientific relationship maintained

## Lessons Learned

### Success Factors
1. **Long-term orientation:** 17+ year partnership
2. **Complementary strengths:** Sanofi commercial, Regeneron science
3. **Shared investment:** Mutual commitment to development
4. **Clear structure:** Defined roles and economics

### Challenges
1. **Complex economics:** Revenue sharing requires transparency
2. **Scale growth:** Managing €13B+ product complexity
3. **Contract evolution:** Original terms may need updating
4. **Governance:** Maintaining alignment at scale

## Key Takeaways

1. **Partnership is critical to both companies** - Neither can afford dissolution
2. **Operational excellence continues** - Despite legal dispute
3. **Long-term perspective essential** - Through 2031+ patent life
4. **Governance matters** - Dispute resolution mechanisms needed
5. **Transparency requirements** - Modern partnerships need clear audit rights
