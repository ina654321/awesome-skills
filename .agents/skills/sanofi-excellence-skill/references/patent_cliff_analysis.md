# Sanofi Patent Cliff Analysis

## Executive Summary

Sanofi faces patent expirations on several key products through 2030, with peak exposure of approximately €3.5B in annual revenue at risk. The company has strategic plans to offset these losses through Dupixent growth and new product launches.

## Products at Risk

### 1. Lantus (Insulin Glargine)

**Product Profile:**
- Revenue: ~€2.5B (2024)
- Launch: 2000
- Indication: Type 1/2 diabetes

**Patent Status:**
| Market | Status | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| US | Biosimilar competition active | Revenue declining |
| EU | Biosimilar competition active | Revenue declining |
| ROW | Variable | Mixed exposure |

**Competition:**
- Semglee (Mylan/Viatris) - interchangeable biosimilar
- Rezvoglar (Eli Lilly) - biosimilar
- Unbranded biosimilars

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- **Toujeo conversion:** Higher-margin successor product
- **Contracting:** Maintain formulary position where possible
- **International focus:** Markets with slower biosimilar uptake
- **Manufacturing efficiency:** Cost reduction

**Expected Erosion:**
- 2024-2027: -15-20% annual decline
- 2027+: Continued gradual decline

---

### 2. Aubagio (Teriflunomide)

**Product Profile:**
- Revenue: ~€1.2B (2024)
- Launch: 2012
- Indication: Relapsing multiple sclerosis

**Patent Status:**
- US: Generic entry 2024
- EU: Generic entry ongoing
- Generic competitors: Multiple

**Competition:**
- Generic teriflunomide
- Oral MS therapies (fingolimod, dimethyl fumarate)
- High-efficacy infusions (Ocrevus, Kesimpta)

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- **Patient migration:** Transition to other Sanofi MS products
- **Rapid generic erosion acceptance:** Harvest remaining value
- **Cost reduction:** Eliminate promotional spend

**Expected Erosion:**
- 2024-2025: -40-50% decline
- 2025+: Minimal residual value

---

### 3. Jevtana (Cabazitaxel)

**Product Profile:**
- Revenue: <€500M (2024)
- Indication: Metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer

**Patent Status:**
- US: Expired (2021)
- EU: Generic competition since 2021

**Status:**
- Already facing generic competition
- Revenue impact largely realized

## Strategic Response

### 1. Portfolio Transformation
- Divested Opella (consumer) - reduces LOE exposure
- Focus on specialty care - longer exclusivity periods
- Pipeline-in-a-product assets for sustained growth

### 2. Launch Acceleration
- Target: >€10B from launches by 2030
- Key assets: Amlitelimab, frexalimab, tolebrutinib
- Rapid ramp to offset LOE

### 3. Dupixent Lifecycle
- €13B → €22B ambition
- New indications (COPD, BP, CSU)
- Geographic expansion
- Combination strategies

### 4. Cost Efficiency
- Post-Opella margin expansion
- Reduced LOE product support costs
- Reinvestment in growth drivers

## Timeline of Exposures

## Risk Factors

### 1. Faster Than Expected Erosion
- Aggressive biosimilar pricing
- PBM formulary exclusions
- Accelerated switching

### 2. Launch Delays
- Pipeline setbacks
- Regulatory delays
- Competitive threats

### 3. Dupixent Concentration
- 32% of revenue from single product
- Partnership risks
- Patent challenge risks

## Conclusion

Sanofi's patent cliff is:
- **Manageable:** €3.5B at risk vs. €12B+ growth potential
- **Well-understood:** Markets priced in LOE impact
- **Offset by innovation:** Dupixent + new launches
- **Declining risk:** Portfolio transformation complete

The company is well-positioned to navigate the patent cliff while delivering double-digit growth.
