# Gilead HIV Franchise Reference

## Overview

Gilead has been the global leader in HIV treatment for over two decades, with a comprehensive portfolio spanning treatment and prevention. The HIV franchise generated **$19.6 billion in 2024 revenue** (+8% YoY), representing approximately 68% of total company sales.

---

## Commercial Portfolio

### Biktarvy (Bictegravir/Emtricitabine/Tenofovir Alafenamide)

**Financial Performance:**
| Year | Global Sales | Growth | U.S. Sales |
|------|--------------|--------|------------|
| 2024 | $13.42B | +13% | $10.9B |
| 2023 | $11.8B | +19% | - |
| 2022 | $10.4B | +18% | - |

**Clinical Profile:**
- **Mechanism:** Integrase strand transfer inhibitor (INSTI) + dual NRTI backbone
- **Dosing:** Once-daily single tablet
- **Efficacy:** Non-inferior/superior to comparator regimens across trials
- **Resistance:** High barrier; M184V mutation compatibility (key differentiator)
- **Safety:** Favorable renal and bone mineral density profile vs TDF-containing regimens

**Market Position:**
- >50% U.S. HIV treatment market share (unprecedented for single product)
- Preferred first-line regimen in DHHS guidelines
- Patent exclusivity extended to 2036 via settlements with Lupin, Cipla, Laurus Labs

**Strategic Importance:**
Biktarvy is Gilead's most important revenue generator and the foundation of HIV strategy through 2036. The M184V compatibility—allowing use in patients with resistance to lamivudine/emtricitabine—provides competitive moat.

### Descovy (Emtricitabine/Tenofovir Alafenamide)

**2024 Sales:** ~$2.4B (+21% in Q4)

**Indications:**
- HIV-1 treatment (in combination with third agent)
- HIV-1 pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP)

**Differentiation vs Truvada:**
- TAF (tenofovir alafenamide) vs TDF (tenofovir disoproxil fumarate)
- Improved renal safety profile
- Better bone mineral density preservation
- Lower plasma tenofovir levels (reduced off-target effects)

**PrEP Positioning:**
- Branded alternative to generic Truvada for PrEP
- Growing share as patients/providers prioritize safety profile
- Foundation for transitioning Truvada patients post-generic entry

---

### Truvada (Emtricitabine/Tenofovir Disoproxil Fumarate)

**Status:** Facing generic competition (U.S. LOE: late 2020)

**Historical Significance:**
- First FDA-approved drug for HIV PrEP (2012)
- Backbone of early combination therapies
- Foundation of Gilead's HIV prevention legacy

**Current Role:**
- Declining revenues (generic competition)
- Maintains presence in resource-limited settings
- Reference point for next-generation PrEP (lenacapavir)

## Pipeline: Lenacapavir

### Overview

Lenacapavir is a **first-in-class HIV capsid inhibitor** with multi-stage mechanism of action, representing the most significant innovation in HIV since combination therapy.

### Mechanism of Action

Unlike existing antiretrovirals that target single stages of viral replication, lenacapavir inhibits HIV at **multiple lifecycle stages**:
1. Viral capsid nuclear entry
2. Capsid assembly
3. Release of viral genetic material

**Key Property:** No known cross-resistance to existing drug classes in vitro

---

### Sunlenca (Lenacapavir for Treatment)

**Status:** Approved (2022)

**Indication:** Multi-drug resistant HIV-1 in heavily treatment-experienced adults

**Dosing:** 
- Initiation: Oral loading dose
- Maintenance: Subcutaneous injection every 6 months

**Pivotal Trial:** CAPELLA
- Population: Heavily treatment-experienced with resistance to multiple classes
- Primary endpoint: Viral suppression at Week 52
- Results: Significant efficacy in difficult-to-treat population

## Development Pipeline: HIV Treatment Options

### Daily Oral Regimens

**Bictegravir/Lenacapavir (BIC/LEN)**
- Single-tablet regimen
- Phase 3 ARTISTRY program ongoing
- Target: Treatment-naive and virologically suppressed switch patients

### Weekly Oral Regimens

**Lenacapavir/Islatravir (LEN/ISL)**
- Partnership with Merck
- Both long-acting agents
- Phase 3 ISLEND program
- Potential for once-weekly oral HIV treatment

### Injectable Regimens

**Twice-Yearly: Lenacapavir + GS-3242**
- GS-3242: Investigational integrase strand transfer inhibitor
- Chosen over GS-1219 after Phase 1 comparison
- Phase 1 data expected 2026

**Quarterly: Lenacapavir + GS-1614**
- GS-1614: Islatravir prodrug
- Earlier-stage development
- Targeting quarterly dosing frequency

### On Clinical Hold

**GS-1720 + GS-4182**
- Status: FDA clinical hold (June 2024)
- Reason: Safety signal of decreased CD4+ T-cell and lymphocyte counts
- Resolution timeline: Unknown
- Impact: Does not affect rest of HIV pipeline

### Competitive Landscape

**Treatment:**
- Gilead: Market leader (Biktarvy, Descovy)
- ViiV Healthcare (GSK): Dovato, Juluca, Cabenuva
- Janssen: Prezcobix, Symtuza

**PrEP:**
- Oral: Truvada (generic), Descovy (branded)
- Injectable: Apretude (ViiV, monthly)
- Upcoming: Yeztugo (Gilead, twice-yearly)

**Key Differentiator:**
Lenacapavir's twice-yearly dosing is unmatched in the market. ViiV's Apretude requires monthly injections, creating adherence burden advantage for Gilead.

## Access Strategy

### High-Burden Countries

Gilead has established access programs to ensure lenacapavir availability in resource-limited settings:
- Voluntary licensing agreements with generic manufacturers
- Low-cost pricing tiers based on country income level
- Partnership with PEPFAR and Global Fund

### U.S. Market

- Patient assistance programs for uninsured
- Copay support for commercially insured
- CDC endorsement for PrEP guidelines
- Integration with Ryan White HIV/AIDS Program
