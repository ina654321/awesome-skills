# Gilead Pipeline 2025-2033 Reference

## Executive Summary

Gilead's development pipeline is designed to deliver **9+ new product launches through 2033**, with strategic focus on:
1. HIV franchise extension beyond Biktarvy LOE (2033/2036)
2. Oncology growth via cell therapy and ADCs
3. Inflammation expansion

**Key Milestone:** Lenacapavir PrEP launch (Summer 2025)

---

### Phase 3 Programs

#### ARTISTRY Program (Bictegravir/Lenacapavir)

**Combination:** BIC 50mg + LEN 25mg (single tablet)

**Indications:**
- Treatment-naive adults with HIV-1
- Virologically suppressed adults switching from current regimen

**Design:** Non-inferiority vs Biktarvy

**Strategic Value:**
- Daily oral option for patients preferring pills over injections
- Extends BIC franchise
- Provides resistance profile options

**Timeline:** 
- Data: 2026-2027
- Launch: 2027-2028

---

#### ISLEND Program (Lenacapavir/Islatravir)

**Partnership:** Merck & Co.

**Combination:** LEN + ISL (weekly oral)

**Mechanism:**
- Lenacapavir: Capsid inhibitor
- Islatravir: Nucleoside reverse transcriptase translocation inhibitor (NRTTI)

**Strategic Value:**
- First weekly oral HIV treatment regimen
- Addresses adherence through reduced dosing frequency
- Leverages partner expertise

**Timeline:**
- Phase 3 initiation: Expected 2025
- Data: 2027-2028
- Launch: 2028-2029

---

### Phase 1/2 Programs

#### GS-3242 + Lenacapavir

**Target:** Twice-yearly injectable HIV treatment

**GS-3242:** Integrase strand transfer inhibitor (INSTI)

**Selection Rationale:**
- Chosen over GS-1219 after Phase 1 comparison
- Superior PK/PD profile
- Better compatibility with lenacapavir

**Timeline:**
- Phase 1 data: 2026
- Phase 2/3: 2026-2028
- Launch: 2028-2030

#### GS-1614 + Lenacapavir

**Target:** Quarterly injectable HIV treatment

**GS-1614:** Islatravir prodrug

**Strategic Value:**
- Bridges gap between daily/weekly oral and twice-yearly injectable
- Provides patient choice in dosing frequency

**Status:** Phase 1

---

### On Clinical Hold

#### GS-1720 + GS-4182

**Target:** Novel HIV treatment combination

**Status:** FDA clinical hold (June 2024)

**Safety Signal:**
- Decreased CD4+ T-cell counts
- Decreased absolute lymphocyte counts

**Impact:**
- Does not affect other HIV pipeline programs
- Resolution timeline unknown
- Gilead committed to working with FDA

## Oncology Pipeline

### Cell Therapy

#### Anito-cel (KITE-819)

**Partnership:** Arcellx

**Target:** BCMA (for multiple myeloma)

**Technology:** D-Domain (novel binding domain vs traditional scFv)

**Phase 2 Data (iMMagine):**
| Endpoint | Result |
|----------|--------|
| Overall Response Rate | 97% |
| Stringent Complete Response | 63% |
| Grade 3+ CRS | 7% |
| Grade 3+ ICANS | 3% |

**Competitive Comparison:**
| Product | ORR | sCR | CRS Gr3+ |
|---------|-----|-----|----------|
| Anito-cel | 97% | 63% | 7% |
| Carvykti | 98% | 78% | 5% |
| Abecma | 72% | 28% | 9% |

**Timeline:**
- Phase 2 completion: 2025
- BLA submission: 2025
- Target launch: 2026

**Market Opportunity:** $12B global MM CAR-T market by 2030

---

#### Yescarta Earlier-Line Studies

**ZUMA-23:** First-line high-risk LBCL
- Design: CAR-T vs standard chemo + transplant
- Status: Phase 2
- Potential: Expand addressable market significantly

**ZUMA-5 Follow-up:** Follicular lymphoma long-term data
- Focus: Durability of responses
- Label expansion potential

---

### Antibody-Drug Conjugates

#### Trodelvy Expansion

**Ongoing Trials:**
- **TROPiCS-03:** Basket study in multiple solid tumors
- **EVOKE-01:** Trodelvy + Keytruda in NSCLC (different from discontinued TROPiCS-04)
- **EVER-132-002:** China registration study

**Discontinued:**
- TROPiCS-04 (NSCLC 2L+): Regulatory feedback led to discontinuation

---

### Immuno-Oncology

#### Anti-TIGIT: Domvanalimab

**Mechanism:** Fc-silent anti-TIGIT antibody

**STAR-121 Study:**
- Population: 1L non-squamous NSCLC
- Arms:
  - Domvanalimab + zimberelimab (PD-1) + chemo
  - Placebo + pembrolizumab + chemo
- Primary endpoint: PFS, OS
- Status: Phase 3, ongoing

**Differentiation:**
- Fc-silent design may improve safety profile
- Competition: Roche (tiragolumab), BMS, others

**Timeline:** Data expected 2025-2026

---

#### Adenosine Pathway

**Etrumadenant (A2a/A2b antagonist):**
- Multiple combination studies
- Early-stage development

**Quemliclustat (CD73 inhibitor):**
- Phase 1/2 studies ongoing
- Rationale: Target immunosuppressive adenosine in TME

## Risk Factors

### Development Risks

1. **Clinical Hold Impact:** GS-1720/GS-4182 delay affects timeline
2. **Competitive Pressure:** ViiV, BMS developing long-acting HIV options
3. **Regulatory Uncertainty:** FDA scrutiny of CAR-T safety
4. **Manufacturing Scale:** Cell therapy capacity constraints

### Mitigation Strategies

1. **Diversified Pipeline:** Multiple programs per therapeutic area
2. **Strategic Partnerships:** Risk-sharing with Merck, Arcellx
3. **Platform Investments:** Manufacturing capacity expansion
4. **Regulatory Engagement:** Early and frequent FDA interaction

## Strategic Pipeline Themes

### 1. Long-Acting Innovation

**Vision:** Transform HIV from daily burden to twice-yearly visit

**Programs:**
- Daily → Weekly → Quarterly → Twice-yearly
- Treatment and prevention options
- Patient choice in dosing frequency

### 2. Oncology Diversification

**Vision:** Build sustainable oncology franchise beyond HIV

**Programs:**
- Cell therapy earlier-line expansion
- ADC technology advancement
- Immuno-oncology validation

### 3. Lifecycle Management

**Vision:** Smooth transition from Biktarvy to next-generation options

**Programs:**
- Patent extension to 2036
- Multiple BIC- and LEN-based combinations
- Portfolio approach to resistance management
