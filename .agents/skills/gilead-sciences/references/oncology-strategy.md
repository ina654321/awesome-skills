# Gilead Oncology Strategy Reference

## Overview

Gilead's oncology business represents a strategic pivot from the declining HCV franchise and a hedge against eventual HIV patent expiration. With **$3.3 billion in 2024 revenue** (+12% YoY), oncology is now the company's second-largest therapeutic area.

**Strategic Evolution:**
- 2017: Entry via Kite Pharma acquisition ($11.9B)
- 2020: Expansion via Immunomedics acquisition ($21B for Trodelvy)
- 2024: Optimization via Kite restructuring and focus on profitable growth

---

## Cell Therapy: Kite Pharma

### Corporate Structure

**Kite Pharma** operates as a standalone business unit within Gilead:
- **CEO:** Cindy Perettie (appointed May 2023)
- **Headquarters:** Santa Monica, CA
- **Manufacturing:** El Segundo, CA
- **Employees:** 4,000+ (post-2024 restructuring)

**History:**
- Founded: 2009 (Arie Belldegrun)
- Acquired by Gilead: 2017 for $11.9 billion
- First CAR-T approval: Yescarta (2017)

### Commercial Products

#### Yescarta (Axicabtagene Ciloleucel)

**2024 Sales:** ~$1.5B (-5% YoY)

**Indications:**
| Approval Date | Indication | Line of Therapy |
|--------------|------------|-----------------|
| Oct 2017 | Relapsed/refractory large B-cell lymphoma (R/R LBCL) | 3L+ |
| Mar 2021 | R/R LBCL | 2L (after chemo) |
| Mar 2021 | R/R follicular lymphoma (FL) | 3L+ |
| Apr 2022 | R/R LBCL | 2L+ (early relapsed/refractory) |

**Clinical Profile:**
- Overall response rate: 72-83% (varies by indication)
- Complete response rate: 51-58%
- Median duration of response: 11+ months

**Competitive Landscape:**
| Product | Company | Indications | Key Differentiation |
|---------|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| Yescarta | Gilead/Kite | LBCL, FL | Earlier line approval |
| Kymriah | Novartis | LBCL, FL, ALL | Pediatric ALL indication |
| Breyanzi | BMS | LBCL | Potentially cleaner safety |
| Carvykti | J&J/Legend | Multiple myeloma | BCMA target |
| Abecma | BMS | Multiple myeloma | BCMA target |

**Safety Considerations:**
- Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS): Grade 3+ in 10-15%
- Neurotoxicity (ICANS): Grade 3+ in 20-30%
- Prophylactic corticosteroid protocol added to label (2021)
- FDA investigation into secondary T-cell malignancies (ongoing)

#### Tecartus (Brexucabtagene Autoleucel)

**2024 Sales:** ~$400M (-15% YoY)

**Indications:**
| Approval Date | Indication |
|--------------|------------|
| Jul 2020 | Relapsed/refractory mantle cell lymphoma (MCL) |
| Oct 2021 | Relapsed/refractory B-cell precursor ALL (adults) |

**Positioning:**
- First and only CAR-T approved for MCL
- Competitive with BTK inhibitors (ibrutinib, acalabrutinib)
- ALL indication addresses high unmet need

### Manufacturing Operations

**El Segundo Facility:**
- Dedicated CAR-T manufacturing
- Vector production capabilities
- Quality control and release testing

**Manufacturing Challenges:**
- Autologous process: 3-4 week turnaround
- Cold chain requirements
- High cost of goods (~$50K+ per treatment)
- Capacity constraints during demand surges

**Competitive Advantage:**
Years of process development and regulatory validation create meaningful barriers to entry for new CAR-T competitors.

### Development Pipeline

#### Anito-cel (BCMA CAR-T for Multiple Myeloma)

**Status:** Phase 2 (iMMagine trial)

**Partnership:** Arcellx (D-Domain technology platform)

**Clinical Data (ASCO 2024):**
- Overall response rate: 97%
- Stringent complete response: 63%
- Safety: Favorable CRS/ICANS profile vs competitors

**Target Launch:** 2026 (U.S.)

**Market Opportunity:**
- Global MM CAR-T market: $12B by 2030 (Gilead estimate)
- Competitive vs Carvykti and Abecma
- Potential for earlier line positioning

**Strategic Significance:**
Rather than building BCMA CAR-T from scratch, Gilead partnered with Arcellx for differentiated technology. This asset-light approach leverages external innovation.

#### Earlier-Line CAR-T Studies

**ZUMA-23:** Yescarta in 1L high-risk LBCL
- Status: Phase 2
- Goal: Move CAR-T earlier in treatment paradigm
- Competition: Chemotherapy + transplant standard of care

**KITE-222:** CAR-T for multiple myeloma (internal program)
- BCMA-targeted
- Alternative to Arcellx partnership

### Kite Restructuring (2024)

**Announcement:** May 2024

**Actions:**
- ~7% workforce reduction (~300 positions)
- 90 new positions created
- Net impact: ~5% reduction

**Rationale:**
- Align organization with areas of future growth
- Improve operational efficiency
- Focus resources on anito-cel launch preparation

**Leadership Quote (Cindy Perettie):**
> "While our business is performing well and we are making a meaningful impact for patients eligible for CAR T therapy, we believe there is a great deal of opportunity to continue to drive the adoption of our therapies."

### Competitive Threats

**In-Class:**
- BMS (Breyanzi, Abecma): Strong pipeline and resources
- Novartis (Kymriah): Pediatric presence and global reach
- J&J/Legend (Carvykti): BCMA leadership

**Out-of-Class:**
- Bispecific antibodies (Roche, Genmab): Off-the-shelf convenience
- T-cell engagers: Potentially lower cost and logistics burden

## Antibody-Drug Conjugates: Trodelvy

### Overview

**Generic Name:** Sacituzumab govitecan-hziy
**Class:** TROP2-directed antibody-drug conjugate (ADC)
**Acquisition:** Immunomedics ($21B, 2020)

### Commercial Performance

| Year | Global Sales | Growth |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 2024 | $1.4B | +6% |
| 2023 | $1.3B | +23% |
| 2022 | $0.9B | - |

### Approved Indications

| Approval Date | Indication | Setting |
|--------------|------------|---------|
| Apr 2020 | Metastatic triple-negative breast cancer (mTNBC) | 3L+ (accelerated) |
| Apr 2021 | mTNBC | 2L+ (full) |
| Apr 2021 | Metastatic urothelial cancer (mUC) | 2L+ |
| Feb 2023 | HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer | 2L+ endocrine-resistant |

### Mechanism of Action

1. **TROP2 Targeting:** Anti-TROP2 antibody binds to tumor cells
2. **Internalization:** ADC is internalized
3. **Payload Release:** SN-38 (topoisomerase I inhibitor) released
4. **DNA Damage:** Cell death via topoisomerase inhibition
5. **Bystander Effect:** Released payload affects neighboring cells

### Clinical Profile

**Efficacy:**
- mTNBC: 35% ORR in heavily pretreated
- mUC: 27% ORR
- HR+/HER2-: 21% ORR with meaningful PFS benefit

**Safety:**
- Neutropenia (Grade 3+: 50%+)
- Diarrhea (Grade 3+: 10%)
- Fatigue, nausea
- Dose reductions/interruptions common

### Recent Setbacks

**TROPiCS-04 (NSCLC):**
- Study evaluating Trodelvy in 2L+ metastatic NSCLC
- Discontinued following regulatory feedback (2024)
- Reason: Benefit-risk profile didn't support continuation

**Bladder Cancer Uncertainty:**
- TROPiCS-04 results raised questions about Trodelvy expansion
- mUC indication maintained but growth limited

**IPR&D Impairment:**
- Q1 2024: $4.2B impairment charge related to Immunomedics assets
- Reflects reduced long-term expectations

### Competitive Landscape

| Product | Company | Target | Indications |
|---------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Trodelvy | Gilead | TROP2 | mTNBC, mUC, HR+/HER2- BC |
| Enhertu | AZ/Daiichi | HER2 | Breast, gastric, lung |
| Padcev | Seagen/MSD | Nectin-4 | Bladder cancer |
| Dato-DXd | AZ/Daiichi | TROP2 | NSCLC, breast (in development) |

**Key Risk:** Dato-DXd (datopotamab deruxtecan) could challenge Trodelvy in TROP2-expressing tumors with potentially better safety profile.

### Development Strategy

**Continued Focus:**
- mTNBC: Maintaining leadership position
- HR+/HER2- breast cancer: Growth driver post-2023 approval
- Combinations: Trodelvy + Keytruda in various settings

**Discontinued Programs:**
- NSCLC (2L+): After TROPiCS-04
- Earlier-line trials under review

## Immuno-Oncology Pipeline

### Anti-TIGIT Program

**Product:** Domvanalimab (Fc-silent anti-TIGIT)

**Mechanism:** TIGIT checkpoint inhibition with reduced Fc effector function (potentially improved safety)

**STAR-121 Study:**
- Phase 3 in 1L non-small cell lung cancer
- Combination: Domvanalimab + zimberelimab (PD-1) + chemotherapy
- Comparator: pembrolizumab + chemo
- Status: Ongoing

**Strategic Rationale:**
- TIGIT is highly validated target (Roche, BMS, others in development)
- Gilead's Fc-silent approach may differentiate on safety
- Large market opportunity in NSCLC if successful

### Adenosine Pathway

**Products:**
- Etrumadenant: Dual A2a/A2b adenosine receptor antagonist
- Quemliclustat: CD73 inhibitor

**Rationale:** Adenosine in tumor microenvironment suppresses immune response; blocking this pathway may enhance anti-tumor immunity.

**Status:** Early-stage development

### Discontinued Programs

**Magrolimab (CD47):**
- Status: Development discontinued (2023)
- Reason: Safety concerns and lack of clear efficacy signal
- Partner: Forty Seven Inc. (acquired by Gilead)

## Inflammation Expansion

### Livdelzi (Seladelpar)

**Acquisition:** CymaBay Therapeutics ($3.9B, 2024)

**Indication:** Primary biliary cholangitis (PBC)

**FDA Approval:** August 2024 (accelerated)

**Mechanism:** Selective PPAR-delta agonist

**Clinical Profile:**
- Primary endpoint: Alkaline phosphatase reduction
- Pruritus (itching) improvement vs placebo
- Safety: Generally well tolerated

**Market Opportunity:**
- PBC affects ~130,000 patients in U.S./EU
- First new treatment option in years
- Alternative to Ocaliva (Intercept), which has tolerability issues

**2024 Performance:**
- Exceeded internal launch expectations
- Strong initial uptake in patients intolerant to Ocaliva

**Strategic Significance:**
Beachhead indication for inflammation franchise. Gilead is exploring seladelpar in other cholestatic liver diseases.

## Strategic Assessment

### Strengths

1. **Cell Therapy Manufacturing:** Kite's El Segundo facility provides competitive moat
2. **Diversified Modalities:** CAR-T, ADC, small molecule, biologics
3. **Early Leadership:** Yescarta's 2L+ approval maintains position
4. **Partnership Strategy:** Arcellx deal demonstrates smart external innovation

### Challenges

1. **Trodelvy Underperformance:** $21B acquisition not yet justified by returns
2. **Cell Therapy Competition:** In-class and out-class pressure
3. **Safety Concerns:** FDA T-cell malignancy investigation
4. **Manufacturing Complexity:** Limits scalability and profitability

### Outlook

**Near-Term (2025-2026):**
- Anito-cel launch in multiple myeloma
- Trodelvy stabilization in breast cancer
- Kite restructuring benefits realization

**Medium-Term (2027-2030):**
- Cell therapy earlier-line expansion
- Inflammation portfolio growth
- Potential new ADC or bispecific entrants

**Long-Term (2030+):**
- Allogeneic (off-the-shelf) cell therapy
- Solid tumor CAR-T breakthrough
- Oncology as 20%+ of Gilead revenue
