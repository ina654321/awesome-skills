## § 7 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Clinical Study Report Development

**Context**: Phase III oncology trial completed; CSR needed for regulatory submission.

**Development Process**:
1. **Kickoff Meeting** (Week 1):
   - Team: Medical writer, statistician, medical monitor, regulatory
   - Review SAP, protocol, TLFs (tables, listings, figures)
   - Confirm CSR structure, timelines, responsibilities

2. **Draft Development** (Weeks 2-6):
   - Writer drafts: Synopsis, methods, patient disposition
   - Statistician provides: Results text for efficacy/safety
   - Medical monitor reviews: Clinical interpretation
   - Integration of TLFs into document

3. **Review Cycles** (Weeks 7-10):
   - Internal review: Medical, statistics, regulatory
   - Author review: Principal investigators
   - Consolidation: Address all comments

4. **Quality Control** (Week 11):
   - Editorial review: Grammar, formatting, consistency
   - Data integrity check: Numbers match TLFs
   - Regulatory compliance review

5. **Finalization** (Week 12):
   - Signatures: Authors, approvers
   - Archival: Version control
   - Submission: To regulatory authority

**Key Challenge**: Efficacy positive but safety signal in hepatic enzymes
**Solution**: Balanced presentation, discussion of benefit-risk, recommended monitoring

### Scenario 2: Manuscript Publication Strategy

**Context**: Groundbreaking immunotherapy data; strategic publication planning.

**Strategy Development**:
1. **Gap Analysis**:
   - Current landscape: Competitor publications
   - Unmet need: Long-term survival data
   - Opportunity: First in specific population

2. **Journal Targeting**:
   - Primary target: NEJM (impact factor 90+)
   - Backup options: Lancet, JCO, Lancet Oncology
   - Selection rationale: Novelty, practice-changing potential

3. **Authorship Planning**:
   - First author: Principal investigator
   - Last author: Academic thought leader
   - Middle authors: Investigators, statisticians
   - Professional writer: Acknowledgment (per GPP3)

4. **Content Development**:
   - Key message: Survival benefit in refractory population
   - Structure: CONSORT-compliant
   - Figures: Survival curves, waterfall plot, swimmer plot

5. **Submission Strategy**:
   - Pre-submission inquiry to NEJM
   - Cover letter highlighting significance
   - Suggested reviewers (non-competitors)
   - Excluded reviewers (competitors)

**Outcome**: Accepted with minor revisions; accompanying editorial

### Scenario 3: Advisory Board Summary Report

**Context**: Post-launch advisory board; insights on real-world use.

**Meeting Details**:
- 12 KOLs (oncologists, community and academic)
- 4-hour meeting, case discussions, polling
- Objective: Understand adoption barriers, gather insights

**Report Structure**:
1. **Executive Summary** (1 page):
   - Key insights, recommendations
   - Action items with owners and timelines

2. **Meeting Context**:
   - Objectives, participants, agenda

3. **Detailed Discussion**:
   - Patient selection: Who benefits most?
   - Dosing/scheduling: Real-world modifications
   - Safety management: AE mitigation strategies
   - Competitive positioning: Differentiation

4. **Polling Results**:
   - Quantitative responses (charts)
   - Qualitative themes

5. **Insights and Recommendations**:
   - Medical education needs identified
   - Publication opportunities
   - Registry/real-world study suggestions

6. **Appendices**:
   - Participant list, agenda, presentation slides

**Distribution**: Internal medical affairs, commercial, regulatory

### Scenario 4: Investigator Brochure Update

**Context**: Annual IB update required with new safety data.

**Update Process**:
1. **Safety Data Review**:
   - New AEs since last update
   - Safety signal evaluation
   - Regulatory feedback incorporated

2. **Section Updates**:
   - Section 5.3: Preclinical safety → No changes
   - Section 6: Effects in humans → New safety section
   - Section 7: Guidance for investigators → Dosing modifications

3. **Key Additions**:
   - Hepatic monitoring recommendations
   - Dose adjustment for hepatic impairment
   - New contraindication (severe hepatic impairment)

4. **Review and Approval**:
   - Medical monitor review
   - Regulatory approval
   - Quality control

5. **Distribution**:
   - Updated IB sent to all sites
   - Version control (Version 6.0, Date: March 2024)
   - Acknowledgment tracking

### Scenario 5: Abstract Development for Congress

**Context**: ASCO abstract deadline; need to submit Phase 2 results.

**Abstract Strategy**:
1. **Target Session**:
   - Oral presentation vs poster
   - Specific track (e.g., Developmental Therapeutics)

2. **Structure** (ASCO format):
   - Background (2-3 sentences)
   - Methods (design, patients, endpoints)
   - Results (key efficacy, safety, biomarker data)
   - Conclusions (clinical implications)

3. **Content**:
   - 2,500 character limit (including spaces)
   - Key data: ORR 45%, mPFS 8.2 months
   - Safety: Manageable toxicity profile
   - Biomarker: PD-L1 positive enrichment

4. **Supporting Materials**:
   - Tables (up to 2 allowed)
   - Figures (not included in character count)
   - References (up to 10)

5. **Submission**:
   - Online portal submission
   - PI approval
   - Disclosure form completion

**Outcome**: Accepted as oral presentation; press release prepared
