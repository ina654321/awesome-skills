# Cell & Gene Therapy at Novartis

## Kymriah (Tisagenlecleucel)

### Overview
- **Type**: CAR-T cell therapy
- **First Approved**: August 2017 (FDA) - World's first CAR-T therapy
- **Indications**:
  - B-cell ALL (acute lymphoblastic leukemia) - pediatric/young adult
  - DLBCL (diffuse large B-cell lymphoma) - adult
  - Follicular lymphoma
- **Mechanism**: CD19-directed genetically modified autologous T-cell immunotherapy

### Manufacturing Process
```
PATIENT LEUKAPHERESIS
         ↓
T-CELL SELECTION (CliniMACS)
         ↓
ACTIVATION & TRANSFECTION (Lentiviral vector)
         ↓
EXPANSION (Bioreactor, 7-10 days)
         ↓
FORMULATION & CRYOPRESERVATION
         ↓
RELEASE TESTING (qPCR, Flow, Sterility)
         ↓
INFUSION AT TREATMENT CENTER
```

### Manufacturing Network

| Facility | Location | Status | Capacity |
|----------|----------|--------|----------|
| Stein | Switzerland | Commercial | Global supply |
| Morris Plains | New Jersey, USA | Commercial + Expansion | Americas |
| Les Ulis | France | Commercial | EU |
| Leipzig (Fraunhofer IZI) | Germany | Clinical/Commercial | EU overflow |
| Kobe (FBRI) | Japan | Commercial | First Asian CAR-T facility |
| Melbourne (Peter Mac) | Australia | Commercial | First Australian CAR-T |

### Key Metrics
- **Vein-to-Vein Time**: 22-28 days
- **Dose**: 0.2-6.0 × 10^8 CAR-positive viable T cells
- **Price**: $475,000 (ALL), $373,000 (DLBCL)
- **2024 Sales**: ~$600M

## Zolgensma (Onasemnogene Abeparvovec)

### Overview
- **Type**: AAV9 gene therapy
- **First Approved**: May 2019 (FDA)
- **Indication**: Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) Type 1
- **Mechanism**: SMN1 gene replacement via single IV infusion

### Manufacturing
- **Vector**: Self-complementary adeno-associated virus serotype 9 (scAAV9)
- **Production**: HEK293 cell culture, triple transfection
- **Dose**: 1.1 × 10^14 vg/kg
- **Price**: $2.125M (one-time treatment)

### Commercial Performance
- **2024 Sales**: ~$1.3B
- **Patients Treated**: 3,000+ globally
- **Markets**: 50+ countries

## Radioligand Therapy

### Pluvicto (Lutetium Lu 177 Vipivotide Tetraxetan)

- **Type**: Targeted radioligand therapy
- **Target**: PSMA (prostate-specific membrane antigen)
- **Indication**: Metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC)
- **Isotope**: Lu-177 (beta emitter, 6.7-day half-life)
- **2024 Sales**: $1.3B (+42% cc)

### Manufacturing Complexity
```
Lu-177 PRODUCTION (Reactor/Cyclotron)
         ↓
PEPTIDE CONJUGATION
         ↓
QUALITY CONTROL (Radio-TLC, HPLC)
         ↓
DOSE CALIBRATION
         ↓
PATIENT-SPECIFIC DISPENSING
         ↓
COLD CHAIN TRANSPORT (24-48 hours)
         ↓
INFUSION AT AUTHORIZED CENTER
```

### Supply Chain
- **Isotope Sources**: IRE (Belgium), CPDC (Canada), NorthStar (USA)
- **Manufacturing**: Indianapolis (USA), Milan (Italy)
- **Treatment Centers**: 150+ authorized globally
- **Investment**: Part of $23B US manufacturing commitment
