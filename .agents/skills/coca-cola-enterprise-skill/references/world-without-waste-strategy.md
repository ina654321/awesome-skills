# World Without Waste Strategy

## Overview

World Without Waste is The Coca-Cola Company's global sustainable packaging program, launched in 2018. It represents one of the most ambitious environmental commitments in the beverage industry.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Design

**Goal**: Make 100% of packaging recyclable globally by 2025

**Progress**:
- 90% of packaging is now recyclable (as of 2024)
- Challenge: Remaining 10% includes complex multi-material packaging
- Innovation: Label-less bottles, simplified designs

**Key Initiatives**:
- rPET (recycled PET) bottles
- Plant-based materials
- Lightweighting (reducing material per bottle)
- Design for recyclability standards

### 2. Collect

**Goal**: Collect and recycle a bottle or can for every one we sell by 2030

**Revised Goal (2024)**: 70-75% collection rate by 2035

**Progress**:
- Collection rates vary significantly by market
- Higher rates in markets with deposit return schemes
- Lower rates where recycling infrastructure is limited

**Key Initiatives**:
- Partnership with Republic Services for rPET supply
- Investment in recycling infrastructure
- Support for Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) policies
- Community collection programs

### 3. Partner

**Goal**: Collaborate to improve recycling systems globally

**Key Partnerships**:
- Ellen MacArthur Foundation
- World Wildlife Fund
- The Recycling Partnership
- Local governments and NGOs

## Specific Targets

### Original Targets (2018)

| Target | Original Goal | Status/Revised |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| 100% Recyclable Packaging | 2025 | 90% achieved; on track |
| 50% Recycled Content | 2030 | Revised to 35-40% by 2035 |
| Collect & Recycle | 100% by 2030 | Revised to 70-75% by 2035 |
| 25% Reusable/Refillable | 2030 | Removed; continue where infrastructure exists |

### Revised Goals (2024 Announcement)

Coca-Cola updated its voluntary environmental goals in December 2024, informed by "learnings gathered through decades of work":

**Packaging**:
- 100% recyclable packaging: Maintained (2025)
- Recycled content: 35-40% by 2035 (was 50% by 2030)
- Collection rates: 70-75% by 2035

**Climate**:
- Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions: Aligned with 1.5°C trajectory by 2035
- (Replaced absolute 25% reduction target from 2015 baseline)

**Water**:
- Replenish 100%+ of water used in finished products: Maintained (achieved since 2015)
- Replenish 100% of water in high-risk locations: Expanded from 175 to 200+ locations by 2035

**Agriculture**:
- Removed specific sustainable agriculture sourcing goal
- Continued commitment to sustainable sourcing practices

## Key Initiatives & Innovations

### rPET (Recycled PET)

**100% rPET Bottles**:
- Rolled out in select markets (Canada, US 13.2oz, Europe)
- "Recycle Me Again" consumer messaging
- Excludes cap and label

**Challenges**:
- rPET supply constraints
- Cost premium over virgin plastic
- Quality consistency
- Collection infrastructure gaps

### Label-Less Bottles

- First launched in Japan (2020)
- Expanded to South Korea, China
- UK trials with Sprite
- Easier recycling (no label separation needed)

### Plant-Based Materials

- PlantBottle: Up to 30% plant-based material
- 100% bioplastic prototypes in development
- IP shared with industry to accelerate adoption

### Refillable/Returnable Systems

- Strong in Latin America, Europe
- Returnable glass bottles
- Refillable PET systems
- ~14% of total volume (as of 2023)

### Lightweighting

- Continuous reduction in plastic per bottle
- New bottle designs across portfolio
- Reduced carbon footprint in production and transport

## Water Stewardship

### Water Replenishment

- **Goal**: Return 100%+ of water used in finished products
- **Status**: Achieved every year since 2015
- **Method**: 248 community water projects in 71 countries

### Water Use Efficiency

- 40% freshwater reduction in material manufacturing (past decade)
- Focus on high-risk watersheds
- Sustainable agriculture programs

## Criticism & Response

### Environmental Criticism

- **Plastic footprint**: Coca-Cola named top plastic polluter in some audits
- **Single-use focus**: Critics argue refillable/reusable should be prioritized
- **Recycling rates**: Gap between collection goals and reality

### Company Response

- Continued investment in recycling infrastructure
- Advocacy for well-designed collection policies
- Transparency in reporting progress and challenges
- Partnership approach to systemic issues

### Industry Context

- Entire beverage industry facing similar challenges
- U.S. Plastics Pact shifted 2025 targets to 2030
- Extended Producer Responsibility policies emerging globally
- Consumer demand for sustainable packaging growing

## Business Case

### Consumer Insights

- 74% of consumers willing to pay more for sustainable packaging (McKinsey)
- Younger consumers prioritize sustainability in purchase decisions
- Brand reputation tied to environmental performance

### Financial Impact

- rPET cost premium offset by brand value
- Efficiency gains from lightweighting
- Risk mitigation from regulatory compliance
- Long-term license to operate

### Competitive Advantage

- Leadership position in beverage sustainability
- Innovation in packaging design
- Strong partnerships across value chain
- Transparent reporting builds trust

## Action Framework

### For Brand Managers

1. **Packaging Design**: Always specify recyclable materials
2. **Consumer Communication**: Tell sustainability stories clearly
3. **Innovation Pipeline**: Prioritize sustainable solutions
4. **Performance Tracking**: Monitor and report progress

### For Bottler Partners

1. **Equipment Investment**: Support cooler recycling programs
2. **Route Optimization**: Reduce distribution emissions
3. **Local Collection**: Participate in community programs
4. **Data Sharing**: Report metrics transparently

### For the System

1. **Collaboration**: Work with industry on standards
2. **Advocacy**: Support effective EPR policies
3. **Innovation**: Share breakthroughs across value chain
4. **Transparency**: Report progress honestly
