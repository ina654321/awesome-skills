# Coca-Cola Bottling Partners

## Overview

The Coca-Cola system operates through a unique partnership model where The Coca-Cola Company owns the brands and manufactures concentrate, while independent bottling partners handle manufacturing and distribution of finished beverages.

## System Evolution

### Refranchising (2010-2020)

Coca-Cola divested most company-owned bottling operations:
- **Before**: Mixed model (company-owned + independent)
- **After**: Predominantly independent bottlers
- **Result**: More focused, larger anchor bottlers

### Benefits of Current Structure

**For Coca-Cola**:
- Capital efficiency
- Local market expertise
- Risk sharing
- Focus on brands and marketing

**For Bottlers**:
- Exclusive territories
- World-class brands
- Marketing support
- Innovation access

**For Consumers**:
- Consistent quality
- Ubiquitous availability
- Local relevance
- Cold refreshment

## Competitive Dynamics

### vs. PepsiCo System

| | Coca-Cola | PepsiCo |
|---|-----------|---------|
| Structure | Franchise (mostly independent) | Mixed (company-owned + independent) |
| Bottler Autonomy | High | Varies by market |
| System Complexity | Multiple anchor bottlers | More consolidated |

### Strategic Challenges

1. **Coordination**: Aligning 300+ bottling partners globally
2. **Speed**: Innovation launch timing across system
3. **Consistency**: Maintaining standards across diverse markets
4. **Investment**: Balancing global and local priorities

---

*The bottling partner system is Coca-Cola's competitive moat—enabling global scale with local execution, capital efficiency with market responsiveness.*
