## Willow Project (Alaska)

### Overview

The Willow project is ConocoPhillips' largest-ever development and a cornerstone of its long-cycle growth strategy.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Location** | National Petroleum Reserve-Alaska (NPR-A) |
| **Type** | Conventional oil development |
| **Investment** | ~$9 billion |
| **Working Interest** | 100% |
| **Peak Production** | 180,000 BOE/day |
| **First Oil** | 2029 |
| **Field Life** | 30+ years |
| **Recovery** | 600+ million barrels |

### Development Plan

**Infrastructure:**
- Central Processing Facility (CPF)
- Drill sites (reduced from 5 to 3 after environmental review)
- Gravel roads and airstrip
- Pipeline to existing Kuparuk infrastructure

**Timeline:**
- **2017:** Lease acquisition
- **2018-2020:** Exploration and appraisal
- **2021-2023:** Environmental review and permitting
- **2024:** Final permits secured
- **2025-2028:** Construction and development drilling
- **2029:** First oil production

### Strategic Rationale

**Why Willow Matters:**
1. **Base Decline Management:** Shale declines 35-40% annually; Willow declines 5-8%
2. **Reserve Life Extension:** Adds 30+ years of production
3. **Diversification:** Balances short-cycle shale with long-life conventional
4. **Infrastructure Leverage:** Utilizes existing Kuparuk/Prudhoe systems
5. **Federal Lease Obligations:** Execute or relinquish valuable leases

**Cost of Supply:**
- Breakeven: ~$45-50/bbl WTI
- Above preferred threshold but justified by strategic value
- Long-life barrels provide through-cycle resilience

### Environmental Considerations

**Mitigation Measures:**
- Reduced pad count (5→3) to minimize surface impact
- Extended-reach drilling from fewer pads
- Wildlife protection protocols
- Reclamation bonds secured

**Legal Journey:**
- Initial approval: Trump administration (2020)
- Legal challenges: Environmental groups
- Revised EIS: Biden administration (2023)
- Final approval: March 2023
- Current status: All permits secured, construction underway

## Port Arthur LNG (Texas)

### Overview

ConocoPhillips' flagship LNG project on the U.S. Gulf Coast, positioned to serve growing global demand.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Location** | Port Arthur, Texas |
| **Type** | LNG liquefaction and export |
| **Investment** | ~$10 billion |
| **Capacity** | 20 MTPA (million tonnes per annum) |
| **Phases** | Two phases, 10 MTPA each |
| **Startup** | Phase 1: 2027; Phase 2: TBD |
| **Technology** | Optimized Cascade® (proprietary) |

### Project Structure

**Phase 1:**
- Liquefaction trains: 2
- Capacity: 10 MTPA
- Storage: Multiple tanks
- Jetty: Deepwater LNG carriers
- Investment: ~$10B (full project)

**Commercial:**
- Initial offtake: 5 MTPA placed (2024)
- Additional offtake: 5 MTPA secured (2025)
- Total commercial: 10 MTPA
- Target markets: Asia, Europe

### Strategic Value

**Natural Gas Monetization:**
- Links domestic gas production to global markets
- Captures international LNG pricing (often oil-linked)
- Provides demand outlet for growing Lower 48 gas production

**Returns Profile:**
- Projected cash flow: $3.5B annually (at $70 WTI equivalent)
- Long-term contracts provide revenue stability
- 20-30 year project life

**Hydrogen Optionality:**
- Infrastructure supports future blue hydrogen production
- Potential CCS integration
- Lower carbon ammonia export capability

### Timeline

| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| FID | Expected 2024-2025 |
| Construction start | Post-FID |
| Phase 1 startup | 2027 |
| Phase 2 FID | TBD (market dependent) |
| Full operation | Late 2020s |
