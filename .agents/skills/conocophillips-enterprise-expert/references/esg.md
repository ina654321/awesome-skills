# ConocoPhillips ESG & Lower Carbon Deep-Dive

**Reference Level 2 — Environmental, Social & Governance**

## Climate Strategy Overview

### Triple Mandate Context

ConocoPhillips addresses climate through its **Triple Mandate:**

1. **Meet Energy Demand:** Deliver competitive barrels in any transition scenario
2. **Generate Competitive Returns:** Superior shareholder returns through cycles
3. **Progress to Net-Zero:** Drive accountability for Scope 1 & 2 emissions

### Plan for the Energy Transition

Published annually, this plan outlines how ConocoPhillips plays a valued role in the energy transition:

| Pillar | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| **Objectives** | Hierarchy: Net-zero ambition → 2030 targets → Near-term implementation |
| **Technology Choices** | Emissions reduction in current operations; evaluate new opportunities |
| **Portfolio Choices** | Carbon pricing in project economics; low cost-of-supply, low GHG intensity |
| **External Engagement** | Stakeholder input informs framework evolution |

## Lower Carbon Initiatives

### Carbon Capture & Storage (CCS)

**Alaska CCS:**
- Evaluating carbon sequestration opportunities
- Potential for blue hydrogen feedstock
- Geological storage assessment ongoing

**Gulf Coast CCS:**
- Monitoring for CCUS infrastructure development
- 45Q tax credit eligibility

### Hydrogen Strategy

**Blue Hydrogen (with JERA):**
- Proposed U.S. Gulf Coast ammonia plant
- Natural gas feedstock + CCS
- Long-term offtake with Uniper
- Status: Evaluation phase, no FID

**Turquoise Hydrogen (Ekona Power):**
- Venture investment in pyrolysis technology
- Methane to hydrogen + solid carbon
- Potential future optionality
- Investment: Part of $79M funding round

**Investment Level:**
- Annual low-carbon spend: ~$50M
- Compare to: $9B Willow, $22.5B Marathon
- Philosophy: Optionality, not strategic pivot

### LNG as Transition Fuel

**Port Arthur LNG:**
- $10B investment (2027 startup)
- Lower carbon intensity than coal
- Asian/European demand for energy transition
- Potential hydrogen linkage long-term

**Qatar LNG Expansion:**
- North Field East/South equity stakes
- Large-scale, low-cost production
- Replaces higher-carbon alternatives

## Operational Emissions Programs

### Methane Management

**Zero Routine Flaring by 2025:**
- Five years ahead of World Bank 2030 target
- Equipment upgrades: flare gas recovery systems
- Operational protocols: minimize flaring events
- Excludes heritage Marathon assets (separate plan)

**OGMP 2.0 Membership:**
- Gold Standard Reporting (2024 award)
- Third-party verification
- Measurement-based emissions accounting
- Industry-leading transparency

**Pneumatic Device Replacement:**
- Converting high-bleed pneumatics to low/no-bleed
- 2024: Significant abatement project completions
- Target: Full conversion by 2030

### Digital Emissions Monitoring

**Technologies Deployed:**
- **Optical Gas Imaging (OGI):** Leak detection surveys
- **Continuous Monitoring:** Fixed sensors at facilities
- **Satellite/Aerial:** Methane detection verification
- **Drones:** OGI cameras for aerial surveys

**Data Improvements (2024):**
- Corrected pneumatic equipment counts
- Expanded flare downtime monitoring (Bakken, Permian)
- Enhanced emission factor accuracy

## Risk Management

### Climate-Related Risks

**Transition Risks:**
- Policy/regulatory changes
- Carbon pricing implementation
- Technology disruption
- Market shifts (demand destruction)

**Physical Risks:**
- Extreme weather events
- Sea level rise (coastal assets)
- Water availability
- Permafrost thaw (Arctic operations)

**Scenario Analysis:**
- Multiple energy transition scenarios modeled
- Portfolio resilience testing
- Strategic flexibility maintained

### Resilience Measures

- Low cost-of-supply portfolio (<$32/bbl average)
- Diversification by geography, commodity, asset type
- Financial flexibility (investment-grade balance sheet)
- Emissions reduction trajectory aligned with targets
