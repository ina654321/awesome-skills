# Western Australia Iron Ore (WAIO) Operations

## Overview

WAIO is BHP's flagship iron ore business and the **world's lowest-cost major iron ore producer**. It represents the backbone of BHP's cash generation and competitive position.

## Cost Position

### Unit Costs
- **Status**: World's lowest-cost major iron ore producer
- **Cash Costs**: Significantly below industry average
- **Advantage**: Integrated system, automation, scale economies

### Cost Drivers
1. **Automation**: 15-20% cost reduction from autonomous haulage
2. **Supply Chain Efficiency**: Port debottlenecking, rail optimization
3. **Scale**: Large production base spreads fixed costs
4. **Mine Life**: Long-life assets reduce sustaining capital intensity

### Integrated Remote Operations Centre (IROC)
- **Location**: Perth (St Georges Terrace)
- **Operational**: Since 2012 (10+ years)
- **Function**: 24/7/365 operation of logistics and processes
- **Coverage**: Mines, rail, port facilities
- **Distance**: 1,100+ km from operations
- **Workforce**: Nearly 50% female, 12% Indigenous

### Automation Technologies
- **Autonomous Haul Trucks**: Caterpillar and Komatsu fleets
- **Autonomous Drilling**: Epiroc Pit Viper rigs (deployment 2025-2026)
- **Autonomous Shiploaders**: Recently initiated at Port Hedland
- **Remote Operations**: Drills, dozers, water carts

## Growth Strategy

### Target: 305 Mtpa
- Current: 263 Mt (FY2025)
- Target: 305 Mtpa

### Pathways

**1. Supply Chain Optimization** (Near-term)
- Port debottlenecking at Port Hedland
- Rail cycle time improvement
- Train automation and scheduling optimization

**2. Mine Expansions** (Medium-term)
- Jimblebar optimization (EPA assessment)
- Mining Area C capacity expansion
- Yandi Lower Channel Deposit development

**3. Infrastructure Investment** (Long-term)
- Additional car dumpers
- Incremental shiploaders
- Rail capacity expansion
- Power infrastructure scaling

### Infrastructure Partnership
- **Partner**: Global Infrastructure Partners (GIP, BlackRock)
- **Structure**: 51% BHP / 49% GIP trust entity
- **Investment**: US$2 billion from GIP
- **Asset**: WAIO inland power network
- **Term**: 25-year usage-based tariff
- **Benefit**: Capital flexibility, maintains operational control
- **Closing**: End of FY2026 (subject to FIRB approval)
