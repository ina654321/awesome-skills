# BHP Copper Strategy

## Overview

BHP holds the **world's largest copper mineral resources** and is the largest global copper producer. Copper is central to our strategy as a "future-facing commodity" essential for:
- Renewable energy infrastructure
- Electric vehicles and batteries
- Data centers and digitalization
- Grid electrification and transmission

## Escondida: The Crown Jewel

### Scale
- Largest copper mine globally by production
- Contributes ~39% of BHP Group Underlying EBITDA
- Revenue: US$10.0B (FY2025)
- Underlying EBITDA: US$5.8B

### Expansion Investment
- **Total Investment**: US$10-14 billion
- **Timeline**: Through 2034
- **Objective**: Maintain production at 1.0+ Mtpa despite natural grade decline

### Key Projects

**1. Autonomous Haulage**
- 50 autonomous trucks deploying over 3 years
- Deployment began in Escondida Norte pit (H2 FY2024)
- Improves safety and productivity

**2. Shovel Upgrades**
- Largest capacity cable-powered shovel in market (first in Latin America)
- Unlocks truck loading efficiency
- Lower unit costs through incremental system performance

**3. Concentrator Optimization**
- Feed grade improved to 0.88% (from 0.82% in FY2023)
- Higher throughput prioritization
- Cathode production adjustment to optimize concentrate output

**4. Water Security**
- Desalination capacity expansion to 3,000 L/s
- Reduced reliance on local aquifers
- Kelar gas-fired power plant supporting growth with lower emissions

## Market Context

### Demand Drivers
- **Electrification**: EVs require 3-4x more copper than ICE vehicles
- **Renewables**: Wind and solar 4-6x more copper-intensive than fossil generation
- **Grid Investment**: Aging infrastructure + load growth
- **Data Centers**: AI driving massive copper demand

### Supply Challenges
- **Grade Decline**: Global average ore grades falling
- **Resource Nationalism**: Increasing government take in producing countries
- **Long Lead Times**: 15+ years from discovery to production
- **Water/Energy Constraints**: Chilean operations face water scarcity

### Price Outlook
- Structural deficit expected through 2030s
- BHP well-positioned with low-cost, long-life assets
