## Fatality Elimination Program

### Philosophy
Eliminating fatalities requires more than compliance—it demands a fundamental shift in how we identify and control risks with fatal potential.

### Three Pillars

#### 1. Engineering Controls
- **Objective**: Design out hazards where possible
- **Approach**: Hard barriers, interlocks, automated systems
- **Investment**: Significant capital deployed across all assets

**Examples:**
- Proximity detection on mobile equipment
- Physical barriers for fall protection
- Automated isolation systems
- Vehicle collision avoidance

#### 2. Field Leadership
- **Objective**: Leaders present where work happens
- **Approach**: Time in field engaging teams, verifying controls
- **Metric**: Leader presence and quality of interactions

**Practices:**
- Daily field leadership walks
- Work observation and feedback
- Pre-task risk assessments
- Post-task reviews and learning

#### 3. BHP Operating System
- **Objective**: Standardized, disciplined operations execution
- **Approach**: Integrated framework for safe work
- **Elements**: Planning, execution, verification, improvement

**Components:**
- Standard operating procedures
- Critical control verification
- Incident investigation
- Continuous improvement cycles

## Critical Risk Management

### 12 Fatal Risks
BHP has identified and standardized controls for the highest-risk activities:

| Risk Category | Examples | Key Controls |
|---------------|----------|--------------|
| Mobile Equipment | Haul trucks, light vehicles | Exclusion zones, proximity detection, traffic management |
| Falling Objects | Loads, tools, rock falls | Securing, barriers, exclusion zones |
| Ground Stability | Pit walls, underground | Geotechnical monitoring, exclusion zones |
| Energy Isolation | Electrical, mechanical | Lock-out/tag-out, verification |
| Working at Height | Elevated work platforms | Fall protection, barriers, training |
| Confined Spaces | Tanks, vessels | Permits, atmospheric testing, rescue |
| Lifting Operations | Cranes, hoists | Competency, inspection, planning |
| Explosives | Blasting | Blast exclusion, storage security |
| Fire | Fuel, chemicals | Detection, suppression, emergency response |
| Water Management | Drowning, flooding | Exclusion zones, barriers, rescue |
| Rail | Train operations | Exclusion zones, signaling, competency |
| Manual Tasks | Musculoskeletal | Mechanical aids, training, job design |

## Investigation Framework

### ICAM (Incident Cause Analysis Method)
BHP uses ICAM for all serious incidents and high-potential events.

#### Analysis Levels
1. **Organizational Factors**
   - Resource allocation
   - Production pressure
   - Contractor management
   - Safety culture

2. **Task Factors**
   - Work design
   - Procedures adequacy
   - Training effectiveness
   - Supervision quality

3. **Individual Factors**
   - Competency and skills
   - Fatigue and fitness
   - Situational awareness
   - Decision-making

4. **Environmental Factors**
   - Weather conditions
   - Lighting and visibility
   - Noise and distractions
   - Equipment condition

### Investigation Triggers
- Fatalities (immediate)
- High-potential injuries
- Serious injuries (lost-time)
- Environmental incidents
- Regulatory notifiable events

## Safety Metrics

### Leading Indicators
| Metric | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| HPIF (High Potential Injury Frequency) | Predicts serious incident potential |
| Critical Control Verifications | Ensures controls are working |
| Field Leadership Interactions | Measures leader presence |
| Safety Conversations | Engagement and awareness |

### Lagging Indicators
| Metric | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Fatalities | Ultimate safety outcome |
| TRIF (Total Recordable Injury Frequency) | Overall injury rate |
| Lost Time Injuries | Severity measure |
| Medical Treatment Injuries | Treatment required |

### HPIF Definition
High Potential Injury Frequency measures incidents with the potential to cause serious harm, even if no actual injury occurred. It captures "near misses" with fatal potential.

## BHP Operating System

### Framework
The BHP Operating System provides the structure for how work is executed safely and efficiently.

#### Core Elements
1. **Plan**: Risk assessment, work planning, resource allocation
2. **Execute**: Follow procedures, verify controls, communicate
3. **Verify**: Check that controls are working, observe behavior
4. **Improve**: Learn from incidents, share knowledge, adapt

#### Maturity Model
- **Level 1**: Compliance-based
- **Level 2**: Systematic approaches
- **Level 3**: Integrated and optimized
- **Level 4**: World-class, continuously improving
