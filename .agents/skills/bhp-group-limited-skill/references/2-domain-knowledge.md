### 2.2 Core Assets & Operations

#### Western Australia Iron Ore (WAIO)
- **Ownership**: 85% BHP / 15% Mitsui & ITOCHU
- **FY2025 Production**: 263 Mt (record) | FY2026 Guidance: 258-269 Mt
- **Cost Position**: World's lowest-cost major iron ore producer
- **Infrastructure**: 5 mines, 4 processing hubs, 1,000+ km rail, 2 port facilities (Port Hedland)
- **Key Mines**: Jimblebar, Newman, Mining Area C, South Flank, Yandi
- **Growth Target**: 305 Mtpa through sustained investment

#### Copper Operations

**Escondida (Chile)** - World's Largest Copper Mine
- **Ownership**: 57.5% BHP
- **FY2025 Production**: 1,125+ kt (up 16% YoY)
- **Revenue**: US$10.0 billion
- **Underlying EBITDA**: US$5.8 billion (39% of Group EBITDA)
- **Expansion**: US$10-14B investment plan to maintain production through 2034

**Pampa Norte (Chile)**
- **Assets**: Spence, Cerro Colorado
- **FY2024 Production**: 265.6 kt
- **Revenue**: US$2.4 billion
- **Spence Growth Option (SGO)**: Record production achieved

**Copper South Australia**
- **Assets**: Olympic Dam, Prominent Hill, Carrapateena
- **Acquired**: OZ Minerals (US$6.27B, 2023)
- **Target**: Pathway to ~650 ktpa aspiration

#### Jansen Potash (Canada)
- **Location**: Saskatchewan, Canada
- **Stage 1**: US$5.7B capex, production late 2026, 4.4 Mtpa capacity
- **Stage 2**: US$4.9B approved, targeting 8.5 Mtpa by 2030
- **Strategic Significance**: Entry into potash for food security, sustainable agriculture

#### Coal Operations
- **Metallurgical Coal**: BMA (50% BHP / 50% Mitsubishi) - Blackwater, Daunia divested 2024
- **Energy Coal**: NSW Energy Coal (Mount Arthur) - in closure pathway
- **Strategy**: Focus on premium hard coking coal for steel decarbonization

#### Nickel (Western Australia)
- **Status**: Operations temporarily suspended (2024)
- **Context**: Weak nickel prices, oversupply from Indonesian production
- **Future**: Preserved for energy transition demand recovery

### 2.3 Safety & Operational Excellence

#### Fatality Elimination Program
- **FY2025**: Zero fatalities
- **High Potential Injury Frequency (HPIF)**: Down 18% YoY, 63% reduction over 5 years
- **Approach**: Engineering controls, field leadership, BHP Operating System

#### BHP Operating System
- Framework for safe, efficient operations execution
- Integrated approach to risk management and control verification
- Continuous improvement methodology

#### Automation & Technology
- **Autonomous Haulage**: Jimblebar fully autonomous; South Flank, MAC, Newman in progress
- **Autonomous Drilling**: Pit Viper rigs at WAIO and Escondida
- **Integrated Remote Operations Centre (IROC)**: Perth-based, 10+ years operation
- **Target**: Near-autonomous load and haul by mid-2020s

### 2.4 Sustainability & ESG

#### Climate & Energy
- **Net Zero Target**: 2050 for operations
- **Medium-term**: 30% emissions reduction by 2030
- **Renewable Energy**: Kelar gas plant (Chile), renewable PPAs across operations
- **Water**: Escondida desalination capacity increasing to 3,000 L/s

#### Tailings Management
- Governance framework aligned with Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management (GISTM)
- Priority on safe, stable tailings storage facilities
- Transparency in tailings facility disclosures

#### Social Investment
- **Indigenous Procurement**: Project Rise (WAIO) - A$300M annual target
- **Community Investment**: US$255M+ since FY2011
- **Traditional Owner Partnerships**: 80+ Indigenous businesses engaged

### 2.5 Strategic Priorities

1. **Grow Copper**: 25%+ production increase by 2030
2. **Develop Potash**: Become leading global producer from Jansen
3. **Optimize Iron Ore**: Maintain lowest-cost position, grow to 305 Mtpa
4. **Capital Discipline**: 50%+ payout ratio, strong balance sheet
5. **Portfolio Reshaping**: Exit petroleum (Woodside merger 2022), focus on future-facing commodities
