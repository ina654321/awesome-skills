## BWRX-300 Small Modular Reactor

### Technology Overview

The BWRX-300 is a 300 MWe water-cooled, natural circulation small modular reactor with passive safety systems. It represents the 10th generation of Boiling Water Reactor (BWR) technology and the simplest BWR design since GE began developing nuclear reactors in 1955.

**Key Specifications:**

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Reactor Type** | Boiling Water Reactor (BWR) |
| **Net Electrical Output** | 300 MWe |
| **Thermal Power** | 870 MWt |
| **Cooling** | Natural circulation (no pumps) |
| **Safety Systems** | Passive, gravity-driven |
| **Fuel** | GNF2 (licensed, proven design) |
| **Fuel Enrichment** | Up to 4.95% U-235 |
| **Fuel Cycle** | 24-month typical |
| **Design Life** | 60 years (extendable) |
| **Construction Timeline** | 24-36 months |
| **Footprint** | < 1 acre (reactor building) |

### Design Philosophy

**"Designed to Cost" Approach:**
- Capital cost per MW competitive with natural gas combined cycle
- 10% size and complexity of large nuclear projects
- Standardized, repeatable design
- Modular construction techniques

**Key Design Features:**

1. **Natural Circulation Cooling**
   - No reactor coolant pumps
   - Gravity-driven heat removal
   - Reduced maintenance and complexity

2. **Passive Safety Systems**
   - Gravity-driven emergency cooling
   - Passive containment cooling
   - No operator action required for 7 days
   - No AC power required for safety functions

3. **Simplified Design**
   - ~90% fewer safety-related valves than typical BWR
   - ~80% less safety-related piping
   - Elimination of recirculation pumps and associated systems

4. **Underground Configuration**
   - Reactor vessel below grade
   - Enhanced seismic protection
   - Reduced security perimeter

5. **Digital Instrumentation & Control**
   - Fully digital I&C system
   - Advanced cybersecurity
   - Modern human-machine interface

### Safety Features

**Defense in Depth (IAEA Standards):**

| Defense Line | Implementation |
|--------------|----------------|
| 1. Prevention | High-quality design, construction, operation |
| 2. Control | Control rods, reactor protection system |
| 3. Protection | Passive safety systems, containment |
| 4. Mitigation | Severe accident management guidelines |
| 5. Emergency Response | Offsite emergency planning |

**Passive Safety Systems:**
- Gravity-driven cooling system (GDCS)
- Passive containment cooling system (PCCS)
- Isolation condenser system (ICS)
- Standby liquid control system (SLC)

### Deployment Status

#### Under Construction

**Canada - Ontario Power Generation (OPG):**
- **Location:** Darlington Nuclear Generating Station
- **Status:** Construction approved, underway
- **First Unit:** Expected completion by 2029-2030
- **Total Planned:** 4 units (1,200 MW total)
- **Significance:** First SMR in Western world
- **Supply Chain:** BWXT Canada, AtkinsRéalis, Aecon

#### Licensing & Development

**United States - Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA):**
- **Location:** Clinch River Site, Oak Ridge, Tennessee
- **Status:** Construction Permit Application submitted to NRC
- **Significance:** First US BWRX-300
- **Timeline:** Licensing 2024-2028, construction TBD

**United Kingdom:**
- **Status:** Generic Design Assessment (GDA) in progress
- **Support:** £33.6M Future Nuclear Enabling Fund grant
- **Supply Chain:** MOUs with UK companies (Sheffield Forgemasters, etc.)

**Poland - Orlen Synthos Green Energy (OSGE):**
- **Status:** Site selected (Włocławek)
- **Planned:** 24 units across 6 sites (7,200 MW total)
- **First Units:** Target 2032-2035

**Sweden - Vattenfall:**
- **Status:** Down-selected for Ringhals site evaluation
- **Planned:** New reactors adjacent to existing plant
- **Timeline:** Decision pending

**Finland - Fortum:**
- **Status:** Early works agreement signed
- **Scope:** Pre-licensing and site adaptation
- **Potential Deployment:** Second half of 2030s

**Southeast Asia:**
- **Status:** MOU with Hitachi to explore deployment
- **Countries:** Multiple ASEAN markets
- **Focus:** Supply chain development
