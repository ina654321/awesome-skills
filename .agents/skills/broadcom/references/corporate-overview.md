# Broadcom Corporate Overview

## Company History

### Origins and Evolution

**Avago Technologies (Pre-2016)**
- Founded 1961 as HP's electronics division
- Pioneered LED displays and optical components
- Spun off as part of Agilent Technologies in 2000
- 2005: Acquired by KKR and Silver Lake (private equity)
- 2009: IPO under Hock Tan's leadership
- Began aggressive acquisition strategy

**The Avago-Broadcom Merger (2016)**
- May 2015: Announced $37B acquisition of Broadcom Corp
- Largest semiconductor M&A deal at the time
- Combined company adopted Broadcom name
- Created 3rd largest U.S. semiconductor company
- Transaction: $17B cash + $20B stock
- Expected synergies: $750M annually within 18 months

**Strategic Transformation Under Hock Tan**

| Era | Focus | Key Moves |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| 2009-2015 | Build foundation | LSI acquisition, cost discipline |
| 2016-2018 | Scale semiconductor | Broadcom merger, Brocade |
| 2018-2022 | Diversify to software | CA Technologies, Symantec |
| 2023-Present | AI infrastructure | VMware, custom silicon expansion |

### Leadership

**Hock Tan - President & CEO**
- Joined Avago as CEO in 2006 (via acquisition of his company)
- Background: VC and operational roles in technology
- Reputation: "Ferocious dealmaker," disciplined capital allocator
- Philosophy: Acquire market leaders, extract synergies, maximize margins
- Notable: Led >$100B in acquisitions over 15 years

**Kirsten Spears - CFO**
- Joined Avago in 2000
- Background: Finance leadership in semiconductor industry
- Role: Capital allocation strategy, shareholder returns

### Corporate Profile

**Headquarters:** San Jose, California  
**Employees:** ~20,000 (highly efficient organization)  
**Ticker:** AVGO (NASDAQ)

### Strategic Philosophy

**Hock Tan's Operating Principles:**

1. **"We acquire to lead"** - Target #1 or #2 positions with 50%+ share
2. **"Margin expansion drives value"** - 70%+ operating margin target
3. **"Disciplined capital allocation"** - Every dollar deployed must generate superior returns
4. **"Pricing power from dominance"** - Market leadership enables value capture
5. **"Fast integration execution"** - Streamline within 24 months post-close

**Quote from Hock Tan (Q4 FY2024 Earnings):**
> "We are well on the path to delivering incremental adjusted EBITDA at a level that significantly exceeds the $8.5 billion we communicated when we announced the [VMware] deal."
