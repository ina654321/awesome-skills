| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|

### Skyrizi (risankizumab-rzaa)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Drug Class** | IL-23 inhibitor (monoclonal antibody) |
| **Approved (U.S.)** | April 2019 (psoriasis) |
| **2024 Revenue** | $11.72 billion (+50.9% YoY) |
| **Administration** | Subcutaneous injection |
| **Dosing** | Every 12 weeks (after initial loading) |

**Approved Indications (as of 2024):**
1. Moderate to severe plaque psoriasis
2. Active psoriatic arthritis
3. Moderate to severe Crohn's disease
4. Moderate to severe ulcerative colitis (June 2024)
5. Active ankylosing spondylitis

**Clinical Differentiation:**
- **Highest IL-23 efficacy:** ~90% PASI 90 response in psoriasis
- **Rapid onset:** Visible improvement within weeks
- **Convenient dosing:** Every 12 weeks after initiation
- **Dual IBD coverage:** Only IL-23 with both CD and UC approvals

**Market Position:**
- Market leader in IL-23 class (vs. Tremfya, Stelara)
- Now exceeding Humira quarterly sales
- Projected to reach $20B+ by 2027

---

### Rinvoq (upadacitinib)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Drug Class** | JAK1 selective inhibitor (small molecule) |
| **Approved (U.S.)** | August 2019 (rheumatoid arthritis) |
| **2024 Revenue** | $5.97 billion (+50.4% YoY) |
| **Administration** | Oral, once daily |
| **Patent Extension** | 2037 (via litigation settlement) |

**Approved Indications (as of 2024):**
1. Rheumatoid arthritis
2. Psoriatic arthritis
3. Ankylosing spondylitis
4. Atopic dermatitis (eczema)
5. Crohn's disease
6. Ulcerative colitis

**Clinical Profile:**
- Oral convenience vs. injectable biologics
- Selective JAK1 inhibition reduces safety concerns
- Efficacy comparable to biologics in head-to-head studies
- Strong performance in dermatology (atopic dermatitis)

**Growth Drivers:**
- Label expansion complete across major immunology indications
- Patent protection extended to 2037
- International growth (less JAK safety stigma)
