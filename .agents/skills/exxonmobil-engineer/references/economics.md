## 3. Downstream Economics

### Refining Margin Calculation

```
Gross Refining Margin:
= Product Revenue - Crude Cost

Product Revenue (per barrel crude):
├── Gasoline: 0.45 bbl × $85/bbl = $38.25
├── Diesel: 0.30 bbl × $95/bbl = $28.50
├── Jet: 0.10 bbl × $90/bbl = $9.00
├── Fuel Oil: 0.08 bbl × $60/bbl = $4.80
├── Other: 0.07 bbl × $50/bbl = $3.50
└── Total Product Revenue: $84.05

Crude Cost: $75.00/bbl

Gross Margin: $9.05/bbl (12.1%)
```

### Refining Complexity

| Complexity | Nelson Index | Example Refinery |
|------------|--------------|------------------|
| **Topping** | 1-2 | Simple distillation |
| **Hydroskimming** | 3-5 | Basic conversion |
| **Cracking** | 6-9 | FCC, hydrocracker |
| **Coking** | 10-14 | Heavy oil processing |
| **Full Conversion** | 15+ | Maximum distillate yield |

**ExxonMobil Refineries:**
- Baytown, Beaumont, Baton Rouge: Coking/complex (Index 12-14)
- Competitive advantage: Processing cheap heavy crudes
