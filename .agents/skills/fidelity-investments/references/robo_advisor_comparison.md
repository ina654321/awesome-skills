## Detailed Provider Analysis

### Fidelity Go

**Strengths:**
- Lowest all-in costs (ZERO underlying fund fees)
- No fee under $25,000
- Strong recent performance (large-cap tilt)
- Unlimited human coaching at $25K+
- Seamless integration with other Fidelity accounts
- Tax-loss harvesting available

**Weaknesses:**
- No tax-loss harvesting below $50,000
- Limited to Fidelity Flex funds (proprietary)
- No direct indexing
- Heavy U.S. large-cap concentration (may underperform in different market regime)
- No ESG/SRI options

**Best For:**
- Fidelity customers wanting integration
- Cost-conscious investors
- Beginning investors starting small
- Those who want human access without high fees

### Betterment

**Strengths:**
- Excellent goal-setting tools
- Tax-loss harvesting on all taxable accounts
- Financial planning packages available
- Crypto portfolios available
n- ESG options

**Weaknesses:**
- Higher underlying fund expenses
- No human advisor at basic tier
- No direct indexing (tax-loss harvesting ETF-based)

**Best For:**
- Goal-oriented investors
- Taxable accounts (excellent TLH)
- Those wanting ESG options

### Wealthfront

**Strengths:**
- Excellent tax-loss harvesting (daily monitoring)
- Direct indexing for large accounts ($100K+)
- Financial planning tools
- High-yield cash account
- Risk parity options

**Weaknesses:**
- $500 minimum
- No human advisors
- Limited account types
- Complex portfolios may be hard to understand

**Best For:**
- Taxable investors with $100K+ (direct indexing)
- Tech-savvy DIY investors
- Those wanting sophisticated strategies

### Vanguard Personal Advisor

**Strengths:**
- Vanguard's legendary low-cost funds
- Human advisor access (hybrid model)
- Comprehensive financial planning
- Strong retirement tools
- Trustworthy brand

**Weaknesses:**
- $50,000 minimum for hybrid service
- 0.30% fee for hybrid (higher than pure robo)
- Limited customization
- Slower to adopt new features

**Best For:**
- Vanguard fund loyalists
- Those wanting human touch
- Long-term retirement investors

### Schwab Intelligent Portfolios

**Strengths:**
- No advisory fee
- Wide ETF selection
- Premium tier with human advisors
- Strong brand

**Weaknesses:**
- Mandatory cash allocation (6-30%) creates drag
- $5,000 minimum
- Tax-loss harvesting only on $50K+
- Cash drag can negate "free" management

**Best For:**
- Schwab customers
- Those who want zero advisory fee (and accept cash drag)

## Fidelity Go Portfolio Construction

### Sample Allocations

**Conservative (Risk Score 1-3):**
- 30% Stocks (Fidelity Flex U.S. & International)
- 60% Bonds (Fidelity Flex Municipal for taxable, Total Bond for retirement)
- 10% Short-term/Money Market

**Moderate (Risk Score 4-7):**
- 60% Stocks (mostly U.S. large-cap, some international)
- 35% Bonds
- 5% Short-term

**Aggressive (Risk Score 8-10):**
- 85% Stocks (heavy U.S. large-cap, small international allocation)
- 12% Bonds
- 3% Short-term

### Equity Breakdown (Typical)

| Category | Allocation | Notes |
|----------|------------|-------|
| U.S. Large-Cap | ~76% of equity | S&P 500 type exposure |
| U.S. Mid/Small | ~14% of equity | Extended market |
| International Developed | ~8% of equity | EAFE equivalent |
| Emerging Markets | ~2% of equity | Minimal exposure |

**Note:** Heavy U.S. large-cap tilt has boosted recent performance but may underperform if small-cap or international rallies.
