# Tax Efficiency Guide for Fidelity Accounts

## Account Type Hierarchy

### The Priority Order for Investments

**Step 1: 401(k) Match (Priority #1)**
- Always capture full employer match
- Immediate return regardless of account type

**Step 2: HSA (if eligible)**
- Triple tax advantage
- Use as "stealth IRA"

**Step 3: Roth IRA (if eligible)**
- Tax-free growth and withdrawals
- No RMDs

**Step 4: Max 401(k)**
- Pre-tax or Roth depending on tax bracket

**Step 5: Taxable Brokerage**
- Flexibility and tax-loss harvesting

## Asset Location Strategy

### Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401k, IRA, HSA)

**Best Holdings:**
- Tax-inefficient bonds (corporate, taxable)
- REITs (ordinary income distributions)
- Active funds (high turnover = capital gains)
- Target-date funds (bond allocation becomes tax-inefficient)
- TIPS (inflation adjustments taxed as income)

**Why:** Shield the least tax-efficient investments from annual taxation

### Taxable Brokerage Accounts

**Best Holdings:**
- Stock index funds (low turnover)
- Municipal bonds (tax-free income)
- ETFs (generally more tax-efficient than mutual funds)
- Individual stocks held long-term
- ZERO funds (0% expense ratio maximizes compounding)

**Why:** These investments generate minimal current-year tax liability

**Tax Equivalent Yield Formula:**
```
Tax-Equivalent Yield = Muni Yield ÷ (1 - Federal Tax Rate - State Tax Rate)
```

**Example:**
- Muni yield: 3.5%
- Federal bracket: 24%
- State bracket: 9%
- Tax-equivalent yield: 3.5% ÷ (1 - 0.24 - 0.09) = 5.22%

## Tax-Loss Harvesting

### What Is It?

Selling investments at a loss to offset capital gains and up to $3,000 of ordinary income annually.

### Fidelity Go Implementation

**Eligibility:**
- Taxable accounts only
- $50,000+ account balance
- Automatic monitoring and execution

**How It Works:**
1. Algorithm monitors for positions with unrealized losses
2. Sells losing positions
3. Buys similar (but not "substantially identical") replacement
4. Losses offset gains and income

### Manual Tax-Loss Harvesting

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Identify positions with unrealized losses
2. Sell to realize loss
3. Wait 30 days OR buy similar but not identical fund
4. Reinvest proceeds

**Wash Sale Rule:**
- Cannot buy "substantially identical" security 30 days before or after sale
- Violation disallows the loss

## Roth Conversion Strategies

### When to Convert

**Ideal Circumstances:**
- Low-income year (job change, sabbatical, early retirement)
- Market downturn (convert depreciated assets)
- Early in career (lower tax bracket)
- Gap years before RMDs/Social Security

### Fidelity Roth Conversion Process

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Log into Fidelity.com
2. Go to "Accounts & Trade" → "Transfer"
3. Select "Convert to Roth IRA"
4. Choose source (Traditional IRA or 401k if eligible)
5. Choose destination (existing or new Roth IRA)
6. Enter amount (can do partial conversions)
7. Submit and pay taxes from external funds

### Roth Conversion Ladder

**Strategy for Early Retirees:**
1. Convert Traditional IRA to Roth IRA
2. Pay taxes from taxable account
3. Wait 5 years
4. Withdraw converted amount penalty-free
5. Bridge gap to age 59.5

**Annual Execution:**
- Convert amount needed for expenses 5 years out
- Stay within target tax bracket
- Repeat each year of early retirement

## Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)

### What Are QCDs?

Direct transfers from IRA to qualified charity:
- Count toward RMD
- Not taxable income
- Up to $105,000/year (2025 limit)

### Fidelity QCD Process

1. Call Fidelity or use online form
2. Request QCD distribution
3. Specify charity (must be 501(c)(3))
4. Fidelity sends check directly to charity
5. Report on tax return (Form 1040)

**Timing:**
- Must be age 70.5+
- Must complete by December 31 to count for that year
- Cannot also claim charitable deduction for same donation

### When QCDs Make Sense

- RMDs push you into higher tax bracket
- Don't need full RMD for living expenses
- Charitably inclined
- Want to reduce taxable estate

## Tax-Efficient Withdrawal Sequences

### Retirement Withdrawal Strategy

**Order of Withdrawals:**
1. **Taxable accounts first**
   - Let tax-advantaged accounts grow
   - Tax-loss harvesting opportunities
   - Capital gains rates often lower than income rates

2. **Traditional 401(k)/IRA**
   - RMDs start at 73
   - Fill up lower tax brackets
   - Consider Roth conversions in low-income years

3. **Roth IRA last**
   - No RMDs
   - Tax-free growth
   - Leave for beneficiaries or late medical expenses

**Modified Strategy If:**
- Large traditional IRA: Consider Roth conversions in 60s
- High tax bracket in retirement: Accelerate Roth withdrawals
- Estate planning focus: Spend taxable, leave Roth to heirs

## HSA as Tax Shelter

### The Ultimate Tax-Advantaged Account

**Triple Tax Advantage:**
1. Tax-deductible contributions
2. Tax-free growth
3. Tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses

### Fidelity HSA Strategy

**For Healthy Individuals:**
1. Max contribution annually ($4,300 individual / $8,550 family, 2025)
2. Invest in ZERO funds (FZROX) for growth
3. Pay medical expenses from taxable account
4. Save receipts indefinitely
5. Reimburse yourself anytime (no deadline)
6. After 65: Use for any purpose (pay income tax, no penalty)

**Receipt Saving Strategy:**
- Create digital folder for medical receipts
- Can reimburse years later
- Let HSA grow tax-free in meantime
- $50 medical expense today = potentially $200+ after 20 years of growth

## Tax Bracket Management

### Marginal vs. Effective Tax Rates

**Marginal Rate:** Tax on next dollar earned
**Effective Rate:** Total tax ÷ Total income

**Example (2025 brackets, single):**
- $100,000 income
- Marginal rate: 24%
- Effective rate: ~15%

### Filling the Bracket

**Strategy:**
- Recognize income up to bracket limit
- Example: In 22% bracket, room to next bracket $20,000
- Consider Roth conversions up to that amount
- Harvest capital gains at 0% rate (if in 12% or lower bracket)

### Capital Gains Harvesting

**0% Capital Gains Rate:**
- Single: Up to $48,350 taxable income (2025)
- Married: Up to $96,700 taxable income (2025)

**Strategy:**
- If in 12% or lower bracket, realize long-term gains at 0%
- Reset basis higher for future sales
- No wash sale rules for gains (only losses)

## Common Tax Mistakes

1. **Holding Tax-Inefficient Funds in Taxable Accounts**
   - REITs, active funds, bonds
   - Solution: Use 401(k) for these

2. **Not Harvesting Losses**
   - Missing opportunity to offset gains
   - Solution: Annual review in December

3. **Triggering Wash Sales**
   - Buying same fund within 30 days
   - Solution: Use different fund family for swap

4. **Ignoring Asset Location**
   - Same allocation across all accounts
   - Solution: Optimize by account type

5. **Not Using QCDs**
   - Taking RMD then donating cash
   - Solution: Direct IRA to charity transfer

6. **Roth Conversions Without Cash for Taxes**
   - Withholding from conversion amount
   - Solution: Pay taxes from outside funds
