|--------------|----------|---------|------------------------|
| 401(k) Elective Deferral | $23,500 | $23,500 + $7,500 = $31,000 | $23,500 + $11,250 = $34,750 |

### The 401(k) Priority Framework

**Step 1: Capture the Match (Priority #1)**
- Always contribute enough to get full employer match
- Common formula: 50% match up to 6% of salary = 3% free money
- Immediate 50% return on investment

**Step 2: Max Out Roth IRA (if eligible)**
- Income limits: $150K single / $236K married (phase-out begins)
- Contribution: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+)
- Tax-free growth and withdrawals

**Step 3: Return to 401(k) and Maximize**
- Pre-tax or Roth 401(k) depending on current vs. expected tax bracket
- Total max: $23,500 ($31,000 if 50+)

**Step 4: Taxable Brokerage**
- For additional savings beyond retirement accounts
- Tax-loss harvesting opportunities

## Rollover Strategies

### When to Rollover

**Rollover TO Fidelity IRA:**
- Leaving employer and want more investment options
- Consolidating multiple old 401(k)s
- Lower fees than previous employer's plan
- Want access to Fidelity's ZERO funds

**Keep in 401(k):**
- Still employed (generally required)
- Need 401(k) loan access (not available in IRA)
- Age 55-59.5 and might need penalty-free access (Rule of 55)
- Have company stock with NUA potential

### Rollover Types

| Type | Tax Impact | Best For |
|------|------------|----------|
| Direct Rollover (Traditional to Traditional) | None | Most rollovers |
| Direct Rollover (Roth to Roth) | None | Roth 401k to Roth IRA |
| Indirect Rollover | 20% withholding, 60-day deadline | Temporary access (avoid if possible) |
| Roth Conversion | Pay tax on full amount | Low-income year, early retirement |

## Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

### RMD Rules Post-SECURE 2.0

| Birth Year | RMD Start Age |
|------------|---------------|
| Before 1951 | Already started |
| 1951-1959 | 73 |
| 1960 or later | 75 |

### RMD Calculation

**Formula:** Account Balance (Dec 31 prior year) ÷ Life Expectancy Factor

**Example:**
- Age 73 balance: $1,000,000
- Life expectancy factor: 26.5
- RMD: $1,000,000 ÷ 26.5 = $37,736

### Fidelity RMD Services

- **Automatic RMD Service:** Calculates and distributes automatically
- **Aggregate RMDs:** Can satisfy RMD from one IRA for all IRAs
- **401(k) RMDs:** Must be taken separately from each 401(k)
- **Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs):** Up to $105,000/year directly to charity (counts toward RMD, not taxable)

## Retirement Income Strategies

### The 4% Rule (Starting Point)

**Traditional Approach:**
- Withdraw 4% of initial portfolio in Year 1
- Adjust for inflation each subsequent year
- 95% success rate over 30 years (historical backtesting)

**Fidelity Refinement:**
- 4-5% if retiring at 65 with 30-year horizon
- 3-4% if retiring earlier (60 or younger)
- 4.5-5.5% if delaying to 70 and have shorter horizon

### Dynamic Spending Strategies

| Market Performance | Spending Adjustment |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| Strong bull market | Can increase 5-10% |
| Mild downturn | Reduce discretionary 10-20% |
| Severe bear market | Cut to essentials, use cash buffer |
| Recovery phase | Gradually restore spending |

### Guaranteed Income Options

**Fidelity Guaranteed Income Direct:**

- Deferred income annuities
- Qualified longevity annuity contracts (QLACs) in 401(k)s

**When to Consider:**
- Need certainty for essential expenses
- Longevity risk concern (family history of living to 90+)
- Risk-averse investor

## Roth Conversion Strategies

### When Conversions Make Sense

1. **Low-Income Years:**
   - Gap years between retirement and RMDs/Social Security
   - Business loss year
   - Sabbatical or career transition

2. **Tax Bracket Arbitrage:**
   - Currently in 12% or 22% bracket, expect to be in 24%+ in retirement
   - State income tax differences

3. **Legacy Planning:**
   - Beneficiaries in higher tax brackets
   - Desire to leave tax-free inheritance

### Fidelity Roth Conversion Process

1. Log into Fidelity.com
2. Navigate to "Rollover" or "Convert IRA"
3. Select Traditional IRA source
4. Select Roth IRA destination
5. Choose amount (can do partial conversions)
6. Pay taxes from outside funds (not from conversion amount)

**Pro Tip:** Do conversions in December when you have full picture of year's income

## Social Security Integration

### Optimal Claiming Strategies

**General Guidelines:**

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Single, healthy | Delay to 70 if possible |
| Single, health concerns | Claim at FRA or earlier |
| Married, higher earner | Delay to 70 |
| Married, lower earner | Claim at FRA (or earlier if needed) |
| Divorced (10+ years) | Can claim on ex's record if beneficial |
| Widowed | Survivor benefits available as early as 60 |

**Fidelity Social Security Calculator:**
- Available in NetBenefits
- Compares claiming strategies
- Integrates with other retirement income

### Social Security Taxation

**Provisional Income Test:**
- < $25,000 single / $32,000 married: 0% taxable
- $25,000-$34,000 single / $32,000-$44,000 married: Up to 50% taxable
- > $34,000 single / $44,000 married: Up to 85% taxable

**Strategy:** Roth withdrawals don't count toward provisional income

## Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) with Fidelity

### Why HSAs Are Powerful

**Triple Tax Advantage:**
1. Tax-deductible contributions
2. Tax-free growth
3. Tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses

### Fidelity HSA Features

- **Investment Options:** Full brokerage access including ZERO funds
- **No Minimums:** Invest from dollar one
- **Debit Card:** For medical expenses
- **Save Receipts:** Can reimburse yourself years later (let investments grow)

### HSA as Stealth IRA

**Strategy:**
1. Max HSA contribution ($4,300 individual / $8,550 family in 2025; +$1,000 if 55+)
2. Pay medical expenses out of pocket
3. Save receipts
4. Invest HSA funds for growth
5. Reimburse yourself anytime in future (no deadline)
6. After 65, can withdraw for any purpose (pay income tax, no penalty)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not Maximizing Employer Match** - Leaving free money on table
2. **Concentrated Company Stock** - More than 10% in single stock is risky
3. **Ignoring Fees** - 1% fee can cost hundreds of thousands over career
4. **Taking Early Withdrawals** - 10% penalty + taxes + lost compounding
5. **Not Updating Beneficiaries** - Outdated designations cause legal nightmares
6. **Ignoring Roth Options** - Tax-free growth is powerful, especially early career
7. **Timing the Market** - Dollar-cost averaging beats market timing
8. **Retiring with Debt** - Mortgage is okay; high-interest debt is not
