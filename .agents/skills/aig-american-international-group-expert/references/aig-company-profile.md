# AIG Company Profile

## History & Evolution

### Founding and Early Growth (1919-2000)

**1919:** Cornelius Vander Starr founded American Asiatic Underwriters in Shanghai, China. Started with general insurance operations.

**1926:** Opened first US office in New York City.

**1939:** Moved headquarters to New York City as World War II began.

**1960s-1970s:** Rapid international expansion across 100+ countries. Became world's largest insurance organization.

**1980s-1990s:** Diversified into financial services, aircraft leasing, and capital markets activities.

**2001:** Maurice "Hank" Greenberg ousted after regulatory investigations into accounting practices.

### The 2008 Financial Crisis

**September 2008:** AIG faced collapse due to credit default swap (CDS) exposure at AIG Financial Products (AIGFP).

**September 16, 2008:** Federal Reserve provided $85 billion emergency loan, taking 79.9% equity stake. Largest government rescue of private company in US history.

**Total Bailout:** Expanded to $182 billion including:
- Federal Reserve credit facility
- Treasury equity investments
- Maiden Lane facilities for asset disposal

**Leadership Changes:**
- Robert Willumstad (CEO June-September 2008)
- Edward Liddy (CEO September 2008-August 2009) - $1 salary
- Robert Benmosche (CEO August 2009-September 2017) - Led turnaround

**Repayment:** AIG fully repaid the bailout with profit to taxpayers by 2012.

### Post-Crisis Restructuring (2012-2020)

**Asset Sales:**
- AIA Group (Asia life insurance) - $6B IPO 2010
- ALICO (foreign life) - $15.5B sale to MetLife 2010
- ILFC (aircraft leasing) - Sale to AerCap 2014
- United Guaranty (mortgage insurance) - Sale to Arch Capital 2016

**Focus Shift:** Concentrated on core property-casualty insurance and US life/retirement.

### Modern Era Transformation (2021-Present)

**Peter Zaffino Era:**
- March 2021: Named CEO
- 2022: Corebridge Financial IPO ($1.7B) - Life & Retirement spinoff
- 2023: Validus Re sale ($3.3B to RenaissanceRe)
- 2023: Crop Risk Services sale
- 2024: AIG Next transformation program
- 2025: Strategic investment in Convex Group
- 2025: Everest Group renewal rights acquisition ($2B premium)

## Competitive Position

### Market Position

**Global Ranking:**
- Top 10 global property-casualty insurer by premium
- Top 3 US commercial lines insurer
- Leading position in specialty insurance via Lloyd's

**Key Competitors:**
- Chubb
- Travelers
- Liberty Mutual
- Hartford
- Zurich
- Allianz

### Competitive Advantages

1. **Global Platform:** True multinational capabilities with local expertise
2. **Relationships:** Deep broker relationships, especially with top-tier firms
3. **Specialty Expertise:** Talbot/Lloyd's platform for complex risks
4. **Financial Strength:** A ratings supporting large line capacity
5. **Risk Services:** Sophisticated loss control and claims capabilities

### Strategic Challenges

1. **Margin Pressure:** Industry-wide casualty reserve concerns
2. **Catastrophe Exposure:** Climate change increasing NatCat severity
3. **Talent Competition:** War for underwriting and technical talent
4. **Digital Transformation:** Legacy system modernization
