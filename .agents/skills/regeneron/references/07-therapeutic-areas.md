# Therapeutic Areas

## Overview

Regeneron organizes its R&D efforts into distinct therapeutic areas, each with dedicated scientific teams, clinical development programs, and commercial strategies. This structure enables deep disease expertise while leveraging cross-cutting platform technologies.

---

## 1. Ophthalmology

### Market Context

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Global Market** | $30+ billion |
| **Key Diseases** | AMD, DME, DR, RVO |
| **Treatment Paradigm** | Intravitreal injections |
| **Competition** | Lucentis, Beovu, Vabysmo, biosimilars |

### Regeneron Position

**Leading Product:** Eylea® / Eylea HD®
- **Market share:** ~46% of anti-VEGF market (US)
- **Differentiation:** Higher dose, extended dosing
- **2024 US sales:** $5.97 billion

### Strategic Priorities

1. **Eylea HD Transition**
   - Migrate patients from Eylea
   - Extend dosing intervals (Q8W → Q12W → Q16W)
   - Defend against Vabysmo (Roche) and biosimilars

2. **New Indications**
   - RVO (sBLA submission Q1 2025)
   - Pre-filled syringe convenience

3. **Pipeline Expansion**
   - Next-generation VEGF inhibitors
   - Combination approaches
   - Gene therapy for inherited retinal diseases

### Key Threats

- **Biosimilars:** Entry expected 2024-2025
- **Vabysmo:** Dual VEGF/Ang-2 mechanism
- **Gene therapy:** One-time treatment potential

## 2. Immunology & Inflammation

### Market Context

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Global Market** | $150+ billion |
| **Key Diseases** | Atopic dermatitis, asthma, COPD, autoimmune |
| **Biologic Penetration** | Increasing across indications |
| **Growth Driver** | Dupixent expansion |

### Regeneron Position

**Anchor Product:** Dupixent® (with Sanofi)
- **Blockbuster status:** $14.15B global sales (2024)
- **Indications:** 7 approved, expanding
- **Differentiation:** IL-4/IL-13 dual blockade

**Emerging Products:**
- Kevzara® (IL-6R): Rheumatoid arthritis
- Itepekimab (IL-33): COPD (Phase 3)

### Strategic Priorities

1. **Dupixent Lifecycle Management**
   - COPD (first biologic approved, Sept 2024)
   - Bullous pemphigoid (sBLA submitted)
   - Chronic spontaneous urticaria (approved)
   - Pediatric expansions

2. **Type 2 Inflammation Leadership**
   - Maintain dominance in atopic diseases
   - Expand into respiratory (COPD, asthma)
   - Address comorbidities

3. **Next-Generation Targets**
   - Itepekimab (IL-33) for COPD
   - Different mechanism from Dupixent
   - Potential for combination

### Disease Areas

| Disease | Current Treatment | Dupixent Status |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Atopic dermatitis | Corticosteroids, immunosuppressants | Approved, leading biologic |
| Asthma | Inhaled corticosteroids, LABA | Approved, add-on therapy |
| COPD | Bronchodilators, steroids | First biologic approved |
| CRSwNP | Surgery, steroids | Approved, reduces surgeries |
| EoE | PPIs, steroids | Approved (pediatric) |
| CSU | Antihistamines | Approved |

## 3. Oncology

### Market Context

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Global Market** | $200+ billion |
| **Key Modalities** | Immunotherapy, targeted therapy, ADCs |
| **Treatment Paradigm** | Combination approaches |
| **Competition** | Intense (Keytruda dominance) |

### Regeneron Position

**Established Product:** Libtayo® (PD-1)
- **2024 sales:** $1.22 billion (+40%)
- **Blockbuster status:** Achieved
- **Differentiation:** First in CSCC, strong efficacy

**Emerging Products:**
- Odronextamab (CD20xCD3): Follicular lymphoma
- Linvoseltamab (BCMAxCD3): Multiple myeloma
- Fianlimab (LAG-3): Combination with Libtayo

### Strategic Priorities

1. **Libtayo Expansion**
   - Adjuvant CSCC (first immunotherapy in setting)
   - Neoadjuvant NSCLC
   - Combination with fianlimab

2. **Hematology Build**
   - Odronextamab for lymphoma
   - Linvoseltamab for myeloma
   - CD3 bispecific platform

3. **Solid Tumor Innovation**
   - Costimulatory bispecifics (CD28-based)
   - Novel targets (GPC3, etc.)
   - Cell therapy integration

### Competitive Dynamics

| Competitor | Product | Challenge |
|------------|---------|-----------|
| **Merck** | Keytruda | Dominant PD-1, hard to displace |
| **BMS** | Opdivo, Opdualag | Established combinations |
| **Genmab** | Epcoritamab | Competing CD3 bispecific |
| **J&J** | Teclistamab | Competing BCMA bispecific |

### Technology Focus

- **Bispecific antibodies:** T cell engagers
- **Checkpoint combinations:** LAG-3, TIGIT
- **Cell therapy:** CAR-T, TCR-T (2seventy bio acquisition)
- **Genetic markers:** RGC for patient selection

## 4. Cardiovascular & Metabolic

### Market Context

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Global Market** | $300+ billion |
| **Key Diseases** | ASCVD, dyslipidemia, diabetes, obesity |
| **Growth Area** | Obesity/GLP-1 explosion |
| **Competition** | Statins, PCSK9 inhibitors, GLP-1s |

### Regeneron Position

**Approved Products:**
- Praluent® (PCSK9): Cholesterol lowering
- Evkeeza® (ANGPTL3): HoFH (ultra-rare)

**Pipeline:**
- ALN-ANGPTL3 (with Alnylam): Dyslipidemia
- Trevogrimab (GDF-15): Obesity (Phase 2)
- GPR75 program: Obesity (RGC-validated)
- GLP-1 combinations: Obesity

### Strategic Priorities

1. **Lipid Management**
   - Evkeeza expansion to severe hypertriglyceridemia
   - ANGPTL3 siRNA development
   - Differentiation from PCSK9 class

2. **Obesity Entry**
   - Trevogrimab data expected 2025
   - GLP-1 combination strategies
   - Differentiated mechanisms (not just GLP-1)

3. **Genetic Validation**
   - Leverage RGC discoveries
   - ANGPTL3, HSD17B13, GPR75
   - First-in-class opportunities

### Obesity Strategy

**Why Enter Now:**
- $50+ billion market by 2030
- High unmet need persists
- Opportunity for differentiation

**Differentiation Approach:**
- GDF-15 pathway (trevogrimab)
- Novel genetic targets (GPR75)
- Combination with GLP-1 (not replacement)
