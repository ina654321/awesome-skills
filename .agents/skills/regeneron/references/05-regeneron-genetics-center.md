# Regeneron Genetics Center (RGC)

## Overview

The **Regeneron Genetics Center® (RGC)** is a wholly owned subsidiary of Regeneron Pharmaceuticals and one of the world's largest and most comprehensive human genetics initiatives. The RGC applies cutting-edge genomic technologies to accelerate drug discovery and development.

> "RGC applies the best and latest technologies in sequencing and analytics to harness the power of human genetics to create game-changing new medicines."
> — Aris Baras, MD, MBA, SVP & Head of RGC

## Mission

**"Genetics to therapeutics, designed for all"**

The RGC harnesses the power of human genetics to:
1. Discover important new medicines
2. Validate existing research programs
3. Optimize clinical trials

## Landmark Discoveries

### ANGPTL3 (2017)

**Discovery:** Loss-of-function mutations associated with:
- Decreased LDL, HDL, and triglycerides
- Protection from atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease

**Translation:**
- Evkeeza® (evinacumab) approved for HoFH
- siRNA program with Alnylam (ALN-ANGPTL3)

### HSD17B13 (2018)

**Discovery:** Loss-of-function variant associated with:
- Reduced risk of chronic liver disease
- Protection from NASH progression

**Translation:**
- Collaboration with Alnylam on ALN-HSD
- siRNA therapeutic in clinical trials

### CIDEB (2022)

**Discovery:** Rare mutations associated with:
- 53% lower risk for NASH
- Protection from liver damage

**Significance:** Novel target for metabolic liver disease

### GPR75 (2021)

**Discovery:** Loss-of-function mutations associated with:
- ~50% lower BMI
- Protection against obesity

**Status:** Preclinical development for obesity

## Diversity Initiatives

### Together for CHANGE (2024)

**Partners:** Mehary Medical College, Novo Nordisk, Roche, J&J

**Goal:**
- Sequence 500,000 individuals of African ancestry
- Address underrepresentation in genomics
- Support STEM education at HBCUs

**Structure:**
- Diaspora Human Genomics Institute (DHGI) governs
- Regeneron provides in-kind sequencing
- Data ownership remains with DHGI

### Why Diversity Matters

**Current Gap:**
- 80%+ of genomic research in populations of European ancestry
- Limits understanding of disease in other populations
- Reduces effectiveness of precision medicine globally

**Regeneron Commitment:**
- Enhanced representation in research
- Transparent collaboration with local organizations
- Equitable benefit sharing

## Applications Across R&D

### Target Discovery
- Identify novel drug targets through protective variants
- Validate targets through human genetic evidence
- Prioritize targets with large effect sizes

### Clinical Trial Design
- Enrich trials with genetically-defined populations
- Identify biomarkers for patient stratification
- De-risk development through genetic validation

### Drug Repurposing
- Identify new indications for approved drugs
- Discover off-target effects through genetic mimicry
- Expand addressable populations

### Safety Assessment
- Identify potential adverse effects through genetic studies
- Understand mechanism-based toxicities
- Inform patient selection criteria

## Patient Impact Story

### Barbara Barnes

A 58-year-old Pennsylvania homemaker who:
- Contributed blood sample to MyCode Community Health Initiative (2016)
- Discovered to have BRCA1 V1736A variant
- Notified through research program
- Received preventive treatment

**Illustrates:** The power of linking genetic research to clinical care
