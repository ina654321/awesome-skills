# Approved Medicines Portfolio

## Overview

Regeneron has **12 FDA-approved medicines** with global commercialization, spanning multiple therapeutic areas. The portfolio represents both proprietary Regeneron programs and products developed through partnerships.

## By Therapeutic Area

---

###  Ophthalmology

#### EYLEA® (aflibercept) Injection

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Approval Date** | November 2011 (FDA) |
| **Target** | VEGF-A, VEGF-B, PlGF (Trap technology) |
| **Mechanism** | Decoy receptor neutralizing angiogenic factors |

**Indications:**
- Wet age-related macular degeneration (wAMD)
- Diabetic macular edema (DME)
- Diabetic retinopathy (DR)
- Macular edema following retinal vein occlusion (RVO)

**Dosing:**
- Initial: Monthly injections
- Maintenance: Every 8 weeks (or per physician discretion)

**2024 Performance:**
- US net sales (Eylea + Eylea HD): $5.97 billion
- Global: Significant contribution through Bayer partnership

---

#### EYLEA HD® (aflibercept) 8 mg

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Approval Date** | August 2023 (FDA) |
| **Differentiation** | High-dose formulation for extended dosing |

**Key Benefits:**
- Less frequent injections (potential for Q12W or Q16W)
- Same efficacy with reduced treatment burden
- Transition option for existing Eylea patients

**2024 Performance:**
- US net sales: $1.2 billion
- 29% sequential growth Q3 2024
- Prefilled syringe application submitted (FDA decision mid-2025)

**Pipeline:**
- Phase 3 QUASAR trial: Positive in RVO (2024)
- sBLA submission planned Q1 2025

### 🩹 Immunology & Inflammation

#### Dupixent® (dupilumab)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Approval Date** | March 2017 (FDA - atopic dermatitis) |
| **Target** | IL-4Rα (blocks IL-4 and IL-13) |
| **Type** | Fully human monoclonal antibody |
| **Partner** | Sanofi (co-development and commercialization) |

**Approved Indications:**
1. Atopic dermatitis (≥6 months)
2. Asthma (≥6 years)
3. Chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps (CRSwNP)
4. Eosinophilic esophagitis (EoE) (≥1 year)
5. Prurigo nodularis
6. Chronic spontaneous urticaria (CSU)
7. Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)

**Administration:**
- Subcutaneous injection
- Every 2 weeks (most indications)
- Pre-filled pen or syringe

**2024 Performance:**
- Global net sales: $14.15 billion (+22% YoY)
- Patients on treatment: 1.3+ million globally
- Regeneron's share of profits: ~$4B+

**Development Pipeline:**
- Bullous pemphigoid (sBLA submitted)
- Lichen simplex chronicus (Phase 3)
- Chronic pruritus of unknown origin (Phase 3)
- Allergic fungal rhinosinusitis

**Key Milestone:**
- First and only biologic approved for COPD (September 2024)

### 🎗️ Oncology

#### Libtayo® (cemiplimab-rwlc)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Approval Date** | September 2018 (FDA) |
| **Target** | PD-1 (checkpoint inhibitor) |
| **Type** | Fully human monoclonal antibody |

**Approved Indications:**
1. Cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (CSCC) - metastatic or locally advanced
2. Basal cell carcinoma (BCC) - locally advanced
3. Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) - PD-L1 high, first-line

**2024 Performance:**
- Global net sales: $1.22 billion (+40% YoY)
- Achieved **blockbuster status** ($1B+)

**Pipeline:**
- Adjuvant CSCC (Phase 3 positive, 2024)
- Neoadjuvant NSCLC
- Intralesional administration for early-stage CSCC
- Combination with fianlimab (LAG-3)

**Competitive Position:**
- Strong efficacy in CSCC (first approved for this indication)
- Differentiation vs. Keytruda, Opdivo

#### Evkeeza® (evinacumab-dgnb)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Approval Date** | February 2021 (FDA) |
| **Target** | ANGPTL3 |
| **Discovery** | Validated through RGC human genetics |

**Indication:**
- Homozygous familial hypercholesterolemia (HoFH)

**Mechanism:**
- Binds and blocks ANGPTL3
- Reduces LDL-C independent of LDL receptor

**Differentiation:**

- Orphan drug with premium pricing
