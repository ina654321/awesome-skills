## B-21 Raider

### Program Overview
The B-21 Raider is the next-generation stealth strategic bomber designed to replace the aging B-1B Lancer and B-2 Spirit. It forms the backbone of the future U.S. Air Force bomber force alongside the modernized B-52J Stratofortress.

### Key Specifications (Unclassified)
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Role** | Long-range strike, strategic bomber |
| **Crew** | 2 (pilot and weapon systems officer) |
| **Stealth** | Next-generation low observable design |
| **Payload** | Conventional and nuclear weapons |
| **Range** | Long-range (specifics classified) |
| **Contractor** | Northrop Grumman (prime) |
| **Unit Cost** | ~$750 million (FY2024 dollars) |

### Program Timeline
| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Oct 2015 | Northrop Grumman selected over Lockheed Martin-Boeing team |
| Dec 2022 | Official public unveiling at Plant 42, Palmdale, CA |
| Nov 2023 | First flight (aircraft 0001) |
| Jan 2024 | First LRIP contract awarded |
| Late 2024 | Second LRIP contract (Lot 2) |
| Sep 2025 | Second aircraft (T-2) first flight |
| Mar 2026 | Production acceleration agreement (+25% capacity) |
| 2027 | Initial operational capability (planned) |

### Technical Features

**Stealth & Survivability:**
- Advanced low-observable shaping and materials
- All-aspect stealth (reduced radar cross-section from all angles)
- Designed to operate in highly contested airspace

**Open Architecture:**
- Software-defined systems
- Rapid upgradeability
- Third-party payload integration capability

**Multi-Domain Integration:**
- JADC2 node capability
- Data-linked battle management
- ISR and communications relay functions

**Autonomous Operations:**
- Optionally manned capability (design allows unmanned operation)
- Advanced mission autonomy

### Production Status
- **Current Fleet:** 2 flight test aircraft (T-1, T-2), 2 ground test articles
- **Under Assembly:** 6+ additional aircraft at Plant 42
- **Planned Fleet:** 100+ aircraft
- **Production Rate:** Ramping to accelerated capacity (March 2026)

### Strategic Significance
The B-21 addresses the "bomber gap" created by B-1 and B-2 retirements. Unlike its predecessors, it was designed for producibility, enabling affordable mass production.

## E-2 Hawkeye

### Program Overview
The E-2 Hawkeye is the U.S. Navy's primary carrier-based airborne early warning and control (AEW&C) aircraft, providing battle management and surveillance.

### Variants
| Variant | Description | Status |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| E-2A | Original production (1964) | Retired |
| E-2B | Upgrade of E-2A | Retired |
| E-2C | Improved radar, engines | Limited service |
| E-2C+ Group II | Advanced Hawkeye precursor | Limited service |
| **E-2D** | **Current production variant** | **Active** |

### E-2D Advanced Hawkeye Specifications
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Crew** | 5 (pilot, co-pilot, 3 mission operators) |
| **Length** | 57 ft 9 in |
| **Wingspan** | 80 ft 7 in (rotating for carrier stowage) |
| **Max Takeoff Weight** | 57,500 lb |
| **Endurance** | 6+ hours (plus aerial refueling) |
| **Radar** | APY-9 UHF AESA radar |
| **Operators** | US Navy, Japan, France |

### APY-9 Radar Capabilities
- **Technology:** Active Electronically Scanned Array (AESA)
- **Frequency:** UHF band (optimized for counter-stealth)
- **Range:** 300+ nautical miles
- **Modes:** Air-to-air, air-to-surface, maritime surveillance
- **Track Capacity:** 2,000+ simultaneous tracks

### International Programs
- **Japan:** 13 E-2D aircraft ordered
- **France:** 3 E-2D aircraft (first production started Dec 2024)
- **Taiwan:** E-2K variant (upgraded E-2T)

## F-35 Lightning II (Subcontractor)

### Northrop Grumman Role
Northrop Grumman is a principal subcontractor to Lockheed Martin on the F-35 program, providing critical systems across all three variants.

### NG Contributions

**1. AN/APG-81 AESA Fire Control Radar**
- Active Electronically Scanned Array radar
- Air-to-air, air-to-ground, electronic warfare modes
- Interleaved multi-mode operation

**2. Distributed Aperture System (DAS)**
- Six IR sensors providing 360° spherical coverage
- Functions: Missile warning, navigation, night vision, situational awareness
- First-ever 360° IR system on a fighter

**3. Communications, Navigation, Identification (CNI)**
- Integrated avionics system
- Software-defined radios
- Secure communications, navigation, IFF

**4. Other Contributions**
- Vehicle management computers
- Center fuselage (Navy variant)
- Various structural components

### Production Status
Northrop Grumman produces F-35 components across multiple facilities, with deliveries supporting the planned 3,000+ aircraft program.

## MQ-4C Triton

### Program Overview
The MQ-4C Triton is a high-altitude, long-endurance (HALE) unmanned aircraft system based on the Global Hawk, optimized for maritime surveillance.

### Specifications
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Wingspan** | 130.9 ft |
| **Length** | 47.6 ft |
| **Height** | 15.4 ft |
| **Max Takeoff Weight** | 32,250 lb |
| **Payload** | 2,400 lb |
| **Ceiling** | 56,000 ft |
| **Endurance** | 24+ hours |
| **Range** | 8,200+ nautical miles |

### Mission Capabilities
- **Multi-Intelligence:** Radar, EO/IR, SIGINT sensors
- **Maritime Surveillance:** Detect/track surface vessels
- **ASW Support:** Magnetic anomaly detection (planned)
- **Search & Rescue:** Wide-area search capability

### MFAS Radar
- **Type:** Multi-Function Active Sensor (MFAS)
- **Technology:** AESA radar optimized for maritime
- **Capabilities:** Surface search, inverse SAR, weather avoidance

### Program Status
| Milestone | Date |
|-----------|------|
| First Flight | May 2013 |
| IOC | 2023 |
| Australia | 1 aircraft delivered, 5 more planned |
| Germany | Purchase approved |
| NATO | Alliance Ground Surveillance consideration |
