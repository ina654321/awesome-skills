# Space Systems

## James Webb Space Telescope (JWST)

### Program Overview
The James Webb Space Telescope is NASA's premier space observatory, the largest and most complex space telescope ever built. Northrop Grumman served as prime contractor for NASA Goddard Space Flight Center.

### Northrop Grumman Contributions

**1. Sunshield**
- **Size:** Tennis court-sized (21.2m x 14.2m deployed)
- **Layers:** Five layers of Kapton with aluminum/silicon coating
- **Function:** Passive cooling to maintain telescope at cryogenic temperatures (~45K)
- **Innovation:** Complex deployment sequence (folded for launch)

**2. Spacecraft Bus**
- **Function:** Houses power, propulsion, communications, attitude control
- **Features:** 10-year mission life, precision pointing
- **Components:** Solar arrays, reaction wheels, star trackers

**3. Optical Telescope Element Integration**
- Integrated the 6.5-meter primary mirror (built by Ball Aerospace)
- Integrated the Integrated Science Instrument Module (ISIM)
- Managed system-level thermal vacuum testing

### Technical Specifications
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Primary Mirror** | 6.5m (18 hexagonal beryllium segments) |
| **Wavelength Coverage** | 0.6 to 28.3 microns (near to mid-IR) |
| **Operating Temperature** | ~40K (instruments), ~6K (MIRI) |
| **Launch Date** | December 25, 2021 |
| **Launch Vehicle** | Ariane 5 |
| **Orbit** | L2 Lagrange point (1.5M km from Earth) |
| **Mission Duration** | 10-year design life |

### Program Partners
| Organization | Contribution |
|--------------|--------------|
| NASA GSFC | Mission lead, ISIM, operations |
| Northrop Grumman | Observatory prime, sunshield, spacecraft bus |
| Ball Aerospace | Optical Telescope Element |
| ESA | NIRSpec instrument, launch services |
| CSA | FGS/NIRISS instrument |
| JPL | MIRI instrument |

### Operations Contract
NASA awarded Northrop Grumman a sole-source operations and sustainment contract (2022-2027, $31M+) to support JWST spacecraft systems, flight software updates, and performance monitoring.

## Military Satellite Systems

### Protected Communications

**Advanced Extremely High Frequency (AEHF) Payloads**
- **Role:** Nuclear-hardened strategic communications
- **Features:** Anti-jam, low probability of intercept
- **Users:** U.S. military, allies (UK, Canada, Netherlands)
- **Northrop Role:** Payload development and integration

**Enhanced Polar System (EPS)**
- **Role:** Polar communications for nuclear command & control
- **Users:** U.S. strategic forces

### Missile Warning

**Defense Support Program (DSP) Heritage**
- Infrared satellites for missile launch detection
- Northrop Grumman heritage from TRW acquisition

**Next-Gen OPIR**
- Follow-on to SBIRS
- Enhanced missile warning capabilities
- Northrop Grumman competing for contracts

### ISR Satellites

**Various Classified Programs**
- Signals intelligence (SIGINT)
- Electro-optical reconnaissance
- Radar imaging

## Launch Systems & Propulsion

### Solid Rocket Motors
Through the 2018 Orbital ATK acquisition, Northrop Grumman is a leading producer of solid rocket motors.

**Products:**
- **GEM:** Graphite Epoxy Motors (strap-on boosters)
- **Orion Motors:** Upper stage propulsion
- **Minuteman III:** Sustainment motors
- **Strategic Missiles:** GBSD/Sentinel propulsion
- **Missile Defense:** GBI boosters

**Facilities:**
- Promontory, Utah (primary production)
- Various other U.S. locations

### Launch Vehicles

**Pegasus**
- Air-launched small satellite launcher
- Dropped from modified L-1011 aircraft
- Orbital ATK heritage

**Minotaur**
- Family of launchers using surplus ICBM motors
- Minotaur I, IV, V configurations
- Cost-effective for government missions

**Antares**
- Medium-lift launcher for ISS cargo (Cygnus)
- Launches from Wallops Island, VA

## E-130J TACAMO

### Program Overview
Northrop Grumman selected as prime contractor to develop nuclear command, control, and communications aircraft for U.S. Navy TACAMO mission.

### Requirements
- **Platform:** C-130J Super Hercules modification
- **Mission:** Take Charge And Move Out (nuclear submarine communication)
- **Capability:** Very Low Frequency (VLF) communications
- **Status:** Development contract awarded 2024

### Significance
Replaces aging E-6B Mercury aircraft for critical nuclear command & control mission, ensuring survivable communications to ballistic missile submarines.
