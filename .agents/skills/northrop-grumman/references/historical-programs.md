# Historical Programs & Heritage

## B-2 Spirit

### Program Overview
The Northrop Grumman B-2 Spirit is a strategic bomber featuring revolutionary flying wing stealth design.

### Specifications
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **First Flight** | July 17, 1989 |
| **IOC** | April 1997 |
| **Crew** | 2 (pilot, mission commander) |
| **Wingspan** | 172 ft (52.4 m) |
| **Length** | 69 ft (21.0 m) |
| **Max Takeoff Weight** | 336,500 lb (152,635 kg) |
| **Range** | 6,000+ nm unrefueled; 10,000+ nm with refueling |
| **Payload** | 40,000 lb (18,144 kg) internal |
| **Speed** | High subsonic |
| **Unit Cost** | ~$2.1 billion (average program cost) |

### Technical Features
- **Flying Wing:** No tail surfaces for reduced radar signature
- **Advanced Composites:** Radar-absorbent materials
- **Low Observable:** Radar cross-section orders of magnitude smaller than conventional aircraft
- **All-Weather:** Precision GPS-guided weapons

### Operational History
- **Combat Debut:** Kosovo War (1999)
- **Subsequent Operations:** Afghanistan, Iraq, Libya
- **Current Status:** 20 aircraft operational (1 lost in 2008 crash)
- **Future:** Retirement planned as B-21 fleet grows

### Legacy
- First operational stealth bomber
- Proved stealth technology at strategic scale
- Lessons applied to B-21 Raider development

## Heritage Companies

### Grumman Aircraft Engineering Corporation (1929-1994)

**Founding:**
- Founded 1929 by Leroy Grumman and partners
- "Iron Works" nickname from robust naval aircraft
- Bethpage, Long Island headquarters

**Key Aircraft:**
| Aircraft | Era | Role |
|----------|-----|------|
| F4F Wildcat | WWII | Carrier fighter |
| F6F Hellcat | WWII | Carrier fighter |
| TBF Avenger | WWII | Torpedo bomber |
| F8F Bearcat | WWII/Korea | Fighter |
| F9F Panther | Korea | Jet fighter |
| F-14 Tomcat | 1970s-2006 | Carrier fighter |
| C-2 Greyhound | 1960s-present | Carrier onboard delivery |
| E-2 Hawkeye | 1960s-present | AEW&C |
| EA-6B Prowler | 1970s-2019 | Electronic attack |

**Apollo Lunar Module:**
- Built all Lunar Modules for Apollo program
- LM-5 (Eagle) landed Apollo 11 on Moon (July 20, 1969)
- Six LM vehicles landed on Moon

### Northrop Aircraft, Inc. (1939-1994)

**Founding:**
- Founded 1939 by Jack Northrop
- Hawthorne, California
- Innovator in flying wing designs

**Key Aircraft:**
| Aircraft | Era | Role |
|----------|-----|------|
| P-61 Black Widow | WWII | Night fighter |
| YB-35 | 1940s | Flying wing bomber (propeller) |
| YB-49 | 1940s | Flying wing bomber (jet) |
| F-5 Freedom Fighter | 1960s-present | Lightweight fighter |
| T-38 Talon | 1960s-present | Supersonic trainer |
| YF-17 Cobra | 1970s | Lightweight fighter prototype |
| B-2 Spirit | 1989-present | Stealth bomber |

**Flying Wing Legacy:**
Jack Northrop's obsession with flying wing designs, starting with N-1M (1940), led directly to the B-2 and B-21. The YB-49's performance validated the concept, though program cancelled in 1949.

## Post-Merger Acquisitions

### Westinghouse Electronic Systems (1996)
- $2.9 billion acquisition
- Added radar and electronic systems expertise
- Heritage in airborne radar (AWACS, fighter radars)

### Litton Industries (2001)
- $3.8 billion stock + $1.3 billion debt
- Shipbuilding and navigation systems
- Guided missile destroyers, amphibious ships

### Newport News Shipbuilding (2001)
- $2.6 billion acquisition
- Nuclear aircraft carrier construction
- Nuclear submarine construction
- Spun off 2011 as Huntington Ingalls Industries

### TRW Inc. (2002)
- Space systems acquisition
- Strategic missile heritage
- Automotive business sold/spun off
- James Webb Space Telescope capabilities

### Orbital ATK (2018)
- $9.2 billion acquisition ($7.8B cash, $1.4B debt)
- Space launch systems
- Solid rocket motors
- Satellites and space components
- Integrated as Northrop Grumman Innovation Systems

## Legacy Programs

### E-8 Joint STARS
- Ground surveillance aircraft
- Modified Boeing 707 airframe
- Northrop Grumman prime contractor
- Operational 1991-2023
- Mission transitioning to other platforms

### EA-6B Prowler
- Electronic attack aircraft
- Carrier-based
- Operational 1971-2019
- Replaced by EA-18G Growler

### F-14 Tomcat
- Supersonic carrier fighter
- Grumman design
- Operational 1974-2006
- Replaced by F/A-18E/F Super Hornet

### RQ-5 Hunter
- Short-range UAV
- U.S. Army service
- 1990s-2010s
- Replaced by newer systems
