## 3. Workflow

### Ford Product Development & Strategy Process

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              FORD STRATEGIC PLANNING WORKFLOW                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

PHASE 1: MARKET INTELLIGENCE
├── Customer research and segmentation
├── Competitive analysis (GM, Stellantis, Tesla, Rivian)
├── Regulatory landscape assessment
└── Fleet customer advisory panels

PHASE 2: PRODUCT PLANNING
├── Powertrain portfolio mix (ICE/Hybrid/EV)
├── Platform strategy (shared vs. dedicated)
├── Feature prioritization based on customer value
└── Pricing strategy and margin analysis

PHASE 3: BUSINESS CASE DEVELOPMENT
├── Volume forecasting by powertrain
├── Manufacturing capacity planning
├── Supply chain and sourcing strategy
└── Investment ROI analysis

PHASE 4: EXECUTION
├── Program management and milestone tracking
├── Cost reduction initiatives
├── Quality improvements
└── Launch execution

PHASE 5: POST-LAUNCH OPTIMIZATION
├── Sales performance monitoring
├── Customer feedback integration
├── Continuous improvement (cost, quality, features)
└── Software/OTA update strategy
```

### Key Stakeholder Management

| Stakeholder | Interests | Engagement Approach |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **UAW** | Job security, wages, benefits | Collaborative negotiations, domestic investment commitments |
| **Dealers** | Profitability, inventory, EV readiness | Training programs, EV infrastructure support |
| **Fleet Customers** | TCO, uptime, service | Ford Pro Intelligence, mobile service, telematics |
| **Retail Customers** | Choice, value, quality | Powertrain options, competitive pricing |
| **Investors** | Returns, dividend, growth story | Clear path to 8% margins, capital discipline |
| **Regulators** | Emissions, safety | Compliance, proactive engagement |
