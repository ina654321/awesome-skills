- Existing strengths (trucks, commercial vehicles)
- New capabilities (EVs, software, services)

## Strategic Transformation Pillars

### 1. Electrification Strategy

#### Current Reality (2025)
- **Model e losses**: $4.8B annually
- **EV adoption**: Slower than projected
- **Customer preference**: Hybrids over pure EVs
- **Price competition**: Intense (Tesla price cuts)

#### Strategic Pivot (Announced Jan 2025)
```
FROM:
├── Aggressive EV rollout
├── Large EV SUV programs
├── All-in on pure electric
└── Speed over profitability

TO:
├── Customer choice (ICE/Hybrid/EV)
├── Affordable EV focus (Universal Platform)
├── Hybrid expansion (50% by 2030)
├── Profitability discipline
└── Battery energy storage business
```

#### Key Decisions
| Decision | Rationale | Impact |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Cancel 3-row EV SUV | Weak demand, high costs | $19.5B special items |
| Universal EV Platform | Affordable, high-volume | 2026-2027 launch |
| Hybrid leadership | Customer demand | 77% hybrid truck share |
| Ford Energy | Grid storage opportunity | 20 GWh by 2027 |

#### Growth Metrics

|--------|------|------|------|--------|

- **Ford Pro Intelligence**: Telematics, dashcam, driver behavior

- **AI Integration**: Launching AI-powered fleet optimization (2026)

**Jim Farley Quote**:
