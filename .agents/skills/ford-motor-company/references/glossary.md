# Ford Motor Company - Automotive Glossary

## A

**ADAS (Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems)**
Electronic systems that help the driver while driving or parking. Ford's BlueCruise is a hands-free ADAS for highway driving.

**Adjusted EBIT**
Earnings Before Interest and Taxes, adjusted for special items. Ford's primary profitability metric for segment performance.

**Aftermarket**
Secondary market for vehicle parts, accessories, and services after original sale.

## B

**Battery Energy Storage System (BESS)**
Large-scale battery systems for grid storage. Ford is entering this market with 20 GWh capacity planned by 2027.

**BlueCruise**
Ford's hands-free highway driving ADAS. Version 1.4 shipping on 2025 models, 1.5 with Automatic Lane Change on Mach-E.

**Blue Oval**
Nickname for Ford based on its logo. Often used to refer to the company: "Blue Oval transformation."

**Body-on-Frame**
Vehicle construction with separate body and frame. Used in trucks and SUVs (Bronco, F-150) for durability and towing.

**BEV (Battery Electric Vehicle)**
Vehicle powered solely by electricity stored in batteries. Ford's Model e division produces BEVs.

## C

**Capex (Capital Expenditure)**
Money spent to acquire or upgrade physical assets. Ford's 2026 guidance: $9.5-10.5B.

**Connected Vehicle**
Vehicle with internet connectivity enabling telematics, OTA updates, and services. Ford has 5.2M+ connected commercial vehicles.

**COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)**
Direct costs of producing vehicles, including materials and labor.

**Crossover (CUV)**
Vehicle combining SUV styling with car-like unibody construction. Examples: Escape, Bronco Sport.

## D

**Dealer Network**
Franchised retailers selling and servicing vehicles. Ford has 3,000+ dealers in US.

**Drive Unit**
Electric motor, gearbox, and power electronics in an EV. Ford designs and manufactures its own.

## E

**EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes)**
Operating profit measure. Ford reports Adjusted EBIT excluding special items.

**E-Telematics**
Ford Pro's EV-specific telematics platform for fleet management, charging optimization, and battery monitoring.

**EV (Electric Vehicle)**
Broad term for vehicles with electric powertrains, including BEV, PHEV, and HEV.

**Extended Range**
Longer-range battery option in Ford EVs (e.g., F-150 Lightning 320 miles).

## F

**F-150 PowerBoost**
Ford's full hybrid system for F-150 combining 3.5L V6 with electric motor (430 HP, 570 lb-ft).

**Fleet Sales**
Vehicles sold to commercial, government, or rental customers. Ford Pro focuses on this segment.

**Ford Credit**
Ford's captive finance company providing loans and leases. Generated $2.6B EBT in 2025.

**Ford Pro**
Ford's commercial vehicle division, including vehicles, software, charging, and services. $66B+ revenue, 42%+ US commercial share.

**Ford+**
Ford's growth and value creation plan combining existing strengths with new capabilities.

**FordPass**
Consumer mobile app for vehicle connectivity, remote start, scheduling service.

**Free Cash Flow**
Cash generated after capital expenditures. Ford 2025: $3.5B; 2026 guidance: $5-6B.

**Frunk**
Front trunk storage in EVs where engine would be in ICE vehicles.

## G

**GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)**
Standard accounting rules. Ford reports both GAAP and non-GAAP (adjusted) metrics.

**GOAT Modes (Goes Over Any Type of Terrain)**
Ford's terrain management system in Bronco for different off-road conditions.

**Gross Margin**
Revenue minus COGS, divided by revenue. Ford's gross margin: 7.53% (2025).

## H

**HEV (Hybrid Electric Vehicle)**
Vehicle combining ICE with electric motor, no plug-in charging. Ford's PowerBoost system.

**HOSS (High-Performance Off-Road Stability Suspension)**
Ford's off-road suspension system, HOSS 3.0 on Bronco Badlands.

**Hybrid**
Vehicle with both ICE and electric powertrain. Ford targeting 50% electrified volume by 2030 (includes hybrids).

## I

**ICE (Internal Combustion Engine)**
Traditional gasoline or diesel engine. Ford Blue focuses on ICE and hybrid vehicles.

**Infotainment**
In-vehicle system combining information and entertainment. Ford's SYNC system.

## J

**JIT (Just-In-Time)**
Inventory management system minimizing parts inventory. Common in automotive manufacturing.

## K

**kWh (Kilowatt-hour)**
Unit of energy used to measure battery capacity. F-150 Lightning: 98-131 kWh.

## L

**LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate)**
Battery chemistry using iron phosphate cathode. More stable, longer life, lower cost. Used in Ford's standard range batteries.

**Lightning**
Name for Ford's electric F-150. Also historic name for performance Ford trucks.

**Lincoln**
Ford's luxury brand. Models: Navigator, Aviator, Nautilus, Corsair.

## M

**Mach-E**
Ford's electric Mustang-inspired SUV. #2 selling electric SUV in US (2024-2025).

**Model e**
Ford's electric vehicle business unit. Separate from Ford Blue and Ford Pro.

**MSRP (Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price)**
List price before incentives and negotiations.

## N

**NACS (North American Charging Standard)**
Tesla's charging connector design adopted by Ford and others. Enables Supercharger access.

**NCM (Nickel Cobalt Manganese)**
Battery chemistry offering higher energy density. Used in Ford's extended range batteries.

## O

**OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer)**
Company that produces vehicles. Ford is an OEM.

**OTA (Over-The-Air)**
Software updates delivered wirelessly to vehicles. Ford Power-Up enables OTA updates.

## P

**PHEV (Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle)**
Hybrid with larger battery enabling electric-only driving, chargeable by plug. Ranger PHEV in Europe.

**Powertrain**
System generating power to move vehicle. Includes engine/motor, transmission, driveshaft.

**PPE (Premium Platform Electric)**
Ford's future EV architecture for Lincoln and premium vehicles.

**Prognostics**
Predicting vehicle maintenance needs before failure. Ford Pro capability.

## R

**Range Anxiety**
Consumer concern about EV battery depletion before reaching destination.

**Raptor**
Ford's high-performance off-road truck/SUV variant (F-150 Raptor, Bronco Raptor, Ranger Raptor).

**Residual Value**
Estimated value of vehicle at end of lease/loan. Important for Ford Credit.

**ROIC (Return on Invested Capital)**
Profitability measure relative to capital invested. Ford adjusted ROIC: 8.8% (2025).

## S

**Sasquatch Package**
Bronco off-road package with 35" tires, locking differentials, lifted suspension.

**SUV (Sport Utility Vehicle)**
Passenger vehicle with off-road styling and capability. Ford's core segment.

**SYNC**
Ford's infotainment system. SYNC 4 standard on most models.

## T

**TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)**
Complete cost of owning/operating vehicle over time. Key metric for fleet buyers.

**Telematics**
Technology sending data over cellular networks. Ford Pro Intelligence uses telematics for fleet management.

**Tremor**
Ford's off-road package below Raptor level (Super Duty Tremor, Maverick Tremor).

**Truck Mix**
Percentage of sales that are trucks. Ford has highest truck mix among major OEMs.

## U

**UAW (United Auto Workers)**
Union representing US auto workers. ~56,500 Ford hourly employees covered.

**Universal EV Platform**
Ford's future flexible EV architecture for affordable, high-volume electric vehicles.

**Unibody**
Vehicle construction where body and frame are integrated. Used in crossovers and cars.

**Upfit**
Customizing commercial vehicles for specific jobs (ambulance, utility, etc.).

## V

**V2L (Vehicle-to-Load)**
Using EV battery to power external devices. F-150 Lightning offers 9.6 kW export.

**V2H (Vehicle-to-Home)**
Using EV battery to power home during outages. Ford Intelligent Backup Power.

## W

**Warranty**
Manufacturer guarantee covering defects. Ford focused on reducing warranty costs.

**Wholesale**
Vehicles sold to dealers (not end customers). Ford reports wholesale volumes.

## Z

**ZEV (Zero Emission Vehicle)**
Vehicle producing no tailpipe emissions. Includes BEV and FCEV (hydrogen).
