# Employee Philosophy: "Take Care of Our Employees"

## Core Tenet

"Take Care of Our Employees" is one of Costco's four foundational business principles (along with obey the law, take care of members, and respect suppliers).

This isn't corporate social responsibility—it's a **competitive strategy**.

## The Business Case

### Founder Jim Sinegal's Philosophy

> "Paying good wages is not in opposition to good productivity. It's not altruism—it's good business."

Sinegal walked the talk: For years, he took a salary of $350,000 when Fortune 100 CEOs averaged $1 million+.

### The Good Jobs Strategy

Research from MIT's Zeynep Ton demonstrates that "good jobs" strategies actually **improve** profitability:

| Metric | Costco | Industry Average |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| **Turnover Rate** | ~8% | ~60% |
| **Average Hourly Wage** | ~$32/hour | ~$17/hour |
| **Employee Coverage by Benefits** | 88% | 55% |
| **Internal Promotion Rate** | 76%+ | Variable |

### Cost-Benefit Analysis

**Higher wages cost more, but save:**
- Recruitment and training costs (lower turnover)
- Error rates (experienced employees)
- Shrinkage/theft (engaged employees)
- Customer service quality (happy employees = happy members)
- Management overhead (self-directed workforce)
