## Supply Chain Strategy

### Supplier Network

**Tier 1 Suppliers:** ~2,000 companies
**Total Supply Base:** ~10,000+ companies

**Key Supplier Categories:**
- Forgings and castings (titanium, nickel superalloys)
- Precision machining
- Electronics and semiconductors
- Composites manufacturing
- Specialized coatings and surface treatments

### Strategic Sourcing Initiatives

**1. Domestic Sourcing Expansion**

**Objective:** Reduce dependence on foreign sources for critical materials

**Actions:**
- Alternative titanium source qualification ($175M investment, 2024)
- Domestic powder metal supply development
- Rare earth element alternative sourcing
- Semiconductor supply chain resilience

**2. Dual-Source Strategy**

**Implementation:**
- Dual-source all A-class (critical) components
- Geographic distribution of sources
- Surge capacity agreements

**Examples:**
- F135 critical components: 2-3 qualified suppliers
- AMRAAM guidance sections: East/West coast suppliers
- GTF hot section: Multiple forge sources

**3. Vertical Integration**

**Strategic Make-Buy Decisions:**
- Additive manufacturing capability expansion
- Precision machining investments
- Composite fabrication integration

## Advanced Manufacturing

### Additive Manufacturing (3D Printing)

**Capabilities:**
| Technology | Materials | Applications |
|------------|-----------|--------------|
| Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS) | Ti-6Al-4V, Inconel 718 | Engine brackets, manifolds |
| Electron Beam Melting (EBM) | Titanium alloys | Structural components |
| Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) | Ultem, PEEK | Prototyping, tooling |
| Binder Jetting | Stainless steel, Inconel | Production parts |

**Production Applications:**
- F135: Additive fuel nozzles, structural brackets
- GTF: Additive turbine components (development)
- Tomahawk: Tested AM structural parts

**Benefits:**
- 50+ parts consolidated to single printed component
- Reduced lead times (months to weeks)
- Design optimization for weight and performance

### Digital Manufacturing

**Digital Thread:**
- Model-based definition (MBD) from design to inspection
- Digital twins for manufacturing process optimization
- Real-time production monitoring

**Smart Factory Initiatives:**
- IoT sensors for equipment health
- Predictive maintenance for production assets
- Automated quality inspection (machine vision)

### Automation & Robotics

**Applications:**
- Automated fiber placement (composites)
- Robotic welding and drilling
- Automated inspection systems
- Collaborative robots (cobots) for assembly

## Supply Chain Risk Management

### Risk Categories & Mitigations

| Risk Category | Examples | Mitigation Strategies |
|--------------|----------|----------------------|
| Geopolitical | Trade restrictions, tariffs | Diversified sourcing, ITAR compliance |
| Single Source | Sole supplier for critical item | Second source qualification, strategic inventory |
| Capacity | Supplier unable to meet demand | Capacity reservations, joint investments |
| Quality | Manufacturing defects | Supplier development, incoming inspection |
| Logistics | Shipping delays, port congestion | Multi-modal transport, regional warehousing |
| Cyber | Supply chain cyber attacks | Supplier cybersecurity requirements |

### Inventory Strategy

**Strategic Stockpiles:**
- Long-lead raw materials (titanium, nickel)
- Obsolescence-prone electronics
- Critical spare parts for production

**Location Strategy:**
- Pre-positioned inventory near major production sites
- Regional distribution centers (Americas, Europe, Asia)
- Direct supplier managed inventory (VMI) programs

### Supplier Development

**Quality Improvement:**
- Supplier quality audit program
- Statistical process control (SPC) requirements
- Corrective action support

**Technical Assistance:**
- Engineering support for process improvements
- Technology transfer programs
- Training and development
