# Raytheon Missile & Defense Systems

## Integrated Air & Missile Defense

### Patriot Air and Missile Defense System

**System Overview:**
Patriot is the world's most advanced and combat-proven air and missile defense system, protecting against tactical ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, and advanced aircraft.

**System Components:**

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| AN/MPQ-65 Radar | C-band multifunction phased array radar |
| AN/MSQ-104/132 ECS | Engagement Control Station |
| M901 Launching Station | Up to 16 missiles per launcher |
| PAC-3 MSE Missile | Hit-to-kill interceptor |
| GEM-T Missile | Guidance Enhanced Missile (air breathing threats) |

**PAC-3 MSE (Missile Segment Enhancement):**
- Hit-to-kill technology (direct body-to-body contact)
- Dual pulse solid rocket motor
- Range: 35+ km
- Altitude: 20+ km
- Dual-band RF seeker

**LTAMDS (Lower Tier Air and Missile Defense Sensor):**
- Next-generation radar replacing MPQ-65
- 360-degree coverage (three antenna arrays)
- Gallium Nitride (GaN) technology
- Compatible with IBCS (Integrated Air and Missile Defense Battle Command System)
- Fielding: 2024-2026

**Global Operators (18+ Nations):**
- United States, Germany, Netherlands, Spain, Greece
- Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, Saudi Arabia, UAE
- Qatar, Romania, Poland, Sweden, Switzerland

| 2024 | Germany | $1.2B | Patriot production |

### NASAMS (National Advanced Surface-to-Air Missile System)

**System Overview:**
Joint development between Raytheon and Norway's Kongsberg Defence & Aerospace. NASAMS provides short-to-medium range air defense using proven air-to-air missiles in a ground-based configuration.

**Key Features:**
- Distributed, networked architecture
- Open systems design
- Multiple missile compatibility
- Road-mobile and relocatable

**System Components:**
- Fire Distribution Center (FDC)
- AN/MPQ-64F1 Sentinel radar
- MSP500 electro-optical sensor (optional)
- Launchers (canister-based)

**Missile Options:**
| Missile | Range | Capability |
|---------|-------|------------|
| AIM-120 AMRAAM | 25-40 km | Standard engagement |
| AMRAAM-ER | 50+ km | Extended range |
| AIM-9X Sidewinder | 10-20 km | Short-range/CIWS |

**Global Operators (13+ Nations):**
- Norway, United States, Netherlands, Finland
- Spain, Oman, Qatar, Indonesia
- Australia, Lithuania, Hungary
- Ukraine (2022+), Taiwan (delivering)

**Recent Contracts:**
- $698.9M (Taiwan, 2024)
- $282M (Ukraine support)
- $329M (U.S. Marine Corps)
- $960.8M (U.S. Navy)

## Naval & Strike Systems

### Tomahawk Cruise Missile

**System Overview:**
The Tomahawk is a long-range, all-weather, subsonic cruise missile in service with the U.S. Navy and Royal Navy since 1984.

**Block V Modernization:**
All Block IV missiles being upgraded/recertified to Block V standard, extending service life by 15 years (through 2040+).

**Block V Variants:**

| Variant | Designation | Capability |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Block V | TACTOM | Modernized NAV/COMMs |
| Block Va | MST | Maritime Strike (moving targets) |
| Block Vb | JMEWS | Joint Multi-Effects Warhead |

**Technical Specifications:**
| Specification | Value |
|--------------|-------|
| Length | 6.18 m (20.3 ft) |
| Diameter | 53.3 cm (21 in) |
| Wingspan | 2.59 m |
| Weight | 1,510 kg (with booster) |
| Range | 1,000+ nautical miles |
| Speed | Subsonic (~880 km/h) |
| Guidance | INS/GPS + TERCOM + DSMAC |

**Launch Platforms:**
- U.S. Navy: CG-47, DDG-51, SSN-774, SSGN-726 classes
- Royal Navy: Astute class submarines

**Recent Contracts:**
- $401.2M (131 Block V missiles, Dec 2024)
- $384M (219 additional Block V, Dec 2025)
- $380.8M (Lot 5/6 recertification, Jan 2026)

**International Sales:**

- Netherlands: 163 Block V (pending)
