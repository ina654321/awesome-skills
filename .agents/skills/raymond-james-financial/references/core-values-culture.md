# Core Values & Culture

## The Raymond James Culture

**"Our business is people and their financial well-being."**

Since opening its doors in 1962, Raymond James has built a distinctive culture centered on relationships, integrity, and long-term thinking. This culture is not merely aspirational — it is operationalized in how the firm treats advisors, clients, and communities.

---

## Four Core Values

### 1. We Put Clients First

**The Foundation:**
The non-negotiable principle that guides every decision at Raymond James. Putting clients first is not just a slogan — it is the firm's pledge.

**The Pledge:**
> "We commit our energies, intellect and knowledge to attaining the financial objectives of our clients by providing the highest possible level of service and delivering superior investment alternatives. We believe that putting the financial well-being of our clients first ultimately serves the best interests of our shareholders, our communities and ourselves."

**In Practice:**
- No proprietary products that create conflicts of interest
- Advice-driven, not transaction-driven relationships
- Transparent fee structures
- Fiduciary mindset in all interactions

### 2. We Act With Integrity

**The Standard:**
Doing the right thing, even when no one is watching. Integrity is woven into every aspect of the firm's operations.

**In Practice:**
- Honest communication with clients about risks and returns
- No pressure tactics or fear-based selling
- Admitting mistakes and making them right
- Regulatory compliance viewed as a floor, not a ceiling

### 3. We Value Independence

**Dual Meaning:**
- **For Clients:** Unbiased advice not tied to proprietary products
- **For Advisors:** Freedom to serve clients their way, with the business model that fits

**In Practice:**
- Multi-channel affiliation options (AdvisorChoice)
- No quotas or product-pushing
- Advisor autonomy in client relationships
- Open architecture investment platform

### 4. We Think Long Term

**The Time Horizon:**
Decades, not quarters. Raymond James makes decisions that compound value over extended timeframes.

**In Practice:**
- 152+ consecutive quarters of profitability through multiple market cycles
- Investment philosophy focused on fundamentals, not trends
- Strategic decisions evaluated on 10+ year horizons
- Relationship building over transactional efficiency

## The Advisor-Centric Culture

### "Advisors as Clients"

Raymond James's most distinctive cultural attribute is how it views financial advisors:

> "The firm looks at advisors as clients and creates a very supportive environment as a result."

**What This Means:**
- Advisor success is the firm's primary objective
- Support services designed from advisor perspective
- Technology investments aimed at reducing advisor burden
- Recruiting focused on cultural fit, not just production

## "Life Well Planned" Brand Philosophy

### The Promise

"Life Well Planned" is more than a tagline — it is Raymond James's approach to financial planning.

**The Philosophy:**
> "If there's one thing we all share, it's that our lives are all unique. At Raymond James, they believe that's worth celebrating. Because financial planning isn't just about numbers and figures, it's about creating the life you envision."

**Key Elements:**

1. **Uniqueness Matters**
   - No two clients are the same
   - Personalized planning, not cookie-cutter solutions
   - Life circumstances drive financial strategy

2. **Holistic Approach**
   - Financial planning integrated with life planning
   - Goals, values, and relationships central to the process
   - Beyond investments to comprehensive wealth management

3. **Partnership Model**
   - Advisor as partner, not product salesperson
   - Ongoing relationship through life transitions
   - Proactive guidance, not reactive service

## Cultural Preservation

### Leadership Commitment

**Paul Shoukry (CEO, as of February 2025):**

> "Since stepping into the role of CEO, many advisors and associates have asked what I plan to bring to the role. My first imperative is clear-cut: preserve the great thing we've all built together."

> "Our advisor-centric, client-first culture is an uncompromising standard. Our strong balance sheet with two-times the required regulatory capital reflects our integrity, stability and duty of responsibility."

**Paul Reilly (Executive Chair, former CEO 2010-2025):**

> "This really is a client-first, long-term-focused organization. It's something that's been woven into our core values from the beginning and something we take great pride in."

**Tom James (Chairman Emeritus, former CEO 1970-2010):**

> "The fact is that commitment to clients and commitment to financial advisors is sacrosanct."

> "If we do what's right for our clients, the firm will do well, and we'll all benefit."

### Cultural Continuity

Despite leadership transitions (Tom James → Paul Reilly → Paul Shoukry), the core culture has remained remarkably consistent. This is intentional:

- Leaders groomed within the cultural framework
- Executive committee includes long-tenured leaders
- Cultural fit is a primary consideration in all hiring
- Regular communication reinforcing values
