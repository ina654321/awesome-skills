# Leadership & History

## Company History

### The Early Years (1962-1983)

**1962 — The Beginning**
- Robert "Bob" James founds Raymond James in St. Petersburg, Florida
- Named after his sons, Raymond and James
- Focus on personalized financial planning (pioneering concept at the time)

**1970 — Tom James Era Begins**
- Tom James becomes CEO
- Would lead the firm for 40 years
- Establishes core values and culture that persist today

**1983 — Going Public**
- Raymond James goes public on NASDAQ
- Later moves to NYSE (ticker: RJF)
- Provides capital for expansion while maintaining culture

### Growth & Expansion (1983-2010)

**Key Developments:**
- Expansion of advisor network nationwide
- Development of multi-channel affiliation options
- Entry into investment banking and capital markets
- Establishment of Raymond James Bank
- International expansion (Canada, Europe)

**1997 — Alex. Brown Connection**
- Alex. Brown (America's first investment bank, founded 1800) acquired by Bankers Trust
- Raymond James was part of syndicate that took Alex. Brown public in 1983
- Would later acquire Alex. Brown's private client business from Deutsche Bank (2016)

**2010 — Leadership Transition**
- After 40 years as CEO, Tom James steps down
- Paul Reilly becomes CEO
- Tom James remains Chairman of the Board
- Transition preserves culture while bringing fresh perspective

### The Reilly Era (2010-2025)

**2012 — Morgan Keegan Acquisition**
- Acquires Morgan Keegan from Regions Financial for $1.2 billion
- Adds 900+ advisors and significantly expands capital markets
- One of largest acquisitions in firm history
- Successfully integrated while maintaining culture

**2016 — Alex. Brown Reborn**
- Acquires Deutsche Bank's U.S. Private Client Services unit
- Revives Alex. Brown name (founded 1800, America's first investment bank)
- 193 of 210 advisors retained (>90%)
- Adds high-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth capabilities

**2020-2024 — Record Growth**
- Five consecutive years of record annual results
- Assets under administration grow from ~$800B to $1.7T+
- Significant technology investments
- Strong organic advisor recruiting

**2024 — Tom James Retires**
- Tom James retires as Chairman Emeritus
- Paul Reilly named Executive Chair
- Paul Shoukry named CEO-designate

### Current Era (2025-Present)

**February 2025 — New Leadership**
- Paul Shoukry becomes CEO
- Paul Reilly becomes Executive Chair
- Leadership committed to preserving culture while evolving for future

**January 2026 — Clark Capital Acquisition**
- Announced acquisition of Clark Capital Management Group
- $46B+ AUM asset manager
- Cultural and strategic fit
- Part of multi-boutique asset management strategy

## Leadership Philosophy

### The CEO Transition (2025)

**Paul Shoukry on becoming CEO:**

> "Having witnessed the transition from Tom James to Paul Reilly in 2010 firsthand, I understand the responsibility of this role. My first imperative is clear-cut: preserve the great thing we've all built together."

**Vision for the Future:**

> "Looking forward, the reality is our industry has become increasingly complex. I'm fortunate to have a strong, tenured leadership team with a demonstrated track record of embodying our culture and values every day. Those values will continue to guide tactical and strategic decisions, both big and small."

**On Technology:**

> "As we think about the future, my vision is to guide Raymond James to fully own its identity as a high-tech, high-touch organization. I see these two elements as complementary to one another."

### Leadership Principles

**Paul Reilly's Legacy (2010-2025):**

Key accomplishments during Paul Reilly's tenure:
- Oversaw Morgan Keegan integration (2012)
- Led Alex. Brown acquisition and revival (2016)
- Navigated firm through multiple market cycles
- Five consecutive years of record results
- Maintained and strengthened culture

**Tom James's Legacy (1970-2010):**

Key accomplishments during Tom James's tenure:
- Grew firm from regional broker to national wealth management leader
- Established "Life Well Planned" philosophy
- Created advisor-centric culture
- Took firm public (1983)
- Maintained profitability through all market conditions

## Cultural Continuity

The remarkable aspect of Raymond James's history is the consistency of its culture across leadership transitions:

- **Tom James (1970-2010):** Established culture
- **Paul Reilly (2010-2025):** Preserved and strengthened
- **Paul Shoukry (2025-present):** Committed to preservation

This continuity is intentional and rare in the financial services industry, contributing significantly to the firm's stability and advisor loyalty.
