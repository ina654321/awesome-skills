## Transaction Types

### Buy-Out
**Description:** Complete transfer of pension liabilities to Prudential

**Process:**
1. Plan sponsor pays single premium to Prudential
2. Prudential assumes all investment and longevity risk
3. Prudential pays retirees directly (annuity certificates issued)
4. Pension plan liability removed from balance sheet

**Accounting Impact:**
- Triggers settlement accounting
- May require additional cash contribution
- Eliminates PBGC premiums for covered participants

**Best For:**
- Plans that want complete de-risking
- Terminating pension plans
- Reducing balance sheet volatility

### Buy-In
**Description:** Insurance contract held as plan asset; Prudential makes payments to the plan

**Process:**
1. Premium paid to Prudential
2. Prudential makes payments to the pension plan (not participants)
3. Assets remain in pension plan
4. Plan continues to pay participants

**Accounting Impact:**
- Does not trigger settlement accounting
- Does not reduce plan liabilities
- PBGC premiums continue
- Can be converted to buy-out later

**Best For:**
- Partial de-risking strategy
- Plans that want to retain administration
- Step toward full buy-out

### Longevity Reinsurance
**Description:** Transfer of longevity risk only; investment risk retained by plan

**Process:**
1. Premium paid based on longevity risk transfer
2. Prudential pays if retirees live longer than assumed
3. Plan retains assets and investment decisions

**Best For:**
- Hedging longevity risk specifically
- Plans with strong investment capabilities
- UK and international markets (more developed)

## Transaction Process

### Six Workstreams

1. **Liability**
   - Transaction strategy from liability perspective
   - Population group selection
   - Mortality experience data collection
   - Data file preparation for pricing

2. **Assets**
   - Asset portfolio analysis
   - Transition planning
   - Asset transfer execution

3. **Pricing**
   - Indicative pricing
   - Competitive bidding (if applicable)
   - Final pricing and terms

4. **Structure & Legal**
   - Contract negotiation
   - Regulatory review
   - Board and fiduciary approvals

5. **Administration**
   - Participant data transfer
   - Systems integration
   - Ongoing administration setup

6. **Communication**
   - Regulatory filings
   - Participant communications
   - Stakeholder management

## Key Considerations

### Fiduciary Requirements
- Plan sponsor must select "safest available annuity"
- DOL guidance on participant communications
- PBGC reporting requirements

### Timing Considerations
- Transaction typically takes 6-12 months
- Best executed when plan is well-funded
- Interest rate environment impacts pricing

### Pricing Factors
- Current interest rates
- Plan demographics and mortality experience
- Asset portfolio characteristics
- Competitive environment

## Products

### Portfolio Protected Buy-Out
- Premium deposited into separate account
- Assets support payment obligations
- Claims-paying ability of PICA backs obligations

### Portfolio Protected Buy-In
- Similar structure to buy-out
- Contract held as plan asset
- Separate account structure available
