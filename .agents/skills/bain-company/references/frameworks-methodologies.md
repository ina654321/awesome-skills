# Bain Frameworks & Methodologies

## Core Problem-Solving Approach

### 1. Issue Trees (MECE Structure)

Issue trees break complex problems into manageable, testable components.

**MECE Principle:**
- **M**utually **E**xclusive: No overlap between categories
- **C**ollectively **E**xhaustive: All possibilities covered

**Types of Issue Trees:**

| Type | Purpose | Example Use Case |
|------|---------|------------------|
| Problem Tree | Find root causes | "Why are profits declining?" |
| Solution Tree | Evaluate options | "How should we enter the European market?" |
| Decision Tree | Guide choices | "Should we acquire Company X?" |

**Example: Profitability Issue Tree**
```
Profit Decline
├── Revenue Decline
│   ├── Volume Decline
│   │   ├── Market shrinkage
│   │   ├── Share loss
│   │   └── Customer churn
│   └── Price Decline
│       ├── Competitive pressure
│       ├── Product mix shift
│       └── Discounting
└── Cost Increase
    ├── Variable Cost Increase
    │   ├── Input price inflation
    │   ├── Supplier issues
    │   └── Efficiency loss
    └── Fixed Cost Increase
        ├── Capacity expansion
        ├── Overhead growth
        └── One-time items
```

### 2. Hypothesis-Driven Problem Solving

**The Process:**
1. **Formulate Hypothesis** - Based on experience and preliminary data
2. **Build Issue Tree** - Structure to test the hypothesis
3. **Prioritize Analysis** - Focus on critical branches
4. **Gather Data** - Prove or disprove hypothesis
5. **Synthesize & Iterate** - Refine hypothesis based on findings

**Example Hypothesis:**
> "The client can improve EBITDA by $50M through procurement optimization ($25M), operational efficiency ($15M), and pricing improvements ($10M)."

### 3. The Pyramid Principle

Structured communication methodology:

**Structure:**
1. **Answer/Conclusion** (Top of pyramid)
2. **Supporting Arguments** (Key points)
3. **Evidence/Data** (Foundation)

**SCQA Framework:**
- **S**ituation: Context and background
- **C**omplication: The problem or challenge
- **Q**uestion: What needs to be decided?
- **A**nswer: The recommendation

### 4. 80/20 Analysis (Pareto Principle)

Focus on the vital few drivers:
- Identify which 20% of efforts drive 80% of impact
- Prioritize high-leverage opportunities
- Avoid "boiling the ocean"

**Application:**
```
Revenue Analysis:
- 20% of customers → 80% of revenue
- 20% of products → 80% of profit
- 20% of markets → 80% of growth
```

### Net Promoter System®

**The Ultimate Question:**
> "How likely are you to recommend [company] to a friend or colleague?" (0-10 scale)

**Categories:**
- **Promoters (9-10):** Loyal enthusiasts who drive growth
- **Passives (7-8):** Satisfied but unenthusiastic; vulnerable
- **Detractors (0-6):** Unhappy customers who can damage brand

**NPS Calculation:**
```
NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors
```

**NPS Prism®:**
- Industry benchmarking database
- Competitive NPS comparisons
- Journey-level diagnostics

## Analytical Tools

### Financial Analysis

**ROIC Tree:**
```
ROIC = NOPAT / Invested Capital

NOPAT = Revenue × Operating Margin
Invested Capital = Working Capital + Fixed Assets

Drivers:
├── Revenue Growth
├── Gross Margin
├── SG&A Efficiency
├── Working Capital Turns
└── Fixed Asset Turns
```

### Market Analysis

**Market Sizing:**
```
TAM = Total Addressable Market (100%)
SAM = Serviceable Addressable Market (reachable)
SOM = Serviceable Obtainable Market (realistic share)
```

**Competitive Positioning:**
- Market share trends
- Relative cost position
- Differentiation factors
- Customer switching costs
