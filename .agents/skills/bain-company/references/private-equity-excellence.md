## Due Diligence Services

### Commercial Due Diligence (CDD)

**Purpose:** Assess market attractiveness and target's competitive position

**Key Questions:**
1. Is the market attractive? (size, growth, profitability)
2. Is the target well-positioned? (share, differentiation, trends)
3. Can the target achieve its business plan?
4. What are the key risks and upsides?

**Analysis Framework:**
```
Market Assessment
├── Market Size & Growth
│   ├── Historical trends
│   ├── Growth drivers
│   └── Future outlook
├── Competitive Dynamics
│   ├── Market concentration
│   ├── Competitive advantages
│   └── Entry barriers
├── Customer Dynamics
│   ├── Switching costs
│   ├── Buying power
│   └── Loyalty/stickiness
└── Profitability
    ├── Industry margins
    ├── Value chain economics
    └── Sustainability
```

### Operational Due Diligence (ODD)

**Purpose:** Validate cost and operational improvement potential

**Focus Areas:**
| Function | Key Questions |
|----------|---------------|
| Procurement | Supplier concentration, contract terms, savings potential |
| Operations | Efficiency gaps, automation opportunities, quality |
| Supply Chain | Working capital, logistics costs, inventory turns |
| SG&A | Organizational efficiency, overhead structure |
| Capex | Maintenance vs. growth, efficiency investments |

**Common Findings:**
- Procurement: 8-12% savings potential typical
- Operations: 10-20% productivity improvement possible
- SG&A: 15-25% reduction often achievable

### Technology Due Diligence

**Purpose:** Assess technology risks and digital opportunities

**Key Areas:**
- IT infrastructure scalability
- Cybersecurity posture
- Technical debt assessment
- Digital product capabilities
- AI/data maturity
- Technology team quality

## Due Diligence Best Practices

### Red Flags

**Market/Commercial:**
- Declining market share in growing market
- Customer concentration (top 5 > 50% of revenue)
- Low switching costs with unhappy customers
- Technology disruption risk

**Operational:**
- Cost structure significantly above benchmarks
- Underinvestment in maintenance
- Key person dependencies
- Regulatory/compliance issues

**Financial:**
- Working capital trends deteriorating
- Quality of earnings concerns
- Unsustainable pricing
- Underfunded liabilities

### Due Diligence Checklist

**Week 1-2: Data Room & Initial Assessment**
- [ ] Review financial statements (3-5 years)
- [ ] Analyze customer concentration
- [ ] Map competitive landscape
- [ ] Identify key hypotheses to test

**Week 3-4: Primary Research**
- [ ] Customer interviews (15-20)
- [ ] Expert calls (industry, competitors)
- [ ] Management presentations
- [ ] Site visits

**Week 5-6: Analysis & Synthesis**
- [ ] Financial modeling (base, bull, bear cases)
- [ ] Benchmark analysis
- [ ] Risk assessment
- [ ] Value creation plan outline

**Week 7-8: Final Reporting**
- [ ] Investment committee presentation
- [ ] Key risk and mitigation plan
- [ ] Go/no-go recommendation
- [ ] Negotiation support

## Exit Preparation

### Exit Readiness Framework

**Strategic Positioning:**
- Clear growth narrative
- Demonstrated value creation
- Sustainable competitive advantages
- Strong management team

**Financial Preparation:**
- Audited financials
- Clean working capital position
- Normalized EBITDA calculation
- Forward projections with clear assumptions

**Operational Excellence:**
- Key initiatives completed
- Performance tracking systems
- Risk mitigation
- Succession planning

### Exit Options Analysis

| Exit Route | Timing | Typical Multiple | Key Considerations |
|------------|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| Strategic Sale | 4-6 years | Premium (strategic value) | Synergies, cultural fit |
| Sponsor-to-Sponsor | 3-5 years | Market | Growth story, platform potential |
| IPO | 5-7 years | Market + liquidity | Scale, profitability, market conditions |
| Recap | 3-4 years | Leverage-dependent | Continued growth, partial liquidity |
