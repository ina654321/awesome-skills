# MetLife Group Benefits Guide

## Group Life Insurance

### Basic Group Term Life

**Overview:**
Employer-paid term life insurance providing financial protection to beneficiaries of deceased employees.

**Typical Coverage Levels:**
- Flat amount: $10,000 - $50,000
- Salary multiple: 1x to 3x annual earnings
- Combination: Base amount + salary multiple

**Key Features:**
- Guaranteed issue (no medical questions) during initial enrollment
- Portable coverage options available (conversion to individual policy)
- Accelerated death benefits (terminal illness)
- Coverage continues during disability

**Eligibility:**
- Full-time employees (typically 30+ hours/week)
- Waiting period: Often first of month following hire or 30-90 days

### Supplemental/Voluntary Life

**Employee-Paid Options:**
- Spouse coverage: Typically $10,000 - $250,000
- Dependent children: Typically $5,000 - $25,000
- Employee buy-up: Additional multiples of salary

**Evidence of Insurability (EOI):**
- Required for late entrants
- Required for amounts above guaranteed issue limits
- Medical underwriting via questionnaire

### Group Universal Life (GUL)

**Features:**
- Permanent life insurance through workplace
- Cash value accumulation
- Portability (maintain if leave employer)
- Flexible premiums
- Loan provisions

**Target:** Employees wanting permanent coverage with group pricing

### Group Variable Universal Life (GVUL)

**Features:**
- Permanent coverage with investment options
- Self-directed investment accounts
- Market-based cash value growth
- Higher risk/reward than GUL

## Group Disability Insurance

### Short-Term Disability (STD)

**Purpose:** Income replacement for temporary disabilities (typically up to 26 weeks)

**Key Terms:**
| Feature | Common Options |
|---------|----------------|
| Elimination Period | 0, 7, 14 days |
| Benefit Duration | 13, 26, 52 weeks |
| Benefit Percentage | 50%, 60%, 66.67% of pre-disability earnings |
| Maximum Weekly Benefit | $1,000 - $2,500+ |

**Covered Conditions:**
- Illness (non-occupational)
- Injury (off-the-job)
- Pregnancy/maternity (typically 6-8 weeks)

### Long-Term Disability (LTD)

**Purpose:** Income replacement for extended disabilities (typically 2 years to retirement)

**Key Terms:**
| Feature | Common Options |
|---------|----------------|
| Elimination Period | 90, 180, 365 days (must coordinate with STD) |
| Benefit Duration | 2 years, 5 years, to age 65 |
| Benefit Percentage | 50%, 60%, 66.67% of pre-disability earnings |
| Maximum Monthly Benefit | $5,000 - $25,000+ |
| Definition of Disability | Own occupation (2 years) → Any occupation |

**Key Provisions:**
- Partial disability benefits
- Cost-of-living adjustments (COLA)
- Survivor benefits
- Return-to-work incentives
- Rehabilitation provisions

## Group Dental Insurance

### Preferred Dentist Program (PDP)

**Network:**
- 500,000+ access points nationwide
- Largest dental network in U.S.
- SpotLite on Oral Health℠ quality designation

**Plan Types:**

#### PDP (DHMO-style)
- Primary dentist selection required
- Lower premiums
- Predetermined copayments
- No annual maximums
- No deductibles

#### PDP Plus (PPO-style)
- Freedom to see any dentist
- Higher in-network reimbursement
- Annual maximums (typically $1,000-$2,500)
- Deductibles (typically $50 individual/$150 family)
- Coinsurance structure

### Coverage Categories

| Category | Typical Coverage | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-------|
| **Preventive** | 100% | Exams, cleanings, X-rays (usually 2/year) |
| **Basic** | 70-80% | Fillings, extractions, periodontal treatment |
| **Major** | 50% | Crowns, bridges, dentures, implants |
| **Orthodontia** | 50% | Children and/or adults; lifetime max |

### Plan Design Options

**Dual Option Plans:**
- Offer both PDP and PDP Plus
- Employees choose based on needs
- Available for groups with 25+ eligible employees

**TakeAlong Dental®:**
- Portable individual dental for terminated employees
- Not COBRA coverage
- Simplified enrollment

## Health Savings & Spending Accounts

### Health Savings Accounts (HSA)

**Features:**
- Must be paired with HSA-qualified high-deductible health plan (HDHP)
- Triple tax advantage: Pre-tax contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for qualified expenses
- Employee and employer contributions
- Portable (employee owns account)
- Investment options at higher balances

**2024 Contribution Limits:**
- Individual: $4,150
- Family: $8,300
- Catch-up (55+): Additional $1,000

### Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)

**Healthcare FSA:**
- Pre-tax contributions
- "Use it or lose it" (with $640 rollover option or 2.5 month grace period)
- 2024 limit: $3,200

**Dependent Care FSA:**
- Childcare and elder care expenses
- 2024 limit: $5,000 ($2,500 if married filing separately)

**Limited Purpose FSA:**
- Dental and vision only (pairs with HSA)

## Funding Mechanisms

### Fully-Insured

**Characteristics:**
- Fixed monthly premium
- Carrier assumes risk
- Predictable budgeting
- State premium taxes apply
- ACA fees apply
- Rate guarantees available (1-3 years)

**Best For:**
- Small to mid-size employers
- Employers seeking budget predictability
- Organizations without claims experience data

### Self-Funded (Self-Insured)

**Characteristics:**
- Employer pays actual claims + administrative fee
- Stop-loss insurance protects against catastrophic claims
- Greater plan design flexibility
- Exempt from state mandates (ERISA preemption)
- Cash flow advantages
- Claims experience transparency

**Stop-Loss Types:**
- **Specific:** Protects against individual catastrophic claims (e.g., $100,000 deductible)
- **Aggregate:** Protects against total claims exceeding expected levels (e.g., 125% of expected)

**Best For:**
- Large employers (500+ typically)
- Organizations with stable workforce
- Employers wanting plan control

### Level-Funded

**Hybrid Approach:**
- Fixed monthly payment
- If claims lower than expected, refund possible
- If claims higher, stop-loss protection
- Simplified administration

**Best For:**
- Mid-size employers (50-500)
- Transitioning from fully-insured
