# Brighthouse Financial Transition Guide

## Overview

On August 4, 2017, MetLife completed the spin-off of Brighthouse Financial, Inc. (NASDAQ: BHF), creating two independent, publicly-traded companies. This was the most significant restructuring in MetLife's recent history and fundamentally changed the company's market focus.

## Strategic Rationale

### Why the Spinoff Occurred

**MetLife's Transformation Goals:**
1. Become a less capital-intensive company
2. Generate stronger free cash flow
3. Focus on workplace/group benefits
4. Simplify operations and reporting
5. Create distinct value propositions for investors

**Statement from Steven A. Kandarian (Former MetLife Chairman, President & CEO):**
> "The spin-off is the centerpiece of MetLife's continuing transformation into a less capital intensive company with stronger free cash flow. MetLife's core businesses – employee benefits, protection and fee-based retail products outside of the United States, and our growing asset management arm – position the company well for profitable growth."

## What Remained with MetLife

### Retained Businesses

| Segment | Description |
|---------|-------------|

| **Retirement & Income Solutions** | Pension risk transfers, structured settlements, institutional annuities |

| **MetLife Holdings** | Legacy individual products in run-off |
| **MetLife Investment Management** | Institutional asset management |
| **Auto & Home** | Personal lines property/casualty (later divested to Farmers) |

### Post-Spinoff MetLife Focus

**Primary Markets:**
1. Workplace benefits (group insurance)
2. Institutional retirement solutions
3. International insurance (40+ countries)
4. Asset management

**No Longer Offered:**
- Individual retail life insurance (new sales) in U.S.
- Individual annuities to retail customers
- Individual long-term care insurance (new sales)

## Transaction Details

### Distribution Mechanics

**Record Date:** July 19, 2017  
**Distribution Date:** August 4, 2017  
**Trading Start:** August 7, 2017

**Exchange Ratio:**
- MetLife shareholders received 1 share of Brighthouse Financial for every 11 shares of MetLife held
- Fractional shares paid in cash
- Approximately 96.8 million Brighthouse shares distributed

**Post-Separation Ownership:**
- MetLife retained 19.2% stake initially
- Divested remaining interest in 2018 through debt-for-equity exchange

## Impact on Policyholders

### Individual Life Insurance Policyholders

**Policies Issued Before August 4, 2017:**

| Policy Type | Servicing Entity |
|-------------|------------------|
| Individual Life (most) | Brighthouse Financial |
| Some policies | MetLife Holdings (run-off) |
| Annuities | Brighthouse Financial |
| Long-Term Care (existing) | Brighthouse Financial |

**Policies Issued After August 4, 2017:**
- Individual life/annuities: Brighthouse Financial only
- Group benefits: MetLife

### Group Benefits Participants

**No Impact:**
- Employer-sponsored group life
- Group disability
- Group dental
- Group vision
- All workplace benefits

These remained with MetLife and continued uninterrupted.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is my MetLife policy now with Brighthouse?**  
A: If it's individual life or annuity issued before 2017, likely yes. If it's group benefits through your employer, it's with MetLife.

**Q: Can I buy a new individual life policy from MetLife?**  
A: No. MetLife no longer sells individual retail life insurance in the U.S. You would need to contact Brighthouse or another carrier.

**Q: What happened to my MetLife agent?**  
A: Many career agents transferred to Brighthouse or became independent. Contact your agent directly to confirm their current affiliation.

**Q: Are MetLife and Brighthouse the same company?**  
A: No. They are completely separate, independent companies since August 2017.

**Q: I have both a MetLife group policy and an old individual policy. Where do I go for each?**  
A: Group policy = MetLife. Individual policy = Check if it says Brighthouse or MetLife on your statement; likely Brighthouse if issued before 2017.
