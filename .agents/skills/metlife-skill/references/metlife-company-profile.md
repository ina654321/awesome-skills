# MetLife Company Profile

## Overview

MetLife, Inc. (NYSE: MET) is one of the world's leading financial services companies, providing insurance, annuities, employee benefits, and asset management to individual and institutional customers. With operations in more than 40 markets, MetLife holds leading positions in the United States, Latin America, Asia, Europe, and the Middle East.

## Business Segments

### 1. U.S. Group Benefits
- **Products:** Group life, disability, dental, vision, accident, critical illness
- **Position:** Market leader in group dental; top 3 in group life and disability
- **2024 Performance:** $6.2B adjusted premiums, fees and other revenues; $416M adjusted earnings

### 2. Retirement & Income Solutions (RIS)
- **Products:** Pension risk transfers, structured settlements, institutional income annuities
- **Growth:** 45% sales increase in 2024 driven by pension risk transfer deals
- **2024 Performance:** $3.6B adjusted premiums, fees and other revenues; $386M adjusted earnings

### 3. Asia
- **Markets:** Japan, Korea, India, China, Hong Kong, Vietnam
- **2024 Performance:** $1.6B adjusted premiums; $443M adjusted earnings (up 50%)
- **AUM:** $130B general account assets

### 4. Latin America
- **Markets:** Chile, Mexico, Brazil, Argentina, Colombia, Uruguay
- **2024 Performance:** $1.5B adjusted premiums; $233M adjusted earnings

### 5. EMEA
- **Markets:** UK, Spain, Poland, Turkey, UAE, Egypt
- **Focus:** Longevity reinsurance, regional diversification

### 6. MetLife Holdings
- **Purpose:** Legacy individual products in run-off
- **Products:** Remaining individual life, long-term care

## The Brighthouse Financial Transition

**What Happened:**
In 2017, MetLife spun off its U.S. retail individual life insurance and annuity business as Brighthouse Financial, Inc. (NASDAQ: BHF).

**Why:**
- Transform into less capital-intensive company
- Stronger free cash flow
- Focus on workplace/group benefits and international

**What Went to Brighthouse:**
- Individual life insurance (whole, universal, variable, term)
- Individual annuities
- Long-term care (existing policies)
- Retail distribution network

**What Stayed with MetLife:**
- Group benefits (life, disability, dental, vision)
- Retirement & Income Solutions
- International operations
- MetLife Investment Management
- Auto & Home (since divested)
