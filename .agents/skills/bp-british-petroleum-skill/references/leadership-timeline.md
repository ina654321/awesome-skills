# BP Leadership Timeline

## Chief Executives Since 1990

### Robert Horton (1990-1992)
- **Tenure:** March 1990 - 1992
- **Role:** Chairman and CEO
- **Context:** Led during early 1990s oil price shock
- **Outcome:** Left during restructuring

### David Simon (1992-1995)
- **Tenure:** 1992 - 1995
- **Context:** Stabilized company after Horton departure

### John Browne (1995-2007)
- **Tenure:** 1995 - May 2007
- **Major Achievements:**
  - Transformed BP into global supermajor
  - Acquired Amoco (1998) and ARCO (2000)
  - "Beyond Petroleum" rebrand
  - Led diversification into renewables
- **Controversies:**
  - Texas City refinery explosion (2005)
  - Prudhoe Bay pipeline spill (2006)
  - Personal scandal led to early retirement
- **Legacy:** Created modern BP scale but safety culture concerns

### Tony Hayward (2007-2010)
- **Tenure:** May 2007 - October 2010
- **Context:** Promised to improve safety focus
- **Crisis:** Deepwater Horizon (April 2010)
  - 11 workers killed
  - Worst offshore oil spill in US history
  - "I'd like my life back" comment damaged reputation
- **Outcome:** Resigned October 2010

### Bob Dudley (2010-2020)
- **Tenure:** October 2010 - February 2020
- **Distinction:** First American CEO of BP
- **Challenge:** Rebuild company after Deepwater Horizon
- **Key Actions:**
  - Managed $65B+ Deepwater costs
  - Divested non-core assets
  - Restored dividend
  - Rosneft investment (2013)
  - Navigated oil price crash (2014-2016)
- **Legacy:** Restored stability but delayed transition strategy

### Bernard Looney (2020-2023)
- **Tenure:** February 2020 - September 2023
- **Strategy:** "Reimagining energy"
  - 50GW renewables target by 2030
  - 40% oil/gas production cut by 2030
  - Net zero by 2050
- **Major Deals:**
  - Full Lightsource BP acquisition
  - Offshore wind expansion
  - EV charging network growth
- **Resignation:** Sept 2023 for failing to disclose past relationships
- **Legacy:** Bold transition vision but execution challenges and premature end

### Murray Auchincloss (2023-2025)
- **Tenure:** 
  - Interim: September 2023
  - Permanent: January 2024
  - Departed: December 18, 2025
- **Background:** 30+ years at BP; former CFO under Looney
- **Challenge:** Lead strategic reset
- **Key Actions:**
  - February 2025 "strategic reset"
  - $20B divestment program
  - Cost reduction ($2B target by 2026)
  - Oil/gas investment increase
  - Transition investment reduction
- **Departure:** Board sought "appropriate leader" to accelerate delivery; stepped down Dec 2025
- **Legacy:** Implemented necessary reset but couldn't complete transformation

### Carol Howle (Interim 2025-2026)
- **Role:** Interim CEO (Dec 18, 2025 - April 1, 2026)
- **Background:** EVP Supply, Trading & Shipping; 25 years at BP
- **Key Actions:**
  - Announced Castrol 65% sale to Stonepeak for $6B (Dec 2025)
  - Managed transition to permanent leadership
- **Future:** Returns to EVP role under O'Neill

### Meg O'Neill (2026-)
- **Start Date:** April 1, 2026
- **Background:**
  - 23 years at ExxonMobil (technical, operational, leadership)
  - Woodside Energy CEO (2021-2025)
  - Led BHP Petroleum acquisition
  - Grew Woodside to largest ASX-listed energy company
- **Distinctions:**
  - First female supermajor CEO
  - First external BP CEO in 116 years
- **Expected Focus:**
  - Execute strategic reset
  - Capital discipline
  - Operational excellence
  - Accelerate portfolio transformation

## Strategic Alignment with Leadership

| CEO | Primary Strategy | Outcome |
|-----|-----------------|---------|
| Browne | Diversification (Beyond Petroleum) | Mixed; safety failures |
| Hayward | Safety focus | Cut short by Deepwater |
| Dudley | Restoration/Stabilization | Successful recovery |
| Looney | Energy transition | Incomplete; poor returns |
| Auchincloss | Strategic reset | Incomplete; stepped down |
| O'Neill | TBD - likely reset continuation | Pending |

## Key Insights

1. **Crisis-Driven Changes:** Major leadership shifts follow crises (Deepwater Horizon, relationships scandal, strategy failure)
2. **Internal vs. External:** First external CEO (O'Neill) after 116 years of insiders
3. **Tenure Shortening:** Average tenure declining due to pressure for rapid results
4. **Strategic Whiplash:** Multiple strategy pivots create organizational and investor uncertainty
5. **Gender Milestone:** O'Neill appointment breaks supermajor glass ceiling
