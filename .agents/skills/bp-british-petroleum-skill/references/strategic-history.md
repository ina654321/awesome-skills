# BP Strategic History

## The "Beyond Petroleum" Era (1998-2007)

### Context
- **CEO:** John Browne (1995-2007)
- **Major Acquisitions:** Amoco (1998), ARCO (2000)
- **Rebrand:** "BP" no longer stood for "British Petroleum" but "Beyond Petroleum"
- **Solar Investment:** BP Solar became major renewable player

### Outcome
- Greenwashing criticism undermined credibility
- Safety issues (Texas City 2005, Prudhoe Bay 2006) damaged reputation
- Deepwater Horizon (2010) effectively ended this strategy

## The Dudley Era: Restoration (2010-2020)

### Context
- **CEO:** Bob Dudley (first American CEO)
- **Challenge:** Rebuild after Deepwater Horizon ($65B+ costs)
- **Focus:** Safety, operational excellence, core hydrocarbons

### Key Actions
- Divested non-core assets to pay Deepwater liabilities
- Restored dividend (suspended 2010, restored 2011)
- Rosneft investment (2013) provided growth but geopolitical risk
- Returned to profitability but transition debate shelved

## The Looney Era: "Reimagining Energy" (2020-2023)

### Context
- **CEO:** Bernard Looney (appointed Feb 2020)
- **Slogan:** "Reimagining energy"
- **Ambition:** Transform BP into integrated energy company

### Commitments (August 2020)
| Target | Original Goal |
|--------|--------------|
| Renewables Capacity | 50GW by 2030 |
| Renewables Investment | $5B/year by 2030 |
| Oil/Gas Production Cut | 40% by 2030 (from 2019) |
| Net Zero | Operations by 2050 |

### Progress by 2023
- Acquired Lightsource BP (solar)
- Expanded offshore wind pipeline
- Grew EV charging network to 22,000+ points
- Started hydrogen and CCS projects
- **Problem:** Share price underperformed, returns uncertain

### Resignation (Sept 2023)
- Left for failing to disclose past relationships
- Transition strategy incomplete
- Created leadership vacuum

## The Auchincloss Era: Strategic Reset (2023-2025)

### Context
- **CEO:** Murray Auchincloss (CFO promoted Sept 2023, permanent Jan 2024)
- **Pressure:** Activist investor Elliott Management (5% stake)
- **Reality:** "We went too far, too fast"

### February 2025 Reset Announcements
| Area | Before Reset | After Reset |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Oil/Gas Investment | ~$8B/year | $10B/year |
| Transition Investment | $5B/year target | $1.5-2B/year |
| Production Target | Cut 40% by 2030 | Grow to 2.3-2.5M boe/d |
| Renewables Focus | Build 50GW | Partner for growth |

### Rationale
- Lower returns from transition investments than expected
- Energy security priorities post-Ukraine
- Investor demand for cash returns over growth
- Competitive underperformance vs Exxon, Shell

### Divestment Program
- Target: $20B by 2027
- Castrol (65%): $6B (Dec 2025)
- US onshore wind: Exited
- Netherlands mobility: Sold
- Lightsource BP: Seeking partners

### Departure (Dec 2025)
- Auchincloss stepped down after less than 2 years
- Board wanted "appropriate leader" to accelerate delivery
- Remained in advisory role through 2026

## The O'Neill Era: Beginning (2026-)

### Context
- **CEO:** Meg O'Neill (effective April 1, 2026)
- **Background:** ExxonMobil (23 years), Woodside CEO (2021-2025)
- **Interim:** Carol Howle (Dec 2025 - April 2026)

### Expected Priorities
- Continue strategic reset execution
- Accelerate divestments and debt reduction
- Improve operational reliability
- Strengthen investor communication
- Bring external discipline and capital efficiency focus

### Historical Significance
- First female supermajor CEO
- First external BP CEO in 116 years
- Brings pure upstream, capital-disciplined mindset

## Strategic Cycle Analysis

```
1998-2007: Diversification (Beyond Petroleum)
    ↓ Safety failures, financial underperformance
2010-2020: Restoration (Core Hydrocarbons)
    ↓ Climate pressure, transition enthusiasm
2020-2023: Transformation (Reimagining Energy)
    ↓ Poor returns, investor pressure
2025-2030: Reset (Disciplined Hydrocarbons + Selective Transition)
```

## Lessons Learned

1. **Speed vs. Returns:** Looney strategy prioritized speed of transition; Auchincloss reset prioritizes capital returns
2. **Energy Security:** Geopolitical events (Ukraine) reshaped priorities
3. **Execution Matters:** BP's transition investments underperformed vs expectations
4. **Investor Alignment:** Supermajor investors prioritize dividends/buybacks over transition leadership
5. **Optionality Value:** Reset maintains transition optionality without capital intensity
