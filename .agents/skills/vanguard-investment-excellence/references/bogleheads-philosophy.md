# Bogleheads Investment Philosophy

## Overview

The Bogleheads philosophy is named after John C. "Jack" Bogle, founder of Vanguard Group and creator of the first index fund available to individual investors (Vanguard 500 Index Fund, 1976). This approach emphasizes simplicity, low costs, broad diversification, and long-term discipline.

---

## The 11 Bogleheads Principles

### 1. Live Below Your Means

**Core Concept:** Spending less than you earn is the foundation of wealth building.

**Practical Application:**
- Save at least 15-20% of gross income for retirement
- Automate savings before discretionary spending
- Avoid lifestyle inflation as income grows
- Track expenses to identify waste

**Why It Matters:** No investment strategy can overcome inadequate savings. The best portfolio cannot save someone who spends everything they earn.

---

### 2. Develop a Workable Plan

**Core Concept:** Create a comprehensive financial plan tailored to your goals.

**Components:**
- Define specific, measurable financial goals
- Establish emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)
- Determine asset allocation based on goals and risk tolerance
- Plan for taxes, insurance, and estate needs
- Review and adjust annually

**Key Questions:**
- When do you want to retire?
- What does retirement look like?
- How much will you need?
- What is your current savings rate?

---

### 3. Never Bear Too Much or Too Little Risk

**Core Concept:** Asset allocation should match your ability, willingness, and need to take risk.

**Risk Capacity Factors:**
- Time horizon (longer = more risk capacity)
- Income stability
- Liquidity needs
- Other assets/resources

**Risk Tolerance Factors:**
- Emotional reaction to market drops
- Sleep-at-night factor
- Past behavior during volatility

**The "Need" Factor:**
- High savings rate → Can accept lower returns/less risk
- Low savings rate → May need higher returns/more risk

**Common Allocation Rule:**
```
Stock % = 110 - Age (aggressive)
Stock % = 100 - Age (moderate)
Stock % = 90 - Age (conservative)
```

---

### 4. Invest Early and Often

**Core Concept:** Time in the market beats timing the market. Compounding works best with time.

**The Cost of Waiting:**
```
Starting at 25 vs. 35 (assuming $500/month, 7% return):
- Age 25 start: ~$1.2M at age 65
- Age 35 start: ~$570K at age 65
- Cost of waiting: $630,000
```

**Dollar-Cost Averaging:**
- Invest fixed amounts regularly regardless of market conditions
- Reduces impact of volatility
- Removes emotion from investing
- "Pay yourself first" automation

---

### 5. Diversify

**Core Concept:** Spread investments across asset classes to reduce risk without sacrificing expected returns.

**Dimensions of Diversification:**
- **Asset Classes:** Stocks, bonds, real estate, commodities
- **Geography:** U.S., developed international, emerging markets
- **Company Size:** Large-cap, mid-cap, small-cap
- **Style:** Growth, value, blend
- **Sectors:** Technology, healthcare, consumer, etc.

**The Three-Fund Portfolio:**
```
1. Total U.S. Stock Market (VTI/VTSAX)
2. Total International Stock (VXUS/VTIAX)
3. Total Bond Market (BND/VBTLX)
```

This simple portfolio provides exposure to:
- ~10,000+ stocks globally
- All sectors and company sizes
- Multiple currencies and economies

---

### 6. Invest with Simplicity

**Core Concept:** Complex portfolios often lead to mistakes, higher costs, and unnecessary maintenance.

**Benefits of Simplicity:**
- Easier to understand and maintain
- Lower costs
- Less rebalancing complexity
- Reduced chance of performance chasing
- Easier for heirs to manage

**Sample Simple Portfolios:**

| Portfolio | Allocation | Funds |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| One-Fund | 100% | Target-Date or LifeStrategy |
| Two-Fund | 60/40 | VTI + BND |
| Three-Fund | 60/20/20 | VTI + VXUS + BND |
| Four-Fund | Add REITs | Above + VNQ |

---

### 7. Use Index Funds When Possible

**Core Concept:** Index funds consistently outperform most actively managed funds after fees.

**SPIVA Research (S&P Indices vs. Active):**
- Over 10 years: ~85% of active managers underperform
- Over 15 years: ~90% of active managers underperform
- The longer the horizon, the worse active performance gets

**Why Indexing Works:**
- Low costs (no research teams, lower turnover)
- Broad diversification
- No manager risk (style drift, departure)
- Tax efficiency
- Guaranteed market-average returns (which beat most)

**When Active Might Make Sense:**
- Specific bond strategies
- Niche markets (small-cap value, emerging markets)
- Personal enjoyment/education
- Limited to <10% of portfolio

---

### 8. Minimize Costs

**Core Concept:** Fees compound against you. Every dollar paid in fees is a dollar not compounding.

**Expense Ratio Impact:**
```
$100,000 invested over 30 years at 7% gross return:

0.03% ER: $761,226 (VTI/VOO)
0.50% ER: $661,437 (-$99,789)
1.00% ER: $574,349 (-$186,877)
1.50% ER: $511,204 (-$250,022)
```

**Hidden Costs to Watch:**
- Loads (front-end or back-end sales charges)
- 12b-1 fees (marketing)
- High turnover (tax costs)
- Advisory fees (AUM-based vs. flat-fee)
- Trading costs

**Vanguard Advantage:**
- Average ER: 0.07%
- Industry average: 0.44%
- Savings compound over decades

---

### 9. Invest for the Long Term

**Core Concept:** Ignore short-term market noise. Focus on decades, not days.

**Market Volatility is Normal:**
- Average intra-year decline: ~14%
- Bear markets (20%+ decline): Occur every 3-5 years
- Corrections (10%+ decline): Occur annually on average
- Despite this, markets trend upward over time

**Historical U.S. Market Returns (1926-2023):**
- Best year: +54%
- Worst year: -43%
- Average year: ~10%
- Positive years: ~75% of time

**Long-Term Perspective:**
- 10-year periods: Positive ~95% of time
- 20-year periods: Positive 100% of time
- Volatility decreases with time horizon

**The "Stay the Course" Mantra:**
"Don't just do something, stand there!" — Jack Bogle

---

### 10. Stay the Course

**Core Concept:** Maintain your investment plan through market volatility.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Panic selling during downturns
- Performance chasing (buying after rallies)
- Market timing attempts
- Abandoning strategy during underperformance
- Checking portfolio too frequently

**Behavioral Techniques:**
- Automate investments (remove decision-making)
- Rebalance on schedule, not emotion
- Limit portfolio checks (quarterly or annually)
- Tune out financial news
- Remember your time horizon

**The Rebalancing Bonus:**
- Selling high, buying low mechanically
- Forces contrarian behavior
- Maintains risk level
- Works best with volatile, uncorrelated assets

---

### 11. Know What You Can Control

**Core Concept:** Focus on controllable factors; accept market uncertainty.

**What You CAN Control:**
- Savings rate
- Asset allocation
- Costs/fees
- Tax efficiency
- Behavior/emotions
- Time horizon (by starting early)

**What You CANNOT Control:**
- Market returns
- Interest rates
- Economic conditions
- Political events
- What other investors do
- News and media narratives

**The Serenity Prayer of Investing:**
"Grant me the serenity to accept the market returns I cannot change,
courage to change the allocation I can,
and wisdom to know the difference."

## Common Bogleheads Portfolio Examples

### Classic Three-Fund Portfolio

```
60% VTI (U.S. Total Stock)
30% VXUS (Total International)
10% BND (Total Bond)

Weighted ER: ~0.05%
Holdings: 10,000+ securities
```

### Two-Fund Ultra-Simple

```
80% VT (Total World Stock)
20% BND (Total Bond)

Weighted ER: ~0.06%
Global diversification in two funds
```

### Four-Fund with REITs

```
55% VTI
25% VXUS
10% BND
10% VNQ (REIT)

Adds real estate exposure
Slightly more complex rebalancing
```

### Age-Based Glide (DIY)

```
Age 25: 90/10 (stock/bond)
Age 35: 80/20
Age 45: 70/30
Age 55: 60/40
Age 65: 50/50 or 40/60
```

## Bogleheads vs. Other Approaches

| Aspect | Bogleheads | Day Traders | Active Managers |
|--------|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Time Horizon | Decades | Minutes/Hours | Quarters/Years |
| Trading Frequency | Minimal | Constant | Moderate |
| Costs | Ultra-low | High (commissions/spread) | High (ER + fees) |
| Diversification | Broad | Concentrated | Varies |
| Expected Return | Market average | Unpredictable | Market minus fees |
| Success Rate | High | Very Low | Low |
| Stress Level | Low | Very High | Moderate |
