# Travelers Underwriting Principles Reference

## Core Philosophy

> "We have a disciplined approach to underwriting and risk management that emphasizes product returns and profitable growth over time rather than premium volume or market share."
> — Travelers 2024 Annual Report

### Guiding Principles

1. **Profitability First** - Combined ratio discipline over volume
2. **Risk-Adjusted Returns** - Price to specific risk characteristics
3. **Long-Term Franchise Value** - Sustainable profitability over short-term gains
4. **Granular Segmentation** - Understand risk at the individual account level
5. **Continuous Refinement** - Learn from experience and adjust quickly

## Risk Assessment Framework

### The Underwriting Cycle

```
┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐    ┌─────────────┐
│   RISK      │───▶│   PRICING   │───▶│  SELECTION  │───▶│  MONITORING │
│ IDENTIFICATION│   │   & TERMS   │    │   & BINDING │    │  & ADJUST   │
└─────────────┘    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘    └─────────────┘
       │                  │                  │                  │
       ▼                  ▼                  ▼                  ▼
  • Application      • Rate adequacy    • Risk appetite    • Loss trends
  • Inspections      • Deductibles      • Portfolio mix    • Rate changes
  • Loss runs        • Limits           • Terms review     • Exit decisions
  • Risk control     • Conditions       • Documentation    • Renewals
```

### Information Gathering

**Required Data Points:**

| Category | Data Elements |
|----------|---------------|
| **Risk Characteristics** | Operations, hazards, protection, location |
| **Loss History** | 5-year loss runs, claim details, reserve changes |
| **Financial Condition** | Financial statements, credit reports, payment history |
| **Exposure Measures** | Payroll, sales, vehicle count, property values |
| **Risk Controls** | Safety programs, maintenance, security systems |
| **Management Quality** | Experience, commitment to safety, claims handling |

### Risk Quality Classification

**Rating Categories:**

| Category | Description | Action |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| **Preferred** | Excellent risk controls, favorable loss history | Standard pricing, pursue aggressively |
| **Standard** | Acceptable risk profile, average loss history | Standard pricing, normal underwriting |
| **Substandard** | Deficient controls, adverse loss history | Surcharge pricing, enhanced terms, or decline |
| **Prohibited** | Outside appetite, excessive hazard | Decline |

## Pricing Methodology

### Rate Adequacy Components

```
Final Rate = Base Rate × Experience Modification × Risk Factor Adjustments
```

**Component Breakdown:**

1. **Expected Losses**
   - Historical loss costs by classification
   - Trend factors (frequency, severity)
   - Catastrophe loading

2. **Expense Loading**
   - Acquisition costs (commissions, premium taxes)
   - Administrative expenses
   - Claims handling costs

3. **Profit Provision**
   - Target return on equity
   - Risk load for uncertainty
   - Capital requirements

### Pricing Adjustments

**Risk Factor Modifications:**

| Factor | Potential Adjustment | Application |
|--------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Experience Rating** | ±40% | Based on actual loss history |
| **Schedule Rating** | ±25% | Risk characteristics assessment |
| **Deductible Credit** | Up to 40% | Higher deductibles = lower premium |
| **Risk Control Credit** | Up to 10% | Superior risk control programs |

### Catastrophe Loading

**Methodology:**
- Model-based catastrophe loss estimates
- Geographic concentration analysis
- Reinsurance cost allocation
- Historical experience adjustment

## Terms and Conditions

### Deductibles

**Purpose:**
- Transfer small loss frequency to insured
- Reduce moral hazard
- Lower premium cost
- Align incentives

**Common Structures:**

| Line of Business | Typical Deductible Range |
|------------------|-------------------------|
| Workers' Compensation | $0 (statutory) to SIR structures |
| Commercial Auto | $500 to $25,000 |
| General Liability | $0 to $25,000 |
| Commercial Property | $1,000 to $500,000+ |
| Cyber Liability | $10,000 to $1M+ |

### Limits and Sublimits

**Policy Limit Considerations:**
- Adequate to protect insured's assets
- Consistent with exposure magnitude
- Reinsurance economics
- Attachment point optimization

**Common Sublimits:**
- Flood (separate limit)
- Earthquake (separate limit)
- Mold/fungus
- Debris removal
- Cyber incidents

### Conditions and Exclusions

**Risk-Specific Conditions:**
- Protective safeguards (sprinkler maintenance)
- Hot work permits
- Motor vehicle record checks
- Certificate of insurance requirements

**Common Exclusions:**
- Expected or intended injury
- Contractual liability (with exceptions)
- Professional services (separate coverage)
- Pollution (with exceptions)
- War and terrorism (with TRIA backstop)

## Portfolio Management

### Risk Appetite Framework

**Dimensions of Risk Appetite:**

1. **Product/Lines of Business**
   - Target mix percentages
   - Maximum concentration limits
   - Minimum return thresholds

2. **Geographic Exposure**
   - State concentration limits
   - Catastrophe zone management
   - International exposure caps

3. **Industry Segments**
   - Hazardous industry limits
   - Preferred class targets
   - Emerging risk evaluation

4. **Customer Size**
   - Small business appetite
   - Middle market focus
   - Large account criteria

### Monitoring and Reporting

**Monthly Reviews:**
- Premium production vs. plan
- Rate change achieved
- Retention rates
- New business quality
- Loss ratio trends

**Quarterly Deep Dives:**
- Portfolio profitability by segment
- Reserve development analysis
- Catastrophe exposure evaluation
- Competitive positioning assessment
- Action plan development

### Corrective Actions

**When Results Deviate:**

| Issue | Response |
|-------|----------|
| **Loss ratio deterioration** | Rate increase, tighter underwriting, exit |
| **Rate inadequacy** | Filing increases, tier adjustments |
| **Adverse selection** | Tighten risk selection, improve pricing |
| **Catastrophe exposure** | Reduce limits, increase deductibles, non-renew |
| **Retention decline** | Service improvements, pricing optimization |

## Underwriting Authorities

### Delegation Framework

**Authority Levels:**

| Level | Authority | Typical Role |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| **1** | Standard risks, automated pricing | Underwriting technician |
| **2** | Moderate complexity, schedule rating | Underwriter |
| **3** | Complex risks, significant modifications | Senior underwriter |
| **4** | Large accounts, program business | Underwriting manager |
| **5** | Catastrophic exposure, exceptions | VP Underwriting |

### Underwriting Exceptions

**Exception Process:**
1. Document risk characteristics
2. Explain deviation from guidelines
3. Demonstrate rate adequacy
4. Obtain appropriate approval
5. Monitor results closely
