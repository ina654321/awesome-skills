## 1. Business Insurance

**2025 Metrics:**
- Net Written Premiums: $22.7B (+3% YoY)
- Segment Income: $3.7B
- Combined Ratio: 88.5%
- Retention: 85%

### Organizational Structure

#### Select Accounts
**Target:** Small businesses (<50 employees)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Distribution** | Independent agents/brokers |
| **Products** | Guaranteed-cost policies, packaged products |
| **Underwriting** | Automated platform for quoting, binding, issuance |
| **Complex Risks** | Referred to company personnel |

**Characteristics:**
- High-volume, standardized products
- Efficient automated processing
- Focus on ease of doing business

#### Middle Market
**Target:** Mid-sized businesses (50-1,000 employees)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Distribution** | Independent agents/brokers |
| **Products** | Full line of commercial coverages |
| **Emphasis** | Guaranteed-cost programs |
| **Underwriting** | Account-specific based on risk characteristics |

**Growth (2025):**
- Middle Market: +3% growth
- Select Accounts: +4% growth
- Combined: ~75% of Business Insurance premiums

#### National Accounts
**Target:** Large corporations

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Distribution** | National/regional brokers |
| **Products** | Loss-sensitive casualty programs |
| **Structures** | Large deductible, retrospectively rated, self-insured retention |
| **Services** | Claims administration, risk management via Constitution State Services |

**Residual Market:**
- Services ~36% of total workers' compensation assigned risk market
- One of largest servicing carriers in US

#### Industry-Focused Divisions
- Construction
- Manufacturing
- Technology
- Healthcare
- Financial services
- Public sector

#### International
- United Kingdom
- Republic of Ireland
- Canada
- Corporate member of Lloyd's

### Key Product Lines

| Product | Market Position | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| Workers' Compensation | #1 in US | Core strength, strong profitability |
| Commercial Multi-Peril | Top 5 | Package policies |
| Commercial Auto | Major player | Fleet coverage |
| General Liability | Top 5 | Casualty coverage |
| Commercial Property | Top 5 | Asset protection |

### Underwriting Approach

1. **Granular Risk Selection**
   - Unique risk characteristics evaluation
   - Loss history analysis
   - Coverage needs assessment

2. **Risk Control Integration**
   - 500+ risk control consultants
   - Proactive hazard identification
   - Safety protocol implementation

3. **Pricing Sophistication**
   - Proprietary data analytics
   - Actuarial models
   - Real-time market adjustments

## 2. Bond & Specialty Insurance

**2025 Metrics:**
- Net Written Premiums: $4.3B (+4% YoY)
- Segment Income: $950M
- Combined Ratio: 81.9%
- Retention: 87%

### Product Categories

#### Surety Bonds

**Types:**
| Bond Type | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Contract Surety** | Construction performance guarantees |
| **Commercial Surety** | License/permit compliance |
| **Court Bonds** | Legal matter guarantees |
| **Fidelity Bonds** | Employee dishonesty protection |

**Underwriting Approach:**
- Three-C Analysis: Character, Capacity, Capital
- Financially-based underwriting
- Collateral requirements for higher risks
- Industry specialization

#### Management Liability

**Coverages:**
- Directors & Officers (D&O)
- Employment Practices Liability (EPL)
- Fiduciary Liability
- Crime/Fidelity

**Target:** Public and private companies, non-profits

#### Professional Liability

**Coverages:**
- Errors & Omissions (E&O)
- Cyber Liability (>20% growth in 2024)
- Technology E&O
- Media liability

### Geographic Reach

| Region | Products |
|--------|----------|
| United States | Full product suite |
| Canada | Select surety and specialty |
| UK/Ireland | Management liability, surety |
| Brazil | Surety (joint venture) |

### Competitive Advantages

1. **Market Leadership**
   - Top-tier surety market position
   - Deep expertise in complex risks

2. **Financial Strength**
   - AA- credit rating supports bonding capacity
   - Ability to write large surety commitments

3. **Expertise**
   - Specialized underwriting teams
   - Industry-specific knowledge
   - Claims handling excellence

## 3. Personal Insurance

**2025 Metrics:**
- Net Written Premiums: $17.5B (+2% YoY)
- Segment Income: $2.1B
- Combined Ratio: 92.5%

### Product Lines

#### Automobile Insurance

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Coverage** | Liability, physical damage, PIP |
| **Distribution** | Independent agents, GEICO agency partnership |
| **Pricing Factors** | Driving record, vehicle type, territory, usage |
| **Innovation** | Usage-based insurance (UBI), telematics |

**2025 Performance:**
- Higher new business in Auto
- Rate actions addressing inflation
- Telematics adoption growing

#### Homeowners Insurance

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Coverage** | Dwelling, contents, liability, additional living expenses |
| **Pricing Factors** | Construction costs, catastrophe exposure, claims history |
| **Risk Selection** | Proprietary wildfire/flood models |
| **Terms** | Deductibles, sub-limits for high-hazard perils |

**2025 Performance:**
- Continued strong renewal premium change
- Selective underwriting in high-catastrophe zones
- Rate adequacy focus

#### Other Personal Lines
- Boat/yacht insurance
- Personal umbrella liability
- Valuable items coverage
- Flood insurance (NFIP participation)

### Product Management Approach

**Integrated Discipline:**
```
Underwriting ←→ Claims ←→ Actuarial ←→ Product Development
```

**Key Principles:**
1. **High-Quality Risk Selection**
   - Proprietary risk differentiation models
   - Granular pricing segmentation

2. **Pricing Discipline**
   - Filed rating plans integrated with underwriting guidelines
   - State regulatory compliance
   - Rate adequacy over volume

3. **Customer Experience**
   - 98% claims satisfaction rate
   - Digital tools and self-service
   - Agency partnership support

### GEICO Partnership

**Arrangement:**
- Since 1995
- Homeowners business distribution
- Agency affiliate writes Travelers homeowners policies
- Significant premium volume contributor

## Distribution Strategy

### Independent Agents & Brokers

**Key Characteristics:**
- Primary distribution channel for all segments
- Less than a dozen intermediaries write significant volume
- Strong, long-term relationships
- Local market expertise

**Support Provided:**
- Marketing materials and co-op programs
- Technology platforms (agent portals)
- Risk control resources
- Claims support

### Consolidation Trends

**Market Dynamics:**
- Increased agent/broker consolidation
- Private equity investment in distribution
- Technology-driven competition
- Direct channel growth by competitors

**Travelers Response:**
- Maintain strong value proposition
- Invest in ease of doing business
- Technology partnerships
- Selective agency appointments
