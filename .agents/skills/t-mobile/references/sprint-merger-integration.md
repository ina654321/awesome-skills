# T-Mobile Sprint Merger Integration Report

## Overview

The merger of T-Mobile and Sprint, completed on April 1, 2020, created the "New T-Mobile" — a transformed competitive force in US wireless. The $26 billion all-stock transaction fundamentally reshaped the US telecom landscape and accelerated 5G deployment.

## Merger Background

### Strategic Rationale

**For T-Mobile:**
- Acquire Sprint's 2.5GHz spectrum (~150MHz mid-band)
- Add 30+ million customers
- Achieve scale to compete with Verizon and AT&T
- Accelerate 5G network buildout

**For Sprint:**
- Struggling as #4 carrier with declining market position
- Limited path to 5G leadership independently
- Debt burden and capital constraints
- Regulatory concerns about long-term viability

### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| April 29, 2018 | Merger agreement announced |
| June 18, 2018 | FCC filing submitted |
| June 28, 2019 | DOJ approval with conditions |
| July 26, 2019 | FCC approval |
| February 11, 2020 | Federal judge approves merger (rejects state AG challenge) |
| April 1, 2020 | Transaction closes; New T-Mobile launches |
| July 2, 2020 | Sprint brand begins transition |
| 2021-2024 | Network integration and spectrum deployment |

### Regulatory Approval

**DOJ Conditions:**
1. Divest Sprint's prepaid business (Boost Mobile) to DISH Network — $1.4B
2. Provide DISH 7-year wholesale MVNO agreement
3. Offer DISH option to purchase 800MHz spectrum — $3.6B (option expired 2024)
4. Maintain 5G buildout commitments

**FCC Commitments:**
- Cover 97% of US population with 5G within 3 years
- Cover 85% of rural Americans with 5G within 3 years
- Cover 99% of US population with 5G within 6 years
- Cover 90% of rural Americans with 5G within 6 years

**State AG Challenge:**
- 14 state Attorneys General sued to block merger
- Argued would reduce competition and raise prices
- February 2020: Federal Judge Victor Marrero ruled for T-Mobile
- Cited Sprint's unsustainable competitive position

## Financial Structure

### Transaction Details

- **Structure**: All-stock merger
- **Value**: $26 billion (at announcement)
- **Exchange Ratio**: 11.0 Sprint shares per T-Mobile share
- **Ownership**: Deutsche Telekom ~43%, SoftBank ~24%, Public ~33%
- **No Cash Component**: Capital-preserving structure

## Network Integration

### Sprint Spectrum Integration

**2.5 GHz (n41) Spectrum:**
- **Acquired**: ~150MHz nationwide
- **Value**: Crown jewel of merger
- **Strategic Importance**: Mid-band sweet spot for 5G (speed + coverage)

**Integration Timeline:**
- **2020**: Initial 2.5GHz deployment in major markets
- **2021**: Accelerated nationwide rollout
- **2022**: 250M+ people covered by Ultra Capacity 5G
- **2023**: 300M+ people covered
- **2024**: Continued densification and optimization

**Competitive Advantage:**
- Verizon and AT&T spent ~$80B on C-band (3.7-4.0GHz) in 2021
- T-Mobile gained 2-year head start with immediate 2.5GHz access
- Verizon/AT&T didn't get full C-band access until July 2023

### Network Consolidation

**Site Rationalization:**
- **Total Sites**: 102,000+ macro cells post-integration
- **Strategy**: Keep best sites from each network
- **Technology**: Multi-vendor RAN (Ericsson, Nokia, Samsung)

**Spectrum Refarming:**
- Sprint 2.5GHz → T-Mobile 5G Ultra Capacity
- Sprint LTE → Migrated or refarmed
- Sprint CDMA → Shutdown completed

### Customer Migration

**Sprint Brand Transition:**
- **2020-2022**: Dual-brand operation
- **2022-2023**: Sprint customers migrated to T-Mobile plans
- **Network**: Sprint devices migrated to T-Mobile network

**Migration Results:**
- Customer churn during integration: Better than expected
- Network experience improvement: Significant
- Customer satisfaction: Maintained or improved

## Strategic Outcomes

## Challenges and Learnings

### Challenges Faced

1. **Integration Complexity**: Merging two large networks, IT systems, and operations
2. **Customer Migration**: Moving millions of customers without churn spikes
3. **Regulatory Scrutiny**: Ongoing oversight and compliance requirements
4. **Competitive Response**: Verizon and AT&T fought back with unlimited plans

### Lessons Learned

1. **Spectrum is King**: The 2.5GHz acquisition justified the entire transaction
2. **Speed Matters**: Rapid deployment maintained competitive advantage
3. **Culture is Critical**: Maintaining T-Mobile's challenger mindset post-scale
4. **Regulatory Strategy**: Proactive remedies can secure approval

## The DISH Remedy: Creating a Fourth Competitor

### Original Intent

**Regulatory Theory:**
- DISH would acquire Boost Mobile (9.3M customers)
- DISH would build independent 5G network
- DISH would become viable fourth national carrier

### DISH Progress

**Timeline:**
- 2020: Acquired Boost Mobile
- 2021-2023: Network buildout
- 2024: Struggled financially, sought partnerships

**Status (2024):**
- 5G network partially built
- Financial challenges
- Partnered with Amazon for Boost Infinite
- Future as standalone competitor uncertain

### Assessment

- T-Mobile fulfilled MVNO agreement obligations
- DISH's emergence as fourth carrier: Delayed/uncertain
- Market effectively became three-player oligopoly
- Regulatory hope of four competitors: Not fully realized

## Long-Term Strategic Impact

### Changed Industry Structure

- **From 4 to 3 national carriers**

- **Accelerated 5G deployment timeline**
- **Price competition intensified then stabilized**

## Conclusion

The T-Mobile Sprint merger stands as one of the most successful telecom mergers in history:

**Success Metrics:**
- ✅ Synergy targets exceeded
- ✅ 5G leadership achieved
- ✅ Customer base doubled
- ✅ Financial performance strong
- ✅ Stockholder returns significant
- ✅ Regulatory commitments met

**Legacy:**
The merger enabled T-Mobile to challenge Verizon and AT&T as a true peer, accelerated American 5G leadership globally, and demonstrated that customer-focused consolidation can benefit consumers and shareholders alike.

**Key Insight:**
The merger proved that in telecommunications, spectrum and network scale are fundamental competitive advantages. The 2.5GHz spectrum acquired from Sprint provided T-Mobile with a multi-year head start in 5G that competitors are still working to match.
