# ASML Export Control Compliance Guide

> Regulatory framework and compliance requirements for ASML products

---

## Regulatory Overview

### Governing Authorities

ASML operates under export control regulations from multiple jurisdictions:

| Jurisdiction | Authority | Regulations |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|
| Netherlands | Dutch Government | Strategic Goods Decree |
| European Union | EU Commission | Dual-Use Regulation |
| United States | U.S. Department of Commerce | EAR (Export Administration Regulations) |
| International | Wassenaar Arrangement | Multilateral controls |

### Controlled Technologies

ASML products subject to export controls include:

| Product Category | Control Status | Primary Regulation |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| EUV Systems | Restricted | Dutch National + US EAR |
| Advanced DUV (NXT:2000i+) | License Required | Dutch + US |
| Standard DUV (NXT:1980Di) | Monitored | Case-by-case assessment |
| Metrology Systems | Recently Added | US EAR (Dec 2024) |
| Software | Controlled | US EAR + Dutch |
| Spare Parts | Component-dependent | Various |

## China Export Restrictions

### Historical Timeline

| Date | Regulatory Action | Impact |
|------|-------------------|--------|
| 2019 | Initial US restrictions | EUV to China blocked |
| 2023 | Dutch restrictions (Sept 1) | NXT:2000i+ licensing |
| 2024 | US expanded controls (Oct) | Additional fab restrictions |
| 2024 | Updated US rule (Dec 31) | Metrology, software added |

### Current Status (2025)

**Prohibited Shipments:**
- All EUV systems (NXE series)
- Advanced DUV: NXT:2000i, NXT:2050i, NXT:2100i
- Related software and technology

**Licensed Shipments:**
- DUV NXT:1980Di (case-by-case)
- Service for installed base
- Non-controlled spare parts

**Monitoring:**
- End-use verification
- Customer screening
- Re-export tracking

## Compliance Framework

### Classification Process

```
Product Classification Flow:
├─ 1. ECCN Determination
│   └─ Export Control Classification Number
├─ 2. Technical Parameters Review
│   └─ Wavelength, resolution, throughput
├─ 3. End-Use Assessment
│   └─ Civil vs. military application
├─ 4. Destination Review
│   └─ Country/entity restrictions
└─ 5. License Determination
    └─ Required / Exception / NLR
```

### Export Control Classification Numbers (ECCN)

| Product | ECCN | Control Reason |
|---------|------|----------------|
| EUV Lithography | 3B001.a | NS (National Security) |
| DUV Immersion | 3B001.a | NS |
| Metrology (advanced) | 3B001 | NS |
| Source components | 3E001 | TS (Technology) |
| Software | 3D001 | NS |

### License Types

| License | Purpose | Duration |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Individual | Single shipment | One-time |
| Bulk | Multiple shipments | 2-4 years |
| General | Broad authorization | Varies |
| Service | Installed base support | Ongoing |

## Customer Due Diligence

### Required Checks

**Screening Process:**

1. **Entity List Check**
   - US BIS Denied Persons List
   - EU Consolidated List
   - UN Sanctions List

2. **End-User Verification**
   - Company registration
   - Business legitimacy
   - Ownership structure

3. **End-Use Statement**
   - Intended application
   - Final product
   - Re-export commitments

4. **Facility Assessment**
   - Location verification
   - End-user certificate
   - Site of installation

### Red Flags

| Indicator | Action Required |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Vague end-use | Enhanced due diligence |
| Unusual routing | Transaction review |
| Cash payments | Compliance escalation |
| Shell company structure | Investigation |
| Military connections | Potential denial |
