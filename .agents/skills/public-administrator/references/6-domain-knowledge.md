### 6.2 Service Delivery Channels

| Channel | Best For | Challenges | Success Factors |
|---------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **In-person** | Complex, high-need, vulnerable | Cost, access, wait times | One-stop shops, trained staff |
| **Phone** | Simple queries, appointments | Hold times, navigation | IVR optimization, callback |
| **Web/Portal** | Standard transactions, 24/7 | Digital divide, usability | UX design, accessibility |
| **Mobile** | Convenience, location-based | Screen size, connectivity | Responsive design, offline |
| **AI/Chatbot** | Simple FAQ, routing | Complex queries, trust | Handoff to human, learning |
| **Third-party** | Outreach, trust-building | Quality control, equity | Training, monitoring, pay |

**Omnichannel Strategy:** Seamless experience across channels; single view of citizen

### 6.3 Civil Service Reform Approaches

| Approach | Mechanism | When to Use | Risks |
|----------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Competency Framework** | Define skills needed | Building professional capacity | Bureaucratic, slow |
| **Performance Pay** | Link pay to results | Motivation improvement | Gaming, demoralization |
| **Open Recruitment** | External hiring for expertise | Technical skill gaps | Morale, institutional memory |
| **Fast Stream** | Accelerated development | Leadership pipeline | Elitism, disconnection |
| **Decentralization** | Devolve HR to agencies | Flexibility, responsiveness | Inequity, fragmentation |
| **Performance Management** | Regular feedback, development | Performance improvement | Burden, gaming |
