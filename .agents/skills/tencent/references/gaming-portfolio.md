## Overview

Tencent is the world's largest gaming company by revenue, dominating both the Chinese domestic market and increasingly influential internationally. Gaming represents nearly 30% of Tencent's total revenue.

**Key Statistics (FY2024):**
- Total Gaming Revenue: ¥197.7B
- Domestic Gaming: ¥139.7B (+10% YoY)
- International Gaming: ¥58.0B (+9% YoY)
- 14 "Evergreen" Games
- 100M+ DAU (Honor of Kings peak)

## Revenue Models

### Free-to-Play (F2P) Premium

**Core Philosophy:**
- Game free to download/play
- Monetize via cosmetic items
- No pay-to-win mechanics
- Battle pass model

**Revenue Breakdown (typical):**
| Category | Share |
|----------|-------|
| Skins/Cosmetics | 60% |
| Battle Pass | 25% |
| Lucky Draws/Gacha | 10% |
| Other | 5% |

### Gacha/Lucky Draw Regulation (China)

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Drop rates must be disclosed
- Spending limits for minors
- No direct gambling mechanics
- Government approval required

**Tencent's Approach:**
- Strict age verification
- Transparent odds
- Optional cosmetics only
- Regulatory-first design

## Regulatory Environment

### China Gaming Regulations

**Key Restrictions:**
- Minor playtime limits (3 hours/week)
- Spending caps for under-18s
- Content approval required
- No loot boxes for minors

**Tencent's Response:**
- Industry-leading compliance systems
- Age verification (facial recognition)
- Parental controls
- Focus on adult monetization

### US Regulatory Pressure

**CFIUS Review (2025):**
- Reviewing Epic Games, Riot Games, Supercell stakes
- National security concerns (data)
- Potential forced divestiture discussions
- Pentagon "Chinese military company" designation

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Data localization
- Operational independence for US studios
- Legal challenges
