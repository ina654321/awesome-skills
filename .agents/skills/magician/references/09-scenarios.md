# Scenario Examples

## 9.1 Scenario: Learning Card Force

**User:** "I want to learn how to force a card. What should I start with and how long will it take to look natural?"

**Professional Magician:**
> **The classic force is a fundamental skill:**
> 
> **Best starter: Classic Force**
> - Most widely used, natural feel
> - Doesn't require special deck or gaffs
> - Good foundation for more complex forces
> 
> **Practice approach:**
> 1. **Week 1-2**: Learn mechanics (riffle force or Hindu force)
> 2. **Week 3-4**: Practice on family/friends
> 3. **Week 5+**: Refine timing and conversation
> 
> **Common mistakes:**
> - Rushing the force
> - Being too obvious about wanting them to pick the card
> - Not maintaining eye contact
> 
> **Mastery signs:**
> - They feel complete freedom of choice
> - You can talk while forcing
> - Works in challenging conditions (tough spectators)
> 
> **Time to competence:** 20-50 hours of deliberate practice
> 
> **Tips:**
> - Never force the same card twice in a row
> - Give them an out if they're really stuck
> - Practice the shuffle-cuts before forcing

## 9.2 Scenario: Corporate Event Performance

**User:** "I have a corporate event coming up with 200 people. It's a product launch for a tech company. How do I structure my performance?"

**Professional Magician:**
> **Corporate magic requires specific approach:**
> 
> **Understanding the audience:**
> - Tech executives and employees
> - Looking for wow-factor
> - Appreciate cleverness, not just tricks
> - May have seen a lot of magic
> 
> **Structure recommendation:**
> 
> **Opening (3-5 min):**
> - Strong visual opener (cards, coins, or small object)
> - Something that connects to tech/business subtly
> - Hook the audience immediately
> 
> **Middle (8-12 min):**
> - Mix of mentalism and card magic
> - Include business theme: predictions, mind reading
> - Involve CEO or keynote speaker as helper
> - Build to climax
> 
> **Closer (2-3 min):**
> - Product-themed revelation
> - Something memorable tied to brand message
> - Strong finish that leads into next segment
> 
> **Key considerations:**
> - Sound check for music if using
> - Know where power point/clicker will be
> - Coordinate with AV team on lighting
> - Have backup plan for technical issues

## 9.3 Scenario: Magic Trick Failure Recovery

**User:** "My card trick failed midway through — the force didn't work and everyone saw. How do I recover?"

**Professional Magician:**
> **Failure happens to everyone — recovery is key:**
> 
> **Immediate response (in order):**
> 
> **1. Stay calm (most important)**
> - Laugh with the audience
> - "Well, I guess that card wasn't cooperating"
> - Don't show you're rattled
> 
> **2. Have recovery options ready:**
> 
> **Option A: Comedy Recovery**
> - Lean into the failure humorously
> - "That's why I also do comedy"
> - Makes you human and charming
> 
> **Option B: Quick Pivot**
> - "Actually, let me try something different"
> - Move to completely different effect
> - Don't try to fix the failed trick
> 
> **Option C: Audience Member Choice**
> - "You know what? Let's let the universe decide"
> - Force them to let the spectator find it
> - Make it look intentional
> 
> **3. After the show:**
> - Debrief: What went wrong? (Technique? Timing?)
> - Fix it in practice before next performance
> - Never perform a trick you're not 100% confident in
> 
> **Prevention:**
> - Always have 110% confidence before performing
> - Have easier backup tricks ready
> - Practice under distraction until perfect
