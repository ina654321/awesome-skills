# Standard Workflow

## 8.1 Learning a New Trick

### Step 1: Understanding
- Watch the effect performed
- Learn the intended impact on audience
- Understand the method conceptually
- Identify required sleight of hand

### Step 2: Practice (Alone)
- Learn the mechanics until comfortable
- Practice without worrying about speed
- Repeat until consistent (90%+ success rate)
- Time yourself; most tricks should take 10-50 hours to master

### Step 3: Practice (Forced Audience)
- Perform for patient friend or family
- Get feedback on clarity
- Work on timing and pacing
- Test under slight pressure

### Step 4: Performance
- Perform for real audience
- Collect feedback
- Refine based on real conditions
- Continue to improve over time

## 8.2 Event Performance Process

### Step 1: Pre-Event
- Confirm booking details: date, time, venue, pay
- Understand audience: corporate, birthday, children, etc.
- Plan set appropriate for audience and time
- Prepare equipment and backup

### Step 2: Arrival
- Arrive 30+ minutes early
- Set up while venue still empty
- Test any equipment
- Greet client and understand expectations

### Step 3: Performance
- Build rapport before starting
- Start with strong, visual effect
- Maintain eye contact and connection
- End with memorable piece
- Allow time for photos/questions

### Step 4: Post-Performance
- Thank client
- Discuss any special moments
- Handle payment professionally
- Follow up with thank-you note

## 8.3 Building a Repertoire

### Structure of a Set
1. **Opener** (2-3 minutes): Strong visual, gets attention
2. **Middle** (5-8 minutes): Build, variety, peaks
3. **Closer** (1-2 minutes): Memorable, strong finish

### Building Variety
- Mix of effects: cards, coins, mentalism
- Different types: visual, mysterious, funny
- Vary difficulty: easier in front of tough crowds
- Keep some "emergency" simple tricks ready

### Adding New Material
- Learn one new piece at a time
- Don't replace working material until new is solid
- Look for gaps in current repertoire
- Consider audience needs when selecting

## 8.4 Troubleshooting

### When Trick Fails
1. **Stay calm** — recovery is possible
2. **Have backup plan** — always know exit strategy
3. **Turn it into comedy** — laugh with audience
4. **Repeat doesn't work** — move on quickly
5. **Don't explain** — never reveal method under pressure

### Handling Difficult Audiences
- The skeptic: Engage with confidence; don't get defensive
- The showoff: Give them a moment but maintain control
- The child: Keep them engaged with simple tasks
- The drunk: Choose safe helpers; have quick effects ready
