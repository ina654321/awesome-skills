# Lam Research - Customer Profiles Reference

## Overview

Lam Research serves the world's leading semiconductor manufacturers across memory, logic, and foundry segments. Customer relationships are characterized by deep co-development partnerships, long-term roadmaps, and shared technology investments.

---

## Memory Customers

### SK Hynix (Korea)

**Profile:**
- World's 2nd largest memory manufacturer
- Leader in HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) for AI
- Revenue contribution to Lam: ~$2B+ annually

**Lam Relationship:**
- Strategic supplier for DRAM and NAND equipment
- Joint development on HBM TSV technology
- Lam's Syndion and SABRE dominant in HBM manufacturing
- Early access partner for new etch technologies

**Key Programs:**
- HBM3/HBM4: TSV etch and copper plating
- Advanced DRAM: Akara for cell etch
- 3D NAND: Sense.i for channel hole etch

**Recent Developments:**
- Expanded partnership for AI memory (2025)
- Co-development on 3D DRAM technology
- Collaboration on advanced packaging

---

### Samsung Electronics (Korea)

**Profile:**
- World's largest memory manufacturer
- Integrated device manufacturer (IDM) - memory + logic
- Major fab investments in Korea and US (Taylor, TX)

**Lam Relationship:**
- Long-standing strategic partnership
- Broadest equipment portfolio adoption
- Joint development on V-NAND and advanced DRAM

**Key Programs:**
- V-NAND: 200+ layer transitions with Lam etch/deposition
- DRAM: Transition to 4F2 architecture
- Foundry: GAA transistor development (2nm)
- HBM: Stack scaling with TSV technology

**Technology Collaboration:**
- GAA nanosheet etch process development
- Advanced patterning for sub-3nm
- Backside power delivery integration

---

## Emerging Customers

### Chinese Memory Manufacturers

**Profile:**
- YMTC (Yangtze Memory Technologies): 3D NAND
- CXMT (ChangXin Memory Technologies): DRAM
- Subject to US export controls

**Lam Relationship:**
- Significant historical revenue (~25-30% of total)
- Current restrictions on advanced equipment
- Mature node (≥14nm) equipment still permitted
- Reliant Systems for older technology

**Impact of Export Controls:**
- Advanced tool shipments restricted
- Revenue decline from China since 2022
- Ongoing compliance and licensing requirements
