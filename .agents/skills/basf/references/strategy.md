# Winning Ways Strategy Reference

## Overview

**Winning Ways** is BASF's strategy launched in September 2024 under CEO Markus Kamieth, focusing on enabling customers' green transformation while improving financial performance.

**Strategic Ambition:**
> "We want to be the preferred chemical company to enable our customers' green transformation."

---

## Four Strategic Levers

### 1. Focus

**Concentrate on Core Strengths:**
- Deep Verbund integration for competitive advantage
- Premium positioning in specialties
- Clear distinction between core and standalone businesses

**Portfolio Actions:**
| Action | Status | Value |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Coatings divestiture to Sherwin-Williams | Complete (Oct 2025) | $1.15B |
| Automotive coatings separation | In progress | Carlyle deal (Q2 2026) |
| Agricultural Solutions partial IPO | Planned 2027 | TBD |
| Wintershall Dea stake management | Ongoing | Strategic options |

### 2. Accelerate

**Speed Up Execution:**
- Faster decision-making through simplified governance
- Streamlined processes for customer response
- Digital tools for operational efficiency
- Cost reduction programs

**Cost Savings Program:**
| Target | Timeline | Progress |
|--------|----------|----------|
| €1.6 billion | End 2025 | On track |
| €2.1 billion | End 2026 | Accelerated |

**Focus Areas:**
- Ludwigshafen site restructuring
- Global business services optimization
- Digital transformation acceleration
- Procurement excellence

### 3. Transform

**Enable Customers' Green Transformation:**
- Loop Solutions: €10 billion target by 2030 (from €5B today)
- Product carbon footprint transparency
- Circular economy solutions
- Sustainable agriculture technologies

**Sustainability Targets:**
| Metric | Target | Baseline |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Scope 1 & 2 emissions | -25% | 2018 |
| Net zero emissions | 2050 | — |
| Loop Solutions sales | €10 billion | 2024: €5B |
| Renewable electricity | 100% (Zhanjiang) | — |

**Transformation Investment:**
- €600 million annually (2025-2028)
- 80% of R&D activities support sustainability
- 54 MW electrolyzer operational at Ludwigshafen

### 4. Win

**Achieve Competitive Differentiation:**
- Technology leadership in core areas
- Customer intimacy and application expertise
- Operational excellence
- Talent and culture

**Financial Targets (2028):**
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| ROCE | Significant improvement vs. 2024 |
| Free cash flow | Positive and growing |
| EBITDA margin | Expansion in core businesses |
| Dividend | Sustainable base + variable |

## Core vs. Standalone Businesses

### Core Businesses (Verbund-Integrated)

| Segment | Integration Level | Strategic Priority |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Chemicals | Deep | Feedstock security, cost leadership |
| Materials | Deep | Technology differentiation |
| Industrial Solutions | Moderate | Application expertise |
| Nutrition & Care | Moderate | Sustainability-driven growth |

**Characteristics:**
- Long value chains within Verbund
- Shared infrastructure benefits
- Volume-driven with scale advantages
- Interdependent operations

### Standalone Businesses (Operationally Independent)

| Segment | Market Position | Strategic Priority |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Surface Technologies | Growing | Battery materials leadership |
| Agricultural Solutions | #3 global | Innovation-driven growth |

**Characteristics:**
- Greater operational flexibility
- Industry-specific metrics
- Speed-to-market focus
- Potential for capital market access

**Agricultural Solutions IPO (2027):**
- Partial listing to unlock value
- Dedicated management focus
- Access to agricultural investors
- Retained majority control

## Regional Strategy

### Europe
- **Challenge:** High energy costs, regulatory pressure
- **Response:** Focus on specialties, efficiency programs
- **Key actions:** Ludwigshafen restructuring, renewable energy

### North America
- **Challenge:** Market softness, competition
- **Response:** Feedstock advantage utilization
- **Key actions:** Freeport optimization, customer partnerships

### Asia Pacific
- **Opportunity:** Largest growth market
- **Investment:** Zhanjiang Verbund
- **Target:** China market leadership with sustainable production

### South America
- **Focus:** Agricultural Solutions growth
- **Strength:** Strong market position in Brazil
- **Development:** Bio-based opportunities

## Capital Allocation Priorities

### 1. Organic Growth
- Zhanjiang completion (2025-2030)
- Maintenance and safety
- Growth projects with >15% ROCE

### 2. Dividends
- Base dividend: €2.25/share minimum
- Additional based on free cash flow
- Target: ~40% payout ratio

### 3. Portfolio Optimization
- Value-accretive divestitures
- Bolt-on acquisitions in specialties
- Share buybacks (opportunistic)

### 4. Balance Sheet Strength
- Maintain investment-grade rating (Single A)
- Equity ratio >40%
- Flexibility for strategic opportunities
