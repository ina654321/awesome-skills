## Join Life Initiative

### Program Overview

**Launched:** 2015

**Purpose:** Identify and promote products made with sustainable materials and processes, making sustainable choices visible and accessible to customers.

### Join Life Standards

**Materials Criteria:**

| Material Type | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Cotton | Organic, recycled, or BCI (Better Cotton Initiative) |
| Polyester | Minimum 50% recycled content |
| Viscose | Sustainably sourced wood pulp (FSC certified) |
| Wool | Responsible wool standard |
| Linen | Sustainable farming practices |

**Production Criteria:**

| Aspect | Standard |
|--------|----------|
| Water Usage | Reduced consumption in production |
| Chemical Management | Zero discharge of hazardous chemicals |
| Energy | Renewable energy preference |
| Waste | Minimized production waste |

### Product Labeling

**Join Life Label:**
- Visible hangtags in stores
- Online filtering and categorization
- Material composition transparency
- Care instructions for longevity

**2024 Performance:**
- Join Life products represent growing % of total assortment
- Customer recognition and demand increasing
- Price parity with non-sustainable alternatives maintained

## Circular Economy Programs

### 1. Garment Collection Program

**How It Works:**
1. Customers bring used clothing to Zara stores
2. Collection boxes in 1,000+ stores globally
3. Partnership with textile recyclers
4. Sorting for: Reuse, Recycling, or Energy recovery

**2024 Achievements:**
- Thousands of tons of textiles collected
- Expanded to more markets
- Integration with pre-owned platform

### 2. Pre-Owned Platform

**Markets:** UK (2022), France (2023), Germany (2023), expanding

**Features:**
- Customers sell Zara items to other customers
- Zara facilitates transaction and authentication
- Repair services offered
- Extends garment lifecycle

**Strategic Value:**
- Addresses circular economy goals
- Customer retention and engagement
- Data on secondhand demand
- Brand relevance with sustainability-conscious consumers

### 3. Repair Services

**Availability:** Select markets and flagships

**Services:**
- Basic alterations
- Button replacement
- Minor repairs
- Tailoring services

## Environmental Impact Reduction

### Carbon Footprint

**2024 Baseline:**
- Scope 1 & 2 (own operations): 100% renewable electricity achieved
- Scope 3 (supply chain): Focus area for reduction

**Reduction Strategies:**

| Area | Action | Impact |
|------|--------|--------|
| Stores | LED lighting, efficient HVAC | -40% energy per m² |
| Logistics | Route optimization, electric vehicles | -15% transport emissions |
| Manufacturing | Renewable energy transition | Supplier engagement |
| Offices | Green building standards | LEED certification |

### Water Stewardship

**Challenges:**
- Textile dyeing is water-intensive
- Water scarcity in manufacturing regions

**Initiatives:**

| Program | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Waterless dyeing | Pilot programs for dry dyeing technology |
| Closed-loop systems | Water recycling in wet processing |
| Cleaner chemistry | ZDHC (Zero Discharge of Hazardous Chemicals) |
| Supplier audits | Water management assessments |

### Waste Reduction

**Operational Waste:**
- Packaging optimization
- Recycling programs at distribution centers
- Zero waste to landfill goal for own operations

**Textile Waste:**
- Leftover fabric strategy (use for other products)
- Limited production runs reduce overstock
- Donation programs for unsold items

## Supply Chain Ethics

### Worker Welfare

**Living Wage Commitment:**
- Target: Living wages throughout supply chain by 2028
- Approach: Collaboration with suppliers, industry initiatives
- Progress: Gradual implementation with monitoring

**Working Conditions:**

| Standard | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| No forced labor | Audited and enforced |
| No child labor | Strict prohibition |
| Safe workplaces | Safety audits |
| Reasonable hours | Working time limits |
| Freedom of association | Respect for workers' rights |

### Audit Program

**Three Pillars:**

1. **Social Audits**
   - Annual assessments of all suppliers
   - Third-party verification
   - Corrective action plans
   - Follow-up monitoring

2. **Environmental Audits**
   - Water and energy usage
   - Chemical management
   - Waste handling
   - Air emissions

3. **Traceability Audits**
   - Supply chain mapping
   - Tier 2 and 3 visibility
   - Material origin verification

### Transparency Initiatives

**Public Disclosure:**
- Annual sustainability report
- GRI (Global Reporting Initiative) standards
- CDP (Carbon Disclosure Project) submission
- Modern slavery statements

**Supply Chain Mapping:**
- Tier 1 suppliers: 100% identified
- Tier 2 suppliers: Expanding visibility
- Material origins: Increasing transparency
