## Amancio Ortega Gaona

### Profile

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Born | March 28, 1936, Busdongo de Arbas, León, Spain |
| Net Worth (2025-2026) | $126-148 billion |
| Global Ranking | 10th-15th richest person worldwide |
| Europe Ranking | 2nd richest (after Bernard Arnault) |
| Inditex Stake | ~59.3% |

### Biography

**Early Life:**
- Son of a railway worker and a housemaid
- Started as a delivery boy for a local shirtmaker in A Coruña
- Learned retail from the ground up

**Entrepreneurial Journey:**
- **1963:** Founded Confecciones GOA (named after his initials)
- **1975:** Opened first Zara store with his wife Rosalía Mera
- **1985:** Created Inditex as holding company
- **2001:** Took Inditex public; became billionaire
- **2011:** Stepped down as Chairman; remained majority shareholder

**Business Philosophy:**
1. **Customer Obsession** - "The customer is always right"
2. **Speed Wins** - Fast fashion before the term existed
3. **Vertical Integration** - Control the value chain
4. **Frugality** - No ostentation; simple headquarters
5. **Long-term Thinking** - Invest for decades, not quarters

**Management Style:**
- Hands-on in early years; visited factories and stores regularly
- Minimal hierarchy; open-door policy
- No private office until necessary
- Prefer practical experience over formal education

**Legacy:**
- Pioneer of fast fashion business model
- Created €150B+ company from small workshop
- Major philanthropist through Amancio Ortega Foundation
- Real estate investor (prime global properties)

### Quotes

> "The customer must always be our obsession."

> "You can do anything you set your mind to."

> "In business, the most important thing is to have a clear idea of what you want to achieve."

## Marta Ortega Pérez

### Profile

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Born | 1984 (age 40-41) |
| Position | Chairperson, Inditex |
| Appointed | April 2022 |
| Father | Amancio Ortega |
| Mother | Flora Pérez Marcote |

### Career Path

**Early Career:**
- Joined Inditex in 2007 (age 23)
- Started in store operations in London
- Worked in various departments to learn the business

**Progression:**
- **2007-2011:** Store operations, visual merchandising
- **2011-2015:** Strategic development roles
- **2015:** Appointed to Amancio Ortega Foundation Board
- **2018:** Promoted to oversee brand image and customer experience
- **2022:** Named Chairperson of Inditex

### Leadership Style

**Key Characteristics:**
- **Fashion-Forward:** Deep understanding of design and aesthetics
- **Customer-Centric:** Continues father's obsession with customer experience
- **Digital Native:** Embraces technology and e-commerce
- **Sustainability-Focused:** Prioritizes environmental commitments
- **Collaborative:** Works closely with CEO Óscar García Maceiras

**Strategic Priorities:**
1. **Digital Transformation** - App, e-commerce, social commerce
2. **Store Experience** - Flagship concept, Store Mode integration
3. **Sustainability** - Join Life expansion, net-zero commitment
4. **Talent Development** - Internal promotion culture
5. **Global Expansion** - US growth, Asia optimization

### Achievements Since 2022

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 2022 | Smooth leadership transition; stock hits record highs |
| 2023 | Sustainability targets announced; live shopping expansion |
| 2024 | Record financial performance; store optimization |

### Fortune Recognition

- **2024:** Named to Fortune's "Most Powerful Women" list

## The Inditex Culture

### Core Values

1. **Customer Obsession**
   - Store managers have direct line to designers
   - Customer feedback drives decisions
   - "The customer is always right"

2. **Speed and Agility**
   - Flat organizational structure
   - Quick decision-making
   - Responsive to market changes

3. **Entrepreneurial Spirit**
   - Risk-taking encouraged
   - Innovation valued
   - Continuous improvement mindset

4. **Frugality**
   - Simple headquarters
   - No executive perks
   - Focus on value, not luxury

5. **Sustainability Commitment**
   - Long-term environmental goals
   - Social responsibility
   - Ethical business practices

### Employee Philosophy

**Internal Promotion:**
- Most managers promoted from within
- Store staff can rise to senior positions
- Amancio Ortega's own path as inspiration

**Training & Development:**
- Comprehensive onboarding
- Continuous learning programs
- International rotation opportunities

**Compensation:**
- Competitive base salaries
- Profit-sharing programs
- Employee benefits
