# TotalEnergies-Adani Partnership

## Overview

The TotalEnergies-Adani partnership represents one of the largest India-focused energy collaborations between a global energy major and an Indian conglomerate. Established in phases starting in 2018, the partnership spans LNG, city gas distribution, renewable energy, and green hydrogen.

---

## Partnership Timeline

### 2018: LNG and Gas Distribution
- **Adani Total Gas Joint Venture**:
  - **Stakes**: 37.4% TotalEnergies, 62.6% Adani
  - **Focus**: City gas distribution (CGD) across India
  - **Scope**: CNG stations, piped natural gas (PNG) for households
  - **LNG Terminals**: Joint development including Dhamra LNG Port (Odisha)

### 2020: Renewable Energy Entry
- **Adani Green Energy (AGEL) Stake**:
  - **Initial Acquisition**: 20% minority stake
  - **Investment**: $2.5 billion
  - **Timing**: January 2021
  - **Assets**: 50% stake in 2.35 GWac operating solar portfolio
  - **Strategic Rationale**: Access to world's largest solar developer

### 2022: Green Hydrogen Expansion
- **Adani New Industries (ANIL)**:
  - **Structure**: 25% TotalEnergies, 75% Adani
  - **Focus**: Green hydrogen production
  - **Target**: 1 million tonnes/year by 2030
  - **Investment**: Part of Adani's $50B green hydrogen commitment
  - **Integration**: 30 GW renewable capacity for hydrogen production

### 2024: Joint Venture Expansion
- **Solar Portfolio JV**:
  - **Capacity**: 1.15 GW
  - **TotalEnergies Commitment**: INR 39.6 billion (~$444 million)
  - **Structure**: Equal JV with AGEL
  - **Status**: Operational

## Stake Reduction (2024-2025)

### Rationale for Reduction

**Financial Optimization:**
- **Value Creation**: Original $2.5B investment worth $6-7B
- **Capital Recycling**: Realize gains while preserving partnership
- **Portfolio Management**: Reduce single-entity concentration

**Governance Considerations:**
- US DOJ indictment of certain Adani executives (November 2024)
- Indictment related to alleged corruption scheme
- Did not target AGEL itself or AGEL-related companies
- TotalEnergies not aware of investigation beforehand

### Reduction Transactions

| Transaction | Date | Stake Sold | Proceeds | Buyer |
|-------------|------|------------|----------|-------|
| Open market sale | Dec 2025 | 1.74% (~2.86 crore shares) | ₹2,778 crore (~$330M) | Various institutional investors |
| Previous sales | 2024-2025 | ~0.76% | - | Market |
| **Total Reduction** | | **~2.5%** | **~$1.1B+** | |

### Post-Reduction Position
- **New Stake**: 17.25%
- **Approach**: Partial monetization while maintaining strategic position
- **Future Sales**: Additional sales possible (up to 6% total reduction discussed)

## Governance Framework

### Minority Shareholder Protections
- **Voting Rights**: Proportional to stake
- **Board Representation**: Observer rights
- **Information Rights**: Financial and operational reporting
- **Tag-Along Rights**: Protection in change of control

### Current Stance (Post-Indictment)
**TotalEnergies Statement (November 2024):**
> "TotalEnergies will take all relevant actions to protect its minority investment in the company, and would not make any new financial contribution as part of its investments in the Adani group of companies."

**Key Commitments:**
- No new financial contributions pending governance clarity
- Protect existing minority investment
- Honor existing contractual obligations
- Maintain joint venture operations where beneficial

## Risk Factors

### Governance Risks
- US DOJ investigation into Adani Group individuals
- Potential reputational impact
- Need for enhanced due diligence

### Market Risks
- Renewable energy tariff competition
- Regulatory changes in India
- Currency (INR/USD) fluctuations

### Operational Risks
- Land acquisition challenges
- Grid connection delays
- Equipment supply chain

### Mitigation Strategies
- Reduced equity exposure (17.25% vs. 20%)
- Joint venture structures (operational control in JVs)
- No new capital commitments pending clarity
- Portfolio diversification (not dependent solely on Adani)

## Future Outlook

### TotalEnergies Strategy

**Near-Term (2025-2026):**
- Continue selective stake reduction if valuation attractive
- Preserve and optimize existing JVs
- No new financial contributions to Adani Group
- Monitor governance developments

**Medium-Term (2027-2030):**
- Maintain strategic India presence
- Evaluate direct development opportunities
- Consider alternative partners (NTPC, Tata Power)
- Scale Clean Firm Power and LNG businesses

**With Adani:**
- Operate existing JVs efficiently
- Capture value from AGEL stake appreciation
- Maintain LNG and gas partnerships

**Beyond Adani:**
- Direct development through TotalEnergies India
- Partnerships with PSUs and other private players
- Technology and trading focus
