# TotalEnergies Factbook 2024

## Company Overview

**TotalEnergies SE** is a global multi-energy company headquartered in Paris, France, with operations spanning approximately 120 countries and over 100,000 employees.

### Corporate Identity
- **Legal Name**: TotalEnergies SE
- **Headquarters**: Paris, France
- **CEO**: Patrick Pouyanné (Chairman and Chief Executive Officer)
- **Employees**: 100,000+
- **Countries of Operation**: ~120
- **Stock Listings**: 
  - Euronext Paris (TTE)
  - London Stock Exchange (TTE)
  - New York Stock Exchange (TTE) - ordinary shares commenced trading December 8, 2025

### History & Transformation
- **1924**: Founded as Compagnie Française des Pétroles (CFP)
- **1985**: Became Total
- **1999**: Merged with Petrofina (Belgium), became Total Fina
- **2000**: Acquired Elf Aquitaine, became TotalFinaElf
- **2003**: Returned to Total name
- **2021**: Rebranded as TotalEnergies to reflect multi-energy transformation

## Multi-Energy Strategy

### Two-Pillar Approach

TotalEnergies pursues a **balanced integrated multi-energy strategy** anchored on two pillars:

| Pillar | Focus | 2030 Target |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Oil & Gas** | Notably LNG | 3% production growth CAGR |
| **Electricity** | The energy of transition | >100 TWh generation |

### Strategic Theme
> "**More Energy, Less Emissions, More Free Cash Flow**"

### Production Growth Target
- **Total Energy Production**: +4% per year CAGR 2024-2030
- **Oil & Gas Production**: ~3% per year CAGR (predominantly LNG)
- **Electricity**: Growing to >100 TWh by 2030 (~20% of total energy production)

## Business Segments

### 1. Exploration & Production (Upstream)
- **2024 Production**: ~2.4 Mboe/d
- **Geographic Presence**: 
  - Middle East: Iraq, Qatar, UAE
  - Americas: USA, Brazil, Argentina, Suriname, Guyana
  - Africa: Angola, Nigeria, Mozambique, Republic of Congo, Libya
  - Asia-Pacific: Australia, Papua New Guinea, Malaysia, Brunei
  - Europe: Norway, UK, Denmark
- **Key Projects 2024-2030**:
  - Brazil: Two major fields
  - Suriname: Deepwater developments
  - Angola: CLOV 3, Begonia
  - Oman: Marsa LNG
  - Nigeria: Ubeta gas project
  - Uganda: Tilenga oil project
  - Iraq: Gas growth integrated project

### 2. Integrated LNG
- **2024 Sales**: 40 Mt
- **Global Ranking**: 3rd largest LNG player worldwide
- **US Position**: Leading exporter (>10 Mt in 2024)
- **Growth Target**: +50% volume growth 2023-2030
- **Key Assets**:
  - Cameron LNG (USA, 16.6%)
  - Ichthys (Australia, 26%)
  - Yamal LNG (Russia, under review)
  - Mozambique LNG (26.5%, operator, force majeure lifted 2025)
  - QatarEnergy LNG expansion (participating interest)
  - Oman LNG projects

### 3. Integrated Power
- **Renewable Capacity Target**: 100 GW by 2030 (35 GW by 2025)
- **Electricity Generation Target**: >100 TWh by 2030
- **Mix**: 70% renewables, 30% flexible generation
- **Technology Portfolio**:
  - Solar: ~50% of renewable capacity
  - Onshore Wind: ~30%
  - Offshore Wind: ~10%
  - Storage: ~10%
- **Key Markets**: USA, EU, Brazil, India
- **Profitability Target**: ≥12% ROACE by 2028-2030
- **Cash Flow**: Net cash positive by 2028

### 4. Refining & Chemicals
- **Refining Capacity**: ~1.5 million barrels/day
- **Key Sites**: 
  - Europe: Antwerp, Le Havre, Normandy, Donges, Feyzin
  - Americas: Port Arthur (USA), Texas
  - Middle East: SATORP (Saudi Arabia, 50%)
  - Asia: Daesan (South Korea, 50%)
- **Biofuels**: La Mède biorefinery (France), Oudalle facility
- **Petrochemicals**: Integrated with refining at major sites

### 5. Marketing & Services
- **Service Stations**: ~15,000 worldwide
- **Network Presence**: Strong in Europe, Africa, Middle East
- **Transition Focus**: Multi-energy stations (EV charging, hydrogen, biofuels)
- **EV Charging**: Expanding network across Europe
- **B2B**: Marine bunkering, aviation fuel, lubricants

## Investment Thesis Summary

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class returns among oil majors (14.8% ROACE)
- Leading LNG position with strong growth trajectory
- Disciplined capital allocation with flexibility
- Strong balance sheet (8.3% gearing)
- Balanced multi-energy strategy

**Strategic Priorities:**
1. Grow LNG volumes by 50% (2023-2030)
2. Build 100 GW renewable capacity
3. Reduce operational emissions (Scope 1+2)
4. Maintain shareholder returns >40% of cash flow
5. Execute major projects (Mozambique, Brazil, Qatar, Iraq)
