# TotalEnergies Integrated Power Strategy

## Strategic Vision

TotalEnergies is building an **integrated electricity business** spanning the entire value chain from power generation to customer sales and trading. This represents the second pillar of the company's multi-energy strategy.

> "Electricity demand, which is vital to the success of the energy transition, is expected to grow sharply, as decarbonization is at the heart of the roadmaps of countries committed to carbon neutrality by 2050."

## Technology Portfolio

### Target Mix (2030)

```
        RENEWABLE CAPACITY MIX (100 GW Target)
        
        Solar        ████████████████████  50% (~50 GW)
        
        Onshore Wind ████████████          30% (~30 GW)
        
        Offshore Wind ████                10% (~10 GW)
        
        Storage      ████                 10% (~10 GW)
```

### Solar
- **Share**: ~50% of renewable portfolio
- **Focus**: Utility-scale projects
- **Key Markets**: USA (Texas, California), Southern Europe, Middle East
- **Strategy**: Lowest LCOE technology; scale through multi-year module agreements

### Onshore Wind
- **Share**: ~30% of renewable portfolio
- **Focus**: Established wind regions
- **Key Markets**: USA, France, Germany, UK, Brazil
- **Strategy**: Selective development in high-resource areas

### Offshore Wind
- **Share**: ~10% of renewable portfolio
- **Focus**: Fixed-bottom and floating projects
- **Key Projects**: Yunlin (Taiwan, 640 MW operational), UK projects, Germany
- **Strategy**: Higher returns but execution risk; selective participation

### Battery Storage
- **Share**: ~10% of portfolio
- **Purpose**: Enable Clean Firm Power offering
- **Projects**: Multiple BESS projects launched in Germany (221 MW)
- **Strategy**: Co-locate with renewables for grid services

### Flexible Generation
- **Share**: ~30% of electricity output (not capacity)
- **Technology**: Combined Cycle Gas Turbines (CCGT)
- **Purpose**: Backup for intermittent renewables
- **Acquisition**: 50% of EPH's 14+ GW flexible generation portfolio (€5.1B)

## Clean Firm Power

### Concept
Clean Firm Power is TotalEnergies' premium electricity offering:
- **100% Renewable Source**: Solar + wind
- **24/7 Availability**: Backed by flexible generation + storage
- **Constant Consumption Profile**: Baseload power supply
- **Premium Pricing**: Above standard pay-as-produced PPAs

### Value Proposition
| Feature | Standard PPA | Clean Firm Power |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| Source | Renewable | Renewable |
| Availability | Intermittent | 24/7 guaranteed |
| Price | Market/contract | Premium (+20-50%) |
| Customer Need | Offsetting | True decarbonization |

### Major Contracts Secured (2024)
| Customer | Volume | Duration | Markets |
|----------|--------|----------|---------|
| Google | 30 TWh | 15 years | USA |
| Google | 1 TWh | 21 years | Malaysia |
| Airbus | 3.3 TWh | 10 years | Germany, UK |
| STMicroelectronics | 1.5 TWh | 15 years | - |
| SWM | 0.8 TWh | 10 years | France |
| Data4 | 0.6 TWh | 10 years | Spain |

### Target Customers
- **Data Centers**: 24/7 power critical for operations
- **Semiconductor Manufacturing**: High reliability needs
- **Aviation/Automotive**: Scope 3 emissions reduction
- **Chemicals/Steel**: Industrial decarbonization

### 1. Develop & Produce Better
**Scale & Cost Reduction:**

### 2. Sell Better

## Risk Management

### Development Risks
| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Permitting delays | Early engagement, local partnerships |
| Grid connection | Utility coordination, transmission investment |
| Supply chain | Long-term module agreements |
| Execution | EPC contractor management |

### Market Risks
| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Power price volatility | Hedging, long-term contracts |
| Merchant exposure | Limit to 30% maximum |
| Counterparty credit | Investment-grade focus |
| Regulatory changes | Diversified geographic portfolio |

### Operational Risks
| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Weather/Resource | Diversified technology mix |
| Equipment failure | O&M contracts, insurance |
| Cybersecurity | IT systems protection |
