## Why LNG is Core to Strategy

### Role in Energy Transition
1. **Bridge Fuel**: Replaces coal in power generation (50% lower emissions)
2. **Energy Security**: Shipable globally, reduces pipeline dependency
3. **Renewable Complement**: Flexible generation backs up intermittent renewables
4. **Transition Pathway**: Platform for future low-carbon molecules (biogas, hydrogen)

### Market Context
> "The growth of renewable electricity, intermittent and seasonal by nature, will require an increase in flexible power generation resources. The dispatchable generation of gas-fired power plants helps secure electricity supply against weather variability affecting renewables."

### Key Growth Drivers

#### 1. Qatar Expansion
- **North Field East (NFE)**: 6.25% interest, 2026-2027
- **North Field South (NFS)**: 9.375% interest, 2027-2028
- **Total Capacity**: Additional 4+ Mtpa equity share

#### 2. US LNG Expansion
- **Plaquemines LNG**: 1.5-2.0 Mtpa offtake
- **Rio Grande LNG**: 1.5-5.4 Mtpa potential
- **Advantage**: Henry Hub pricing, destination flexibility

#### 3. Mozambique LNG Restart
- **Force Majeure**: Lifted October 2025
- **Capacity**: 12.9 Mtpa (26.5% share = ~3.4 Mtpa)
- **First LNG**: Expected 2028-2029
- **Financing**: $4.7B US EXIM support confirmed

#### 4. Oman (Marsa LNG)
- **Capacity**: 1.0 Mtpa
- **Stake**: 100% operator
- **Advantage**: Solar-powered liquefaction (low carbon)

- Cost inflation
- Shipping availability and rates

- Carbon pricing impact
- Methane regulations
