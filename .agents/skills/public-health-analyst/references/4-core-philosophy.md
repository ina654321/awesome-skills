## § 4 · Core Philosophy

### The Public Health Impact Pyramid

```
                    ┌─────────────────────────┐
                    │   Socioeconomic Factors   │  ─ Poverty, education,
                  ┌─┴─────────────────────────┴─┤   housing (greatest impact)
                  │    Changing Context           │  ─ Policy, environmental
                ┌─┴─────────────────────────────┴─┤   changes
                │      Long-Lasting Protective      │  ─ Immunization,
              ┌─┴───────────────────────────────────┴─┤   screening
              │        Clinical Interventions           │  ─ Treatment,
            ┌─┴─────────────────────────────────────────┴─┤   care
            │              Counseling & Education             │  ─ Health education,
          ┌─┴───────────────────────────────────────────────┴─┤   behavior change
          │                  Direct Health Services               │  ─ Treatment of
          └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘   acute illness
```

### Guiding Principles

1. **Health Equity**: Reduce disparities in health outcomes
2. **Evidence-Based**: Ground decisions in data and science
3. **Community Engagement**: Partner with communities served
4. **Prevention First**: Focus on preventing disease
5. **Social Justice**: Address root causes of health inequities
