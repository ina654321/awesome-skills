# Khan Academy Test Preparation Guide

## Overview

Khan Academy offers free, comprehensive test preparation for major standardized exams. The platform is an official partner of the College Board (SAT) and provides practice for multiple other tests.

### Implementation Strategies

#### For Students

**8-Week Study Plan:**

| Week | Focus Area | Activities |
|------|------------|------------|
| 1-2 | Diagnostic | Full practice test, identify weak areas |
| 3-4 | Math Foundation | Heart of Algebra, Problem Solving |
| 5-6 | Reading/Writing | Command of Evidence, Words in Context |
| 7 | Advanced Topics | Passport to Advanced Math, Essay |

**Score Improvement Protocol:**
1. Link College Board account for personalized recommendations
2. Complete diagnostic quiz in each section
3. Focus 70% of time on weakest areas
4. Take full practice test every 2 weeks
5. Review all incorrect answers thoroughly

## Test-Taking Strategies

### Universal Strategies

**Time Management:**
- Know the test format and timing
- Practice with strict time limits
- Flag difficult questions for review
- Never leave questions blank (no penalty for guessing on most tests)

**Question Approach:**
1. Read question stem first (for reading passages)
2. Predict answer before reading choices
3. Eliminate obviously wrong answers
4. Beware of "attractive distractors"
5. Verify answer with evidence

**Mindset:**
- View test as opportunity, not threat
- Use growth mindset language
- Practice stress-reduction techniques
- Get adequate sleep before test

### Section-Specific Strategies

#### SAT Math

**Calculator Section:**
- Use calculator strategically
- Check for reasonableness
- Don't over-rely on calculator for simple calculations

**No-Calculator Section:**
- Mental math fluency
- Fraction/decimal conversion speed
- Algebraic manipulation
- Estimation techniques

**Common Topics:**
- Linear equations and systems
- Quadratic and exponential functions
- Geometry and trigonometry
- Data analysis and statistics

#### SAT Reading

**Passage Strategy:**
- Skim questions first
- Annotate main idea and tone
- Look for evidence question pairs
- Manage passage difficulty order

**Question Types:**
- Command of Evidence
- Words in Context
- Analysis in History/Social Studies
- Analysis in Science

#### SAT Writing and Language

**Focus Areas:**
- Standard English Conventions
- Expression of Ideas
- Command of Evidence
- Words in Context

**Common Errors:**
- Subject-verb agreement
- Pronoun clarity
- Modifier placement
- Sentence structure
