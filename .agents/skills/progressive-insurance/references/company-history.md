# Progressive Corporation: Company History

## Founding and Early Years (1937-1960s)

### 1937: The Beginning
- **Founded**: March 10, 1937
- **Founders**: Joseph Lewis and Jack Green
- **Location**: Cleveland, Ohio
- **Initial Focus**: Auto insurance for high-risk drivers
- **Original Name**: Progressive Mutual Insurance Company

Joseph Lewis and Jack Green started Progressive with a simple but revolutionary idea: provide auto insurance to drivers who couldn't get coverage elsewhere. At the time, the auto insurance market was dominated by traditional insurers who avoided "non-standard" risks.

### 1956: Direct Premium Payment Plan
Progressive introduced a revolutionary concept: paying premiums in installments rather than lump sums. This made insurance accessible to working-class drivers who couldn't afford annual premiums upfront.

### 1957: Name Change
Became "Progressive Casualty Insurance Company" to reflect broader product offerings.

## Expansion and Innovation (1970s-1990s)

### 1971: Public Ownership
Progressive went public, trading on NASDAQ. The IPO provided capital for aggressive expansion.

### 1987: Immediate Response Claims Service
Progressive pioneered 24/7 claims service with the promise of immediate response. Competitors operated 9-5; Progressive recognized accidents don't follow business hours.

### 1990: Direct Quote Comparison
Progressive became the first major insurer to offer rate comparisons with competitors. The "Auto Insurance by Phone" campaign let customers compare Progressive rates against State Farm and Allstate quotes.

### 1992: Public Traded on NYSE
Moved to New York Stock Exchange under ticker symbol **PGR**.

### 1994: Internet Pioneering
Progressive launched its first website, among the first insurance companies online. The site offered quotes and basic policy management.

## The Digital Revolution (2000s)

### 2008: Flo Debuts
- **Date**: January 14, 2008
- **Character**: Flo (played by Stephanie Courtney)
- **Agency**: Arnold Worldwide
- **Concept**: Virtual "Insurance Superstore" with enthusiastic sales clerk

Flo transformed Progressive from a well-run but little-known insurer into a cultural phenomenon. The character's retro diner aesthetic, upbeat personality, and catchphrases ("Wow! I say it louder") made Progressive memorable.

**Impact:**
- Brand recognition soared from ~30% to >90%
- Quote volume increased significantly
- Created differentiation from GEICO's gecko and cavemen

### 2008: Snapshot Launch
Progressive launched Snapshot, one of the first mass-market usage-based insurance (UBI) programs:
- Initial version: Plug-in OBD-II device
- Tracked: Mileage, time of day, hard braking
- Value proposition: Safe drivers save money

Snapshot represented Progressive's data-driven philosophy applied to pricing.

### 2009: Mobile App Launch
Progressive released its first mobile app, allowing customers to:
- View insurance cards
- Pay bills
- File claims
- Track Snapshot data

## Modern Era (2010-Present)

### 2012: Homeowners Expansion
Aggressive push into homeowners insurance to enable bundling with auto policies. Recognized that bundled customers have dramatically higher retention rates.

### 2015: Commercial Auto Growth
Launched Progressive Advantage brand for commercial auto:
- Small business focus
- Online quoting for simple risks
- Agent channel for complex commercial risks

### 2016: Leadership Transition

**Tricia Griffith became CEO** (July 2016):
- Previously: Personal Lines COO, Chief Human Resources Officer
- Joined Progressive in 1988 as a claims representative
- Rose through the ranks over 28 years
- First female CEO of a major US auto insurer
- Known for operational excellence and data-driven decision making

### 2017: Snapshot Mobile
Expanded Snapshot to use smartphone sensors in addition to plug-in devices:
- Broader adoption (lower barrier to entry)
- Added: Phone handling detection
- Improved: GPS-based route analysis

### 2020-2021: COVID-19 Response
- Provided premium rebates/refunds ($1B+) as driving decreased
- Accelerated digital transformation
- Expanded Snapshot adoption as drivers reconsidered their insurance needs

### 2022-2023: Profitability Turnaround
After years of growth over profit, Progressive executed a dramatic shift:
- **Tricia Griffith**: "Our only concern last year was getting the right rate"
- Aggressive rate increases across all states
- Temporary growth slowdown for profitability
- **Result**: Industry-leading combined ratios while competitors struggled

### 2024: Market Leadership
- **Revenue**: $75.4 billion (21% growth)
- **Market Position**: Tied/Leading US auto insurer (#1 or #2)
- **Policies**: 33.9 million in force
- **Employees**: 66,300+
- **Profitability**: Record net income (~$10B)

Warren Buffett specifically praised Progressive's performance in his 2024 Berkshire Hathaway letter, acknowledging their competitive threat to GEICO.

## Corporate Culture

### Core Values ("The Progressive Way")
1. **Integrity**: Do what's right
2. **Golden Rule**: Treat others as you want to be treated
3. **Objectives**: Aim high, work smart, deliver results
4. **Excellence**: Continuous improvement

### "Name Your Price" Tool Philosophy
Progressive's iconic tool lets customers set their budget and see coverage options that fit. Embodies the customer-centric, transparent approach that differentiates Progressive from traditional insurers.

### Data-Driven DNA
From the beginning, Progressive has been an analytics company that happens to sell insurance:
- Early adoption of credit-based insurance scores
- Pioneering telematics/UBI with Snapshot
- Real-time competitive pricing analysis
- A/B testing of marketing creatives
