# New Glenn Technical Specifications

## Overview
New Glenn is Blue Origin's heavy-lift orbital launch vehicle, named after NASA astronaut John Glenn. It represents Blue Origin's entry into the orbital launch market with a partially reusable design.

## Vehicle Configuration

### First Stage (GS1 - Guided Stage 1)
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Height | 57.5 m |
| Diameter | 7 m |
| Engines | 7 × BE-4 |
| Propellant | LNG (liquefied natural gas) / LOX |
| Thrust (Sea Level) | 3.85 MLbf (17.1 MN) |
| Thrust (Vacuum) | 4.2 MLbf (18.7 MN) |
| Reusability | Yes, vertical landing on Jacklyn ship |
| Design Life | 25 flights minimum, 100 goal |
| Turnaround Target | 16 days |

## Launch Site
- **Location:** Launch Complex 36 (LC-36), Cape Canaveral Space Force Station, Florida
- **Latitude:** 28.5° N
- **Azimuth:** Due east for equatorial orbits
- **Facilities:** Horizontal integration facility, vertical integration facility

## Recovery System
- **Landing Platform:** Jacklyn (LPV1 - Landing Platform Vessel 1)
- **Location:** ~1,000 km downrange (Atlantic Ocean)
- **Method:** Propulsive vertical landing with deployable landing legs
- **TBD:** Targeted Booster Drop-off (MECO at ~70 km altitude)
- **Thermal Protection:** Reusable thermal protection system on windward side

## Flight History

### NG-1 (January 16, 2025)
- **Payload:** Blue Ring Pathfinder
- **Outcome:** Successful orbital insertion; booster lost during descent
- **Significance:** First orbital flight for Blue Origin
- **Quote:** "I'm incredibly proud New Glenn achieved orbit on its first attempt" — Dave Limp, CEO

### NG-2 (November 13, 2025)
- **Payload:** ESCAPADE (Mars mission)
- **Booster:** GS1-SN002 "Never Tell Me The Odds"
- **Outcome:** Successful launch; booster landing primary objective
- **Significance:** First successful New Glenn booster landing

### NG-3 (Planned 2026)
- **Payload:** Blue Moon Mark 1 (lunar lander pathfinder)
- **Significance:** First lunar mission attempt

## Manufacturing
- **Kent, WA:** Headquarters, engine development, some vehicle assembly
- **Huntsville, AL:** Engine manufacturing (BE-4), refurbishment facility
- **Cape Canaveral, FL:** Vehicle integration, launch operations

## Development Timeline
- **2012:** Development initiated
- **2016:** First hardware fabrication
- **2024:** First vehicle rollout to pad
- **2025:** Maiden flight (NG-1)
- **2025:** First successful booster landing (NG-2)

## Key Technologies
1. **7-meter Diameter:** Largest fairing in commercial market
2. **LNG/LOX First Stage:** First operational LNG rocket engine
3. **LH2/LOX Upper Stage:** High-energy upper stage for demanding orbits
4. **Ship Landing:** Ocean-based recovery (no return to launch site)
5. **16-day Turnaround:** Rapid reuse target

## Future Development
- **Project Jarvis:** Evaluating reusable second stage
- **Increased Flight Rate:** Target 12 launches per year by 2027
- **Fairing Recovery:** Potential future enhancement
