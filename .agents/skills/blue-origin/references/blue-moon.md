# Blue Moon Lunar Lander

## Overview
Blue Moon is Blue Origin's family of lunar landers designed to enable sustained human presence on the Moon. The program includes both cargo and crewed variants, supporting NASA's Artemis program and commercial lunar activities.

## Program Philosophy
> "Establishing permanent human presence on the Moon is a vital step to realizing the Blue Origin vision."
> — Blue Origin

## Blue Moon Mark 1 (MK1)

### Purpose
Uncrewed cargo lander for delivering payloads to the lunar surface

### Specifications
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Type | Uncrewed cargo lander |
| Height | ~8 meters |
| Payload Capacity | 3,000 kg to lunar surface |
| Launch Vehicle | New Glenn (7m fairing) |
| Propulsion | 1 × BE-7 engine |
| Propellant | LH2 / LOX |
| Landing Sites | Anywhere on lunar surface |
| Refueling | Not required (single launch) |

### Mission Profile
1. Launch on New Glenn
2. TLI burn by New Glenn upper stage
3. Lunar orbit insertion (optional)
4. Powered descent using BE-7
5. Soft landing on lunar surface
6. Payload deployment

### Pathfinder Missions
| Mission | Date | Description |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Pathfinder 1 | 2026 | Demonstrate lander systems, BE-7 performance, life support |
| Pathfinder 2 | 2026+ | Customer payload delivery mission |

### CLPS Connection
- Part of NASA Commercial Lunar Payload Services provider pool
- 30× capacity of typical CLPS landers (~100 kg)
- Targeting NASA and commercial customers

## Blue Moon Mark 2 (MK2)

### Purpose
Crewed lunar lander for NASA Artemis missions and commercial human exploration

### Specifications
| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Type | Crewed lunar lander (HLS) |
| Height | 16 meters |
| Dry Mass | 16,000 kg |
| Gross Mass | 45,000+ kg (with propellant) |
| Crew Capacity | 4 astronauts |
| Surface Duration | Up to 30 days |
| Payload (surface) | 20,000 kg (with return) / 30,000 kg (one-way) |
| Propulsion | 1 × BE-7 (deep throttle) |
| Propellant | LH2 / LOX |
| Refueling | Required in lunar orbit |

### NASA Contract
- **Program:** Sustaining Lunar Development (SLD)
- **Award:** $3.4 billion
- **Mission:** Artemis V (planned ~2029)
- **Status:** Under development

### Architecture
- **Single-stage reusable design**
- Lands, stays on surface, takes off with same vehicle
- Refueled in lunar orbit for return journey
- Optimized for New Glenn 7m fairing

### Key Technologies

#### BE-7 Engine
- **Type:** Dual expander cycle, LH2/LOX
- **Thrust:** ~10,000 lbf (44.5 kN)
- **Throttle Range:** 10:1 (critical for landing/ascent)
- **Application:** Both descent and ascent

#### Zero-Boil-Off Cryogenics
- Active cooling system maintains cryogenic propellants
- Target temperature: 20 Kelvin for LH2
- Enables long-duration storage in lunar orbit and on surface
- Critical for reusable architecture

#### Cislunar Transporter
- **Developer:** Lockheed Martin
- **Purpose:** Deliver propellant from Earth to lunar orbit
- **Function:** Refuel Blue Moon MK2 in NRHO (Near Rectilinear Halo Orbit)

### Evolution from Original HLS Bid
- **Original (2020):** Three-stage modular architecture
  - Transfer Element: Northrop Grumman
  - Descent Element: Blue Origin
  - Ascent Element: Lockheed Martin
- **Current (2023):** Single-stage reusable design
  - Simplified architecture
  - Reduced cost ($3.4B vs. ~$6B original bid)
  - Single BE-7 engine (vs. multiple BE-7s in descent element)

## Mission Timeline (Artemis V)

### Pre-Mission
- Blue Moon MK2 launched to lunar orbit on New Glenn
- Cislunar Transporter delivers propellant for refueling
- Vehicle checkout in NRHO

### Landing Phase (Day 0)
- T-2 hours: Final checks, crew ingress
- T-0: Undock from Gateway/Orion
- T+10 min: Powered descent initiation
- T+12 min: Landing at lunar south pole

### Surface Operations (Days 0-30)
- Crew of 4 astronauts
- EVA sorties for exploration
- Sample collection (target: 100+ kg)
- In-situ resource utilization experiments
- Habitat deployment

### Ascent Phase (Day 30)
- T-2 hours: Pre-flight checks
- T-0: Liftoff from lunar surface
- T+8 min: Ascent to lunar orbit
- T+2 hours: Rendezvous with Orion/Gateway

## Strategic Significance

### NASA Artemis Program
- Second HLS provider alongside SpaceX Starship
- Provides redundancy for lunar access
- Different architecture (single-stage vs. Starship's multi-stage)

### Commercial Lunar Economy
- Enables sustained lunar presence
- Infrastructure for lunar resources
- Path to Mars via lunar experience

### Blue Origin Investment
- Company contributing "well north" of $3.4B contract value
- Self-funded MK1 pathfinder missions
- Long-term vision for lunar economy
