## § 7 · Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: Breakthrough Therapy Designation Request

**Context**: Novel oncology therapy showing 60% ORR in Phase 1b vs 20% standard of care in relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma.

**BTD Request Package**:
1. **Cover Letter**: Summary of designation criteria met
2. **Background**: Disease seriousness (fatal if untreated), unmet need
3. **Preliminary Clinical Evidence**:
   - Study design: Phase 1b, single-arm, n=25
   - Efficacy: ORR 60% (15/25), CR 20%, mDOR not reached
   - Comparison: Historical SOC ORR 20%, PFS 4 months
   - Substantial improvement: 3x response rate, durable responses
4. **Mechanistic Rationale**: Novel target, strong preclinical support
5. **Development Plan**: Phase 2/3 design, biomarker strategy

**Outcome**: BTD granted → Intensive FDA engagement → Rolling review enabled

### Scenario 2: 510(k) Submission for Diagnostic Device

**Context**: New in vitro diagnostic for early sepsis detection.

**Device Classification Analysis**:
- Predicate search: Class II 510(k) cleared devices for same intended use
- Substantial equivalence: Same technological characteristics, same intended use
- Different question: Improved sensitivity (clinical performance)

**510(k) Content**:
1. **Device Description**: Principle of operation, components, controls
2. **Indications for Use**: "Qualitative detection of procalcitonin..."
3. **Predicate Comparison**: Side-by-side table with K-number reference
4. **Performance Data**:
   - Analytical: LOD, LOQ, precision, interference
   - Clinical: Sensitivity 95%, Specificity 92%, NPV 99%
5. **Proposed Labeling**: Instructions for use, limitations
6. **Sterilization/Shelf Life**: Validation data
7. **Biocompatibility**: ISO 10993 testing

**Review Timeline**: 90 days traditional, RTA (refuse to accept) check at Day 15

### Scenario 3: Response to Complete Response Letter

**Context**: NDA received CRL citing deficiencies in manufacturing (CMC) and need for additional safety data.

**Response Strategy**:
1. **CRL Analysis**:
   - CMC deficiencies: Drug substance specification issues
   - Safety concern: Signal in liver enzymes requiring longer follow-up
   - Other items: Minor labeling edits

2. **Response Package**:
   - CMC: Revised specifications, additional validation batches, stability data
   - Safety: 6-month safety update from ongoing studies (n=500 additional patients)
   - Labeling: Revised warnings and precautions section

3. **Resubmission**:
   - Class 2 resubmission (6-month review)
   - Pre-meeting with FDA to confirm adequacy
   - Complete response to all CRL items

**Outcome**: Approval following resubmission

### Scenario 4: Orphan Drug Designation Strategy

**Context**: Developing therapy for rare genetic disorder (prevalence 0.5/10,000).

**ODD Request**:
1. **Disease Prevalence**: Literature review showing < 200,000 US patients
2. **Scientific Rationale**: Preclinical efficacy in disease model
3. **Medical Plausibility**: Mechanism of action supports indication
4. **Regulatory Strategy**:
   - ODD provides 7-year exclusivity
   - Tax credit for 50% of clinical development costs
   - Waiver of FDA application fee (~$3M)
   - Grants available for development

**Clinical Development Planning**:
- Natural history study to define endpoints
- Registry development for recruitment
- International collaboration (US + EU orphan status)
- Adaptive trial design for limited population

### Scenario 5: Labeling Negotiations

**Context**: NDA approval imminent; negotiating prescribing information.

**Key Sections**:
1. **Indications**: Broad vs narrow (efficacy vs reimbursement considerations)
2. **Dosage**: Dosing regimen, dose modifications
3. **Warnings and Precautions**: Safety signals requiring prominence
4. **Adverse Reactions**: Table of ≥2% and > placebo incidence
5. **Clinical Studies**: Description of pivotal trials supporting efficacy
6. **Use in Specific Populations**: Pregnancy, pediatric, hepatic/renal impairment

**Negotiation Points**:
- Sponsor seeks: "Improves survival" (OS benefit)
- FDA proposes: "Delays disease progression" (PFS endpoint)
- Resolution: "Improves progression-free survival; OS data immature"

**Approval**: Final labeling agreed, REMS not required
