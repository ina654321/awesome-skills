# Airbus Commercial Aircraft Reference

## Overview

Airbus Commercial Aircraft is the company's largest division, representing approximately 71% of consolidated revenue (€51.5B of €73.4B in FY2025). The division has delivered more commercial aircraft than Boeing for 7 consecutive years.

## Aircraft Programs

### A220 Family

**Origins:** Former Bombardier C Series, acquired by Airbus in July 2018

**Models:**
- A220-100: 100-135 passengers, range 3,450nm
- A220-300: 120-160 passengers, range 3,350nm

**Key Features:**
- Pratt & Whitney PW1500G geared turbofan engines
- 20% fuel burn advantage over comparable aircraft
- Carbon composite wings
- 3-3 cabin configuration with wider middle seat

**2025 Performance:**
- Deliveries: 93 aircraft
- Orders: 49 aircraft
- Final assembly: Mirabel, Canada and Mobile, Alabama

**Customers:** Delta, AirBaltic (launch operator), jetBlue, Air Canada, Air France

### A320neo Family

**The Best-Selling Commercial Aircraft Program**

**Variants:**
| Variant | Typical Seats | Max Range | Key Application |
|---------|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| A319neo | 140-160 | 3,750nm | Thin routes, hot/high |
| A320neo | 165-195 | 3,500nm | Core single-aisle |
| A321neo | 185-236 | 4,000nm | High-density routes |
| A321LR | 185-220 | 4,700nm | Long-haul narrowbody |
| A321XLR | 180-220 | 4,700nm+ | Transatlantic narrowbody |

**Engine Options:**
- CFM International LEAP-1A
- Pratt & Whitney PW1100G-JM (Geared Turbofan)

**Performance Improvements vs CEO:**
- 20% fuel burn reduction
- 50% noise reduction
- 5% lower operating costs
- 900kg additional payload/range

**2025 Performance:**
- Deliveries: 607 aircraft
- Orders: 656 aircraft
- Production rate: Target 75/month by 2027

**Production Sites:**
- Toulouse, France (2 FALs)
- Hamburg, Germany (3 FALs including A321XLR)
- Mobile, Alabama (2 FALs - second opened Oct 2025)
- Tianjin, China (1 FAL, expanded 2025)

### A330neo Family

**Models:**
- A330-800: 220-260 passengers, 8,150nm range
- A330-900: 260-300 passengers, 7,200nm range

**Key Features:**
- Rolls-Royce Trent 7000 engines (derived from 787 Trent 1000)
- 14% fuel burn improvement vs A330ceo
- Common type rating with A350 (90% commonality)
- Airspace cabin (A350-derived)

**2025 Performance:**
- Deliveries: 36 aircraft
- Orders: 100 aircraft (strong widebody demand)

### A350 Family

**Flagship Long-Haul Program**

**Variants:**
| Variant | Typical Config | Range | MTOW |
|---------|---------------|-------|------|
| A350-900 | 315 passengers | 8,100nm | 283t |
| A350-1000 | 369 passengers | 8,400nm | 319t |
| A350F | Cargo | 4,700nm (payload) | 319t |

**Technology:**
- 53% composite materials by weight
- 25% fuel burn reduction vs previous generation
- Advanced aerodynamics (morphing winglet)
- Fly-by-wire with sidestick controls

**Cabin:**
- 18-inch standard economy seat
- Lower cabin altitude (6,000ft vs 8,000ft)
- Higher humidity (20% vs 4%)
- Airspace cabin design

**2025 Performance:**
- Deliveries: 57 aircraft
- Orders: 193 aircraft
- A350F: Entering service 2025-2026

### Supply Chain

**Key Suppliers:**
- **Engines:** CFM International (GE/Safran), Pratt & Whitney, Rolls-Royce
- **Avionics:** Thales, Collins Aerospace, Honeywell
- **Structures:** Spirit AeroSystems (acquired partially 2025), GKN Aerospace
- **Landing Gear:** Safran Landing Systems

**2025 Challenges:**
- Engine delivery constraints (CFM LEAP, PW1100G)
- ~60 "gliders" (aircraft without engines) at peak
- A320 fuselage panel quality issue (Dec 2025)
- Target: 820 deliveries adjusted to ~790 due to issues
