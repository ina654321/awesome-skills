# Airbus Corporate Overview

## Company Profile

**Full Name:** Airbus SE (Societas Europaea)
**Headquarters:** Toulouse, France
**Founded:** 1970 (as Airbus Industrie), 2001 (EADS), 2014 (renamed Airbus Group), 2017 (Airbus SE)
**Stock Exchange:** Euronext Paris (EADSY ADR in US)
**Employees:** 165,294 (FY2025)

## History

### Origins (1960s-1970s)

**Genesis:**
- French-German-British government initiative
- Goal: Challenge American dominance in commercial aviation
- Airbus A300: World's first twin-engine widebody

**Key Milestones:**
- 1967: Franco-German treaty signed
- 1970: Airbus Industrie formally established
- 1972: A300 first flight
- 1974: A300 enters service with Air France

### Expansion (1980s-1990s)

**Single-Aisle Revolution:**
- 1987: A320 launch (fly-by-wire, side-stick)
- 1988: A320 first flight
- 1988: Entry into service with Air France
- 1990s: A321, A319, A318 derivatives

**Corporate Structure:**
- 1992: Airbus becomes subsidiary of Airbus Industrie
- 2000: A3XX (A380) program launched

### EADS Era (2000-2014)

**Merger:**
- 2000: Aerospatiale-Matra, DASA, CASA merge to form EADS
- 2001: EADS officially established
- Airbus becomes division of EADS

**Expansion:**
- 2005: A380 first flight
- 2007: A380 enters service
- 2008: A400M first flight

### Modern Airbus (2014-Present)

**Renaming:**
- 2014: EADS renamed Airbus Group
- 2017: Airbus SE (Societas Europaea)

**Strategic Moves:**
- 2018: Acquired majority stake in C Series (became A220)
- 2020: Announced ZEROe hydrogen concept
- 2021: Major restructuring due to COVID-19
- 2024: Agreement to acquire Spirit AeroSystems work packages

## Business Divisions

### 1. Airbus Commercial Aircraft

**Revenue:** €51.5 billion (70% of total)
**CEO:** Christian Scherer (transitioning to Lars Wagner)
**Products:** A220, A320, A330, A350 families
**Sites:** Toulouse, Hamburg, Mobile, Mirabel, Tianjin

### 2. Airbus Helicopters

**Revenue:** €9.0 billion (12% of total)
**CEO:** Bruno Even (Matthieu Louvot from April 2026)
**Products:** H125 to H225, NH90, Tiger
**Headquarters:** Marignane, France

### 3. Airbus Defence & Space

**Revenue:** €13.4 billion (18% of total)
**CEO:** Mike Schoellhorn
**Products:** A400M, A330 MRTT, Eurofighter, satellites
**Headquarters:** Munich, Germany
