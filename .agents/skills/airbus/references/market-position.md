## Market Segments

### Narrowbody Market (150-240 seats)

**Market Size:**
- 70% of commercial aircraft deliveries
- Fastest-growing segment
- Replacement cycle ongoing

**Airbus Position:**
- Market leader with A320neo family
- A321neo/XLR capturing upsizing trend
- A220 addressing 100-150 seat segment

**Key Trends:**
1. **Upgauging:** Airlines prefer larger aircraft (A321)
2. **Long-range narrowbody:** A321XLR opening new markets
3. **Frequency vs. capacity:** Route optimization

### Widebody Market (250+ seats)

**Market Size:**
- 30% of deliveries
- Higher value per unit
- Post-COVID recovery ongoing

**Airbus Position:**
- A350: Strong in long-haul premium segment
- A330neo: Regional and secondary routes
- Competitive vs 787, strong vs 777X delays

**Key Trends:**
1. **Point-to-point:** Bypassing hubs
2. **Premium economy growth:** A350 advantage
3. **Freighter demand:** A350F entering market

### Regional Market (Below 150 seats)

**Market Size:**
- Growing segment
- Scope clause restrictions in US
- Emerging market importance

**Airbus Position:**
- A220: Leading modern regional jet
- No direct competitor in 100-150 seat
- Potential A220-500 stretch
