# Airbus Helicopters Reference

## Overview

Airbus Helicopters is the world's largest helicopter manufacturer by revenue and deliveries. Headquartered in Marignane, France, the division operates globally with 12,000+ employees.

**2025 Performance:**
- Orders: 544 gross / 536 net (from 205 customers in 50 countries)
- Deliveries: 392 helicopters
- Market share: 51% civil/parapublic, 28% military
- Revenue: €9.0 billion (+13% YoY)

## Civil & Parapublic Portfolio

### Light Single-Engine

#### H125 (formerly AS350 B3e)

**Specifications:**
- MTOW: 2,250 kg (4,960 lb)
- Capacity: Pilot + 4-5 passengers
- Engine: Safran Arriel 2D (952 shp)
- Max speed: 287 km/h (155 kts)
- Range: 640 km (345 nm)

**Key Features:**
- Market leader in light single segment
- "Squirrel" heritage—over 38 million flight hours
- Proven in extreme conditions (Mt. Everest operations)
- Simple, reliable, cost-effective

**2025 Deliveries:** 129 units (33% of total)

**Production:**
- Marignane, France
- Columbus, Mississippi (USA)
- Vemagal, Karnataka, India (under construction—4th FAL)

**Applications:**
- Utility and aerial work
- Tourism and sightseeing
- Training
- Private ownership

#### H130 (formerly EC130 T2)

**Specifications:**
- MTOW: 2,500 kg (5,511 lb)
- Capacity: Pilot + 6-7 passengers
- Engine: Safran Arriel 2D
- Fenestron shrouded tail rotor

**Key Features:**
- 50% quieter than comparable helicopters
- Wide cabin, panoramic windows
- Popular for tourism and VIP transport

**2025 Deliveries:** 47 units

### Light Twin-Engine

#### H135 (formerly EC135 T3/P3)

**Specifications:**
- MTOW: 2,980 kg (6,570 lb)
- Engines: 2 × Safran Arrius 2B2 or PW206B2
- Capacity: Pilot + 6-7 passengers
- Max speed: 259 km/h (140 kts)

**Key Features:**
- No-tail-rotor (NOTAR) or conventional options
- Compact footprint for urban operations
- Excellent autorotation characteristics

**2025 Deliveries:** 45 units

**Primary Markets:**
- Emergency medical services (EMS)
- Law enforcement
- Corporate transport
- Military training

#### H140 (NEW 2025)

**Launch:** VERTICON 2025, Dallas

**Specifications:**
- T-tail configuration (innovative for light twins)
- Advanced avionics suite
- Improved hot-and-high performance

**2025 Orders:** 61 firm orders at launch

**Positioning:** Next-generation light twin bridging H135 and H145

#### H145 (formerly EC145 T2)

**The Best-Seller**

**Specifications:**
- MTOW: 3,700 kg (8,157 lb)
- Engines: 2 × Safran Arriel 2E (FADEC)
- Capacity: Pilot + 8-9 passengers
- Max speed: 278 km/h (150 kts)
- Range: 680 km (370 nm)

**Key Features:**
- Fenestron tail rotor
- Compact size, large cabin volume
- Single-pilot IFR certified
- Rear clamshell doors for easy loading

**2025 Deliveries:** 149 units (38% of total)
**2025 Orders:** 149 (civil/military combined)

**Variants:**
- H145 (civil)
- H145M (military with weapons capability)
- UH-72 Lakota (US Army variant)
- ACH145 (corporate configuration)

**Major 2025 Orders:**
- Spain: 50 H145Ms
- Germany: 20 additional H145Ms (total fleet 82)

### Medium Twin-Engine

#### H160

**The New Generation**

**Specifications:**
- MTOW: 6,050 kg (13,340 lb)
- Engines: 2 × Safran Arrano (1,100 shp each)
- Capacity: Pilot + 11-12 passengers
- Max speed: 296 km/h (160 kts)
- Range: 880 km (475 nm)

**Key Features:**
- 5-blade Blue Edge rotor (reduced noise, improved efficiency)
- Spheriflex main rotor hub
- Helionix avionics suite
- First all-composite airframe in class

**2025 Deliveries:** 30 units
**Market Position:** ~50% of civil/parapublic medium twin market

**Variants:**
- H160 (civil)
- H160M Guépard (military variant for French armed forces)
- ACH160 (corporate configuration)

**First Flight:** Military H160M completed first flight 2025

### Super Medium Twin-Engine

#### H175 (formerly EC175)

**Specifications:**
- MTOW: 7,800 kg (17,196 lb)
- Engines: 2 × Pratt & Whitney PT6C-67E
- Capacity: Pilot + 16-18 passengers
- Max speed: 315 km/h (170 kts)

**Key Features:**
- Designed for offshore oil & gas
- Long range (1,100+ km)
- Excellent payload capability
- Twin-mission capability (passenger/cargo)

**2025 Deliveries:** 15 units

**Major Orders:**
- Spain: First orders for H175M military variant (2025)

### Heavy Twin-Engine

#### H215 / H225 (Super Puma Family)

**H215 (modernized AS332L1):**
- MTOW: 9,150 kg
- Proven platform, cost-effective heavy lift
- 2025: Greece ordered 8 for firefighting

**H225 (EC225):**
- MTOW: 11,200 kg (24,692 lb)
- 19 passengers, long-range SAR capability
- 2025: Morocco ordered 10 H225Ms

## Military Helicopters

### NH90

**Multi-Role Military Helicopter**

**Program:** Joint European program (France, Germany, Italy, Netherlands)

**Variants:**
- NH90 TTH (Tactical Transport Helicopter)
- NH90 NFH (NATO Frigate Helicopter - naval)

**Specifications:**
- MTOW: 10,600 kg (23,369 lb)
- Capacity: 20 troops / 2,500 kg internal / 4,200 kg external
- Max speed: 300 km/h (162 kts)

**2025 Activity:**
- Spain: 31 ordered across armed forces
- Netherlands: 3 additional units
- Germany: First NH90 Sea Tiger delivered (anti-submarine variant)
- Greece: Follow-on support contract

**Deliveries:** 34 units in 2025

### Tiger

**Attack Helicopter**

**Program:** France-German-Spanish collaboration

**Variants:**
- Tiger HAD (French/Spanish)
- Tiger UHT (German)

**Status:**
- Ongoing modernization programs
- Future combat system integration studies

## Innovation Programs

### Racer High-Speed Demonstrator

**Objective:** Develop high-speed rotorcraft technology

**2025 Achievement:**
- Cruise speed: 440 km/h (240 kts)
- Exceeded original performance targets
- Compound helicopter design (main rotor + lateral pusher propellers)

**Applications:** Future high-speed platforms for SAR, EMS, military

### VSR700 Unmanned Aerial System

**Specifications:**
- Based on Cabri G2 helicopter
- 700 kg MTOW
- 8+ hour endurance
- Autonomous capabilities

**2025 Progress:**
- First contracts signed
- 6 units for French Ministry of Armed Forces
- Integration with HTeaming (crewed-uncrewed teaming)
