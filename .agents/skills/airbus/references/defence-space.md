# Airbus Defence & Space Reference

## Overview

Airbus Defence & Space is Europe's largest defence contractor by revenue and the second-largest space company globally. The division provides military aircraft, satellites, and secure communications systems.

**2025 Financial Performance:**
- Revenue: €13.4 billion (+11% YoY)
- EBIT Adjusted: €798 million (turnaround to profitability)
- Order Intake: €17.7 billion
- Order Book: €61.4 billion
- Employees: ~37,000

**Strategic Focus Areas:**
1. Air Power (airlifters, tankers, combat aircraft)
2. Space Systems (satellites, launchers, exploration)
3. Connected Intelligence (secure communications, cybersecurity)

## Military Aircraft

### A400M Atlas

**Role:** Strategic/tactical airlifter

**Specifications:**
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Maximum payload | 37 tonnes (81,600 lb) |
| Cargo hold volume | 340 m³ (12,000 ft³) |
| Cargo hold dimensions | 17.7m × 4.0m × 3.85m |
| Max range (payload) | 3,300 nm (37t) / 4,800 nm (20t) |
| Max speed | Mach 0.72 (555 km/h) |
| Service ceiling | 40,000 ft |
| Runway requirement (max payload) | 3,100 ft (unpaved capable) |

**Key Capabilities:**
- **Strategic lift:** Carries outsized cargo (helicopters, armored vehicles)
- **Tactical operations:** Short/unpaved runway capability
- **Air-to-air refueling:** 50t fuel capacity, boom and hose systems
  - Compatible: Eurofighter, F-16, F-18, Rafale, C-130, helicopters
- **Aerial delivery:** Certified to 25t drops
- **Paratroop:** 116 fully equipped paratroopers

**Payload Examples:**
- 1 × NH90 helicopter (internal)
- 1 × Stryker armored vehicle
- 2 × Light strike vehicles
- 9 × military pallets + 116 troops
- Medevac: 66 stretchers + medical team

**Program Status (2025):**
- Total ordered: 178 aircraft (7 partner nations)
- Delivered: 131 aircraft
- German fleet: 47 aircraft (largest operator)
- French fleet: 25 aircraft
- UK fleet: 22 aircraft
- Production secured through 2029

**2025 Financial:**
- €73 million charge in Q4 (long-term cost assumptions)
- Cash positive for full year 2025

### A330 MRTT (Multi Role Tanker Transport)

**Role:** Aerial refueling and strategic transport

**Specifications:**
- Fuel capacity: 111 tonnes (245,000 lb)
- Passengers: 300 (transport configuration)
- Cargo: 45 tonnes or 8 military pallets

**Refueling Systems:**
- Aerial refueling boom system (ARBS)
- Hose and drogue under-wing pods
- Fuselage refueling unit (hose and drogue)

**2025 Status:**
- 111 aircraft ordered globally
- Operators: Australia, France, Netherlands, Saudi Arabia, Singapore, South Korea, Spain, UK, UAE, NATO MMF
- New orders: 2 in 2025

**Key Advantage:**
- Based on proven A330 airframe
- Widebody passenger comfort for transport missions
- Large fuel offload capacity
- Civil certification baseline

### Eurofighter Typhoon

**Role:** Multi-role combat aircraft

**Program:** European consortium—Airbus (46%), BAE Systems (33%), Leonardo (21%)

**Specifications:**
- Max speed: Mach 2.0+
- Range: 1,800 nm (combat radius)
- Weapons: 13 hardpoints, various missiles, bombs

**Production Tranches:**
- Tranche 1: Early production
- Tranche 2: Enhanced capability
- Tranche 3: Current standard (AESA radar)
- Tranche 4/5: Enhanced variants

**2025 Developments:**
- Germany ordered 20 Tranche 5 aircraft
- Production increasing from 14 to 20 aircraft/year
- Turkey joined as new customer
- Italy and Germany placed additional orders

### C295 / CN235

**C295 Tactical Transport:**

- Maritime patrol, AEW, SIGINT variants

**CN235 Light Transport:**

### Future Combat Air System (FCAS/SCAF)

**Program:** Franco-German-Spanish next-generation combat aircraft

**Components:**

**Status (2025):**

### Eurodrone

**Program:** European consortium (Airbus, Leonardo, Dassault)

## Space Systems

### Satellites

**Earth Observation:**
- **Pléiades Neo:** Very high-resolution optical constellation (30cm resolution)
- **TerraSAR-X:** Radar imaging (all-weather, day/night)
- **Copernicus:** ESA Earth observation program (Sentinel satellites)

**Telecommunications:**
- **OneSat:** Flexible software-defined satellites
  - 2024: €1+ billion charges due to delays
  - Restructuring ongoing
- **Eurostar:** Geostationary platform
- **Secure milcom:** Military communications satellites

**Navigation:**
- Galileo satellites (European GNSS)
- EGNOS augmentation system

### Launchers

**ArianeGroup (50% Airbus / 50% Safran):**
- **Ariane 6:** New European heavy launcher
  - First flight: July 2024
  - Operational flights ongoing
- **Vega C:** Light-lift launcher

### Space Exploration

**ISS Modules:**
- Columbus laboratory (operational)
- European Service Module for Orion (NASA Artemis program)
  - ESM-1: Delivered, Artemis I successful
  - ESM-2: Artemis II
  - ESM-3: Artemis III

**Future Programs:**
- Argonaut lunar lander (ESA)
- European Large Logistics Lander (EL3)

## Restructuring & Transformation

**2024-2025 Changes:**
- 2,000+ positions reduced (management and back-office)
- Regional offices consolidated: 10 → 4
- Organization aligned to three business lines:
  - Air Power
  - Space Systems  
  - Connected Intelligence

**Results:**
- H1 2025: +17% revenue, return to profitability
- Full year 2025: Adjusted EBIT positive
