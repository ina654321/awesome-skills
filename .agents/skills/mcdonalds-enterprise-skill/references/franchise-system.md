# McDonald's Franchise System Guide

## Overview

McDonald's operates the world's most successful franchise system with approximately **95% of restaurants (41,000+) owned and operated by independent franchisees**. The franchise model is the cornerstone of McDonald's global expansion strategy.

## Franchise Model Types

### 1. Conventional Franchise

**Description:**
McDonald's owns or secures a long-term lease on the restaurant land and building. The franchisee provides equipment, signage, seating, and décor.

**Markets:**
- United States
- Canada
- United Kingdom
- Australia
- Most developed markets

**Revenue to McDonald's:**
- Rent (percentage of sales or fixed)
- Royalties (4% of gross sales)
- Initial franchise fee ($45,000)

**Franchisee Investment:**
- Initial franchise fee: $45,000
- Equipment, signage, seating: $500,000-1,200,000
- Real estate deposit: Varies
- Working capital: $250,000+
- **Total: $1.0-2.2 million**

**Agreement Term:** 20 years

### 2. Developmental License

**Description:**
The licensee owns the land, building, and all capital investment. McDonald's has no capital invested and receives only royalties.

**Markets:**
- China
- Japan
- Brazil
- Mexico
- Most of Latin America
- Many Asian markets

**Revenue to McDonald's:**
- Royalties (percentage of sales)
- Initial fees
- No rent (licensee owns real estate)

**Franchisee Investment:**
- Full restaurant development cost
- All real estate costs
- Higher capital requirement than conventional

**Benefits:**
- Minimal capital from McDonald's
- Rapid market expansion
- Local expertise and ownership

### 3. Affiliate Structure

**Description:**
McDonald's holds an equity stake in the operating company, sharing both risk and reward.

**Markets:**
- China (through CITIC/Carlyle partnership)
- Strategic priority markets

**Structure:**
- McDonald's: Minority equity stake
- Local Partner: Majority ownership and operations
- Shared governance and decision-making

## Franchisee Selection Criteria

### Financial Requirements

| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| Non-borrowed personal resources | $500,000+ |
| Initial investment capability | $1.0-2.2 million |
| Ongoing working capital | $250,000+ |
| Net worth | $1.5 million+ |

### Experience Requirements
- Restaurant/retail operations experience preferred
- Business management experience
- Track record of success
- Alignment with McDonald's values

### Selection Process
1. **Application**: Online submission with financial documentation
2. **Review**: Background checks, financial verification
3. **Interviews**: Multiple rounds with operations leadership
4. **Training Acceptance**: Commitment to Hamburger University program
5. **Approval**: Final approval from franchisee selection committee

## Franchisee Training

### Hamburger University

**Location:** Chicago, Illinois (global headquarters)

**Programs:**
- Restaurant Leadership Program (12-18 months)
- Operations Consultant Course
- Business Leadership Program
- Executive Education

**Graduates:** 80,000+ annually across all programs

**Curriculum:**
- Operations excellence (QSC)
- Business management
- Financial management
- Leadership development
- Technology systems

### Field Support

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Operations Consultant | Day-to-day restaurant support |
| Field Service Manager | Multi-unit oversight |
| Market Manager | Geographic market leadership |
| Regional Director | Strategic regional management |

## Franchisee Governance

### National Franchisee Leadership Alliance (NFLA)

**Purpose:** Formal input mechanism for franchisee voices in company decisions

**Structure:**
- Elected leadership from franchisee community
- Committee structure mirroring corporate functions
- Regular meetings with McDonald's leadership

### Advisory Councils

| Council | Focus Area |
|---------|------------|
| Operations Council | Standards, procedures, equipment |
| Marketing Council | Advertising, promotions, brand |
| Menu Council | Product development, LTOs |
| Supply Chain Council | Sourcing, distribution, quality |

## Franchisee Economics

### Revenue Model

| Revenue Stream | Typical Range |
|----------------|---------------|
| Food & Beverage Sales | 85-90% of revenue |
| Other Operating Income | 10-15% of revenue |

### Cost Structure

| Cost Category | % of Sales |
|---------------|------------|
| Food & Paper | 28-32% |
| Labor | 25-30% |
| Occupancy | 8-12% |
| Marketing | 4% |
| Royalties | 4% |
| Utilities & Other | 5-8% |
| **Total Costs** | **75-85%** |
| **Cash Flow Margin** | **15-25%** |

### Profitability Benchmarks

| Metric | Top Quartile | Average | Bottom Quartile |
|--------|--------------|---------|-----------------|
| Annual Sales | $4.0M+ | $3.0M | $2.2M |
| Cash Flow Margin | 20%+ | 13% | 8% |
| Cash Flow | $600K+ | $300K | $150K |

## Franchisee Relations Best Practices

### Communication
- **Monthly Newsletter**: Corporate updates, success stories
- **Town Halls**: CEO-led virtual meetings
- **Regional Meetings**: Market-specific discussions
- **One-on-Ones**: Operations consultant check-ins

### Recognition Programs
- **Ray Kroc Awards**: Top 1% of franchisees globally
- **Outstanding Restaurant Awards**: Operational excellence
- **Community Service Awards**: Local impact

### Conflict Resolution
1. Operations Consultant intervention
2. Field Service Manager escalation
3. Market-level mediation
4. NFLA involvement
5. Formal arbitration (rare)

## Key Success Factors

### For McDonald's Corporation
- Maintain brand standards without micromanaging
- Provide marketing support that drives traffic
- Ensure supply chain reliability
- Offer competitive technology solutions
- Communicate transparently

### For Franchisees
- Follow the system—don't reinvent
- Invest in crew training and development
- Maintain QSC standards religiously
- Engage with local community
- Reinvest in restaurant maintenance

## Common Challenges

| Challenge | Solution Approach |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Rising labor costs | Technology, productivity improvements |
| Supply chain disruptions | Diversification, inventory management |
| Competitive pressure | Value offerings, marketing differentiation |
| Technology investment | Financing options, ROI demonstration |
| Work-life balance | Multi-unit ownership, strong management teams |
