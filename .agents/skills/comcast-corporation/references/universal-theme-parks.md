# Universal Destinations & Experiences

## Overview

**Universal Destinations & Experiences** (formerly Universal Parks & Resorts) is the theme park division of NBCUniversal, a subsidiary of Comcast Corporation. With world-class destinations in the United States, Japan, and China, Universal theme parks generated $9.8B in revenue in 2025.

## Park Portfolio

### United States

#### Universal Orlando Resort (Florida)

**Overview:** Premier multi-park resort destination

**Parks:**
1. **Universal Studios Florida**
   - Classic movie-themed attractions
   - The Wizarding World of Harry Potter - Diagon Alley
   - Transformers, Fast & Furious, Simpsons

2. **Universal's Islands of Adventure**
   - Thrill rides and immersive lands
   - The Wizarding World of Harry Potter - Hogsmeade
   - Jurassic Park, Marvel Super Hero Island
   - Seuss Landing

3. **Universal's Volcano Bay**
   - Water theme park
   - TapuTapu wearable technology
   - South Pacific theme

4. **Universal Epic Universe** (Opened May 22, 2025)
   - $7.7B investment
   - 5 immersive worlds
   - Doubles Orlando resort size
   - 750-acre development
   - Three new hotels

**Epic Universe Worlds:**
| World | IP/Theme | Key Attractions |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| Celestial Park | Original/Space | Central hub, Helios Grand Hotel |
| The Wizarding World of Harry Potter - Ministry of Magic | Harry Potter/Fantastic Beasts | Ministry attraction, 1920s Paris |
| Super Nintendo World | Nintendo | Mario Kart, Donkey Kong, Peach's Castle |
| How to Train Your Dragon - Isle of Berk | DreamWorks | Dragon rides, themed village |
| Dark Universe | Universal Monsters | Frankenstein, Dracula, Creature attractions |

**CityWalk Orlando:**
- Dining and entertainment complex
- Shopping, nightlife, cinema
- Free admission

**On-Site Hotels (Orlando):**
- Universal Helios Grand Hotel (in Epic Universe)
- Universal Stella Nova Resort
- Universal Terra Luna Resort
- Hard Rock Hotel
- Portofino Bay Hotel

## Competitive Position

### vs. Disney Parks

**Disney Strengths:**
- Larger overall attendance
- More parks globally
- Deeper character IP
- Magic Band technology
- Cruise line integration

**Universal Strengths:**
- Thrill rides (more intense attractions)
- Harry Potter (competitive with Disney's top IP)
- Value pricing
- Epic Universe innovation
- Nintendo exclusivity

**Market Position:**
- Orlando: #2 to Disney World
- Hollywood: #1 in LA market
- Japan: #2 to Tokyo Disney

### Competitive Strategy

**Differentiation:**
- Thrill-focused attractions
- Immersive lands (Wizarding World model)
- Adult-oriented entertainment (Halloween Horror Nights)
- Movie studio heritage
- Value proposition

**IP Strategy:**
- Licensed: Harry Potter, Nintendo, Marvel (islands only)
- Owned: Jurassic Park, Fast & Furious, Minions, Classic Monsters
