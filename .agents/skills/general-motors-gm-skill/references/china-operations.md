# China Operations Deep Dive

## Historical Overview

### Market Entry & Growth (1997-2017)

| Year | Milestone | Impact |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 1997 | SAIC-GM joint venture formed | Market entry |
| 2002 | SGMW formed (with Wuling) | Mini-vehicle segment |
| 2004 | 500K units sold | Market share: 10% |
| 2010 | 2M units sold | Post-financial crisis growth |
| 2014 | 3.5M units sold | Peak market share: 14% |
| 2017 | 4M units sold | All-time high volume |

### Peak Performance Era (2010-2017)

**Financial Contribution:**
- Annual profits: $2B+ consistently
- China share of global profits: 25-30%
- Market share: 14% (2017)
- Key brands: Buick (dominant), Chevrolet, Cadillac

## Strategic Response

### 2024-2025 Restructuring

**Operational Changes:**
1. **Plant closures**: Reduced capacity by ~40%
2. **Inventory reduction**: Dealer stock optimization
3. **Workforce reduction**: Voluntary separations, early retirement
4. **Product line simplification**: Eliminate slow-selling models

**Leadership Changes:**
- Jiang Qingxiong appointed SAIC-GM GM (2023)
- Lu Xiao appointed (2025): "2024 was the most difficult year"

### Recovery Strategy (2025-2027)

**Phase 1: Stabilize (2025)**
- Target: Return to profitability by Q4 2025
- Actions: Cost reduction, inventory discipline
- Product: Launch Ultium-based NEVs locally

**Phase 2: Differentiate (2026-2027)**
- SAIC-GM NEV portfolio expansion
- Leverage SGMW for volume
- Software differentiation (Ultifi localized)

**Phase 3: Strategic Decision (Post-2027)**
- JV renewal terms determine long-term commitment
- Options: Continued JV, licensing model, managed exit

### New Product Pipeline

**2025-2026 Launches:**
- Cadillac Optiq (compact luxury SUV)
- Buick NEVs: Electra series
- Chevrolet NEVs: Menlo EV successor
- Wuling EVs: Continued expansion

## Geopolitical Considerations

### US-China Trade Relations

**Direct Impact:**
- GM China operations: Minimal trade disruption
- Local production for local market
- No significant US-China vehicle exports

**Indirect Risks:**
- Technology transfer restrictions
- EV supply chain decoupling
- Joint venture pressure (technology sharing)

### European Tariffs

**2024 EU Anti-Subsidy Tariffs:**
- SAIC-GM/SAIC: 35.3% (provisional)
- Context: Limited current exports to EU
- Future impact: Limits China-as-export-base strategy
