# Electric Vehicle Strategy

## Strategic Evolution

### Original Plan (2020-2022)

| Commitment | Detail | Current Status |
|------------|--------|----------------|
| $35B investment | Through 2025 | Mostly spent |
| 30 new EVs globally | By 2025 | ~15 launched |
| 1M EV capacity | North America 2025 | ~200K actual |
| 2035 all-EV fleet | Cadillac leading | **Abandoned** |

### Strategic Pivot (2024-2025)

**Market Reality:**
- EV growth slowed from 65% (2022) to 15% (2025)
- GM EV inventory: 90+ days supply (vs. 60 optimal)
- Consumer concerns: Price, charging, range anxiety persist

**New Approach:**
| Element | Old Strategy | New Strategy |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Timeline | All-EV by 2035 | Market-responsive flexibility |
| Mix target | 100% EV by 2035 | 50% EV by 2035 (implied) |
| ICE position | Phase out | Maintain through 2030s |
| Hybrid/PHEV | None | Reintroduced for compliance |

## Technology Roadmap

### Battery Technology

**Current: NCMA Chemistry**
- Energy density: Industry competitive
- Cost: ~$100/kWh
- Supplier: LG Energy Solution (joint ventures)

**Near-term: LMR (Lithium Manganese-Rich)**
- Launch: 2026-2027
- Cost reduction: Target $70/kWh
- Benefits: Lower cobalt, reduced cost

**Future: Solid-State**
- Partner: Solid Power
- Timeline: 2028+ for commercialization
- Benefits: Higher density, faster charging, improved safety

### Charging Infrastructure

**DC Fast Charging Capability:**
- Current: 150-350 kW (model dependent)
- Future: 800V architecture for 350kW+ standard

**Charging Partnerships:**
- EVgo: Public charging network
- Tesla Supercharger: NACS adapter (2024+)
- Dealer charging: 3,000+ dealers adding fast chargers

### Software-Defined Vehicle

**Ultifi Platform:**
- Over-the-air (OTA) update capability
- Cloud-based services
- Subscription revenue target: $20B annually by 2030

**Current Services:**
| Service | Price | Attach Rate |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Super Cruise | $25/month | 30%+ on eligible vehicles |
| OnStar | $15-45/month | Legacy subscriber base |
| Connected services | $10-20/month | Growing |

## Market Challenges & Response

### Consumer Barriers

| Barrier | GM Response |
|---------|-------------|
| Price premium | Equinox EV at $35K (Tesla Model Y: $45K) |
| Range anxiety | 300+ mi standard on new models |
| Charging time | 150-350 kW fast charging capability |
| Home charging | Dealer installation partnerships |
| Resale concerns | Lease emphasis, buyback programs |

### Competitive Pressure

| Competitor | Challenge | GM Counter |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| Tesla | Price cuts, brand strength | Undercut on price, dealer service |
| Ford | Lightning early entry | Silverado EV scale, range |
| Hyundai/Kia | Design, value | Brand loyalty, truck expertise |
| Chinese OEMs | Low-cost EVs | Limited US presence (tariffs) |

### Regulatory Environment

**EPA Emissions Standards:**
- 2027-2032: Increasing stringency
- Flexibility: Hybrid/PHEV compliance path
- GM position: Supports standards with flexibility

**State Requirements:**
- California ZEV mandate: Follows with modifications
- Texas/Florida: No mandates, market-driven
- GM approach: National product strategy
