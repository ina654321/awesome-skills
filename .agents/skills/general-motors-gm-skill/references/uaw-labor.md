# UAW Relations & Labor Strategy

## 2023 UAW Strike: Historical Context

### Strike Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Start Date** | September 15, 2023 |
| **End Date** | October 30, 2023 (GM agreement) |
| **Duration** | 6 weeks |
| **Workers Affected** | 145,000 across Detroit Three |
| **GM Workers** | ~46,000 at peak |
| **Strategy** | "Stand Up Strike"—targeted plant walkouts |

### Strike Impact

**Economic Effects:**
- Estimated GDP impact: 0.1% Q3, 0.5% Q4 (annualized)
- Lost production: ~1M units (annual rate) in Q4
- GM lost profit: ~$1.2B (estimated)

**Affected GM Plants:**
- Wentzville, MO: Colorado/Canyon (pickup trucks)
- Toledo, OH: Transmission plant
- Lansing, MI: Delta Township (Buick Enclave, Chevy Traverse)
- Spring Hill, TN: Cadillac/Lyriq, engines
- Arlington, TX: Full-size SUVs (Tahoe, Yukon, Escalade)
- Parts distribution centers: 38 locations

## Labor Relations Strategy

### Recent History (2019-2023)

| Event | Outcome | Management Lesson |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 2019 GM strike | 40 days, $4B+ cost | Need proactive engagement |
| 2023 "Stand Up" | 6 weeks, historic gains | New UAW leadership more aggressive |
| Ultium Cells organizing | UAW successfully organized | EV transition labor risk |

### Current Approach (2025)

**Management Priorities:**
1. **Maintain production continuity**: Avoid work stoppages
2. **Productivity improvements**: 3-5% annual efficiency gains
3. **Automation investment**: Offset labor cost increases
4. **Flexible scheduling**: Overtime management, temp worker utilization

**Negotiation Preparation (2028 Contract):**
- UAW President Shawn Fain re-elected: Likely aggressive stance
- EV profitability: If achieved, UAW will demand share
- Job security: Battery plant workforce expansion

## Competitive Labor Dynamics

### Industry Wage Pressure

**Post-UAW Impact:**
- Non-union automakers raised wages 10-15% (2023-2024)
- Toyota, Honda, Hyundai: Voluntary increases to avoid UAW organizing
- Tesla: Increased wages in response

**Right-to-Work States:**
- Southern states (TN, AL, SC): Lower unionization risk
- GM Spring Hill in Tennessee: Strategic non-union advantage
- EV battery plants: UAW targeting for organization
