### Capacity Adjustments (2024-2025)

**Orion Assembly (Michigan):**
- Previous: Chevrolet Bolt EV, Bolt EUV
- Change: Retooling for ICE trucks (2025)
- Reason: Match production to EV demand
- Investment: $1.6B charge

**CAMI (Canada):**
- Previous: BrightDrop commercial EVs
- Change: Production suspended (May 2025)
- Employment: 500 layoffs
- Future: Seeking alternative product assignment

**Factory Zero:**
- Investment: $2.2B conversion (2020-2022)
- Purpose: Dedicated EV truck/SUV plant
- Products: Hummer EV, Silverado EV, Escalade IQ
- Utilization: Ramping toward 150K+ annually

### Automation Strategy

**Current Automation Levels:**
- Body shop welding: 90%+ automated
- Paint shop: 95%+ automated
- Final assembly: 30-40% automated
- Material handling: Increasing cobot deployment

**Investment Priorities:**
1. Quality: Automated inspection systems
2. Ergonomics: Exoskeletons, assist devices
3. Flexibility: Quick-change tooling for multi-model
4. EV-specific: Battery handling, high-voltage safety

### Labor Cost Management

**2023 UAW Contract Impact:**
- Wage increases: 25% over 4.5 years
- Labor cost per vehicle: +$900 (estimated)
- Productivity offset target: 3-5% annually

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Automation: Robotic welding, material handling
- Mexico sourcing: 25% labor cost advantage
- Supplier integration: Reduced logistics costs
- Overtime management: Balanced capacity
