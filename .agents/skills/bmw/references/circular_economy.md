# BMW Circular Economy Strategy

## Vision

> "We pursue the vision of the circular economy, in which raw materials are kept in circulation for as long as possible and without any loss in quality."

BMW aims to transform from a linear "take-make-dispose" model to a circular system where materials are reused, recycled, and regenerated.

## Strategic Pillars

### 1. Secondary First Material Sourcing

| Material | Current Status | 2030 Target |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Aluminum** | 25-50% recycled | 75%+ recycled |
| **Steel** | 20-25% recycled | 50%+ recycled |
| **Plastics** | 15-20% recycled | 35%+ recycled |
| **Cobalt** | 20% recycled | 50%+ recycled |
| **Lithium** | 10% recycled | 30%+ recycled |

### 2. Design for Recycling

**Principles:**
- Material purity (avoid mixed materials where possible)
- Easy disassembly
- Standardized connectors
- Recycled material compatibility
- Labeling for recyclers

### 3. End-of-Life Management

**BMW Recycling & Dismantling Centre (RDZ):**
- Founded: 1994
- Location: Landshut, Germany
- Function: Research, development, training
- Impact: Technologies shared with recycling industry

### Battery Recycling Partnership

**BMW + SK tes Partnership (2024):**
- **Scope:** Europe-wide battery recycling
- **Process:** Cobalt, nickel, lithium extraction
- **Output:** Returns to new battery production
- **Goal:** Closed-loop battery materials

**Process Flow:**
```
End-of-Life Battery → Disassembly → Material Recovery
                                           ↓
New Battery Production ← Refined Materials ←┘
```
