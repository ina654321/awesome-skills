# BMW Product Decision Framework

## Philosophy

BMW product decisions balance four core imperatives:
1. **Freude am Fahren** — Enhance driving pleasure
2. **Premium Positioning** — Maintain luxury leadership
3. **Sustainability** — Advance circular economy
4. **Profitability** — Generate shareholder value

## Decision Gates

### G1: Driving Pleasure Check

**Question:** Does this enhance or maintain the driving experience?

| Score | Criteria | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 90-100% | Significantly enhances engagement | ✅ Proceed |
| 80-89% | Maintains BMW dynamics | ✅ Proceed |
| 70-79% | Neutral impact | ⚠️ Review for improvement |
| 60-69% | Minor compromise | ❌ Redesign required |
| <60% | Degrades experience | ❌ Reject |

**Validation Methods:**
- Nürburgring lap time and subjective assessment
- Steering feel evaluation
- Chassis dynamics testing
- Customer clinic feedback

### G2: Premium Positioning Check

**Question:** Does this reinforce BMW's luxury brand perception?

| Metric | Benchmark | Minimum Threshold |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Material quality** | Mercedes S-Class | ≥ Audi A8 level |
| **Craftsmanship** | Hand-finished details | No visible cost-cutting |
| **Innovation** | Leading or unique | Not me-too |
| **Exclusivity** | Limited availability | Maintain scarcity value |

**Brand Dilution Indicators:**
- Price point below €30,000 (BMW brand)
- Shared visible components with mass brands
- Decontenting visible to customers
- Discount dependency

### G3: Technology Neutrality

**Question:** Are we maintaining powertrain/flexibility options?

| Strategy | Platform Design | Timeline |
|----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Multi-pathway** | ICE/PHEV/BEV on shared architecture | Through 2027 |
| **EV-native** | Dedicated BEV platform (Neue Klasse) | 2025+ |
| **Hydrogen ready** | Architecture supports FCEV | 2028+ |

**Principle:** Design for maximum flexibility to respond to market/regulatory changes.

### G4: Sustainability Check

**Question:** Does this advance circular economy goals?

| Factor | Minimum Standard | Target |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| **Recycled materials** | 20% | 40%+ |
| **CO2 reduction** | Maintain vs predecessor | 20%+ improvement |
| **End-of-life recyclability** | 85% | 95%+ |
| **Supply chain ethics** | Compliance | Leadership |

**Lifecycle Assessment:**
- Cradle-to-grave CO2 calculation
- Material sourcing transparency
- Water and energy usage
- End-of-life recovery rate

### G5: Customer Value Check

**Question:** Does the customer perceive value exceeding cost?

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **NPS improvement** | +5 points | Post-launch survey |
| **Willingness to pay** | 10%+ over competition | Conjoint analysis |
| **Feature importance** | Top 3 in segment | Customer research |
| **TCO advantage** | Competitive | 5-year ownership cost |

### G6: Heritage Respect

**Question:** Does this honor BMW's DNA?

| Element | Non-Negotiable | Evolutionary Allowed |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Kidney grille** | Must be present | Size/shape evolution |
| **Hofmeister kink** | Must be present | Interpretation OK |
| **50:50 weight** | Target for all RWD | AWD approximations |
| **Rear-drive bias** | Default architecture | AWD with RWD feel |
| **Driver focus** | Cockpit design | Modern interfaces |

### G7: Competitive Differentiation

**Question:** Does this differentiate from Tesla/Mercedes/Audi?

| Competitor | Their Strength | BMW Differentiation |
|------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Tesla** | Technology, charging | Driving dynamics, quality |
| **Mercedes** | Luxury, comfort | Sportiness, engagement |
| **Audi** | Design, tech integration | Pure driving pleasure |
| **Porsche** | Performance | Accessibility, versatility |

## Decision Matrix

### New Model Go/No-Go

| Criteria | Weight | Score (1-10) | Weighted |
|----------|--------|--------------|----------|
| Driving pleasure | 25% | | |
| Premium positioning | 20% | | |
| Customer value | 20% | | |
| Sustainability | 15% | | |
| Heritage respect | 10% | | |
| Differentiation | 10% | | |
| **TOTAL** | 100% | | **Score** |

**Thresholds:**
- 8.5+: Proceed with full investment
- 7.0-8.4: Proceed with modifications
- 6.0-6.9: Reassess fundamentals
- <6.0: Reject

### Feature Prioritization

| Priority | Criteria | Examples |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **P0: Must Have** | Brand defining, regulatory | Kidney grille, safety systems |
| **P1: Should Have** | Competitive parity | Touchscreen, connectivity |
| **P2: Nice to Have** | Differentiation | Unique lighting, materials |
| **P3: Consider** | Cost-dependent | Massage seats, rear entertainment |

## Product Lifecycle Decision Points

### Phase 1: Concept (Months 0-12)

**Key Decisions:**
- Segment entry/exit
- Platform selection
- Powertrain strategy
- Investment approval

**Deliverables:**
- Business case
- Competitive analysis
- Customer research
- Technical feasibility

### Phase 2: Development (Months 12-36)

**Key Decisions:**
- Design freeze
- Specification lock
- Supplier selection
- Pricing strategy

**Deliverables:**
- Design sign-off
- Technical specifications
- Sourcing agreements
- Launch plan

### Phase 3: Launch (Months 36-48)

**Key Decisions:**
- Marketing positioning
- Volume allocation
- Pricing adjustments
- Feature updates

**Deliverables:**
- Marketing campaign
- Sales targets
- Quality metrics
- Customer feedback

### Phase 4: Lifecycle (Years 4-8)

**Key Decisions:**
- Mid-cycle refresh scope
- Pricing actions
- Special editions
- End-of-life timing

**Deliverables:**
- LCI plan
- Competitive response
- Limited run concepts
- Successor planning

## Escalation Protocol

### Decision Authority Matrix

| Decision Type | Level | Timeline |
|---------------|-------|----------|
| **Architecture** | Board of Management | 18-24 months |
| **Model line entry/exit** | Board of Management | 12-18 months |
| **Powertrain strategy** | Product Committee | 6-12 months |
| **Major features** | VP Product | 3-6 months |
| **Minor features** | Product Manager | 4-8 weeks |
| **Pricing** | VP Product + Sales | 3-6 months |

### Conflict Resolution

**When stakeholders disagree:**
1. **Data-driven:** Customer research decides
2. **Brand-driven:** Heritage/tradition guidance
3. **Market-driven:** Competitive intelligence
4. **Escalation:** Board of Management for strategic decisions
