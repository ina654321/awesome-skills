# Category Management Playbook

## Category Roles

### Destination Categories
- **Definition:** Primary reason customers choose Lowe's
- **Characteristics:** Deep assortment, competitive pricing, strong brand presence
- **Examples:** Paint, lawn & garden, tools, appliances

### Routine Categories
- **Definition:** Regular purchase needs
- **Characteristics:** Consistent demand, reliable availability
- **Examples:** Hardware, electrical, plumbing supplies

### Convenience Categories
- **Definition:** One-stop shopping completion
- **Characteristics:** Breadth over depth, easy to shop
- **Examples:** Cleaning supplies, storage, automotive

### Seasonal Categories
- **Definition:** Time-sensitive demand
- **Characteristics:** Peak seasons, high inventory risk/reward
- **Examples:** Holiday decor, snow removal, grills

## Assortment Architecture

### Good-Better-Best Strategy

| Tier | Positioning | Margin | Examples |
|------|-------------|--------|----------|
| Good (Opening Price) | Value-conscious | 25-30% | Project Source |
| Better (Core) | Quality & value balance | 30-35% | Kobalt, allen + roth |
| Best (Premium) | Top performance | 20-25% | National brands |

### SKU Productivity

**Metrics:**
- Sales per SKU
- Margin per SKU
- Inventory turns
- Weeks of supply

**Actions:**
- Rationalize bottom 20% performers annually
- Expand top performers
- Test new items in limited stores

## Pricing Strategy

### Price Zones

| Zone | Characteristics | Strategy |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| 1 | High competition, price-sensitive | Aggressive KVI pricing |
| 2 | Moderate competition | Competitive parity |
| 3 | Low competition, affluent | Maintain margin |

### KVI (Known Value Item) Management

**Identification:**
- Top 100 items by volume
- High price awareness
- Competitive benchmarking

**Pricing Rules:**
- Match or beat Home Depot by 5%
- Daily competitive monitoring
- Automatic price adjustments

## Space Management

### Planogram Development Process

1. **Category Review:** Sales data, trends, seasonality
2. **Assortment Decision:** SKU list, new items, discontinuations
3. **Space Allocation:** Linear feet by subcategory
4. **Layout Design:** Shelf placement, adjacencies
5. **Implementation:** Communication, store execution
6. **Post-Analysis:** Sales lift, compliance check

### Space Productivity Metrics

- Sales per linear foot
- Sales per square foot
- Margin per square foot
- Inventory per square foot

## Supplier Management

### Vendor Scorecard

| Metric | Weight | Target |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Fill Rate | 25% | >95% |
| On-Time Delivery | 20% | >95% |
| Cost Competitiveness | 20% | Market-leading |
| Quality | 15% | <1% defect rate |
| Innovation | 10% | New products annually |
| Marketing Support | 10% | Co-op funding |

### Joint Business Planning

**Annual Process:**
1. Review prior year performance
2. Set mutual growth targets
3. Align on promotional calendar
4. Plan new product launches
5. Negotiate terms and funding
