## § 4 — Workflow

### § 4.1 Mercedes-Benz Product Development Process

#### Phase 1: Concept & Target Setting (Months 0-12)

| Task | ✓ Done When | ✗ FAIL If | Output |
|------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Market analysis | Competitive gap identified | No differentiation vs. BMW/Audi | Product concept document |
| Customer research | VOC insights quantified | Subjective preference only | Customer requirement spec |
| Target setting | QCD (Quality, Cost, Delivery) defined | Missing measurable targets | Target agreement |
| Architecture decision | Platform selected | Incompatible with MB.OS | Technical concept |
| Business case | ROI >15% validated | Negative NPV | Project approval |

#### Phase 2: Design & Development (Months 12-36)

| Task | ✓ Done When | ✗ FAIL If | Output |
|------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Exterior design | 90%+ customer approval | Design direction unclear | Frozen exterior |
| Interior design | HMI validation complete | Usability test failures | Frozen interior |
| Powertrain spec | Efficiency targets met | CO2 exceeds target | Technical specification |
| Safety concept | 5-star NCAP simulation | Structure deformation excessive | Safety validation plan |
| MB.OS integration | Domain integration verified | System incompatibilities | Software architecture |

#### Phase 3: Validation (Months 36-48)

VALIDATION PYRAMID:

Level 5: Series Validation
- 100+ pre-series vehicles
- 5M+ test kilometers
- Global climate zones
- Customer clinics

Level 4: System Integration
- Powertrain dyno: 50,000+ hours
- Chassis rig: 1M+ km equivalent
- Electromagnetic compatibility
- Crash tests: 100+ vehicles

Level 3: Component Validation
- Durability: 2× service life
- Environmental: -40°C to +60°C
- Vibration & shock
- EMC immunity

Level 2: Software Validation
- MIL (Model-in-Loop)
- SIL (Software-in-Loop)
- HIL (Hardware-in-Loop)
- Vehicle-in-Loop

Level 1: Supplier Components
- PPAP approval
- Cpk >1.67 capability
- 0km quality audit

#### Phase 4: Production Launch (Months 48-54)

| Milestone | Criteria | Sign-off |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| PVS (Pilot Series) | 100 vehicles, 98% quality | Manufacturing VP |
| 0-Series | 1,000 vehicles, process stable | Quality VP |
| SOP (Start of Production) | Full rate, <0.5% defects | CEO |
| Market Launch | Dealer training complete | Sales VP |

### § 4.2 Quality Assurance Workflow

QUALITY GATES:

Gate 1: Concept Quality
- Design FMEA complete
- Critical characteristics identified
- Quality targets defined

Gate 2: Prototype Quality
- First parts inspection
- Fit & function validation
- Early warning metrics

Gate 3: Pre-Series Quality
- Process capability studies
- PPM targets verified
- Customer audit readiness

Gate 4: SOP Quality
- 0km audit <50 defects/vehicle
- Warranty forecast <1%
- Dealer feedback positive
