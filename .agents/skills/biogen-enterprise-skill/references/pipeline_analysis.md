## Late-Stage Pipeline (Phase 3 / Registrational)

### 1. Litifilimab (Anti-BDCA2 Antibody)

**Overview:** First-in-class Type I interferon inhibitor for lupus

**Mechanism:**
- Anti-BDCA2 monoclonal antibody
- Inhibits plasmacytoid dendritic cell production of Type I IFNs
- Addresses key driver of lupus pathogenesis

**Indications:**

#### Cutaneous Lupus Erythematosus (CLE)
- **Status:** Phase 3 ongoing
- **Regulatory:** FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation (2025)
- **Market:** ~150,000 US patients
- **Differentiation:** No approved targeted therapies for CLE specifically

#### Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)
- **Status:** Two Phase 3 studies (LILAC, LOTUS) fully enrolled
- **Data readout:** H2 2026
- **Market:** ~300,000 US patients
- **Competition:** Benlysta (GSK), Saphnelo (AstraZeneca)

**Clinical Data:**
- Phase 2 (LILAC): Significant skin response vs. placebo
- Type I IFN signature suppression demonstrated

**Strategic Importance:**
- **Peak sales potential:** $1-2B
- **Platform validation:** First BDCA2 inhibitor to reach Phase 3
- **Pipeline synergy:** Validates Type I IFN pathway for other indications

---

### 2. Felzartamab (Anti-CD38 Antibody)

**Overview:** Anti-CD38 monoclonal antibody for antibody-mediated rejection (AMR) in kidney transplantation

**Mechanism:**
- Depletes CD38+ plasma cells
- Reduces donor-specific antibodies (DSAs)
- Prevents/treats AMR in transplant recipients

**Indication:** Antibody-mediated rejection in kidney transplant
- **Status:** Three Phase 3 studies initiated
  - Treatment of acute AMR
  - Prevention of chronic AMR
  - Desensitization protocol
- **Data readout:** 2026-2027

**Market:**
- ~10,000+ kidney transplants with AMR annually in US
- High unmet need: Current standard (IVIG, rituximab) inadequate

**Strategic Importance:**
- **Peak sales potential:** $500M-1B
- **First-mover advantage:** First anti-CD38 in transplant setting
- **Rare disease focus:** Aligns with Biogen expertise

---

### 3. Dapirolizumab Pegol (Anti-CD40L)

**Overview:** Anti-CD40L antibody for systemic lupus erythematosus

**Mechanism:**
- Blocks CD40-CD40L interaction
- Prevents T-cell dependent B-cell activation
- Reduces autoantibody production

**Indication:** SLE
- **Status:** Phase 3 ongoing
- **Partner:** UCB (co-development)
- **Data readout:** 2026+

**Clinical Data:**
- Phase 2b (PHOENYCS GO): Significant SRI-4 response improvement
- Well-tolerated; no thromboembolic events (historical concern with CD40L class)

**Strategic Importance:**
- **Partnership model:** Shared risk with UCB
- **Complementary to litifilimab:** Different mechanism, both in SLE
- **Platform potential:** CD40L inhibition applicable to other autoimmunity

---

### 4. LEQEMBI (Lecanemab) Lifecycle Expansion

**Overview:** Multiple programs extending LEQEMBI's market position

#### Preclinical Alzheimer's Disease
- **Study:** AHEAD 3-45
- **Population:** Cognitively normal with elevated amyloid
- **Design:** Phase 3, 216 participants per arm
- **Significance:** Potential to 3x addressable market if approved
- **Partnership:** NIH/NIA funded (public-private partnership)

#### Subcutaneous Autoinjector (IQLIK)
- **Status:** 
  - Maintenance: Approved (August 2025)
  - Initiation: PDUFA May 24, 2026 (Priority Review)
- **Impact:** Transforms treatment paradigm; 30-40% market expansion potential

#### Tau Combination Therapy
- **Asset:** BIIB080 (anti-tau ASO)
- **Status:** Phase 2
- **Strategy:** Combination with lecanemab for enhanced efficacy

---

### 5. Salanersen (Anti-Huntingtin ASO)

**Overview:** Antisense oligonucleotide for spinal muscular atrophy

**Mechanism:**
- Targets SMN2 pre-mRNA
- Distinct mechanism from SPINRAZA (different binding site)
- Potential for enhanced efficacy

**Indication:** SMA
- **Status:** Phase 1b/3
- **Partner:** Ionis Pharmaceuticals
- **Data readout:** 2026+

**Strategic Importance:**
- **SPINRAZA lifecycle management:** Next-generation ASO
- **Competitive positioning:** vs. Zolgensma, Evrysdi

## Mid-Stage Pipeline (Phase 2)

### 6. Zorevunersen (Antisense Oligonucleotide)

**Overview:** ASO for Dravet syndrome (genetic epilepsy)

**Mechanism:**
- Targets SCN1A gene
- Increases production of functional Nav1.1 sodium channels
- Addresses underlying genetic cause

**Indication:** Dravet syndrome
- **Status:** Phase 2/3
- **Partner:** Stoke Therapeutics ($165M upfront, 2025)
- **Data readout:** 2027+

**Market:**
- ~20,000 US patients
- High unmet need: Current treatments (stiripentol, fenfluramine) symptomatic only

**Strategic Importance:**
- **Rare disease expansion:** Beyond neuromuscular to genetic epilepsy
- **ASO delivery learning:** Stoke partnership for enhanced CNS delivery

---

### 7. BIIB080 (Anti-Tau ASO)

**Overview:** Antisense oligonucleotide targeting tau for Alzheimer's disease

**Mechanism:**
- Reduces tau protein production
- Tau is second hallmark of AD (after amyloid)
- Potential for combination with anti-amyloid therapy

**Status:** Phase 2
- **Regulatory:** FDA Fast Track Designation (2025)
- **Strategy:** Combination with lecanemab

**Strategic Importance:**
- **AD combination approach:** Amyloid + tau dual targeting
- **Platform extension:** ASO technology beyond SMA/ALS

## Early-Stage Pipeline (Phase 1)

### 8. BIIB145 (BTK Degrader)

**Overview:** Bruton's tyrosine kinase degrader for autoimmune diseases

**Mechanism:**
- Protein degradation technology (PROTAC-like)
- Eliminates BTK protein (vs. inhibition)
- Deeper, more durable B-cell suppression

**Indications:** Autoimmune diseases (MS, SLE, others)
- **Status:** Phase 1 initiated (2025)

**Strategic Importance:**
- **Technology platform:** First protein degrader in Biogen pipeline
- **Competitive response:** BTK inhibitors entering MS; degraders may be next-gen

---

### 9. BIIB142 (IRAK4 Degrader)

**Overview:** Interleukin-1 Receptor-Associated Kinase 4 degrader

**Mechanism:**
- Targets IRAK4, key node in TLR/IL-1R signaling
- Broad anti-inflammatory effect

**Indication:** Autoimmune diseases
- **Status:** Phase 1 initiated (2025)

**Strategic Importance:**
- **Novel mechanism:** First IRAK4 degrader in clinical development
- **Multiple indications:** Potential in RA, SLE, IBD

## Platform Technologies

### Antisense Oligonucleotide (ASO) Platform

**Partnership:** Ionis Pharmaceuticals

**Assets:**
- SPINRAZA (approved)
- QALSODY (approved)
- Salanersen (Phase 3)
- BIIB080 (Phase 2)

**Advantages:**
- Validated delivery to CNS via intrathecal injection
- Mechanism-specific knockdown of disease-causing proteins
- Ionis partnership provides access to leading ASO technology

### Monoclonal Antibody Platform

**Internal Capabilities:**
- Full antibody discovery and development
- Blood-brain barrier penetration strategies
- Fc engineering for optimized effector function

**Key Assets:**
- TYSABRI (anti-α4 integrin)
- LEQEMBI (anti-amyloid)
- Litifilimab (anti-BDCA2)
- Felzartamab (anti-CD38)

### Small Molecule Platform

**Key Assets:**
- VUMERITY/SKYCLARYS (Nrf2 activators)
- ZURZUVAE (GABA-A modulator)

### Protein Degradation Platform

**New Capability:** Acquired/Developed 2024-2025

**Assets:**
- BIIB145 (BTK degrader)
- BIIB142 (IRAK4 degrader)

**Strategic Value:**
- Next-generation mechanism beyond small molecule inhibitors
- Potential for enhanced efficacy, duration
