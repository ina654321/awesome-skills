## Alzheimer's Disease Market

### Market Overview
- **Addressable Population:** ~6M Americans with AD; ~2M in early stages
- **Treatment-Ready:** ~1M diagnosed with MCI/mild AD
- **Market Size:** Emerging; potential $10-20B at maturity

### Anti-Amyloid Competitive Landscape

#### LEQEMBI (Biogen/Eisai)
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | Anti-protofibril + anti-plaque |
| **Efficacy** | 27% CDR-SB slowing |
| **Dosing** | IV bi-weekly → monthly; SC weekly (maintenance) |
| **Safety** | ARIA-E 12.6%, ARIA-H 17.3% |
| **Market Share** | >60% anti-amyloid market |
| **Key Advantage** | Dual mechanism, SC option, Eisai partnership |

#### Kisunla (Eli Lilly)
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Generic Name** | Donanemab |
| **Mechanism** | Anti-plaque only |
| **Efficacy** | 35% CDR-SB slowing (higher than LEQEMBI) |
| **Dosing** | IV monthly (titration to stopping) |
| **Safety** | ARIA-E 24.0%, ARIA-H 31.4% (higher than LEQEMBI) |
| **Market Share** | ~35% anti-amyloid market |
| **Key Advantage** | Higher efficacy; can stop treatment |

#### Competitive Comparison

| Factor | LEQEMBI | Kisunla |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Efficacy | ★★★☆☆ | ★★★★☆ |
| Safety | ★★★★☆ | ★★☆☆☆ |
| Convenience | ★★★★☆ (SC option) | ★★★☆☆ |
| Market Position | Leader (60%+) | Challenger (35%) |

### Market Barriers

| Barrier | Impact | Biogen Strategy |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| **Diagnostic Infrastructure** | Limited PET/CSF capacity | Partnerships with imaging centers |
| **Infusion Capacity** | Bottleneck for IV therapies | SC autoinjector launch |
| **Specialist Shortage** | Limited cognitive neurologists | Primary care education |
| **Reimbursement** | CMS coverage restrictions | Advocacy, real-world evidence |
| **Patient Identification** | 50-70% undiagnosed | AI screening tools |

## Spinal Muscular Atrophy Market

### Market Overview
- **US Prevalence:** ~11,000 patients
- **Market Size:** ~$2.5B (US)
- **Growth:** Mature; competition from gene therapy

### Competitive Dynamics

| Product | Company | Mechanism | 2024 US Revenue | Position |
|---------|---------|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| **SPINRAZA** | Biogen/Ionis | ASO (SMN2 splicing) | ~$1.0B | Market leader |
| **Zolgensma** | Novartis | Gene therapy (AAV9-SMN1) | ~$1.2B | One-time treatment |
| **Evrysdi** | Roche/PTC | SMN2 splicing (oral) | ~$800M | Growing share |

### Competitive Assessment

| Factor | SPINRAZA | Zolgensma | Evrysdi |
|--------|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | ASO (lifelong) | Gene therapy | Small molecule |
| **Dosing** | Intrathecal every 4mo | One-time IV | Daily oral |
| **Onset** | Gradual | Rapid | Moderate |
| **Cost** | ~$750K/year | ~$2.1M one-time | ~$340K/year |
| **Limitations** | Lumbar punctures | Age/weight limits, antibodies | Efficacy vs. SPINRAZA |

### Biogen's Strategy
- **High-dose regimen:** 28mg vs. 12mg (Japan/EU approved; US PDUFA April 2026)
- **Earlier treatment:** Newborn screening expansion
- **Combination approaches:** With gene therapy (investigational)

## ALS (Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis) Market

### Market Overview
- **US Prevalence:** ~30,000 patients
- **Market Size:** ~$1B
- **Segmentation:** Sporadic (90%), Familial (10%)

### Competitive Dynamics

| Product | Company | Mechanism | Status | Position |
|---------|---------|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Rilutek/Riluzole** | Various | Glutamate modulation | Generic | Standard of care |
| **Radicava** | Mitsubishi | Free radical scavenger | Approved | Limited efficacy |
| **QALSODY** | Biogen/Ionis | ASO (SOD1 targeting) | Approved (2023) | SOD1+ only (~400 pts) |
| **Tofersen** | Biogen/Ionis | Same as QALSODY | Approved | First targeted ALS therapy |
| **AMX0035** | Amylyx | URSO/TUDCA | Withdrawn | Failed Phase 3 |
| **Tofersen (biomarker)** | Biogen | Same | Approved | For SOD1 carriers |

### Competitive Assessment

**QALSODY Limitations:**
- Genetic testing required (SOD1 mutation)
- Small addressable population (~2% of ALS)
- Intrathecal administration

**Growth Strategy:**
- Expand genetic testing (partnerships with Invitae)
- Real-world evidence generation
- Earlier treatment initiation

## Competitive Summary: Biogen's Strategic Position

### Strengths
1. **Neuroscience Leadership:** Deep expertise, established infrastructure
2. **ASO Platform:** Partnership with Ionis provides competitive moat
3. **First-Mover Advantages:** SPINRAZA, SKYCLARYS, QALSODY
4. **LEQEMBI Position:** Market leader in emerging Alzheimer's market

### Vulnerabilities
1. **MS Franchise:** Facing generic/biosimilar threat + BTK competition
2. **Alzheimer's Execution:** Market development challenges
3. **Biosimilars:** Declining business with limited strategic value

### Opportunities
1. **Rare Disease Expansion:** SKYCLARYS pediatric, zorevunersen
2. **Immunology:** Litifilimab launch, felzartamab
3. **Alzheimer's Growth:** SC formulation, preclinical AD expansion

### Threats
1. **Competition in Growth Markets:** Lilly in Alzheimer's, Roche in SMA
2. **Pricing Pressure:** Payer scrutiny on novel therapies
3. **Pipeline Risk:** Phase 3 attrition (lupus historically challenging)
