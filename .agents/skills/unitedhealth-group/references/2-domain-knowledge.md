### §2.3 UnitedHealthcare Insurance Segments

**Employer & Individual (E&I):**
- Full-year 2024 revenues: $78.2 billion
- Domestic commercial growth: 2.4 million consumers
- 25% of largest fee-based employer customers offering innovative transparency plans

**Medicare & Retirement:**
- Full-year 2024 revenues: $139.5 billion
- Medicare Advantage membership: 9.4 million (2024) → 9.9 million (2025)
- Market leadership position with 29% national MA market share
- C-SNP growth: 110% increase (406,508 enrollees)
- D-SNP growth: 15% increase (321,517 enrollees)

**Community & State (Medicaid):**
- Full-year 2024 revenues: $80.6 billion
- Serving 7.4 million members (post-redetermination)
- Expanded state relationships: Michigan, Idaho, Nevada, Georgia
- Projected return to membership growth in 2025

### §2.4 Optum Services Platform

**Optum Health (Care Delivery):**
- Revenue: $105.4 billion (2024)
- 4.7 million patients under value-based care arrangements
- 2025 target: +650,000 additional patients (5.4M total)
- 90,000+ aligned physicians
- Services: Primary care, urgent care, outpatient surgery, home health

**Optum Insight (Health IT & Analytics):**
- Revenue: $18.8 billion (2024)
- Change Healthcare acquisition: $13 billion (2022)
- Processes 15 billion healthcare transactions annually
- Revenue backlog growth: $700 million YoY
- Cyberattack impact (Feb 2024): $867 million business disruption

**Optum Rx (Pharmacy Benefit Management):**
- Revenue: $133+ billion (2024)
- Adjusted scripts: 1.62 billion (vs 1.54 billion prior year)
- Client retention: 98%+
- 750 new clients added in 2024
- Commitment: 100% PBM rebates passed to clients by 2028

### §2.6 Value-Based Care Program

**Performance Outcomes (vs Fee-for-Service):**
- 12% lower healthcare costs
- 26% reduction in hospital admissions
- 60% of UnitedHealthcare members served by value-based physicians
- Improved chronic disease management
- Better preventive care compliance

**Key Components:**
- Risk-sharing arrangements with providers
- Social determinants of health (SDOH) integration
- Care coordination across settings
- Population health analytics and risk stratification
- Alternative payment models (APMs)

### §2.7 Change Healthcare Acquisition & Cyberattack

**Acquisition Context:**
- Purchase price: $13 billion (2022)
- Largest healthcare transaction clearinghouse in US
- 15 billion transactions processed annually
- Critical infrastructure for claims processing nationwide

**February 2024 Cyberattack:**
- Ransomware attack disrupted prescriptions nationwide
- Direct response costs: ~$2.2 billion
- Business disruption impact: $867 million (Optum Insight)
- Provider liquidity support: $9 billion in accelerated payments
- DOJ antitrust investigation intensified post-attack

### §2.9 Regulatory Environment

**Key Regulators:**
- **CMS:** Medicare Advantage rate setting, Star Ratings, risk adjustment
- **State DOIs:** Insurance licensing, rate filings, market conduct
- **DOJ:** Antitrust scrutiny of vertical integration (UHC + Optum)
- **FTC:** PBM pricing transparency, competitive practices
- **FDA:** For Optum Rx formulary decisions

**Current Challenges:**
- CMS Medicare Advantage rate pressures (2024 funding reductions)
- DOJ investigation into UHC/OptumHealth business practices
- PBM rebate transparency legislation
- Medicare Advantage coding intensity scrutiny
