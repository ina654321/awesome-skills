# IDM 2.0 Strategy Reference

> **Document:** Intel IDM 2.0 Transformation Strategy  
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-21

---

## Overview

**IDM 2.0** (Integrated Device Manufacturer 2.0) is Intel's strategic transformation to:
1. Maintain internal manufacturing leadership
2. Use external foundries when optimal
3. Open Intel fabs to external customers (IFS)

This strategy was architected by former CEO Pat Gelsinger (2021-2024) and is being executed with financial discipline under current CEO Lip-Bu Tan (2025-present).

## IDM 2.0 Three Pillars

```
                    IDM 2.0 STRATEGY
                         │
         ┌───────────────┼───────────────┐
         │               │               │
    ┌────▼────┐    ┌────▼────┐    ┌────▼────┐
    │ INTERNAL │    │ EXTERNAL│    │ FOUNDRY │
    │   FAB    │    │  FOUNDRY│    │ SERVICES│
    │          │    │         │    │  (IFS)  │
    └────┬─────┘    └────┬────┘    └────┬────┘
         │               │               │
    Intel products   TSMC, Samsung   External customers
    on Intel nodes   when leading    on Intel nodes
```

### Pillar 1: Internal Manufacturing

**Objective:** Regain process leadership with Intel 18A

**"5 Nodes in 4 Years" Roadmap:**
- Intel 7 → Intel 4 → Intel 3 → Intel 20A → Intel 18A
- (2021) → (2023) → (2024) → (2024) → (2025)

**Investment:**
- $100B+ over 5 years (U.S. focus)
- CHIPS Act: Up to $8.5B grants + $11B loans
- Ohio: $20B+ Phase 1, up to $100B total

### Pillar 2: External Foundry Usage

**Objective:** Use best available process when Intel is behind

**Current External Usage:**
- **TSMC N3B**: Lunar Lake compute tile, Arrow Lake graphics
- **TSMC N5/N6**: Meteor Lake/Arrow Lake auxiliary tiles
- **TSMC 5nm**: Gaudi 3 AI accelerator

**Decision Framework:**
```
New product design?
├── Can Intel node meet requirements?
│   ├── Yes (18A competitive) → Use Intel
│   └── No (TSMC ahead) → Use TSMC, port to Intel later
└── Is volume sufficient for dedicated capacity?
    ├── Yes → Prioritize internal
    └── No → Use external
```

### Pillar 3: Intel Foundry Services (IFS)

**Objective:** Monetize manufacturing capacity, achieve scale

**Business Model:**
- External customers pay for capacity
- Internal products pay "market rates" (arm's length)
- Foundry P&L separate from Product P&L

**Target Customers:**
- Fabless semiconductor companies
- System companies (Microsoft, Amazon)
- Government/military (RAMP-C)

## Leadership Transition: Gelsinger → Lip-Bu Tan

### Pat Gelsinger Era (2021-2024)

**Focus:**
- Technology leadership at all costs
- "5 Nodes in 4 Years" acceleration
- Aggressive capacity expansion

**Key Decisions:**
- Announced Ohio fab complex ($20B+)
- Committed to Germany and Poland fabs
- Hired 20,000+ employees
- Suspended dividend (Q4 2024)

### Lip-Bu Tan Era (2025-present)

**Focus:**
- Financial discipline
- Capital efficiency
- Sustainable business model

**Key Changes:**

| Area | Gelsinger Approach | Lip-Bu Tan Approach |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Capital Expenditure** | $25-27B/year | $18B/year target |
| **Workforce** | Expansion | 15% reduction (~15,000) |
| **International Fabs** | Germany + Poland committed | **Cancelled** |
| **Ohio Timeline** | Aggressive 2025 production | **Slowed** to match demand |
| **Dividend** | Suspended | Remains suspended |
| **Foundry Priority** | Technology leadership | **Profitability focus** |
| **14A Node** | Commit regardless | **Conditional** on customer commitments |

### Lip-Bu Tan's Key Statements

> "We are laser-focused on strengthening our core product portfolio and AI roadmap... We are also taking the necessary actions to build a more financially disciplined foundry business."

> "I will ensure the success of our foundry business... If you want a local supply chain, you have to pay for it."

> "18A is the most durable node in our modern history — powering chips from 2025 into the 2030s."

## Financial Strategy

### Cost Reduction Program

**Target:** $10B+ cost savings by 2025

**Actions Taken:**
1. **Operating Expenses**: $5.6B → $4.8B (Q2 2025, -13% YoY)
2. **R&D Efficiency**: Focus on fewer, higher-impact projects
3. **Workforce Reduction**: 15% headcount reduction
4. **Project Cancellations**: Germany, Poland fabs cancelled
5. **Construction Slowdown**: Ohio pace matched to demand

### Financial Targets (2025-2026)

| Metric | 2024 | 2025 Target | 2026 Target |
|--------|------|-------------|-------------|
| Operating Expenses | ~$20B | $17B | $16B |
| Capital Expenditure | $25-27B | $18B | TBD |
| Gross Margin | 36-40% | Improve | >50% (long-term) |
| Foundry Revenue | ~$17B | Growing | $10B+ (external) |

### Profitability Path

**Near-Term (2025):**
- Break-even on non-GAAP basis
- Stabilize market share
- Execute 18A ramp

**Medium-Term (2026-2027):**
- Return to profitability
- Foundry external revenue growth
- Product competitiveness restored

**Long-Term (2028+):**
- Sustainable margins >50%
- Foundry business profitable
- Potential dividend restoration

## Capacity Allocation Strategy

### Current Allocation (2025)

| Priority | Allocation | Use Case |
|----------|------------|----------|
| Strategic Products | 40% | Panther Lake, Xeon 6, Gaudi 3 |
| Foundry Customers | 35% | Committed external capacity |
| Technology Development | 15% | Yield learning, process R&D |
| Buffer/Flex | 10% | Demand surge, opportunistic |

### Decision Framework for Conflicts

```
Internal Product vs. Foundry Customer Conflict?
│
├── Is product launch date committed to market?
│   ├── YES → Prioritize product (revenue impact)
│   └── NO → Evaluate foundry customer contract
│
├── Is foundry customer strategic (multi-year)?
│   ├── YES → Negotiate compromise
│   └── NO → Product priority, offer later capacity
│
└── Can capacity expand with external funding?
    ├── YES → Pursue customer-funded expansion
    └── NO → Auction to highest strategic value
```

## Competitive Position

### IDM 2.0 vs. Pure-Play Models

| Dimension | Intel IDM 2.0 | TSMC Pure-Play | AMD Fabless |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Manufacturing Control** | High | N/A (outsourced) | None |
| **Process Optimization** | Product-specific | General-purpose | Limited influence |
| **Capacity Guarantee** | Internal priority | Market-based | Contract-based |
| **Cost Structure** | Higher fixed costs | Variable | Lowest fixed costs |
| **Technology Access** | Leading + external | Leading only | TSMC-dependent |
| **Supply Chain Risk** | Diversified | Concentrated (Taiwan) | Concentrated |

### Strategic Advantages of IDM 2.0

1. **Manufacturing-Design Co-Optimization**: Intel can optimize process for products
2. **Supply Chain Resilience**: U.S.-based capacity for critical products
3. **Margin Capture**: Keep foundry margin on internal products
4. **Scale Economics**: External customers improve fab utilization
5. **Government Support**: CHIPS Act favors U.S. manufacturing
