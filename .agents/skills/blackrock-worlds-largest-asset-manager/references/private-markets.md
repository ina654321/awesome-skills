## Investment Strategies

### Infrastructure

**Investment Thesis:**
- Essential assets with high barriers to entry
- Long-term contracted/regulated cash flows
- Inflation protection through escalation clauses
- ESG alignment (energy transition, digital connectivity)

**Sector Focus:**
```
Energy Transition (40%)
├── Renewable generation (solar, wind)
├── Battery storage
├── Grid infrastructure
└── Electrification assets

Digital Infrastructure (30%)
├── Data centers
├── Cell towers
├── Fiber networks
└── Subsea cables

Transportation (20%)
├── Airports
├── Ports
├── Rail
└── Toll roads

Other (10%)
├── Utilities
├── Water
└── Social infrastructure
```

**Return Profile:**
- Target IRR: 10-14%
- Cash yield: 5-8% annually
- Duration: 15-25 year holds
- Risk: Lower volatility than equities

### Private Credit

**Market Opportunity:**
- Banks have retreated from middle market lending post-GFC
- Regulatory capital requirements (Basel III) constrain bank lending
- Direct lending market has grown 10x since 2010
- Current market size: ~$1.5 trillion globally

**BlackRock/HPS Strategy:**
| Segment | Target | Risk Profile |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| Senior Direct Lending | Core allocation | Investment grade equivalent |
| Unitranche | Income focus | Blended senior/junior |
| Asset-Based Lending | Diversification | Collateralized |
| Specialty Finance | Yield enhancement | Esoteric expertise required |
| Opportunistic/Distressed | Return maximization | Higher risk, cycle-dependent |

**Key Advantages:**
- Floating rate (benefits from rising rates)
- Covenant protection
- Direct relationships with borrowers
- Information advantage through due diligence

**Return Profile:**
- Target IRR: 9-14%
- Current yields: 10-13% (senior), 12-16% (subordinated)
- Duration: 3-7 years
- Risk: Illiquidity, credit risk, economic sensitivity

## Risk Management

### Private Markets Risk Framework

**Key Risks:**
| Risk Type | Mitigation |
|-----------|------------|
| **Illiquidity** | Match liabilities, ladder commitments |
| **Valuation Uncertainty** | Independent third-party valuation |
| **Concentration** | Sector, vintage, and GP diversification |
| **Leverage** | Conservative LTVs, covenant protection |
| **Operational** | Extensive due diligence, ongoing monitoring |

**Aladdin Integration:**
- Private company risk factor monitoring (2,000+ companies)
- Cash flow forecasting and commitment pacing
- Vintage year concentration analysis
- GP performance benchmarking (via Preqin)
- ESG risk integration

### Liquidity Management

**For Institutional Portfolios:**
```
Recommended Liquidity Ladder:

Year 1-2: 95% liquid (public markets, cash)
Year 3-5: 80% liquid (add semi-liquid alternatives)
Year 5+: 60% liquid (full private markets allocation)
```

**Cash Flow Forecasting:**
- Preqin models provide capital call/distribution projections
- Aladdin aggregates across all GP commitments
- Stress testing for "denominator effect" scenarios

## Case Studies

### Case 1: UK Pension Fund Infrastructure Allocation

**Client:** £10B defined benefit scheme
**Objective:** 10% allocation to infrastructure for liability matching

**Implementation:**
- £600M to GIP-managed global infrastructure fund
- £300M to renewable energy separate account
- £100M to UK-focused social infrastructure

**Outcome:**
- Cash yield: 6.5% (matches liability discount rate)
- Inflation linkage protects real value
- ESG alignment with trustees

### Case 2: Insurance Private Credit Portfolio

**Client:** $5B life insurer
**Objective:** Yield enhancement while maintaining investment grade risk

**Implementation:**
- $2B senior direct lending (HPS strategy)
- $1.5B asset-based lending
- $1B mezzanine/subordinated
- $500B opportunistic/reserve

**Outcome:**
- Portfolio yield: 9.2% vs. 5.5% public IG bonds
- Risk-adjusted return improvement
- Floating rate protects against rising rates
