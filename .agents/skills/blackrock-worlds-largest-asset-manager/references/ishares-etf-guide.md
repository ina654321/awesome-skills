## How to Select iShares ETFs

### Decision Framework

```
1. DEFINE OBJECTIVE
   └── What outcome? (Growth, income, inflation protection, etc.)

2. DETERMINE ASSET CLASS
   └── Equity / Fixed Income / Alternatives / Multi-Asset

3. CHOOSE MARKET SEGMENT
   └── Geographic region, sector, factor, or theme

4. EVALUATE OPTIONS
   ├── Expense ratio (all-in cost)
   ├── Liquidity (average daily volume)
   ├── Tracking difference (vs. benchmark)
   └── Tax efficiency (distributions)

5. IMPLEMENT & MONITOR
   └── Trade execution, rebalance frequency, review
```

### Key Metrics Comparison

| Metric | What to Look For | Why It Matters |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| **Expense Ratio** | Lower is better | Direct impact on returns |
| **AUM** | >$500M preferred | Liquidity, sustainability |
| **Volume** | >$10M ADV preferred | Tight spreads, easy exit |
| **Tracking Difference** | Close to zero | Measures implementation quality |
| **Tax Cost Ratio** | Lower is better | After-tax returns |

## Tax Efficiency

### Why ETFs Are Tax-Efficient

1. **In-Kind Redemptions:** APs receive securities, not cash — no forced sales
2. **Lower Turnover:** Index tracking = less trading
3. **No Capital Gains Until Sale:** Unlike mutual funds

### iShares Tax Statistics

| Category | Statistic |
|----------|-----------|
| U.S. Style Box ETFs (5-year) | 0% paid capital gains distributions |
| Active Mutual Funds (5-year) | 76% paid capital gains |
| Average Capital Gain (Mutual Funds) | 3.3% of NAV |
| Average Capital Gain (Active ETFs) | 1.8% of NAV |

### Tax-Loss Harvesting with iShares

Pairs for tax-loss swapping:
- IVV ↔ VOO (S&P 500)
- ITOT ↔ VTI (Total Market)
- IEFA ↔ VEA (Developed International)
- IEMG ↔ VWO (Emerging Markets)
