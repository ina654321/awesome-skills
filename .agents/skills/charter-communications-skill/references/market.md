## Regulatory Environment

### Federal Regulation

**FCC Jurisdiction:**
- Broadband reclassification (Title I vs. Title II)
- Net neutrality rules
- Privacy regulations
- E911 requirements
- Accessibility (CVAA)

**Current Issues (2024-2025):**
- Net neutrality reinstatement (2024)
- Digital discrimination rules
- Broadband nutrition labels
- Spectrum auction participation

**Net Neutrality Impact:**
- Cannot block, throttle, or paid prioritization
- Disclosure requirements
- No impact on network management practices

### State & Local Regulation

**Franchise Agreements:**
- Local cable franchises required for video service
- Typically 10-15 year terms
- Franchise fees: Up to 5% of video revenue
- PEG (Public, Educational, Government) access requirements
- Build-out commitments

**State Broadband Offices:**
- BEAD program coordination
- State broadband maps
- State grant programs
- Digital equity planning

**Key State Issues:**
- California: Net neutrality, privacy laws
- New York: Affordable broadband mandate
- Connecticut: Charter HQ state, favorable business climate
- Texas: Franchise reform, statewide video franchise

### Government Programs

**Rural Digital Opportunity Fund (RDOF):**
- FCC program for unserved areas
- Charter won: $1.2B over 10 years
- Obligation: Serve ~1M locations
- Timeline: 2022-2028

**Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment (BEAD):**
- $42.5B federal program (IIJA)
- State-administered
- Charter positioned for partnerships
- Focus: 25/3 Mbps unserved areas

**Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP):**
- $30/month subsidy ($75 tribal)
- 23M+ households enrolled
- **Sunset: 2024** (not renewed by Congress)
- Impact: Charter lost 110K+ customers post-sunset

**Spectrum Internet Assist:**
- Charter's low-income program
- 30 Mbps for $19.99/month
- Continues post-ACP

## Franchise & ROW

### Pole Attachments

**Regulatory Framework:**
- FCC rules for telecommunications attachments
- State laws vary
- Make-ready charges
- Timelines: 148 days standard

**Challenges:**
- Utility pole ownership (power companies)
- Make-ready costs increasing
- Attacher disputes
- Storm restoration priority

### Right-of-Way

**Access Types:**
1. **Public ROW**: Streets, highways
2. **Private Easements**: Negotiated access
3. **Railroad Crossings**: Special agreements
4. **Federal Lands**: Permits required

**Rural Challenges:**
- Longer distances between homes
- Private road access negotiations
- Underground utility conflicts
- Easement acquisition costs

### Fixed Wireless Competition

**T-Mobile Home Internet:**
- 5M+ customers
- $50/month pricing
- 100-300 Mbps typical speeds
- Growing threat in suburban markets

**Verizon 5G Home:**
- 3M+ customers
- $35-70/month pricing
- 300-1000 Mbps in mmWave areas
- Directly targeting Charter markets

**Charter's Response:**
- Speed upgrades (300 Mbps minimum)
- Price positioning
- Reliability messaging
- Mobile counter-offers

### Fiber Competition

**AT&T Fiber Expansion:**
- 25M+ fiber locations
- 5 Gbps symmetrical
- Aggressive pricing
- Overlap in 30+ Charter markets

**Verizon Fios:**
- 7M+ fiber customers
- Limited footprint expansion
- Strong in Northeast

**Charter's Response:**
- DOCSIS 3.1 high-split for speed parity
- Rural expansion to unserved areas
- Bundle stickiness through mobile
- Service reliability advantages
