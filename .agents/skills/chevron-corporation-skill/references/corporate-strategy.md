# Chevron Corporate Strategy Reference

## Strategic Direction

> "We leverage our strengths to safely deliver lower carbon energy to a growing world. We aim to grow our oil and gas business, lower the carbon intensity of our operations and grow new businesses in renewable fuels, carbon capture and offsets, hydrogen, power generation for data centers, and emerging technologies."
> — Mike Wirth, Chairman and CEO

## Three Strategic Priorities

### 1. Grow Traditional Oil & Gas Business
- **Capital-efficient production growth**: 4-7% annual production growth target
- **Advantaged assets**: Focus on highest-return opportunities
- **Reserve replacement**: Maintain >100% replacement ratio
- **Key growth engines**:
  - Permian Basin (approaching 1 million boe/d)
  - Tengiz FGP expansion (Kazakhstan)
  - Gulf of Mexico deepwater
  - Guyana Stabroek (via Hess)

### 2. Lower Carbon Intensity of Operations
- **Target**: First quartile GHG intensity among peers
- **Methane**: Reduce methane emissions intensity
- **Flaring**: Eliminate routine flaring
- **Energy efficiency**: Lower energy intensity of operations
- **Net zero aspiration**: Upstream Scope 1&2 by 2050

### 3. Grow New Lower Carbon Businesses
- **Investment**: $10B through 2028
- **Focus sectors**: Aviation, marine, heavy-duty transport, industrial
- **Core capabilities**: CCUS, hydrogen, renewable fuels, offsets
- **2030 Targets**:
  - Renewable fuels: 100,000 b/d capacity
  - Hydrogen: 150,000 tonnes/year
  - Carbon capture: 25 million tonnes/year

## M&A Strategy

### Acquisition Criteria
1. **Strategic fit**: Advantaged assets with long-duration inventory
2. **Financial returns**: Accretive to cash flow and earnings
3. **Capital efficiency**: Prefer shorter-cycle, scalable investments
4. **Risk profile**: Manageable execution and integration risk

### Recent Transactions

| Transaction | Year | Value | Strategic Rationale |
|-------------|------|-------|---------------------|
| Hess Corporation | 2024-2025 | $53B | Guyana exposure, Bakken shale, extend growth |
| PDC Energy | 2023 | ~$7.6B | DJ Basin scale, synergies |
| Renewable Energy Group | 2022 | $3.15B | Renewable fuels platform |

### Divestiture Program
- **Target**: $10-15B before-tax proceeds through 2028
- **Focus**: Non-core, higher carbon intensity assets
- **Recent sales**: Canada, Alaska, Congo assets

## Portfolio Prioritization

### Tier 1: Advantaged Assets (Core)
- Permian Basin (shale leader)
- Tengizchevroil (Kazakhstan giant field)
- Australia LNG (Gorgon, Wheatstone)
- Guyana Stabroek (world-class offshore)
- Gulf of Mexico deepwater

### Tier 2: Cash Generation
- Southeast Asia natural gas
- Eastern Mediterranean (Leviathan, Tamar)
- Downstream & chemicals (CPChem)
- Legacy US/International oil & gas

### Tier 3: Optimization Candidates
- Higher-cost international operations
- Non-core refining assets
- Assets with regulatory challenges (California)

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. ExxonMobil
- **Strengths**: Lower carbon intensity, capital efficiency, Permian position
- **Challenges**: Scale in Guyana (pending Hess), downstream integration

### vs. Shell/BP
- **Strengths**: Traditional oil & gas growth, returns discipline
- **Approach**: More measured energy transition vs. European peers

### Differentiation
- Capital discipline and returns focus
- Shorter-cycle investment flexibility
- Lower carbon intensity operations
- Dividend aristocrat status (37+ years growth)

## Risk Management

### Commodity Price Risk
- **Hedging**: Limited; accept price volatility
- **Flexibility**: Short-cycle shale provides optionality
- **Breakeven**: ~$50/bbl free cash flow neutral

### Geopolitical Risk
- **Diversification**: Global portfolio reduces single-country exposure
- **Partnerships**: JV structures share risk (TCO, CPChem, etc.)
- **Kazakhstan exposure**: Tengiz is critical asset; political risk managed

### Energy Transition Risk
- **Balanced approach**: Maintain oil & gas while growing new energies
- **Optionality**: Preserve flexibility as transition evolves
- **Customer focus**: Serve hard-to-abate sectors
