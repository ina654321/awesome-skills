# Dieselgate Aftermath & Legacy

**Event Date:** September 18, 2015 (EPA notice of violation)  
**Scope:** 11 million vehicles worldwide with EA189 defeat device

---

## Timeline of Events

### 2015: Crisis Eruption
- **Sept 18:** EPA issues notice of violation to VW
- **Sept 20:** VW admits to installing defeat devices
- **Sept 23:** CEO Martin Winterkorn resigns
- **Nov 3:** EPA finds additional defeat device software
- **Total Impact:** €16.2B provision in 2015

### 2016-2017: Global Settlements
- **US Settlement:** $4.3B criminal/civil penalties
- **Vehicle Buybacks:** 500,000+ vehicles in US
- **Executive Prosecutions:** Several arrests and convictions
- **European Actions:** Recall programs, regulatory investigations

### 2018-2020: Legal Resolution
- **US Criminal Plea:** VW AG pleads guilty to felony charges
- **Executive Trials:** Ongoing in Germany, US
- **Consumer Settlements:** EU class actions progress
- **Total Costs:** Exceeding €30B globally

### 2021-Present: Ongoing Impact
- **Residual Litigation:** Individual claims continue
- **Governance Changes:** Implemented
- **Strategic Pivot:** Accelerated electrification

## US Settlement Details

### Criminal Penalties
- VW AG: $2.8B criminal fine
- VW AG: $1.5B civil penalties

### Vehicle Programs
- **Buyback Option:** 475,000+ vehicles
- **Repair Option:** Emissions modifications
- **Compensation:** $5,100-$10,000 per owner
- **Environmental Remediation:** $2.7B fund
- **Clean Energy Investment:** $2B over 10 years (Electrify America)

### Executive Accountability
- **Oliver Schmidt:** 7 years prison (US)
- **James Liang:** 40 months prison
- **German Investigations:** Multiple executives charged

## European Response

### Recall Programs
- **Affected Vehicles:** 8.5 million in EU
- **Fix Types:** Software updates, hardware modifications
- **Customer Compensation:** Limited vs. US
- **Timeline:** 2016-2020

### Regulatory Actions
- **EU Investigation:** DG COMP proceedings
- **Member State Actions:** National investigations
- **Emissions Testing:** New WLTP, RDE procedures

### Consumer Litigation
- **Germany:** Dieselgate class actions (ongoing)
- **UK:** Group litigation order
- **Netherlands:** Collective action
- **Belgium, France, Italy:** Various claims

## Governance Transformation

### Board Changes
- **Separation:** Chair/CEO roles clarified
- **Independent Directors:** Increased representation
- **Compliance Committee:** Board-level oversight

### Management Changes
- **CEO:** Matthias Müller (2015-2018) → Herbert Diess (2018-2022) → Oliver Blume (2022-)
- **Compliance Function:** Elevated to C-level
- **Regional Accountability:** Enhanced local oversight

### Compliance Systems
1. **Code of Conduct:** Updated and mandatory training
2. **Whistleblower System:** Global hotline, protections
3. **Risk Management:** Enhanced ERM framework
4. **Internal Audit:** Expanded scope and independence

## Strategic Pivot: Zukunftspakt

### Post-Dieselgate Strategy
The crisis accelerated VW's transformation:

#### Electrification Acceleration
- **Original:** Gradual hybridization
- **Post-Dieselgate:** Aggressive BEV push
- **Investment:** €50B+ in electrification

#### ID. Series Launch
- ID.3 as " Beetle moment"
- Clean break from diesel past
- New brand identity

#### Sustainability Integration
- **ESG Reporting:** Enhanced transparency
- **Carbon Neutral:** 2050 target (now 2040)
- **Circular Economy:** Battery recycling focus

## Lessons Learned

### Corporate Culture
- **Engineering Excellence:** Must include ethical conduct
- **Transparency:** Proactive disclosure vs. reactive
- **Compliance:** Embedded in product development

### Regulatory Engagement
- **Proactive Dialogue:** Early engagement with regulators
- **Global Coordination:** Consistent standards
- **Testing Transparency:** Independent verification

### Crisis Management
- **Speed of Response:** Days, not weeks
- **Stakeholder Communication:** Multi-channel, frequent
- **Accountability:** Personal consequences

## Dieselgate as Catalyst

While devastating financially and reputationally, Dieselgate served as a catalyst for:
1. **Accelerated Electrification:** Forced EV commitment
2. **Cultural Change:** Compliance-first culture
3. **Transparency:** Industry-leading ESG reporting
4. **Innovation:** Software-first approach (despite Cariad struggles)

The crisis ultimately positioned VW to lead the industry's transformation, though at enormous cost.
