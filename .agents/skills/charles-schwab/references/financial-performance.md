## The Cash Sorting Crisis (2023)

### What Happened

When the Federal Reserve raised interest rates from near-zero to 5%+ in 2022-2023:

1. **Schwab Bank Deposits**: Yielded ~0.5% on sweep accounts
2. **Money Market Funds**: Offered 4.5-5.5% yields
3. **Client Behavior**: Moved cash from bank to money funds
4. **Result**: Bank deposits declined ~17% year-over-year

### Impact on Schwab

| Metric | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Bank Deposits | $366.7B (down 17% YoY) |
| FHLB Borrowings | $45.6B peak |
| Net Interest Margin | Compressed to ~1.8% |
| Supplemental Funding Cost | ~5% interest rate |
| Share Price | Down ~40% at trough |

### Response

1. **Liquidity Management**:
   - Borrowed from Federal Home Loan Bank
   - Paused share buybacks
   - Maintained strong capital ratios

2. **Communication**:
   - CEO Bettinger: "Liquidity to cover 100% of bank deposits"
   - Transparent about temporary measures
   - Reassured clients and shareholders

3. **Recovery** (2024):
   - Cash sorting slowed significantly
   - Net interest margin improved to ~2.0%
   - Borrowings reduced by billions
   - Stock recovered significantly

### Lessons Learned

- Diversified business model proved resilient
- Client cash management needs improvement
- Interest rate sensitivity requires active management
- Scale provides stability during crises
