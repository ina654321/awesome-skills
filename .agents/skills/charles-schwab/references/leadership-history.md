# Charles Schwab - Leadership & History

## Founding Story

### The Beginning (1963-1975)

**1963**: Charles R. "Chuck" Schwab and two partners launch the "Investment Indicator" investment newsletter, gaining 3,000 subscribers at $84/year.

**April 1971**: First Commander Corporation incorporated in California as a subsidiary of Commander Industries, Inc. The company operates as a traditional broker-dealer while continuing the newsletter.

**November 1971**: Charles Schwab and four partners acquire all outstanding stock from Commander Industries.

**1972**: Charles Schwab consolidates full ownership.

**1973**: Company officially renamed **Charles Schwab & Co., Inc.**

### The Revolutionary Moment (1975)

**May 1, 1975** - "May Day": SEC deregulates brokerage commissions, ending fixed rates. While traditional brokers raised prices, Schwab saw opportunity and pioneered the **discount brokerage model** - offering significantly lower commissions to attract individual investors.

This decision fundamentally changed Wall Street, democratizing investing for middle-income Americans.

## Major Historical Events

### 1981-1987: The Bank of America Era
- **1981**: Bank of America acquires Schwab for $55 million
- **1983-1984**: Rapid expansion, but culture clash emerges
- **1987**: Charles Schwab leads management buyback for $280 million (5x purchase price)
- Lesson: Independence essential to client-first culture

### 1990s: Technology Revolution
- **1992**: StreetSmart - first Windows-based trading software
- **1995**: Online trading launches
- **1996**: eSchwab - Internet trading platform
- **1998**: Peak of dot-com boom; stock hits all-time high

### 2000s: Challenges and Reinvention
- **2000**: Acquired U.S. Trust ($2.7B) - later sold
- **2001-2002**: Dot-com bust hits trading volumes
- **2003**: Charles Schwab returns as CEO, cuts fees
- **2004**: Schwab becomes bank holding company
- **2007-2008**: Financial crisis - company remains stable but conservative

### 2010s: Innovation and Disruption
- **2011**: Schwab ETF OneSource launched (commission-free ETF trading)
- **2015**: Schwab Intelligent Portfolios launched (robo-advisor)
- **2019**: October - Eliminated commissions on stocks, ETFs, options
  - Immediately matched by Fidelity, TD Ameritrade, E*TRADE
  - Destroyed ~$5B annual industry revenue
  - Demonstrated Schwab's willingness to disrupt itself

### 2020s: Scale and Integration
- **November 2019**: Announced TD Ameritrade acquisition ($26B)
- **October 2020**: TD Ameritrade deal closes
- **2021**: Headquarters moves to Westlake, Texas
- **2022**: SEC settlement regarding Intelligent Portfolios cash disclosures
- **September 2023**: TD Ameritrade client migration complete
- **2023**: Banking crisis - "cash sorting" challenge
- **2024**: Assets surpass $10 trillion; CEO transition

## Corporate Culture

### Core Values
1. **Through Clients' Eyes**: Make decisions from client perspective
2. **Integrity**: Do what's right, not what's easy
3. **Innovation**: Challenge status quo to benefit investors
4. **Respect**: Value diversity and individual contributions
5. **Teamwork**: Collaborate across boundaries

### "No Trade-offs" Philosophy
Clients should not have to choose between:
- Low costs OR quality service
- Technology OR human guidance
- Self-directed OR advised

### Servant Leadership
- Walt Bettinger championed this approach
- Leaders exist to serve employees who serve clients
- 2024 median employee compensation: $125,038
- CEO-to-worker pay ratio: 211:1
