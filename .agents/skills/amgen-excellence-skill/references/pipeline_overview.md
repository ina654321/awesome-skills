# Amgen Pipeline Overview Reference

**Last Updated:** March 2026  
**R&D Investment:** $6.0 billion annually (2024)

## Executive Summary

Amgen's pipeline is the broadest and deepest in the company's 45+ year history. With significant investment in R&D ($6B+, up 25% in 2024), the company is advancing numerous potential first-in-class and best-in-class medicines across four therapeutic areas. The integration of deCODE genetics continues to improve target validation and success rates.

## Key Pipeline Programs

### MariTide (AMG 133) - Priority Program

**Overview:**
- Most anticipated pipeline asset
- Potential game-changer in obesity market

**Mechanism:**
- Dual agonist: GLP-1 receptor + GIP receptor
- Multi-specific antibody construct
- Monthly dosing potential (vs. weekly competitors)

**Differentiation:**
| Feature | MariTide | Competitors |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Dosing | Monthly | Weekly |
| Mechanism | GLP-1 + GIP dual | Single or dual |
| Format | Antibody-based | Peptide-based |
| Convenience | Potentially superior | Current standard |

**Development Timeline:**
- Phase 2: Top-line results reported 2024
- Phase 3: Preparation/planned initiation
- Potential approval: Late 2020s (if successful)

**Market Context:**
- Obesity market: $50B+ potential
- Leaders: Novo Nordisk (Wegovy/Ozempic), Eli Lilly (Zepbound/Mounjaro)
- MariTide monthly dosing could capture significant share if approved

**Risk Factors:**
- Intense competition from well-funded rivals
- Phase 2 to Phase 3 translation risk
- Manufacturing scale-up challenges

### TEZSPIRE (Tezepelumab) Expansion

**Approved:**
- Severe asthma (2021)
- Chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps (CRSwNP) - Oct 2025

**Phase 3 Expansion:**
- Additional inflammatory indications
- Broad potential given TSLP's role in inflammation

**Strategic Importance:**
- First-in-class TSLP inhibitor
- Strong growth trajectory
- Platform for inflammation portfolio

### Rocatinlimab (AMG 451)

**Target:** OX40 inhibitor
**Indication:** Atopic dermatitis
**Status:** Phase 3

**Market:**
- Competes with Dupixent (Sanofi/Regeneron)
- Large unmet need in atopic dermatitis
- OX40 novel mechanism vs. IL-4/IL-13

### UPLIZNA (Inebilizumab) Expansion

**Approved:**
- NMOSD (2020)
- IgG4-related disease (April 2025)

**Phase 3:**
- Myasthenia gravis (FDA decision pending)
- Large market opportunity if approved

## Pipeline Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Phase 3 Failure:**
   - Despite genetic validation, clinical translation risk remains
   - Historical failure rates ~50% even in Phase 3

2. **Competition:**
   - Obesity market increasingly crowded
   - First-mover advantages significant

3. **Manufacturing Scale:**
   - MariTide and other biologics require massive capacity
   - Scale-up challenges potential bottleneck

4. **Regulatory:**
   - FDA approval timelines uncertain
   - Safety requirements increasingly stringent

### Mitigation Strategies

- **Diversified pipeline** across multiple programs
- **Human genetic validation** through deCODE
- **Manufacturing investments** ahead of approval
- **Global regulatory strategy** (FDA + EMA + Japan)
