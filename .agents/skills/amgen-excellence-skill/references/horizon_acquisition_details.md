# Horizon Therapeutics Acquisition Reference

**Acquisition Value:** $27.8 billion (all-cash)  
**Announced:** December 12, 2022  
**Completed:** October 6, 2023  
**Strategic Significance:** Largest acquisition in Amgen history

---

## Executive Summary

The acquisition of Horizon Therapeutics represented Amgen's strategic entry into the rare disease market and significantly expanded its inflammation portfolio. The deal added first-in-class medicines for underserved patient populations and reduced Amgen's reliance on legacy products facing biosimilar competition.

---

## Strategic Rationale

### 1. Rare Disease Market Entry
- High unmet medical needs
- Premium pricing with strong reimbursement
- Specialized commercial model
- Durable franchises with limited competition

### 2. First-in-Class Medicines
- Tepezza: Only approved treatment for thyroid eye disease
- Krystexxa: Transformative therapy for refractory gout
- UPLIZNA: Novel approach to NMOSD and IgG4-RD

### 3. Portfolio Diversification
- Reduced dependence on Enbrel, Prolia (facing biosimilars)
- Added growth drivers with patent protection runway
- Balanced revenue across therapeutic areas

### 4. Commercial Synergies
- Amgen's global reach (100+ countries)
- Manufacturing expertise
- R&D capabilities for pipeline expansion

## FTC Consent Order

### Key Restrictions

**Bundling Prohibition:**
- Amgen prohibited from bundling Horizon products (Tepezza, Krystexxa) with existing Amgen products
- Cannot use rebates on Amgen blockbusters to secure formulary placement for Horizon products
- Cannot condition discounts on preferential treatment of Horizon products

**Why This Matters:**
- Traditional Amgen strategy involves portfolio contracting
- Horizon products are medical benefit (infused), Amgen products largely pharmacy benefit
- Required separate commercial strategy development

### Compliance Structure
- Regulatory review of insurance contracts
- Behavioral oversight commitments
- Potential penalties for violations

## Key Horizon Products

### Tepezza (teprotumumab-trbw)

**Indication:** Thyroid eye disease (TED)

**Market Position:**
- First and only FDA-approved treatment for TED
- Orphan drug designation
- Breakthrough therapy designation
- Infused therapy (medical benefit)

**Clinical Profile:**
- IGF-1R inhibitor mechanism
- Reduces proptosis (eye bulging)
- Improves quality of life
- 8-infusion treatment course

**Commercial Performance:**
| Year | Sales | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 2022 | $1.96B | Peak Horizon standalone |
| 2023 | $1.77B | Amgen partial year |
| 2024 | ~$1.5B+ | Integration period |
| Q1 2025 | $381M | Below expectations |

**Challenges:**
- Q1 2025 sales missed expectations ($381M vs. ~$450M consensus)
- Wholesaler inventory adjustments cited
- Viridian Therapeutics developing competitor (veligrotug)
- Hearing loss side effect lawsuits (inherited from Horizon)

**Competitive Threat:**
- Viridian's veligrotug: Phase 3 data positive (early 2025)
- Potential FDA filing 2025-2026
- Could be first competitor to Tepezza

### Krystexxa (pegloticase)

**Indication:** Chronic refractory gout

**Market Position:**
- Transformative efficacy for severe cases
- Patients who have failed conventional therapy
- Orphan drug status

**Clinical Profile:**
- Recombinant uricase enzyme
- Rapidly reduces uric acid levels
- Infused therapy with pre-medication

**Commercial Performance:**
| Year | Sales | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 2024 | ~$900M+ | Strong performance |
| Q1 2025 | $236M | Below expectations |

**Challenges:**
- Q1 2025 shortfall ($236M vs. ~$293M expected)
- Similar wholesaler inventory dynamics as Tepezza
- Smaller patient population but high value

### UPLIZNA (inebilizumab-cdon)

**Indication:** NMOSD (neuromyelitis optica spectrum disorder)

**Recent Approvals:**
- Original: NMOSD (2020)
- Expansion: IgG4-related disease (April 2025)
- Potential: Myasthenia gravis (FDA decision pending)

**Market Position:**
- Competes with Alexion's Soliris, Ultomiris
- CD19-directed B-cell depletion
- Differentiated mechanism

**Commercial Performance:**
- Growing but smaller than Tepezza/Krystexxa
- Q1 2025: $91M (below ~$111M expected)
- Label expansion driving growth

### TAVNEOS (avacopan)

**Indication:** ANCA-associated vasculitis

**Profile:**
- Oral C5a receptor inhibitor
- First approved oral therapy for this indication
- Replaces chronic steroid use

**Position:** Growth driver with strong clinical profile

## Integration Status

### 2024 Performance
- **Revenue Contribution:** $4.2 billion (full year)
- **Comparison:** $954 million in 2023 (partial year)
- **Growth:** Significant YoY increase

### 2025 Early Challenges
**Q1 2025 Headlines:**
- All three key products below expectations
- Management attributed to wholesaler inventory changes
- Stressed "18 months since close" timeframe

**Management Response:**
- CFO Peter Griffith: "Just a reminder that the transaction closed in October of 2023... It's been about a year and a half, although so much has happened in the world it might feel like more than that."

### Integration Activities
1. **Commercial Integration:**
   - Amgen global sales force deployment
   - Separate Horizon commercial structure maintained initially
   - Gradual integration to preserve expertise

2. **Manufacturing:**
   - Horizon products manufactured at contract sites
   - Potential for Amgen manufacturing integration

3. **R&D:**
   - Leveraging Horizon rare disease expertise
   - Pipeline expansion opportunities

4. **Corporate Functions:**
   - HR, finance, IT integration ongoing
   - Workforce optimization (350+ positions reduced)

## Strategic Assessment

### Strengths of Acquisition
1. Rare disease expertise and infrastructure
2. First-in-class products with limited competition
3. Reduced biosimilar exposure
4. Specialty care capabilities

### Risks and Challenges
1. FTC consent restrictions on bundling
2. Integration complexity and timeline
3. Competitive threats (Viridian in TED)
4. Higher cost structure than traditional Amgen products
5. Q1 2025 performance raising questions

### Long-Term Outlook
- Rare disease franchise expected to stabilize and grow
- Tepezza facing first competition 2026+
- Krystexxa maintains strong position
- UPLIZNA label expansions driving growth
- Platform for future rare disease BD&L
